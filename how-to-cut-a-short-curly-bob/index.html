How To Cut A Short Curly Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Mar 18, 2023 · 8 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially when dealing with curls. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and flattering short curly bob right at home. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps for cutting a short curly bob, from preparation to finishing touches. Plus, we’ll share some tips and hair care advice to ensure your new cut looks great and stays healthy. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents:

  • Preparation
  • Sectioning and Cutting
  • Texturizing
  • Styling
  • Tips for Cutting a Short Curly Bob
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Short Curly Bobs

Preparation

Before you begin cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

It’s also a good idea to start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would, and let it air dry or blow dry on a low setting. Avoid using any heavy styling products, as they can weigh down your curls and make them harder to cut.

Sectioning and Cutting

First, separate your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Clip each section up and out of the way, leaving only the bottom section loose. This will make it easier to cut the back of your hair.

Start with the back section and comb through it to remove any tangles. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers, and cut off the desired length. Remember to cut less than you think you need, as curly hair tends to spring up when it dries. Continue cutting small sections until you have trimmed the entire back section.

Unclip the side sections and repeat the same process, cutting hair in small sections until you reach the desired length. Be sure to check your work in a mirror as you go along, to ensure that both sides are even.

Texturizing

If you want to add some texture to your cut, use thinning shears to remove bulk from the ends of your hair. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers, and use the shears to make small snips along the ends. This will create a more layered look and help your curls bounce and move more easily.

Styling

Once you have finished cutting and texturizing, it’s time to style your new short curly bob. Apply a small amount of curl defining cream or mousse to damp hair, and use your fingers to scrunch and shape your curls. Let your hair air dry, or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your curls without disrupting their shape.

Tips for Cutting a Short Curly Bob

  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as curly hair tends to shrink as it dries. Instead, cut it when it’s dry and in its natural state.
  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut less than you think you need. You can always go back and trim more if necessary.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean, precise cuts.
  • When using thinning shears, be careful not to overdo it. Too much thinning can make your hair look wispy and thin.

FAQs

How often should I cut my short curly bob?

It’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own hair if it’s very curly?

Yes, you can cut your own hair regardless of its texture. Just be sure to follow the proper techniques and use the right tools.

What’s the best way to maintain a short curly bob?

Regular trims, deep conditioning treatments, and avoiding heat styling are all key to maintaining healthy, bouncy curls.

Hair Care for Short Curly Bobs

Curly hair requires special care to stay healthy and frizz-free. Here are some tips for keeping your short curly bob looking its best:

  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your curls.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle wet hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and always use a heat protectant spray if you do.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

Cutting a short curly bob can seem daunting, but with these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful and flattering cut right at home. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and check your work as you go along. With a little practice, you’ll be a pro at cutting your own curly hair in no time!
