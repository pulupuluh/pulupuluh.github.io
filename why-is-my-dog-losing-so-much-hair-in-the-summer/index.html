Why Is My Dog Losing So Much Hair In The Summer Understanding The Causes

Written by William May 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Summertime is the perfect season for outdoor activities with your furry friend. However, as the temperature rises, some pet owners may notice their dog losing more hair than usual. This can be alarming, but it's important to understand that shedding is a normal process for dogs. In this blog post, we will discuss the reasons why dogs lose so much hair in the summer and how you can take care of your dog's coat during this season.

Causes of Hair Loss in Dogs During Summer

As the temperature rises, dogs tend to shed more hair to regulate their body temperature. This is a natural process called "blowing coat" where dogs shed their old fur and replace it with a new one. However, there are several other reasons why your dog may be losing more hair than usual during summer:

  • Stress: Dogs can experience stress due to changes in their routine or environment. This can lead to excessive shedding.
  • Poor Nutrition: Your dog may be losing hair due to a lack of essential nutrients in their diet. Make sure your dog is eating a balanced and nutritious diet.
  • Parasites: Fleas, ticks, and other parasites can cause your dog to lose hair. Regular parasite prevention is essential to maintain your dog's coat.
  • Medical Issues: Certain medical conditions such as allergies, hormonal imbalances, and infections can also lead to hair loss in dogs.

If you notice any other symptoms such as itching or redness along with hair loss, it's best to consult your veterinarian.

How to Take Care of Your Dog's Coat During Summer

Proper grooming and maintenance are crucial to keep your dog's coat healthy and shiny during summer. Here are some tips:

  • Brush your dog's coat regularly to remove loose hair and prevent matting.
  • Bathe your dog with a mild shampoo to keep their coat clean and free from parasites.
  • Trim your dog's hair regularly, especially around their paws and ears.
  • Provide your dog with a balanced and nutritious diet.
  • Make sure your dog stays hydrated by providing them with plenty of water.

Step-by-Step Guide to Maintain Your Dog's Coat in Summer

Follow these simple steps to maintain your dog's coat during summer:

  1. Start by brushing your dog's coat to remove loose hair and tangles.
  2. Trim your dog's hair, especially around their paws and ears.
  3. Bathe your dog with a mild shampoo and rinse thoroughly.
  4. Dry your dog's coat using a towel or a blow dryer on a low setting.
  5. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your dog's coat to keep it shiny and healthy.
  6. Brush your dog's coat again to distribute the conditioner and remove any remaining loose hair.

Tips for Dealing with Excessive Shedding in Dogs

If you notice your dog shedding excessively during summer, here are some tips:

  • Invest in a high-quality shedding brush to remove loose hair.
  • Feed your dog a balanced and nutritious diet to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Consult your veterinarian if you notice any other symptoms along with hair loss.

FAQs About Dog Hair Loss in Summer

Q: Is it normal for dogs to lose hair in summer?

A: Yes, it's normal for dogs to shed more hair in summer to regulate their body temperature.

Q: How can I prevent my dog from shedding excessively in summer?

A: Regular grooming and maintenance, a healthy diet, and parasite prevention can help prevent excessive shedding in dogs.

Q: When should I be concerned about my dog's hair loss?

A: If you notice any other symptoms such as itching, redness, or bald patches along with hair loss, it's best to consult your veterinarian.

Hair Care Products for Dogs During Summer

Here are some recommended hair care products for dogs during summer:

  • Shampoo: Use a mild shampoo such as oatmeal-based shampoo to keep your dog's coat clean and free from parasites.
  • Conditioner: Apply a leave-in conditioner to your dog's coat to keep it shiny and healthy.
  • Brush: Invest in a high-quality shedding brush to remove loose hair and prevent matting.

By following these tips and taking care of your dog's coat during summer, you can keep them healthy and comfortable throughout the season.
