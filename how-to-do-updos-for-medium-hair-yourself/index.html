How To Do Updos For Medium Hair Yourself

Written by Robby May 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Updos are a great way to style your hair for a special occasion or even just for a day out. If you have medium-length hair, you might think that updos are not an option for you. However, with a little creativity and some practice, you can easily create a variety of updos for medium hair yourself. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to do updos for medium hair yourself, as well as tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Do Updos for Medium Hair Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Creating Updos for Medium Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Updos

Step-by-Step Guide

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools for creating updos for medium hair. These include a brush or comb, bobby pins, hair elastics, and hairspray. Now, let's get started:

Step 1: Brush and Divide Your Hair

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots. Then, divide your hair into three sections: one at the top of your head, one at the middle, and one at the nape of your neck.

Step 2: Create a Ponytail

Take the top section of your hair and create a ponytail at the crown of your head. Secure it with a hair elastic.

Step 3: Twist and Pin

Take the middle section of your hair and twist it. Then, wrap the twisted section around the ponytail and secure it with bobby pins.

Step 4: Create Another Ponytail

Take the bottom section of your hair and create another ponytail at the nape of your neck. Secure it with a hair elastic.

Step 5: Twist and Pin Again

Take the ponytail from the bottom section of your hair and twist it. Then, wrap the twisted section around the previous twist and secure it with bobby pins.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

Once you have secured all the twists with bobby pins, finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the updo in place.

Tips for Creating Updos for Medium Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you create updos for medium hair:

  • Experiment with different twists, braids, and knots to create unique updos.
  • Use bobby pins that match the color of your hair to make them less noticeable.
  • Try adding hair accessories, such as a headband or hairpin, to dress up your updo.
  • Practice makes perfect! Don't be discouraged if your first few attempts don't turn out the way you want. Keep trying until you find a style that works for you.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about updos for medium hair:

Can I create an updo with short hair?

While updos are generally easier to create with medium or long hair, it is possible to create an updo with short hair. You may need to use more bobby pins or hair elastics to keep your hair in place.

What type of hairspray should I use?

Look for a hairspray that provides a strong hold without making your hair feel stiff or crunchy. Avoid using too much hairspray, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Can I create an updo without using any hair products?

While it is possible to create an updo without using any hair products, using hairspray or a styling product can help hold your hair in place and prevent flyaways.

Hair Care Tips for Updos

Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and looking great when creating updos:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often. These can damage your hair and make it more difficult to style.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray or other styling products, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Try not to wash your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more difficult to style.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create a variety of updos for medium hair yourself. Whether you're getting ready for a wedding, a party, or just a day out, an updo is a great way to add some glamour to your look. So grab your hair tools and get creative!
