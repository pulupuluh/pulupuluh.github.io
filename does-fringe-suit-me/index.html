Does Fringe Suit Me The Ultimate Guide To Finding Your Perfect Fringe

Written by Bardi May 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you thinking about getting a fringe, but not sure if it will suit you? Fringes can transform your look and give you a fresh new style, but it's important to choose the right one for your face shape and hair type. In this guide, we'll take you through everything you need to know about finding the perfect fringe for you.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding a fringe that suits you is to determine your face shape. There are six main face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and long. To find out which one you have, pull your hair back and take a look in the mirror.

Rounded Face

If your face is round, you have full cheeks and a curved chin. To balance out your features, go for a long, side-swept fringe that falls just below your eyebrows. Avoid blunt or straight fringes, as they can make your face appear even rounder.

Oval Face

If your face is oval, you have a slightly curved jawline and your face is longer than it is wide. Lucky for you, pretty much any fringe will suit your face shape! Opt for a blunt fringe if you want a statement look, or a soft, wispy fringe for a more natural look.

Square Face

If your face is square, you have a strong jawline and your forehead, cheekbones, and jaw are roughly the same width. A soft, wispy fringe that falls just below your eyebrows will soften your features and add some femininity. Avoid blunt or heavy fringes, as they can make your face appear boxy.

Heart Face

If your face is heart-shaped, you have a wider forehead and a narrow chin. A long, side-swept fringe that falls just below your eyebrows will balance out your features and draw attention away from your forehead. Avoid blunt or heavy fringes, as they can make your forehead appear even wider.

Diamond Face

If your face is diamond-shaped, you have a narrow forehead and chin with wide cheekbones. A soft, wispy fringe that falls just below your eyebrows will soften your features and add some balance. Avoid blunt or heavy fringes, as they can make your cheeks appear even wider.

Long Face

If your face is long, you have a high forehead and a long, narrow chin. A long, side-swept fringe that falls just below your eyebrows will break up the length of your face and add some width. Avoid blunt or heavy fringes, as they can make your face appear even longer.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Own Fringe

If you're feeling brave and want to cut your own fringe, follow these simple steps:

  1. Start with dry hair and comb it straight forward.
  2. Decide on the length of your fringe and mark it with a small snip.
  3. Using hairdressing scissors, cut your fringe in a straight line, starting from the middle and working your way outwards.
  4. Check your fringe in the mirror and trim any uneven pieces.
  5. Style your fringe as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

To keep your fringe looking fresh and stylish, follow these tips:

  • Wash your fringe every other day to avoid oily build-up.
  • Blow-dry your fringe in the direction you want it to sit.
  • Use a round brush to smooth out your fringe and create a slight curve.
  • Trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to maintain its length and shape.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some of the most commonly asked questions about fringes:

Can I pull off a fringe if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! Just make sure you choose a fringe that works with your natural curl pattern. A soft, curly fringe can be a great way to add some definition to your curls.

Will a fringe make my forehead look smaller?

Yes, a fringe can help to balance out a larger forehead. A long, side-swept fringe is a great option for this.

Can I still wear my hair up with a fringe?

Yes, you can! A fringe can actually add some interest to an updo. Just make sure you pin it back securely so it doesn't get in your eyes.

Final Thoughts

So, does fringe suit me? With the right length and style, a fringe can suit anyone. Just make sure you choose a fringe that complements your face shape and hair type. And if you're cutting your own fringe, take your time and be patient - it's always better to start with a longer length and trim it gradually.

Remember to maintain your fringe with regular trims and styling, and enjoy your new look!
