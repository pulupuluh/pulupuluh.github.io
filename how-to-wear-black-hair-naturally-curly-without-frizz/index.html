How To Wear Black Hair Naturally Curly Without Frizz

Written by Aprilia May 15, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of battling with frizz when wearing your natural curls? If you have black hair, you know all too well the struggle of maintaining defined curls while keeping frizz at bay. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to wear black hair naturally curly without frizz. Here is a table of content for easy navigation:

Table of Contents

  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips to Prevent Frizz
  4. FAQs

How to Wear Black Hair Naturally Curly Without Frizz

The key to wearing black hair naturally curly without frizz is to keep your hair moisturized and defined. Here are some steps you can take to achieve this:

1. Start with a Good Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner will help keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz. Look for products that contain natural oils, such as coconut oil, jojoba oil or argan oil, which are great for keeping your hair moisturized.

2. Use a Leave-in Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and defined. This will help prevent frizz and make it easier to style your curls.

3. Apply a Styling Product

Using a curl defining cream, gel or mousse will help define your curls and keep them in place. Make sure to apply the product evenly to each section of your hair to avoid clumping.

4. Do Not Touch Your Hair While It Dries

Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser on low heat. Do not touch your hair while it dries, as this can cause frizz. Once your hair is completely dry, you can fluff it with your fingers to add volume.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to wear black hair naturally curly without frizz:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner. Rinse your hair thoroughly and gently squeeze out excess water.

2. Apply Leave-in Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, working it through each section with your fingers or a wide-tooth comb. Make sure to cover each strand of hair evenly.

3. Apply Styling Product

Apply a curl defining cream, gel or mousse to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly. Use your fingers to separate your curls and help define them.

4. Let Your Hair Air Dry or Use a Diffuser

Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser on low heat. Do not touch your hair while it dries, as this can cause frizz. Once your hair is completely dry, you can fluff it with your fingers to add volume.

Tips to Prevent Frizz

Here are some additional tips to help prevent frizz:

  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water.
  • Avoid touching your hair while it dries, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.

FAQs

Q: Can I wear my natural curls without using any products?

A: While it is possible to wear your natural curls without using any products, using a curl defining cream, gel or mousse can help define your curls and prevent frizz.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It is recommended to wash your hair every 7-10 days to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my natural hair?

A: It is recommended to use sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.

Hair Care for Black Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for your black hair:

  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or relaxers on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can wear your black hair naturally curly without frizz. Remember, the key is to keep your hair moisturized and defined, and to avoid touching your hair while it dries. With a little bit of patience and practice, you can achieve beautiful, defined curls that are free from frizz.
