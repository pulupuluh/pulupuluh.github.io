Do You Cut Curly Hair Dry Or Wet A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair requires a different approach when it comes to cutting. One of the biggest debates in the curly hair community is whether to cut hair dry or wet. In this article, we will explore the pros and cons of each method and help you decide which one is best for you.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Description

The debate about cutting curly hair dry or wet has been going on for years. Some stylists swear by cutting hair wet, while others prefer to cut hair dry. Both methods have their pros and cons and can produce great results when done correctly.

When you cut curly hair wet, the hair is stretched out, making it easier to see the length and shape of the hair. However, when the hair dries, it can shrink up to two inches, making it difficult to achieve the desired length and shape. Cutting hair dry allows you to see the true length and shape of the hair, but it can be more challenging to achieve a precise cut.

How to Cut Curly Hair

Before we dive into the debate about cutting curly hair dry or wet, let's talk about the basics of cutting curly hair. Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. Here are some tips for cutting curly hair:

  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to avoid frizz and split ends.
  • Cut your hair in its natural state. Curly hair looks different when it's wet and dry, so it's best to cut it when it's dry.
  • Don't cut your hair too short. Curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so it's best to leave it a little longer than you want it to be.
  • Cut your hair one curl at a time. This will help you achieve a more precise cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of cutting curly hair, let's talk about the step-by-step process:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair.
  2. Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  3. Section your hair using clips or hair ties.
  4. Cut your hair one curl at a time, starting with the bottom layer and working your way up.
  5. Trim any split ends or damaged hair.
  6. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for cutting curly hair:

  • Don't use a razor to cut your hair. This can cause frizz and split ends.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to cut a little bit at a time and see how your curls react.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, visit a professional stylist who specializes in cutting curly hair.

FAQs

Should I cut my curly hair dry or wet?

The answer to this question depends on your personal preference and the type of curls you have. If you have looser curls, cutting your hair wet may be easier. If you have tighter curls, cutting your hair dry may be the better option. Ultimately, it's up to you to decide which method works best for your hair.

Can I cut my own curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. However, if you're unsure about cutting your hair, it's best to visit a professional stylist who specializes in cutting curly hair.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to trim your curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair is just as important as cutting it. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Sleep on a satin or silk pillowcase to avoid frizz and breakage.

Whether you choose to cut your curly hair dry or wet, the most important thing is to take care of your curls. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls.
