How To Hide Long Hair In A Beanie Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Mar 09, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Beanies are a great way to keep warm during the colder months, but they can be a problem for people with long hair. Long hair can get tangled and messy inside a beanie, making it difficult to stay comfortable and stylish. In this article, we'll show you how to hide long hair in a beanie so you can look your best no matter the weather.

How to Hide Long Hair in a Beanie

There are a few different ways you can hide long hair in a beanie. Here are some tips:

1. Put Your Hair in a Bun

One of the easiest ways to hide long hair in a beanie is to put your hair in a bun. This will keep your hair out of the way and prevent it from getting tangled inside the beanie. To create a bun, gather your hair into a ponytail and twist it into a bun at the back of your head. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place.

2. Braid Your Hair

Another option is to braid your hair before putting on the beanie. This will keep your hair neat and tidy, preventing it from getting tangled inside the beanie. You can braid your hair into a single braid or multiple braids depending on your preference.

3. Use a Hair Tie

If you don't want to put your hair in a bun or braid it, you can use a hair tie to keep your hair in place. Simply gather your hair into a ponytail and tie it off with a hair tie. This will keep your hair out of the way and prevent it from getting tangled inside the beanie.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to hide long hair in a beanie:

Step 1: Choose Your Method

Decide whether you want to put your hair in a bun, braid it, or use a hair tie.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

If you're putting your hair in a bun or braiding it, brush your hair to remove any tangles. If you're using a hair tie, simply gather your hair into a ponytail.

Step 3: Create Your Bun or Braid

If you're putting your hair in a bun, twist your hair into a bun at the back of your head and secure it with bobby pins. If you're braiding your hair, create a single braid or multiple braids depending on your preference.

Step 4: Put on Your Beanie

Put on your beanie, making sure that it covers your ears and forehead. Pull the beanie down over your head until it's snug and comfortable.

Step 5: Adjust Your Hair

If your hair is sticking out of the beanie, tuck it back in using bobby pins or a hair tie. Make sure that your hair is comfortable and not too tight.

Tips for Hiding Long Hair in a Beanie

Here are some tips to help you hide long hair in a beanie:

1. Use Bobby Pins

Bobby pins are a great tool for keeping your hair in place inside a beanie. Use them to secure your bun or braid in place, or to tuck any loose hairs back into the beanie.

2. Choose a Stretchy Beanie

A stretchy beanie will be more comfortable and easier to wear if you have long hair. Look for a beanie made of a stretchy material like wool or acrylic.

3. Experiment with Different Styles

Don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles to see what works best for you. Try different braids, buns, and hair ties to find the style that keeps your hair secure and comfortable.

FAQs

Q: Can I wear a beanie with long hair?

A: Yes, you can wear a beanie with long hair. Just make sure to put your hair in a bun, braid it, or use a hair tie to keep it out of the way.

Q: Will a beanie damage my hair?

A: No, wearing a beanie won't damage your hair. Just make sure that your hair isn't too tight or uncomfortable inside the beanie.

Q: Can I wear a beanie if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can wear a beanie if you have curly hair. Just make sure to put your hair in a bun, braid it, or use a hair tie to keep it out of the way.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

1. Use a Good Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy. Look for products that are designed for your hair type.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Don't wash your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

3. Use a Heat Protectant

If you use heat tools like a curling iron or straightener, make sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

4. Get Regular Haircuts

Get regular haircuts to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to hide your long hair in a beanie and stay warm and stylish all winter long!
