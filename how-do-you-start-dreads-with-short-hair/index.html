How Do You Start Dreads With Short Hair

Written by Christine May 10, 2023 · 6 min read

Starting dreadlocks can be a daunting task, especially if you have short hair. However, it is possible to start dreads with short hair, and this article will guide you through the process.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Dreads with Short Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

How to Start Dreads with Short Hair

The first step in starting dreads with short hair is to let your hair grow out to at least 2 inches. This will give your hair enough length to start the locking process.

Next, choose a method of starting your dreads. There are several methods to choose from, including backcombing, twisting, and braiding.

Backcombing involves teasing the hair backwards towards the scalp, which creates knots that will eventually turn into dreads. Twisting involves twisting sections of hair together to form a dread. Braiding involves braiding the hair tightly and leaving it in for several weeks to allow the hair to lock.

Once you have chosen a method, it is important to maintain your dreads by keeping them clean and well-moisturized.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to start dreads with short hair:

  1. Let your hair grow out to at least 2 inches
  2. Choose a method of starting your dreads (backcombing, twisting, or braiding)
  3. Section your hair into small sections
  4. Begin the chosen method on each section of hair
  5. Use a small amount of beeswax or locking gel to help the hair lock
  6. Continue the chosen method on each section of hair until all of your hair is in dreads
  7. Maintain your dreads by keeping them clean and moisturized

Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you start dreads with short hair:

  • Be patient – it takes time for dreads to form, especially with short hair
  • Avoid using too much product – this can cause buildup and prevent the hair from locking
  • Consider using a dreadlock shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a silk or satin scarf to prevent frizz
  • Avoid using heat on your hair – this can damage the hair and prevent it from locking

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads with short hair:

  • Can you start dreads with really short hair? Yes, but it may take longer for the hair to lock. It is recommended to wait until the hair is at least 2 inches long.
  • How long does it take for dreads to form? It can take anywhere from 3 months to a year for dreads to form, depending on the method used and the texture of the hair.
  • Do you need to cut your hair to start dreads? No, you can start dreads with any length of hair.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once you have started your dreads, it is important to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are some tips for hair care for dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a dreadlock shampoo
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils that can cause buildup
  • Use a light leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or other harsh chemicals

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can enjoy beautiful, healthy dreadlocks, no matter how short your hair is.
