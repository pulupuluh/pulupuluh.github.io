How To Start Dreadlocks With Long Hair

Written by Eveline Aug 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Starting dreadlocks with long hair can be a long and tedious process. However, with proper care and maintenance, it can result in beautiful and healthy dreadlocks. In this article, we will guide you through the steps on how to start dreadlocks with long hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

Before starting your dreadlocks, it is important to understand that it is a long-term commitment. You will need to have patience and dedication to maintain your dreadlocks. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Wash your hair thoroughly with a residue-free shampoo to remove any product build-up. You may also want to consider using a clarifying shampoo to ensure that your hair is clean and free from any oils or residues. Towel dry your hair, leaving it slightly damp.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using a comb or your fingers. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreadlocks you want. Make sure that the sections are even in size and shape.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the root of each section, use a dread comb to backcomb your hair. Hold the section of hair firmly and comb downwards towards the scalp. Repeat this process until the hair starts to form a tight dreadlock.

Step 4: Roll Your Hair

After backcombing, roll the dreadlock between your palms to smooth out any bumps or kinks. This will also help to compress the hair and make it tighter. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to starting dreadlocks with long hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo.
  2. Section your hair into even sections.
  3. Backcomb each section using a dread comb.
  4. Roll each section between your palms.
  5. Secure each dreadlock with a rubber band or hair tie.
  6. Leave your hair to dry naturally.
  7. Remove the rubber bands or hair ties once the hair is dry.
  8. Maintain your dreadlocks by palm rolling and separating them regularly.
  9. Keep your hair and scalp clean by washing it with a residue-free shampoo.
  10. Be patient and enjoy the journey of growing your dreadlocks.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you start your dreadlocks:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair before starting your dreadlocks.
  • Moisturize your scalp and hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Palm roll your dreadlocks regularly to keep them tight and neat.
  • Separate your dreadlocks regularly to prevent them from merging together.
  • Avoid using products that contain oils or waxes as they can cause build-up and prevent your hair from locking.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with long hair:

How long does it take to start dreadlocks with long hair?

It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year to start dreadlocks with long hair, depending on how fast your hair locks and how well you maintain them.

Can I start dreadlocks with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair. However, it may take longer for your hair to lock as relaxed hair is usually more fragile and prone to breakage.

Can I wash my hair once I start my dreadlocks?

Yes, you can wash your hair once you start your dreadlocks. However, it is important to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using products that contain oils or waxes.

Hair Care

Here are some tips on how to care for your hair once you start your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Moisturize your scalp and hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Palm roll your dreadlocks regularly to keep them tight and neat.
  • Separate your dreadlocks regularly to prevent them from merging together.
  • Avoid using products that contain oils or waxes as they can cause build-up and prevent your hair from locking.

Starting dreadlocks with long hair requires patience and dedication, but the end result is worth it. Follow these steps and tips to start your journey to beautiful and healthy dreadlocks.
