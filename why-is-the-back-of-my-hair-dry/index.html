Why Is The Back Of My Hair Dry

Written by Aprilia May 03, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you wondering why the back of your hair is dry no matter how much you moisturize it? This is a common problem that many people face, and there are several reasons why this might be happening. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind dry hair and provide tips on how to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Description

Dry hair is a common problem that can be caused by a variety of factors. The back of the hair is particularly prone to dryness due to several reasons. The hair at the back of our head is more tightly coiled than the hair at the top, making it more difficult for natural oils to travel down the hair shaft. Additionally, the back of the head is more exposed to friction from clothing and pillowcases, which can lead to breakage and dryness.

How to

If you're struggling with dry hair at the back of your head, there are a few things you can do to help alleviate the problem:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo

Using a moisturizing shampoo can help to hydrate your hair and prevent dryness. Look for shampoos that contain natural oils, such as argan oil or coconut oil, which can help to nourish and moisturize your hair.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning your hair once or twice a week can help to keep it hydrated and healthy. Use a deep conditioning treatment that contains ingredients like shea butter, jojoba oil, and honey, which can help to moisturize and nourish your hair.

3. Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and breakage. Try to limit your hair washing to once or twice a week, and use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.

Step by Step

Here are some steps you can take to keep the back of your hair hydrated and healthy:

1. Start with a Moisturizing Shampoo

Choose a shampoo that contains natural oils like argan oil or coconut oil. Wet your hair thoroughly and apply the shampoo, focusing on the back of your head.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the back of your head. Leave the treatment on for the recommended time, then rinse it out with cool water.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the back of your head. This will help to keep your hair hydrated throughout the day.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and lead to dryness and breakage. Try to avoid using these tools, or use them sparingly.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for keeping the back of your hair hydrated:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

FAQ

1. Why is the back of my hair dry?

The hair at the back of our head is more tightly coiled than the hair at the top, making it more difficult for natural oils to travel down the hair shaft. Additionally, the back of the head is more exposed to friction from clothing and pillowcases, which can lead to breakage and dryness.

2. How can I keep the back of my hair hydrated?

You can keep the back of your hair hydrated by using a moisturizing shampoo, deep conditioning regularly, avoiding over-washing your hair, using a leave-in conditioner, and avoiding heat styling.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your hair hydrated and healthy. In addition to the tips mentioned above, make sure to eat a balanced diet, drink plenty of water, and protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions. With some extra care and attention, you can keep the back of your hair hydrated and healthy.
