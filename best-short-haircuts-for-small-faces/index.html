Best Short Haircuts For Small Faces A Guide To Flattering Styles

Written by Robby Feb 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you looking for a new haircut that will flatter your small face? With so many styles to choose from, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. In this guide, we'll explore the best short haircuts for small faces, including how to choose the right style, tips for maintaining your cut, and more!

Table of Contents:

  • How to Choose the Right Short Haircut for Small Faces
  • The Pixie Cut
  • The Bob Cut
  • The Lob Cut
  • FAQs

How to Choose the Right Short Haircut for Small Faces

Before you choose a short haircut, it's important to consider your face shape. If you have a small face, you'll want to choose a style that will enhance your features and balance out your proportions. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid haircuts that are too long or too short
  • Look for styles with layers or texture to add volume to your hair
  • Consider your hair type and texture when choosing a cut
  • Choose a style that complements your personal style and fashion sense

The Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic short haircut that can be a great choice for small faces. This style is typically cut close to the head and features short layers that add texture and volume. The pixie cut can be customized to suit your face shape and personal style, and it's a great option if you're looking for a low-maintenance cut that still looks chic.

One thing to keep in mind with the pixie cut is that it can be a bit of a drastic change if you're used to longer hair. However, if you're ready for a bold new look, the pixie cut can be a great choice!

The Bob Cut

The bob cut is another classic short hairstyle that can be a great option for small faces. This style typically features hair that is cut to chin-length or just above the shoulders, and it can be customized with layers, bangs, or other details. The bob cut is versatile and can work well for a variety of face shapes, including small faces.

One thing to keep in mind with the bob cut is that it may require more maintenance than some other short haircuts. You'll need to schedule regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy, and you may also need to style your hair with a blow dryer or other tools to achieve the look you want.

The Lob Cut

The lob cut, or long bob, is a slightly longer version of the classic bob cut. This style typically features hair that is cut to just above the shoulders or a few inches below. The lob cut can be a great option for small faces, as it can help to elongate the face and create a more balanced look.

The lob cut is versatile and can be customized with layers, bangs, or other details. It's also a great option if you're looking for a low-maintenance cut that still looks stylish.

FAQs

How often should I get my short haircut trimmed?

Short haircuts typically require more frequent trims than longer hairstyles. You should plan to get your hair trimmed every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking neat and tidy.

What hair products should I use to maintain my short haircut?

The hair products you use will depend on your hair type and the specific style you have. However, some products that can be helpful for maintaining short haircuts include texturizing spray, styling gel or mousse, and a quality hairbrush or comb.

Can I still wear my short haircut in different styles?

Yes! Even though short haircuts are typically more limited in terms of styling options, there are still plenty of ways to switch up your look. You can try adding volume with texturizing spray, slicking your hair back with styling gel, or adding a headband or other hair accessory to change up your style.

Hair Care Tips

Maintaining a short haircut requires some special care to keep your hair looking healthy and stylish. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and free of product buildup
  • Use a quality conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray
  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims and touch-ups

By following these tips and choosing the right short haircut for your small face, you can achieve a stylish and flattering look that you'll love!
