What Is Better For Wavy Hair Gel Or Mousse

Written by Aprilia Aug 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of dealing with unruly, frizzy waves? Do you constantly struggle with finding the right product to tame your locks? Look no further than this guide on whether gel or mousse is better for wavy hair. We'll break down the pros and cons of each product and give you tips on how to achieve the perfect wavy look.

Description

Gel and mousse are two popular hair styling products that can be used to enhance the natural texture of wavy hair. Gel is a thick, sticky substance that provides a strong hold and helps to define curls and waves. Mousse, on the other hand, is a foam-like product that provides a lighter hold and adds volume and body to hair.

How to

When using gel, start with damp hair and apply a quarter-sized amount of product evenly throughout your hair. Scrunch your hair from the ends to the roots to encourage the formation of waves. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.

When using mousse, apply a golf ball-sized amount of product to damp hair, focusing on the roots. Use a wide-tooth comb to evenly distribute the product throughout your hair, then scrunch your hair from the ends to the roots. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.

Step by Step

If you're still unsure about which product to use, follow these steps to determine which is better for your hair:

Step 1:

Start with clean, damp hair.

Step 2:

Apply a quarter-sized amount of gel to one section of your hair and a golf ball-sized amount of mousse to another section.

Step 3:

Scrunch both sections of hair from the ends to the roots.

Step 4:

Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.

Step 5:

Compare the results and determine which product gives you the desired look.

Tips

When using gel or mousse, it's important to remember the following tips:

  • Start with a small amount of product and add more as needed.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to evenly distribute the product throughout your hair.
  • Scrunch your hair from the ends to the roots to encourage the formation of waves.
  • Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying to prevent frizz.

FAQ

Q: Can I use gel and mousse together?

A: Yes, you can use gel and mousse together to achieve a stronger hold and added volume.

Q: Will gel or mousse make my hair crunchy?

A: Gel can sometimes make hair feel crunchy, but this can be avoided by using a small amount of product and scrunching your hair while it dries. Mousse typically doesn't make hair feel crunchy.

Q: Will gel or mousse weigh down my hair?

A: Gel can sometimes weigh down hair, especially if too much product is used. Mousse is typically lighter and less likely to weigh down hair.

Hair Care

Regardless of which product you choose, it's important to take care of your wavy hair to keep it looking its best. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoid washing your hair too often to prevent dryness. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized, and protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot styling tools. Finally, trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.
