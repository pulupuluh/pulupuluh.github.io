How To Cut Curly Hair Long Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of your curly hair getting in your eyes? Long bangs can be a great solution, but cutting curly hair can be tricky. Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut curly hair long bangs, step-by-step. We'll also cover tips, FAQs, and hair care to keep your curls looking their best.

How to Cut Curly Hair Long Bangs

Cutting curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right technique, anyone can do it. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Now, let's get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb through your curls to remove any tangles.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut into bangs. Use the comb to create a triangle shape. The base of the triangle should be at the top of your forehead and the point should be in the middle of your head.
  3. Spray the section of hair with water. This will help weigh down the curls and make them easier to cut.
  4. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about an inch away from your forehead. Use the scissors to cut across the hair in a straight line.
  5. Release the hair and check the length. If it's too long, repeat step 4, cutting off a little at a time until you reach your desired length.
  6. Use the comb to blend the bangs into the rest of your hair. You want a seamless transition, not a harsh line.

Congratulations, you just cut your own curly hair long bangs!

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Curly hair can be unpredictable, so here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Cut your hair when it's dry. Curly hair can shrink up to an inch when it dries, so cutting it wet can result in a shorter cut than you intended.
  • Start with small cuts. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's in a ponytail. This can result in uneven bangs.
  • Curly hair can be more fragile than straight hair, so be gentle when cutting and styling.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair:

Q: Should I cut my hair wet or dry?
A: It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink up to an inch when it dries. Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?
A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well it holds its shape. Generally, every 6-8 weeks is a good rule of thumb. Q: Can I cut my curly hair with regular scissors?
A: No, you should always use sharp hair cutting scissors. Regular scissors can result in split ends and uneven cuts.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a little extra TLC to look its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls in the shower.
  • Avoid heat styling as much as possible. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.

Now that you know how to cut curly hair long bangs, you can achieve the look you've been dreaming of. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and be gentle with your curls. Happy cutting!
