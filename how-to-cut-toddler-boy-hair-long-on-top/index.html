How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair Long On Top

Written by Daniel Jul 09, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting a toddler’s hair can be a daunting task, especially when you want to achieve a specific style such as long hair on top. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut toddler boy hair long on top in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. We’ll cover everything from the tools you’ll need, the steps to follow, tips to get the best results, and how to care for your toddler’s hair after the cut.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You’ll Need
  2. How to Cut Toddler Boy Hair Long on Top
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for a Successful Haircut
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care for Toddlers

Tools You’ll Need

Before you begin cutting your toddler’s hair, make sure you have the right tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers with guards (optional)
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Spray bottle with water

How to Cut Toddler Boy Hair Long on Top

To achieve a long hair on top style, you’ll need to leave the hair on top longer than the hair on the sides and back. Here’s how to do it:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wet your toddler’s hair with the spray bottle and comb it through to remove any tangles.
  2. Section off the hair on top of your toddler’s head with the comb and clip it out of the way.
  3. Using the clippers or scissors, cut the hair on the sides and back to the desired length. Start with a longer guard or length and work your way down to create a gradual fade.
  4. Blend the hair on the sides and back with the longer hair on top by using a scissors-over-comb technique or by using the clippers without a guard.
  5. Release the hair on top and comb it straight up. Use scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Start by cutting a little at a time to avoid cutting too much.
  6. Blend the hair on top with the hair on the sides and back by using a scissors-over-comb technique or by using the clippers without a guard.
  7. Check for any uneven spots and trim as necessary.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way down to avoid cutting too much.
  • Use a scissors-over-comb technique to blend the hair on the sides and back with the hair on top.
  • Take breaks if your toddler becomes restless or agitated.
  • Watch tutorials or ask a professional for guidance if you’re unsure about any steps.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting toddler boy hair long on top:

1. How often should I cut my toddler’s hair?

It depends on how fast your toddler’s hair grows. On average, toddlers’ hair grows about half an inch per month. You can trim their hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the style.

2. Should I use clippers or scissors?

It’s up to you and your preference. Clippers are faster and easier to use, but scissors allow for more precision and control.

3. What if my toddler won’t sit still?

Take breaks if your toddler becomes restless or agitated. You can also try distracting them with a toy, snack, or video.

Hair Care for Toddlers

After the haircut, it’s important to take care of your toddler’s hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your toddler’s hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle their hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Keep their hair moisturized with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.

Cutting toddler boy hair long on top doesn’t have to be stressful or difficult. With the right tools, techniques, and tips, you can achieve a great haircut that your toddler will love. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
