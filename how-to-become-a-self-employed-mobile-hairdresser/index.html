How To Become A Self Employed Mobile Hairdresser

Written by Bardi Jun 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you passionate about hair and want to be your own boss? Being a self employed mobile hairdresser can be a great career choice. You can work around your own schedule, choose your own clients, and even work from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps to become a self employed mobile hairdresser in relaxed English.

  2. How to
  3. Step by Step

Description

A self employed mobile hairdresser is a hair stylist who provides hair services to clients at their own location. You can either work from home or travel to your clients' homes, depending on your preference. As a self employed mobile hairdresser, you'll need to have the necessary skills to provide a range of hair services, such as cutting, coloring, and styling. You'll also need to have good communication skills to build relationships with your clients and market your services.

How to

To become a self employed mobile hairdresser, you'll need to follow these steps:

Step 1: Gain the Necessary Skills

Before you can start your own mobile hairdressing business, you'll need to have the necessary skills to provide hair services to your clients. You can either attend a hairdressing school or complete an apprenticeship with a licensed hair stylist. This will help you gain hands-on experience and learn the latest hair trends and techniques.

Step 2: Get the Right Equipment

You'll need to invest in the right equipment to provide hair services to your clients. This includes hair cutting scissors, hair clippers, hair dryers, straighteners, curling irons, and other hairdressing tools. You'll also need to have a portable salon chair, a portable wash basin, and a trolley to carry your equipment.

Step 3: Register Your Business

You'll need to register your business with the relevant authorities in your area. This includes getting a business license, registering for taxes, and obtaining any necessary permits or certifications.

Step 4: Market Your Services

You'll need to market your services to build your client base. This includes creating a website, social media pages, and business cards. You can also offer discounts or referral programs to attract new clients.

Step by Step

Here's a step by step guide to becoming a self employed mobile hairdresser:

Step 1: Research the Market

Research the market to determine if there's a demand for mobile hairdressing services in your area. You can do this by conducting surveys or talking to other mobile hairdressers.

Step 2: Gain the Necessary Skills

Attend a hairdressing school or complete an apprenticeship with a licensed hair stylist to gain the necessary skills to provide hair services to your clients.

Step 3: Get the Right Equipment

Invest in the right equipment to provide hair services to your clients, including hair cutting scissors, hair clippers, hair dryers, straighteners, curling irons, a portable salon chair, a portable wash basin, and a trolley to carry your equipment.

Step 4: Register Your Business

Register your business with the relevant authorities in your area, including getting a business license, registering for taxes, and obtaining any necessary permits or certifications.

Step 5: Market Your Services

Market your services by creating a website, social media pages, and business cards. Offer discounts or referral programs to attract new clients.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you succeed as a self employed mobile hairdresser:

  • Build relationships with your clients to retain them and receive referrals
  • Keep up with the latest hair trends and techniques to provide the best services
  • Stay organized and keep track of your appointments and payments
  • Be flexible with your schedule to accommodate your clients

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about becoming a self employed mobile hairdresser:

Do I need a license to become a self employed mobile hairdresser?

It depends on the regulations in your area. Some areas require hairdressers to be licensed, while others don't.

How much can I earn as a self employed mobile hairdresser?

Your earnings will depend on the number of clients you have and the services you provide. You can charge more for specialized services, such as hair coloring or extensions.

Do I need insurance as a self employed mobile hairdresser?

Yes, it's important to have liability insurance to protect yourself and your clients in case of any accidents or injuries.

Hair Care

As a hairdresser, you'll need to provide advice on hair care to your clients. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent dryness
  • Use a heat protectant when using heat styling tools
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair

By following these steps and tips, you can become a successful self employed mobile hairdresser. Good luck!
