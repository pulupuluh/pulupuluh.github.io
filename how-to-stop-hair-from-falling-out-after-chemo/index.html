How To Stop Hair From Falling Out After Chemo

Written by Robby Apr 06, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Dealing with cancer is difficult enough, but losing your hair can make it even more challenging. Chemotherapy often causes hair loss, which can be devastating for many people. However, there are ways to stop hair from falling out after chemo. In this article, we will discuss some tips and tricks that you can use to prevent hair loss during and after chemotherapy.

Description

Hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy. Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells, and unfortunately, hair cells are some of the fastest dividing cells in the body. This means that chemotherapy drugs can damage hair follicles, causing hair to fall out.

The good news is that hair loss is usually temporary, and most people's hair grows back after chemotherapy is over. However, it can take several months for hair to regrow, and it may be different in texture or color than before.

How to Stop Hair from Falling Out After Chemo

There are several things you can do to prevent or minimize hair loss during and after chemotherapy.

1. Cold Caps

Cold caps are a form of scalp cooling that can help reduce hair loss during chemotherapy. These caps are worn before, during, and after chemotherapy sessions, and they work by reducing blood flow to the scalp, which can help protect hair follicles from chemotherapy drugs.

It is essential to note that cold caps are not effective for everyone, and they may not work for all types of chemotherapy drugs. It is essential to talk to your doctor about whether cold caps are an option for you.

2. Minoxidil

Minoxidil is a medication that is commonly used to treat hair loss. It is available over-the-counter and can be applied topically to the scalp. Minoxidil works by increasing blood flow to the scalp, which can help stimulate hair growth.

It is essential to note that minoxidil may not work for everyone, and it may take several months to see results. It is also important to talk to your doctor before using minoxidil, as it may interact with other medications.

3. Hair Care

During and after chemotherapy, it is essential to treat your hair gently. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools, as these can further damage the hair follicles. Instead, use gentle shampoos and conditioners and let your hair air dry whenever possible.

You can also try using a wide-tooth comb or a soft-bristled brush to detangle your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause it to break or fall out.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions on how to use cold caps to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy:

Step 1: Choose Your Cold Cap

There are several different types of cold caps available, and it is essential to choose one that is right for you. Some cold caps require a special freezer, while others can be stored in a regular freezer.

Step 2: Pre-Cool Your Scalp

Before applying the cold cap, it is essential to pre-cool your scalp. You can do this by applying a cool, wet towel to your scalp for a few minutes before applying the cold cap.

Step 3: Apply the Cold Cap

Once your scalp is pre-cooled, it is time to apply the cold cap. Place the cap on your head, making sure that it covers your entire scalp. Some cold caps require a chin strap to hold them in place.

Step 4: Wear the Cold Cap

You will need to wear the cold cap during your chemotherapy session and for a few hours afterward. Some cold caps need to be changed every 30 minutes, while others can be worn for the entire session.

Step 5: Remove the Cold Cap

After your chemotherapy session is over, you can remove the cold cap. You may experience some discomfort when removing the cap, but this is normal.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair loss during and after chemotherapy:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or relaxers.
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss and chemotherapy:

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

A: Yes, most people's hair grows back after chemotherapy is over. However, it may take several months for hair to regrow, and it may be different in texture or color than before.

Q: Can I dye my hair during or after chemotherapy?

A: It is best to avoid using hair dyes or other harsh chemicals on your hair during and after chemotherapy. These can further damage hair follicles and slow down the regrowth process.

Q: Can I use hair extensions or wigs during chemotherapy?

A: Yes, you can use hair extensions or wigs during chemotherapy. However, it is important to choose ones that are gentle on your scalp and do not further damage your hair follicles.

Hair Care

After chemotherapy, it is important to take good care of your hair to promote healthy regrowth. Here are some tips:

  • Use gentle shampoos and conditioners
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water

Remember, hair loss during and after chemotherapy is a common side effect, but there are things you can do to prevent or minimize it. Talk to your doctor about the best options for you, and take good care of your hair to promote healthy regrowth.
