How To Trim The Back Of Your Own Long Hair

Written by Bardi Jun 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Trimming the back of your own long hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not used to doing it yourself. However, with a little bit of practice and patience, you can give yourself a salon-worthy trim right in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to trim the back of your own long hair in relaxed English language.

Description

Trimming the back of your own long hair might seem like a difficult task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. In this article, we'll provide you with a detailed guide on how to trim the back of your own hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand English language. We'll cover everything from how to prepare your hair for trimming to how to maintain your hair after you've trimmed it.

How to

Before you start trimming your hair, it's important to prepare your hair and tools properly. Here's how:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner, and then dry it completely. Make sure to comb your hair properly to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Gather Your Tools

You'll need a sharp pair of hair-cutting scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and some clips to section your hair. Make sure your scissors are sharp so that they can easily cut through your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Clip the top section of your hair out of the way, leaving only the bottom section of your hair exposed.

Step 4: Start Trimming

Start trimming your hair by taking small sections of your hair and holding them between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair straight across, making sure to trim only a small amount at a time.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've trimmed a section of your hair, comb it out and check your progress. Make sure the hair is even and that you haven't missed any spots.

Step 6: Repeat the Process

Continue trimming your hair in small sections until you've trimmed the entire bottom section of your hair. Then, unclip the top section of your hair and repeat the process until you've trimmed all of your hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind while trimming the back of your own long hair:

  • Always trim your hair when it's dry.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Trim only a small amount of hair at a time.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or frustrated.
  • Ask for help if you're not confident in your ability to trim your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I trim my hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to trim your hair when it's dry, as wet hair tends to stretch and can result in uneven trimming.

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: It's recommended that you trim your hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to trim my hair?

A: No, regular scissors are not sharp enough to cut through hair and can result in split ends and damage to your hair.

Hair Care

After you've trimmed your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain its health and length. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heating tools such as straighteners and curling irons too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Trimming the back of your own long hair can seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. Follow the steps and tips in this article to give yourself a salon-worthy trim right in the comfort of your own home.
