How To Look After Curly Hair Between Washes

Written by Petter May 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair is naturally dry, which means it requires special attention to keep it healthy and hydrated. One of the best ways to do this is by washing your hair less frequently. However, this can be tricky because curly hair tends to tangle easily and can become frizzy if not properly cared for. In this blog post, we will discuss how to look after curly hair between washes and provide tips to keep your curls looking their best.

How to Look After Curly Hair Between Washes

When it comes to looking after curly hair between washes, the key is to keep it moisturized and detangled. Here are some tips to help you look after your curly hair:

1. Use a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase can help to reduce friction and prevent tangles. Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and pull on the hair, which can lead to breakage and frizz.

2. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Curly hair needs moisture, and a leave-in conditioner can help to provide it. Apply a leave-in conditioner after washing your hair, and then again between washes to keep your curls hydrated.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Curly hair is prone to tangles, and a wide-tooth comb can help to detangle your hair without causing damage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can damage curly hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

5. Use a Hair Oil

A hair oil can help to seal in moisture and prevent frizz. Apply a few drops of hair oil to the ends of your hair between washes to keep your curls looking shiny and healthy.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide to looking after your curly hair between washes:

Step 1: Detangle Your Hair

Start by detangling your hair with a wide-tooth comb, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Apply a Hair Oil

Apply a few drops of hair oil to the ends of your hair to seal in moisture and prevent frizz.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

You can either leave your hair down or style it in a protective style, such as braids or a bun. If you choose to style your hair, be gentle and avoid pulling on your curls.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining curly hair:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Curly hair should not be washed every day as this can strip your hair of its natural oils. Try to wash your hair no more than once or twice a week.

2. Use a Microfiber Towel

A microfiber towel is gentler on curly hair than a regular towel, which can cause frizz. Use a microfiber towel to gently squeeze the water out of your hair after washing.

3. Avoid Sulfates and Silicones

Sulfates and silicones can be drying and damaging to curly hair. Look for hair products that are sulfate-free and silicone-free.

FAQs

Q. Can I brush my curly hair?

A. It's best to avoid brushing curly hair as this can cause frizz and breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb instead.

Q. How often should I wash my curly hair?

A. Curly hair should be washed no more than once or twice a week.

Q. Can I use a regular towel to dry my hair?

A. It's best to use a microfiber towel to gently squeeze the water out of your hair after washing.

Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

Here are some hair care products that are great for curly hair:

  • Leave-In Conditioner
  • Hair Oil
  • Wide-Tooth Comb
  • Microfiber Towel
  • Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Looking after curly hair between washes can be challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking their best. Follow these tips and tricks to keep your curls hydrated, detangled, and frizz-free.
