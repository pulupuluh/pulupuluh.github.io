Will A Side Fringe Suit Me Tips And Faq

Written by Robby Mar 06, 2023 · 8 min read
Will a Side Fringe Suit Me? Tips and FAQ | Hair Care 2023

Are you considering getting a side fringe but not sure if it will suit you? A side fringe can be a great way to change up your look and add some edge to your hairstyle. In this article, we’ll go over some tips and frequently asked questions to help you determine if a side fringe is right for you.

How to Determine if a Side Fringe Will Suit You

Before you commit to a side fringe, there are a few things you can consider to help determine if it will suit you:

Face Shape

Your face shape can play a big role in whether or not a side fringe will suit you. Generally, side fringes work best on oval or heart-shaped faces. If you have a round face, a side fringe may make your face appear wider. If you have a square face, a side fringe can help soften your features.

Hair Texture

The texture of your hair can also impact how a side fringe will look on you. If you have thin or fine hair, a side fringe can add some dimension and depth. If you have thick or curly hair, a side fringe can help balance out your hair and prevent it from appearing too heavy.

Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle when deciding if a side fringe is right for you. If you’re someone who likes to wear your hair back in a ponytail or bun, a side fringe may not work as well for you. A side fringe requires more maintenance and styling than a traditional fringe, so be sure you’re willing to put in the effort.

Step by Step Guide to Getting a Side Fringe

If you’ve decided that a side fringe is right for you, here’s a step by step guide to getting one:

Step 1: Choose Your Fringe

There are a few different types of side fringes to choose from, including a straight fringe, a wispy fringe, or a layered fringe. Consider your face shape and hair texture when choosing the type of fringe you want.

Step 2: Book an Appointment

Once you’ve decided on the type of fringe you want, book an appointment with a hairstylist. They can help you determine the best length and style for your face shape and hair texture.

Step 3: Maintenance

A side fringe requires more maintenance than a traditional fringe. Be prepared to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh and styled. You’ll also need to style your fringe daily to keep it looking its best.

Tips for Styling a Side Fringe

Here are a few tips for styling your new side fringe:

Blow Dry

Blow dry your fringe with a round brush to add volume and shape. Use a heat protectant to prevent damage.

Straighten

If you have a straight fringe, use a flat iron to keep it looking sleek and polished.

Curl

If you have a wispy or layered fringe, use a curling iron to add some texture and movement.

Accessorize

Add some accessories to your fringe, such as a headband or clip, to change up your look.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about side fringes:

Will a side fringe work with curly hair?

Yes! A side fringe can work well with curly hair. Just be sure to choose a fringe that complements your curl pattern.

How often do I need to trim my side fringe?

You should trim your side fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh and styled.

Can I wear my side fringe up?

Yes, you can wear your side fringe up. Just be sure to use bobby pins or a clip to keep it in place.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining a Side Fringe

Here are a few hair care tips to help you maintain your side fringe:

Use a Heat Protectant

When blow drying or styling your fringe with heat tools, be sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage.

Wash Regularly

Wash your hair regularly to prevent oil buildup on your fringe.

Use Dry Shampoo

If you don’t want to wash your hair every day, use a dry shampoo to keep your fringe looking fresh.

Trim Regularly

Be sure to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking its best.

Overall, a side fringe can be a great way to change up your look and add some edge to your hairstyle. By considering your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle, you can determine if a side fringe is right for you. If you do decide to get a side fringe, be prepared for some maintenance and styling, but the end result can be well worth it!
