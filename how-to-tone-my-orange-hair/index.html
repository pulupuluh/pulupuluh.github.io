How To Tone My Orange Hair A Step By Step Guide In 2023

Are you struggling with orange hair? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people experience this problem when attempting to dye their hair at home. However, there are steps you can take to tone your orange hair and achieve your desired color. In this article, we'll go over how to tone your orange hair, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Tone My Orange Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Tone My Orange Hair

The first step in toning your orange hair is to determine the underlying cause. Orange hair can be the result of a variety of factors, including:

  • Inadequate processing time
  • Incorrect dye selection
  • High levels of pigmentation

Once you have identified the cause of your orange hair, you can take the appropriate steps to tone it.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps to follow when toning your orange hair:

  1. Choose the right toner: Select a toner that matches the underlying pigment in your hair. For orange hair, choose a blue or purple toner to neutralize the orange tones.
  2. Prepare the toner: Mix the toner with a developer according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  3. Apply the toner: Apply the toner mixture to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down. Be sure to saturate your hair evenly.
  4. Wait: Allow the toner to process for the recommended time. This can vary depending on the toner and your hair type.
  5. Rinse: Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.
  6. Condition: Apply a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair.

Tips for Toning Orange Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when toning your orange hair:

  • Use a blue or purple toner to neutralize orange tones.
  • Avoid using a toner with a high developer volume, as this can cause further damage to your hair.
  • Consider using a color-correcting shampoo to maintain your desired color.
  • Consult with a professional stylist if you're unsure about toning your hair at home.

FAQs

Can I tone my hair at home?

Yes, you can tone your hair at home. However, it's important to follow the instructions carefully and choose the right toner for your hair type.

How often should I tone my hair?

This can vary depending on your hair type and the toner you use. Generally, toning your hair every 4-6 weeks can help maintain your desired color.

Will toning my hair cause damage?

Toning your hair can cause damage if you use a toner with a high developer volume or leave it on for too long. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully and use a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair.

Hair Care Advice

To keep your hair healthy and vibrant, follow these hair care tips:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and steps, you can tone your orange hair and achieve the color you want. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and vibrant.
