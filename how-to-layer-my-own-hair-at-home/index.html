How To Layer Your Own Hair At Home

Written by Robby Jun 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Layered hair is always in style and can give your hair a fresh and modern look. However, going to a salon can be expensive and time-consuming. But don't worry, you can easily layer your own hair at home with just a few simple steps. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of layering your hair at home.

How to Layer Your Own Hair at Home

Before we begin, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, hair clips, and a comb. It's also important to have a mirror that you can easily move around to get a good view of the back of your head.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important to have clean, dry hair before you start layering. This will make it easier to see the different lengths of your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section your hair. Start by dividing your hair into two sections down the middle of your head. Then, take each section and divide it in half again, creating four sections in total. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer. Unclip one of the bottom sections and comb through it to remove any tangles. Hold the hair straight up with your fingers and cut the ends at a slight angle. This will create a subtle layering effect. Repeat this process with the other three sections.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you've finished with the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Unclip one of the middle sections and repeat the same process as before. However, this time, start cutting the hair at a slightly higher angle to create a more noticeable layering effect. Repeat with the other three sections.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, it's time to cut the top layer. Unclip the top sections and repeat the same process as before, but this time, start cutting the hair at a steeper angle. This will create the most noticeable layering effect. Repeat with the other three sections.

Tips for Layering Your Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can be more difficult to judge the length and layers.
  • Be patient and take your time. Layering your hair can be a time-consuming process, but it's worth it for the end result.

FAQ: Layering Your Own Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering your own hair:

Can I layer my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can layer curly hair. However, it's important to be careful and take small cuts to avoid damaging the curls.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim the ends or even out the layers. However, if you're unsure, it's best to leave it to a professional.

Hair Care After Layering

After layering your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the layers and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid over-brushing your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Congratulations, you now know how to layer your own hair at home! With these simple steps, you can achieve a fresh, modern look without breaking the bank or spending hours in a salon.
