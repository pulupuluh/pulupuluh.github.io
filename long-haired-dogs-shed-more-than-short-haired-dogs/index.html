Long Haired Dogs Shed More Than Short Haired Dogs Why And How To Control It

Written by Oliver Sep 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are a pet owner, you might have noticed that long haired dogs shed more than short haired dogs. Shedding can be a nuisance, especially if you have allergies or if you are constantly cleaning up after your furry friend. But why do long haired dogs shed more than short haired dogs? And what can you do to control it? In this article, we will answer these questions and provide some tips on how to manage shedding in long haired dogs.

Why do Long Haired Dogs Shed More Than Short Haired Dogs?

Long haired dogs shed more than short haired dogs because they have more hair follicles per square inch of skin. This means that they have more hair to shed, and it can accumulate quickly. Additionally, the longer hair can get tangled and matted, which can cause shedding. Long haired dogs also shed more during seasonal changes, such as in the spring and fall when their coats are transitioning.

How to Control Shedding in Long Haired Dogs

While it is impossible to completely stop shedding in long haired dogs, there are some things you can do to control it:

  • Regular grooming: Brushing your dog's coat regularly can help remove loose hair and prevent matting. It is recommended to brush your dog's coat at least once a week, and more frequently during shedding season.
  • Bathing: Bathing your dog can also help reduce shedding. However, over-bathing can strip the coat of its natural oils, so it is important to only bathe your dog when necessary.
  • Diet: Feeding your dog a balanced diet with essential fatty acids can help improve the health of their skin and coat, reducing shedding.
  • Veterinary care: Sometimes excessive shedding can be a sign of an underlying health issue. Regular visits to the vet can ensure that your dog is healthy and shedding is not a symptom of a larger problem.

Step-by-Step Guide to Grooming Your Long Haired Dog

Grooming your long haired dog can be a daunting task, but it is important for their health and wellbeing. Here is a step-by-step guide to grooming your long haired dog:

  1. Start by brushing your dog's coat with a slicker brush to remove any tangles and mats.
  2. Use a comb to go over your dog's coat, making sure to get any remaining tangles and mats.
  3. Trim any excess hair around your dog's paws and ears with scissors.
  4. Clean your dog's ears with a veterinarian-approved solution.
  5. Trim your dog's nails with a clipper or file.
  6. Finish by brushing your dog's coat once more to remove any remaining loose hair.

Tips to Reduce Shedding in Long Haired Dogs

Aside from regular grooming, there are some additional tips you can follow to reduce shedding in long haired dogs:

  • Invest in a high-quality vacuum cleaner that is designed for pet hair.
  • Use a lint roller or tape to remove hair from furniture and clothing.
  • Wash your dog's bedding and toys regularly.
  • Consider using a shedding blade or deshedding tool to remove excess hair.

FAQ

Q: My long haired dog sheds excessively, should I be concerned?

A: Excessive shedding can be a sign of an underlying health issue, so it is recommended to consult with your veterinarian to rule out any potential problems.

Q: Can I completely stop my long haired dog from shedding?

A: No, shedding is a natural process for dogs and cannot be completely stopped. However, regular grooming can help control shedding.

Q: Can diet affect shedding in long haired dogs?

A: Yes, feeding your dog a balanced diet with essential fatty acids can help improve the health of their skin and coat, reducing shedding.

Hair Care for Long Haired Dogs

Long haired dogs require extra care and attention when it comes to their hair. Here are some tips on how to care for your long haired dog's hair:

  • Brush your dog's coat regularly to prevent tangles and mats.
  • Trim excess hair around your dog's paws and ears to prevent matting.
  • Use a veterinarian-approved ear cleaning solution to clean your dog's ears.
  • Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent them from getting too long.
  • Feed your dog a balanced diet with essential fatty acids to improve the health of their skin and coat.

In conclusion, long haired dogs shed more than short haired dogs due to the number of hair follicles per square inch of skin. While shedding cannot be completely stopped, regular grooming and proper care can help control it. By following the tips and advice in this article, you can keep your long haired dog's shedding under control and enjoy a cleaner, healthier home.
