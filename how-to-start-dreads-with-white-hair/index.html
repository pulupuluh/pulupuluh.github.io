How To Start Dreads With White Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Apr 22, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have white hair and are interested in starting dreadlocks, you may be wondering what the process entails. While dreads can be started on any hair type, white hair can present some unique challenges. In this guide, we will go over the steps you need to take to start dreads with white hair, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you along the way.

How to Start Dreads with White Hair

Before you start the process of creating dreadlocks, it's important to understand that it can take time and patience to achieve the look you want. With white hair, the process may take longer as the hair is often finer and more delicate than other hair types. Follow these steps to start your dreads:

Step 1: Decide on the Size of Your Dreads

The first step in starting dreads is to decide on the size you want them to be. Small dreads will take longer to create but will give you a more textured look, while larger dreads will be quicker to create and give you a smoother look.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Before starting the process of creating dreads, it's important to wash your hair thoroughly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue from your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is clean and dry, use a comb to section it into the size and shape you want your dreads to be. Use hair ties to secure each section.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots of each section, use a backcombing technique to create knots in your hair. This will help to create the texture and hold needed for your dreads to form.

Step 5: Use a Dread Wax

Once you have backcombed each section, apply a dread wax to each dread. This will help to hold the knots in place and assist in the locking process.

Step 6: Palm Roll Your Dreads

After applying the dread wax, use your palms to roll each dread back and forth. This will help to smooth out any bumps or lumps in the hair and encourage the locking process.

Step 7: Repeat the Process

Repeat steps 4-6 for each section of hair until you have created all of your dreads.

Step 8: Maintenance

Once your dreads are formed, it's important to maintain them by regularly washing and reapplying dread wax.

Tips for Starting Dreads with White Hair

Starting dreads with white hair can be a bit more challenging than other hair types, but there are some tips you can follow to make the process easier:

- Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue from your hair before starting the process. - Be gentle when backcombing your hair to avoid breakage. - Use a dread wax to help hold the knots in place and encourage the locking process. - Palm roll your dreads regularly to smooth out any bumps or lumps in the hair. - Be patient! It can take time for dreads to form, especially with white hair.

FAQs

How long does it take to start dreads with white hair?

The process of creating dreads can take anywhere from a few hours to a few days, depending on the size and number of dreads you want. With white hair, the process may take longer as the hair is often finer and more delicate than other hair types.

Can I start dreads with short white hair?

Yes, you can start dreads with short white hair. However, the process may take longer as the hair needs to be long enough to backcomb and knot.

What kind of wax should I use for my dreads?

It's important to use a dread wax specifically designed for starting and maintaining dreadlocks. Look for a natural, beeswax-based product that is free from chemicals and additives.

Hair Care for Dreads with White Hair

Once you have started your dreads, it's important to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for hair care with dreads:

- Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo. - Avoid using conditioner, as it can loosen the knots in your hair. - Dry your dreads thoroughly after washing to avoid mildew or mold. - Avoid using heavy oils or products on your hair, as they can build up and cause damage. - Use a protective style, such as a head wrap, when sleeping or exercising to prevent damage to your dreads.

Starting dreads with white hair can be a fun and rewarding process. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve the look you want and keep your dreads healthy and strong.
