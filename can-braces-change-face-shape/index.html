Can Braces Change Face Shape

Written by Christine Jun 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you considering braces and wondering if they can change your face shape? It's a common question many people have, and the answer is yes, braces can change your face shape. In this article, we'll explore how braces can affect your face shape, how to take care of your braces properly, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Description

Braces are orthodontic devices used to straighten teeth and improve oral health. They work by applying pressure to the teeth, gradually shifting them into proper alignment. While braces primarily focus on correcting dental issues, they can also have an impact on the face shape.

How Braces Can Change Face Shape

Braces can change your face shape by altering the position of your teeth and jaw. As the teeth shift into the correct position, it can change the appearance of your face. For example, if you have an overbite, braces can bring your upper jaw forward, giving your face a more balanced look. Alternatively, if you have an underbite, braces can push the lower jaw back, creating a more symmetrical appearance.

Additionally, braces can help correct other facial issues, such as a narrow palate or crowded teeth. By widening the palate or creating more space for teeth, braces can improve the overall shape and appearance of your face.

Step by Step Guide: How Braces Can Change Face Shape

Step 1: Consultation

The first step in determining if braces can change your face shape is to schedule a consultation with an orthodontist. They will examine your teeth and jaw and determine if braces are the right treatment for you.

Step 2: Treatment Plan

Once you decide to move forward with braces, your orthodontist will create a treatment plan. This plan will outline the type of braces you will need, how long you will need to wear them, and how they will impact your face shape.

Step 3: Braces Application

The next step is to have the braces applied. This process involves cleaning and drying your teeth, applying a bonding agent, and attaching brackets to your teeth. The brackets are then connected with wires, which will apply pressure to your teeth to move them into the correct position.

Step 4: Adjustment Appointments

During your treatment, you will need to schedule adjustment appointments with your orthodontist. They will tighten the wires and adjust the brackets to continue shifting your teeth into the right position. These appointments are crucial to the success of your treatment.

Step 5: Completion

Once your treatment is complete, you will have a beautiful smile and a more balanced face shape. However, it's essential to keep up with regular dental appointments and wear a retainer to maintain your results.

Tips for Taking Care of Your Braces

Proper care of your braces is crucial to ensuring they work effectively and don't cause any damage to your teeth. Here are some tips for taking care of your braces:

  • Brush and floss regularly to remove any food particles stuck in your braces
  • Avoid hard or chewy foods that can damage your braces
  • Wear a mouthguard if you play sports to protect your teeth and braces
  • Attend all adjustment appointments to ensure your treatment stays on track

FAQs

Q: Will braces hurt?

A: It's common to experience some discomfort or soreness after getting braces or after an adjustment appointment. However, this pain is temporary and can be managed with over-the-counter pain relievers.

Q: How long do I need to wear braces?

A: The length of your treatment will depend on your individual needs and the severity of your dental issues. On average, treatment can last anywhere from 18 to 36 months.

Q: Can I play sports with braces?

A: Yes, you can play sports with braces, but it's important to wear a mouthguard to protect your teeth and braces from damage.

Hair Care and Braces

While braces can change your face shape, it's essential to remember that they won't affect your hair. However, if you're self-conscious about your appearance, changing up your hairstyle can be a great way to boost your confidence. Speak with your stylist about different hairstyles that can complement your new smile and face shape.

Conclusion

If you're considering braces and wondering if they can change your face shape, the answer is yes. Braces can help correct dental issues and improve the overall shape and appearance of your face. By following proper care instructions and attending regular appointments, you'll have a beautiful smile and a more balanced face shape in no time.
