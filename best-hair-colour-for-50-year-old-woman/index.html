Best Hair Colour For 50 Year Old Woman A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jul 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're a 50 year old woman who's looking to change up your hair colour, you're in the right place! In this article, we'll be discussing the best hair colours for women over 50. We'll go over how to choose the right colour, step by step instructions on how to dye your hair, tips for maintaining your new colour, and frequently asked questions. Let's get started!

How to Choose the Right Hair Colour

Choosing the right hair colour can be overwhelming, but it doesn't have to be! Here are some tips to help you choose the perfect colour for you:

Consider Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone is an important factor when it comes to choosing a hair colour. If you have warm undertones, consider going for warm colours like golden blondes or caramel browns. If you have cool undertones, consider going for cool colours like ash blondes or cool browns.

Think About Your Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle when choosing a hair colour. If you have a busy lifestyle and don't have a lot of time to maintain your hair, consider going for a low-maintenance colour like highlights or balayage. If you have more time to spend on your hair, consider going for a full colour.

Look at Your Natural Hair Colour

Look at your natural hair colour when choosing a hair colour. If you're looking for a subtle change, consider going for a colour that's close to your natural hair colour. If you're looking for a more dramatic change, consider going for a colour that's significantly different from your natural hair colour.

Step by Step: How to Dye Your Hair

Now that you've chosen your hair colour, it's time to dye your hair! Here's a step by step guide to help you:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start dyeing your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Apply petroleum jelly around your hairline and on your ears to prevent the dye from staining your skin.

Step 2: Mix the Dye

Follow the instructions on the hair dye box to mix the dye. Make sure to wear gloves to protect your hands from staining.

Step 3: Apply the Dye

Start applying the dye at the roots and work your way down to the ends. Make sure to saturate your hair with the dye.

Step 4: Wait

Follow the instructions on the hair dye box for how long to leave the dye on your hair.

Step 5: Rinse

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water until the water runs clear.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Hair Colour

Now that you've dyed your hair, it's important to maintain your new colour. Here are some tips to help you:

Use a Colour-Safe Shampoo

Use a colour-safe shampoo to help maintain your new colour. Colour-safe shampoos are formulated to help prevent your colour from fading.

Avoid Hot Water

Avoid washing your hair with hot water as it can cause your colour to fade faster. Use lukewarm water instead.

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons as they can cause your colour to fade faster. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray.

FAQ

Q: Can I dye my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair at home. Just make sure to follow the instructions on the hair dye box carefully.

Q: How often should I touch up my hair colour?

A: It depends on the type of colour you have and how fast your hair grows. Generally, you should touch up your hair colour every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Will dyeing my hair damage it?

A: Dyeing your hair can cause damage, but it depends on the type of dye you use and how often you dye your hair. To minimize damage, use a dye that's formulated for your hair type and don't dye your hair too often.

Hair Care

When it comes to hair care, it's important to use products that are formulated for your hair type. Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

Avoid Over-Brushing

Avoid over-brushing your hair as it can cause damage and breakage. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead.

In conclusion, choosing the right hair colour can make a huge difference in how you look and feel. Follow these tips and tricks to help you choose the perfect colour and maintain it for months to come.
