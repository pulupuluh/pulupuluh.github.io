How To Trim Long Hair Evenly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Mar 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Trimming long hair can be a daunting task, especially if you want to keep the length but need to get rid of split ends or damaged hair. However, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve an even and healthy-looking trim without having to visit the salon. In this guide, we'll show you how to trim long hair evenly at home.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Trimming Your Hair
  • Aftercare
  • Tips for Trimming Long Hair Evenly
  • FAQs

Tools You'll Need

Before you start trimming your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • Hair clips or ties to section your hair
  • A handheld mirror or a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start trimming, wash and dry your hair thoroughly. This will make it easier to see any split ends or damaged hair that needs to be trimmed. Avoid using any styling products or oils that can make your hair slippery or difficult to handle.

Sectioning Your Hair

Use hair clips or ties to section your hair into manageable parts. Start by dividing your hair in half, from the top of your head to the nape of your neck. Secure one section with a clip or tie, and work on the other section first. Then, divide the section you're working on into smaller parts, clipping away the sections you're not working on.

Trimming Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of the section between your fingers, and use your scissors to trim off any split ends or damaged hair. Make sure to cut straight across, and avoid cutting at an angle, which can create uneven layers.

Continue working through each section of your hair, trimming off any damaged ends as you go. Remember to keep your cuts small and consistent, as it's easier to go back and trim more if needed than to undo a big mistake.

Aftercare

Once you've finished trimming your hair, give it a final comb through and check for any uneven spots. If you notice any areas that need further trimming, go back and make small adjustments as needed.

After you're finished, give your hair a good wash and condition to remove any stray hairs. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair nourished and healthy-looking.

Tips for Trimming Long Hair Evenly

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up gradually
  • Take breaks if your arms or hands get tired
  • Trim your hair in good lighting
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head
  • Don't trim your hair when it's wet, as this can result in uneven cuts

FAQs

Can I trim my hair when it's wet?

No, it's best to trim your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it really is, which can result in uneven cuts.

How often should I trim my hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends from spreading.

Can I use regular scissors to trim my hair?

No, it's best to use sharp hair cutting scissors, as regular scissors can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.

Should I trim my hair myself or visit a salon?

If you're confident in your ability to trim your own hair, it's fine to do it yourself. However, if you're not sure or have never trimmed your own hair before, it's best to visit a salon to avoid any mistakes or uneven cuts.

Hair Care

Trimming your hair regularly is just one part of maintaining healthy and beautiful hair. Make sure to also follow a good hair care routine, including washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using hair masks or treatments to keep it nourished, and avoiding excessive heat styling or chemical treatments that can damage your hair.
