Which Is Better Taper Or Fade

Written by William Jun 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

When it comes to men's haircuts, there are two popular styles that often get confused with each other: the taper and the fade. While they may look similar, there are some key differences between the two. So, which is better - taper or fade? In this article, we'll break down the differences and help you decide which style is right for you.

Description

The taper and the fade are both short haircuts that gradually get shorter towards the neck. The main difference between the two is where the cut begins. A taper typically starts with longer hair at the top and gradually tapers down towards the neck. A fade, on the other hand, starts with shorter hair at the top and gradually fades down to a shorter length.

How to

If you're looking to get a taper or a fade, there are a few things you should keep in mind. First, make sure you have a clear idea of what you want before you go to the barber. Bring in pictures or examples of the style you're looking for to ensure you and your barber are on the same page.

When it comes to the actual cut, the barber will use clippers to gradually shorten your hair towards the neck. For a taper, the barber will leave more length at the top and gradually taper down. For a fade, the barber will start with a shorter length at the top and gradually fade down to an even shorter length.

Step by Step

If you're interested in trying a taper or a fade, here's a step-by-step guide to getting the perfect cut:

  1. Decide on the style you want (taper or fade)
  2. Bring in pictures or examples to show your barber
  3. Discuss any concerns or questions with your barber
  4. Sit back and relax while your barber works their magic
  5. Enjoy your new haircut!

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when getting a taper or fade:

  • Choose a reputable barber with experience in men's haircuts
  • Communicate clearly with your barber to ensure you get the cut you want
  • Maintain your haircut by getting regular trims
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you

FAQ

What's the difference between a taper and a fade?

A taper starts with longer hair at the top and gradually tapers down towards the neck. A fade starts with shorter hair at the top and gradually fades down to a shorter length.

Which style is better?

It ultimately comes down to personal preference and the look you're going for. Tapers are great for a more classic, timeless look, while fades are often seen as more modern and edgy.

How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you want to maintain your style. As a general rule, it's a good idea to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

Hair Care

Whether you get a taper or a fade, taking care of your hair is important to maintain your style. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that's right for your hair type
  • Use a comb or brush to style your hair
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray when using styling tools

So, which is better - taper or fade? Ultimately, it comes down to personal preference and the look you're going for. Both styles have their pros and cons, so take some time to consider what will work best for you. And remember, the most important thing is to find a style that makes you feel confident and comfortable.
