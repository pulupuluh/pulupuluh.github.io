How To Cut My Own Hair Short Female A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to cut your own hair short, female style! Whether you're tired of the same old look, want to save money on salon visits, or just want to try something new, cutting your own hair can seem daunting at first. But with the right tools, techniques, and a little bit of practice, you can achieve a stylish, flattering haircut from the comfort of your own home. Read on for our step-by-step guide, tips, and FAQs.

How to Cut My Own Hair Short Female: Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather some essential tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Towel or cape to catch stray hairs

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Cut My Own Hair Short Female

Step 1: Preparation

Start by washing and drying your hair, and combing out any tangles. If you have curly or wavy hair, you may want to leave it slightly damp for easier cutting. Cover your shoulders with a towel or cape to catch stray hairs, and tie back any hair that you don't want to cut.

Step 2: Sectioning

Use your comb to divide your hair into sections, starting with the back. Create a horizontal parting from ear to ear, and clip the top section up out of the way. Repeat this process with the sides, creating a vertical parting from the center of your forehead to the nape of your neck on each side.

Step 3: Cutting the Back

Using your comb, pull the back section straight down and cut across at your desired length. If you want a layered look, cut at an angle. Work in small sections, and check your progress in the mirror frequently. If you have clippers, you can use them to create a tapered or undercut look.

Step 4: Cutting the Sides

Starting at the front, pull a small section of hair out from the side parting and cut to the desired length. Use this section as a guide for the rest of the side, working your way towards the back. Repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Blending and Finishing

Use your comb to blend any harsh lines or uneven sections, and trim any stray hairs. Check your hair in the mirror from all angles to make sure it looks even and balanced. If you want a shorter or choppy look, you can use your scissors to add texture or layers.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Short Female

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length.
  • Use hair clips or ties to keep sections out of the way.
  • Invest in sharp, high-quality scissors for a clean cut.
  • Take breaks and step back from the mirror to avoid over-cutting.
  • Watch tutorial videos or ask a friend for help if you're unsure.

FAQ: How to Cut My Own Hair Short Female

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly or wavy?

Yes, you can! However, keep in mind that curly or wavy hair can shrink up when dry, so cut it slightly longer than your desired length.

How often should I cut my own hair?

This depends on your hair type, length, and desired style. As a general rule, aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed. If you're unsure, cut a little bit at a time and check your progress frequently. If you do make a mistake, you can always visit a salon or barber for a professional fix.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair Short Female

After cutting your own hair, it's important to care for it properly. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid heat styling or chemical treatments for a few days. Trim any split ends regularly, and schedule regular salon visits for more complex cuts or maintenance.

We hope this guide has helped you learn how to cut your own hair short, female style! Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to experiment and try new things. Happy cutting!
