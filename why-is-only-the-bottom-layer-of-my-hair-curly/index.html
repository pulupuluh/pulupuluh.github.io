Why Is Only The Bottom Layer Of My Hair Curly

Written by Petter Feb 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have relaxed hair, you may have noticed that only the bottom layer of your hair is curly while the rest of your hair is straight. This can be frustrating, especially if you want to achieve a uniform look. In this blog post, we will explore why this happens and what you can do about it.

Description

The bottom layer of your hair is often curlier than the rest of your hair because it is closer to your scalp. The natural oils produced by your scalp help to moisturize and nourish your hair, making it healthier and more resilient. However, as your hair grows longer, it becomes less exposed to these oils, which can cause it to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage.

Additionally, the heat and chemicals used during the relaxing process can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it even more susceptible to damage. This damage can cause your hair to lose its curl pattern, resulting in a straighter appearance.

How to

If you want to achieve a more uniform look, there are a few things you can do to encourage your hair to curl more evenly:

1. Moisturize regularly

Moisturizing your hair regularly can help to keep it healthy and prevent breakage. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and follow up with a leave-in conditioner or oil to seal in moisture.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can cause your hair to become dry and damaged, which can lead to a loss of curl pattern. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

3. Use a diffuser

A diffuser attachment on your hair dryer can help to encourage curl formation and prevent frizz. Use it on a low heat setting and scrunch your hair while drying.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for encouraging your hair to curl more evenly:

1. Wash your hair

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to cleanse and condition your hair, and rinse thoroughly.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil

Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair to seal in moisture and prevent breakage.

3. Use a diffuser

Attach a diffuser to your hair dryer and use it on a low heat setting. Scrunch your hair while drying to encourage curl formation.

4. Avoid heat styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, but if you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for maintaining healthy, curly hair:

1. Protect your hair while sleeping

Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage while you sleep, or wear a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to protect your hair.

2. Trim your hair regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can lead to a loss of curl pattern.

3. Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

FAQ

Q: Can I restore my hair's curl pattern?

A: It is possible to restore your hair's curl pattern, but it may take time and effort. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and consider consulting a professional stylist for advice.

Q: Will cutting my hair make it curlier?

A: Cutting your hair may help to encourage curl formation, but it will not necessarily make your hair curlier. The key to achieving a uniform look is to maintain healthy, well-moisturized hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It is best to wash your hair no more than once or twice a week, as frequent washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

Hair Care

Caring for relaxed hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve healthy, beautiful hair. Remember to moisturize regularly, avoid heat styling, and protect your hair while sleeping. If you have any concerns about your hair's health or appearance, consult a professional stylist for advice.
