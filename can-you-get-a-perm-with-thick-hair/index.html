Can You Get A Perm With Thick Hair

Written by Bardi Apr 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your straight and flat hair? Do you want to add more texture and volume to your hair? One option that you might consider is getting a perm. However, if you have thick hair, you might wonder if a perm is a good idea. In this article, we will answer the question, "Can you get a perm with thick hair?"

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get a Perm With Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Getting a Perm With Thick Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Getting a Perm

Description

A perm is a chemical treatment that changes the structure of your hair, making it curly or wavy. It involves applying a solution to your hair and then wrapping it around rods. The solution breaks down the bonds in your hair, and the rods shape your hair into curls or waves. After a certain amount of time, the rods are removed, and a neutralizer is applied to your hair to re-bond the hair structure in its new curly or wavy configuration.

Thick hair can be more resistant to chemical treatments, so it's essential to choose the right perm solution and rods to achieve the desired results.

How to Get a Perm With Thick Hair

Before getting a perm, you should consult with a professional hairstylist to determine if your hair is suitable for a perm, and to decide which type of perm will suit you the best. There are different types of perms, including:

  • Alkaline perm
  • Acid perm
  • Exothermic perm
  • Digital perm

The hairstylist will also assess the thickness and health of your hair to determine the right perm solution, rod size, and processing time.

Step-by-Step Guide

The following is a step-by-step guide on how to get a perm with thick hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up or residue.
  2. Towel dry your hair.
  3. Apply a protective cream or lotion around your hairline and ears to protect your skin from the perm solution.
  4. Divide your hair into sections and wrap each section around the perm rods. The size of the rods will depend on the desired curl size and the thickness of your hair.
  5. Apply the perm solution to your hair, making sure that each section is fully saturated.
  6. Cover your hair with a plastic cap and wait for the perm solution to process. The processing time will depend on the thickness of your hair and the type of perm solution used.
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  8. Apply the neutralizer to your hair, making sure that each section is fully saturated.
  9. Wait for the neutralizer to process, then rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  10. Remove the perm rods and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Getting a Perm With Thick Hair

Here are some tips to consider when getting a perm with thick hair:

  • Choose a hairstylist who has experience working with thick hair and has a good understanding of different perm solutions and rod sizes.
  • Avoid washing your hair for two days after getting a perm to allow the curls to set.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help maintain the health of your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools for a few days after getting a perm, as this can damage your curls.
  • Consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your curls.

FAQs

1. Can you get a perm with naturally thick hair?

Yes, you can get a perm with naturally thick hair. However, it's essential to consult with a professional hairstylist to determine the right type of perm solution and rod size to use.

2. Can you get a perm with chemically relaxed hair?

It's not recommended to get a perm with chemically relaxed hair, as the perm solution can cause damage to the hair and scalp. If you want to add more texture to your relaxed hair, consider getting a texturizer instead of a perm.

3. How long does a perm last?

A perm can last anywhere from two to six months, depending on the hair's texture, thickness, and how well it is maintained.

Hair Care After Getting a Perm

After getting a perm, it's essential to take care of your hair to maintain the health of your curls. Here are some tips for hair care after getting a perm:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least two days after getting a perm.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage to your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as this can damage your curls.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl enhancer to help define your curls.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your curls and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, getting a perm with thick hair is possible, but it's essential to consult with a professional hairstylist and take proper care of your hair after the treatment.
