How to Cut the Back of Your Own Hair Straight Across

Cutting the back of your own hair straight across can be challenging, especially if you don't have much experience or the right tools. However, with a bit of patience and practice, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Here are the basic steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror to see the back of your head. Here's how to cut the back of your own hair straight across:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. This will make it easier to see the natural fall of your hair and ensure an even cut.
  2. Brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Divide your hair into two sections by parting it down the middle. Use the comb to create a straight line from your forehead to the nape of your neck.
  4. Clip one section of your hair out of the way using hair clips.
  5. Take the unclipped section of hair and comb it straight down your back. Make sure it's smooth and even.
  6. Use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head. Hold it up so you can see the reflection of the back of your head in the bathroom mirror.
  7. Use the scissors to cut across the bottom of your hair, following the line you've created with the comb. Start from the middle and work your way outwards, cutting small sections at a time.
  8. Repeat on the other side of your head, making sure to match the length and angle of the first side.
  9. Check the back of your head again using the handheld mirror to ensure an even cut. Make any necessary adjustments.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting the Back of Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting the back of your own hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut and avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to cut small sections at a time to avoid making a mistake.
  • Check the back of your head regularly using the handheld mirror to ensure an even cut.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, start with a small trim and work your way up to a full cut.
  • Watch video tutorials or ask a friend for help if you're still unsure about the process.

FAQs

Here are some common questions and answers about cutting the back of your own hair:

Can I cut my hair straight across without a mirror?

No, it's important to use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head and ensure an even cut.

How often should I cut the back of my hair?

It depends on your hair type and style, but most people need a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a professional hairstylist to fix the issue or try to fix it yourself by trimming the uneven areas.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Now that you've successfully cut the back of your own hair straight across, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curlers too often to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use hair products like serums and oils to keep your hair shiny and moisturized.

Cutting the back of your own hair straight across can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it's definitely doable. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a professional-looking haircut at home.
