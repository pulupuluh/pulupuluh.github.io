How To Tell If Haircut Will Look Good On You

Written by Oliver Jun 16, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you tired of getting a haircut that just doesn't suit you? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll teach you how to tell if a haircut will look good on you. We'll cover everything from face shape to hair texture so you can make an informed decision about your next haircut. So, let's get started!

Face Shape

The first thing to consider when choosing a new haircut is your face shape. Different hairstyles suit different face shapes, so it's important to choose one that complements your features. Here's a quick guide:

  • If you have a round face, go for hairstyles that add height and lengthen your face.
  • If you have a square face, go for hairstyles that soften your features and add volume to the top of your head.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, go for hairstyles that add volume to the bottom of your face and balance out the top.
  • If you have an oval face, lucky you! You can pretty much pull off any hairstyle.

Keep in mind that these are just general guidelines. Your hairdresser can help you choose a haircut that suits your face shape and personal style.

Hair Texture

Another important factor to consider is your hair texture. Certain haircuts work better with certain textures, so it's important to choose a style that works with what you've got. Here's a quick guide:

  • If you have fine hair, go for hairstyles that add volume and texture, like layers or a bob.
  • If you have thick hair, go for hairstyles that thin out your hair and make it more manageable, like a shag or long layers.
  • If you have curly hair, go for hairstyles that embrace your curls, like a curly bob or long layers.
  • If you have straight hair, go for hairstyles that add movement and texture, like a choppy bob or layers.

Again, these are just general guidelines. Your hairdresser can help you choose a haircut that works with your hair texture and face shape.

Personal Style

Your personal style should also play a role in choosing a haircut. Your haircut should reflect your personality and make you feel confident and comfortable. Here are some things to consider:

  • Do you prefer a classic, timeless look or something more trendy and edgy?
  • Do you like to experiment with different styles and colors, or do you prefer a low-maintenance look?
  • Do you have any specific hair goals, like growing out your hair or trying a new color?

Make sure you communicate your personal style to your hairdresser so they can choose a haircut that suits you.

Hair Length

The length of your hair can also affect how a haircut looks on you. Here are some general guidelines:

  • If you have short hair, go for hairstyles that add volume and texture, like a pixie cut or a bob.
  • If you have medium-length hair, go for hairstyles that add movement and dimension, like long layers or a shag.
  • If you have long hair, go for hairstyles that accentuate your length and add softness, like face-framing layers or a boho-inspired cut.

Again, these are just general guidelines. Your hairdresser can help you choose a haircut that works with your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.

Hair Color

Your hair color can also affect how a haircut looks on you. Different haircuts can highlight or downplay certain shades, so it's important to choose a style that works with your color. Here are some general guidelines:

  • If you have blonde hair, go for hairstyles that add depth and dimension, like balayage or a bob with choppy layers.
  • If you have red hair, go for hairstyles that complement your fiery hue, like a long layered cut or a side-swept fringe.
  • If you have brown hair, go for hairstyles that add warmth and richness, like a layered lob or a textured pixie cut.
  • If you have black hair, go for hairstyles that add shine and dimension, like a sleek bob or long layers with highlights.

Again, these are just general guidelines. Your hairdresser can help you choose a haircut that works with your hair color, face shape, and personal style.

Haircut Ideas

Now that you know what factors to consider when choosing a new haircut, here are some haircut ideas to inspire you:

  • A long bob with face-framing layers
  • A shag with wispy bangs
  • A textured pixie cut
  • A curly bob with highlights
  • A sleek, blunt bob
  • A layered lob with a side-swept fringe
  • A classic, timeless cut like a bob or a pixie

Remember, these are just ideas. Your hairdresser can help you choose a haircut that suits your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you choose a haircut that looks good on you:

  • Bring pictures of haircuts you like to your hair appointment.
  • Ask your hairdresser for their professional opinion.
  • Consider your lifestyle and how much time you want to spend styling your hair.
  • Be open to trying something new!

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing a haircut:

How do I know if a haircut will look good on me?

Consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Your hairdresser can help you choose a haircut that suits you.

What haircut makes you look younger?

A layered bob or a lob with face-framing layers can make you look younger.

What haircut suits a round face?

Hairstyles that add height and lengthen your face, like a long bob or a pixie cut with volume on top, suit a round face.

Hair Care

Once you've chosen a new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to keep your haircut looking fresh.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.

With these tips, you'll be able to choose a haircut that looks good on you and keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Happy hairstyling!
