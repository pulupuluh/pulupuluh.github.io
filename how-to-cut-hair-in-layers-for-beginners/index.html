How To Cut Hair In Layers For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 01, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers to your hair can give it more volume, movement, and texture. But if you're a beginner, cutting hair in layers can seem daunting. Don't worry – we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to cut hair in layers for beginners. We'll cover everything you need to know, from tools and techniques to tips and hair care. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Techniques for Cutting Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Layers

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting hair in layers, make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp, or they'll damage your hair and create split ends. You can find hair cutting scissors at your local beauty supply store or online.

Techniques for Cutting Layers

There are different techniques you can use to cut hair in layers, but we'll cover the basic ones:

  • Straight Layering: This technique involves cutting the hair straight across at different lengths to create layers.
  • Graduated Layering: This technique involves cutting the hair at an angle to create layers that are longer at the bottom and shorter at the top.
  • Razor Layering: This technique involves using a razor to create soft, textured layers.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the tools and techniques, let's dive into the step-by-step guide on how to cut hair in layers for beginners:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and drying it completely. You want your hair to be clean and free of tangles before you start cutting it.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. You can start by dividing your hair in half, then dividing each half into two more sections. This will give you four sections to work with.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Decide where you want to start cutting and hold the hair between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle or straight across, depending on the technique you're using.

Repeat this process for each section of hair, making sure to cut at the same angle or length to create even layers.

Step 4: Check Your Layers

After you've cut all the sections, check your layers to make sure they're even. You can do this by combing your hair straight down and looking for any uneven spots. If you find any, trim them with your scissors.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your layers, style your hair as desired. You can use a blow dryer, curling iron, or straightener to create different looks.

Tips for Cutting Layers

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting hair in layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a comb to create a guide for where you want to cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can appear longer than it actually is.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair in layers if it's short?

Yes, you can cut hair in layers no matter what length it is. However, if your hair is very short, you may not be able to create as many layers as you would with longer hair.

Should I cut my hair in layers if it's thin?

Yes, cutting your hair in layers can actually make it look thicker and fuller. The layers create movement and texture, which can give the illusion of more volume.

Can I cut my hair in layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair in layers. However, make sure to cut it when it's dry and to use a technique that won't create frizz.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After you've cut your hair in layers, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layers and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut hair in layers for beginners, go ahead and give it a try! With the right tools, techniques, and tips, you can create a stylish and voluminous look that's perfect for any occasion.
