What Shoes Were Popular In The 60S

Description

The 1960s were a time of change and revolution, and fashion was no exception. One of the most iconic fashion pieces of the era was the shoes. The 60s saw a shift towards more playful and colorful styles, with an emphasis on comfort and practicality. From the classic Mary Jane to the go-go boot, there were many shoes that defined the era.

How to Wear 60s Shoes

If you're looking to incorporate 60s shoes into your wardrobe, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, embrace color and patterns. The 60s were all about bold prints and bright hues, so don't be afraid to experiment. Second, consider the occasion. While some 60s shoes, like the go-go boot, are perfect for a night out, others, like the Mary Jane, are more suited for casual wear. Finally, have fun with it! The 60s were all about breaking the rules and expressing yourself through fashion.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide to wearing 60s shoes:

  1. Choose a 60s shoe style that speaks to you, like the Mary Jane or go-go boot.
  2. Pair your shoes with an outfit that complements them. For example, if you're wearing go-go boots, consider a mini skirt and a bold top.
  3. Accessorize with vintage-inspired jewelry and a fun hairstyle.
  4. Rock your 60s shoes with confidence!

Tips for Choosing 60s Shoes

When it comes to choosing 60s shoes, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Look for shoes that are comfortable and practical. The 60s were all about comfort and ease of movement.
  • Consider the occasion. Some 60s shoes, like the go-go boot, are perfect for a night out, while others, like the Mary Jane, are more suited for casual wear.
  • Embrace color and patterns. The 60s were all about bold prints and bright hues, so don't be afraid to experiment.
  • Accessorize with vintage-inspired jewelry and a fun hairstyle to complete the look.

FAQs

What were the most popular 60s shoe styles?

The most popular 60s shoe styles were the Mary Jane, go-go boot, and platform shoe.

What colors were popular for 60s shoes?

Bright colors like red, yellow, and green were popular for 60s shoes, as were bold prints like paisley and floral.

What materials were used for 60s shoes?

Materials like patent leather, vinyl, and suede were popular for 60s shoes.

Hair Care to Match Your 60s Style

When it comes to 60s style, your hair is just as important as your shoes. Here are a few tips for achieving the perfect 60s hairstyle:

  • Embrace volume. Tease your hair at the crown and use hairspray to keep it in place.
  • Experiment with bangs. The 60s saw a resurgence in bangs, so try out a full or side-swept fringe.
  • Try a beehive. This iconic 60s hairstyle is perfect for special occasions.
  • Accessorize with a headband or scarf. This is a great way to add a pop of color or pattern to your hair.

With these tips, you'll be able to rock 60s shoes and hair with confidence!
