How To Cut My Long Hair To Shoulder Length A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 30, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your long hair and want to switch it up to a more manageable length? Cutting your hair to shoulder length can be a great way to refresh your look and make styling easier. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your long hair to shoulder length. Here's what we'll cover:

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Cut Long Hair to Shoulder Length
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Cutting Long Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care After Cutting

Description

Shoulder-length hair is a versatile length that works with many face shapes and hair types. It's easy to style and maintain, making it a popular choice for many women. Cutting your long hair to shoulder length can be done at home or at a salon, depending on your comfort level and experience with hair cutting.

How to Cut Long Hair to Shoulder Length

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to gather the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, hair clips or ties, and a mirror. It's also helpful to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by dividing your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips or ties to secure each section. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and avoid any uneven layers.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Determine how short you want your hair to be. Shoulder length is typically defined as the hair falling just below the shoulders. Use a ruler or measuring tape to ensure that you cut your hair to the desired length.

Step 3: Cut the Back Section

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you have cut all of the back section to your desired length.

Step 4: Cut the Front Section

Move on to the front sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the same length as the back section. Repeat this process for all of the front sections.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

Check your hair in the mirror to ensure that it is even. If you notice any uneven sections, trim them with your scissors until you achieve a uniform length.

Tips for Cutting Long Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your long hair to shoulder length:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Take breaks if your arms and hands become tired from holding your hair up for an extended period.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can be difficult to gauge the length accurately.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair to shoulder length if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair to shoulder length. Keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may need to cut it slightly longer than your desired length.

Do I need to wash my hair before cutting it?

It's not necessary to wash your hair before cutting it, but it can make it easier to work with. If your hair is particularly tangled, you can spray it with water to dampen it and make it more manageable.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair to shoulder length, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can dry out your hair and cause split ends.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair and keep it hydrated.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it can cause breakage.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Cutting your long hair to shoulder length can be a great way to change up your look and make styling easier. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a beautiful, even cut that will leave you feeling confident and refreshed. Happy cutting!
