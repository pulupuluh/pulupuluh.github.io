How To Bleach Very Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver May 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, bleaching your very short hair can be a bold and daring choice. However, bleaching your hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have never done it before. This article will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to bleach very short hair, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Bleach Very Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Bleaching Very Short Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Bleaching

How to Bleach Very Short Hair

Bleaching very short hair requires a little bit of preparation and a lot of patience. Here are the steps you need to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup and prepare your hair for bleaching.
  2. Section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to apply the bleach evenly.
  3. Put on gloves to protect your hands and mix the bleach according to the instructions on the package. Be sure to mix the bleach in a well-ventilated area.
  4. Apply the bleach to your hair, starting from the back and working your way to the front. Use a brush or applicator to apply the bleach evenly. Be careful not to get bleach on your scalp or skin.
  5. Leave the bleach on for the recommended time, usually between 20-45 minutes, depending on the desired level of lightness.
  6. Check the color of your hair every 5-10 minutes. When you have reached the desired level of lightness, rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.
  7. Apply a toner to your hair to neutralize any yellow or orange tones. Leave the toner on for the recommended time, then rinse thoroughly with cool water.
  8. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid styling or using heat on your hair for at least 24 hours.

Tips for Bleaching Very Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when bleaching very short hair:

  • Use a high-quality bleach and developer to minimize damage to your hair.
  • Do a strand test before bleaching your entire head to ensure that your hair can handle the bleach.
  • If you have dark hair, you may need to bleach your hair multiple times to achieve the desired level of lightness.
  • Avoid washing your hair for 24-48 hours before bleaching to allow your natural oils to protect your scalp.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment after bleaching to restore moisture to your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people ask about bleaching very short hair:

  • Can I bleach my hair if I have a sensitive scalp? If you have a sensitive scalp, it's best to do a patch test before bleaching your hair. If you experience any discomfort or irritation, do not proceed with bleaching.
  • Can I bleach my hair if it's already been dyed? Yes, you can bleach your hair if it's been dyed, but you may need to bleach it multiple times to remove all the color.
  • What should I do if my hair turns orange? If your hair turns orange, you can use a blue or purple toner to neutralize the color. If the orange is very intense, you may need to bleach your hair again.

Hair Care After Bleaching

Bleaching your hair can be damaging, so it's important to take good care of your hair after the process. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to restore moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, including blow drying and straightening, for at least 24 hours after bleaching.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Limit your exposure to the sun, as bleached hair is more vulnerable to damage from UV rays.

By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a stunning bleached look for your very short hair. Remember to take care of your hair after the process to keep it healthy and looking its best.
