How To Make Natural Afro Hair Grow Longer The Ultimate Guide

Written by Robby Aug 05, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you tired of having short hair and want to grow your natural afro hair longer? If so, you’re in the right place. In this article, we’ll provide you with tips, tricks, and hair care advice that will help you achieve longer and healthier hair. So, without further ado, let’s get started!

  • Tips for Growing Natural Afro Hair Longer

How to Make Natural Afro Hair Grow Longer

The first step to growing your natural afro hair longer is to understand your hair type. Afro hair is naturally curly, coily, and dry, which makes it more prone to breakage. Therefore, it’s essential to keep your hair moisturized and healthy. Here are some tips to help you grow your natural afro hair longer:

1. Proper Nutrition

Your hair needs proper nutrition to grow long and healthy. Eat a well-balanced diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals. Foods that are rich in protein include eggs, lean meat, fish, and beans. Vitamins and minerals that are essential for hair growth include vitamin A, vitamin E, vitamin C, zinc, and iron.

2. Hydrate Your Body

Drinking plenty of water is essential for hair growth. Water hydrates your body, which in turn hydrates your hair. Aim to drink at least eight glasses of water per day to keep your hair and body hydrated.

3. Avoid Heat and Chemicals

Heat and chemicals can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. Also, avoid using harsh chemicals such as relaxers and hair dyes.

4. Protective Styles

Protective styles help to keep your hair protected from the elements and reduce breakage. Some protective styles include braids, twists, weaves, and wigs. However, be careful not to keep these styles in for too long as they can also damage your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to help you grow your natural afro hair longer:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while it’s wet.

Step 2: Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and cover it with a plastic cap. Leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out with cool water.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized throughout the day. Use a dime-sized amount and apply it to your hair from root to tip.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Style your hair in a protective style such as braids, twists, or a bun. Make sure the style is not too tight as this can lead to breakage.

Step 5: Moisturize Your Hair Daily

Moisturize your hair daily with a water-based moisturizer. Apply a small amount of the moisturizer to your hair and massage it in.

Tips for Growing Natural Afro Hair Longer

1. Trim Your Hair

Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends. Split ends can lead to breakage and prevent your hair from growing longer.

2. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp helps to increase blood flow to your hair follicles, which can promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for at least five minutes every day.

3. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Use a satin pillowcase to reduce friction on your hair while you sleep. Cotton pillowcases can cause friction, which can lead to breakage and damage to your hair.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles such as ponytails and braids as these can cause tension on your hair and lead to breakage.

FAQs

1. How Long Does It Take for Natural Afro Hair to Grow?

On average, hair grows about half an inch per month. However, the rate of hair growth can vary from person to person.

2. Can I Use Hair Growth Products on Natural Afro Hair?

Yes, you can use hair growth products on natural afro hair. Look for products that contain natural ingredients such as castor oil, coconut oil, and shea butter.

Hair Care for Natural Afro Hair

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and moisturized.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

3. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from the elements by wearing a hat or scarf when you’re outside. Also, avoid exposing your hair to harsh chemicals and heat.

4. Use Natural Products

Use natural products that are free from harsh chemicals and sulfates. Look for products that contain natural ingredients such as shea butter, castor oil, and coconut oil.

5. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Be gentle with your hair when you’re styling and detangling it. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid pulling or tugging on it.

By following these tips and tricks, you can grow your natural afro hair longer and healthier. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you’ll start to see results in no time!
