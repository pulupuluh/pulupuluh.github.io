How To Shave Your Own Mullet A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 14, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're looking for a bold and daring hairstyle, a mullet might be just what you need. While many people think of mullets as outdated or tacky, they've actually been making a comeback in recent years. However, getting a mullet haircut at a salon can be expensive, and not everyone has the time or money to invest in regular hair appointments. That's why we've put together this step-by-step guide on how to shave your own mullet at home. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced hair stylist, we've got you covered.

How to Shave Your Own Mullet

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, you'll need to make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers with various guards
  • A comb
  • A pair of scissors
  • A mirror (preferably one that's large enough to see the back of your head)
  • A towel or cape to catch any hair

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair so that you can see exactly what you're working with. Wash your hair as you normally would, and then dry it thoroughly with a towel or blow dryer.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, separate the top section of your hair from the bottom section. Clip the top section up and out of the way for now.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Using your clippers with a short guard, begin cutting the hair on the sides of your head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the comb to guide the clippers and create a straight line. Repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Trim the Top

Next, use your scissors to trim the hair on the top section of your head. You can customize the length and shape of your mullet here, depending on your personal preference. Keep in mind that the longer the hair on top, the more dramatic your mullet will be.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Now it's time to blend the layers together. Use your clippers with a longer guard to carefully blend the hair on the sides with the hair on top. This will create a smooth transition between the two sections.

Step 7: Cut the Back

This is the trickiest part of the process, but it's also the most important. Using your mirror to see the back of your head, carefully trim the hair at the nape of your neck. You can use your clippers or scissors for this step, depending on your preference. Take your time and be patient, as this is the part of your mullet that will be most visible.

Step 8: Clean Up the Edges

Using your clippers without a guard, carefully clean up the edges of your mullet. This will ensure that your haircut looks neat and polished.

Step 9: Style Your Mullet

Now that you've shaved your own mullet, it's time to style it. You can use a little bit of hair gel or pomade to create definition and hold. You can also experiment with different styles, such as a messy or spiky mullet.

Tips for Shaving Your Own Mullet

  • Start with a longer guard on your clippers and gradually work your way down to a shorter guard.
  • Take breaks as needed to rest your arms and hands, especially if you're not used to cutting your own hair.
  • Be patient and don't rush the process. It's better to take your time and get it right than to make a mistake and have to start over.

FAQ: Shaving Your Own Mullet

Is it easy to shave your own mullet?

Shaving your own mullet can be challenging, especially if you're not used to cutting your own hair. However, with practice and patience, it can be done.

What tools do I need to shave my own mullet?

You'll need clippers with various guards, a comb, a pair of scissors, a mirror, and a towel or cape to catch any hair.

How can I make my mullet look stylish?

Experiment with different styles and hair products, such as hair gel or pomade. You can also ask for advice from a friend or hairstylist.

Hair Care for Your Mullet

Now that you've shaved your own mullet, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as with a blow dryer, as this can cause damage.
  • Use hair products sparingly and avoid ones that contain harsh chemicals.
  • Get regular trims to keep your mullet looking neat and tidy.

Now that you know how to shave your own mullet, it's time to get started. With these tips and tricks, you'll be sporting a bold and daring hairstyle in no time. Happy shaving!
