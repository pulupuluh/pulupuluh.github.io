Face Framing Bangs For Dark Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 04, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your plain hair and want to add some dimension to your look? One easy way to do this is by adding face-framing bangs to your dark hair. This trendy hairstyle has been popular for years and shows no signs of slowing down. In this guide, we'll show you how to get the perfect face-framing bangs for your dark hair.

Description

Face-framing bangs are a hairstyle where the bangs are cut shorter on the sides and longer in the middle. This creates a frame around your face and helps draw attention to your eyes, cheekbones, and jawline. The style works well for all hair types and is particularly popular among people with dark hair.

How to Get Face Framing Bangs for Dark Hair

Step 1: Find the Right Stylist

Finding the right stylist is key to getting the perfect face-framing bangs for your dark hair. Look for a stylist who has experience cutting bangs and working with dark hair. You may also want to bring in pictures of the style you're looking for to ensure you and your stylist are on the same page.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Once you've found the right stylist, you'll need to decide on the length of your bangs. Face-framing bangs typically start at the top of your cheekbones and gradually get longer towards the center of your forehead. Your stylist can help you decide on the perfect length based on your face shape and hair texture.

Step 3: Maintain Your Bangs

Once you've got your face-framing bangs, you'll need to maintain them to keep them looking their best. Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them at the right length. You may also want to invest in a good dry shampoo to keep your bangs looking fresh between washes.

Tips for Face Framing Bangs for Dark Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting face-framing bangs for your dark hair:

  • Consider your face shape when deciding on the length of your bangs
  • Ask your stylist for layers to add dimension to your hair
  • Use a round brush when blow-drying your bangs to create volume
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles, such as side-swept bangs or blunt bangs

FAQs

How do I know if face-framing bangs are right for me?

Face-framing bangs work well for all face shapes and hair types. However, if you have a particularly wide or round face, you may want to avoid bangs that are too short or blunt.

How do I style my face-framing bangs?

You can style your face-framing bangs in a variety of ways, including blow-drying them with a round brush, straightening them with a flat iron, or curling them with a curling iron.

How often should I trim my bangs?

You should aim to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them at the right length.

Hair Care for Face Framing Bangs for Dark Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your face-framing bangs looking their best:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent your bangs from becoming greasy
  • Use a dry shampoo to keep your bangs looking fresh between washes
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush to avoid tangles and breakage

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to get the perfect face-framing bangs for your dark hair. Remember to find the right stylist, decide on the perfect length, and maintain your bangs to keep them looking their best. Whether you prefer side-swept or blunt bangs, this hairstyle is sure to add some dimension to your look.
