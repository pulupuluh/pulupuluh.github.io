How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair With Cowlick A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia May 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

As a parent, cutting your toddler boy's hair can be a daunting task. It becomes even more challenging when your little one has a cowlick. However, with the right tools and technique, you can give your toddler a great haircut even with a cowlick. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut toddler boy hair with cowlick. We will cover everything from the necessary tools to the final touches. Let's dive in.

Table of Contents

  • Tools you will need
  • How to cut toddler boy hair with cowlick
  • Step-by-step guide
  • Tips for cutting toddler boy hair with cowlick
  • FAQs
  • Hair care tips for toddler boys

Tools you will need

Before you start cutting your toddler boy's hair, you will need the following tools:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Styling gel or pomade (optional)

Once you have gathered all the necessary tools, you are ready to start cutting your toddler boy's hair.

How to cut toddler boy hair with cowlick

Cowlicks can be challenging to deal with, but they can be tamed with the right technique. Here is how to cut toddler boy hair with cowlick:

Step-by-step guide

  1. Start by washing your toddler's hair and towel drying it. Ensure that the hair is damp but not soaking wet.
  2. Place the haircutting cape or towel around your toddler's neck and secure it with a clip or safety pin.
  3. Use the comb to part the hair in the direction of the cowlick. The part should be about an inch wide.
  4. Start cutting the hair with scissors, one section at a time. Hold the hair between your fingers and trim it to the desired length.
  5. When you reach the cowlick, comb the hair in the opposite direction of the cowlick and cut it with scissors. This will help to prevent the cowlick from sticking up.
  6. Use the clippers to trim the sides and back of the hair. Start with a larger guard and work your way down to a smaller one for a more gradual fade.
  7. Blend the top and sides of the hair with scissors or clippers to create a seamless transition.
  8. Once you are done cutting, style the hair with gel or pomade if desired.
  9. Remove the haircutting cape or towel and clean up any stray hairs with a brush or vacuum.
  10. Finally, give your toddler a big hug and tell them how handsome they look!

Tips for cutting toddler boy hair with cowlick

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting toddler boy hair with cowlick:

  • Use sharp scissors and clippers to ensure clean cuts.
  • Work slowly and carefully, especially around the cowlick.
  • Use the comb to keep the hair taut while cutting.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take your time and do it in small sections.
  • If your toddler is wiggly, distract them with a toy or a snack.
  • If you're not confident in your cutting skills, consider taking your toddler to a professional stylist.

FAQs

How often should I cut my toddler's hair?

It depends on how fast their hair grows. On average, toddlers need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my toddler's hair?

Don't panic! Mistakes happen. You can try to fix it by blending the hair or trimming it shorter. If all else fails, take your toddler to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair care tips for toddler boys

Here are some tips to keep your toddler boy's hair looking healthy and shiny:

  • Wash their hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Brush their hair regularly to prevent tangles.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on their hair.
  • Cut their hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Encourage your toddler to wear a hat or use sunscreen when playing outdoors to protect their hair and scalp from the sun.

Cutting your toddler boy's hair with a cowlick doesn't have to be a stressful experience. With the right tools, technique, and a little bit of patience, you can give your little one a great haircut. We hope that this step-by-step guide has been helpful. Good luck!
