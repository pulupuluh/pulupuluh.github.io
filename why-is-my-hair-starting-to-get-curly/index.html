Why Is My Hair Starting To Get Curly

If you're someone who has always had straight hair but has recently noticed some curls or waves, you may be wondering why this is happening. There are several reasons why your hair may be starting to get curly, including genetics, changes in hormone levels, and damage to the hair.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

The first step in figuring out why your hair is starting to get curly is to determine your hair type. Hair types are generally categorized into four categories: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. To determine your hair type, wash and dry your hair without using any products, and examine your hair's natural texture.

Straight Hair Type

Straight hair is usually very smooth and shiny, and it doesn't have any curls, waves, or kinks. If your hair is straight, it will likely fall flat against your head without any volume or texture.

Wavy Hair Type

Wavy hair has some natural texture and movement. It may have loose curls or waves that are more defined in some areas than others. If you have wavy hair, it will likely have some volume and bounce.

Curly Hair Type

Curly hair has defined curls or waves that may be tight or loose. If you have curly hair, it may be prone to frizz and dryness, but it will also have a lot of volume and texture.

Coily Hair Type

Coily hair has very tight coils or curls that may be difficult to see when the hair is dry. If you have coily hair, it may be prone to dryness and breakage, but it will also have a lot of texture and volume.

Step by Step Guide to Determine Your Hair Type

Here's a step-by-step guide to determining your hair type:

  1. Wash your hair without using any products.
  2. Towel dry your hair gently.
  3. Let your hair air dry completely without using a blow dryer.
  4. Examine your hair's natural texture and movement.
  5. Compare your hair to the descriptions of the four hair types above to determine which one best describes your hair.

Tips for Caring for Your Curly Hair

If you've determined that your hair is starting to get curly, it's important to start caring for your hair properly to keep your curls healthy and defined. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushing can disrupt your curls.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

FAQs

Q: Can stress cause curly hair?

A: Yes, stress can cause changes to your hair texture and even cause it to become curly.

Q: Can pregnancy cause curly hair?

A: Yes, changes in hormone levels during pregnancy can cause changes to your hair texture, including making it curly.

Q: Can damage to the hair cause curly hair?

A: Yes, damage to the hair from things like heat styling and chemical treatments can cause changes to the hair texture, including making it curly.

Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, it's important to use hair care products that are specifically designed for your hair type. Here are some products that can help keep your curls healthy and defined:

  • Sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Curl defining cream or gel
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Deep conditioning treatment
  • Satin or silk pillowcase

In conclusion, if you've noticed that your hair is starting to get curly, there are several reasons why this may be happening. By determining your hair type and caring for your curls properly, you can keep your hair healthy and looking its best.
