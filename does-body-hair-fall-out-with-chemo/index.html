Does Body Hair Fall Out With Chemo

Written by William Apr 19, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Chemotherapy is a treatment that uses drugs to kill cancer cells. It is an effective way to treat cancer, but it can also cause some side effects. One of the most common side effects of chemotherapy is hair loss. Many people wonder if body hair falls out with chemo. In this article, we will answer that question and provide tips for caring for your hair during chemotherapy.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells in the body, including cancer cells and hair follicle cells. This is why hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy. However, not all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss, and not all people who receive chemotherapy experience hair loss. The amount of hair loss can also vary from person to person.

How to

If you are receiving chemotherapy and are concerned about hair loss, there are some things you can do to prepare for it. Here are some tips:

  • Consider cutting your hair short before starting chemotherapy. This can make hair loss less noticeable.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for sensitive scalps.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage hair.
  • Avoid coloring or perming your hair during chemotherapy.
  • Wear a wig or scarf to cover your head if you feel self-conscious about hair loss.

Step by Step

Here are some steps you can take to care for your hair during chemotherapy:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, as this can cause breakage. Instead, gently pat your hair dry.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb or a soft-bristled brush to detangle your hair.
  4. Avoid using hair accessories that pull on your hair, such as tight headbands or ponytail holders.
  5. Consider using a satin or silk pillowcase, as these materials are gentler on hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your hair during chemotherapy:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can dry out your scalp.
  • Use a moisturizing scalp treatment to help soothe dryness and itching.
  • Wear a hat or scarf in the sun to protect your scalp from UV rays.
  • Consider using a cold cap during chemotherapy to help reduce hair loss.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss and chemotherapy:

Does body hair fall out with chemo?

Yes, body hair can fall out with chemotherapy. However, not all people who receive chemotherapy experience hair loss, and the amount of hair loss can vary from person to person.

Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

Yes, hair usually grows back after chemotherapy. However, it may be a different texture or color than before. It can take several months for hair to grow back.

Can I dye my hair during chemotherapy?

It is generally not recommended to dye or perm your hair during chemotherapy, as it can damage your hair and scalp. However, you should discuss this with your doctor and hair stylist to determine the best course of action for your individual situation.

Hair Care

It is important to take care of your hair during chemotherapy to minimize hair loss and promote healthy regrowth. Use gentle products and avoid heat styling tools and hair accessories that can damage your hair. Consider getting a short haircut before starting chemotherapy and use a wig or scarf to cover your head if you feel self-conscious about hair loss. With proper care, your hair will likely grow back after chemotherapy.
