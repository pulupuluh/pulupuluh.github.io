How To Soften Natural Hair Without Relaxer A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 17, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you have natural hair, you might find it difficult to manage and style. While many people opt for relaxers to tame their hair, there are other ways to soften your natural hair without using harsh chemicals. In this article, we will guide you on how to soften natural hair without relaxer and provide you with some helpful tips to manage and care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Soften Natural Hair without Relaxer
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Softening Natural Hair
  • FAQs
  • Natural Hair Care

Description

Natural hair is beautiful, but it can also be challenging to manage. One of the most common problems that people with natural hair face is dryness and brittleness. Dry hair is prone to breakage and split ends, which can be frustrating and damaging to your hair's health. Relaxers smooth out the hair's cuticles, making it easier to manage and style. However, relaxers contain harsh chemicals that can damage your hair and scalp.

Fortunately, there are several ways to soften natural hair without using a relaxer. Softening natural hair involves moisturizing and conditioning your hair to make it more pliable and easier to manage. In the following sections, we will guide you through the steps to soften natural hair without relaxer.

How to Soften Natural Hair without Relaxer

Softening natural hair without relaxer involves a combination of moisturizing and conditioning techniques. Here are some tips to help you achieve softer, more manageable hair:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo

Start by using a moisturizing shampoo that is specifically formulated for natural hair. Look for shampoos that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil, which can help to hydrate and nourish your hair.

2. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair is essential for softening your natural hair. You should deep condition your hair at least once a week to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Use a deep conditioning treatment that is rich in natural ingredients like avocado oil, honey, and aloe vera.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing and conditioning your hair, use a leave-in conditioner to help lock in moisture. Apply the leave-in conditioner to your hair while it is still damp, and be sure to distribute it evenly throughout your hair.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair and make it more difficult to manage. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.

5. Use Natural Oils

Natural oils like coconut oil, olive oil, and jojoba oil can help to soften your hair and make it more manageable. Apply a small amount of oil to your hair after washing and conditioning it to help lock in moisture and prevent breakage.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to soften natural hair without relaxer:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo. Be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove all shampoo residue.

Step 2: Deep Condition Your Hair

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, and leave it in for at least 15 minutes. For best results, use a plastic cap or a warm towel to help the treatment penetrate your hair.

Step 3: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly to remove all conditioner residue.

Step 4: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair while it is still damp. Be sure to distribute the conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

Step 5: Apply Natural Oil

Apply a small amount of natural oil to your hair to help lock in moisture and prevent breakage. Be sure to distribute the oil evenly throughout your hair.

Tips for Softening Natural Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you soften your natural hair:

  • Protect your hair at night by wrapping it in a silk scarf or sleeping on a silk pillowcase.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair.
  • Limit your use of heat styling tools, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

FAQs

Q: Can I soften my natural hair without using any products?

A: Yes, you can. One way to soften your natural hair without using any products is to do a hot oil treatment. Heat up some natural oil, like coconut oil, and apply it to your hair. Cover your hair with a plastic cap or a warm towel, and let it sit for at least 30 minutes. Rinse your hair thoroughly, and you should notice a difference in how soft and manageable your hair is.

Q: How often should I wash my natural hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and texture. Some people with natural hair can go a week or more without washing their hair, while others need to wash their hair every few days. You should wash your hair when it feels dirty or oily, but be careful not to over-wash your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Natural Hair Care

Caring for natural hair can be challenging, but it is essential to keep your hair healthy and looking its best. Softening your hair is just one part of a comprehensive hair care routine. Here are some additional tips to help you care for your natural hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid pulling your hair too tightly, as this can cause breakage and hair loss.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals, which can help to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Protect your hair from sun damage by wearing a hat or scarf when you are outside.

By following these tips and techniques, you can soften your natural hair without using a relaxer. Remember to be patient and consistent in your hair care routine, and you will see results over time. With proper care and attention, your natural hair can be healthy, soft, and beautiful.
