How To Use Thinning Shears On Hair Extensions A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 15, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Thinning shears are an essential tool in any hairdresser's kit. They are used to remove bulk from hair and create texture, which is why they are often used on hair extensions too. However, if you're new to using thinning shears, you might be unsure about how to use them properly on hair extensions. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about using thinning shears on hair extensions.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Hair Extensions

Before you start using thinning shears on hair extensions, it's important to understand that not all extensions can be cut with thinning shears. For example, if you have clip-in extensions, you cannot use thinning shears on them as they are not attached to your natural hair. However, if you have sew-in or bonded extensions, you can use thinning shears on them.

Thinning shears are used to remove bulk from hair, so they are especially useful if you have thick hair extensions. They work by cutting some of the hair strands while leaving others intact. This creates a textured, layered look that is perfect for those who want to add volume to their hair.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to use thinning shears on hair extensions:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair extensions. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and identify the areas where you need to remove bulk.
  2. Section your hair extensions by creating a part at the back of your head, from ear to ear. Clip the top section of your hair extensions away from the bottom section.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom section and comb it straight down. This will help you see the length of the hair and identify the areas where you need to remove bulk.
  4. Hold the thinning shears with the teeth facing upwards and the blades facing downwards. Cut into the hair at a 45-degree angle, starting from the middle of the hair strand and working your way downwards. Make sure to cut only a few strands at a time to avoid cutting too much hair.
  5. Continue cutting into the hair until you have removed the desired amount of bulk. Then, move on to the next section of hair and repeat the process.
  6. Once you have finished thinning the bottom section of your hair extensions, repeat the process on the top section of your hair extensions.
  7. After you have finished thinning your hair extensions, run your fingers through your hair to ensure that the layers are blended and there are no uneven areas.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Hair Extensions

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on hair extensions:

  • Always use sharp, high-quality thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair extensions.
  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair and work your way up to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Don't use thinning shears on the ends of your hair extensions as this can create a blunt, uneven look.
  • Remember that once you cut the hair with thinning shears, you cannot add the hair back. So, make sure to cut a little at a time and check your progress frequently.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning shears on hair extensions:

Can I use thinning shears on all types of hair extensions?

No, you cannot use thinning shears on clip-in extensions as they are not attached to your natural hair. However, you can use thinning shears on sew-in or bonded extensions.

Can I use regular scissors instead of thinning shears?

You can use regular scissors to cut your hair extensions, but they will not create the same textured, layered look that thinning shears do. Thinning shears are specifically designed to remove bulk and create texture, so they are the best tool for the job.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair extensions?

This depends on how quickly your hair extensions grow and how often you wear them. As a general rule, you should use thinning shears on your hair extensions every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent them from looking too bulky.

Hair Care Tips for Hair Extensions

Here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind when you have hair extensions:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair extensions from drying out.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools on your hair extensions as they can damage the hair.
  • Brush your hair extensions gently with a wide-tooth comb or a special hair extension brush to prevent tangling.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair extensions as this can cause them to tangle.
  • Don't use too much product on your hair extensions as this can weigh them down and make them look greasy.

By following these tips and using thinning shears properly, you can maintain the health and appearance of your hair extensions and achieve the textured, layered look you desire.
