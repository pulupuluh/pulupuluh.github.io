How Long Should It Take To Grow Dreads

Written by Robby Mar 09, 2023 · 10 min read

When it comes to growing dreads, many people wonder how long it will take to achieve their desired length. The truth is that there is no one-size-fits-all answer, as the time it takes to grow dreads can vary depending on a number of factors. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the key factors that can impact the length of time it takes to grow dreads, as well as provide some tips and advice for those looking to grow their own dreads.

Table of Contents

  • Factors That Impact Dread Growth
  • How to Grow Dreads
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Dreads
  • Tips for Growing Dreads
  • FAQs About Growing Dreads
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Factors That Impact Dread Growth

The time it takes to grow dreads can vary depending on a number of factors, including:

  • Hair Type: Different hair types grow at different rates, which can impact the time it takes to grow dreads. For example, if you have fine hair, it may take longer to grow dreads than someone with thick hair.
  • Dread Size: The size of your dreads can also impact the length of time it takes to grow them. Larger dreads may take longer to grow than smaller ones, as there is more hair involved.
  • Length: The length of your hair when you start growing dreads can also impact the time it takes to achieve your desired length. If you start with short hair, it may take longer to grow longer dreads than if you start with longer hair.
  • Maintenance: How you maintain your dreads can also impact growth time. Proper maintenance and care can help your dreads grow faster, while neglecting them can slow down growth.
  • Overall Health: The health of your hair and scalp can also impact dread growth. If your hair and scalp are healthy, your dreads are more likely to grow quickly and reach their desired length.

While these factors can impact the length of time it takes to grow dreads, it’s important to remember that everyone’s hair is unique and will grow at its own pace. Some people may be able to grow long, healthy dreads in just a few months, while others may take years to achieve their desired length.

How to Grow Dreads

If you’re looking to grow dreads, there are a few key steps you can take to help speed up the process:

  • Pick a Method: There are several methods for growing dreads, including the backcombing method, the twist and rip method, and the neglect method. Do some research to find the method that works best for you.
  • Start with Clean Hair: Before starting the dread process, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products or buildup.
  • Maintain Your Dreads: Proper maintenance is key to growing healthy dreads. Be sure to wash your hair regularly, use a residue-free shampoo, and avoid over-styling or manipulating your dreads too much.
  • Be Patient: Growing dreads takes time and patience. Don’t get discouraged if your dreads aren’t growing as quickly as you’d like – just keep taking care of them and they will eventually reach their desired length.

Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Dreads

If you’re new to growing dreads, here is a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Choose a method for growing your dreads.
  2. Start with clean hair.
  3. Section your hair into small, even sections.
  4. Begin backcombing or twisting and ripping each section of hair.
  5. Secure each dread with a rubber band or hair tie.
  6. Leave your dreads in for several weeks, removing the rubber bands as needed.
  7. Maintain your dreads by washing them regularly and using a residue-free shampoo.
  8. Be patient and wait for your dreads to grow to your desired length.

Tips for Growing Dreads

Here are a few more tips to help you grow healthy, beautiful dreads:

  • Protect Your Hair: Wear a hat or headscarf to protect your dreads from damage caused by the sun or wind.
  • Avoid Chemicals: Avoid using chemical treatments or dyes on your hair, as they can damage your dreads and slow down growth.
  • Eat a Healthy Diet: Eating a diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can help promote healthy hair growth.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drinking plenty of water can also help keep your hair and scalp healthy, which can promote faster dread growth.

FAQs About Growing Dreads

Here are some common questions people have about growing dreads:

How long does it take to grow dreads?

The length of time it takes to grow dreads can vary depending on a number of factors, including hair type, dread size, length, maintenance, and overall health. Some people may be able to grow long, healthy dreads in just a few months, while others may take years to achieve their desired length.

Can I grow dreads if I have straight hair?

Yes, you can grow dreads with straight hair. However, it may take longer to achieve your desired length than if you have curly or wavy hair.

Do I need to cut my hair to grow dreads?

No, you don’t need to cut your hair to grow dreads. However, starting with shorter hair may make it more difficult to achieve longer dreads, as there will be less hair to work with.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Proper hair care is essential for growing healthy, beautiful dreads. Here are some tips to help you take care of your dreads:

  • Wash Your Hair: Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup and keep your scalp healthy.
  • Avoid Over-Styling: Avoid over-styling or manipulating your dreads too much, as this can cause damage and slow down growth.
  • Use Natural Oils: Use natural oils like coconut or jojoba oil to keep your scalp moisturized and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Trim Your Ends: Regularly trimming the ends of your dreads can help prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy.

By following these tips and taking good care of your dreads, you can help promote healthy growth and achieve your desired length in no time!
