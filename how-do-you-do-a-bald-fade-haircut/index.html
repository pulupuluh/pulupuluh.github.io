How Do You Do A Bald Fade Haircut

Written by Petter May 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and versatile haircut that can be worn by men of all ages, a bald fade might be just what you need. This trendy hairstyle is characterized by a gradual transition from short hair on the sides and back to a bald or skin-like finish on the top of the head. The good news is that you don't need to be a professional barber to achieve a bald fade haircut. In this blog post, we'll guide you through the steps to get this look at home.

How to Do a Bald Fade Haircut

Before you start cutting, you'll need a few tools, including:

  • Clippers with various guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircut cape or towel
  • Clipper oil
  • Brush

Once you have all the necessary tools, you can start the haircut following these steps:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Wash and dry the hair before the haircut. Make sure that the hair is free of any tangles or knots. Comb the hair thoroughly to get rid of any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Choose the Length of the Fade

Before starting the haircut, decide how long you want the fade to be. You can choose to have a high fade, mid-fade, or low fade. The high fade is the shortest, while the low fade is the longest.

Step 3: Start with the Longer Guard Size

Start with the longest guard size and run the clippers over the hair on the sides and back of the head. Use an upward motion to create a blended effect between the short hair and the longer hair on top.

Step 4: Switch to the Shorter Guard Size

Switch to a shorter guard size and go over the hair again, starting from the bottom of the hairline and working upwards. Blend the hair as you go, using the longer guard size to create a smooth transition between the different lengths of hair.

Step 5: Use Scissors to Cut the Top of the Hair

Use scissors to cut the hair on top of the head to your desired length. Start at the front and work your way to the back, using the comb to keep the hair in place as you cut.

Step 6: Blend the Top and Sides

Blend the hair on top with the hair on the sides and back to create a smooth transition. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a seamless finish.

Step 7: Clean up the Edges

Use the clippers without a guard to clean up the edges around the hairline, sideburns, and neck. Use gentle strokes and take your time to avoid any mistakes.

Step 8: Brush and Style the Hair

Brush the hair to remove any loose hairs and style it using your preferred hair product. You can choose to leave the hair messy or slicked back depending on your personal style.

Tips for a Perfect Bald Fade Haircut

  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Use a mirror to check the symmetry of the haircut.
  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid any mistakes.
  • Use clipper oil to keep the blades lubricated and prevent them from overheating.
  • Practice makes perfect, so don't be discouraged if your first attempt doesn't turn out the way you want it to.

FAQ

Q: Can I do a bald fade on my own?

A: Yes, you can do a bald fade on your own with the right tools and some practice.

Q: How long does a bald fade last?

A: A bald fade can last anywhere from 2-4 weeks depending on how fast your hair grows.

Q: Can I get a bald fade with curly hair?

A: Yes, you can get a bald fade with curly hair. However, it's important to find a barber who is experienced with cutting curly hair to ensure that the haircut is done correctly.

Hair Care after a Bald Fade Haircut

After getting a bald fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair product like pomade or wax to style your hair.
  • Get regular haircuts to maintain the shape of the haircut.

Now that you know how to do a bald fade haircut, it's time to put your skills to the test. With some practice and patience, you can achieve a stylish and trendy haircut that will turn heads.
