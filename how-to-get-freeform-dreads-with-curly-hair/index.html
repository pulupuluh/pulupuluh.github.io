How To Get Freeform Dreads With Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline May 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Freeform dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle choice for many people with curly hair. This style is achieved by allowing the hair to naturally dread without the use of any styling products or techniques. While this may seem like an easy process, it can be difficult to achieve the desired look. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to getting freeform dreads with curly hair.

How to Get Freeform Dreads with Curly Hair

Before we get into the steps to achieve freeform dreads, it's important to understand that this style requires patience and commitment. Freeform dreads can take anywhere from six months to a year to fully form, so be prepared for a long-term commitment. With that said, let's get started.

Step 1: Stop Using Shampoo

The first step in achieving freeform dreads is to stop using shampoo. Shampoo strips the hair of its natural oils, which are essential for helping the hair to naturally dread. Instead, use a natural conditioner or apple cider vinegar to cleanse the scalp and hair.

Step 2: Let Your Hair Grow

Next, let your hair grow out. The longer your hair, the easier it will be to form dreadlocks. As your hair grows, avoid brushing or combing it. This will allow the hair to naturally tangle and form dreads.

Step 3: Separate the Dreads

As your hair begins to tangle and form dreads, it's important to separate them to prevent them from merging together. Use your fingers to gently pull apart any dreads that are starting to merge together.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you understand the basic steps to getting freeform dreads, let's break it down into a step-by-step guide.

Step 1: Stop Using Shampoo

As mentioned earlier, stop using shampoo and instead use a natural conditioner or apple cider vinegar to cleanse your scalp and hair.

Step 2: Let Your Hair Grow

Allow your hair to grow out without brushing or combing it. This will allow the hair to naturally tangle and form dreads.

Step 3: Separate the Dreads

As your hair begins to form dreads, use your fingers to gently pull apart any dreads that are starting to merge together.

Step 4: Be Patient

Freeform dreads take time to form, so be patient and allow your hair to naturally dread.

Tips for Maintaining Freeform Dreads

Once you've achieved the desired look, it's important to maintain your freeform dreads to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips to help you maintain your dreads:

TIP 1: Keep Your Scalp Healthy

Healthy hair starts with a healthy scalp. Keep your scalp moisturized with natural oils and avoid using harsh chemicals that can dry out your scalp.

TIP 2: Be Gentle with Your Dreads

Avoid pulling or tugging on your dreads, as this can cause them to break or become damaged. Instead, be gentle when washing and styling your hair.

TIP 3: Use Natural Products

When choosing hair care products, opt for natural products that are free of harsh chemicals and sulfates. These products are gentler on your hair and will help keep your dreads looking their best.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take to form freeform dreads?

A: Freeform dreads can take anywhere from six months to a year to fully form.

Q: Can I style my freeform dreads?

A: Yes, you can style your freeform dreads. However, be gentle when styling to avoid damaging your dreads.

Q: Do I need to use wax or products to form freeform dreads?

A: No, freeform dreads are formed naturally without the use of any styling products or techniques.

Hair Care for Freeform Dreads

Maintaining healthy hair is crucial for keeping your freeform dreads looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

TIP 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly with a natural conditioner or apple cider vinegar. This will help keep your scalp and hair healthy.

TIP 2: Moisturize Your Hair

Moisturize your hair with natural oils like coconut oil or olive oil. This will help keep your hair soft and healthy.

TIP 3: Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals or sulfates on your hair, as these can dry out your scalp and hair.

By following these tips and steps, you can achieve and maintain freeform dreads with curly hair. Remember, patience is key, so be prepared for a long-term commitment to this hairstyle.
