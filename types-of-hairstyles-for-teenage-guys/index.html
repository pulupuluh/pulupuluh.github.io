Types Of Hairstyles For Teenage Guys A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Apr 09, 2023 · 6 min read

As a teenage guy in 2023, you probably have a lot on your plate. Between school, sports, and socializing, it can be tough to find the time to experiment with your hair. But trust us, a great hairstyle can make all the difference in your confidence and overall style. So, we’ve put together a comprehensive guide to the best types of hairstyles for teenage guys.

Table of Contents

  • Short Hair
  • Long Hair
  • Curly Hair
  • Fade Haircut
  • Spiky Hair
  • Messy Hair
  • Slicked Back Hair
  • Faux Hawk
  • Bangs
  • Crew Cut

Short Hair

Short hair is a classic look that will never go out of style. It’s easy to maintain and looks great on almost everyone. For teenage guys, a short haircut can be a great choice if you want a low-maintenance style that’s easy to manage.

If you’re looking for a short hairstyle, there are a few different options to choose from. The buzz cut is a popular choice for guys who want a simple, no-fuss look. Another option is the crew cut, which is slightly longer on top and shorter on the sides.

How to Style Short Hair

Styling short hair is easy. Simply use a small amount of hair product, such as gel or pomade, to create texture and hold. You can also use a comb or your fingers to style your hair into a messy, tousled look.

Tips for Short Hair

  • Invest in a good pair of clippers if you plan on maintaining your short hair yourself
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your scalp healthy

FAQ

Q: Can I still style my short hair for special occasions?

A: Absolutely! You can use a little bit of product and a comb to create a slicked back or side-swept look for more formal events.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair requires minimal maintenance, but it’s still important to take care of your scalp and hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy, and avoid using too much product as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Long Hair

Long hair can be a great way to express your individuality and stand out from the crowd. However, it does require a bit more maintenance than shorter styles. If you’re considering growing out your hair, there are a few things you should keep in mind.

How to Style Long Hair

Long hair can be styled in a variety of ways, from a messy, beachy look to a sleek, polished style. Use a comb or your fingers to create the look you want, and use a small amount of product to add texture and hold.

Tips for Long Hair

  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as straighteners or curling irons, as they can damage your hair

FAQ

Q: Can I still play sports with long hair?

A: Of course! Just make sure to tie your hair back securely to prevent it from getting in your face or getting tangled.

Hair Care for Long Hair

Long hair requires a bit more maintenance than shorter styles. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy, and avoid using hot tools or harsh chemicals as they can damage your hair.

Curly Hair

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it’s naturally voluminous and textured, it can also be difficult to manage. If you have curly hair, there are a few different hairstyles you can try to make the most of your natural texture.

How to Style Curly Hair

Curly hair can be styled in a variety of ways, from a messy, tousled look to a defined, sculpted style. Use a curl-defining product, such as mousse or gel, to enhance your natural texture and hold your style in place.

Tips for Curly Hair

  • Avoid brushing your hair, as it can cause frizz and disrupt your natural curl pattern
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it’s wet
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, but be careful not to use too much heat or to straighten your hair too frequently, as it can cause damage.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it looking its best. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and frizz, and avoid using hot tools or harsh chemicals.

Fade Haircut

The fade haircut is a popular choice for teenage guys, as it’s a versatile style that can be customized to suit your individual preferences. The fade refers to the gradual blending of hair lengths from short to long, creating a seamless and polished look.

How to Style a Fade Haircut

A fade haircut can be styled in a variety of ways, depending on the length and texture of your hair. Use a comb or your fingers to create your desired look, and use a small amount of product to add texture and hold.

Tips for a Fade Haircut

  • Find a skilled barber or hairstylist who can create a fade that suits your face shape and hair type
  • Experiment with different lengths and textures to find the style that works best for you
  • Get regular trims to maintain your fade and keep it looking sharp

FAQ

Q: Can I still style my hair if I have a fade?

A: Absolutely! The great thing about a fade is that it can be styled in a variety of ways, from a sleek, polished look to a messy, tousled style.

Hair Care for a Fade Haircut

A fade haircut requires regular maintenance to keep it looking sharp. Visit your barber or hairstylist every few weeks for a trim, and use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

Spiky Hair

Spiky hair is a bold and edgy style that’s popular among teenage guys. This style involves using a comb or your fingers to create spikes throughout your hair, adding texture and volume.

How to Style Spiky Hair

To style spiky hair, use a strong-hold gel or pomade and work it through your hair with your fingers. Use a comb to create spikes throughout your hair, and use your fingers to tweak and adjust the spikes until they’re just right.

Tips for Spiky Hair

  • Experiment with different lengths and textures to find the style that works best for you
  • Use a strong-hold hair product to keep your spikes in place all day
  • Don’t overdo it with the spikes – less is often more when it comes to this style

FAQ

Q: Can I still wear a hat with spiky hair?

A: Yes, but be careful not to flatten your spikes when you put on or take off your hat.

Hair Care for Spiky Hair
