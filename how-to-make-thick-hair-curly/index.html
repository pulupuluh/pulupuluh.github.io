How To Make Thick Hair Curly A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 24, 2023 · 8 min read

Do you envy those with luscious curls and wish you could get them too? Well, the good news is, you don't have to be born with curly hair to enjoy the look. Even if you have naturally thick, straight hair, there are ways to get those bouncy curls you've always wanted. In this article, we'll explore how to make thick hair curly, step-by-step, and provide tips and advice to help you achieve the perfect curls.

Description

Thick hair can be a challenge to curl, but with the right technique, it's possible to achieve beautiful, bouncy curls that last all day. In this article, we'll explore various methods for curling thick hair, from using heat styling tools to natural methods like braiding and twisting. We'll also provide tips and advice on how to care for your curls to keep them looking their best.

How to Make Thick Hair Curly: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Prep your hair

The key to successful curls is starting with clean, dry hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to cleanse and condition your hair, and then towel-dry it thoroughly. If you have naturally oily hair, you may want to use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil before curling.

Step 2: Apply heat protectant

Before using any heat styling tools, it's important to protect your hair from damage. Apply a heat protectant spray or cream to your hair, focusing on the ends, where damage is most likely to occur.

Step 3: Use a curling iron

One of the easiest ways to make thick hair curly is to use a curling iron. Choose a curling iron with a barrel size that matches the type of curl you want – smaller barrels create tighter curls, while larger barrels create looser waves. Section your hair into small sections and wrap each section around the curling iron, holding for a few seconds before releasing.

Step 4: Try a curling wand

If you prefer a more natural-looking curl, a curling wand may be a better option. Like a curling iron, a curling wand heats up and curls your hair, but without the clamp, giving you more control over the size and shape of your curls.

Step 5: Use hot rollers

If you prefer a heat-free option, hot rollers are a great way to make thick hair curly. Simply roll small sections of hair around the heated rollers, securing with a clip, and leave for 10-20 minutes before removing. The result is soft, bouncy curls without any damage from heat styling tools.

Step 6: Braid your hair

If you prefer a more natural, bohemian look, braiding your hair is a great way to make thick hair curly. Simply braid your hair and leave overnight, or for a few hours, before undoing the braid to reveal beautiful, natural-looking waves.

Tips for Making Thick Hair Curly

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect curls:

  • Use a high-quality curling iron or wand for best results.
  • Don't overload your hair with product – a little goes a long way.
  • Experiment with different barrel sizes to find the perfect curl for your hair type.
  • Don't forget to use heat protectant to prevent damage.
  • Use hairspray to hold your curls in place.
  • Try different methods, like braiding and twisting, to find the method that works best for you.

FAQs

Can I curl thick hair without heat?

Yes, there are several ways to make thick hair curly without using heat. Try braiding your hair or using hot rollers for a heat-free option.

How do I keep my curls looking good all day?

Use a high-quality hairspray to hold your curls in place, and avoid touching or brushing your hair throughout the day. You can also use a curl-refreshing spray to revive your curls if they start to fall flat.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Once you've achieved the perfect curls, it's important to take care of them to ensure they stay looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls, rather than a brush.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing product to keep your curls looking defined and bouncy.

With these tips and techniques, you can make thick hair curly and enjoy the bouncy, beautiful curls you've always wanted. Try different methods, experiment with different products, and don't be afraid to get creative to find the perfect curls for your hair.
