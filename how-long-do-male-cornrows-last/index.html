How Long Do Male Cornrows Last

Written by Oliver Aug 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you considering getting cornrows but wondering how long they will last? Male cornrows have become a popular hairstyle in recent years, with many men opting for this low-maintenance and stylish look. In this article, we will discuss how long male cornrows can last and provide tips on how to extend their lifespan.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Get Male Cornrows
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Male Cornrows
  • Tips to Make Male Cornrows Last Longer
  • FAQs About Male Cornrows
  • Hair Care for Male Cornrows

How to Get Male Cornrows

Before we dive into how long male cornrows last, let's first talk about how to get them. Cornrows are created by braiding the hair in a continuous, raised pattern along the scalp. This style is typically done with natural hair, but extensions can also be added for a fuller look.

To get male cornrows, you will need to find a skilled stylist who has experience with this style. The stylist will begin by sectioning your hair and then braiding it in a pattern that suits your hair type and desired look. The process can take anywhere from 2 to 6 hours depending on the complexity of the style and the length of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Male Cornrows

Here is a step-by-step guide to getting male cornrows:

  1. Wash and condition your hair to remove any product buildup.
  2. Section your hair into the desired pattern using a comb.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the first section and divide it into three equal parts.
  4. Begin braiding the hair by crossing the right section over the middle section, then the left section over the new middle section.
  5. Add more hair to each section as you braid along the scalp.
  6. Continue braiding until you reach the end of the section, then secure the braid with a small rubber band.
  7. Repeat steps 3-6 for each section of hair until the entire head is braided.
  8. Trim any loose ends and apply a light oil to the scalp to prevent dryness.

Tips to Make Male Cornrows Last Longer

Now that you know how to get male cornrows, let's talk about how to make them last longer. On average, male cornrows can last anywhere from 2 to 6 weeks depending on how well they are cared for. Here are some tips to help extend their lifespan:

  • Wear a silk or satin scarf or bonnet to bed to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid exposing the cornrows to water for extended periods of time, such as during swimming or showering.
  • Apply a light oil or leave-in conditioner to the scalp and braids to prevent dryness and itching.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on the braids, as this can cause them to loosen and unravel.
  • Use a mild shampoo to cleanse the scalp, and avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down the hair.

FAQs About Male Cornrows

1. Are male cornrows damaging to the hair?

No, male cornrows are not inherently damaging to the hair. However, if they are braided too tightly or left in for too long, they can cause breakage and hair loss. It is important to find a skilled stylist who can braid your hair in a way that is not too tight or uncomfortable.

2. Can you wash your hair with cornrows?

Yes, you can wash your hair with cornrows, but you should avoid getting the braids too wet. Use a mild shampoo and apply it directly to the scalp, being careful not to disturb the braids. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water, and avoid using hot water or heavy products that can weigh down the hair.

3. How often should you get your cornrows redone?

On average, male cornrows can last anywhere from 2 to 6 weeks depending on how well they are cared for. You should plan to get them redone once they start to look frizzy or when the new growth becomes too noticeable. It is important not to leave them in for too long, as this can cause breakage and hair loss.

Hair Care for Male Cornrows

Finally, it is important to take care of your hair while it is in cornrows. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Apply a light oil or leave-in conditioner to the scalp and braids to prevent dryness and itching.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down the hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and overall health.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, which can also help promote hair growth.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling wands, as these can cause damage and breakage.

In conclusion, male cornrows can last anywhere from 2 to 6 weeks depending on how well they are cared for. By following the tips and advice in this article, you can help extend their lifespan and maintain healthy hair. Remember to find a skilled stylist who can braid your hair in a way that is not too tight or uncomfortable, and to take care of your hair while it is in cornrows.
