What Size Box Braids Last Longer A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Jul 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Box braids are a popular protective hairstyle that can last anywhere from a few weeks to a few months. However, the size of the braids can play a big role in how long they last. In this article, we will be discussing what size box braids last longer and provide you with tips on how to care for them to ensure they last as long as possible.

Table of Contents

  • What are Box Braids?
  • What Size Box Braids Last Longer?

  • Tips for Maintaining Box Braids

What are Box Braids?

Box braids are a type of protective hairstyle that involves sectioning the hair into small, square-shaped boxes and braiding each section. This style is popular among women with natural hair, but it can also be done on relaxed hair. Box braids can be styled in many different ways and can last for weeks or even months with proper care.

What Size Box Braids Last Longer?

The size of box braids can have a big impact on how long they last. Generally, the smaller the braids, the longer they will last. Micro braids, which are very small and thin, can last up to 3 months with proper care. Small box braids, which are slightly larger than micro braids, can last up to 2 months. Medium-sized box braids can last up to 6 weeks, while larger box braids may only last 4 weeks or less.

It's important to choose a size that works for you and your lifestyle. Smaller braids may take longer to install, but they can last longer and require less maintenance. Larger braids may be quicker to install, but they may not last as long and may require more maintenance.

How to Install Box Braids

Box braids can be installed at home or at a salon. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to install box braids:

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Section your hair into small, square-shaped boxes.
  3. Take a small section of hair and divide it into three equal parts.
  4. Braid the hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Secure the braid with a small elastic band or a piece of hair.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 for each section of hair.
  7. Trim any loose ends and style as desired.

It's important to take breaks during the installation process to prevent fatigue and to avoid pulling the hair too tightly, which can cause damage and breakage.

Tips for Maintaining Box Braids

Here are some tips for maintaining box braids:

  • Keep your scalp clean and moisturized by washing your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down the hair and cause buildup.
  • Wear a satin or silk scarf or bonnet at night to prevent frizz and maintain the style.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling or pulling on the braids, which can cause damage and breakage.

FAQs

How long do box braids typically last?

Box braids can last anywhere from a few weeks to a few months, depending on the size of the braids and how well they are maintained.

Can box braids be washed?

Yes, box braids can be washed. It's important to use a gentle shampoo and to avoid scrubbing the scalp too vigorously to prevent frizz and damage to the braids.

Can box braids be styled?

Yes, box braids can be styled in many different ways, including updos, ponytails, and braided styles.

Hair Care for Box Braids

It's important to take care of your hair and scalp while wearing box braids to ensure that they last as long as possible and to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Moisturize your scalp regularly with a lightweight oil or scalp treatment.
  • Avoid over-manipulating the braids or pulling on them too tightly.
  • Wear a protective style, such as a satin or silk scarf or bonnet, when sleeping or exercising.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down the hair and cause buildup.
  • Visit a stylist for regular maintenance, such as trims and touch-ups.

In conclusion, the size of box braids can have a big impact on how long they last. Smaller braids may take longer to install, but they can last longer and require less maintenance. Larger braids may be quicker to install, but they may not last as long and may require more maintenance. It's important to choose a size that works for you and your lifestyle and to take proper care of your hair and scalp while wearing box braids.
