How To Cut My Own Hair At The Back A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every month to get a haircut? Do you want to save some money by learning how to cut your own hair at the back? If yes, then you have come to the right place. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own hair at the back without any help.

Description

Many people find it difficult to cut their own hair, especially at the back. It can be challenging to get the right angle and length, and it can also be hard to reach the back of your head. However, with the right tools and technique, anyone can learn how to cut their own hair at the back.

How To

To cut your own hair at the back, you will need a few things:

  • A handheld mirror
  • A pair of sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A hair tie or clip

Once you have these things, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner, and then dry it with a towel or blow dryer. Wet hair is harder to cut, so it's best to work with dry hair.

Step 2: Tie Your Hair Up

Use a hair tie or clip to tie your hair up into a ponytail. Make sure it is high up on your head, near the crown. This will give you better access to the back of your head.

Step 3: Use the Handheld Mirror

Hold the handheld mirror up to the back of your head, and use the mirror to see the back of your head in the reflection. This will help you to see what you are doing and make sure you are cutting your hair evenly.

Step 4: Trim the Ends

Take your scissors and trim the ends of your hair. Start by cutting a small amount first, and then work your way up. Be careful not to cut too much at once, or you may end up with uneven hair. Keep checking the back of your head in the handheld mirror to make sure you are cutting evenly.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

If you have layers in your hair, use the scissors to blend them together. Hold the scissors vertically, and make small snips in the layers to blend them. This will give your hair a more natural look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you cut your own hair at the back:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair at the back:

Q: Can I cut my hair by myself?

A: Yes, you can. With the right tools and technique, anyone can learn how to cut their own hair at the back.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, most people cut their hair every 4-6 weeks.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't worry. Hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed. If you make a mistake, you can always go to a salon to get it fixed.

Hair Care

Now that you know how to cut your own hair at the back, it's essential to take care of your hair. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy.

Cutting your own hair at the back can be challenging, but it's also rewarding. With these tips and techniques, you can save money and time by cutting your own hair at home. Remember to take care of your hair and have fun experimenting with different styles!
