How To Cut Your Own Curtain Bangs 2020 A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 02, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without committing to a full-on chop, curtain bangs may be the way to go. These face-framing layers have been on-trend for a while now, and for good reason—they're versatile, easy to style, and look great on just about everyone. Plus, with salons closed and social distancing still in place, learning how to cut your own curtain bangs at home has become a necessity for many. Here's how to do it:

Description

Curtain bangs are a type of fringe that are parted down the middle, framing the face on either side. They're typically longer on the sides and shorter in the middle, creating a soft, face-framing effect. The name "curtain bangs" comes from the way they part down the middle, much like curtains on a window.

How to Cut Your Own Curtain Bangs

To cut your own curtain bangs, you'll need a few supplies:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle with water

Once you've gathered your supplies, follow these steps:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off the hair you want to cut. Use a comb to create a straight part down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the back of your head. Then, clip or tie back the rest of your hair to keep it out of the way.

Step 2: Wet Your Bangs

Spray your bangs with water until they're damp but not soaking wet. This will help you get a more accurate cut.

Step 3: Determine Your Bang Length

Decide how short you want your bangs to be. Keep in mind that you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut. For a subtle look, start with longer bangs and work your way up to a shorter length.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold your scissors vertically and start cutting into your bangs, making small snips at a time. Work from the middle outwards, cutting a little bit at a time to ensure you don't take off too much. Keep checking in the mirror to make sure your bangs are even.

Step 5: Blend Your Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs to the desired length, use your scissors to create a soft, feathered effect along the edges. This will help blend your bangs into the rest of your hair for a more natural look.

Step 6: Style Your Bangs

Now that your bangs are cut, it's time to style them. Use a blow dryer and a round brush to create volume and shape, or let them air dry for a more natural look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Curtain Bangs

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own curtain bangs:

  • Start with longer bangs and work your way up to a shorter length.
  • Cut a little bit at a time to ensure you don't take off too much.
  • Hold your scissors vertically for a more natural, feathered effect.
  • Use a mirror to check that your bangs are even.
  • Take breaks if you need to—cutting your own hair can be tiring!

FAQ

Can I cut my own curtain bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can! Keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may want to cut your bangs a little longer than you think you need them.

What if I mess up?

Don't worry—hair grows back! If you're not happy with the way your bangs turned out, you can always pin them back, wear a hat, or book a virtual consultation with a stylist for some professional advice.

Hair Care Tips for Curtain Bangs

Now that you've cut your own curtain bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your bangs regularly to prevent them from getting oily.
  • Blow dry or style your bangs every day to keep them looking fresh.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can transfer oils from your hands onto your hair.
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your bangs between washes.

Cutting your own curtain bangs can be a fun and easy way to switch up your look. With a little patience and some basic supplies, you can achieve a salon-worthy style from the comfort of your own home.
