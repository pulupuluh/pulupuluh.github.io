How To Make Thick Natural Hair Curly A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 16, 2023 · 9 min read

If you have thick natural hair and are looking to add some curl to it, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we’ll be discussing how to make thick natural hair curly.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Make Thick Natural Hair Curly
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Making Thick Natural Hair Curly
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Description

Thick natural hair can be beautiful, but it can also be hard to manage. If you’re looking to add some curl to your hair, there are a few different methods you can try. Some people opt for chemical treatments like perms or relaxers, while others prefer to use heat styling tools like curling irons or hot rollers. However, these methods can be damaging to your hair over time. If you’re looking for a more natural way to make your hair curly, there are a few things you can try.

How to Make Thick Natural Hair Curly

There are several ways to make thick natural hair curly without using chemicals or heat. Here are a few methods to try:

1. Twist Out Method

The twist out method involves twisting small sections of hair and leaving them in overnight. In the morning, you’ll have natural-looking curls. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair.
  3. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly from the roots to the ends.
  4. Repeat this process until all of your hair is twisted.
  5. Cover your head with a silk scarf or bonnet and go to bed.
  6. In the morning, unravel the twists and fluff your hair to create volume.

2. Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a great way to add curls to your hair without using heat. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair.
  3. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly from the roots to the ends.
  4. Wrap the twisted hair around itself to create a small knot.
  5. Secure the knot with a bobby pin or hair clip.
  6. Repeat this process until all of your hair is in knots.
  7. Cover your head with a silk scarf or bonnet and go to bed.
  8. In the morning, unravel the knots and fluff your hair to create volume.

3. Flexi Rods

Flexi rods are a great way to get soft, bouncy curls without using heat. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair.
  3. Take a small section of hair and wrap it around a flexi rod.
  4. Bend the ends of the flexi rod to secure it in place.
  5. Repeat this process until all of your hair is in flexi rods.
  6. Cover your head with a silk scarf or bonnet and go to bed.
  7. In the morning, remove the flexi rods and fluff your hair to create volume.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to making thick natural hair curly:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair.
  3. Choose your preferred method for making your hair curly (twist out, Bantu knots, or flexi rods).
  4. Follow the steps for your chosen method.
  5. Cover your head with a silk scarf or bonnet and go to bed.
  6. In the morning, remove the scarf or bonnet and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Making Thick Natural Hair Curly

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results when making your thick natural hair curly:

  • Use a good quality leave-in conditioner or styling cream to help define your curls.
  • Cover your head with a silk scarf or bonnet to prevent frizz and maintain moisture.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can be damaging to your hair.
  • Be patient – it may take a few tries to get the results you want.
  • Experiment with different methods to find the one that works best for your hair type and texture.

FAQs

Q: Can I use heat to make my thick natural hair curly?

A: While it is possible to use heat to make your hair curly, it can be damaging to your hair over time. It’s best to use natural methods like the twist out, Bantu knots, or flexi rods.

Q: How long will my curls last?

A: This will depend on your hair type and texture, as well as the method you use to make your curls. Generally, curls made using natural methods will last longer than those made using heat styling tools.

Q: Can I make my hair curly without using any products?

A: While it is possible to make your hair curly without using any products, using a leave-in conditioner or styling cream can help define your curls and make them last longer.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Maintaining healthy, curly hair requires a little extra care. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Detangle your hair gently using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and maintain moisture.

By following these tips and using the natural methods outlined in this article, you can achieve beautiful, curly hair without damaging your locks.
