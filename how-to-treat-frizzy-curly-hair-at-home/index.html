How To Treat Frizzy Curly Hair At Home Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Aug 20, 2023 Â· 12 min read

Are you dealing with frizzy curly hair that seems impossible to manage? Don't worry, you're not alone. Frizz is a common issue among people with curly hair, and it can be frustrating to deal with. However, there are several things you can do at home to tame your frizzy curls and keep them looking their best. In this article, we'll discuss some tips and tricks for treating frizzy curly hair at home.

How to: Pre-Shampoo Treatment

Before you even step into the shower, you can start treating your frizzy curly hair with a pre-shampoo treatment. This involves applying a nourishing oil, such as coconut oil or olive oil, to your hair and letting it sit for at least 30 minutes before washing your hair. This will help to moisturize your curls and reduce frizz.

Step-by-Step: Pre-Shampoo Treatment

1. Choose a nourishing oil, such as coconut oil or olive oil.
2. Apply the oil to your hair, focusing on the ends and any particularly dry or frizzy areas.
3. Massage the oil into your hair and scalp.
4. Cover your hair with a shower cap or towel and let the oil sit for at least 30 minutes.
5. Wash your hair as usual with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

How to: Shampoo and Conditioner

Choosing the right shampoo and conditioner is essential for managing frizzy curly hair. Look for products that are designed specifically for curly hair and that contain moisturizing ingredients, such as coconut oil, shea butter, or argan oil. Avoid shampoos and conditioners that contain sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and make frizz worse.

Step-by-Step: Shampoo and Conditioner

1. Wet your hair thoroughly.
2. Apply a small amount of shampoo to your scalp and massage gently.
3. Rinse the shampoo out of your hair.
4. Apply conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.
5. Let the conditioner sit for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

How to: Detangling

Detangling your frizzy curly hair can be a challenge, but it's important to do it gently to avoid causing damage or breakage. Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet and coated with conditioner. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up, being careful not to pull or tug too hard.

Step-by-Step: Detangling

1. Apply conditioner to your wet hair.
2. Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair, starting at the ends.
3. Work your way up towards the roots, being careful not to pull or tug too hard.
4. Rinse the conditioner out of your hair.

How to: Drying

Drying your frizzy curly hair can be a challenge, as it's important to avoid using too much heat or roughing up your hair with a towel. Instead, try using a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair. You can also let your hair air-dry, but be sure to avoid touching or messing with your curls while they dry.

Step-by-Step: Drying

1. Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair.
2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to your hair.
3. Let your hair air-dry, or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.
4. Be sure to avoid touching or messing with your curls while they dry.

How to: Styling

Styling your frizzy curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, defined curls. Apply a styling product, such as a curl cream or gel, to your wet hair, then use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting. You can also try plopping your hair, which involves wrapping your wet hair in a t-shirt or microfiber towel to encourage your curls to form.

Step-by-Step: Styling

1. Apply a styling product, such as a curl cream or gel, to your wet hair.
2. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.
3. Alternatively, try plopping your hair by wrapping it in a t-shirt or microfiber towel.
4. Be sure to avoid touching or messing with your curls while they dry.

Tips for Managing Frizzy Curls

Here are some additional tips for managing frizzy curly hair:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to avoid roughing up your curls while you sleep.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Try using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make frizz worse.
  • Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.

FAQ: Common Questions About Frizzy Curly Hair

Q: Why does curly hair get frizzy?
A: Curly hair is naturally prone to frizz because it tends to be drier than straight hair. This is because the natural oils produced by your scalp have a harder time traveling down the length of curly hair strands.

Q: How can I prevent frizz?
A: To prevent frizz, it's important to keep your hair moisturized by using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and applying a leave-in conditioner or styling product. You should also avoid using heat styling tools and roughing up your hair with a towel.

Q: Can I still straighten my curly hair?
A: While it's best to avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage and frizz, you can still straighten your curly hair if you use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting on your flat iron or curling iron.

Hair Care Tips for Frizzy Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for frizzy curly hair:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet and coated with conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make frizz worse.
  • Try using a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove buildup from styling products and hard water.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid touching or playing with your hair throughout the day, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

By following these tips and tricks for treating frizzy curly hair at home, you can keep your curls looking their best and avoid frustrating frizz. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair, and don't be afraid to seek out professional help if you need it. With a little patience and care, you can have beautiful, defined curls that you'll love showing off.
