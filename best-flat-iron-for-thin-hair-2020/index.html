Best Flat Iron For Thin Hair 2020 Your Ultimate Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 23, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have thin hair, you know how difficult it can be to find the right flat iron that won’t damage your hair or make it look flat and lifeless. That’s why we’ve put together this ultimate guide to help you find the best flat iron for your thin hair in 2020. From how to choose the right flat iron to tips on how to care for your hair, we’ve got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Right Flat Iron for Thin Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Flat Ironing Thin Hair
  • Tips for Flat Ironing Thin Hair

How to Choose the Right Flat Iron for Thin Hair

When it comes to choosing the right flat iron for thin hair, there are a few key things to consider:

Plate Material

The plate material of your flat iron can make a big difference in how it affects your hair. Ceramic and tourmaline plates are great for thin hair because they distribute heat evenly and help reduce frizz.

Plate Size

The size of your flat iron plates will depend on your hair type and length. For thin hair, it’s best to choose a flat iron with plates that are 1 inch or less in width. This will allow you to easily style your hair without causing damage or weighing it down.

Temperature Settings

The temperature settings on your flat iron are important because they can help prevent damage to your hair. For thin hair, it’s best to choose a flat iron with adjustable temperature settings so you can choose the right temperature for your hair type.

Brand and Price

Brand and price are also important factors to consider when choosing a flat iron for thin hair. Look for a reputable brand that has good reviews and a reasonable price point.

Step-by-Step Guide to Flat Ironing Thin Hair

Now that you’ve chosen the right flat iron for your thin hair, it’s time to learn how to use it properly. Follow these steps for a sleek and smooth look:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before using your flat iron, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Adjust Your Flat Iron Temperature

Adjust the temperature of your flat iron based on your hair type. For thin hair, use a lower temperature setting to prevent damage.

Step 4: Start Flat Ironing

Starting at the roots, clamp a small section of hair between the plates of your flat iron and glide it down to the ends. Repeat this process on each section of hair until you’ve flat ironed all of your hair.

Tips for Flat Ironing Thin Hair

Flat ironing thin hair can be tricky, but these tips can help make the process easier:

Tip 1: Use a Heat Protectant

Always use a heat protectant spray before flat ironing your hair to prevent damage.

Tip 2: Don’t Overdo It

Don’t flat iron your hair too often or at too high of a temperature. This can cause damage and make your hair look flat and lifeless.

Tip 3: Use the Right Products

Use products that are specifically designed for thin hair, such as volumizing mousse or dry shampoo.

Tip 4: Choose the Right Brush

Choose a brush that is gentle on your hair, such as a boar bristle brush.

FAQs

Q: Can I flat iron my thin hair every day?

A: It’s best to avoid flat ironing your hair every day, as this can cause damage. Try to limit flat ironing to once or twice a week.

Q: What temperature should I use on my flat iron for thin hair?

A: For thin hair, it’s best to use a temperature between 300-350 degrees Fahrenheit.

Q: Can I use a flat iron on wet hair?

A: No, it’s important to only use a flat iron on dry hair to prevent damage.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

Caring for your thin hair is important to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Tip 1: Use Gentle Products

Use gentle products that won’t weigh down your hair or cause damage.

Tip 2: Avoid Over-Washing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, causing it to look flat and lifeless. Try to wash your hair every other day or every few days.

Tip 3: Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Tip 4: Use a Volumizing Shampoo

Using a volumizing shampoo can help give your thin hair some extra body and volume.

With these tips and the right flat iron, you can achieve sleek and smooth hair without causing damage or making it look flat and lifeless. Happy styling!
