How To Cut Your Own Hair Layered Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Mar 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon and spending a fortune on a haircut? Do you want to try cutting your own hair at home? If you're looking for a new hairstyle, a layered bob might be just what you need. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own hair layered bob in a relaxed English language.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Layered Bob
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Description

A layered bob is a versatile haircut that can be styled in many different ways. It's a great option for those who want to add volume and movement to their hair. This haircut features shorter layers at the back and longer layers at the front, which gives it a tapered, angled look. Cutting your own hair layered bob can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done successfully.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Layered Bob

The first step in cutting your own hair layered bob is to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a mirror and a spray bottle filled with water on hand.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important to cut your hair when it's dry, so you can see the length and texture better.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips to separate the top, sides, and back of your hair.
  3. Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair from the nape of your neck and hold it straight up. Use the comb to create a 90-degree angle and cut the hair at the desired length. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head.
  4. Move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair from one side and hold it between your fingers. Use the comb to create a 45-degree angle and cut the hair at the desired length. Repeat on the other side.
  5. Finally, work on the top section. Take a small section of hair from the crown of your head and hold it straight up. Use the comb to create a 90-degree angle and cut the hair at the desired length. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way toward the front of your head.
  6. Once you've finished cutting your hair, use the comb to blend the layers together. You can also use the scissors to trim any uneven sections.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a comb to create the desired angle before cutting.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring.
  • Don't rush the process. It's better to take your time and get it right.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair layered bob if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair layered bob if you have curly hair. However, it's important to be careful and take your time. Curly hair can be unpredictable, so it's a good idea to start with small sections and work your way up.

What scissors should I use to cut my own hair?

You should use a pair of sharp scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Don't use regular scissors or kitchen scissors, as they can damage your hair.

How often should I cut my hair layered bob?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. Generally, you should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and shape of your layered bob.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layered bob.
  • Avoid using too many hair products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Cutting your own hair layered bob can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut without leaving your home. Remember to take your time, start with small sections of hair, and use the tips and tricks we've provided in this article. Happy cutting!
