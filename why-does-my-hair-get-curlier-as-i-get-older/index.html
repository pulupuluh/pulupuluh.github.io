Why Does My Hair Get Curlier As I Get Older

Written by Daniel Aug 23, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As we age, we may notice changes in our hair texture, with many people experiencing an increase in curliness or waviness. This can be a confusing development for those who have spent years trying to tame their unruly locks, but it's actually a natural part of the aging process. In this article, we'll explore why hair can get curlier as we get older, as well as provide tips for caring for and styling your newfound curls.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

One of the main reasons why hair can get curlier as we age is due to changes in our hormones. As we enter perimenopause and menopause, our bodies produce less estrogen, which can cause a decrease in sebum production. Sebum is the natural oil that our scalps produce, and it helps to keep our hair moisturized and smooth. When sebum production decreases, our hair can become drier and more prone to frizz, which can lead to increased curliness.

In addition to hormonal changes, our hair can also be affected by environmental factors, such as exposure to sun, wind, and humidity. Over time, these external factors can cause damage to our hair, which can lead to changes in texture and curl pattern. This is why it's important to protect your hair from the elements by wearing a hat or using a protective styling product.

How To

If you're new to the world of curly hair, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. Here are some tips to help you embrace your newfound curls:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can be harsh on curly hair, so it's important to use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for a shampoo that is specifically formulated for curly hair, and avoid products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair even further.

2. Condition, condition, condition

Curly hair needs lots of moisture to stay healthy and defined, so it's important to use a deep conditioner at least once a week. Look for a conditioner that is rich and creamy, and leave it on your hair for at least 5-10 minutes to allow it to penetrate deeply.

3. Ditch the brush

Curly hair is best styled with your fingers or a wide-tooth comb, as brushes can disrupt the natural curl pattern and cause frizz. After washing your hair, use your fingers to gently separate your curls, and apply a styling product to help define and hold your curls in place.

Step By Step

Here's a simple step-by-step routine for caring for curly hair:

1. Shampoo with a sulfate-free shampoo

Wet your hair thoroughly, and apply a small amount of shampoo to your scalp. Massage gently with your fingertips, and rinse thoroughly with warm water.

2. Condition with a deep conditioner

Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends. Leave the conditioner on for at least 5-10 minutes, and rinse thoroughly with cool water.

3. Apply a curl-defining product

While your hair is still damp, apply a curl-defining product to help enhance your natural curl pattern. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair upwards towards your scalp, and allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser on low heat.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage.
  • Wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf at night to help preserve your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage, which can cause your curls to become limp and lifeless.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find the ones that work best for your hair type and curl pattern.

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause my hair to become curlier?

A: While stress can certainly affect your body in a variety of ways, there isn't enough evidence to suggest that it can cause your hair to become curlier. However, stress can cause hair loss and damage, so it's important to find healthy ways to manage stress in your life.

Q: Is it normal for my hair to become curlier as I age?

A: Yes, it's completely normal for your hair to change texture and become curlier as you age. This is due to a variety of factors, including hormonal changes and environmental damage.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can certainly straighten your curly hair if you prefer a sleeker look. However, it's important to use heat protectant products and avoid using hot tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair over time.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve healthy, defined curls that you'll love. Remember to be patient with your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for you.
