Can You Have Dreads With Straight Hair

Written by Robby Jun 04, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have straight hair and have been considering getting dreadlocks, you may be wondering if it’s even possible. The good news is that yes, you can have dreads with straight hair! Here, we’ll cover everything you need to know about getting dreads with straight hair, including how to do it, what to expect, and how to care for your new hairstyle.

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are often associated with the Rastafarian movement and have been worn by many famous musicians, including Bob Marley, Lauryn Hill, and Lenny Kravitz. Traditionally, dreadlocks are created by allowing hair to grow and mat together over time. However, there are also methods for creating dreads artificially, which can be done with straight hair.

How to

The process of creating dreads with straight hair is called “dread perming.” This involves using a chemical process to create the texture needed to form dreadlocks. It’s important to note that dread perming is not a one-time process. You will need to maintain your dreads regularly to keep them looking good.

Step by Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide to creating dreads with straight hair:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly and let it dry completely.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections using clips or hair bands.
  3. Apply dread perm solution to each section of hair, starting at the root and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Wrap each section of hair around a small perm rod or pipe cleaner and secure it in place.
  5. Apply heat to the wrapped hair using a hair dryer or hooded dryer. This will activate the dread perm solution and create the necessary texture for dreadlocks.
  6. Allow the hair to cool completely before removing the perm rods or pipe cleaners.
  7. Gently separate the hair into individual dreadlocks using a comb or your fingers.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting dreads with straight hair:

  • Make sure to go to a professional stylist who has experience with dread perming. This is not a DIY process!
  • Be prepared for some discomfort during the process, as the dread perm solution can be harsh on the scalp.
  • Understand that dreadlocks require maintenance. You will need to regularly wash, separate, and retwist your dreads to keep them looking their best.
  • Consider using natural hair products that are gentle on your hair and scalp.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting dreads with straight hair:

Can I get dreads with any length of hair?

Yes, you can get dreads with any length of hair, but it may be easier to create them with longer hair.

How long will it take for my dreads to form?

It can take several months for your dreads to fully form and mature. During this time, you will need to be patient and take good care of your hair.

Can I wash my dreads?

Yes, you can and should wash your dreads regularly to keep them clean and healthy. However, be sure to use a gentle, residue-free shampoo and avoid over-washing, as this can cause your dreads to unravel.

Hair Care

Caring for your dreads is essential to keeping them looking great. Here are some tips for maintaining your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your dreads regularly, but be gentle when doing so. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid over-washing.
  • Separate your dreads regularly to prevent them from merging together and forming larger, harder-to-maintain dreads.
  • Retwist your dreads every few weeks to keep them looking neat and tidy.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils on your dreads, as these can cause buildup and make them harder to maintain.

With proper care and maintenance, dreads can be a beautiful and unique hairstyle for anyone, regardless of their hair type. If you’re considering getting dreads with straight hair, be sure to do your research and find a stylist who can help you achieve the look you want.
