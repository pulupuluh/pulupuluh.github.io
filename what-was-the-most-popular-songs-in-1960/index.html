1. "Theme from A Summer Place" - Percy Faith

The first song on our list is "Theme from A Summer Place" by Percy Faith. This instrumental piece was used as the theme song for the movie "A Summer Place" and became a huge hit in 1960. It spent nine weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and is still a classic today. The song is known for its memorable melody and beautiful orchestration.

2. "He'll Have to Go" - Jim Reeves

"He'll Have to Go" by Jim Reeves is another popular song from 1960. This country ballad is about a man trying to win back his love and was a huge success for Reeves. It reached number two on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and number one on the country chart. The song is known for its smooth vocals and romantic lyrics.

3. "Cathy's Clown" - The Everly Brothers

"Cathy's Clown" by The Everly Brothers was a big hit in 1960. It was written by the brothers themselves and features their signature harmonies. The song is about a man who has been betrayed by his love and is now feeling like a fool. It spent five weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and is considered one of the best songs of the decade.

4. "Running Bear" - Johnny Preston

"Running Bear" by Johnny Preston was another popular song in 1960. The song tells the tragic love story of Running Bear and Little White Dove, two Native American lovers from different tribes. The song has a catchy melody and features a unique sound effect that imitates the sound of a tom-tom drum. It spent three weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart.

5. "Teen Angel" - Mark Dinning

"Teen Angel" by Mark Dinning is a haunting ballad that was a big hit in 1960. The song is about a young couple who are in love, but tragedy strikes when the girl dies in a car accident. The song is known for its emotional lyrics and Dinning's powerful vocals. It spent two weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and is still a popular song today.

6. "Save the Last Dance for Me" - The Drifters

"Save the Last Dance for Me" by The Drifters is a classic song from 1960. The song is about a man who is worried that his love will be stolen away from him at a dance. The song has a catchy melody and features the signature harmonies of The Drifters. It spent three weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart.

7. "Stay" - Maurice Williams and the Zodiacs

"Stay" by Maurice Williams and the Zodiacs is a doo-wop classic from 1960. The song is about a man begging his love to stay with him instead of leaving. The song has a catchy melody and features Williams' falsetto vocals. It spent one week at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and is still a popular song today.

8. "Stuck on You" - Elvis Presley

"Stuck on You" by Elvis Presley was a big hit in 1960. The song is about a man who is in love and can't stop thinking about his love. The song has a catchy melody and features Presley's signature vocals. It spent four weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and is still a favorite of Presley fans today.

9. "It's Now or Never" - Elvis Presley

"It's Now or Never" is another popular song by Elvis Presley from 1960. The song is based on the Italian song "O Sole Mio" and features Presley's powerful vocals. The song is about a man who is pleading with his love to be with him. It spent five weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and is one of Presley's most popular songs.

10. "Are You Lonesome Tonight?" - Elvis Presley

"Are You Lonesome Tonight?" is yet another popular song by Elvis Presley from 1960. The song is a ballad about a man who is wondering if his love is thinking of him. The song has a beautiful melody and features Presley's emotive vocals. It spent six weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and is still a favorite of Presley fans today.
