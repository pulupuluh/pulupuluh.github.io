How Can I Revive My Wig A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Apr 16, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Wigs are a great way to switch up your style without committing to a drastic haircut or color change. However, over time, wigs can become dull, tangled, and lifeless. In this guide, we'll teach you how to revive your wig and bring it back to life. From daily maintenance to deep conditioning treatments, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Daily Maintenance
  • Deep Conditioning
  • Detangling
  • Cutting and Styling
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Final Thoughts

Daily Maintenance

The key to keeping your wig looking fresh is to maintain it on a daily basis. Here are some tips to help you do that:

  • Use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your wig
  • Avoid using regular hair products on your wig, as they can cause buildup
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help maintain its shape
  • Avoid exposing your wig to heat, as it can damage the fibers

By following these simple steps, you can keep your wig looking its best for longer.

Deep Conditioning

Even with daily maintenance, wigs can still become dry and brittle over time. That's why it's important to give your wig a deep conditioning treatment every once in a while. Here's how:

  1. Fill a sink or basin with cool water
  2. Add a small amount of wig conditioner to the water
  3. Gently submerge your wig in the water and let it soak for 5-10 minutes
  4. Rinse your wig with cool water until all the conditioner has been removed
  5. Blot your wig dry with a towel and let it air dry on a wig stand or mannequin head

This deep conditioning treatment will help restore moisture to your wig and leave it looking shiny and healthy.

Detangling

Tangled wigs can be a nightmare to deal with, but there are ways to make the process easier. Here's how to detangle your wig:

  1. Start by gently combing out any knots with a wide-tooth comb
  2. Apply a wig detangler or leave-in conditioner to the ends of your wig
  3. Use your fingers to gently work through any remaining tangles
  4. Once your wig is tangle-free, use a wig brush to smooth out the hair

Remember to always be gentle with your wig to avoid causing any damage.

Cutting and Styling

If your wig is looking a little dull or outdated, a new cut or style can make all the difference. Here's how to cut and style your wig:

  1. Start by washing and detangling your wig
  2. Decide on the new style you want to achieve
  3. Use sharp scissors to carefully cut the hair to the desired length
  4. Use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to style the hair
  5. Finish with a light hairspray or wig spray to keep everything in place

Remember to take your time when cutting and styling your wig to avoid any mistakes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I wash my wig?

You should wash your wig every 6-8 wears, or as needed if it becomes dirty or sweaty.

Can I use regular hair products on my wig?

No, you should avoid using regular hair products on your wig as they can cause buildup and damage the fibers.

Can I use heat tools on my wig?

It's best to avoid using heat tools on your wig, as they can damage the fibers. If you must use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Final Thoughts

Reviving your wig may seem like a daunting task, but with a little bit of effort and the right tools, you can bring your wig back to life. Remember to maintain your wig on a daily basis, give it a deep conditioning treatment every once in a while, and be gentle when detangling and styling. With these tips, your wig will look as good as new in no time.
