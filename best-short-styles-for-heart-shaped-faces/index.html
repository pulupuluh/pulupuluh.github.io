Best Short Styles For Heart Shaped Faces

Written by Eveline Apr 19, 2023 Â· 11 min read

In this article, we will discuss the best short styles for heart shaped faces. If you have a heart-shaped face, you have a wide forehead and a narrow chin. The goal is to find a hairstyle that balances your features and makes your face look more proportional. We will provide a description, how-to, step-by-step, tips, FAQ, and hair care tips for each style.

Table of Contents

  • Pixie Cut
  • Bob Cut
  • Lob Cut
  • Layered Cut
  • Fringe Cut

Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic short hairstyle that works well for heart shaped faces. It is a low-maintenance style that is easy to style and maintain. The short layers add volume and texture to the hair, which helps to balance out the forehead and narrow chin. To achieve this style, follow these steps:

How to:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray.
  3. Use a flat iron to straighten your hair.
  4. Apply a styling wax or pomade to add texture and volume to your hair.
  5. Use your fingers to style your hair into a messy, textured look.

Tips:

  • Choose a pixie cut that is longer in the front and shorter in the back to add volume to the top of your head.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add more body to your hair.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

FAQ:

  • Q: Will a pixie cut make my face look too round?
  • A: No, as long as you choose a style that is longer in the front and shorter in the back, it will add volume to the top of your head and balance out your face.

Hair Care:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Bob Cut

The bob cut is another classic short hairstyle that works well for heart shaped faces. It is a versatile style that can be worn straight or curly. The length of the hair should be chin-length or slightly longer, which helps to balance out the forehead and narrow chin. To achieve this style, follow these steps:

How to:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add volume.
  3. Use a flat iron to straighten your hair or curling iron to add curls.
  4. Apply a styling product to add texture and hold.
  5. Use your fingers to style your hair into a messy, textured look.
