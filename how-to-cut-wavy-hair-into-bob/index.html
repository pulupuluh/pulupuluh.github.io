How To Cut Wavy Hair Into Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 23, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your wavy hair always getting in your face? A bob haircut might be the solution for you! Not only is it a trendy and chic hairstyle, but it is also low maintenance and easy to style. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how to cut wavy hair into a bob. From the tools you need to the final touches, we’ve got you covered!

Tools You Will Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the necessary tools. Here are the items you will need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section off your hair
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section in place so that they don’t get in the way while you’re cutting.

Step 3: Choose Your Bob Length

Decide on the length of your bob. Keep in mind that wavy hair tends to bounce up when it dries, so it will look shorter than it actually is when it’s wet.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the back sections, cut your hair in a straight line. If you want your hair to be shorter in the back, cut it at a slight angle. After you cut each section, check the length and trim any stray hairs.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

Move on to the front sections of your hair. Hold the hair out straight and cut it in a straight line. Make sure the length of the sides matches the length of the back.

Step 6: Add Layers (Optional)

If you want to add some texture to your bob, you can add layers. Take a small section of hair and cut it at an angle. Continue cutting small sections until you achieve your desired look.

Step 7: Trim Your Bangs (Optional)

If you have bangs, trim them to the desired length. Cut them straight across or at an angle for a more edgy look.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Now that you’ve cut your hair into a bob, style it to your liking. Use a curling iron or flat iron to add waves or straighten your hair. Use hair products like mousse or hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s cut.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you if you’re not comfortable cutting your own hair.

FAQ

Can I cut my wavy hair into a bob if it’s curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair into a bob. Keep in mind that curly hair will look shorter than it actually is when it’s dry, so cut it a little longer than your desired length.

Do I need to straighten my hair before cutting it?

No, you don’t need to straighten your hair before cutting it. In fact, it’s better to cut it when it’s in its natural wavy state so that you can see the true length of your hair.

How often should I trim my bob?

You should trim your bob every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care

To keep your wavy bob looking its best, use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for wavy hair. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Now that you know how to cut wavy hair into a bob, it’s time to try it out for yourself! Remember to take your time and use caution when cutting your hair. With the right tools and technique, you’ll have a stylish and low-maintenance hairstyle that you’ll love.
