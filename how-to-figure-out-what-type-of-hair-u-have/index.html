How To Figure Out What Type Of Hair You Have

Understanding your hair type is essential to properly caring for your hair. Not all hair is created equal, and different hair types require different care. In this article, we'll go over how to figure out what type of hair you have and provide tips for caring for your hair type.

Table of Contents

How to Figure Out Hair Type

The first step in figuring out your hair type is to observe your hair. Look at your hair's texture, thickness, and curl pattern. You can also take a quiz or consult with a hairstylist to determine your hair type.

Texture

Texture refers to the thickness or diameter of an individual hair strand. To determine your hair's texture, take a single strand of hair and rub it between your fingers. If you can't feel the hair, it's fine. If the hair feels slightly rough, it's medium. If the hair feels thick and coarse, it's coarse.

Thickness

Thickness refers to the density of your hair, or how many strands of hair you have on your head. To determine your hair's thickness, examine your hair in a well-lit area. If you can see your scalp, you have thin hair. If you can't see your scalp, you have thick hair.

Curl Pattern

Curl pattern refers to the shape of your hair strands. To determine your hair's curl pattern, look at your hair when it's wet and has no product in it. If your hair is straight, you have type 1 hair. If your hair has a slight wave, you have type 2 hair. If your hair has loose curls, you have type 3 hair. If your hair has tight curls or coils, you have type 4 hair.

Hair Type Categories

Now that you know how to determine your hair type, let's go over the four main hair type categories:

Type 1: Straight

Type 1 hair is straight and lacks any noticeable curl pattern. This hair type is typically fine and can get oily easily.

Type 2: Wavy

Type 2 hair has a slight wave pattern and is thicker than type 1 hair. This hair type can be prone to frizz.

Type 3: Curly

Type 3 hair has loose curls and is typically described as having a "S" shape. This hair type can be prone to dryness and frizz.

Type 4: Coily

Type 4 hair has tight curls or coils and is typically described as having a "Z" shape. This hair type is prone to dryness and breakage.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you know your hair type, it's important to care for your hair accordingly. Here are some tips for caring for each hair type:

Type 1 Hair Care Tips

  • Use a lightweight shampoo and conditioner to avoid weighing down hair
  • Avoid heavy styling products that can make hair look greasy
  • Avoid over-washing hair to prevent stripping natural oils

Type 2 Hair Care Tips

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to combat frizz
  • Avoid brushing hair when dry to prevent frizz
  • Use a curl-enhancing cream or mousse to define waves

Type 3 Hair Care Tips

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to combat dryness
  • Apply leave-in conditioner or oil to define curls
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying hair to enhance curls

Type 4 Hair Care Tips

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to combat dryness
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to define coils
  • Avoid harsh styling products that can cause breakage

FAQ

What if my hair has multiple textures?

It's common for hair to have multiple textures, especially if you have a lot of hair. In this case, it's important to care for each section of hair based on its individual texture.

Can my hair type change over time?

Yes, your hair type can change due to hormonal changes, medication, or damage. It's important to reassess your hair type periodically to ensure you're caring for it properly.

Can I use the same products on my hair as someone with the same hair type?

Not necessarily. Everyone's hair is unique, and what works for one person may not work for another. It's important to experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair.

How often should I wash my hair?

This varies based on your hair type and personal preferences. As a general rule, type 1 hair can go longer between washes than type 3 or 4 hair. It's important to listen to your hair and wash it when it feels dirty or oily.

By following these tips and understanding your hair type, you'll be well on your way to having healthy, beautiful hair.
