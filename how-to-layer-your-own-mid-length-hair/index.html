How To Layer Your Own Mid Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 10, 2023 · 8 min read

If you want to add more depth and dimension to your mid-length hair, layering is the way to go. Layers can give your hair more volume, movement, and texture. The best part? You can layer your own hair at home with just a few tools and techniques. In this guide, we’ll show you how to layer your own mid-length hair in a relaxed and easy-to-follow manner.

How To Layer Your Own Mid Length Hair

Before we get started, it’s important to note that layering your own hair can be challenging, especially if you’ve never done it before. But with a little patience and practice, you can achieve salon-worthy results at home. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror (preferably a large one)

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

The first step in layering your own hair is to wash and dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can appear longer than it is, and you don’t want to accidentally cut off too much.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section your hair into two parts. Create a horizontal parting at the back of your head, just below your crown. Clip the top section of your hair up and out of the way, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 3: Create the First Layer

Starting with the bottom section of your hair, take a small section (about 1 inch wide) and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it straight across with your scissors. This will be the first layer.

Step 4: Continue Cutting

Now, take another small section of hair just above the first layer. Comb it straight down and place it between your fingers. Cut this section slightly shorter than the first layer. This will create a gradual layering effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair, each slightly shorter than the last, until you reach the end of the bottom section.

Step 5: Layer the Top Section

Once you’ve finished the bottom section, unclip the top section of your hair and repeat the same process. Start with a small section of hair at the back and work your way forward, cutting each section slightly shorter than the last.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

After you’ve finished layering your hair, check for evenness. Comb your hair straight down and look for any uneven or choppy areas. If you notice any, go back and trim them until your layers are even.

Tips for Layering Your Own Hair

Layering your own hair can be tricky, but these tips can help you achieve the best results:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven layers.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to start with small sections and work your way up.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Layering your own hair can be tiring, so take breaks to rest your arms and neck.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help. If you’re struggling, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQ: Layering Your Own Mid Length Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering your own mid-length hair:

1. How often should I layer my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to change your hairstyle. Generally, it’s recommended to get a haircut every 6 to 8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

2. Can I layer my hair if it’s curly?

Yes, you can layer curly hair. However, it’s best to go to a professional stylist who specializes in curly hair to avoid any mishaps.

3. Can I layer my hair if it’s thin?

Yes, layering can actually make thin hair appear thicker and more voluminous. Just be careful not to cut too much or you may end up with even thinner hair.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Now that you’ve layered your own mid-length hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are some hair care tips for layered hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make your layers look frizzy.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layers and prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair oil or serum to add shine and tame any flyaways.
  • Consider getting a deep conditioning treatment once a month to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Layering your own mid-length hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and trendy look without ever leaving your home. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and don’t be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
