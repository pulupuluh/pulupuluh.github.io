How To Make Your Hair Curl Up At The Ends For Guys

Written by Daniel May 07, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're looking for a new hairstyle to try, why not experiment with curls? Curls add texture and volume to your hair, giving it a more interesting and dynamic look. In this article, we'll show you how to make your hair curl up at the ends for guys.

Description

Curly hair has been in style for decades, and it's not going away anytime soon. From the tightest ringlets to loose waves, there's a curl pattern for everyone. But if you're not naturally blessed with curls, don't worry - there are ways to create them. In this article, we'll focus on how to make your hair curl up at the ends, a style that's both classic and modern.

How to

Before we dive into the steps, let's talk about the tools you'll need. To create curls, you'll need a curling iron or wand, heat protectant spray, and a comb or brush. Here's how to make your hair curl up at the ends:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start with clean, dry hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type. Towel dry your hair and comb through any tangles.

Step 2: Apply heat protectant spray

Before you start using heat tools, it's important to protect your hair from damage. Spray a heat protectant product throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Heat up your curling iron or wand

Follow the instructions on your curling iron or wand to heat it up. For most tools, you'll want to set the temperature to around 350-400 degrees Fahrenheit.

Step 4: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

Step 5: Curl your hair

Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron or wand. Hold it for 10-15 seconds, then release. Repeat this process for each section of hair, focusing on the ends to create the curl.

Step 6: Comb through your curls

Once you've curled all of your hair, use a comb or brush to gently loosen the curls. This will give them a more natural, relaxed look.

Step 7: Apply finishing product

To set your curls in place and add shine, apply a finishing product such as hairspray or serum. Be careful not to use too much, as this can weigh down your curls.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect curl:

  • Experiment with different curling iron or wand sizes to find the one that works best for you.
  • Don't curl your hair too tightly - this can make your curls look unnatural.
  • Use a high-quality heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.
  • If you have thick or long hair, consider curling in smaller sections for more defined curls.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making your hair curl up at the ends:

Can I curl my hair without heat?

Yes! There are a variety of ways to create curls without using heat tools. You can try braiding your hair overnight, using flexi rods, or using a curling cream.

How long will my curls last?

This depends on your hair type and the products you use. Generally, curls will last for a few hours to a few days. To extend the life of your curls, try using a dry shampoo or refreshing spray.

Can I use this method on short hair?

Absolutely! This method works on hair of all lengths. Just adjust the size of your curling iron or wand to fit your hair length.

Hair Care

To keep your curls looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to make your hair curl up at the ends in no time. Have fun experimenting with different curl patterns and styles!
