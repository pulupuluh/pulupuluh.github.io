How To Cut My Little Boy s Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting your little boy's hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're new to it. But with the right tools and techniques, you can easily give him a stylish haircut at home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your little boy's hair with scissors, step by step. We'll also give you some tips on how to make the process easier and answer some frequently asked questions. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Cutting Your Little Boy's Hair
  • Tips for Cutting Your Little Boy's Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your little boy's hair, you'll need to gather some tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors (preferably hair cutting scissors)
  • Comb
  • Hair clippers (optional)
  • Cape or towel
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Hair ties (optional)

Preparation

Now that you have your tools ready, it's time to prepare your little boy for his haircut. Here's what you need to do:

  1. Choose a comfortable and well-lit place to cut your little boy's hair.
  2. Cover him with a cape or towel to avoid getting hair on his clothes.
  3. Spray his hair with water to make it easier to cut.
  4. Decide on the style of haircut you want to give him and find a picture for reference.

Cutting Your Little Boy's Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your little boy's hair. Here's how:

  1. Start by trimming the hair around his ears and neckline. Use scissors or clippers to create a straight line.
  2. Divide his hair into sections with hair ties if needed. Start at the back and work your way towards the front.
  3. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it at the desired length. Repeat this for the rest of the sections.
  4. Blend the sections together by combing the hair upwards and cutting it at an angle. This creates a natural-looking transition between different lengths of hair.
  5. Trim the hair on top of his head at an angle, leaving it slightly longer towards the front. Use your fingers to guide the scissors and create texture.
  6. Trim the bangs at an angle, starting from the middle and working your way towards the sides. Cut them longer than you think you need, as wet hair tends to shrink when it dries.
  7. Check the haircut from different angles and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Your Little Boy's Hair

Here are some tips to make cutting your little boy's hair easier:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid pulling or damaging the hair.
  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Don't rush and take breaks if your little boy gets restless.
  • Use a mirror to check the haircut from different angles.
  • Ask for help if you're not confident in your skills.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your little boy's hair:

How often should I cut my little boy's hair?

It depends on the length and style of his hair, but most boys need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't worry, hair grows back! If you make a mistake, try to fix it by blending the hair or cutting it shorter. If it's a major mistake, you can always visit a professional hairstylist.

Should I cut my little boy's hair when it's wet or dry?

It's usually easier to cut hair when it's wet, as it's more manageable and easier to comb. However, some haircuts require cutting dry hair, so refer to your reference picture for guidance.

Hair Care

After cutting your little boy's hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Wash his hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid tangling or pulling the hair.
  • Trim his hair regularly to maintain the style.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or harsh chemicals on his hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily cut your little boy's hair with scissors and give him a stylish haircut at home. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
