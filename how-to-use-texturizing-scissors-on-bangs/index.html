How To Use Texturizing Scissors On Bangs A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 17, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of your boring, straight-cut bangs? Do you want to add some texture and depth to your hair? Texturizing scissors might be just what you need! In this guide, we'll show you how to use texturizing scissors on your bangs to achieve the perfect look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Texturizing Scissors on Bangs
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Texturizing Scissors on Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Texturizing

How to Use Texturizing Scissors on Bangs

Texturizing scissors are designed to create a more natural, tousled look by thinning out your hair. When used correctly, they can add volume and depth to your bangs.

Here's what you need to do:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and blow-drying your hair. It's important to work with clean, dry hair so you can accurately see how much you need to thin out your bangs.
  2. Comb your bangs straight down and divide them into small sections. Make sure the sections are even in size.
  3. Hold the texturizing scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut into each section. Don't cut straight across – instead, make small snips to thin out the hair.
  4. Repeat this process for each section of your bangs, making sure to keep the length and thickness consistent throughout.
  5. Once you're done, use a comb to blend the cut pieces into the rest of your hair. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and make small adjustments as needed.

Tips for Using Texturizing Scissors on Bangs

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind as you use texturizing scissors on your bangs:

  • Always start with small snips – you can always cut more later, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp and in good condition. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve the look you want.
  • Work slowly and carefully. It's better to take your time and get it right than rush and end up with a bad haircut.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go. This can help you spot any uneven spots or areas that need more thinning.

FAQs

Here are some common questions people have about using texturizing scissors on their bangs:

Can I use regular scissors instead of texturizing scissors?

You can, but texturizing scissors are specifically designed to thin out hair and create a more natural, tousled look. Using regular scissors may not achieve the same effect.

Can I use texturizing scissors on curly hair?

Yes, but you'll need to be extra careful to avoid cutting too much hair or creating uneven spots.

How often should I use texturizing scissors on my bangs?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to thin out your bangs. Some people prefer to do it every few weeks, while others only do it a few times a year.

Hair Care After Texturizing

After you've used texturizing scissors on your bangs, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and undo the effects of texturizing scissors.
  • Consider using a hair serum or oil to add shine and moisture to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to use texturizing scissors on your bangs, you can experiment with different styles and looks. Just remember to take your time, work carefully, and take good care of your hair afterwards.
