What Causes Hair To Thin On The Sides

Written by William Aug 25, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're noticing that your hair is thinning on the sides, you're not alone. This is a common issue that many people face, and it can be caused by a variety of factors. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why hair thins on the sides and provide tips on how to prevent it from happening.

Genetics

One of the most common causes of hair thinning on the sides is genetics. If your parents or grandparents had thinning hair, you're more likely to experience it as well. This is because genetics can determine the thickness of your hair follicles, and if they're naturally thin, your hair will be too.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also cause hair to thin on the sides. This is especially true for women who are going through menopause, as the decrease in estrogen can lead to hair loss. Men who have high levels of DHT (dihydrotestosterone) can also experience thinning hair on the sides and crown.

Nutritional Deficiencies

Your diet can also play a role in hair thinning on the sides. If you're not getting enough nutrients, such as iron, biotin, and vitamin D, your hair may become weak and brittle, leading to thinning. Make sure you're eating a balanced diet with plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins to support healthy hair growth.

Hairstyle Choices

Wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids, weaves, and ponytails, can also cause hair to thin on the sides. This is because these hairstyles pull on the hair follicles, causing them to weaken and eventually break. To prevent this, try wearing looser hairstyles and avoid pulling your hair back too tightly.

Stress and Anxiety

Finally, stress and anxiety can also lead to hair thinning on the sides. When you're stressed, your body produces cortisol, which can disrupt the hair growth cycle and lead to hair loss. To prevent this, try to manage your stress levels through exercise, meditation, or therapy.

How to Prevent Hair Thinning on the Sides

While you can't always prevent hair thinning on the sides, there are some steps you can take to minimize the risk:

  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of nutrients
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair
  • Manage your stress levels
  • Talk to your doctor about hormone imbalances

FAQ

Here are some common questions about hair thinning on the sides:

Can hair thinning on the sides be reversed?

It depends on the cause of the thinning. If it's due to genetics or hormonal changes, it may be more difficult to reverse. However, if it's due to nutritional deficiencies or styling choices, it may be possible to improve the thickness of your hair.

Is hair thinning on the sides permanent?

Again, it depends on the cause of the thinning. If it's due to genetics or hormonal changes, it may be permanent. However, if it's due to nutritional deficiencies or styling choices, it may be reversible.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Hair

If you're experiencing hair thinning on the sides, here are some tips to help care for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Try a volumizing hair spray
  • Consider using a hair growth serum

By taking good care of your hair and addressing any underlying issues, you can help prevent hair thinning on the sides and promote healthy hair growth.
