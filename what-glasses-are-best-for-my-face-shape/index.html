What Glasses Are Best For My Face Shape A Complete Guide

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the best glasses for your face shape is determining what your face shape is. There are six common face shapes: oval, round, square, heart-shaped, diamond, and triangular.

To determine your face shape, look in the mirror and note the following:

  • The width of your forehead
  • The width of your cheekbones
  • The width of your jawline
  • The length of your face
  • The shape of your chin

Once you have noted these features, compare them to the descriptions below to determine your face shape.

Best Glasses for Oval Face Shapes

Oval faces have balanced proportions, with a slightly curved jawline that is narrower than the forehead. Oval faces can wear most frame styles, but rectangular frames or frames with a strong browline can add definition to the face.

Recommended frames for oval face shapes:

  • Rectangular frames
  • Wayfarer frames
  • Aviator frames
  • Browline frames

Best Glasses for Round Face Shapes

Round faces have softer features with a rounded chin and forehead. The width and length of the face are almost equal. To balance the softness of the face, angular frames with sharp edges can add definition.

Recommended frames for round face shapes:

  • Rectangular frames
  • Square frames
  • Geometric frames
  • Wayfarer frames

Best Glasses for Square Face Shapes

Square faces have a strong jawline and a forehead that is almost the same width. To soften the angles of the face, round or oval frames can be a good choice.

Recommended frames for square face shapes:

  • Round frames
  • Oval frames
  • Butterfly frames
  • Wayfarer frames

Best Glasses for Heart-Shaped Face Shapes

Heart-shaped faces have a wider forehead and cheekbones that narrow to a pointed chin. To balance the face, frames that are wider at the bottom than the top can be a good choice.

Recommended frames for heart-shaped face shapes:

  • Cat-eye frames
  • Round frames
  • Aviator frames
  • Wayfarer frames

Best Glasses for Diamond Face Shapes

Diamond faces have a narrow forehead and jawline with wide cheekbones. To soften the angles of the face, frames with curved edges can be a good choice.

Recommended frames for diamond face shapes:

  • Oval frames
  • Round frames
  • Butterfly frames
  • Wayfarer frames

Best Glasses for Triangular Face Shapes

Triangular faces have a wide jawline and narrow forehead. Frames that are wider at the top than the bottom can help balance the face.

Recommended frames for triangular face shapes:

  • Aviator frames
  • Cat-eye frames
  • Wayfarer frames
  • Browline frames

How to Choose the Right Frames

Now that you know your face shape and which frames are recommended for you, it's time to choose the right frames. Here are some factors to consider when selecting glasses:

  • Your personal style
  • Your skin tone
  • The size of your head
  • The shape of your nose
  • The color of your eyes

When trying on frames, make sure they fit comfortably and securely. The frames should not slide down your nose or pinch your temples.

Tips for Choosing Glasses

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing glasses:

  • Consider the occasion - Do you need glasses for work or for casual wear?
  • Choose a color that complements your skin tone and hair color
  • Don't be afraid to try on different styles and shapes - You might be surprised at what looks good on you!
  • Invest in high-quality lenses - Your glasses are an investment, so make sure you get the best lenses possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I wear any glasses with my face shape?

While certain frames are recommended for specific face shapes, you can wear any glasses you like. It ultimately comes down to your personal style and what makes you feel comfortable.

How do I know if my glasses fit correctly?

Your glasses should fit comfortably without sliding down your nose or pinching your temples. The frames should also be centered on your face with equal distance from your eyes.

What if I have a combination of face shapes?

If you have a combination of face shapes, choose frames that complement both shapes. For example, if you have a heart-shaped face with a strong jawline, you could choose frames that are wider at the bottom to balance the face.

How often should I replace my glasses?

It's recommended to replace your glasses every 1-2 years, or if your prescription changes. If your glasses become damaged or scratched, it's also a good idea to replace them.

Hair Care Tips for Glasses Wearers

Wearing glasses can sometimes cause your hair to get stuck in the frames or slide off your face. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking great while wearing glasses:

  • Use a silicone-based hair serum to smooth down flyaways
  • Choose hairstyles that won't interfere with your frames, such as low ponytails or buns
  • Invest in hair accessories like headbands or clips to keep your hair off your face

With these tips and recommendations, you'll be able to find the perfect pair of glasses for your face shape and style.
