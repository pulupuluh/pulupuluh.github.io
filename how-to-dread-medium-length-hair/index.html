How To Dread Medium Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read
If you're looking to dread your medium length hair, you've come to the right place. Dreading hair is a great way to express yourself and showcase your unique style. However, it can be a daunting process, especially if you're new to it. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to dread medium length hair and provide you with useful tips and information to make the process easier.

Description

Dreadlocks, or dreads, are a hairstyle that involves twisting or matting the hair into rope-like strands. This style has been around for centuries and is popular among people from various cultures and backgrounds. Dreads can be worn in many different ways, from long and flowing to short and close-cropped. In this article, we'll focus on how to dread medium length hair.

How to Dread Medium Length Hair

Now, let's get started with the step-by-step process of dreading medium length hair:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start dreading your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils. Then, dry your hair completely.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use a comb to section it into small, equal parts. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Take one section of hair and hold it taut. Then, use a backcombing technique to create knots and tangles in the hair. Use a dread comb or a fine-toothed comb to backcomb the hair from the tips to the roots. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 4: Twist Your Hair

After backcombing, twist each section of hair in the same direction to form a dreadlock. You can also use a natural wax or locking gel to help the hair hold its shape.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreads

Once you've created your dreads, it's important to maintain them properly. This includes washing them regularly with a residue-free shampoo and avoiding products that can cause buildup. You should also palm roll your dreads regularly to keep them tight and well-formed.

Tips for Dreading Medium Length Hair

Here are some useful tips to help you dread your medium length hair successfully: - Be patient: Dreading hair can take time, especially for medium-length hair. Don't rush the process or try to force your hair into dreads too quickly. - Use natural products: Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or synthetic ingredients, as these can damage your hair and make it harder to dread. - Experiment with styles: Dreads can be worn in many different styles, from updos to braids. Don't be afraid to experiment and find a style that works for you.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreading medium length hair:

Can I dread my hair if it's been chemically treated?

Yes, you can still dread your hair if it's been chemically treated, but it may take longer for the dreads to form. Be sure to use gentle products and avoid using heat on your hair.

How often should I wash my dreads?

You should wash your dreads at least once a week, but you may need to wash them more frequently if you have an active lifestyle or sweat a lot.

Hair Care for Dreads

Maintaining healthy hair is important, especially when you have dreads. Here are some hair care tips to keep your dreads looking and feeling their best: - Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz. - Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and make it harder to dread. - Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf. - Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Dreading medium length hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. By following the steps and tips in this article, you can achieve the dreads you've been dreaming of. Remember to be patient, use natural products, and take care of your hair to keep your dreads looking their best. Happy dreading!
