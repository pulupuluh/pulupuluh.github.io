How To Pick The Right Hair Color For Roots

Written by Frank Aug 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Choosing the perfect hair color for your roots can be a daunting task, especially if you're not sure where to start. In this article, we'll take you through the steps on how to pick the right hair color for your roots. We'll cover everything from choosing the right shade to hair care tips to maintain your new hair color. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Step 2: Choose the Right Shade
  • Step 3: Consider Your Lifestyle
  • Step 4: Hair Care Tips
  • FAQs

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right hair color for your roots is to determine your skin tone. Skin tones can be categorized into cool, warm, and neutral. To determine your skin tone, look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear blue, you have a cool skin tone. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If you have a mix of blue and green veins, you have a neutral skin tone.

Step 2: Choose the Right Shade

Once you have determined your skin tone, you can start choosing the right shade for your hair color. If you have a cool skin tone, choose hair colors that have cool undertones such as ash blonde, platinum blonde, or cool brown. If you have a warm skin tone, choose hair colors that have warm undertones such as golden blonde, caramel, or auburn. Neutral skin tones can pull off both cool and warm shades.

Another factor to consider is the natural color of your hair. If you have naturally dark hair, it's best to stick with shades that are no more than two shades lighter or darker. Going too light or too dark can result in an unnatural look. If you have naturally light hair, you can experiment with different shades.

Step 3: Consider Your Lifestyle

When choosing the right hair color for your roots, it's important to consider your lifestyle. If you have a busy schedule and don't have time for frequent touch-ups, choose a low maintenance hair color such as balayage or ombre. These hair colors require less maintenance and can grow out gracefully. If you're not afraid of maintenance, you can go for a bold hair color such as vibrant red or pastel pink.

Step 4: Hair Care Tips

Once you have chosen the right hair color for your roots, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the color. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid washing your hair too often
  • Avoid using hot tools as they can fade the color
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment at least once a week
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater as they can fade the color

FAQs

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have damaged hair?

A: It's best to avoid dyeing your hair if you have damaged hair. Dyeing your hair can further damage it and result in breakage. It's important to repair your hair first before dyeing it.

Q: How often should I touch up my roots?

A: It depends on the hair color and how fast your hair grows. Generally, it's best to touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks to maintain the color.

Q: Can I dye my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair at home. However, it's important to follow the instructions carefully and do a patch test before applying the dye to your hair.

Choosing the right hair color for your roots doesn't have to be overwhelming. By following these steps and taking care of your hair, you can achieve a beautiful and natural-looking hair color. Experiment with different shades and have fun with your new hair color!
