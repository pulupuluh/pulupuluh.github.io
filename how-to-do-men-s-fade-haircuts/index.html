How To Do Men s Fade Haircuts A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking for a fresh, modern haircut, a fade might be just what you need. Fades have been popular for years, and they're not going away anytime soon. In this guide, we'll show you how to do men's fade haircuts at home, so you can save money and get the look you want. Here's what we'll cover:

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Do Men's Fade Haircuts

A fade is a hairstyle that gradually gets shorter from top to bottom. There are many different types of fades, including high fades, low fades, and mid fades. Fades can be combined with other haircuts, like the undercut or the pompadour, to create a unique look.

To do a fade at home, you'll need a few tools:

  • Clipper set with guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Hair product (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to do a basic fade:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet, it will be harder to cut accurately.
  2. Decide where you want the fade to start. This will depend on the type of fade you want. For a high fade, start near the top of your head. For a low fade, start closer to your ears. For a mid fade, start in the middle.
  3. Choose the guard size you want to start with. A larger guard will leave more hair, while a smaller guard will leave less.
  4. Use the clippers to cut the hair above the fade line. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, going against the direction of hair growth.
  5. Blend the hair between the fade line and the top of your head. Switch to a smaller guard size and use a flicking motion to blend the two sections of hair together.
  6. Switch to an even smaller guard size and repeat the process, blending the hair between the fade line and the previous section.
  7. Repeat with even smaller guard sizes until you reach the desired length.
  8. Trim any longer hair with scissors.
  9. Clean up the edges around your ears and neck with clippers or scissors.
  10. Style your hair as desired with product.

Tips for a Perfect Fade

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with a longer guard size than you think you need. You can always go shorter, but you can't go longer once you've cut the hair.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair as you're cutting. This will help you cut more evenly and avoid leaving any patches.
  • Take your time. Fades require precision and patience. Rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Practice on a willing friend or family member before trying it on yourself.

FAQs

How often should I get a fade?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you want to maintain the look. Some people get fades every two weeks, while others can go a month or more between haircuts.

Can I do a fade with long hair?

Yes, but it will take longer and require more blending. It's best to go to a professional if you're not comfortable doing it yourself.

What's the difference between a fade and a taper?

A fade gradually gets shorter from top to bottom, while a taper gradually gets shorter from the ears down. Tapers are often used to blend longer hair on top with shorter hair on the sides and back.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your fade looking fresh, try these tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Condition your hair to keep it soft and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, like blow dryers or flat irons.
  • Use a hair product, like pomade or wax, to help hold your style in place.
  • Get regular haircuts to maintain the shape of your fade.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to do a fade haircut like a pro. Good luck!
