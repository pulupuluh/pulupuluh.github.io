What Makes My Hair Less Puffy Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter May 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you know how frustrating it can be to deal with puffy, frizzy hair. While some people love the voluminous look, others prefer a more polished, sleek style. In this article, we'll explore what makes your hair less puffy and share some tips and tricks to help you achieve your desired look.

Description

Puffy hair is caused by several factors, including humidity, damaged hair, and the use of the wrong hair products. Humidity can cause your hair to absorb moisture from the air, which can lead to frizz and puffiness. Damaged hair is more prone to frizz and breakage, which can cause your hair to look puffy. Using the wrong hair products, such as those containing harsh chemicals, can also contribute to puffy hair.

How to Make Your Hair Less Puffy

Step 1: Wash Your Hair Properly

One of the most important things you can do to prevent puffy hair is to wash your hair properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type. Avoid using hot water, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz. Instead, use lukewarm water to wash your hair.

Step 2: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help hydrate and nourish your hair. This will help prevent frizz and keep your hair looking smooth and shiny. Look for a leave-in conditioner that is specifically formulated for curly or wavy hair.

Step 3: Use a Microfiber Towel

Avoid using a regular towel to dry your hair, as it can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a microfiber towel or a cotton T-shirt to gently blot your hair dry. This will help absorb the excess moisture without causing damage to your hair.

Step 4: Use the Right Hair Products

Using the right hair products is crucial when it comes to preventing puffy hair. Look for products that are designed for your hair type, such as curl-enhancing creams, gels, and mousses. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals, such as sulfates and alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

Step 5: Use a Diffuser

If you like to blow dry your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help prevent puffy hair. A diffuser will help distribute the heat evenly and reduce the amount of air flow, which can help prevent frizz and puffiness.

Tips for Preventing Puffy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you prevent puffy hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can transfer oils from your hands to your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove buildup from your hair and scalp.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends, which can make your hair look puffy and frizzy.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to make my hair less puffy?

A: Yes, you can use a hair straightener to make your hair less puffy. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair. Also, avoid using the straightener on wet hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Q: Can I use a hair mask to prevent puffy hair?

A: Yes, using a hair mask once a week can help prevent puffy hair. Look for a hair mask that is specifically formulated for curly or wavy hair, and leave it on for at least 10-15 minutes before rinsing it off.

Hair Care for Less Puffy Hair

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve less puffy hair and enjoy a sleek, polished look. Remember to use the right hair products, avoid harsh chemicals, and take care of your hair by getting regular trims and using a clarifying shampoo. With a little bit of effort and the right products, you can have the hair of your dreams!
