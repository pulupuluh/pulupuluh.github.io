How To Use Blending Scissors A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Blending scissors, also known as thinning shears or texturizing shears, are a must-have tool for any hair stylist or barber. These scissors are specially designed to remove bulk and create texture in the hair, making them an essential tool for creating seamless layers and blending different lengths of hair. In this guide, we'll teach you everything you need to know about how to use blending scissors, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and frequently asked questions.

What are Blending Scissors?

Blending scissors are a type of hair cutting tool that features teeth on one blade and a straight edge on the other. The teeth are designed to remove hair in a graduated manner, creating a softer, more natural-looking blend between different lengths of hair. Blending scissors are typically used on the mid-lengths and ends of the hair to create texture and remove bulk, but they can also be used to create soft layers and add volume to the hair.

How to Use Blending Scissors

Using blending scissors is a relatively simple process, but it does require some technique and practice to master. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into manageable sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't accidentally cut too much hair at once.

Step 2: Hold the Scissors Correctly

Hold the blending scissors with the teeth facing up and the straight edge facing down. Your thumb should be in the smaller hole and your ring finger and pinky finger should be in the larger hole. Your index and middle fingers should grip the handle of the scissors.

Step 3: Choose the Right Section of Hair

Select the section of hair that you want to blend. This is typically the mid-lengths and ends of the hair, but it can also be used to create layers or add volume to the hair.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting at the top of the section, use the blending scissors to make small, quick cuts in a diagonal direction. This will remove small sections of hair and create texture and movement in the hair.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Continue cutting in a diagonal direction, moving down the section of hair, until the desired amount of blending has been achieved. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once or to create uneven sections.

Step 6: Repeat on the Other Side

Repeat the process on the other side of the head, making sure to cut the hair in the same direction and at the same angle as the first side.

Tips for Using Blending Scissors

Here are a few tips to help you get the most out of your blending scissors:

- Use blending scissors sparingly. While they can be a great tool for creating texture and movement in the hair, it's important not to overdo it. Too much blending can create uneven sections and make the hair look thin and wispy.

- Practice on mannequin heads or practice sections of hair before working on a client. This will help you get a feel for the scissors and develop your technique without the pressure of working on a real person.

- Use the right type of blending scissors for the job. Different types of blending scissors have different teeth patterns and blade sizes, so it's important to choose the right one for the job you're doing.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about blending scissors:

Q: Can blending scissors be used on all hair types?

A: Blending scissors can be used on most hair types, but they are best suited for medium to thick hair that needs to be thinned out or textured.

Q: How often should blending scissors be sharpened?

A: Blending scissors should be sharpened regularly to ensure that they are cutting cleanly and efficiently. It's recommended to have them sharpened every 6-12 months, depending on how often they are used.

Q: Can blending scissors be used to create bangs?

A: While blending scissors can be used to create soft layers in the hair, they are not typically used to create bangs. A straight edge scissor or razor is usually a better choice for creating a blunt or textured fringe.

Hair Care After Using Blending Scissors

After using blending scissors, it's important to take care of the hair to prevent damage and maintain the style. Here are a few tips:

- Use a quality shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type.

- Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage the hair and cause split ends.

- Use a heat protectant spray or serum before using hot tools to help minimize damage.

- Get regular trims to keep the hair healthy and prevent split ends from forming.

By following these tips and techniques, you can use blending scissors to create beautiful, textured hairstyles that look natural and effortless.
