How To Tell If A Dachshund Puppy Is Long Or Short Haired

Written by Daniel May 21, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're considering getting a dachshund puppy, one of the first things you'll need to decide is whether you want a long haired or short haired dachshund. While some people have a preference for one over the other, others may not know the difference or how to tell them apart. In this article, we'll provide you with a guide on how to tell if a dachshund puppy is long or short haired, as well as some hair care tips to keep your furry friend looking their best.

Table of Contents

  • How to Tell if a Dachshund Puppy is Long or Short Haired
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQ

Step by Step Guide

The easiest way to tell if a dachshund puppy is long or short haired is to look at their coat. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Look at the Length

The first thing you should do is look at the length of the fur on the puppy's body. If the fur is short and lies close to the skin, it's a short haired dachshund. If the fur is longer and softer, it's a long haired dachshund.

Step 2: Check the Ears

Next, check the fur on the puppy's ears. Short haired dachshunds have short, smooth fur on their ears, while long haired dachshunds have longer, wavy fur that may even curl.

Step 3: Look at the Tail

Finally, look at the fur on the puppy's tail. Short haired dachshunds have a smooth, short-haired tail, while long haired dachshunds have a furry, plumed tail.

By following these steps, you'll be able to tell if a dachshund puppy is long or short haired with ease.

Tips for Hair Care

Whether you decide to get a long haired or short haired dachshund, proper hair care is important to keep your furry friend looking and feeling their best. Here are some tips:

Brush Regularly

Regular brushing is key to maintaining your dachshund's coat, especially if you have a long haired dachshund. Brushing helps to remove any tangles or mats and keeps the coat looking shiny and healthy.

Bathe as Needed

While you don't need to bathe your dachshund as often as you might bathe yourself, it's still important to keep them clean. Bathe your dachshund as needed, using a mild dog shampoo and warm water.

Trim Nails

Dachshunds have long nails that can easily become overgrown. Make sure to trim your dachshund's nails regularly to prevent them from becoming too long and causing discomfort.

FAQ

Q: Are long haired dachshunds more high maintenance than short haired dachshunds?

A: Yes, long haired dachshunds require more grooming than short haired dachshunds. They need to be brushed more often to prevent tangles and mats, and their fur may require regular trimming.

Q: Do dachshunds shed a lot?

A: Yes, dachshunds do shed, especially during shedding season. However, regular brushing can help to minimize shedding and keep your home clean.

Q: Can I groom my dachshund at home?

A: Yes, you can groom your dachshund at home. However, if you're not comfortable doing it yourself, it's best to take them to a professional groomer.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be able to keep your dachshund looking their best, whether they're long or short haired.
