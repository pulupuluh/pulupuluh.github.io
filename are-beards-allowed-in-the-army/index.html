Are Beards Allowed In The Army

Written by Aprilia Mar 14, 2023 ¬∑ 6 min read

If you are planning to join the army and you have a beard, the question that may come to your mind is whether or not you can keep it. The answer is not straightforward. While the army has strict grooming standards, there are some exceptions to the rule.

Description

In this blog post, we will discuss the rules and regulations regarding beards in the army. We will also provide tips on how to take care of your hair while in the army.

How to

If you are planning to join the army, it is important that you understand the grooming standards. The army has strict rules regarding hair length, facial hair, and tattoos. The grooming standards are designed to maintain a professional appearance and to ensure uniformity among soldiers.

Step by Step

The first step is to understand the rules regarding beards in the army. According to army regulations, soldiers are not allowed to have beards, except for certain medical or religious reasons. If you have a medical condition that prevents you from shaving, you may be allowed to keep a beard. Similarly, if you have a religious reason for keeping a beard, you may be granted an exemption.

The second step is to ensure that your beard is well-groomed. If you are allowed to keep a beard, it must be neat and trimmed. The army requires that beards be no longer than two inches in length, and they must not interfere with wearing protective equipment such as gas masks.

Tips

If you are allowed to keep a beard in the army, it is important that you take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a beard oil or balm to keep your beard moisturized and soft.
  • Trim your beard regularly to keep it neat and tidy.
  • Use a beard comb or brush to keep your beard looking its best.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your beard, such as bleach or hair dye.

FAQ

Q: What are the exceptions to the rule regarding beards in the army?

A: Soldiers may be allowed to keep a beard for medical or religious reasons.

Q: How long can a beard be in the army?

A: Beards in the army must be no longer than two inches in length.

Q: Can soldiers use beard oil in the army?

A: Yes, soldiers who are allowed to keep a beard may use beard oil or balm to keep it moisturized and soft.

Hair Care

In addition to the rules regarding beards, the army also has strict rules regarding hair length and style. Male soldiers are required to keep their hair short and neatly groomed. The hair must not touch the collar, and it must be tapered at the back and sides. Female soldiers are required to keep their hair neat and well-groomed, and it must be worn in a bun or ponytail.

It is important that soldiers take care of their hair while in the army. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye.
  • Visit a barber or hair stylist regularly to keep your hair well-groomed.

Conclusion

In conclusion, beards are generally not allowed in the army, except for certain medical or religious reasons. If you are allowed to keep a beard, it must be neat and trimmed. It is important that soldiers take care of their hair and beards while in the army to maintain a professional appearance.
