How To Cut Short Layers In Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William May 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without losing length, cutting short layers in your long hair is a great option. Short layers can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair, making it look fuller and more dynamic. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting short layers in long hair, from start to finish.

How To

Before we get started, here are a few things you'll need:

  • A pair of sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • Hair ties
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Now, let's dive into the steps:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry, as wet hair can be deceiving and appear longer than it actually is. So, wash and dry your hair before you get started.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into three sections: the top, middle, and bottom. Use hair ties to secure the top and middle sections, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 3: Cut the bottom section

Take the bottom section of your hair and comb it straight down. Starting at the back, use your scissors to cut small, diagonal sections of hair, snipping off the ends to create layers. Work your way around the back and then move to the sides, cutting at an angle to create a graduated effect.

Step 4: Release the middle section

Once you've finished cutting the bottom section, release the middle section and repeat the same process. Start at the back and work your way around, cutting small, diagonal sections to create layers.

Step 5: Cut the top section

Finally, release the top section and repeat the process once more. However, for the top section, don't cut as much hair as you did for the bottom and middle sections. This will help create a more natural, blended look.

Step 6: Blend the layers

Once you've finished cutting all three sections, comb your hair and check for any uneven spots or choppy areas. Blend any harsh lines or uneven layers by snipping off small amounts of hair until it looks smooth and even.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting short layers in long hair:

  • Take your time and cut slowly. It's better to cut too little hair than too much.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut straight across. Instead, cut at an angle to create a graduated effect.
  • Start with small sections and work your way up to larger ones.
  • Check your hair in natural light to ensure you've cut it evenly.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting short layers in long hair:

How often should I cut my hair to maintain the layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how much you want to maintain the layers. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut short layers in curly hair?

Absolutely! Short layers can add volume and bounce to curly hair. However, it's important to cut curly hair when it's dry to ensure you're cutting it at the right length.

Will cutting short layers in long hair make it look thinner?

No, cutting short layers can actually make your hair look fuller and thicker by adding texture and movement.

Hair Care

To maintain your short layers and keep your hair healthy, here are a few hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add moisture and prevent frizz.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to prevent damage.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the layers.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you're now ready to cut short layers in long hair like a pro. Good luck and have fun experimenting with your new style!
