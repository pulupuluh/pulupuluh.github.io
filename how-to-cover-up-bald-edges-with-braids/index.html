How To Cover Up Bald Edges With Braids A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Apr 28, 2023 · 8 min read

Welcome to our comprehensive guide on how to cover up bald edges with braids. If you’re someone who struggles with hair loss or thinning edges, don’t worry, you’re not alone. It’s a common problem for many people, but there are ways to conceal it. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through the steps to achieve a beautiful and natural-looking hairstyle using braids.

Description

Bald edges can be caused by many factors such as genetics, tight hairstyles, and even certain medical conditions. No matter the cause, it can be frustrating to deal with. Braids are a great way to cover up bald edges because they can be styled in different ways and can be customized to your preferences. With the right technique and a little patience, you can achieve a flawless braid style that will cover up your bald edges.

How to Cover Up Bald Edges with Braids

Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start braiding, it’s important to prep your hair. This involves washing and conditioning your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to keep your hair hydrated and reduce breakage. After washing, detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair and let it air dry or use a hooded dryer.

Step 2: Create a Part

Using a rat-tail comb, create a part in the front of your hair where you want to start braiding. Make sure the part is straight and even. You can choose to make a middle part or a side part depending on your preference.

Step 3: Start Braiding

Begin braiding your hair using the three-strand method. Take a small section of hair from the front and divide it into three equal parts. Cross the right strand over the middle strand, then cross the left strand over the middle strand. Continue braiding until you reach the end of your hair. Secure the braid with a hair tie or a rubber band.

Step 4: Repeat the Process

Repeat the braiding process along your hairline, making sure to keep the braids tight but not too tight to avoid damaging your hair. You can also choose to add extensions to the braids for length and volume.

Step 5: Style Your Braids

Once you’ve braided your hair, you can style it in different ways. You can create a bun, a ponytail, or leave the braids loose. You can also add accessories such as beads, cuffs, or rings to make your style more unique.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when covering up bald edges with braids:

  • Use a gentle touch when braiding to avoid causing further hair loss.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent breakage and promote hair growth.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hairline and cause further damage.
  • Choose extensions or braiding hair that matches your natural hair texture and color.
  • Avoid over-styling your hair and give it time to rest and recover.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about covering up bald edges with braids:

Can braids cause hair loss?

Yes, if braids are too tight or if they’re left in for too long, they can cause hair loss. That’s why it’s important to use a gentle touch when braiding and to take breaks in between styles.

How long should I keep my braids in?

Braids can be left in for up to six weeks, but it’s recommended to take them out after four weeks to avoid damage to your hair.

Can I still wash my hair with braids?

Yes, you can still wash your hair with braids, but you need to be gentle to avoid causing frizz or unraveling the braids. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot water.

Hair Care

It’s important to take care of your hair when covering up bald edges with braids. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage while you sleep.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to promote hair growth.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated.

By following these tips and steps, you can achieve a beautiful and natural-looking hairstyle while covering up bald edges with braids. Remember to take care of your hair and be gentle when braiding to avoid further damage. With a little patience and practice, you can master the art of braiding and achieve a flawless style.
