How To Get My Curly Hair Back After Bleaching

Written by Petter Aug 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have bleached your curly hair and lost some of your curls, you might be wondering how to get them back. In this article, we will discuss some tips and tricks that can help you to restore your curly hair after bleaching. Here's what we will cover:

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get My Curly Hair Back After Bleaching
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Restoring Your Curly Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care to Maintain Your Curls

Description

Bleaching curly hair can cause damage to the hair cuticle, which can lead to the loss of curl pattern. This damage can be caused by the chemicals in the bleach, which can break down the protein bonds in the hair. Additionally, the heat used during the bleaching process can further damage the hair and cause it to lose its elasticity.

If you have bleached your curly hair and are now experiencing a loss of curl pattern, don't worry! There are steps you can take to restore your curls and get them back to their natural state.

How to Get My Curly Hair Back After Bleaching

The key to restoring your curls after bleaching is to nourish and strengthen your hair. Here are some steps you can take:

1. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair is essential to restore moisture and elasticity to your strands. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help repair the damage caused by the bleach. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, coconut oil, and shea butter to help strengthen and moisturize your hair.

2. Use Protein Treatments

Protein treatments can help restore the protein bonds in your hair that were broken down during the bleaching process. Look for products that contain hydrolyzed protein or keratin to help rebuild your hair's strength.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause further damage to your hair and make it harder to restore your curls. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low.

4. Trim Your Ends Regularly

Trimming your ends regularly can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can contribute to the loss of curl pattern. Aim to trim your ends every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and strong.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to restoring your curls after bleaching:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle sulfate-free shampoo. Rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Deep Condition

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for at least 30 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 3: Apply Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 4: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.

Step 5: Air Dry Your Hair

Air dry your hair to avoid heat damage. If you must use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment and keep the heat setting low.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, style it as desired. Avoid using heat styling tools if possible.

Tips for Restoring Your Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for restoring your curly hair after bleaching:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as relaxers or texturizers.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and other environmental factors that can cause damage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Take a multivitamin or biotin supplement to promote healthy hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long will it take to restore my curls after bleaching?

It can take several months to restore your curls after bleaching, depending on the severity of the damage. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Can I dye my hair after bleaching?

It is generally not recommended to dye your hair immediately after bleaching, as this can cause further damage. Wait at least 4-6 weeks before dyeing your hair again.

Hair Care to Maintain Your Curls

Once you have restored your curls, it's important to maintain them with a healthy hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and strong.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you can restore your curly hair after bleaching and keep it healthy and strong.
