How To Cut Black Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 14, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle? Do you want to add some depth and dimension to your black hair? Layered haircuts are a great way to achieve a new look without sacrificing length. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut black hair in layers. From preparation to aftercare, we've got everything you need to know to get started.

Table of Contents:

  • Preparation
  • Sectioning the Hair
  • Cutting the Layers
  • Sectioning the Top Layer
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Black Hair in Layers
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Layered Haircut

Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle with water

It's also a good idea to have a picture of the desired hairstyle to refer to throughout the process.

Sectioning the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair. Divide the hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Secure each section with clips or hair ties.

Starting at the back, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and angle the scissors diagonally downward. Cut a small section of hair at the ends. Continue cutting small sections of hair at a time. Remember to cut diagonally for a layered look.

Cutting the Layers

Once you have cut the back section, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair at the front and pull it straight. Cut the ends diagonally. Continue cutting small sections of hair at a time until you have finished both sides.

Sectioning the Top Layer

Now it's time to cut the top layer. Section the hair at the crown of the head and comb it straight. Cut the ends diagonally. Continue cutting small sections of hair at a time.

Finishing Touches

Once you have cut all the layers, it's time to add some finishing touches. Blend the layers by combing the hair and checking for any uneven sections. If necessary, trim any stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting Black Hair in Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure even layers.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Angle the scissors diagonally downward for a layered look.
  • Refer to a picture of the desired hairstyle throughout the cutting process.

FAQs

How often should I get a layered haircut?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the style.

Will cutting my hair in layers damage it?

No, cutting hair in layers should not damage it. However, it's important to take care of your hair after getting a layered haircut to prevent split ends and breakage.

Can I cut my own hair in layers?

While it's possible to cut your own hair in layers, it's recommended to have a professional do it to ensure an even cut.

Hair Care After Layered Haircut

After getting a layered haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim any split ends regularly to prevent breakage.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy.

Now that you know how to cut black hair in layers, you can achieve a new look without sacrificing length. Remember to take your time and start with small sections of hair for an even cut. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful layered hairstyle.
