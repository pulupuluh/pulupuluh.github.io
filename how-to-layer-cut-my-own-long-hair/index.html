How To Layer Cut Your Own Long Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 28, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're tired of your long hair looking flat and lifeless, then it's time to consider layering. Layering is a great way to add volume, movement, and texture to your hair. In this article, we'll show you how to layer cut your own long hair. We'll cover everything from how to section your hair to what tools you'll need. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Sectioning Your Hair
  2. Tools You'll Need
  3. Cutting Your Hair
  4. Tips for a Successful Layered Cut
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care after Layering

Sectioning Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to section it properly. This will ensure that you get an even and balanced cut. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by parting your hair down the middle.
  2. Then, take a section of hair from the top of your head and clip it away.
  3. Next, take another section of hair from just below the first section and clip it away as well.
  4. Continue this process until you reach the nape of your neck.
  5. Repeat the process on the other side of your head.

Now that your hair is properly sectioned, you're ready to start cutting.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips

Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it difficult to achieve a clean cut.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Take the first section of hair and comb it straight down.
  2. Hold the hair between your fingers and slide your fingers down to the desired length.
  3. Cut the hair straight across.
  4. Move on to the next section of hair and repeat the process.
  5. Make sure to cut each section of hair to the same length.

Remember, you can always cut more hair off, but you can't put it back on. So, start with a little bit of hair and work your way up to the desired length.

Tips for a Successful Layered Cut

Here are some tips to keep in mind while layering your hair:

  • Start with clean, dry hair.
  • Use sharp scissors and a comb to achieve a clean cut.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure an even cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Take breaks if you need them.

FAQ

Can I layer cut my own hair?

Yes, you can layer cut your own hair. However, it's important to be patient and take your time. Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to the desired length.

How often should I layer cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a layered cut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and fresh.

Hair Care after Layering

After you've layered your hair, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.

With these tips, you'll be able to maintain your layered cut and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.

Conclusion

Layer cutting your own long hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Remember to take your time, start with small sections of hair, and be patient. And, after you've achieved the perfect layered cut, take good care of your hair to maintain its health and beauty.
