The Best Wax For Thick Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 16, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you tired of struggling with your thick hair every morning? Do you spend hours trying to tame your unruly mane with various hair products, only to have it frizz up again in a matter of minutes? If so, it's time to invest in the best wax for thick hair. In this guide, we'll take you through everything you need to know about choosing the right wax for your hair type and give you some tips on how to use it effectively.

Why Wax is Great for Thick Hair

If you have thick hair, you know how difficult it can be to style. It often takes a lot of product and time to get your hair looking the way you want it to. That's where wax comes in. Wax is a versatile styling product that can help you achieve a variety of hairstyles. It's especially great for thick hair because it provides hold and texture without weighing your hair down or making it look greasy.

How to Choose the Best Wax for Thick Hair

Not all waxes are created equal, and what works for someone else might not work for you. When choosing the best wax for thick hair, there are a few things to keep in mind:

1. Consider Your Hair Type

Thick hair comes in many different textures, from curly to straight to wavy. The type of wax you choose will depend on your specific hair type. If you have curly or wavy hair, you'll want a wax that provides a strong hold to keep your curls or waves in place. If you have straight hair, you can get away with a lighter hold wax.

2. Look for Natural Ingredients

Many waxes on the market are filled with chemicals that can damage your hair over time. Look for waxes that contain natural ingredients like beeswax, shea butter, and coconut oil. These ingredients will not only help you achieve your desired style but also nourish and protect your hair.

3. Consider the Hold

The hold of a wax refers to how long it will keep your hair in place. If you want your style to last all day, you'll need a wax with a strong hold. However, if you prefer a more natural look, a lighter hold wax might be a better option.

How to Use Wax on Thick Hair

Once you've found the best wax for your hair type, it's time to start using it. Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your wax:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wax works best on clean, dry hair. Make sure to wash and dry your hair thoroughly before applying wax.

2. Warm the Wax

Most waxes need to be warmed up before use. Rub a small amount of wax between your palms to warm it up before applying it to your hair.

3. Apply the Wax

Start by applying a small amount of wax to your hair, focusing on the ends and working your way up. Use your fingers to style your hair into your desired shape.

4. Add More Wax as Needed

If you need more hold or texture, you can always add more wax. Just be careful not to use too much, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

Tips for Using Wax on Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the most out of your wax:

1. Don't Overdo It

As mentioned earlier, using too much wax can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy. Start with a small amount and add more as needed.

2. Experiment with Different Styles

Wax is a versatile product that can help you achieve a variety of styles. Don't be afraid to experiment with different looks to see what works best for you.

3. Use a Comb or Brush

If you're having trouble getting your hair to cooperate, try using a comb or brush to help distribute the wax evenly.

4. Wash Your Hair Thoroughly

Wax can build up in your hair over time, so make sure to wash your hair thoroughly to avoid buildup.

FAQs About Using Wax on Thick Hair

1. Can wax damage my hair?

It's unlikely that wax will damage your hair if you use it correctly. However, using too much wax can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy, so it's important to use it in moderation.

2. How often should I use wax?

You can use wax as often as you like, but it's important to wash your hair thoroughly to avoid buildup.

3. Can I use wax on wet hair?

Wax works best on dry hair. Applying wax to wet hair can dilute the product and make it less effective.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Finally, here are some general hair care tips for those with thick hair:

1. Use a Clarifying Shampoo

Since thick hair can be prone to buildup, it's important to use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any product buildup.

2. Use a Deep Conditioner

Thick hair can be prone to dryness, so it's important to use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair over time. Try to avoid using them as much as possible and opt for heat-free styling methods instead.

With the right wax and a little bit of know-how, you can finally tame your thick hair and achieve the styles you've always wanted. Remember to choose a wax that's appropriate for your hair type, use it in moderation, and take care of your hair with the right products and techniques.
