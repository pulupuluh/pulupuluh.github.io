How To Keep Wet Curly Hair From Frizzing Overnight

Written by Frank Jul 30, 2023 · 7 min read

Do you struggle with waking up to frizzy, unruly curls in the morning? Wet curly hair is especially prone to frizzing overnight, but there are a few simple steps you can take to prevent it. In this article, we’ll go over how to keep wet curly hair from frizzing overnight, including tips, tricks, and product recommendations.

Table of Contents

  • How to Keep Wet Curly Hair from Frizzing Overnight
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Overnight Frizz
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products to Help Prevent Frizz

How to Keep Wet Curly Hair from Frizzing Overnight

The key to preventing overnight frizz is to set your curls in place while they’re wet. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Start by applying a leave-in conditioner to your wet hair. This will help to hydrate your curls and prevent them from drying out and frizzing overnight. Be sure to distribute the conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

Next, apply a styling product that will help to define and hold your curls in place. This could be a gel, mousse, or cream, depending on your hair type and preferences. Again, make sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Plop Your Hair

Now it’s time to “plop” your hair. This means wrapping your curls up in a microfiber towel or t-shirt to help set them in place. Simply lay the towel or t-shirt flat on a surface, then flip your hair over onto it. Gather the fabric around your head and secure it in place with a hair tie or clip.

Step 4: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Finally, make sure you’re sleeping on a silk pillowcase. Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and lead to frizz, whereas silk is much gentler on your hair. If you don’t have a silk pillowcase, you could also wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a quick summary of the steps involved in keeping wet curly hair from frizzing overnight:

  1. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your wet hair
  2. Apply a styling product to define and hold your curls
  3. Plop your hair by wrapping it in a microfiber towel or t-shirt
  4. Sleep on a silk pillowcase or wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet

Tips for Preventing Overnight Frizz

Here are a few additional tips for preventing overnight frizz:

  • Don’t touch your hair too much while it’s drying. This can disturb the curl pattern and lead to frizz.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Try not to sleep with wet hair. If you must, make sure it’s completely dry before you go to bed.
  • Consider using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without disturbing them.

FAQs

How do I refresh my curls in the morning?

If you wake up with frizzy curls, you can refresh them by spritzing them with water or a curl refreshing spray, then scrunching them to redefine the curl pattern. You could also use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help smooth out any frizz.

What kind of products should I use on my curly hair?

Curly hair tends to be dry and prone to frizz, so it’s important to use products that hydrate and nourish your curls. Look for products that are specifically formulated for curly hair, such as leave-in conditioners, curl creams, and gels.

Should I brush my curly hair?

No, brushing curly hair can disrupt the curl pattern and cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it’s wet.

  • Leave-in conditioner: Shea Moisture Jamaican Black Castor Oil Leave-In Conditioner
  • Curl cream: DevaCurl SuperCream Coconut Curl Styler
  • Gel: Aunt Jackie’s Flaxseed Don’t Shrink Curling Gel
  • Curl refreshing spray: Ouidad Botanical Boost Curl Energizing & Refreshing Spray
  • Silk pillowcase: Alaska Bear Natural Silk Pillowcase
