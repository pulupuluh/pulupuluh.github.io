How To Cut Long Curly Male Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 06, 2023 Â· 13 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge to cut, especially when it's long. But with the right technique, you can create a stylish and manageable haircut that suits your face shape and hair texture. In this article, we'll show you how to cut long curly male hair, from preparation to finishing touches. Whether you're a professional hairdresser or a DIY enthusiast, our guide will help you achieve great results.

Description

Long curly hair can be stunning, but it can also be a hassle to maintain. If you're tired of dealing with tangles and frizz, it may be time for a haircut. Whether you want to trim your ends or go for a dramatic transformation, cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. You need to account for the curl pattern, the density, and the shrinkage factor, among other things. This guide will give you the knowledge and skills to cut long curly male hair with confidence.

How to Cut Long Curly Male Hair

Step 1: Wash and Condition the Hair

Before you cut the hair, you need to make sure it's clean and moisturized. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a hydrating conditioner to remove dirt and buildup, and to soften the hair. Rinse thoroughly and gently squeeze out excess water with a towel. Don't rub the hair or use a blow dryer, as this can cause frizz and damage.

Step 2: Detangle the Hair

Curly hair tends to tangle easily, so you need to detangle it before cutting. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to remove knots and snarls, starting from the ends and working your way up. Be gentle and patient, and don't force the hair. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or a detangling spray to make the process easier.

Step 3: Section the Hair

To cut the hair evenly and systematically, you need to divide it into sections. Use hair clips or elastics to create four or more sections, depending on the thickness and length of the hair. Start with the back sections and work your way to the front, using the natural parting as a guide.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Now comes the fun part: cutting the hair. There are different techniques and tools you can use, depending on the style you want to achieve and your level of skill. Here are some options:

  • Wet cut: This involves cutting the hair when it's wet, using scissors, a razor, or clippers. Wet cutting can give you more control and precision, but it may not show the true shape of the curls.
  • Dry cut: This involves cutting the hair when it's dry, using scissors or shears. Dry cutting can show the natural bounce and movement of the curls, but it may be harder to get a clean line.
  • Twist and snip: This involves twisting a small section of hair and cutting off the ends that stick out. This can create a textured and layered look, but it may not work for all hair types and lengths.
  • Layered cut: This involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create layers and dimension. Layering can add volume and shape to the hair, but it may require more maintenance and styling.

Whichever method you choose, make sure you cut the hair in small sections and check the symmetry and balance as you go. Don't cut too much at once, as curly hair tends to bounce up and shrink when dry. You can always trim more later if needed.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut all the sections, you need to blend them together to create a seamless and natural look. Use a comb or your fingers to blend the hair at the crown, the sides, and the back. Check for any unevenness or gaps and trim them if necessary. You can also use thinning shears or texturizing scissors to remove bulk and create a softer transition.

Step 6: Finish the Hair

Now that you've cut and blended the hair, it's time to finish it off. You can style it according to your preference, using a diffuser, a curl cream, a gel, or a mousse. You can also leave it natural and let it air dry. Whatever you choose, make sure you apply a heat protectant and avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down the curls and make them greasy.

Tips for Cutting Long Curly Male Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting long curly male hair:

  • Use sharp and high-quality scissors or shears to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Cut the hair when it's dry if you want to see the true shape of the curls.
  • Leave some length at the ends to avoid a blunt and choppy look.
  • Use point cutting or slide cutting to create texture and movement.
  • Avoid thinning the hair too much, as this can make it look stringy and weak.
  • Consult with the client and show them the progress as you cut, to make sure they're satisfied.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my long curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair growth rate and your desired length. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 8-12 weeks to maintain the shape and health of the hair. If you want to grow your hair longer, you may need to cut it less frequently, but make sure you still trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Q: Can I cut my own long curly hair?

A: Yes, you can, but it's important to be cautious and patient. Cutting curly hair can be tricky, especially if you're not familiar with the technique. Make sure you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully. It's also a good idea to practice on a small section first, to see how the curls react.

Q: What is the best haircut for long curly male hair?

A: There's no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, as the best haircut depends on various factors, such as face shape, hair texture, curl pattern, and personal style. However, some popular haircuts for long curly male hair include the layered cut, the undercut, the afro, the tapered cut, and the curly fringe. Consult with a professional stylist or do some research to find the style that suits you best.

Hair Care for Long Curly Male Hair

After you've cut your long curly male hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a hydrating conditioner to cleanse and moisturize the hair.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and repair the hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling wands, as they can damage the hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry the hair gently and enhance the curls.
  • Avoid brushing or combing the hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush when it's wet.
  • Protect the hair from the sun, wind, and chlorine by wearing a hat or a swim cap.

Conclusion

Cutting long curly male hair can be a challenge, but it can also be a rewarding and creative experience. By following our step-by-step guide and tips, you can create a haircut that highlights the beauty and uniqueness of curly hair. Whether you want a subtle trim or a bold transformation, remember to be patient, gentle, and confident. With the right technique and attitude, you can achieve great results and make your curly hair the envy of others.
