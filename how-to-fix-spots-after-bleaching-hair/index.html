How To Fix Spots After Bleaching Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Aug 16, 2023 · 7 min read

If you've ever bleached your hair, you know how tricky it can be to get the exact shade you want. Unfortunately, sometimes it doesn't go as planned, and you end up with spots or patches that are too light or too dark. Don't panic – there are several ways to fix spots after bleaching hair. In this post, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix Spots After Bleaching Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Fixing Spots After Bleaching Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Fixing Spots

How to Fix Spots After Bleaching Hair

There are several ways to fix spots after bleaching hair, depending on the severity of the issue. Here are a few options:

Bleach the Spots Again

If the spots are too light, you can bleach them again to achieve the desired shade. Be careful not to leave the bleach on for too long, as this can damage your hair.

Use a Toner

If the spots are too dark, you can use a toner to lighten them. Toners come in a variety of shades, so choose one that matches the rest of your hair.

Color the Spots

If the spots are too light or too dark to fix with bleach or toner, you can color them to match the rest of your hair. This is a good option if you don't want to bleach your hair again.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to fixing spots after bleaching hair:

  1. Assess the severity of the spots.
  2. Determine the best course of action (bleach, toner, or color).
  3. If using bleach, mix it according to the instructions on the package.
  4. Apply the bleach to the spots, being careful not to overlap onto the rest of your hair.
  5. Leave the bleach on for the recommended amount of time (usually 20-30 minutes).
  6. Rinse the bleach out thoroughly with cool water.
  7. If using a toner, mix it according to the instructions on the package.
  8. Apply the toner to the spots, being careful not to overlap onto the rest of your hair.
  9. Leave the toner on for the recommended amount of time (usually 10-20 minutes).
  10. Rinse the toner out thoroughly with cool water.
  11. If coloring the spots, choose a color that matches the rest of your hair.
  12. Apply the color to the spots, being careful not to overlap onto the rest of your hair.
  13. Leave the color on for the recommended amount of time (usually 30-45 minutes).
  14. Rinse the color out thoroughly with cool water.

Tips for Fixing Spots After Bleaching Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when fixing spots after bleaching hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment after bleaching or coloring your hair to prevent damage.
  • Always follow the instructions on the bleach, toner, or color package.
  • Do a strand test before applying any bleach, toner, or color to your hair.
  • Don't try to fix spots that are close to your scalp – this can irritate your skin.

FAQs

How long should I wait before fixing spots after bleaching hair?

You should wait at least a week before fixing spots after bleaching hair to give your hair time to recover.

Can I fix spots after bleaching hair at home?

Yes, you can fix spots after bleaching hair at home. However, if you're not comfortable doing it yourself, it's best to consult a professional.

How can I prevent spots after bleaching hair?

To prevent spots after bleaching hair, be sure to apply the bleach evenly and avoid overlapping onto already bleached areas.

Hair Care After Fixing Spots

After fixing spots after bleaching hair, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to fix spots after bleaching hair, you can tackle the issue with confidence. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
