How To Cut Men s Hair Mullet A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and edgy hairstyle for men, the mullet is a great option. This classic haircut has been making a comeback in recent years with modern twists. However, getting a mullet haircut can be intimidating, especially if you haven't done it before. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of how to cut men's hair mullet step by step.

Description: What is a Mullet Haircut?

A mullet is a versatile haircut that features short hair on the top and sides and long hair at the back. The back of the hair can be cut at different lengths depending on the preference of the person getting the haircut. The mullet was popularized in the 1980s and was associated with rockstars and athletes. However, the modern mullet has evolved and is now a trendy and fashionable haircut for men.

How to Cut Men's Hair Mullet: Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Gather the Necessary Tools

The first step in cutting a mullet is to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, a clipper, and a trimmer. Make sure your tools are clean and sanitized before you start.

Step 2: Start with the Top and Sides

Begin by sectioning the hair on the top and sides of the head. Use the comb to create a clean parting and clip the hair away. Use the clipper to cut the hair to the desired length. You can leave it slightly longer if you want to create texture.

Step 3: Move to the Back

Next, move to the back of the head. Use the clipper to cut the hair at the desired length. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up. You can create a gradual or more drastic taper depending on the look you're going for.

Step 4: Feather the Hair

Once you've cut the hair at the back, it's time to feather it. Use the scissors to create texture by cutting into the hair at an angle. This will help blend the shorter and longer hair together for a seamless look.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Next, blend the hair together by using the trimmer to create a fade. Start at the bottom of the hair and work your way up, using different guard sizes to create a smooth transition.

Step 6: Create the Mullet Shape

Now it's time to create the distinctive mullet shape. Use the scissors to cut the hair at the back into a V shape. Make sure it's centered and even. You can also create layers for added texture.

Step 7: Refine the Shape

Once you've created the mullet shape, refine it by trimming any stray hairs and making sure the length is even. Use the scissors to blend the hair together for a seamless finish.

Tips for Cutting Men's Hair Mullet

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect mullet:

  • Use a mirror to check your work from all angles
  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter
  • Don't cut too much hair at once
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp and easier to cut
  • Take breaks to avoid fatigue and mistakes

FAQs

Can I Cut a Mullet at Home?

Yes, you can cut a mullet at home as long as you have the necessary tools and follow the steps carefully. However, if you're not confident in your skills, it's best to go to a professional hairstylist.

How Long Should the Hair at the Back of a Mullet Be?

The length of the hair at the back of a mullet depends on personal preference. It can be short, medium or long. However, make sure it's proportional to the rest of the haircut.

Hair Care for Men's Mullet Haircut

To maintain your mullet haircut, it's important to take good care of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type and style. Avoid using too much product as it can weigh down the hair and make it look greasy.

Cutting men's hair mullet can be a fun and exciting experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and modern look. Follow our step-by-step guide and take care of your hair for a perfect mullet hairstyle.
