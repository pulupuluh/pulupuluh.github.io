What To Say For A Military Haircut A Relaxed Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 26, 2023 Â· 10 min read

When it comes to getting a military haircut, knowing what to say to your barber or stylist is crucial. Whether you're in the military yourself or just looking for a clean, low-maintenance haircut, there are certain phrases and terms you should be familiar with to ensure you get the look you want. In this guide, we'll break down exactly what to say for a military haircut in relaxed English language.

Description

A military haircut is a short, simple style that's popular among men in the armed forces. It's also a popular choice for men who want a low-maintenance haircut that looks clean and professional. The style typically features short hair on the sides and back of the head, with slightly longer hair on top that can be styled in a variety of ways. Military haircuts are often referred to by specific names, such as a crew cut, high and tight, or buzz cut.

How to

When you go to your barber or stylist, you'll want to be clear about what type of military haircut you want. Here are some phrases and terms you can use to describe the style:

Crew Cut

A crew cut is a classic military haircut that features short hair on the sides and back of the head, with slightly longer hair on top that's usually styled with a little bit of product. To ask for a crew cut, you can simply say, "I want a crew cut."

High and Tight

A high and tight is a variation of the crew cut that features even shorter hair on the sides and back of the head, with the hair on top buzzed very short. To ask for a high and tight, you can say, "I want a high and tight."

Buzz Cut

A buzz cut is a very short military haircut that's easy to maintain and requires little styling. To ask for a buzz cut, you can say, "I want a buzz cut."

Fade

A fade is a haircut that features a gradual transition from short hair on the sides and back of the head to longer hair on top. To ask for a fade with a military-style haircut, you can say, "I want a high fade with a crew cut on top."

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a military haircut:

Step 1: Choose a Style

Before you go to the barber or stylist, decide on the type of military haircut you want. Look at pictures online for inspiration and choose a style that you think will look good on you.

Step 2: Find a Barber or Stylist

Look for a barber or stylist who has experience giving military haircuts. You can ask for recommendations from friends or look online for reviews.

Step 3: Communicate Clearly

When you sit down in the chair, be clear about what type of military haircut you want. Use the phrases and terms we've outlined above to describe the style.

Step 4: Follow the Barber's Lead

Once you've communicated what you want, let the barber or stylist do their job. They may make suggestions or adjustments based on your hair type and face shape, so be open to their advice.

Step 5: Maintain Your Haircut

To keep your military haircut looking sharp, you'll need to maintain it regularly. Depending on the length of your hair, you may need to get a trim every 2-4 weeks. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and avoid over-styling with products.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when getting a military haircut:

  • Bring a picture: If you're not sure how to describe the style you want, bring a picture to show your barber or stylist.
  • Communicate clearly: Use simple phrases and terms to describe the style you want, and be clear about any specific details like the length or fade.
  • Be open to suggestions: Your barber or stylist may have suggestions for how to adjust the style to better suit your face shape and hair type.
  • Maintain regularly: To keep your military haircut looking sharp, schedule regular trims and use quality hair care products.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about military haircuts:

What is a military haircut?

A military haircut is a short, simple haircut that's popular among men in the armed forces. It typically features short hair on the sides and back of the head, with slightly longer hair on top that can be styled in a variety of ways.

What are some popular military haircuts?

Some popular military haircuts include the crew cut, high and tight, and buzz cut.

How do I ask for a military haircut?

To ask for a military haircut, use simple phrases and terms like "crew cut" or "high and tight." Be clear about the length and style you want, and consider bringing a picture for reference.

How often do I need to maintain my military haircut?

Depending on the length of your hair, you may need to get a trim every 2-4 weeks to maintain your military haircut. Use quality hair care products to keep your hair healthy and avoid over-styling.

Hair Care

To keep your military haircut looking sharp, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid over-styling with products like gel or pomade, which can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Schedule regular trims to maintain your haircut and prevent split ends.
  • Be gentle when towel-drying your hair to avoid breakage and damage.

With these tips and phrases in mind, you should be able to confidently ask for a military haircut and maintain it for a sharp, professional look.
