How To Cut Medium Length Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair is beautiful and versatile, but it can be tricky to cut. If you have medium length curly hair and you're looking to give it a trim, you're in the right place. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut medium length curly hair step-by-step. We'll also share some tips and answer frequently asked questions to help you get the best results.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Medium Length Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Cut Medium Length Curly Hair

The first step in cutting curly hair is to make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section off your hair. It's also helpful to have a mirror or someone to help you with the back of your head.

When it comes to cutting curly hair, it's important to keep in mind that curly hair shrinks when it dries. This means that you should cut your hair a little longer than you want it to be when it's dry. You can always go back and trim more if needed, but it's harder to add length once it's been cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut medium length curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help you see your natural curl pattern and make it easier to cut.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles and divide it into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.
  3. Take one section at a time and clip the other sections out of the way. Start at the bottom of the section and trim 1/4 inch off at a time. Keep in mind that you want to cut your hair longer than you want it to be when it's dry.
  4. Repeat this process on all four sections, making sure to cut your hair evenly.
  5. Once you've trimmed all four sections, shake your hair out and see how it looks. If you need to trim more, go back and repeat the process.
  6. Once you're happy with the length, you can add some layers if you want. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle to create layers.
  7. Finish by styling your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Always cut curly hair when it's dry. This will help you see the natural curl pattern and prevent any surprises once it dries.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't use a razor to cut curly hair. This can cause damage and create frizz.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting curly hair can be time-consuming, but it's worth it for the results.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair:

Can I cut my own curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair, but it can be tricky. It's best to have someone help you with the back of your head.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to trim curly hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

Do I need to use any special products after cutting my curly hair?

It's a good idea to use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and prevent frizz.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair is important to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Don't brush curly hair when it's dry. This can cause frizz and breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage curly hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut your medium length curly hair with confidence. Remember to be patient and take your time, and you'll achieve beautiful results.
