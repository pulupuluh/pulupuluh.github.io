How To Do A Deva Cut At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly or wavy hair, you may have heard of the Deva Cut. This specialized haircut is designed to enhance the natural texture of your hair and create a more defined shape. While it's typically done by a trained stylist, you can also do a Deva Cut at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step, so you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home.

Description

A Deva Cut is a specialized haircut that's designed to enhance the natural texture of curly or wavy hair. It's typically done using a dry cutting technique, where the hair is cut curl by curl to create a more defined shape. This technique helps to prevent the shrinkage that can happen when curly hair is cut while wet, and it also allows the stylist to see the natural curl pattern and create a customized cut that works with it.

How to Do a Deva Cut at Home

While it's always best to see a trained stylist for a Deva Cut, it is possible to do it at home with the right tools and technique. Here's how:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

To do a Deva Cut at home, you'll need a few essential tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A wide-tooth comb
  • A mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean, and that you have a clean workspace set up before you begin.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. Once you're out of the shower, gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt. Avoid rubbing your hair dry, as this can cause frizz.

Next, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on low heat. Don't fully dry your hair, as you'll want to cut it when it's still slightly damp.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your wide-tooth comb, divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then divide each side into two or three sections, depending on the thickness of your hair. Clip each section up and out of the way, leaving one section at the bottom loose to begin cutting.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair Curl by Curl

Starting at the bottom of your hair, take a small section of hair that's about the width of your comb. Spray it lightly with water, then use your fingers to gently clump the curls together. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and cut the ends with your scissors, being careful not to cut too much off.

Continue working your way up your hair, cutting each section curl by curl. Make sure to cut each section evenly, and avoid cutting too much off at once.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've cut all the sections of your hair, use your mirror to check your work. Make any necessary adjustments to even out the length or shape of your hair.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

After you've finished cutting your hair, style it as you normally would. You can use a curl cream or gel to enhance your curls and create a more defined shape.

Tips for Doing a Deva Cut at Home

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the best results when doing a Deva Cut at home:

  • Start with clean, damp hair
  • Work in small sections to ensure even cutting
  • Use sharp, clean scissors
  • Check your work in the mirror frequently
  • Don't cut too much off at once
  • Take your time and be patient

FAQ

Can I do a Deva Cut on my own?

While it's always best to see a trained stylist for a Deva Cut, it is possible to do it at home with the right tools and technique. Just be sure to take your time and work in small sections to ensure even cutting.

What type of scissors should I use?

Use a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors that are designed specifically for cutting hair. Avoid using regular household scissors, as they may not be sharp enough and can cause damage to your hair.

How often should I do a Deva Cut?

This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Most people with curly hair can go about 3-4 months between Deva Cuts, but you may need to do it more or less frequently depending on your individual needs.

Hair Care After a Deva Cut

After you've done a Deva Cut, it's important to continue taking care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can cause damage to your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls moisturized
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy the benefits of a Deva Cut for months to come.
