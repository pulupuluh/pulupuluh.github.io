How To Cut Thick Curly Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of spending hundreds of dollars at the salon just to get a haircut for your thick curly hair? Do you want to save money and try cutting your hair at home? If you answered yes to these questions, then this article is for you! In this step-by-step guide, we will teach you how to cut thick curly hair yourself.

Description

Thick curly hair can be difficult to manage, especially if you have a lot of it. Going to the salon every few months can be expensive and time-consuming. Cutting your hair at home can save you money and give you the freedom to experiment with different styles. However, cutting thick curly hair is not easy, but with the right tools and techniques, you can do it yourself.

How to Cut Thick Curly Hair Yourself

Cutting your hair at home requires some preparation and practice. Here are some tips to help you cut your thick curly hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your thick curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will make it easier to comb and cut.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles and knots. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to avoid damaging your hair.
  3. Section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.
  4. Starting with one section at a time, trim the ends of your hair. Use hair cutting scissors or shears to cut your hair in a straight line. Cut small sections at a time to avoid making mistakes.
  5. Repeat the same process with the other sections of your hair.
  6. If you want to add layers to your hair, use the same technique but cut the hair at an angle. Start by cutting a small section of hair at a 45-degree angle and then cut the rest of the hair in the same way.
  7. After cutting your hair, comb it again to remove any stray hairs.
  8. If you want to style your hair, use a hairdryer or a diffuser to dry your hair. You can also use hair styling products to add volume or texture to your hair.
  9. Once you are done, clean up any mess and dispose of the hair properly.

Tips for Cutting Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you cut your thick curly hair:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors or shears. Do not use regular scissors as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a mirror to see the back of your head while cutting your hair.
  • Cut your hair when it is dry as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.
  • Do not cut too much hair at once as it can make your hair look uneven.
  • If you are not confident in cutting your hair, start with small trims and work your way up.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting thick curly hair yourself:

  • Can I cut my curly hair with regular scissors?
  • No, you should use hair cutting scissors or shears as they are designed to cut hair without damaging it. Regular scissors can cause split ends and damage your hair.
  • How often should I cut my curly hair?
  • It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to trim it. Generally, it is recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.
  • What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?
  • If you make a mistake, do not panic. You can always fix it by trimming the hair again or going to a salon to get it fixed.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Cutting your hair is just one part of the hair care routine. Here are some tips to help you take care of your thick curly hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet as it can cause breakage.
  • Use hair masks or deep conditioning treatments once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Now that you know how to cut your thick curly hair yourself, you can save money and experiment with different styles. Remember to be patient and practice, and you will soon become a pro at cutting your own hair!
