How To Cut Curly Hair Diy A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of paying a fortune at the salon just to get a haircut? Or maybe you're hesitant to go to a salon due to the pandemic? Whatever your reason may be, cutting your curly hair at home is not as challenging as it may seem! With the right tools, knowledge, and a bit of practice, you can give yourself a stylish haircut that suits your curls. In this blog post, we'll share some tips on how to cut curly hair DIY.

Why Cut Curly Hair DIY?

There are several reasons why you should try cutting your curly hair at home:

  • You can save money by avoiding expensive salon visits.
  • You can experiment with different haircuts and styles without worrying about the cost.
  • You can cut your hair whenever you want, without waiting for an appointment.
  • You can maintain your curly hair's health and shape by regularly trimming split ends and removing damaged hair.

However, cutting curly hair DIY requires some preparation and knowledge to avoid any mishaps. Let's see how to prep your hair for a DIY haircut.

How to Prep Your Hair for DIY Haircutting?

Before you start cutting your curly hair, you need to prep it properly. Here's what you should do:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to remove any buildup and detangle your curls.
  • Comb your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb to remove any knots and tangles.
  • Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or elastics.
  • Make sure you have good lighting and a mirror to see your hair clearly.

Now that you have prepped your hair let's look at the tools you need for cutting curly hair DIY.

Tools You Need for Cutting Curly Hair DIY

Here are the essential tools you need to cut curly hair DIY:

  • Sharp scissors: Invest in a good pair of sharp scissors made for cutting hair. Avoid using regular kitchen or paper scissors.
  • Clippers: If you want to give yourself a buzz cut or trim your hair's sides and back, clippers are handy.
  • Comb: Use a wide-tooth comb to comb your curls while cutting them.
  • Hair clips: Use hair clips or elastics to divide your hair into manageable sections.
  • Spray bottle: Fill a spray bottle with water to dampen your hair while cutting.
  • Mirror: Use a large mirror or a handheld mirror to see the back of your head.

Now that you have gathered the tools let's dive into the step-by-step guide for cutting curly hair DIY.

Step-by-Step Guide for Cutting Curly Hair DIY

Follow these steps to cut curly hair DIY:

  1. Start with clean, dry, and detangled hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: front, back, and two sides. Clip them separately.
  3. Start with the back of your head. Take a small section of hair, comb it, and hold it between your index and middle finger.
  4. Slide your fingers down the hair, away from your head, and cut the ends with scissors. Cut a little at a time, starting with half an inch, and then adjust as needed.
  5. Repeat the previous step with the rest of the back sections.
  6. Move to the sides of your head. Take a small section of hair, comb it, and hold it between your fingers. Cut the ends like before, making sure they blend well with the back sections.
  7. Repeat the previous step with the other side.
  8. Finally, move to the front sections. Comb your hair forward, and trim the ends evenly. You can also add layers or bangs at this point if you want.
  9. Once you have finished cutting, check your hair in the mirror to make sure it's even and balanced.

Now that you have cut your curly hair DIY, let's see some tips to make the process easier.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair DIY

Here are some tips for cutting curly hair DIY:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little at a time to avoid any drastic changes.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's soaking wet as it may shrink when it dries. Instead, cut it when it's damp or dry.
  • Use a misting spray bottle to dampen your hair while cutting. This will make it easier to handle and cut.
  • Always cut your hair in a well-lit area with a mirror to see your hair's back.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, start with small trims and work your way up.

Now that you know how to cut curly hair DIY, let's answer some frequently asked questions.

FAQs About Cutting Curly Hair DIY

1. How often should I cut my curly hair?

You should trim your curly hair every six to eight weeks to maintain its shape and health.

2. Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

It's best to cut your curly hair when it's damp or dry as it may shrink when it dries.

3. Should I use hair clippers or scissors to cut my curly hair?

It depends on the haircut you want. Use clippers for buzz cuts or trimming the sides and back of your hair. Use scissors for all other haircuts.

4. How do I cut layers in my curly hair?

To cut layers in your curly hair, take a section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut upwards at a slight angle to create layers. Repeat with the rest of your hair.

Now that we have answered some FAQs, let's see how to take care of your curly hair after cutting DIY.

How to Take Care of Curly Hair After Cutting DIY?

After cutting your curly hair DIY, follow these tips to take care of it:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to cleanse and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to nourish and define your curls.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase or use a satin hair bonnet to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a hair sunscreen or swim cap.

Now that you know how to take care of your curly hair after cutting DIY, let's wrap up this blog post.

Final Thoughts

Cutting curly hair DIY may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools, knowledge, and practice, you can give yourself a stylish haircut that suits your curls. Remember to prep your hair properly, use sharp scissors, and follow the step-by-step guide we shared in this blog post. Don't forget to take care of your curly hair after cutting DIY with regular trims and a good hair care routine. Happy cutting!
