How Do I Cut Layers In The Back Of My Hair

Written by Luffy Aug 01, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to add some volume and movement to your hair, then cutting layers is a great option. Layers can add texture and depth to your hair, making it look fuller and more dynamic. However, cutting layers in the back of your hair can be a bit tricky. In this blog post, we'll show you how to cut layers in the back of your hair step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layers in the Back of Your Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in the Back of Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care after Cutting Layers

How to Cut Layers in the Back of Your Hair

The first thing you need to do is decide what kind of layers you want. Do you want long layers or short layers? Do you want them to be choppy or smooth? Once you've decided on the style of layers, you can start cutting.

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. You may also want to have a mirror or someone to help you.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the natural fall of your hair and where the layers should be cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to section your hair. Divide it into three sections: the top, middle, and bottom. Clip the sections you're not working on out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start with the bottom section. Take a small section of hair and pull it straight down. Use the scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle. This will create a soft, gradual layer.

Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've cut the entire bottom section. Move on to the middle section and then the top section, cutting each section in the same way.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, it's time to blend them. Comb through your hair and use the scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a natural, seamless look.

Tips for Cutting Layers in the Back of Your Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to cut more hair later than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your hair as you cut. This will help you see if you're cutting evenly.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. This can result in uneven layers.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting layers can be tiring, so don't rush.

FAQs

Can I cut my own layers?

Yes, you can cut your own layers. Just make sure you have the right tools and take your time.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. On average, most people need to get their hair cut every 6-8 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care after Cutting Layers

After cutting layers in your hair, it's important to take good care of it. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. You may also want to use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

Now that you know how to cut layers in the back of your hair, you can experiment with different styles and looks. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and be patient. Happy cutting!
