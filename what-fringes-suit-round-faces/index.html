What Fringes Suit Round Faces A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you a round-faced beauty looking to spice up your hair game? If yes, you've come to the right place. Fringes are an excellent way to switch up your hairstyle and add some sass to your look. But with so many options out there, it can be challenging to know which fringes suit round faces best. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about fringes for round faces, including the best styles, how to choose the right one, and how to care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Choose the Perfect Fringe for Your Face Shape
  3. Step by Step Guide for Styling Your Fringe
  4. Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Your Fringe

Description

Round faces have soft, curved features, with the width and length of the face being roughly the same. The best fringes for round faces are those that help to elongate the face and create the illusion of angles. Avoid fringes that sit at chin-level or shorter, as they tend to emphasize the roundness of the face. Instead, opt for longer, side-swept styles that add dimension and create a flattering frame for your face.

How to Choose the Perfect Fringe for Your Face Shape

When choosing a fringe, it's essential to consider the shape of your face. For round faces, the goal is to create angles and elongate the face. Here are some of the best fringe styles for round faces:

Side-Swept Fringe

The side-swept fringe is an excellent choice for round faces, as it adds dimension and creates the illusion of length. To achieve this look, part your hair to the side and sweep your fringe to the opposite side, allowing it to fall just below your brow bone. This style works well with both long and short hair and can be worn straight or curled.

Long, Layered Fringe

A long, layered fringe can help to add angles to a round face. This style is achieved by cutting your fringe to sit just below your eyebrows and allowing it to blend into the rest of your hair. The layers help to create texture and movement, while the length adds dimension and elongates the face.

Asymmetrical Fringe

An asymmetrical fringe can also work well for round faces. This style is achieved by cutting your fringe at an angle, with one side longer than the other. The longer side should sit just below your brow bone, while the shorter side should sit just above your cheekbone. This style adds angles and creates a flattering frame for the face.

Step by Step Guide for Styling Your Fringe

Styling your fringe can be a breeze with the right tools and techniques. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the perfect look:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Using a comb, part your hair to the side.
  3. Take your fringe and sweep it to the opposite side, allowing it to fall just below your brow bone.
  4. Use a flat iron or curling wand to style your fringe as desired.
  5. Apply a small amount of hairspray to hold your fringe in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

Maintaining your fringe doesn't have to be complicated. Here are some tips to help keep your fringe looking its best:

  • Trim your fringe regularly to keep it at the desired length.
  • Use dry shampoo to keep your fringe looking fresh between washes.
  • Avoid touching your fringe throughout the day, as this can cause it to become oily and lose its shape.
  • Avoid over-styling your fringe, as this can cause damage and breakage.

FAQs

Q: How do I know if a fringe will suit my face?

A: The best way to know if a fringe will suit your face is to consult with a hairstylist. They can help you choose a style that complements your face shape and features.

Q: Can I still wear a fringe if I have curly hair?

A: Yes! Fringes can work well with curly hair, but it's essential to choose a style that suits your hair type and face shape. Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best fringe for you.

Hair Care for Your Fringe

Caring for your fringe is just as important as choosing the right style. Here are some tips to keep your fringe looking healthy and vibrant:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your fringe clean and fresh.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your fringe too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to your fringe to keep it soft and shiny.
  • Avoid wearing hats or headbands that can flatten your fringe.

Now that you know what fringes suit round faces, it's time to book that salon appointment and switch up your look. Remember to consult with a hairstylist to determine the best style for you, and don't forget to take care of your fringe to keep it looking its best.
