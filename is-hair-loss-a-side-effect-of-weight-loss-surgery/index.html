Is Hair Loss A Side Effect Of Weight Loss Surgery

Written by Bardi Jun 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're considering weight loss surgery, you may be wondering if hair loss is a potential side effect. The short answer is yes, hair loss can be a side effect of weight loss surgery. However, it's important to note that not everyone who undergoes weight loss surgery experiences hair loss, and for those who do, it's usually temporary.

Why Does Hair Loss Occur After Weight Loss Surgery?

The exact reason why hair loss occurs after weight loss surgery is not fully understood, but it's believed to be related to the rapid weight loss and changes in hormone levels that occur after surgery. When you lose a significant amount of weight quickly, your body may enter a state of shock or stress, which can cause hair follicles to enter a resting phase, leading to hair loss.

How Long Does Hair Loss Last After Weight Loss Surgery?

For most people, hair loss after weight loss surgery is temporary and resolves within six to twelve months. During this time, it's important to take good care of your hair and scalp to minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

How to Minimize Hair Loss After Weight Loss Surgery

While hair loss after weight loss surgery may be unavoidable for some, there are things you can do to minimize the amount of hair loss you experience and promote healthy hair growth.

Eat a Nutritious Diet

Eating a nutritious diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals is essential for healthy hair growth. After weight loss surgery, it's important to follow your doctor's dietary guidelines to ensure that you're getting the nutrients your body needs.

Take Supplements

Your doctor may recommend that you take supplements, such as biotin or zinc, to support healthy hair growth after weight loss surgery. Follow your doctor's recommendations and take any supplements as directed.

Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or relaxers, as these can damage your hair and scalp and contribute to hair loss.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing your hair in tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, as these can pull on your hair and contribute to hair loss.

Be Gentle When Brushing or Combing Your Hair

Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid pulling on your hair and causing damage. Use a wide-tooth comb and start at the ends of your hair, working your way up to the roots.

FAQ

Does Everyone Experience Hair Loss After Weight Loss Surgery?

No, not everyone who undergoes weight loss surgery experiences hair loss. Hair loss is more common in people who lose weight quickly or have a family history of hair loss.

Is Hair Loss After Weight Loss Surgery Permanent?

For most people, hair loss after weight loss surgery is temporary and resolves within six to twelve months. However, in rare cases, hair loss may be permanent.

When Should I See a Doctor About Hair Loss After Weight Loss Surgery?

If you're experiencing significant hair loss after weight loss surgery or if your hair loss is accompanied by other symptoms, such as scalp irritation or pain, it's important to see a doctor. Your doctor can help determine the cause of your hair loss and recommend treatment options.

Hair Care Tips After Weight Loss Surgery

While hair loss after weight loss surgery can be concerning, it's usually temporary and can be minimized with proper hair care. Follow these tips to promote healthy hair growth after weight loss surgery:

  • Eat a nutritious diet
  • Take supplements as directed by your doctor
  • Avoid harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair

By taking good care of your hair and scalp, you can minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth after weight loss surgery.
