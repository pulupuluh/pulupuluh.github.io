Haircut To Reduce Volume Of Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having big, voluminous curls that are difficult to manage? If so, you may want to consider getting a haircut that can help reduce the volume of your curly hair. In this guide, we will discuss everything you need to know about getting a haircut to reduce the volume of your curly hair. From how to prepare for your appointment to tips on how to care for your hair after the cut, we have got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare for Your Haircut
  • Types of Haircuts to Reduce Volume
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Haircut to Reduce Volume
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Hair After the Cut
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Prepare for Your Haircut

Before you head to the salon or barbershop, there are a few things you should do to prepare for your haircut. First, make sure you have a clear idea of the type of haircut you want. You can do this by researching different styles online or by consulting with your stylist.

It's also important to wash and condition your hair before your appointment. This will ensure that your stylist can see your hair's natural texture and can work with it more easily. Make sure your hair is completely dry before your appointment to avoid any miscommunication between you and your stylist.

Types of Haircuts to Reduce Volume

There are several types of haircuts that can help reduce the volume of curly hair. Some popular options include:

Layered Haircuts

Layered haircuts involve cutting the hair at different lengths, which can help to reduce volume and add definition to your curls.

Bob Haircuts

Bob haircuts are another great option for reducing volume. A bob haircut involves cutting the hair to a shorter length, which can help to weigh down your curls and make them appear less voluminous.

Pixie Haircuts

If you're looking for a more drastic change, you may want to consider a pixie haircut. This short style can help to reduce volume and make your curls more manageable.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Haircut to Reduce Volume

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a haircut to reduce volume:

  1. Consult with your stylist to determine the best type of haircut for your hair type and face shape.
  2. Wash and condition your hair before your appointment.
  3. Bring inspiration photos to your appointment to show your stylist the type of haircut you want.
  4. Communicate with your stylist throughout the appointment to ensure you're happy with the length and shape of your new haircut.
  5. Style your hair as desired after the cut.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair After the Cut

After you've gotten your haircut to reduce volume, there are a few things you can do to maintain your new style:

  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help enhance your curls without adding too much volume.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair and make it appear more voluminous.
  • Consider getting regular trims to maintain the shape of your new haircut.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I get my haircut to reduce volume?

This will depend on your hair type and the length of your new haircut. In general, you should plan on getting a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your new style.

Will a haircut to reduce volume make my curls less curly?

No, a haircut to reduce volume should not affect the curliness of your hair. It should simply help to make your curls more manageable and less voluminous.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

In addition to getting a haircut to reduce volume, there are a few things you can do to care for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair and make it appear more voluminous.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a more manageable, less voluminous hairstyle that allows your natural curls to shine.
