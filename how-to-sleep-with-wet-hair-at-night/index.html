How To Sleep With Wet Hair At Night A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 03, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Do you ever find yourself rushing to take a shower before bed but don't have time to dry your hair? Or maybe you prefer to air dry your hair but don't want to wake up with a tangled mess? Sleeping with wet hair can seem like a daunting task, but with these tips and tricks, you can wake up with beautiful, healthy hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Sleep with Wet Hair
  2. Tips for Sleeping with Wet Hair
  3. Frequently Asked Questions
  4. Hair Care Tips for Wet Hair

How to Sleep with Wet Hair

Before you go to bed, make sure to follow these steps to prepare your wet hair:

Step 1: Dry Your Hair as Much as Possible

Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water from your hair. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel as it can cause breakage and frizz.

Step 2: Apply Hair Oil or Serum

Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to prevent split ends and promote shine.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair. Start from the ends and work your way up to prevent breakage.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

If you have curly or wavy hair, you can use a hair gel or mousse to enhance your natural texture. If you have straight hair, you can use a hair wrap or hair clips to keep your hair in place.

Step 5: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can prevent your hair from becoming tangled and help maintain its natural oils.

Tips for Sleeping with Wet Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you sleep comfortably with wet hair:

  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair as much as possible.
  • Apply hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to prevent split ends and promote shine.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair to prevent breakage.
  • Style your hair before bed to prevent it from becoming tangled.
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent your hair from becoming tangled and help maintain its natural oils.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair in extremely cold temperatures as it can cause damage to your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it bad to sleep with wet hair?

It is not necessarily bad to sleep with wet hair, but it can cause damage and breakage if not properly prepared. Follow the steps above to ensure your hair stays healthy and beautiful.

Can sleeping with wet hair cause a cold?

No, sleeping with wet hair cannot cause a cold. Colds are caused by viruses, not by being cold or wet.

How long does it take for hair to air dry?

The time it takes for hair to air dry depends on the length and thickness of your hair. On average, it can take anywhere from 30 minutes to 2 hours.

Hair Care Tips for Wet Hair

Wet hair is more fragile and susceptible to breakage, so it's important to take extra care when handling it. Here are some hair care tips for wet hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using a regular towel to dry your hair as it can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Apply hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to prevent split ends and promote shine.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you can sleep comfortably with wet hair and wake up with beautiful, healthy hair.
