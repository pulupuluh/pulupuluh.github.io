How To Cut Teenage Guys Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Long hair has always been a popular choice for teenage guys. However, it can be difficult to maintain and style without regular haircuts. If you're a parent or a friend looking to help a teenage guy cut his long hair, this guide is for you. In this article, we'll cover the basics of how to cut teenage guys long hair, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Cut Teenage Guys Long Hair

Cutting long hair can be intimidating, especially if you're not a professional stylist. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give a teenage guy a great haircut at home. Before you get started, it's important to have the following items:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb or hairbrush
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • Clippers (optional, for trimming)

Once you have these items, you're ready to start cutting!

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to cut teenage guys long hair:

  1. Start by washing and drying the hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure a clean cut.
  2. Comb or brush the hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Section off the hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back.
  4. Starting with the top section, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the scissors to cut off the desired length, being careful not to cut too much at once.
  5. Continue cutting small sections of hair until the top section is complete.
  6. Move on to the side sections, using the same technique to cut the desired length.
  7. Finally, move to the back section, using the clippers (if desired) to trim the hair shorter.
  8. Check for any uneven spots or missed sections, and trim as needed.
  9. Use the spray bottle to wet the hair and comb through it to ensure an even cut.
  10. Dry and style the hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Teenage Guys Long Hair

Here are some additional tips for cutting teenage guys long hair:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter, as it's easier to trim more hair than to add it back.
  • Use clips to hold back sections of hair you're not currently working on.
  • Take breaks as needed to avoid hand fatigue.
  • Ask the person you're cutting for feedback throughout the process.
  • If you're unsure about a cut, start with a small section and work your way up.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting teenage guys long hair:

Can I cut long hair with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Hair cutting scissors are sharper and more precise, which will result in a cleaner cut.

Is it better to cut hair wet or dry?

It's generally better to cut hair when it's wet, as it's easier to control and less likely to split. However, if you're using clippers, it's better to cut hair when it's dry.

How often should I cut long hair?

It depends on the person's hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, aim to cut hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Advice

Here are some tips for caring for long hair after it's been cut:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add moisture and shine.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of the cut.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to confidently cut teenage guys long hair at home. Happy cutting!
