Why Is My Old Dog Losing Her Hair

Written by Daniel Mar 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have an old dog, you may have noticed that they are losing their hair. This can be a sign of a few different things, from allergies to hormonal imbalances. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your old dog might be losing their hair and what you can do about it.

Description

Older dogs are more prone to hair loss than younger dogs. The reason for this is that as dogs age, their bodies go through many changes that can affect their coat. Additionally, older dogs may have weakened immune systems or underlying health issues that can contribute to hair loss.

How to Diagnose Hair Loss in Old Dogs

If you notice that your old dog is losing their hair, it is important to take them to the vet for a diagnosis. Your vet will examine your dog and may run some tests to determine the cause of the hair loss. Some common tests that your vet may perform include:

  • Blood tests to check for hormonal imbalances
  • Skin scrapings to check for skin conditions
  • A biopsy to check for cancer

Once your vet has diagnosed the cause of the hair loss, they will be able to recommend a treatment plan.

Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Hair Loss in Old Dogs

The treatment for hair loss in old dogs will depend on the underlying cause. Some common treatments include:

  • Medications to treat hormonal imbalances
  • Antibiotics or antifungal medications to treat skin infections
  • Special diets to address allergies

In some cases, your vet may also recommend supplements or other treatments to support the health of your dog's coat and skin.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss in Old Dogs

While you may not be able to prevent all cases of hair loss in old dogs, there are some steps you can take to support your dog's overall health and reduce their risk of hair loss. These include:

  • Feeding your dog a high-quality diet
  • Providing regular grooming, including brushing and bathing
  • Keeping your dog up to date on their vaccinations and parasite prevention
  • Regularly monitoring your dog's skin and coat for changes

FAQs

Q: Is hair loss in old dogs normal?

A: While some hair loss is normal in older dogs, excessive hair loss can be a sign of an underlying health issue.

Q: Can allergies cause hair loss in old dogs?

A: Yes, allergies are a common cause of hair loss in old dogs.

Q: Can I treat my old dog's hair loss at home?

A: It is important to work with your vet to diagnose and treat your dog's hair loss. While there are some home remedies that may help support your dog's coat and skin health, these should not be used as a substitute for veterinary care.

Hair Care for Old Dogs

Older dogs require special care to support the health of their coat and skin. Some tips for caring for your old dog's hair include:

  • Brushing your dog's coat regularly to remove loose hair and distribute natural oils
  • Bathing your dog as needed with a gentle, moisturizing shampoo
  • Trimming your dog's hair around their eyes, ears, and anus to prevent irritation and infection
  • Providing your dog with a comfortable, warm place to sleep to prevent drafts and dry skin

By following these tips and working with your vet to address any underlying health issues, you can help keep your old dog's coat looking and feeling healthy.
