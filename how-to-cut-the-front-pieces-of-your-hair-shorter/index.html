How To Cut The Front Pieces Of Your Hair Shorter

Written by Christine Jul 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having long hair in the front that always gets in your face? Do you want to try out a shorter look but don't want to go to the salon? Cutting the front pieces of your hair shorter is an easy and quick way to change up your hairstyle. In this article, we'll go over how to achieve this look yourself, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Cutting the front pieces of your hair shorter can be a great way to frame your face and give your hair a new look. It's also an easy way to try out a shorter hairstyle without committing to cutting all of your hair. This technique works best for those with longer hair, as it allows you to maintain length in the back while still achieving a shorter look in the front.

How To

To cut the front pieces of your hair shorter, you'll need a few tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror

It's important to use sharp hair cutting scissors, as dull ones can lead to split ends and an uneven cut. Once you have your tools, you're ready to get started.

Step By Step

Follow these steps to cut the front pieces of your hair shorter:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Using your comb, separate the front pieces of your hair from the rest of your hair.
  3. Decide on the length you want to cut your hair. It's best to start with a small amount and gradually cut more if necessary.
  4. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and use your scissors to cut straight across at the desired length.
  5. Repeat on the other side, making sure to cut the same amount of hair for symmetry.
  6. Check in the mirror to make sure the length is even on both sides.
  7. You can also add layers to the front pieces of your hair by holding the hair at an angle and cutting into it with the scissors.
  8. Once you're happy with the length and style, use your comb to blend the front pieces of your hair into the rest of your hair.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting the front pieces of your hair shorter:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and gradually cut more if necessary. It's easier to cut more hair than it is to add it back.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid split ends and an uneven cut.
  • Use a mirror to check the length and symmetry as you go.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself, consider asking a friend or family member to help.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting the front pieces of your hair shorter:

Is it easy to cut your own hair?

It can be easy to cut your own hair, especially if you're just trimming the front pieces. However, if you're looking for a more drastic change or a complex cut, it may be best to go to a professional.

Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.

How often should I trim the front pieces of my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to maintain the length. As a general rule, getting a trim every 6-8 weeks can help prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut.

Hair Care

Once you've cut the front pieces of your hair shorter, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp healthy.

Cutting the front pieces of your hair shorter can be a fun and easy way to change up your hairstyle. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a stylish and flattering look in no time.
