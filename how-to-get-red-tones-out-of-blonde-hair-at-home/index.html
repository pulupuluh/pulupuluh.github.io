How to Get Red Tones Out of Blonde Hair at Home - Tips and Tricks

Are you tired of seeing red tones in your blonde hair? Don’t worry, you’re not alone. Many people struggle with getting rid of red tones in their blonde hair. In this article, we’ll show you how to get rid of red tones in blonde hair at home.

How to Get Red Tones Out of Blonde Hair at Home

The first thing you need to do is figure out what’s causing the red tones in your hair. If you recently dyed your hair blonde, it’s possible that the red tones are just leftover pigment from your previous color. If you haven’t dyed your hair recently, the red tones could be from mineral buildup in your hair.

The easiest way to get rid of red tones in blonde hair is by using a purple shampoo. Purple shampoo is specifically designed to neutralize yellow and orange tones in blonde hair. However, if your hair has a lot of red tones, purple shampoo may not be enough to completely get rid of them.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a step by step guide on how to get red tones out of blonde hair at home:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup in your hair.
  2. Apply the purple shampoo to your hair and let it sit for 5-10 minutes. Rinse thoroughly.
  3. If the red tones are still present, mix 1 part baking soda with 2 parts water and apply the mixture to your hair. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes and rinse thoroughly.
  4. If the red tones are still present, mix 1 part apple cider vinegar with 2 parts water and apply the mixture to your hair. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes and rinse thoroughly.
  5. Finish by conditioning your hair to prevent it from drying out.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to keep in mind when trying to get rid of red tones in blonde hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup in your hair before using purple shampoo.
  • Don’t leave purple shampoo in your hair for too long, as it can turn your hair purple.
  • Be careful when using baking soda and apple cider vinegar, as they can be harsh on your hair if used too frequently.
  • Consider using a toner to neutralize any remaining red tones in your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting rid of red tones in blonde hair:

Can I use lemon juice to get rid of red tones in my hair?

Lemon juice is not recommended for getting rid of red tones in blonde hair, as it can dry out your hair and cause damage.

How often should I use purple shampoo?

You can use purple shampoo once or twice a week, depending on how often you wash your hair.

Can I use regular shampoo instead of clarifying shampoo?

Clarifying shampoo is recommended, as it’s specifically designed to remove buildup in your hair.

Hair Care

After getting rid of red tones in your blonde hair, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you should be able to get rid of red tones in your blonde hair at home. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
