How To Wear Wig With Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 25, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have thick hair, you may be wondering how to wear a wig without it looking bulky or unnatural. Fortunately, there are a few tips and tricks you can use to make your wig look seamless and natural. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process step-by-step, from choosing the right wig to styling it for a flawless finish.

Choosing the Right Wig

The first step to wearing a wig with thick hair is to choose the right wig. Look for a wig that has a cap that is made of breathable material, such as lace or netting. This will allow your scalp to breathe and prevent your hair from getting too hot or itchy under the wig.

You should also look for a wig that has a lot of volume and texture, as this will help to blend in with your natural hair. Avoid wigs that are too flat or straight, as these can make your hair look bulky and unnatural.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you put on your wig, you’ll need to prepare your hair. Start by washing and conditioning your hair, as this will help to keep your scalp clean and healthy. It’s also a good idea to brush your hair out thoroughly, as this will help to prevent tangles and knots.

If your hair is very long or thick, you may want to consider braiding it or putting it in a ponytail before you put on your wig. This will help to keep your hair in place and prevent it from getting tangled or bulky under the wig.

Putting on Your Wig

Once your hair is prepared, you’re ready to put on your wig. Start by putting the wig on your head and adjusting the straps to fit securely. You should also use bobby pins or wig clips to secure the wig in place, especially around the hairline.

If your wig has bangs or a part, make sure to adjust them so that they look natural and blend in with your own hair. You may also want to use a wig cap or hair net to keep your hair in place and prevent it from poking through the wig.

Styling Your Wig

Once your wig is securely in place, you can start to style it. If your wig is made of synthetic hair, you should avoid using heat tools, as these can damage the fibers. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently style your wig.

If your wig is made of human hair, you can use heat tools to style it, just like you would with your own hair. However, be careful not to use too much heat, as this can damage the hair and cause it to break or become frizzy.

Tips for Wearing a Wig with Thick Hair

Here are a few tips to help you wear a wig with thick hair:

  • Choose a wig that matches your hair color and texture as closely as possible
  • Use bobby pins or wig clips to secure the wig in place
  • Use a wig cap or hair net to keep your hair in place
  • Avoid using heat tools on synthetic wigs
  • Be gentle when styling your wig to avoid damaging the fibers

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about wearing a wig with thick hair:

Can I wear a wig if I have thick hair?

Yes, you can wear a wig even if you have thick hair. However, you may need to prepare your hair differently and choose a wig that is designed to accommodate thick hair.

Will a wig make my hair look bulky or unnatural?

If you choose the right wig and prepare your hair properly, your wig should look natural and seamless. However, if your wig is too flat or straight, it may make your hair look bulky or unnatural.

Hair Care

When wearing a wig, it’s important to take care of both the wig and your natural hair. Here are some tips for keeping your hair and wig in good condition:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly
  • Use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to style your wig
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape
  • Avoid using heat tools on synthetic wigs
  • Protect your hair and wig from sun damage

With these tips and tricks, you can wear a wig with thick hair and achieve a natural, seamless look. Whether you’re looking for a wig for a special occasion or just want to switch up your look, a wig can be a fun and versatile option.
