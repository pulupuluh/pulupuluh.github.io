How To Thin Thick Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thick bangs, you may find that they can be difficult to manage and style. Fortunately, thinning your bangs can help make them more manageable and give them a softer, more natural look. In this article, we'll show you how to thin thick bangs in a step-by-step guide, along with some tips on how to care for your newly-thinned hair.

How to Thin Thick Bangs

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, you'll need a few tools: a good pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror. You may also want to have some hair clips on hand to help you section off your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off your bangs from the rest of your hair. Use hair clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Using your scissors, start at the outer edge of your bangs and work your way in, cutting small sections of hair at a time. Be sure to cut at an angle, rather than straight across, to avoid creating a blunt line. Take breaks to check your progress in the mirror and make sure your bangs are thinning out evenly.

Step 4: Blend Your Bangs

Once you've thinned out your bangs, you may notice that they look a bit choppy. To blend them in with the rest of your hair, use your scissors to make small, quick chops along the length of your bangs. This will help create a more natural, textured look.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

After you've thinned out your bangs, you may find that they're easier to style. You can blow dry them with a round brush to create volume, or use a straightening iron for a sleeker look.

Tips for Thinning Your Bangs

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when you're thinning your bangs:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Take breaks to check your progress and make sure your bangs are thinning out evenly.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short, as they will appear even thicker once they start to grow out.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you're unsure about how to thin your bangs.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning thick bangs:

How do I know if my bangs are too thick?

If your bangs are difficult to style, constantly falling in your face, or looking too heavy, they may be too thick. Thinning them out can help make them more manageable.

Can I thin my bangs with regular scissors?

While you can technically use regular scissors to thin your bangs, it's best to invest in a pair of hair cutting scissors. These are specially designed to make clean, precise cuts without damaging your hair.

How often should I thin my bangs?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick your bangs are. As a general rule, you may need to thin your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and style.

Hair Care Tips for Thinned Bangs

Once you've thinned out your bangs, it's important to take care of them properly to keep them looking healthy and stylish. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and nourished.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make it look dry and frizzy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your bangs looking neat and tidy.

Thinning thick bangs can be a simple and effective way to make your hair more manageable and stylish. By following these tips and using the right tools, you can achieve the perfect look for your hair type and personal style.
