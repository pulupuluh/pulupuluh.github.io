How to Tie Short Natural Hair

When it comes to tying short natural hair, there are many options to choose from. Some popular styles include:

  • Pineapple
  • Bantu knots
  • Twist-out
  • Braid-out
  • Afro puff

Each of these styles has its own unique look and can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. Below, we'll go over how to achieve each of these styles.

Pineapple

The pineapple is a popular style for those with short natural hair. It's simple to do and keeps your hair out of your face while you sleep.

To achieve this style, follow these steps:

  1. Gather your hair at the top of your head.
  2. Secure your hair with a scrunchie or hair tie.
  3. Fluff out your hair to create volume.

This style is perfect for those who want to maintain their curls or waves while they sleep. It's also a great option for those who want to keep their hair out of their face while working out.

Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a great option for those who want to achieve a curly or wavy look. This style is achieved by twisting sections of your hair into small knots.

To achieve this style, follow these steps:

  1. Divide your hair into small sections.
  2. Twist each section of hair tightly.
  3. Wrap each twisted section into a small knot.
  4. Secure each knot with a bobby pin.
  5. Leave the knots in for several hours or overnight.
  6. Remove the knots and fluff out your hair.

This style is perfect for those who want to achieve a curly or wavy look without using heat. It's also a great option for those who want to switch up their hairstyle from day to day.

Twist-Out

A twist-out is a popular style for those with short natural hair. This style is achieved by twisting sections of your hair and allowing them to dry before unraveling them.

To achieve this style, follow these steps:

  1. Divide your hair into small sections.
  2. Apply a styling product to each section of hair.
  3. Twist each section of hair tightly.
  4. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  5. Unravel each twist and fluff out your hair.

This style is perfect for those who want to achieve a defined curl pattern without using heat. It's also a great option for those who want to switch up their hairstyle from day to day.

Braid-Out

A braid-out is a great option for those who want to achieve a wavy or curly look. This style is achieved by braiding sections of your hair and allowing them to dry before unraveling them.

To achieve this style, follow these steps:

  1. Divide your hair into small sections.
  2. Apply a styling product to each section of hair.
  3. Braid each section of hair tightly.
  4. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  5. Unravel each braid and fluff out your hair.

This style is perfect for those who want to achieve a wavy or curly look without using heat. It's also a great option for those who want to switch up their hairstyle from day to day.

Afro Puff

The afro puff is a classic style for those with short natural hair. It's simple to do and can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion.

To achieve this style, follow these steps:

  1. Gather your hair at the top of your head.
  2. Secure your hair with a scrunchie or hair tie.
  3. Fluff out your hair to create volume.

This style is perfect for those who want to keep their hair out of their face while still looking stylish. It's also a great option for those who want to show off their natural curls or waves.

Tips and Tricks

Now that you know how to achieve different styles for short natural hair, here are some tips and tricks to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage while sleeping.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

FAQs

How often should I wash my short natural hair?

It's recommended to wash your hair every 1-2 weeks to prevent buildup and keep your scalp healthy.

How can I prevent shrinkage in my natural hair?

You can prevent shrinkage in your natural hair by stretching your hair using various methods, such as braiding or twisting your hair while damp.

What products should I use for my short natural hair?

You should use products that are specifically designed for natural hair, such as leave-in conditioners, styling creams, and oils.
