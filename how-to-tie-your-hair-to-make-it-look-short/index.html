How To Tie Your Hair To Make It Look Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel May 29, 2023 · 9 min read

If you have long hair but want to experiment with a shorter hairstyle, you don't need to run to the salon just yet. With a few simple tricks and the right hair ties, you can tie your hair to make it look short! In this blog post, we'll guide you through the process with step-by-step instructions and helpful tips.

How to Tie Your Hair to Make it Look Short

Before we get started, it's important to note that this trick works best on hair that is shoulder-length or longer. If your hair is shorter than that, you may not be able to achieve the desired effect.

To make your hair look shorter, you'll need to tie it up in a way that creates the illusion of a shorter length. There are a few different methods you can try, depending on the look you're going for:

The Half-Up, Half-Down Method

This method is great for creating the illusion of a bob. Start by dividing your hair into two sections - the top half and the bottom half. Pull the top half of your hair up and secure it with a hair tie at the back of your head. Leave the bottom half of your hair loose.

You can then use a straightening iron or a curling iron to add some waves or curls to the bottom half of your hair, which will help to create the illusion of a shorter length.

The Low Ponytail Method

This method is ideal for creating the illusion of a short, chic haircut. Gather all of your hair at the nape of your neck and secure it with a hair tie. Make sure the hair tie is tight and secure, so that your hair doesn't slip out.

You can then use a pair of scissors to trim the ends of the ponytail to the desired length. This will create the appearance of a shorter haircut, without actually cutting your hair.

The Messy Bun Method

The messy bun is a great option for creating a short, playful look. Start by tying your hair up in a high ponytail. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun, and secure it with another hair tie. Pull out some pieces of hair around your face to create a messy, undone look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the different methods for tying your hair to make it look short, let's go through the steps for each one:

The Half-Up, Half-Down Method

  1. Divide your hair into two sections - the top half and the bottom half.
  2. Pull the top half of your hair up and secure it with a hair tie at the back of your head.
  3. Leave the bottom half of your hair loose.
  4. Use a straightening iron or a curling iron to add some waves or curls to the bottom half of your hair.

The Low Ponytail Method

  1. Gather all of your hair at the nape of your neck and secure it with a hair tie.
  2. Make sure the hair tie is tight and secure.
  3. Use a pair of scissors to trim the ends of the ponytail to the desired length.

The Messy Bun Method

  1. Tie your hair up in a high ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun.
  3. Secure the bun with another hair tie.
  4. Pull out some pieces of hair around your face to create a messy, undone look.

Tips for Tying Your Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when tying your hair to make it look short:

  • Use hair ties that match your hair color, so that they blend in.
  • Don't tie your hair too tight, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Experiment with different methods to find the one that works best for your hair type and face shape.
  • Add some hair accessories, like barrettes or headbands, to enhance the look.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will tying my hair up too often cause damage?

Tying your hair up too often, or tying it too tight, can cause breakage and damage. Make sure to give your hair a break from tying it up, and use hair ties that are gentle on your hair.

Can I use this trick on short hair?

This trick works best on hair that is shoulder-length or longer. If your hair is shorter than that, you may not be able to achieve the desired effect.

Can I use this trick on curly hair?

Absolutely! In fact, curly hair can create a more natural-looking short hairstyle when tied up in a bun or ponytail.

Hair Care for Tied Hair

Tying your hair up frequently can cause damage and breakage, so it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, tied-up hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid tying your hair up when it's wet, as this can cause even more damage.
  • Use a gentle hair tie, like a scrunchie, to avoid breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can tie your hair to make it look short and experiment with different hairstyles without committing to a drastic haircut. Have fun and play around with different looks!
