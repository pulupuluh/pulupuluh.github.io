Why Does My Hair Go Frizzy When I Blow Dry It

Written by Robby Jun 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Blow drying your hair can be a quick and easy way to achieve a sleek and polished look. However, for many people, blow drying also means dealing with frizzy hair. If you're one of those people who struggle with frizz after blow drying, you're not alone. In this article, we'll explain why your hair goes frizzy when you blow dry it and what you can do to prevent it.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Frizz happens when the cuticles of your hair strands lift up, allowing moisture to get in and cause the hair to swell. Blow drying can contribute to frizz because the high heat and air flow can dry out your hair, causing it to become brittle and more prone to breakage. Additionally, if you're not using a heat protectant or if you're using the wrong styling products, your hair can become even more vulnerable to frizz.

How to

To prevent frizz when blow drying your hair, there are a few things you can do:

Use a Heat Protectant

A heat protectant spray or serum will help to shield your hair from the high heat of your blow dryer. Apply it evenly throughout your hair before you start blow drying.

Choose the Right Styling Products

Some styling products can actually contribute to frizz. Look for products that are designed specifically for frizz control, such as smoothing serums or creams.

Use a Diffuser Attachment

A diffuser attachment can help to distribute the heat more evenly and reduce the amount of air flow, which can help to prevent frizz.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to blow drying your hair without causing frizz:

  1. Start with clean, towel-dried hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum.
  3. Divide your hair into sections.
  4. Attach a diffuser to your blow dryer.
  5. Start blow drying your hair on the lowest heat setting.
  6. Use a round brush to smooth out each section of hair as you blow dry it.
  7. Once you've finished blow drying, apply a smoothing serum or cream.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent frizz when blow drying your hair:

  • Don't blow dry your hair when it's soaking wet. Use a towel to gently blot excess moisture before you start.
  • Don't rub your hair vigorously with a towel. This can cause the cuticles to lift and contribute to frizz.
  • Don't use a brush with plastic bristles. Opt for a brush with natural bristles instead.
  • Avoid using a high heat setting on your blow dryer. Stick to the lowest heat setting that will still effectively dry your hair.

FAQ

Q: Is it better to air dry or blow dry your hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Air drying can be a gentler option, but it can take longer and may not give you the polished look that blow drying can.

Q: Can blow drying my hair too often cause damage?

A: Yes, blow drying your hair too often or using high heat settings can cause damage, including split ends and breakage.

Q: Can I still use a blow dryer if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, but you may want to use a diffuser attachment to help maintain your curls and reduce frizz.

Hair Care

In addition to using the right products and techniques when blow drying your hair, it's important to take good care of your hair overall. This includes:

  • Using a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoiding excessive heat styling
  • Getting regular trims to prevent split ends

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can prevent frizz when blow drying and keep your hair looking healthy and polished.
