How To Cut Your Hair Shorter In The Back A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle or simply want to try something new, cutting your hair shorter in the back can be a great option. Not only is it a chic and trendy style, but it can also help to add volume and texture to your hair. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your hair shorter in the back, along with some helpful tips and FAQs to ensure you get the perfect look.

How to Cut Your Hair Shorter in the Back

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be tricky and may not always turn out the way you want it to. If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, it's best to visit a professional stylist. However, if you're feeling confident and want to give it a try, here's how to cut your hair shorter in the back:

1. Gather Your Supplies

You'll need a few basic supplies to cut your hair, including:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean, as dull scissors can lead to uneven cuts and damage to your hair.

2. Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may not give you the desired length you're looking for. So, wash and dry your hair thoroughly before you start cutting.

3. Section Your Hair

Use the comb to section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. Clip the top part of your hair on top of your head, away from the bottom part.

4. Cut the Bottom Part of Your Hair

Starting at the nape of your neck, comb out a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the scissors to cut the hair at the desired length, keeping your fingers as a guide to ensure an even cut. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head.

5. Blend the Top Part of Your Hair

Once you've cut the bottom part of your hair, unclip the top part and blend it with the bottom part. Comb out a small section of hair from the top and hold it between your fingers. Use the scissors to cut the hair at the same length as the bottom part, blending the two sections together. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way around your head until you've achieved the desired length.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting your hair shorter in the back:

  1. Gather your supplies: sharp scissors, comb, hair clips, and mirror.
  2. Wash and dry your hair.
  3. Section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom.
  4. Cut the bottom part of your hair, starting at the nape of your neck and working your way up to the crown of your head.
  5. Unclip the top part of your hair and blend it with the bottom part, cutting small sections of hair at the same length.
  6. Check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair Shorter in the Back

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair shorter in the back:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up, rather than cutting too much all at once.
  • Use your fingers as a guide to ensure an even cut.
  • Check your hair in a mirror from all angles to ensure an even cut.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, it's best to seek the help of a professional stylist.

FAQs About Cutting Your Hair Shorter in the Back

Can I cut my hair shorter in the back without cutting the rest of my hair?

Yes, you can! Simply section off the back part of your hair and cut it to the desired length, leaving the rest of your hair untouched.

Can I cut my hair shorter in the back with a bob cut?

Definitely! A bob cut is a great style for cutting your hair shorter in the back. You can opt for a classic bob, an inverted bob, or a graduated bob, depending on your preference.

How often should I cut my hair if I want to maintain a shorter length in the back?

It's best to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain the length and shape of your hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair Shorter in the Back

After cutting your hair shorter in the back, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

That's it! We hope this guide on how to cut your hair shorter in the back has been helpful. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to seek the help of a professional if you're unsure about cutting your own hair. Happy cutting!
