How To Get Thick Straight Hair Curly A Step By Step Guide

Having straight hair can be a blessing, but sometimes we feel like changing our look and adding some curls to our hair. If you have thick straight hair, you might think that getting curls is impossible, but we're here to tell you that it's not. In this article, we'll show you how to get thick straight hair curly in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

  • How to Get Thick Straight Hair Curly

How to Get Thick Straight Hair Curly

Before we start, it's important to note that there are different ways to get curly hair, and what works for one person might not work for another. However, we'll show you the most common and effective ways to get curls in your thick straight hair:

  • Using a curling iron
  • Using hot rollers
  • Using a straightener
  • Using a perm

Step-by-Step Guide

Using a Curling Iron

1. Start by washing your hair and blow-drying it until it's fully dry.

2. Divide your hair into small sections.

3. Take a section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron, starting from the roots and going towards the ends.

4. Hold the curling iron in place for 10-15 seconds, then release the hair.

5. Repeat the process on all sections of your hair.

6. Once you're done curling all your hair, shake your head a bit to loosen the curls and apply some hairspray to hold them in place.

Using Hot Rollers

1. Wash your hair and blow-dry it until it's fully dry.

2. Divide your hair into sections.

3. Take a section of hair and wrap it around a hot roller, starting from the ends and going towards the roots.

4. Secure the hot roller in place with a clip or a bobby pin.

5. Repeat the process on all sections of your hair.

6. Leave the hot rollers in your hair for 20-30 minutes.

7. Once you're done, remove the hot rollers and shake your head a bit to loosen the curls.

8. Apply some hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Using a Straightener

1. Wash your hair and blow-dry it until it's fully dry.

2. Divide your hair into small sections.

3. Take a section of hair and clamp it with a straightener at the roots.

4. Twist the straightener away from your face and pull it down towards the ends of your hair.

5. Repeat the process on all sections of your hair.

6. Once you're done, shake your head a bit to loosen the curls and apply some hairspray to hold them in place.

Using a Perm

1. Go to a salon and ask for a perm. A perm is a chemical treatment that adds curls to your hair.

2. The stylist will apply a perm solution to your hair and wrap it around rods.

3. The perm solution will break the bonds in your hair and reshape them into curls.

4. After a few hours, the rods will be removed, and your hair will be rinsed and styled.

Tips for Getting Curly Hair

Getting curly hair can be tricky, especially if you have thick straight hair. Here are some tips that can help you get the best results:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from damage.
  • Don't use too much hairspray, as it can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.
  • Experiment with different curling techniques to find what works best for you.
  • Don't curl your hair too often, as it can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

FAQ

Can I get curly hair without heat?

Yes, you can get curly hair without heat by using hair rollers or braiding your hair overnight.

How long do the curls last?

The duration of the curls depends on the method you use and your hair type. Curls created with a perm can last for several months, while curls created with a curling iron or hot rollers can last for a day or two.

How can I make my curls last longer?

You can make your curls last longer by using a strong-hold hairspray, sleeping on a silk pillowcase, and avoiding touching your hair too much.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires different care than straight hair, as it tends to be drier and more prone to frizz. Here are some tips for taking care of curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.

Now that you know how to get thick straight hair curly, it's time to try it out and see how it looks on you. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
