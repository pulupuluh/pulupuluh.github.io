Understanding Hair Thinning After Stopping Accutane

Written by Aprilia Sep 14, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have recently stopped taking Accutane, you may have noticed some changes in your hair. One of the most common side effects of Accutane is hair thinning. While this side effect is usually temporary, it can be alarming and frustrating for those who experience it. In this article, we will discuss what causes hair thinning after stopping Accutane and provide tips on how to manage it.

What Causes Hair Thinning After Stopping Accutane?

The exact cause of hair thinning after stopping Accutane is not fully understood. However, it is believed to be related to the drug's effect on the sebaceous glands in the scalp. Accutane reduces the production of oil in the sebaceous glands, which can lead to dryness and irritation of the scalp. This can cause hair to become brittle and weak, leading to breakage and thinning.

How to Manage Hair Thinning After Stopping Accutane?

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

If you are experiencing hair thinning after stopping Accutane, it is important to be gentle with your hair. Avoid using harsh hair products or styling tools that can further damage your hair. Instead, use gentle shampoos and conditioners that are designed for thinning hair, and avoid heat styling whenever possible.

2. Increase Your Nutrient Intake

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in nutrients can help promote hair growth and prevent hair thinning. Make sure to include foods that are high in protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and E in your diet.

3. Consider Hair Supplements

If you are not getting enough nutrients from your diet, you may want to consider taking hair supplements. Look for supplements that contain biotin, zinc, and iron, as these nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth.

4. Manage Your Stress Levels

Stress can have a negative impact on your overall health, including your hair. If you are experiencing hair thinning after stopping Accutane, it is important to manage your stress levels. Try incorporating relaxation techniques such as meditation or yoga into your daily routine.

5. Talk to Your Doctor

If you are experiencing severe hair thinning after stopping Accutane, it is important to talk to your doctor. They may be able to recommend treatments or medications that can help promote hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is hair thinning after stopping Accutane permanent?

No, hair thinning after stopping Accutane is usually temporary. In most cases, hair will begin to grow back within a few months after stopping the medication.

2. Can I prevent hair thinning while taking Accutane?

While it is not always possible to prevent hair thinning while taking Accutane, there are some things you can do to minimize the risk. Be gentle with your hair, avoid using harsh hair products, and eat a balanced diet that is rich in nutrients.

3. How long does it take for hair to grow back after stopping Accutane?

It can take several months for hair to grow back after stopping Accutane. However, the exact timeline can vary depending on the individual.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Hair

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Look for shampoos and conditioners that are designed for thinning hair. These products are usually formulated with ingredients that help promote hair growth and prevent breakage.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can further damage brittle hair, leading to breakage and thinning. Try to avoid using heat styling tools whenever possible, or use them on a low heat setting.

3. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair

Brush or comb your hair gently to avoid pulling or breaking it. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush that is designed for thinning hair.

4. Protect your hair from the sun

Exposure to the sun's UV rays can damage hair and lead to breakage and thinning. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

5. Consider hair extensions or a hairpiece

If you are self-conscious about your thinning hair, you may want to consider using hair extensions or a hairpiece. These can help add volume and thickness to your hair, giving you a more youthful appearance.


In conclusion, hair thinning after stopping Accutane is a common side effect that can be managed with the right treatment and care. By being gentle with your hair, increasing your nutrient intake, managing your stress levels, and talking to your doctor, you can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent further thinning.
