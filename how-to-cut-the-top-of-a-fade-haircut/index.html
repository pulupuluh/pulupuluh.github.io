How To Cut The Top Of A Fade Haircut

Written by Aprilia Jul 16, 2023 · 8 min read
How to Cut the Top of a Fade Haircut | Step by Step Guide

Fade haircuts have been a popular style for men for many years. They’re versatile, low maintenance, and can be customized to suit any head shape and hair type. However, cutting the top of a fade haircut can be tricky if you don’t know what you’re doing. In this step by step guide, we’ll show you how to cut the top of a fade haircut like a pro.

How to Cut the Top of a Fade Haircut

The top of a fade haircut is the most visible part, so it’s important to get it right. Here’s how to do it:

1. Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair on top of the head. Use a comb to create a parting from the forehead to the crown, then clip the hair on the sides and back out of the way.

2. Choose the Right Length

Determine the length you want to cut the hair on top. This will depend on the style you’re going for and the length of the hair on the sides and back. Use scissors or clippers to cut the hair to the desired length.

3. Blend the Top and Sides

Using clippers, blend the hair on top with the hair on the sides. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to achieve a gradual fade.

4. Check for Evenness

Check for evenness by running your fingers through the hair and looking for any longer or shorter spots. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure a clean, even cut.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step by step guide to cutting the top of a fade haircut:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure a clean, even result.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Using a comb, create a parting from the forehead to the crown, then clip the hair on the sides and back out of the way.

Step 3: Determine the Length

Determine the length you want to cut the hair on top. Use scissors or clippers to cut the hair to the desired length.

Step 4: Blend the Top and Sides

Using clippers, blend the hair on top with the hair on the sides. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to achieve a gradual fade.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

Check for evenness by running your fingers through the hair and looking for any longer or shorter spots. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure a clean, even cut.

Step 6: Style the Hair

Once the cut is complete, style the hair as desired using a hair dryer and styling products.

Tips for Cutting the Top of a Fade Haircut

Here are some tips to help you achieve a great result:

  • Use a comb to create clean, even partings.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to achieve a gradual fade.
  • Check for evenness by running your fingers through the hair and looking for any longer or shorter spots.
  • Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that may have been missed by the clippers.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting the top of a fade haircut:

What length should I cut the hair on top?

The length you cut the hair on top will depend on the style you’re going for and the length of the hair on the sides and back. It’s best to consult with your client or refer to a style guide for guidance.

What’s the best way to blend the top and sides?

Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to achieve a gradual fade. Use a comb to blend the hair on top with the hair on the sides, and make any necessary adjustments to ensure a clean, even cut.

Hair Care Tips for a Fade Haircut

Here are some tips to help maintain a fade haircut:

  • Wash your hair regularly to remove product buildup and keep the scalp clean.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, which can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Visit your barber regularly for touch-ups and maintenance.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut the top of a fade haircut and achieve a clean, stylish result.
