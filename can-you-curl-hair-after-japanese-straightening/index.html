Can You Curl Hair After Japanese Straightening

Written by Oliver May 23, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you are considering getting a Japanese straightening treatment but are worried about not being able to curl your hair anymore, you are not alone. This is a common concern for many women who want to keep their styling options open. The good news is that you can curl your hair after Japanese straightening, but there are a few things you need to know to ensure that your curls look great and your hair stays healthy.

How to Curl Hair After Japanese Straightening

If you have recently had a Japanese straightening treatment, you need to wait at least two weeks before you attempt to curl your hair. This will give your hair enough time to adjust to the new texture and reduce the risk of damage. Once the two weeks have passed, you can start curling your hair using the following steps:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start curling your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to protect it from heat damage. Divide your hair into sections, depending on how you want to curl it.

Step 2: Curl Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and wrap it around a curling iron or wand. Hold the hair in place for a few seconds and release. Repeat this process for the rest of your hair until you have achieved your desired look.

Step 3: Finish with Hairspray

Once you have curled your hair, finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Tips for Curling Hair After Japanese Straightening

Here are some tips to help you curl your hair after Japanese straightening:

  • Use a curling iron or wand with adjustable temperature settings to avoid overheating your hair.
  • Start with a low temperature and gradually increase it until you find the right temperature for your hair.
  • Use a curling iron or wand with a ceramic or tourmaline coating to distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using metal hair accessories that can damage your hair.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray, as it can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

FAQ

Can I curl my hair the same day I get Japanese straightening?

No, you should wait at least two weeks before curling your hair after Japanese straightening to avoid damaging your hair.

What type of curling iron should I use after Japanese straightening?

You should use a curling iron or wand with adjustable temperature settings, a ceramic or tourmaline coating, and a barrel size that suits the type of curls you want to achieve.

Can I use hair products after Japanese straightening?

Yes, you can use hair products after Japanese straightening, but make sure they are suitable for your hair type and do not contain harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

Hair Care After Japanese Straightening

After Japanese straightening, it is important to take good care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips to help you care for your hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

Now that you know that you can curl your hair after Japanese straightening, you can enjoy the best of both worlds. With the right tools and techniques, you can create beautiful curls that complement your new straightened hair. Just remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
