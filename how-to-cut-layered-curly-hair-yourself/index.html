How To Cut Layered Curly Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it looks great when well-maintained, it can be challenging to style and maintain. And when it comes to getting a haircut, it can be tough to find a stylist who understands how to cut curly hair properly. That's why many curly-haired people prefer to cut their hair themselves. In this article, we'll show you how to cut layered curly hair yourself in a few simple steps.

Description

The method we'll be discussing is suitable for people with naturally curly hair, whether it's loose waves or tight coils. The process involves creating layers to add volume and movement to your hair. Layered haircuts are ideal for curly hair because they help to prevent the weight of the hair from pulling the curls down.

How to Cut Layered Curly Hair Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair-cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair as you cut.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or using a diffuser to dry it gently. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry because curly hair shrinks when wet, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from one of the back sections and twist it tightly. Hold the twisted hair between your fingers and cut the ends with the scissors. Be sure to cut in a straight line, and don't cut too much at once. Repeat this process on all the sections of your hair, working your way from the back to the front.

Step 5: Create Layers

Once you've cut all your hair to the desired length, it's time to create layers. Take a small section of hair from the front of your hair and twist it tightly. Hold the twisted hair between your fingers and cut the ends. This time, instead of cutting straight across, cut at an angle to create layers. Repeat this process on all the sections of your hair until you've created the desired layers.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

If you notice any harsh lines or unevenness in your layers, use the scissors to blend them in. Take small sections of hair and snip away any uneven ends until the layers blend seamlessly into each other.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting layered curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little at a time.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to unevenness.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your hair as you cut.
  • Be patient and take your time.

FAQ

Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

It's not recommended to cut curly hair when it's wet because it can shrink when it dries, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every three to four months to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my hair when it's straight or curly?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry because you can see how the curls fall and cut accordingly. Cutting curly hair when it's straight can lead to unevenness and an unflattering cut.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's essential to take care of it properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair to keep your curls hydrated and healthy. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser to dry it gently. And remember to trim your hair every few months to keep it looking its best.
