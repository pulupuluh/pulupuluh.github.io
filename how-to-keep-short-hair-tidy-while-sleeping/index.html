How To Keep Short Hair Tidy While Sleeping Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Jul 26, 2023 · 10 min read

Short hair can be easy to style and maintain during the day, but what about when it comes time to hit the hay? If you're tired of waking up with a messy mane, don't worry – there are plenty of ways to keep your short hair looking neat and tidy while you sleep. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for keeping your hair looking great, even after a night's rest.

Table of Contents

  • How to Keep Short Hair Tidy While Sleeping
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Short Hair

How to Keep Short Hair Tidy While Sleeping

Here are some ways to keep your short hair looking neat and tidy while you sleep:

1. Tie Your Hair Up

The easiest way to keep your hair in place while you sleep is to tie it up. Use a soft, fabric hair tie to avoid damaging your hair or causing breakage. A loose bun or ponytail can keep your hair from getting tangled while you sleep.

2. Use a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and tangles in your hair, leading to a messy look in the morning. A silk or satin pillowcase, on the other hand, is gentle on your hair and can help prevent breakage and frizz.

3. Sleep on Your Back

If you're a chronic stomach or side sleeper, you may notice that your hair gets flattened or tangled as you sleep. Try sleeping on your back instead, which can help keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

4. Apply Product Before Bed

If you're prone to frizz or flyaways, apply a small amount of product – such as a leave-in conditioner or hair oil – before bed. This can help keep your hair smooth and hydrated while you sleep.

5. Wrap Your Hair in a Scarf

If you have especially short hair, you may find that tying it up doesn't work well. Instead, try wrapping your hair in a scarf before bed. This can help keep your hair in place and prevent tangles.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your short hair tidy while you sleep:

Step 1: Brush or Comb Your Hair

Before bed, make sure your hair is detangled and smooth. Use a brush or comb to gently remove any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Apply Product

If desired, apply a small amount of product to your hair. This can help keep it smooth and hydrated while you sleep.

Step 3: Tie Your Hair Up or Wrap it in a Scarf

If your hair is long enough, tie it up in a loose bun or ponytail. If not, wrap it in a scarf.

Step 4: Sleep on a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and tangles in your hair.

Step 5: Wake Up and Style

In the morning, remove the hair tie or scarf and style your hair as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for keeping your short hair looking neat and tidy while you sleep:

1. Avoid Tight Hair Ties

A tight hair tie can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Use a soft, fabric hair tie instead.

2. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help prevent frizz and keep your hair hydrated. Apply a small amount before bed.

3. Sleep on a Silk or Satin Bonnet

If you have very short hair, a silk or satin bonnet can help keep your hair in place while you sleep.

4. Experiment with Different Styles

If tying your hair up or wrapping it in a scarf doesn't work for you, experiment with different styles – such as braids or twists – to keep your hair in place while you sleep.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about keeping short hair tidy while sleeping:

Can I sleep with wet hair?

It's best to avoid sleeping with wet hair, as it can cause breakage and damage. If you must sleep with wet hair, use a silk or satin pillowcase and avoid tying it up tightly.

Should I brush my hair before bed?

Yes, it's a good idea to brush or comb your hair before bed to remove any knots or tangles. This can help prevent breakage and keep your hair looking neat.

What if I have curly hair?

If you have curly hair, you may find that tying it up or wrapping it in a scarf isn't the best option. Instead, try a silk or satin bonnet or experiment with different styles that work for your hair type.

Hair Care for Short Hair

To keep your short hair looking its best, make sure to follow a good hair care routine. This can include:

1. Regular Trims

Even if you're growing your hair out, it's important to get regular trims to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

2. Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. You may not need to wash your hair every day – try every other day or every few days instead.

3. Conditioner

Use a conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and smooth. You can also use a leave-in conditioner for extra hydration.

4. Protect from Heat

If you use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage. You can also try air-drying your hair instead of using a blow dryer.

5. Avoid Over-Brushing

Brushing your hair too much can cause breakage and damage. Use a gentle touch and avoid brushing your hair when it's wet or damp.

With these tips and tricks, you can keep your short hair looking neat and tidy even while you sleep. Try out different methods to find what works best for you and your hair type. Happy sleeping!
