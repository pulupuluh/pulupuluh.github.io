How To Cut Long Choppy Layers At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 12, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're looking to freshen up your long locks, choppy layers are a great way to add movement and dimension to your hair. And the best part? You can do it yourself at home! In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut long choppy layers at home. Here's what we'll cover:

Table of Contents

  2. How to Cut Long Choppy Layers at Home
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Cutting Long Choppy Layers at Home
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care After Cutting Choppy Layers at Home

Description

Choppy layers are a popular hairstyle that can be achieved by cutting the hair at different lengths. This creates a textured, layered look that adds volume and movement to your hair. It's a great option if you have long hair and want to switch up your look without losing length.

Cutting long choppy layers at home can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's totally doable. Plus, it can save you time and money compared to going to a salon.

How to Cut Long Choppy Layers at Home

To cut long choppy layers at home, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section off your hair

It's also helpful to have a mirror or someone to assist you with the back of your head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut long choppy layers at home:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Brush or comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Section off your hair. Use clips to separate your hair into sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up.
  3. Choose the length of your first layer. Hold a section of hair in front of you and decide how short you want the first layer to be. Cut the hair at a slight angle, starting from the back and working your way towards the front. Repeat on the other side to ensure evenness.
  4. Move up to the next section of hair. Take the next section of hair above the first layer and use it as a guide to cut the rest of the layers. Cut the hair at a slightly longer length than the previous section.
  5. Continue cutting layers. Repeat this process until you reach the top section of your hair.
  6. Blend the layers. Use your scissors to blend the layers together by cutting into the ends of the hair at an angle. This will create a softer, more natural look.
  7. Check for evenness. Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments.
  8. Style your hair. Once you're happy with the length and shape of your choppy layers, style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Long Choppy Layers at Home

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting long choppy layers at home:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to control the length and shape of your layers when you're working with smaller sections.
  • Cut less hair than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more, but it's harder to fix a haircut that's too short.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven layers.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and stressful, so don't be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.

FAQs

Can I cut choppy layers into curly hair?

Yes! Choppy layers can work well with curly hair, but you may want to cut the layers a bit longer than you would for straight hair to account for how much the curls will shrink up.

What if I mess up my haircut?

Don't panic! Hair grows back, and you can always go to a salon to have it fixed if you're not happy with the results. But chances are, with a little bit of practice, you'll get the hang of cutting your own hair.

How often should I cut my choppy layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain the length and shape of your layers. Generally, every 6-8 weeks is a good time frame to trim your layers.

Hair Care After Cutting Choppy Layers at Home

After cutting your choppy layers at home, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layers.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a fresh, new look by cutting long choppy layers at home. Happy cutting!
