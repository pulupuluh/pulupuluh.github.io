How To Get Bangs To Stay To The Side

Written by Petter Aug 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have bangs, you know how frustrating it can be when they won't stay put. Whether you're trying to create a side-swept look or just want to keep them out of your eyes, getting bangs to stay to the side can be a challenge. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect side-swept bangs.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Bangs to Stay to the Side
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Keeping Bangs to the Side
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Bangs

How to Get Bangs to Stay to the Side

The key to getting bangs to stay to the side is to train them to fall in that direction. This may take some time and effort, but with the right techniques, you can achieve the look you want.

1. Blow Dry Your Bangs to the Side

Start by blow-drying your bangs to the side with a round brush. Use a low heat setting and focus the dryer on the roots, pulling the brush through the hair as you go. This will help train your bangs to fall to the side and create a smooth, sleek look.

2. Use a Flat Iron

Once your bangs are dry, use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or bumps. Start at the roots and slowly glide the flat iron down to the ends, gently curving the hair towards the side you want it to fall. This will help set your bangs in place and keep them from flipping back over to the other side.

3. Apply Hairspray

To finish the look, apply a light mist of hairspray to keep your bangs in place. Hold the can about 12 inches away from your hair and spray in short bursts. Avoid applying too much hairspray, as this can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you get bangs to stay to the side:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Blow-dry your bangs to the side using a round brush.
  3. Use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or bumps.
  4. Apply a light mist of hairspray to keep your bangs in place.

Tips for Keeping Bangs to the Side

Here are some additional tips to help you keep your bangs to the side:

  • Use a high-quality flat iron to achieve a smooth, sleek look.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can cause them to become greasy and lose their shape.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo to help absorb excess oil and keep your hair looking fresh.
  • If your bangs start to fall out of place, use bobby pins to pin them back until you can fix them.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting bangs to stay to the side:

Q. How long does it take to train your bangs to fall to the side?

A. It can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to train your bangs to fall to the side. The key is to be patient and consistent with your styling techniques.

Q. What if my bangs are too short to stay to the side?

A. If your bangs are too short to stay to the side, consider using bobby pins or hair clips to hold them in place until they grow out a bit.

Hair Care for Bangs

Here are some tips for caring for your bangs:

  • Wash your bangs regularly to keep them clean and free of oil.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products on your bangs, as this can weigh them down and make them harder to style.
  • Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking neat and tidy.
  • Consider using a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or flat ironing your bangs.

With these tips and tricks, you can get your bangs to stay to the side and achieve the perfect side-swept look. Remember to be patient and consistent with your styling techniques, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and tools until you find what works best for you.
