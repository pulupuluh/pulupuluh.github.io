How To Cut My Own Fringe At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without breaking the bank, cutting your own fringe at home is a great option. Not only is it cost-effective, but it also gives you the freedom to experiment with different styles and lengths. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own fringe at home, as well as provide tips for maintaining your new look.

How to Cut My Own Fringe at Home

Before you get started, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before you start cutting. This will help you get a more accurate idea of how your fringe will look once it's styled. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section off the hair you want to cut. Depending on the style you're going for, you may want a thicker or thinner section of hair. Use your fingers to pull the rest of your hair back and secure it with a hair tie or clip.

Step 3: Cut Your Fringe

Hold the section of hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut it to your desired length. It's best to start with a longer length and trim as needed. Remember, you can always cut more off, but you can't add it back once it's gone.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

Release the section of hair and check your progress in the mirror. If your fringe is too long, trim it a bit more. If it's too short, you may need to wait for it to grow out or consider using hair extensions to add length.

Step 5: Style Your Fringe

Once you're happy with the length of your fringe, style it as desired. You can blow dry it with a round brush to create volume, or straighten it for a sleeker look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Fringe at Home

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your own fringe:

  • Start with a longer length than you think you want. You can always trim it shorter, but you can't add length once it's gone.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time. It's better to go slow and be precise than to rush and make mistakes.
  • Don't cut your fringe too short. It's better to err on the side of caution and cut less off than you think you need.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own fringe, consider practicing on a wig or mannequin head first.

FAQ

Can I cut my own fringe if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own fringe if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so you may want to cut it longer than you think you need.

How often should I trim my fringe?

How often you trim your fringe will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly you want to change up your style. As a general rule, aim to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Hair Care for Your New Fringe

Once you've cut your own fringe, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Shampoo and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your fringe too often. This can cause damage and split ends.
  • If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.
  • Avoid touching your fringe too much, as this can transfer oils from your skin onto your hair and make it look greasy.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own fringe at home and enjoy a fresh new look without the expense of a salon visit. Happy cutting!
