How To Cut Your Own Short Hair At The Back Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every time you need a haircut? Cutting your own hair might seem daunting, but it's not as complicated as it seems. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your own short hair at the back. We'll cover everything from how to section your hair to tips on achieving the perfect cut. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Sectioning your hair
  2. Equipment you'll need
  3. Cutting your hair
  4. Tips and tricks
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair care after the cut

Sectioning your hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's crucial to section it properly. This will help you get an even cut and avoid any mistakes. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by brushing your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles and knots.
  2. Divide your hair into three sections, using a comb or your fingers. The first section should be the crown of your head, the second section should be the sides, and the third section should be the back.
  3. Secure each section with a hair clip or an elastic band, so they don't get in the way when you're cutting.

Equipment you'll need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right equipment. Here's what you'll need:

  • A pair of sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A handheld mirror
  • Hair clips or elastic bands to section your hair
  • A clean towel to cover your shoulders

Cutting your hair

Now that you've sectioned your hair and gathered all the equipment, it's time to start cutting. Follow these steps to get an even and precise cut:

  1. Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair, about one inch wide, and hold it between your fingers.
  2. Use the scissors to cut the hair in a straight line, following the shape of your head. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as it's easier to cut more hair later than to add it back.
  3. Repeat the process, working your way through the back section of your hair.
  4. Once you're done with the back section, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair, about half an inch wide, and hold it between your fingers.
  5. Use the scissors to cut the hair in a straight line, following the shape of your head. Be careful not to cut too much, as it's easier to cut more hair later than to add it back.
  6. Repeat the process, working your way through the sides of your hair.
  7. Finally, move on to the crown of your head. Take small sections of hair, about half an inch wide, and cut them in a straight line, following the shape of your head.
  8. Once you're done with all the sections, check your hair in the handheld mirror to make sure it's even.

Tips and tricks

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve a great cut:

  • Start with small sections of hair, especially if you're a beginner. It's better to cut less hair at once than to cut too much.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't forget to check your hair in the handheld mirror to make sure it's even.
  • If you're not sure about the length, start with a longer length and cut more as you go.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

You can cut your hair when it's wet or dry, but it's easier to cut it when it's wet. Wet hair is easier to work with and less likely to move around, making it easier to achieve an even cut.

Do I need to wash my hair before cutting it?

It's not necessary to wash your hair before cutting it, but it's better to have clean hair so that you can see the natural texture and shape of your hair.

Is it possible to cut my hair without a mirror?

It's not recommended to cut your hair without a mirror, as it's difficult to see the back of your head. A handheld mirror is essential to ensure an even and precise cut.

Hair care after the cut

Now that you've cut your hair, it's essential to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for maintaining your short hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Don't use too much heat on your hair, as it can damage it. Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your cut.
  • Consider using hair masks or oils to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

That's it! Now you know how to cut your own short hair at the back. With the right equipment, proper sectioning, and some practice, you'll be able to achieve a great cut from the comfort of your home.
