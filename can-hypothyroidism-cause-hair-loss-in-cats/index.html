Can Hypothyroidism Cause Hair Loss In Cats

Written by William Apr 24, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're a cat owner, you likely know that cats are prone to losing their hair. However, if your cat seems to be losing more hair than usual, you may be wondering if there could be an underlying health issue causing the hair loss. One potential culprit could be hypothyroidism, a condition in which the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough thyroid hormone.

What is Hypothyroidism in Cats?

Hypothyroidism is a condition in which the thyroid gland, located in the neck, doesn't produce enough thyroid hormone. This hormone is responsible for regulating the body's metabolism, which affects everything from energy levels to hair growth. When a cat has hypothyroidism, their metabolism slows down, which can lead to a variety of symptoms, including hair loss.

How Does Hypothyroidism Cause Hair Loss in Cats?

Hypothyroidism can cause hair loss in cats because the thyroid hormone plays a role in the growth and maintenance of hair follicles. When there isn't enough thyroid hormone in the body, hair growth can slow down, leading to thinning or bald patches. Additionally, hypothyroidism can cause the skin to become dry and itchy, which can cause cats to scratch and groom excessively, leading to further hair loss.

What Are the Symptoms of Hypothyroidism in Cats?

In addition to hair loss, hypothyroidism in cats can cause a variety of other symptoms, including:

  • Weight gain
  • Lethargy
  • Increased appetite
  • Constipation
  • Depression
  • Coarse, dry fur
  • Brittle nails
  • Slow heart rate

How Is Hypothyroidism Diagnosed in Cats?

If you suspect that your cat may have hypothyroidism, the first step is to take them to a veterinarian. Your vet will perform a physical exam and take a blood sample to test for thyroid hormone levels. If your cat's thyroid hormone levels are low, they may be diagnosed with hypothyroidism.

How Is Hypothyroidism Treated in Cats?

If your cat is diagnosed with hypothyroidism, they will likely be prescribed a synthetic thyroid hormone supplement, which they will need to take daily for the rest of their life. This medication can help regulate your cat's metabolism and improve their hair growth.

How Can I Help Prevent Hair Loss in My Cat?

In addition to treating hypothyroidism, there are several things you can do to help prevent hair loss in your cat:

  • Feed your cat a healthy diet that is high in protein and nutrients
  • Make sure your cat is getting enough exercise
  • Regularly groom your cat to remove loose hair and prevent matting
  • Take your cat to the vet for regular check-ups

In addition to hair loss, hypothyroidism in cats can cause weight gain, lethargy, increased appetite, constipation, depression, coarse, dry fur, brittle nails, and a slow heart rate.

Hair Care for Cats

While hair loss can be a symptom of hypothyroidism in cats, it's important to remember that some degree of hair loss is normal for cats. However, if you notice excessive hair loss or other symptoms of hypothyroidism, it's important to take your cat to the vet for a check-up. By following a healthy diet, providing regular exercise, and grooming your cat regularly, you can help prevent hair loss and keep your cat healthy and happy.
