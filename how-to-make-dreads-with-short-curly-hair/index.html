How To Make Dreads With Short Curly Hair

Written by William Jun 29, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your short curly hair and want to switch things up with some dreadlocks? Making dreads with short curly hair might seem challenging, but it's possible with the right technique and tools. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of making dreads with short curly hair, step by step. We'll also provide you with some tips and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Dreads with Short Curly Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Making Dreads with Short Curly Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Make Dreads with Short Curly Hair

Making dreads with short curly hair involves a process called "twist and rip." This method is suitable for all hair types, including short curly hair. Here's what you'll need:

  • Dread wax or locking gel
  • Rubber bands
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • A crochet hook
  • A hair clip

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to make dreads with short curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Towel-dry your hair until it's damp but not dripping wet.
  3. Part your hair into sections using a fine-tooth comb. The sections should be as wide as you want your dreads to be.
  4. Take one section and twist it in one direction until it starts to form a knot.
  5. Hold the knot with one hand and use the other hand to rip the hair in the opposite direction. This will create a "ripped" look and help the hair knot up faster.
  6. Repeat steps 4 and 5 until the entire section is knotted up.
  7. Secure the end of the dread with a rubber band.
  8. Repeat steps 4-7 for all of the sections.
  9. Once all the sections are complete, use a crochet hook to pull any loose hairs into the dread.
  10. Apply dread wax or locking gel to each dread and palm roll them to help them lock up faster.
  11. Secure the dreads at the roots with rubber bands and leave them in for a few days to set.
  12. Remove the rubber bands and style your dreads as desired.

Tips for Making Dreads with Short Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you make dreads with short curly hair:

  • Be patient – making dreads takes time and effort, especially with short curly hair.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup before starting.
  • Use dread wax or locking gel to help the hair lock up faster.
  • Palm roll your dreads regularly to help them lock up faster.
  • Use a crochet hook to pull any loose hairs into the dread.
  • Don't wash your dreads for at least a week after making them to allow them to set.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make dreads with short curly hair?

Yes, you can make dreads with short curly hair using the "twist and rip" method.

How long does it take to make dreads with short curly hair?

The time it takes to make dreads with short curly hair depends on the length and thickness of your hair. It can take anywhere from a few hours to a few days.

Do I need to use dread wax or locking gel?

No, you don't need to use dread wax or locking gel, but it can help the hair lock up faster and make the dreads look neater.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you've made your dreads, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using conditioner, as it can loosen the dreads.
  • Avoid using heavy products, as they can weigh down the dreads and cause buildup.
  • Palm roll your dreads regularly to help them lock up faster.
  • Wrap your dreads in a silk or satin scarf at night to prevent frizz and breakage.

Now that you know how to make dreads with short curly hair, it's time to give it a try! Remember to be patient and take care of your dreads properly for the best results.
