How To Cover A Bald Spot With Hair Extensions A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

In today's world, hair extensions are not just a luxury item for celebrities. They are a common beauty accessory used by many individuals to enhance their hair's length, volume, and texture. Hair extensions can also be a great solution for people with bald spots and thinning hair. In this article, we will discuss how to cover a bald spot with hair extensions, step by step.

Description

A bald spot can be caused by many factors, such as genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and certain medical conditions. Whatever the reason, it can be frustrating and embarrassing for people who suffer from it. Hair extensions can provide a temporary solution to cover the bald spot and give the appearance of fuller, thicker hair.

How to Cover a Bald Spot with Hair Extensions

The first step in covering a bald spot with hair extensions is to choose the right type of extensions. There are many different types of hair extensions available, such as clip-ins, sew-ins, tape-ins, and fusion extensions. However, not all of them are suitable for covering bald spots. Tape-ins and fusion extensions, for example, require a certain amount of natural hair to attach to, which may not be possible for people with bald spots. Clip-in extensions are the best option for covering bald spots because they can be easily attached and removed without causing any damage to the natural hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cover a bald spot with clip-in hair extensions:

Step 1: Choose the right color and texture

Choose clip-in extensions that match your natural hair color and texture as closely as possible. This will help the extensions blend in and look more natural.

Step 2: Section the hair

Section the hair where the bald spot is located and clip it away from the rest of the hair. This will make it easier to attach the extensions to the natural hair without them slipping or sliding.

Step 3: Attach the extensions

Take a weft of hair extensions and attach it to the natural hair using the clips. Start at the bottom of the section and work your way up, making sure to space the wefts out evenly. You may need to trim the wefts to fit the size of the bald spot.

Step 4: Style the hair

Once all the wefts are attached, style the hair as desired. You can use a curling iron or flat iron to blend the extensions with the natural hair. Be careful not to apply too much heat to the extensions as this can damage them.

Tips for Covering a Bald Spot with Hair Extensions

Here are some tips to help you cover a bald spot with hair extensions:

  • Choose clip-in extensions as they are the easiest to attach and remove.
  • Match the extensions to your natural hair color and texture as closely as possible.
  • Section the hair where the bald spot is located and clip it away from the rest of the hair.
  • Start attaching the wefts at the bottom of the section and work your way up.
  • Trim the wefts to fit the size of the bald spot.
  • Style the hair as desired, but be careful not to apply too much heat to the extensions.

FAQs

Can hair extensions cause further hair loss?

No, hair extensions do not cause hair loss. However, if they are not applied or removed properly, they can cause damage to the natural hair.

How long can I wear clip-in hair extensions?

You can wear clip-in hair extensions for up to 8 hours a day. It is recommended that you remove them before going to bed to prevent damage to the natural hair.

Can I swim with clip-in hair extensions?

No, it is not recommended to swim with clip-in hair extensions as the chlorine and saltwater can damage them.

Hair Care for Hair Extensions

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair extensions:

  • Brush your extensions gently with a soft-bristle brush to prevent tangling.
  • Wash your extensions every 6-8 weeks with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools on the extensions as much as possible.
  • Store your extensions in a cool, dry place when not in use.

With these tips and tricks, you can cover a bald spot with hair extensions and feel confident and beautiful once again. Remember to choose the right type of extensions, attach them properly, and take good care of them to ensure they last as long as possible.
