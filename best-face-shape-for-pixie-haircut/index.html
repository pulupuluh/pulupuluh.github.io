Best Face Shape For Pixie Haircut

Written by Christine Jun 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're thinking about getting a pixie haircut, you may be wondering if it will work for your face shape. The good news is that there's a pixie style that can flatter just about any face shape. In this article, we'll take a look at the best face shapes for a pixie cut, as well as provide some tips on how to care for and style your new 'do.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Determine Your Face Shape
  2. Best Face Shapes for a Pixie Haircut
  3. Tips for Styling Your Pixie Cut
  4. FAQs about Pixie Haircuts
  5. Hair Care for a Pixie Cut

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the best pixie cut for you is to determine your face shape. Here are the most common face shapes:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond

To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back. Use a washable marker or lipstick to trace the outline of your face on the mirror. Step back and take a look at the shape you've drawn. You can also use a measuring tape to determine the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline.

Best Face Shapes for a Pixie Haircut

Now that you know your face shape, let's take a look at the best pixie cuts for each shape:

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, you'll want to create angles and length with your pixie cut. A layered pixie cut with longer layers on top and shorter layers at the sides can be very flattering. Avoid blunt cuts and heavy bangs, as these can make your face appear even rounder.

Oval Face Shape

Lucky you! Oval face shapes can pull off just about any pixie cut. You can go for a short and spiky cut or a longer, layered style. Experiment with different lengths and textures to find the look that works best for you.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, you'll want to soften your angles with your pixie cut. A layered pixie with side-swept bangs can be very flattering. Avoid straight, blunt cuts that accentuate your jawline.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, you'll want to balance out your forehead and jawline with your pixie cut. A layered pixie with longer layers at the sides can be very flattering. Avoid heavy, blunt cuts that accentuate your forehead.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, you'll want to create width at your temples and jawline with your pixie cut. A layered pixie with volume at the sides can be very flattering. Avoid heavy, blunt cuts that accentuate your cheekbones.

Tips for Styling Your Pixie Cut

Once you've found the perfect pixie cut for your face shape, it's time to learn how to style it. Here are some tips:

  • Invest in a good quality styling product, such as a pomade or wax, to help you mold and shape your hair.
  • Use a flat iron or curling iron to add texture and volume to your hair.
  • Play around with different parting styles to find the one that suits you best.
  • Consider adding highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your hair.

FAQs about Pixie Haircuts

Here are some commonly asked questions about pixie haircuts:

Q: Will a pixie cut work for my hair type?

A: Pixie cuts can work for just about any hair type, from straight to curly. Talk to your stylist about the best cut and styling options for your hair type.

Q: How often will I need to get my pixie cut trimmed?

A: Pixie cuts require regular trims to maintain their shape. You'll likely need to get a trim every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I still style my pixie cut for formal events?

A: Absolutely! Pixie cuts can be styled in a variety of ways, from sleek and polished to tousled and textured.

Hair Care for a Pixie Cut

Finally, let's talk about hair care for your new pixie cut. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid drying it out.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add moisture to your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you're ready to rock your new pixie cut!
