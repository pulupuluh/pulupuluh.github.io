How To Grow Natural Hair Past Shoulder Length A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 16, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having hair that just won't seem to grow past your shoulders? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle to grow their hair to longer lengths. However, with the right techniques and hair care routine, it is possible to achieve that luscious, long hair you've always dreamed of. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to grow natural hair past shoulder length.

Table of Contents

  • How to Grow Natural Hair Past Shoulder Length

How to Grow Natural Hair Past Shoulder Length

Before we dive into the specifics of growing long hair, it's important to understand a few things about hair growth. The average rate of hair growth is about half an inch per month. However, this rate can vary depending on factors such as genetics, age, and overall health.

When it comes to growing long hair, the key is to minimize damage and breakage. This means avoiding heat styling, chemical treatments, and harsh brushing. Instead, focus on nourishing and protecting your hair with a healthy hair care routine.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are some steps to follow if you want to grow natural hair past shoulder length:

  1. Start with a healthy foundation. This means eating a balanced diet, staying hydrated, and taking care of your overall health.
  2. Trim your hair regularly. While it may seem counterintuitive to cut your hair when you're trying to grow it out, regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage.
  3. Avoid heat styling. Heat can damage your hair and cause breakage, so try to limit your use of hot tools as much as possible.
  4. Use protective styles. Braids, twists, and buns can help protect your hair from breakage and promote growth.
  5. Deep condition regularly. Deep conditioning treatments can help nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  6. Avoid harsh chemicals. Chemical treatments like relaxers and perms can weaken your hair and cause breakage.
  7. Be gentle when brushing. Use a wide-tooth comb or brush with soft bristles to minimize breakage.
  8. Be patient. Growing long hair takes time and dedication, so don't get discouraged if you don't see results right away.

Tips for Growing Long Hair

Here are some additional tips that can help you grow long, healthy hair:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Consider taking hair growth supplements or using hair growth products.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Get plenty of rest and manage your stress levels, as stress can affect hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about growing natural hair past shoulder length:

Q: Can I grow my hair past shoulder length if it's naturally curly?

A: Yes, absolutely! Curly hair can grow just as long as straight hair, but it may require a slightly different hair care routine to minimize frizz and breakage.

Q: How long does it take to grow hair past shoulder length?

A: This can vary depending on factors like genetics and overall health, but it typically takes anywhere from 1-2 years to grow hair past shoulder length.

Q: Can I use hair extensions to achieve longer hair?

A: While hair extensions can give you the appearance of longer hair, they won't actually help your natural hair grow longer. In fact, wearing extensions for too long can actually cause damage and breakage.

Hair Care Routine for Long Hair

Finally, here's a sample hair care routine that you can use to promote long, healthy hair:

  1. Wash your hair once or twice a week with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Deep condition your hair once a week.
  3. Avoid heat styling as much as possible.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb or brush with soft bristles to detangle your hair.
  5. Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.
  6. Trim your hair every 8-12 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can grow your natural hair past shoulder length and achieve the long, luscious locks you've always wanted.
