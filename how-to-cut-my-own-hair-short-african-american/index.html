How To Cut My Own Hair Short African American A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia May 04, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts? Do you want to learn how to cut your own hair short African American style? Well, you’re in luck! In this article, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to achieve a short haircut at home. From tools needed to tips and tricks, we’ve got you covered. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools Needed
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Cleaning Up
  • Tips and Tricks
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care

Tools Needed

Before we begin, let’s gather the necessary tools for this haircut:

  • Clippers with guards
  • Shears
  • Comb
  • Sectioning Clips
  • Barber Cape or Towel
  • Handheld Mirror or Wall Mirror
  • Haircutting Scissors
  • Water Spray Bottle
  • Styling Products (optional)

Once you have all of your tools ready, let’s move on to prepping your hair.

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to prepare it properly. Follow these steps:

  1. Wash your hair and dry it completely.
  2. If you have thick hair, section it into manageable parts. Use sectioning clips to hold each section in place.
  3. Put on a barber cape or towel to protect your clothing from hair clippings.
  4. Decide on the length you want for your hair and select the appropriate clipper guard.
  5. Attach the clipper guard to your clippers and turn them on to make sure they’re working properly.

You’re now ready to start cutting your hair.

Cutting Your Hair

Follow these steps to cut your hair:

  1. Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up to the crown of your head.
  2. Use the clippers to make a straight line across the back of your head. Use the handheld mirror to check your progress.
  3. Move to the sides of your head and use the clippers to create a gradient effect.
  4. Use the shears to trim the top of your head to the desired length.
  5. Blend the sides and the top of your head together using the shears.
  6. Check your haircut in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.

Congratulations, you’ve just cut your own hair! Now it’s time to clean up.

Cleaning Up

Follow these steps to clean up:

  1. Remove the clipper guard and use the clippers to trim around your ears and neckline.
  2. Use the shears to trim any stray hairs.
  3. Take a shower to wash away any hair clippings.

You’re now done with the haircut. Let’s move on to tips and tricks.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few tips and tricks to make your haircut even better:

  • Start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head and make sure it’s even.
  • Watch YouTube tutorials to get a better understanding of the haircutting process.
  • Invest in high-quality tools to make the process easier.
  • Use styling products to enhance your new haircut.

Now, let’s answer some frequently asked questions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use regular scissors instead of haircutting scissors?

No, regular scissors don’t have the sharpness and precision needed for a proper haircut. Invest in high-quality haircutting scissors for the best results.

How do I avoid cutting too much hair?

Start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down until you reach your desired length. It’s always better to cut less hair than too much.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair’s growth rate and the desired length of your haircut. Generally, it’s recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

Now that you’ve cut your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Moisturize your hair with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Visit a professional hairstylist for regular trims and maintenance.

And there you have it, a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair short African American style. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut from the comfort of your own home!
