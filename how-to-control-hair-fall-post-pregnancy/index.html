How To Control Hair Fall Post Pregnancy

Written by Aprilia May 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you experiencing hair fall after pregnancy? You're not alone. Many new mothers experience hair fall after giving birth, and while it's a perfectly normal occurrence, it can be distressing. The good news is that there are steps you can take to control hair fall post pregnancy. In this article, we'll give you tips and tricks on how to keep your hair healthy and reduce hair fall.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Control Hair Fall Post Pregnancy
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips to Prevent Hair Fall
  4. FAQs

How to Control Hair Fall Post Pregnancy

The first step in controlling hair fall post pregnancy is to understand what causes it. During pregnancy, the hormone levels in a woman's body change, resulting in thicker, shinier hair. After giving birth, the hormone levels return to normal, and the hair that would have normally fallen out during pregnancy begins to shed. This can lead to excessive hair fall.

To control hair fall, you need to take care of your hair and scalp. Here are some tips:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Keep Your Scalp Clean
A clean scalp is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure to wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo that suits your hair type. Avoid using hot water and harsh chemicals that can damage your hair and scalp.

Step 2: Massage Your Scalp
Massaging your scalp can improve blood circulation, which stimulates hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes every day.

Step 3: Eat a Balanced Diet
A healthy diet can promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to eat foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling
Heat styling tools can damage your hair and cause hair fall. Avoid using them as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Step 5: Get Enough Sleep
Sleep is essential for overall health, including hair health. Aim to get 7-8 hours of sleep every night to promote healthy hair growth.

Tips to Prevent Hair Fall

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair fall:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid tying your hair too tightly, as it can pull on the hair and cause it to fall out.
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or headbands that can restrict blood flow to the scalp.
  • Take a hair supplement that contains vitamins and minerals essential for hair growth.

FAQs

Q: Is it normal to experience hair fall after pregnancy?
A: Yes, it's perfectly normal to experience hair fall after pregnancy. It's a result of the hormonal changes that occur in your body.

Q: How long does hair fall last after pregnancy?
A: Hair fall usually lasts for a few months after pregnancy, but it can vary from woman to woman.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause hair fall?
A: Yes, breastfeeding can cause hair fall in some women due to hormonal changes.

Hair Care Routine

A good hair care routine can go a long way in preventing hair fall. Here's a simple routine you can follow:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner after shampooing to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel to dry it. Instead, gently pat it dry.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it's more prone to breakage. Wait until it's dry or use a wide-toothed comb to detangle it.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

By following these tips and a proper hair care routine, you can control hair fall post pregnancy and enjoy healthy, beautiful hair. Remember, be patient and give your hair time to grow back.
