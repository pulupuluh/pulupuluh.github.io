How To Cut Your Own Hair Into A Point

Written by Aprilia Apr 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our blog on how to cut your own hair into a point! In this post, we'll cover everything you need to know to get the perfect pointed haircut at home. From choosing the right tools to finishing up with proper hair care, we've got you covered.

Description

A pointed haircut is a style where the hair is cut into a triangular shape at the end, creating a pointed tip. This style can be achieved on any length of hair, and it's a great way to add some edginess to your look. If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and try something new, cutting your own hair into a point is a great option.

How To

To cut your own hair into a point, you'll need a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle

Once you've gathered your tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you get a better idea of the length and texture of your hair, making it easier to cut. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use the comb to section your hair into four parts - two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section up so it's out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the back first

Start with the back sections of your hair. Take down one section at a time and comb it straight down. Use the scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle, starting at the middle of the hair and working your way down to the tip. Repeat this on the other side.

Step 4: Cut the front

Next, move on to the front sections of your hair. Take down one section at a time and comb it straight down. Use the scissors to cut the hair at a slightly steeper angle than the back, starting at the middle of the hair and working your way down to the tip. Repeat this on the other side.

Step 5: Blend the hair

Once you've cut all four sections, blend the hair together by combing it straight down and cutting any uneven pieces. Make sure the point is even on both sides.

Step 6: Finish up

Use the spray bottle to mist your hair, then comb it straight down and check for any stray pieces. Trim any uneven pieces as needed, then style your hair as usual.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to avoid accidentally cutting too much hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Take breaks if you start to feel tired or overwhelmed.
  • Ask a friend for help if you're nervous about cutting your hair alone.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair into a point if I have curly hair?

Yes! Curly hair can be cut into a pointed style, but it's important to work with your natural texture. Use a diffuser on your hair dryer to enhance your curls, and cut each section of hair individually to avoid creating uneven spots.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, you can always trim the hair a little shorter or try to blend it in with the rest of your hair. If the mistake is major, you may need to visit a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair into a point, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Visit a professional stylist every 6-8 weeks to maintain your point and keep your hair healthy.

That's it - you're ready to cut your own hair into a point! With the right tools, techniques, and care, you can achieve a stunning pointed style that's sure to turn heads.
