How To Cut Side Bangs For Wavy Hair A Complete Guide

Are you tired of your old hairstyle and want to try something new? Side bangs are a perfect way to add a fresh, new look to your wavy hair. But cutting side bangs can be a bit intimidating, especially if you've never done it before. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of how to cut side bangs for wavy hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Side Bangs for Wavy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Instructions

How to Cut Side Bangs for Wavy Hair

Cutting side bangs for wavy hair is not as difficult as it seems. Here are the basic steps:

1. Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp.

2. Decide on the Length and Angle of Your Bangs

The first step in cutting side bangs is to decide on the length and angle you want. Keep in mind that wavy hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so you may want to cut your bangs a bit longer than you initially planned. It's also a good idea to start with a longer length and then gradually cut more if needed.

3. Section Off Your Hair

Using hair clips, section off the hair you want to cut into a "V" shape. Make sure the point of the "V" is at the center of your forehead.

4. Cut Your Bangs

Starting at the center of your forehead, use your scissors to cut your bangs at the desired length and angle. Work your way outwards, cutting small sections at a time. Keep in mind that it's easier to cut more hair than to add it back, so take your time and cut slowly.

5. Check for Symmetry

After you've cut your bangs, check to make sure they're even on both sides. If needed, make small adjustments until they're symmetrical.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you know the basic steps of cutting side bangs for wavy hair, here's a more detailed guide:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Wavy hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so make sure you dry it in the style you want your bangs to be.

Step 2: Decide on the Length and Angle of Your Bangs

Using a comb, section off the hair you want to cut into a "V" shape. Make sure the point of the "V" is at the center of your forehead. Then, decide on the length and angle you want for your bangs.

Step 3: Dampen Your Bangs

Use a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your bangs. This will make them easier to cut.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Starting at the center of your forehead, use your scissors to cut your bangs at the desired length and angle. Work your way outwards, cutting small sections at a time. Remember to cut slowly and check for symmetry as you go.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs, style them as desired. You can use a straightener or curling iron to add more definition to your waves.

Tips for Cutting Side Bangs

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting side bangs for wavy hair:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Cut your bangs when they're damp, as this will make them easier to manage.
  • Use a comb to section off your hair, and hair clips to keep it in place.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut slowly and check for symmetry as you go.

FAQs

Can I cut my own side bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own side bangs. Just make sure you have the right tools and take your time.

How often should I trim my side bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look unkempt. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim a bit more to even things out. If you're really unhappy with the results, you can always visit a salon to have a professional fix it for you.

Hair Care for Wavy Hair with Side Bangs

Now that you have side bangs, it's important to take care of them. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for wavy hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to keep them looking neat and tidy.

By following these tips and techniques, you can easily cut side bangs for your wavy hair at home. With a little practice, you'll be able to create a new, stylish look that complements your natural waves perfectly.
