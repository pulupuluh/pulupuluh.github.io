How To Fix A Balding Lace Closure A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 01, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you experiencing balding or thinning in your lace closure? Don't worry, it's a common problem that can be easily fixed. In this article, we'll show you how to fix a balding lace closure in just a few simple steps.

Description

A lace closure is a hairpiece that is sewn or glued onto your scalp to give the appearance of a natural hairline. Over time, the hair on the closure can become thin or bald due to factors like age, stress, or improper care. This can be frustrating, but fixing a balding lace closure is a simple and affordable solution.

How to Fix a Balding Lace Closure

Step 1: Detach the Closure

The first step in fixing a balding lace closure is to remove it from your scalp. If it's sewn in, carefully cut the thread or use a seam ripper to detach the closure from the braids. If it's glued in, use a lace release or acetone to dissolve the adhesive.

Step 2: Clean the Closure

Once the closure is removed, gently clean it with a sulfate-free shampoo and lukewarm water. Avoid rubbing or scrubbing the closure, as this can damage the delicate lace. Rinse thoroughly and let it air dry.

Step 3: Apply a Hair Growth Treatment

To promote hair growth on the balding areas of the closure, apply a hair growth treatment like minoxidil or castor oil. Use a cotton swab or dropper to apply the treatment directly to the affected areas. Be sure to follow the instructions on the product label.

Step 4: Reattach the Closure

Once the hair growth treatment has had time to work (usually a few weeks), reattach the closure to your scalp. If it's sewn in, have a professional stylist re-braid your hair and sew the closure back on. If it's glued in, apply a new layer of adhesive and carefully press the closure onto your scalp.

Tips for Maintaining a Healthy Lace Closure

To prevent balding or thinning in your lace closure, here are some tips for maintaining a healthy scalp and hairpiece:

  • Wash your hair and closure regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools
  • Use a satin or silk scarf to protect your hair and closure while sleeping
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage
  • Avoid tight or heavy styling that can pull on the closure and cause damage

FAQ

Can I fix a balding lace closure without removing it?

No, it's best to remove the closure in order to properly clean and treat it. Trying to apply a hair growth treatment without detaching the closure can result in uneven or ineffective results.

How long does it take for a hair growth treatment to work?

Results can vary depending on the product and individual, but most hair growth treatments require several weeks to several months of consistent use before noticeable results are seen.

Can I use a hair growth treatment on my natural hair as well?

Yes, hair growth treatments can be used on both natural hair and hairpieces like lace closures. However, be sure to follow the instructions on the product label and consult with a healthcare professional if you have any concerns.

Hair Care

Your lace closure is an investment, so it's important to take care of it properly. By following these tips and steps, you can fix a balding lace closure and maintain a healthy hairpiece for years to come.

Remember to always use gentle products and avoid harsh treatments that can damage the delicate lace. With patience and consistency, you can achieve a full and healthy hairline that looks and feels natural.
