How To Thicken Short Thin Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Apr 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Short, thin hair can be difficult to style and can leave you feeling self-conscious. However, there are several ways to add volume and thickness to your hair without damaging it. In this article, we will discuss how to thicken short thin hair and provide tips and tricks to achieve a fuller look.

Description

Thick, voluminous hair has always been a sign of beauty and health. However, not everyone is blessed with naturally thick hair. If you have short, thin hair, you may feel like your hair never looks full or voluminous. Fortunately, there are several ways to add thickness to your hair, from hair care products to styling techniques.

How to Thicken Short Thin Hair

1. Use the Right Hair Care Products

The right hair care products can make a big difference in the appearance of your hair. Look for volumizing shampoos and conditioners that are designed to add thickness and body to your hair. Avoid heavy conditioners that can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. Additionally, consider using a hair thickening serum or spray to add volume to your roots.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down can help add volume and lift to your roots. Start by flipping your hair over and blow drying the roots. Once the roots are dry, flip your hair back over and continue blow drying the rest of your hair. Use a round brush to add even more volume and curl to your hair.

3. Use Hot Rollers or Curling Irons

Hot rollers or curling irons can help add volume and curl to your hair. Use large hot rollers or a curling iron to add big, bouncy curls to your hair. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair. Once your hair is curled, use a volumizing hairspray to hold the style in place.

4. Try Hair Extensions

Hair extensions can be a great way to add thickness and length to your hair. Look for clip-in extensions that are made from real hair for a natural look. You can also opt for a sew-in weave or tape-in extensions for a more permanent solution.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using heavy conditioners that can weigh down your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Hair Thickening Serum or Spray

Apply a hair thickening serum or spray to your roots to add volume and thickness to your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Flip your hair over and blow dry the roots to add volume and lift. Once the roots are dry, flip your hair back over and continue blow drying the rest of your hair.

Step 4: Use Hot Rollers or Curling Irons

Use hot rollers or a curling iron to add volume and curl to your hair. Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is curled, use a volumizing hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Thicker Hair

1. Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down your hair

2. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage

3. Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair

4. Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

FAQ

Q: Can I add thickness to my hair without using extensions?

A: Yes, you can use hair thickening products and styling techniques to add volume and thickness to your hair.

Q: What is the best type of extensions for thin hair?

A: Clip-in extensions made from real hair are a great option for adding thickness and length to thin hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people wash their hair every day, while others only wash it once or twice a week.

Hair Care for Thicker Hair

To keep your hair looking thick and healthy, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining thick, voluminous hair:

- Use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed to add volume and thickness to your hair

- Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down your hair

- Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage

- Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair

- Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

Conclusion

Thick, voluminous hair is possible, even if you have short, thin hair. By using the right hair care products and styling techniques, you can add volume and thickness to your hair without damaging it. Experiment with different products and methods to find the best solution for your hair type and style.
