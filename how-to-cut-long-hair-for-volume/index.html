How To Cut Long Hair For Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Long hair can be beautiful, but it can also be heavy and flat. If you're looking for ways to add volume to your long locks, a simple haircut can make all the difference. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut long hair for volume, including tips and tricks for achieving a full, bouncy look.

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks

How to Cut Long Hair for Volume

The key to cutting long hair for volume is to add layers. Layers can help to remove some of the weight and bulk of long hair, creating movement and bounce. However, it's important to note that not all layers are created equal. To achieve maximum volume, you'll want to ask your stylist for long, face-framing layers that start at your cheekbones or chin.

What You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror

How Long Should Your Hair Be?

The length of your hair will depend on your personal preference and face shape. However, in general, longer hair tends to be heavier and flatter. If you're looking for maximum volume, you may want to consider cutting your hair to shoulder length or just below.

Step-by-Step Guide

Ready to get started? Here's how to cut long hair for volume:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair.

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you to see the natural texture and movement of your hair, making it easier to cut and style.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections.

Using your comb, divide your hair into sections. Start by creating a part down the middle of your head, then divide your hair into two equal sections. Clip one section out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the first section.

Starting at the front of your hairline, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle, starting just below your cheekbones or chin. Repeat this process, working your way around your head and cutting the hair in small sections.

Step 4: Cut the second section.

Unclip the second section of hair and repeat the same process, cutting the hair at a slight angle just below your cheekbones or chin.

Step 5: Blend the layers.

Once you've cut both sections of hair, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and cut into the ends at a slight angle to create a seamless transition between the layers.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few tips and tricks to help you get the most out of your new layered haircut:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help create body and fullness.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  • Use a round brush to create curls and waves, which can help to add movement and texture to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, which can weigh your hair down and make it look flat.
  • Consider getting regular trims to maintain the shape and volume of your haircut.

FAQs

How often should I get my hair cut for volume?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and volume of your hair. However, this may vary depending on your hair type and personal preference.

Can I cut my own hair for volume?

While it's possible to cut your own hair, it's recommended to visit a professional stylist for the best results. A stylist can help to create a customized haircut that suits your face shape and hair type, while also ensuring that the layers are cut evenly and blended seamlessly.

Hair Care for Volume

Once you've cut your hair for volume, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain the shape and fullness. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, which can help to prevent breakage and damage.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it look flat.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot styling tools, which can help to prevent damage and breakage.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look flat.
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes, which can help to add volume and texture.

With these tips and tricks, you can easily cut long hair for volume and enjoy a full, bouncy look that suits your face shape and personal style.
