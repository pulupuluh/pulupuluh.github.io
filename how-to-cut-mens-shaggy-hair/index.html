If you're looking for a fresh new look, cutting your shaggy hair might just be the answer. It's a hairstyle that is versatile, easy to maintain, and perfect for those who want to add some texture and volume to their hair. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut mens shaggy hair, including tips and hair care advice to keep your locks looking their best.

How to Cut Mens Shaggy Hair

The shaggy haircut is all about creating layers and texture. It's a low maintenance style that can be adapted to suit any face shape and hair type. Here are the basic steps involved in cutting shaggy hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair, making it easier to cut. Use a shampoo and conditioner that works well for your hair type.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting from the top of the head and working your way down. Use hair clips to secure the sections, so they don't get in the way when you're cutting.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Starting from the back of the head, use a pair of scissors to cut the hair in a straight line. Cut the hair to the desired length, keeping in mind that shaggy hair is all about creating layers and texture. Use your fingers to create layers, cutting the hair at different lengths to achieve the desired effect.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut the hair into layers, use thinning shears to blend the layers together. This will help create a more natural look, blending the layers so that there are no harsh lines.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

After cutting your hair, style it using your preferred styling products. Shaggy hair looks great when styled with a texture spray or a molding clay, giving it a messy and effortless look.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're still unsure about how to cut your shaggy hair, here's a more detailed guide:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You'll need a few tools to cut your shaggy hair, including:

  • A pair of scissors
  • Thinning shears
  • Hair clips
  • Hair cutting comb

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair using a shampoo and conditioner that works well for your hair type. Dry your hair using a towel or a blow dryer.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting from the top of the head and working your way down. Use hair clips to secure each section so that they don't get in the way while cutting.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Starting from the back of the head, use a pair of scissors to cut the hair in a straight line. Cut the hair to the desired length, keeping in mind that shaggy hair is all about creating layers and texture. Use your fingers to create layers, cutting the hair at different lengths to achieve the desired effect.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Use thinning shears to blend the layers together. This will help create a more natural look, blending the layers so that there are no harsh lines.

Step 6: Trim the Bangs

If you have bangs, use scissors to trim them to the desired length. Cut the bangs at an angle to create a more natural look.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

After cutting your hair, style it using your preferred styling products. Shaggy hair looks great when styled with a texture spray or a molding clay, giving it a messy and effortless look.

Tips for a Perfect Cut

Here are some tips to help you get the perfect shaggy haircut:

  • Start with clean and dry hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Don't cut your hair too short, as shaggy hair is all about creating layers and texture.
  • Blend the layers together using thinning shears to create a natural look.
  • Trim the bangs at an angle to create a more natural look.
  • Use styling products to give your hair texture and volume.

FAQs

How often should I cut my shaggy hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want to maintain your shaggy haircut. On average, you should cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Can I cut my shaggy hair at home?

Yes, you can cut your shaggy hair at home if you have the right tools and know-how. Just make sure to follow the steps carefully and take your time.

What styling products should I use for shaggy hair?

Texture spray and molding clay are great products to use for shaggy hair, as they help create volume and texture.

Hair Care After Cutting Shaggy Hair

After cutting your hair, it's essential to take care of it properly to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that works well for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style.
  • Use styling products to give your hair texture and volume.

Cutting shaggy hair can be a fun and easy way to change up your look. Just follow these steps carefully and take your time, and you'll have a fresh new haircut in no time!
