How to Backcomb Hair 80s Style

Backcombing involves teasing the hair strands towards the roots, creating volume and texture. Here are the basic steps for backcombing hair 80s style:

1. Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Apply some volumizing mousse or spray to your roots for extra lift. Brush your hair to remove any tangles and make sure it's completely detangled.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting at the crown of your head. Clip the top section away for now and work on the lower sections first.

3. Tease Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Using a fine-toothed comb, backcomb the hair towards your scalp in short, quick strokes. Repeat this process with each section, working your way up to the crown of your head.

For a more dramatic look, tease your hair in sections that are smaller than the desired result to create more volume.

4. Smooth Your Hair

After teasing each section, gently smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section to create a sleek, polished finish.

5. Finish Your Look

Spray some hairspray over your hair to hold the style in place. Use your fingers to shape the hair and create your desired look. Add some accessories like a headband, scrunchie, or hair clips to complete your 80s style.

Tips for Backcombing Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect backcombed hair 80s style:

1. Use the Right Tools

Use a fine-toothed comb or a teasing brush to backcomb your hair. These tools are designed to create the right amount of tension and control for teasing.

2. Start Small

Tease your hair in small sections to create more volume and control. This will also prevent your hair from becoming too tangled or damaged.

3. Don't Overdo It

Backcombing can be damaging to your hair, so don't overdo it. Tease your hair only as much as you need to achieve the desired volume and texture.

4. Use Hairspray

Use a strong-hold hairspray to keep your backcombed hair in place. Spray it over your hair after you're done backcombing and shaping your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is backcombing bad for your hair?

Backcombing can be damaging to your hair if done too frequently or aggressively. Use a gentle touch and don't backcomb your hair every day.

How do you remove backcombing from your hair?

The easiest way to remove backcombing from your hair is to brush it out gently using a wide-toothed comb or a detangling brush. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

Can you backcomb short hair?

Yes, you can backcomb short hair. Use a smaller comb or a teasing brush to tease your hair in small sections for a more subtle effect.

Hair Care for Backcombed Hair

Backcombing can be damaging to your hair if done too frequently or aggressively. Here are some tips to care for your backcombed hair:

1. Wash Your Hair Less

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause damage. Try washing your hair every other day or every three days to prevent damage.

2. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help repair and nourish your hair. Look for a treatment that's specifically designed for damaged or color-treated hair.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can cause damage to your hair. Try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to minimize heat damage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can backcomb your hair 80s style while keeping it healthy and strong. Whether you're going for a full-on rocker look or a more subtle tease, backcombing is a fun and easy way to add volume and texture to your hair.
