How To Fix Bald Spot On Side Of Head

Written by Bardi Jun 09, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're experiencing a bald spot on the side of your head, you're not alone. Many people experience hair loss, and it can be frustrating and embarrassing. However, there are several things you can do to fix a bald spot on the side of your head. In this article, we'll cover some effective solutions for treating hair loss and regrowing hair on the side of your head.

Description

A bald spot on the side of your head can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, hormonal changes, and medical conditions. Male and female pattern baldness can also cause hair loss on the sides of the head. If you're experiencing hair loss on the side of your head, it's important to identify the cause so you can choose the best treatment option.

How to Fix Bald Spot on Side of Head

1. Minoxidil

Minoxidil is a topical solution that can help regrow hair. It's available over-the-counter and can be applied to the scalp twice a day. Minoxidil has been shown to be effective in regrowing hair in both men and women. However, it may take several months to see results.

2. Hair Transplant

A hair transplant is a surgical procedure in which hair follicles are taken from one part of the scalp and transplanted to the balding area. This procedure can be expensive and may require multiple sessions to achieve the desired results.

3. Laser Therapy

Laser therapy is a non-invasive treatment that uses low-level laser therapy to stimulate hair growth. This treatment can be done in a clinic or at home with a laser cap or helmet. Laser therapy is relatively painless and has been shown to be effective in regrowing hair.

4. Medications

There are several medications that can help treat hair loss. Finasteride is a medication that is commonly used to treat male pattern baldness. It works by blocking the hormone DHT, which is responsible for hair loss. However, it's important to speak with a healthcare provider before taking any medication.

Step by Step

Step 1: Identify the Cause

The first step in treating a bald spot on the side of your head is to identify the cause. This may involve speaking with a healthcare provider or dermatologist to determine if there is an underlying medical condition causing the hair loss.

Step 2: Choose a Treatment Option

Once you've identified the cause, you can choose a treatment option that works best for you. This may involve using a topical solution, undergoing a hair transplant, or using laser therapy.

Step 3: Follow the Treatment Plan

It's important to follow the treatment plan as directed by your healthcare provider or dermatologist. This may involve using a topical solution twice a day, attending multiple sessions for a hair transplant, or using a laser cap or helmet regularly.

Tips

- Be patient. It may take several months to see results from a hair loss treatment.

- Follow a healthy diet and exercise regularly to promote hair growth.

- Avoid harsh chemicals and styling products that can damage the hair.

FAQ

Can stress cause hair loss on the side of the head?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss on the side of the head. Stress can disrupt the hair growth cycle and cause hair to fall out prematurely.

Can genetics cause hair loss on the side of the head?

Yes, male and female pattern baldness can cause hair loss on the sides of the head. This type of hair loss is typically hereditary.

Is a hair transplant painful?

A hair transplant is typically done under local anesthesia, so the procedure itself is relatively painless. However, there may be some discomfort during the recovery period.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair to prevent further hair loss and promote hair growth. This may involve using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and protecting the hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.

With the right treatment plan and hair care routine, you can fix a bald spot on the side of your head and regrow your hair. Speak with a healthcare provider or dermatologist to determine the best treatment option for you.
