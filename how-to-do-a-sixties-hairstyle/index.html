How To Do A Sixties Hairstyle A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 12, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking to bring a little retro charm to your hairstyle, look no further than the swinging sixties. Known for its bold, voluminous, and playful looks, the sixties hairstyle is a favorite among fashionistas and vintage lovers alike. Whether you're getting ready for a themed party or just want to add a touch of nostalgia to your everyday look, here's how to do a sixties hairstyle in just a few easy steps.

Description

The sixties hairstyle is all about volume, texture, and playful shapes. From the iconic beehive to the mod pixie cut, there are plenty of styles to choose from. The key to nailing the sixties look is to embrace your natural hair texture, whether you have curly, wavy, or straight hair.

How to

To get started, you'll need a few tools and products:

  • Comb
  • Bobby pins
  • Hairspray
  • Teasing brush
  • Hair ties

Step 1: Prep your hair

Start by washing your hair and blow-drying it until it's completely dry. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to protect it from heat damage.

Step 2: Tease your hair

Using a teasing brush, backcomb your hair at the crown of your head to create volume. Start at the roots and gently tease your hair in small sections, working your way toward the front of your head.

Step 3: Create a bouffant

Gather the teased hair at the crown of your head and gently push it forward to create a bouffant. Use bobby pins to secure the hair in place.

Step 4: Style the sides

Take the hair on the sides of your head and pull it back, securing it with bobby pins behind the bouffant. Leave a few strands loose at the front to frame your face.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray

Spray your hair with a generous amount of hairspray to hold the style in place. Use a light mist of hairspray on the front strands to keep them from falling flat.

Tips

  • For an extra sixties touch, add a headband or scarf to your hair.
  • If you have short hair, try a mod pixie cut with side-swept bangs.
  • Experiment with different hair textures, from sleek and straight to tousled and curly.
  • For a more modern take on the sixties hairstyle, add some highlights or balayage to your hair.

FAQ

What if my hair is too thin for a bouffant?

If you have fine or thin hair, you can still create a sixties-inspired look by adding volume with a volumizing mousse and teasing the hair at the roots. You can also opt for a sleek, straight style with a deep side part for a mod look.

Can I do a sixties hairstyle with curly hair?

Absolutely! The sixties were all about embracing natural hair textures, so curly hair is perfect for this style. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls, and add some mousse or curl cream to define your curls.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and strong, make sure to use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and deep condition your hair once a week. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and use a heat protectant spray when you do. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, and avoid using harsh chemicals or hair dyes that can damage your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be rocking a sixties hairstyle in no time. Whether you're channeling your inner Twiggy or just want to add a little retro flair to your look, the sixties hairstyle is a classic that never goes out of style.
