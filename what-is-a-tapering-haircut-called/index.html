What Is A Tapering Haircut Called

Written by Oliver Jul 30, 2023 · 7 min read

A tapering haircut is a popular hairstyle for men that involves cutting the hair gradually shorter from the top of the head down to the neckline. This style creates a clean and polished look that is versatile and easy to maintain. However, not everyone is familiar with the term "tapering haircut." So, what is a tapering haircut called in relaxed English language?

What is a Tapering Haircut Called?

A tapering haircut is commonly referred to as a "fade" or "faded" haircut. The term "fade" refers to the gradual blending of the hair from longer to shorter, creating a fade effect. This style has become increasingly popular in recent years and can be seen on men of all ages and backgrounds.

How to Get a Tapering Haircut

If you're interested in getting a tapering haircut, there are a few steps you should follow:

  1. Choose the right barber or stylist. Look for someone who has experience with tapering haircuts and can give you the look you want.
  2. Decide on the length and style you want. Do you want a high fade or a low fade? A short crop or a longer style on top?
  3. Communicate with your barber or stylist. Make sure they understand what you want and can give you the cut you're looking for.
  4. Maintain your haircut. Tapering haircuts require regular maintenance to keep them looking sharp. Visit your barber or stylist for touch-ups every few weeks.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting a Tapering Haircut

If you're looking to cut your own hair at home, here's a step-by-step guide to cutting a tapering haircut:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back of the head, starting with a longer guard and gradually working your way down to a shorter guard.
  3. Blend the hair on the sides and back into the hair on top using scissors or clippers.
  4. Trim the hair on top of the head to your desired length and style.
  5. Blend the hair on top into the hair on the sides and back using scissors or clippers.
  6. Check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Maintaining a Tapering Haircut

Here are a few tips for keeping your tapering haircut looking great:

  • Visit your barber or stylist regularly for touch-ups.
  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and nourished.
  • Consider using a styling product to enhance your haircut and keep it looking polished.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about tapering haircuts:

How often should you get a tapering haircut?

You should get a tapering haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.

What is the difference between a taper and a fade?

A taper is a gradual decrease in length from the top of the head down to the neckline, while a fade is a more drastic and abrupt change in length, usually involving a clipper guard.

Can women get a tapering haircut?

Absolutely! Tapering haircuts are not just for men and can be a great way for women to add some edge and style to their haircut.

Hair Care for Tapering Haircuts

Proper hair care is important for keeping your tapering haircut looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Consider using a styling product to enhance your haircut and keep it looking polished.
  • Visit your barber or stylist regularly for touch-ups and maintenance.

Overall, a tapering haircut is a versatile and stylish option for men and women of all ages. Whether you call it a "fade" or a "taper," this haircut is sure to turn heads and make you feel confident and polished.
