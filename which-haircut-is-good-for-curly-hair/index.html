Which Haircut Is Good For Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide In 2023

Written by Aprilia Aug 22, 2023 Â· 12 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be voluminous, bouncy, and full of life, it can also be challenging to manage, style, and maintain. One of the best ways to tame curly hair is by getting the right haircut that flatters your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. In this guide, we'll share the best haircuts for curly hair and tips on how to care for your curls. Let's dive in!

  2. How to Choose the Right Haircut for Curly Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Haircut for Curly Hair
  4. Tips for Styling and Maintaining Curly Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Description

Curly hair can be categorized into three types: wavy, curly, and coily. Wavy hair has loose curls that are usually easy to manage. Curly hair has tighter curls that can be prone to frizz and dryness. Coily hair has tight curls that can range from S-shaped to Z-shaped and can be the most challenging to manage.

When it comes to haircuts for curly hair, the goal is to create a shape that enhances your curls while minimizing frizz and bulk. The right haircut can also make your hair more manageable, require less styling time, and highlight your best features.

How to Choose the Right Haircut for Curly Hair

Choosing the right haircut for curly hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not sure where to start. Here are some tips to help you choose the best haircut for your curls:

  • Consider your face shape: The right haircut should complement your face shape and enhance your best features. If you have a round face, go for a haircut that adds height and volume at the crown. If you have a square face, opt for a soft, layered haircut that frames your face.
  • Think about your hair texture: The best haircut for curly hair depends on your hair texture. If you have fine hair, go for a layered haircut that adds volume and movement. If you have thick hair, consider a blunt haircut with long layers to reduce bulk.
  • Consider your lifestyle: Choose a haircut that suits your lifestyle and maintenance routine. If you're always on the go, opt for a low-maintenance haircut that requires minimal styling. If you have the time and patience, go for a more elaborate haircut that allows for versatility and creativity.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Haircut for Curly Hair

Getting a haircut for curly hair requires some preparation and communication with your hairstylist. Here's a step-by-step guide to getting the perfect haircut for your curls:

  1. Do your research: Look for inspiration photos of haircuts that you like and that fit your hair type, face shape, and personal style. Bring these photos to your hairstylist to show them what you want.
  2. Communicate with your hairstylist: Let your hairstylist know your hair concerns, preferences, and lifestyle. They can recommend a haircut that suits your needs and expectations.
  3. Get your hair dry-cut: Curly hair should be cut when it's dry, so your hairstylist can see the shape and length of your curls. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer, resulting in a haircut that's too short or uneven.
  4. Ask for layers: Layers can add dimension, movement, and volume to curly hair. Ask your hairstylist to add long layers that blend seamlessly with your curls.
  5. Consider a DevaCut: A DevaCut is a specialized haircut for curly hair that's done on dry hair using a technique that enhances your natural curls. It's a popular choice for those with coily or kinky hair types.
  6. Style and maintain your curls: Once you get your new haircut, ask your hairstylist for tips on how to style and maintain your curls. They can recommend products, techniques, and tools that work best for your hair type and lifestyle.

Tips for Styling and Maintaining Curly Hair

Styling and maintaining curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls. Here are some tips for styling and maintaining curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo: Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness and frizz. Use a sulfate-free shampoo that's gentle on your curls and scalp.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner: A leave-in conditioner can hydrate and nourish your curls, reduce frizz, and define your curls' shape. Apply it to damp hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths.
  • Avoid brushing your hair: Brushing curly hair can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls gently.
  • Try the "pineapple" technique: The "pineapple" technique involves gathering your hair at the top of your head, securing it with a scrunchie or hair tie, and letting the curls fall loosely around your face. It helps preserve your curls' shape and prevent flattening while you sleep.
  • Use a diffuser: A diffuser attachment on your hairdryer can help dry your curls without causing frizz. Use it on a low heat and speed setting, scrunching your curls gently as you go.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools can damage your curls and cause dryness and frizz. If you must use them, apply a heat protectant spray and use a low heat setting.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about haircuts for curly hair:

  • What is the best haircut for curly hair? The best haircut for curly hair depends on your hair type, face shape, and personal style. Some popular haircuts for curly hair include the layered bob, shag haircut, and curly pixie cut.
  • Should I get bangs with curly hair? Bangs can be flattering with curly hair, but they require some maintenance and styling. Consider getting long, side-swept bangs that blend with your curls.
  • How often should I get a haircut for curly hair? It depends on your hair growth rate and maintenance routine. Generally, you should get a haircut every 6-12 weeks to maintain your haircut's shape and remove split ends.
  • Can I straighten my curly hair after a haircut? Yes, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting to avoid damaging your curls.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires some TLC to stay healthy, shiny, and bouncy. Here are some hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Avoid over-washing: Curly hair doesn't need to be washed as often as straight hair. Wash your hair every 2-3 days or as needed to avoid drying out your curls.
  • Deep condition regularly: Deep conditioning treatments can help hydrate and nourish your curls, reduce frizz, and improve your hair's elasticity. Apply a deep conditioner once a week or as needed.
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase: Silk pillowcases are gentle on your curls and don't cause friction or frizz. They also help retain moisture and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns can cause tension and breakage on your curls. Opt for loose hairstyles that don't pull on your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun: UV rays can damage your curls and cause dryness and frizz. Wear a hat, scarf, or use a UV-protectant spray to shield your hair from the sun.

With the right haircut and hair care routine, you can embrace your curly hair and rock it with confidence.
