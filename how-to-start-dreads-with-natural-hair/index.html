How To Start Dreads With Natural Hair

Written by Frank Aug 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Welcome to our ultimate guide on how to start dreads with natural hair. If you're looking to embrace your natural hair and start your dreadlocks journey, you're in the right place. In this post, we'll cover everything you need to know about starting dreads with natural hair, from how to prep your hair to the best products to use. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • The Method
  • Tips
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Preparation

Before you start your dreadlocks journey, it's important to prepare your hair properly. Here are the steps you'll need to follow:

Step 1: Trim Your Ends

Trimming your ends is essential before starting dreadlocks. This will help prevent split ends and breakage, ensuring that your dreads grow strong and healthy.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair will remove any dirt or product buildup, allowing your hair to lock easier. Make sure to use a clarifying shampoo to get your hair as clean as possible.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is completely dry before starting your dreads. Wet hair is harder to work with and can lead to mildew and bacteria growth.

The Method

Now that your hair is prepped and ready, it's time to start your dreads. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into small, equal parts. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separate.

Step 2: Backcomb Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and backcomb it using a fine-tooth comb. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends. Repeat this process until each section of hair is backcombed.

Step 3: Twist and Roll

After backcombing, use your fingers to twist and roll each section of hair. This will help form the dreadlocks. Make sure to roll each section in the same direction to keep your dreads uniform.

Step 4: Secure Your Dreads

Use a hair tie or rubber band to secure each dread at the base. This will help keep your dreads in place as they mature.

Tips

Starting dreads with natural hair can be a long and time-consuming process. Here are some tips to help you along the way:

  • Be patient. It can take months or even years for your dreads to fully mature.
  • Avoid using too much product. Product buildup can prevent your dreads from locking properly.
  • Avoid using heat. Heat styling tools can damage your hair and prevent it from locking.
  • Protect your hair at night. Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about starting dreads with natural hair:

Q: Can I start dreads with short hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreads with short hair. However, the process may take longer and you may need to use extensions to achieve the desired length.

Q: Can I wash my hair with dreads?

A: Yes, you can wash your hair with dreads. However, you should avoid using regular shampoo and opt for a residue-free shampoo instead.

Q: How often should I re-twist my dreads?

A: It's best to re-twist your dreads every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.

Hair Care

Once you've started your dreads, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that contain silicone.
  • Protect your hair from harsh weather conditions.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

That's it! You now know how to start dreads with natural hair. Remember to be patient, take care of your hair, and enjoy the journey.
