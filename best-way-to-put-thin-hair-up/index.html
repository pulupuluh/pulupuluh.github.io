Best Way To Put Thin Hair Up

Written by Frank Mar 05, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have thin hair, it can be challenging to put it up without it looking limp and lifeless. However, with the right technique and products, you can achieve a voluminous and stylish updo. In this article, we’ll share the best way to put thin hair up, step by step, along with some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Put Thin Hair Up
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Putting Thin Hair Up
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

How to Put Thin Hair Up

The best way to put thin hair up is by using hair accessories that add volume and texture to your hair. You can also use styling products that give your hair grip and hold. The key is to avoid pulling your hair too tightly or using heavy products that weigh your hair down.

Here are some hair accessories and products that work best for thin hair:

  • Bobby pins
  • Scrunchies
  • Claw clips
  • Hair elastics without metal pieces
  • Texturizing sprays
  • Hair powders
  • Dry shampoos
  • Lightweight mousses

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to put your thin hair up:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is oily or greasy, use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil.
  2. Add texture to your hair by using a texturizing spray or hair powder. Apply it to the roots and mid-lengths of your hair and work it in with your fingers.
  3. Gather your hair into a ponytail at the crown of your head. Use a hair elastic without metal pieces to avoid breakage.
  4. Loosen the ponytail slightly to create a more relaxed look.
  5. Tease the ponytail with a fine-tooth comb to add volume.
  6. Twist the ponytail into a bun and secure it with bobby pins. If you want a messy bun, leave some strands loose around your face.
  7. Finish with a light hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Putting Thin Hair Up

Here are some additional tips to help you put your thin hair up:

  • Avoid pulling your hair too tightly, as it can cause breakage and make your hair look even thinner.
  • Use hair accessories that add volume and texture to your hair, such as scrunchies and hair elastics without metal pieces.
  • Tease your hair at the roots to add volume.
  • Use lightweight styling products that won’t weigh your hair down.
  • If you’re using bobby pins, place them with the wavy side facing down to grip your hair better.

FAQs

Q: Can I put my thin hair up in a high ponytail?

A: Yes, you can put your thin hair up in a high ponytail, but avoid pulling it too tightly. Use a scrunchie or hair elastic without metal pieces to avoid breakage.

Q: How can I make my thin hair look thicker?

A: You can make your thin hair look thicker by adding volume and texture with hair accessories and styling products. Tease your hair at the roots, use a texturizing spray or hair powder, and avoid heavy products that weigh your hair down.

Q: Can I use hair extensions to make my thin hair look thicker?

A: Yes, you can use hair extensions to make your thin hair look thicker, but make sure to use high-quality extensions that match your hair color and texture.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your thin hair healthy and voluminous:

  • Wash your hair every other day to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and techniques, you can put your thin hair up in a stylish and voluminous updo. Remember to use hair accessories and products that add texture and hold, and avoid pulling your hair too tightly or using heavy products that weigh it down. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a beautiful and effortless updo that lasts all day.
