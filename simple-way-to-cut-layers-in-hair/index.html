Simple Way To Cut Layers In Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your flat, one-length hair? Layers can add dimension and movement to your hair, making it look more dynamic and voluminous. However, going to a salon can be costly and time-consuming. But don't worry, cutting layers in your hair is easier than you think! In this article, we will guide you through the simple way to cut layers in hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Layers in Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Layers in Hair
  4. Frequently Asked Questions
  5. Hair Care After Cutting Layers

How to Cut Layers in Hair

Before we get started, let's talk about the tools you'll need. You'll need a pair of good quality hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips. You may also want to have a mirror, a spray bottle filled with water, and a towel on hand.

When it comes to cutting layers in hair, there are a few different techniques you can use. Some people prefer to cut their hair dry, while others prefer to cut it wet. Wet hair is easier to manage, but keep in mind that it will shrink when it dries, so you may want to cut it a bit longer than you want it to be.

Another important factor is the type of layers you want. Long layers are great for adding volume and movement to long hair, while short layers work well for shorter haircuts like bobs or pixies.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps for cutting layers in hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and free of any styling products.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: two on top and two on the bottom. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom section, take a small piece of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and use your scissors to cut at an angle. This will create a soft, blended layer.

Repeat this process on both sides of the head, working your way up to the top sections. Be sure to cut each section at the same angle to create even layers.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, it's time to blend them. Take a comb and comb through your hair, pulling it straight up. Use your scissors to lightly chop into the ends of your hair. This will blend the layers together and create a seamless look.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Hair

Here are some tips to help you cut layers in your hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's cut.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut your hair in a well-lit area and use a mirror to check your progress.
  • When cutting layers in curly hair, cut less hair than you think you need to. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you don't want to cut too much.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own layers in hair?

Yes, you can cut your own layers in hair. It just takes a bit of practice and patience.

How often should I cut layers in my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your layers. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

Can I cut layers in my hair if it's already layered?

Yes, you can cut layers in your hair even if it's already layered. Just be sure to cut each layer at the same angle to ensure even layers.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting layers in your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Cutting layers in hair can give you a fresh new look without having to spend a lot of money at the salon. With a bit of practice and patience, you can easily cut your own layers at home. Just be sure to follow these steps and take care of your hair properly afterwards. Happy cutting!
