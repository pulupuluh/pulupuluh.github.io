How To Straighten Thick Coarse Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 04, 2023 · 11 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to straighten thick coarse curly hair. If you have this hair type, you know how challenging it can be to keep it tamed and manageable. Fortunately, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve straight, sleek hair that looks and feels amazing. In this article, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to straighten your thick coarse curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Straighten Thick Coarse Curly Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Straightening Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Description

Thick coarse curly hair is a beautiful hair type, but it can be challenging to manage. It tends to be frizzy, dry, and difficult to style. Straightening your hair can help you achieve a sleek, polished look. However, it’s important to take care of your hair and protect it from heat damage.

How to Straighten Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Straightening thick coarse curly hair can be a time-consuming process, but the results are worth it. Here are the steps you need to follow to straighten your hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to hydrate your hair and prevent it from becoming dry and brittle. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry it gently.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Before using any heat styling tools, it’s important to apply a heat protectant. This will help to prevent damage to your hair and keep it healthy. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a blow dryer with a diffuser attachment to dry your hair. This will help to reduce frizz and maintain the natural texture of your hair. Dry your hair in sections, starting at the roots and working your way to the ends.

Step 4: Straighten Your Hair

Use a flat iron to straighten your hair. Work in small sections, starting at the roots and slowly moving down to the ends of your hair. Use a comb to keep your hair taut and straight as you straighten it. Repeat this process until all of your hair is straightened.

Step 5: Apply a Smoothing Serum

Finish off your straightened hair by applying a smoothing serum. This will help to reduce frizz and add shine to your hair. Apply a small amount of serum to your palms and run it through your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to straighten thick coarse curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry it gently.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly. This will help to protect your hair from heat damage when using styling tools.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will help to prevent breakage and damage when you straighten your hair.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a blow dryer with a diffuser attachment to dry your hair. Dry your hair in sections, starting at the roots and working your way to the ends. Use a round brush to help smooth your hair as you dry it.

Step 5: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to straighten your hair in small sections.

Step 6: Straighten Your Hair

Use a flat iron to straighten your hair. Work in small sections, starting at the roots and slowly moving down to the ends of your hair. Use a comb to keep your hair taut and straight as you straighten it. Repeat this process until all of your hair is straightened.

Step 7: Apply a Smoothing Serum

Finish off your straightened hair by applying a smoothing serum. This will help to reduce frizz and add shine to your hair. Apply a small amount of serum to your palms and run it through your hair.

Tips for Straightening Your Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when straightening your hair:

  • Use a high-quality flat iron with ceramic plates to help protect your hair from heat damage.
  • Don’t straighten your hair when it’s wet, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray or serum before using any heat styling tools.
  • Work in small sections when straightening your hair to ensure that each section is properly straightened.
  • Use a comb to help keep your hair taut and straight as you straighten it.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about straightening thick coarse curly hair:

Q: Can I straighten my hair if it’s damaged?

A: It’s best to avoid straightening your hair if it’s damaged, as this can cause further damage. If your hair is damaged, focus on repairing it before attempting to straighten it.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It’s best to avoid straightening your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage. Try to limit straightening to once or twice a week.

Q: How can I keep my straightened hair looking smooth and shiny?

A: Use a smoothing serum or oil to help keep your hair looking smooth and shiny. Avoid using too much product, as this can make your hair look greasy.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you care for your thick coarse curly hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help hydrate your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to help repair damage and add moisture.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as relaxers or perms, as these can cause damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.

That’s it! We hope this guide has been helpful in showing you how to straighten thick coarse curly hair. Remember to take care of your hair and protect it from heat damage, and you’ll be able to achieve straight, sleek hair that looks and feels amazing.
