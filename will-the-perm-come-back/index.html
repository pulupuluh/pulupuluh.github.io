Will The Perm Come Back A Comprehensive Guide To Perming In 2023

Description

Perms were all the rage in the 80s and 90s, but they fell out of favor in the 2000s. However, in recent years, we've seen a resurgence of perms in popular culture. Celebrities like Emma Stone and Olivia Munn have rocked perm hairstyles on the red carpet, and social media influencers have embraced the trend as well.

So, will the perm come back in 2023? It's hard to say for sure, but it's definitely possible. With the rise of the "soft perm" and other gentle perm techniques, more people are considering the style as a way to add texture and volume to their hair.

How to Perm Your Hair

Before you decide to perm your hair, it's important to understand the process. A perm involves using chemicals to break down the bonds in your hair, then reforming them into a new shape. This can be damaging to your hair if not done correctly, so it's important to go to a professional stylist who has experience with perms.

Types of Perms

There are several types of perms to choose from, depending on the look you're going for. Some of the most popular types include:

  • Spiral perm
  • Body wave perm
  • Spot perm
  • Straight perm
  • Stack perm

Choosing the Right Perm

The type of perm you choose will depend on your hair type, length, and the look you're going for. Your stylist can help you choose the right type of perm for your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Perming Your Hair

If you've decided to get a perm, here's what you can expect during the process:

  1. Your stylist will wash your hair and apply a solution to break down the bonds in your hair.
  2. They will then wrap your hair around perm rods and apply another solution to reform the bonds in the new shape.
  3. You'll then sit under a dryer for a specified amount of time to set the perm.
  4. Your stylist will then rinse out the chemicals and apply a neutralizer to stop the chemical process.
  5. Your hair will be styled and you'll be ready to go!

Tips for Maintaining Your Perm

Here are some tips for keeping your perm looking great:

  • Avoid washing your hair for the first 48-72 hours after getting a perm.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for permed hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your perm, as this can damage the curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions About Perms

1. Are perms damaging to your hair?

If done correctly by a professional stylist, perms shouldn't be too damaging to your hair. However, over-processing or using harsh chemicals can cause damage and breakage.

2. How long does a perm last?

Perms typically last three to six months, depending on your hair type and how well you take care of your hair.

3. Can you color your hair after getting a perm?

It's best to wait at least a week after getting a perm before coloring your hair, as the chemicals can be harsh on your hair.

Hair Care After Perming

After getting a perm, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the natural oils from your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage the curls.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

So, will the perm come back in 2023? Only time will tell. But if you're considering getting a perm, make sure to do your research and go to a professional stylist who can help you choose the right type of perm for your hair.
