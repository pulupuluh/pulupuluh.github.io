Is Layering Your Hair Make It Look Thicker

Written by Frank Mar 29, 2023 Â· 9 min read

When it comes to hair, we all want to have the perfect locks. The type of hair that makes heads turn and people asking, "what's your secret?" One of the most common questions we get about hair is, "is layering your hair make it look thicker?" The answer is yes, layering your hair can make it look thicker, but there's more to it than just getting a haircut. In this article, we'll explore the benefits of layering your hair, how to do it, and how to take care of it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Benefits of Layering Your Hair

Layering your hair has several benefits, one of which is making it look thicker. When you cut your hair into layers, you create the illusion of volume and movement. This is because the different lengths of hair create dimension and texture, making your hair appear fuller and more voluminous. Additionally, layering your hair can give your face a more flattering shape and highlight your best features.

Another benefit of layering your hair is that it can make it easier to manage. Layers can help distribute the weight of your hair more evenly, making it less heavy and easier to style. This is especially true if you have thick or curly hair, which can be difficult to manage without the right cut.

How to Layer Your Hair

If you're interested in layering your hair to make it look thicker, there are a few things you should consider. First, you'll need to find a stylist who has experience with layered haircuts. Look for someone who has a portfolio of layered haircuts that you like and who can recommend the best cut for your hair texture and face shape.

When it comes to layering your hair, there are a few different techniques that stylists use. Some of the most common techniques include long layers, short layers, and choppy layers. Long layers are great for adding volume and movement to long hair, while short layers work well for shorter haircuts. Choppy layers can create a more textured, edgy look.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're ready to layer your hair, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Brush your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Section your hair into four parts: top, bottom, left, and right.
  4. Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  5. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, making sure to cut less hair at the top and more at the bottom.
  6. Repeat this process with each section of hair, gradually working your way up to the top section of hair.
  7. Once you've finished cutting all the sections, use your fingers to blend the layers together.
  8. Finish with a styling product, if desired.

Tips for Layering Your Hair

If you're layering your hair for the first time, here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Communicate with your stylist. Make sure you're both on the same page about the type of cut you want and how much hair you want to be removed.
  • Consider your face shape. Different layering techniques can flatter different face shapes, so it's important to choose the right cut for your features.
  • Think about your hair texture. If you have thin hair, you may want to avoid choppy layers, which can make your hair look even thinner.
  • Be realistic about your expectations. While layering your hair can make it look thicker, it's not a miracle cure. If you have very fine or thin hair, you may need to use volumizing products or extensions to achieve the look you want.

FAQs

Here are some commonly asked questions about layering your hair:

Will layering my hair make it look shorter?

Not necessarily. While layering your hair can remove some length, it can also create the illusion of length if done correctly. Talk to your stylist about how much length you're comfortable with losing.

Can I layer my hair at home?

It's not recommended. Layering your own hair can be difficult, and it's easy to make mistakes that can be hard to fix. It's best to leave it to a professional stylist.

How often should I get my hair layered?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. For most people, getting a haircut every 6-8 weeks is a good rule of thumb.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Once you've layered your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its shape and volume. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to give your hair extra lift.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, which can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Blow-dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  • Use a round brush to create lift and movement while you blow-dry.
  • Avoid using a straightening iron or curling iron too often, as this can damage your hair and make it look flat.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve the thicker, more voluminous hair you've always wanted. Remember, communication with your stylist is key, and regular maintenance is important for keeping your hair looking its best.
