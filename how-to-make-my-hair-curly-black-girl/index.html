How to Make My Hair Curly Black Girl: Tips, Tricks and Hair Care

Are you tired of having straight hair and want to switch it up to curly locks? If you're a black girl with relaxed hair, it might seem like a daunting task. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will show you how to make your hair curly, with tips, tricks, and hair care advice to keep your curls looking fabulous.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make My Hair Curly Black Girl
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Make My Hair Curly Black Girl

There are a few ways you can make your hair curly. Here are some of the most popular:

Using Rollers

One of the easiest ways to make your hair curly is by using rollers. You can use either hot or cold rollers, depending on your preference. Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start. Section your hair and roll each section onto a roller, starting from the ends and rolling upwards. Leave the rollers in for a few hours, or overnight for best results. Once you take the rollers out, use your fingers to separate the curls and give them some volume.

Using a Curling Iron

Another way to make your hair curly is by using a curling iron. Apply some heat protectant to your hair first, to avoid damage. Section your hair and start curling each section, starting from the ends and working your way up. Hold the curling iron for a few seconds before releasing the curl. Once you've curled all your hair, use your fingers to separate the curls and give them some volume.

Using a Perm

If you want to make your curls more permanent, you can get a perm. A perm is a chemical treatment that changes the structure of your hair. It can be done at a salon or at home, but it's best to get it done by a professional. The process involves applying a perm solution to your hair, which breaks down the bonds in your hair. Once the solution is rinsed out, your hair is neutralized and set into its new shape. Perms can last for several months.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to make your hair curly:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into manageable sections.
  3. Choose your preferred method of making your hair curly (rollers, curling iron, or perm).
  4. Follow the instructions for your chosen method.
  5. Once your hair is curly, use your fingers to separate the curls and give them some volume.

Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep your curly hair looking fabulous:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as it can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated.

FAQs

How long will my curls last?

It depends on the method you used to make your curls. If you used rollers or a curling iron, your curls will last until your next wash. If you got a perm, your curls can last for several months.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It's best to wash your curly hair once or twice a week, to avoid drying it out. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated.

Can I still straighten my hair after making it curly?

Yes, you can still straighten your hair after making it curly. Just make sure you use a heat protectant to avoid damage.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as it can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated.

With these tips, tricks, and hair care advice, you can achieve beautiful, curly hair as a black girl with relaxed hair. Experiment with different methods and find the one that works best for you. Happy styling!
