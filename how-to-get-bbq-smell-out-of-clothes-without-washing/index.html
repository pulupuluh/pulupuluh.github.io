How To Get Bbq Smell Out Of Clothes Without Washing Tips To Keep Your Clothes Fresh

Written by William Aug 07, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of the lingering BBQ smell on your clothes? You don’t want to wash your clothes every time you have a BBQ party, but the smell is just too strong to ignore. Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! In this article, we’ll share some tips on how to get BBQ smell out of clothes without washing them.

How to Get BBQ Smell Out of Clothes Without Washing: Tips to Keep Your Clothes Fresh

How to?

Here are some simple tips to get rid of the BBQ smell from your clothes without washing:

Step by Step

1. Vinegar

Mix equal parts of water and white vinegar in a spray bottle. Spray the solution on your clothes and let it dry. The vinegar smell will fade away, along with the BBQ smell.

2. Baking Soda

Sprinkle baking soda on your clothes and leave it on for a few hours. The baking soda will absorb the BBQ smell. Shake off the excess baking soda and you’re good to go.

3. Lemon Juice

Squeeze fresh lemon juice on your clothes and let it dry in the sun. The acid in the lemon juice will neutralize the BBQ smell.

4. Charcoal

Place a few pieces of charcoal in a paper bag and put your clothes inside. Seal the bag and leave it overnight. The charcoal will absorb the BBQ smell.

Tips

- Hang your clothes outside in the sun and fresh air. The UV rays and fresh air will naturally remove the BBQ smell.

- Store your clothes in a separate compartment or closet away from your BBQ gear.

- Use fabric fresheners or sprays to mask the BBQ smell temporarily.

- Wash your clothes with a vinegar solution or add baking soda to the wash to remove the BBQ smell.

FAQ

Q: Can I use fabric softener to remove the BBQ smell?

A: Fabric softener only masks the smell temporarily. It’s better to use natural remedies like vinegar, baking soda, or lemon juice to remove the BBQ smell.

Q: Can I use bleach to remove the BBQ smell?

A: Bleach can damage your clothes and is not recommended to remove the BBQ smell. Use natural remedies instead.

Hair Care

Don’t forget about your hair! The BBQ smell can also linger in your hair. Here are some tips to get rid of the smell:

- Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo.

- Rinse your hair with apple cider vinegar and water.

- Spritz your hair with a hair mist or perfume to mask the BBQ smell.

With these simple tips, you can easily get rid of the BBQ smell from your clothes without washing them. Enjoy your BBQ parties without worrying about the smell on your clothes!
