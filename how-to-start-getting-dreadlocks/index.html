How To Start Getting Dreadlocks A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 12, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you interested in starting your dreadlock journey? Congratulations, you’ve come to the right place. Before you dive in, it’s important to understand the process and care that comes with having dreadlocks. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about how to start getting dreadlocks, from the beginning stages to maintenance and care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Getting Dreadlocks
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Your Dreadlock Journey
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

How to Start Getting Dreadlocks

Dreadlocks, or “locs”, are formed by allowing hair to matt and tangle together over time. The process can take several months to years, depending on your hair texture, length, and maintenance routine. There are different methods to start your dreadlocks, such as backcombing, twisting, and interlocking. However, the most popular and natural method is called the “neglect” or “freeform” method, where you simply let your hair do its thing.

Before You Start

Before you start your dreadlock journey, it’s important to have a consultation with a professional loctician or stylist who can assess your hair type, texture, and health. They can also recommend the best method and products for your hair. It’s also important to have realistic expectations and understand that dreadlocks require time, patience, and dedication.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to start getting dreadlocks:

  1. Stop brushing or combing your hair regularly. This will allow your hair to start tangling and matting naturally.
  2. Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo to remove any product buildup and oils that can hinder the locking process.
  3. Separate your hair into sections, depending on how thick you want your dreadlocks to be. Use hair ties or clips to keep them separated.
  4. Leave your hair alone and let it do its thing. Don’t try to force your hair to lock or manipulate it too much, as this can damage your hair and slow down the process.
  5. As your hair starts to matt and tangle, you can gently separate the sections to prevent them from growing together.
  6. Be patient and let your hair lock naturally. This can take several months to years, depending on your hair type and length.

Tips for Starting Your Dreadlock Journey

Here are some tips to help you start your dreadlock journey:

  • Be patient and embrace the process.
  • Avoid using wax or heavy products that can cause buildup and prevent your hair from locking.
  • Cover your hair at night with a satin or silk bonnet or pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Keep your scalp clean and healthy by washing your hair regularly and using a scalp oil or spray to soothe any itchiness or dryness.
  • Don’t compare your hair journey to others. Everyone’s hair is unique and will lock at its own pace.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting your dreadlock journey:

How often should I wash my hair?

You should wash your hair at least once a week or every two weeks, depending on your hair type and lifestyle. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid over-washing, as this can dry out your hair and scalp.

Can I start my dreadlocks myself?

While it’s possible to start your dreadlocks yourself, it’s recommended to have a consultation with a professional loctician or stylist who can assess your hair and recommend the best method and products for your hair.

How can I prevent my dreadlocks from unraveling?

You can prevent your dreadlocks from unraveling by keeping them separated and using a locking gel or spray to help them hold their shape. You can also palm roll your dreadlocks to help them tighten and lock.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Here are some tips for maintaining and caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo and avoid over-washing.
  • Use a locking gel or spray to help your dreadlocks hold their shape.
  • Palm roll your dreadlocks to help them tighten and lock.
  • Cover your hair at night with a satin or silk bonnet or pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Keep your scalp clean and healthy by using a scalp oil or spray to soothe any itchiness or dryness.

Starting your dreadlock journey can be a fun and rewarding experience. Remember to be patient, embrace the process, and take good care of your hair. With time and dedication, you’ll have beautiful and unique dreadlocks!
