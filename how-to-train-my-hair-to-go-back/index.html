How To Train Your Hair To Go Back Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Jul 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your hair always being in your face? Do you want to train your hair to go back but don't know where to start? Look no further than this guide on how to train your hair to go back!

Why Train Your Hair to Go Back?

There are many reasons why you might want to train your hair to go back. It can be a stylish look for both men and women, it can keep your hair out of your face during physical activity, and it can even help prevent breakouts on your forehead. Plus, it's an easy hairstyle to maintain once you've trained your hair to go back.

How to Train Your Hair to Go Back

Training your hair to go back is a process that requires patience and consistency. Here are the steps you can take to train your hair to go back:

Step 1: Start with Wet Hair

Begin by washing your hair and towel-drying it until it is damp. This will help your hair stay in place as you style it.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product, such as gel or pomade, to your hair. Make sure to use a product that is appropriate for your hair type and texture. Use your fingers or a comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair Back

Using a comb, comb your hair straight back from your forehead. Start at the hairline and work your way back, using the comb to create a smooth, even part. Make sure your hair is lying flat against your scalp.

Step 4: Use a Hair Dryer

Use a hair dryer on a low heat setting to dry your hair while it is still combed back. This will help set the style in place.

Step 5: Repeat Daily

Repeat this process daily until your hair begins to naturally fall back without the need for styling product or a comb.

Tips for Training Your Hair to Go Back

Here are some additional tips to help you train your hair to go back:

  • Be patient - it can take several weeks or even months to train your hair to go back consistently.
  • Use a high-quality styling product that is appropriate for your hair type and texture.
  • Consider using a hair band or headband to keep your hair in place while you're training it to go back.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about training your hair to go back:

How long does it take to train your hair to go back?

It can take several weeks or even months to train your hair to go back consistently. Be patient and consistent with your styling routine.

Can I train my hair to go back if I have curly or wavy hair?

Yes, it is possible to train your hair to go back if you have curly or wavy hair. However, you may need to use different styling products or techniques to achieve the desired look.

Will training my hair to go back damage it?

No, training your hair to go back should not damage it as long as you use high-quality styling products and avoid using excessive heat or harsh chemicals.

Hair Care for Training Your Hair to Go Back

It's important to take care of your hair while you're training it to go back. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using excessive heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or flat irons.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and tricks, you can train your hair to go back and achieve a stylish, low-maintenance hairstyle.
