How To Cut Your Own Hair Short Black Girl A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a black girl looking to cut your own hair short, you've come to the right place. Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and guidance, you can do it yourself. In this article, we'll go through a step-by-step guide on how to cut your hair short, as well as tips and FAQs to help make the process easier. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Your Hair Short
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Your Hair Short
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair Short

How to Cut Your Hair Short

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, it's important to understand the basics of cutting your hair short. Short haircuts are typically defined as cuts that fall above the shoulders. They can range from a pixie cut to a bob. When cutting your hair short, it's important to have the right tools, including hair clippers, scissors, and a comb. You'll also want to have a mirror and a spray bottle filled with water to help you see and manage your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools ready, let's go through the step-by-step process of cutting your hair short.

  1. Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: the front, back, and two sides. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.
  2. Starting with the back section, wet your hair with the spray bottle and comb it straight down. Cut the hair in a straight line, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Use the hair clippers to trim the ends.
  3. Move on to the side sections, wetting and combing them down. Use the hair clippers to create a tapered look, trimming the sides shorter than the back. Make sure both sides are even.
  4. Finally, move on to the front section. Wet and comb your hair forward, and cut it to your desired length. You can create bangs or leave them longer, depending on your preference.
  5. Once you've finished cutting your hair, use the scissors to trim any uneven or stray hairs. Use the mirror to check the back of your head and make sure everything is even.

Congratulations, you've successfully cut your hair short!

Tips for Cutting Your Hair Short

Here are some tips to make the process of cutting your hair short easier:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't undo a big cut.
  • Use hair clippers with a guard to ensure an even cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and stressful, so don't feel like you need to do it all in one sitting.
  • Watch tutorials and read articles like this one to get a better understanding of the process.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair short:

  • Can I cut my hair short if it's already relaxed? Yes, you can cut your hair short if it's relaxed. Just make sure to use the right tools and techniques to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Do I need to wash my hair before cutting it? It's recommended to cut your hair when it's clean and dry, as wet hair can be harder to manage. However, if you prefer to cut your hair when it's wet, make sure to comb it straight down to ensure an even cut.
  • How often should I cut my hair? It's recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your short haircut.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair Short

After cutting your hair short, it's important to take care of it to ensure it stays healthy and stylish. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain your short haircut.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.

Cutting your own hair short can be a fun and empowering experience. With the right tools and guidance, you can achieve the perfect short haircut in the comfort of your own home. So, grab your tools and get cutting!
