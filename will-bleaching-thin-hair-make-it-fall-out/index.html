Will Bleaching Thin Hair Make It Fall Out

Written by Aprilia Apr 05, 2023 · 7 min read

Table of Content: Description, How to Bleach Thin Hair, Step by Step Guide on Bleaching Thin Hair, Tips for Bleaching Thin Hair without Damaging It, Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) about Bleaching Thin Hair, Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Bleached Thin Hair

Description

Bleaching thin hair can be a scary thought. Many people worry that bleaching their hair will cause it to fall out, but is this really the case? In short, the answer is yes, bleaching thin hair can cause it to fall out. However, it is important to understand why this happens and how you can prevent it from happening to your hair.

How to Bleach Thin Hair

Bleaching thin hair requires a different approach than bleaching thicker hair. You need to be more careful and cautious to prevent damage. Here are some steps to follow when bleaching thin hair:

Step by Step Guide on Bleaching Thin Hair

  1. Choose the right bleach: Use a bleach that is specifically designed for thin hair. It should be gentle and have moisturizing properties.
  2. Prepare your hair: Wash your hair and make sure it is completely dry before bleaching. This will help prevent damage and breakage.
  3. Protect your scalp: Apply petroleum jelly or a protective cream to your scalp to prevent the bleach from burning your skin.
  4. Apply the bleach: Follow the instructions on the bleach kit and apply the bleach to your hair. Make sure to avoid the roots and focus on the ends of your hair.
  5. Check your hair: Check your hair every 10 minutes to see how it is processing. Do not leave the bleach on for too long as this can cause damage to your hair.
  6. Rinse your hair: Once your hair has reached the desired level of lightness, rinse it thoroughly with warm water.

Tips for Bleaching Thin Hair without Damaging It

Here are some tips to help you bleaching your thin hair without damaging it:

  • Use a low volume developer: Use a 10 or 20 volume developer to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair: Use a deep conditioning treatment before and after bleaching to protect your hair from damage.
  • Avoid heat styling: Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair for a few days after bleaching to prevent further damage.
  • Be gentle: Be gentle when brushing or styling your hair to prevent breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) about Bleaching Thin Hair

Q: Can bleaching thin hair cause it to fall out?

A: Yes, bleaching thin hair can cause it to fall out. This is because the bleach can weaken the hair, causing it to break and fall out.

Q: How can I prevent my thin hair from falling out when bleaching it?

A: You can prevent your thin hair from falling out by using a gentle bleach, protecting your hair and scalp, and using a low volume developer.

Q: Is it safe to bleach thin hair?

A: Yes, it is safe to bleach thin hair as long as you take the necessary precautions and follow the instructions carefully.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Bleached Thin Hair

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining bleached thin hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair as much as possible.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Overall, while bleaching thin hair can cause it to fall out, taking the necessary precautions and following the instructions carefully can help you achieve the look you want without damaging your hair. Remember to take care of your hair after bleaching it to keep it healthy and strong.
