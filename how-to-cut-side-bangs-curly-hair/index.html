How To Cut Side Bangs On Curly Hair Step By Step Guide

Description

If you have naturally curly hair and want to add some style to your look, side bangs can be a great option. Side bangs can frame your face and add some flair to your curls. However, cutting bangs on curly hair can be tricky, and it's important to know what you're doing to avoid a disaster. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut side bangs on curly hair, as well as some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect look.

How to Cut Side Bangs on Curly Hair

Cutting side bangs on curly hair is not the same as cutting them on straight hair. Curly hair has a tendency to shrink up when it dries, so you need to take that into account when cutting your bangs. Here are the basic steps to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would, and let it air dry until it's about 80% dry.
  2. Part your hair where you want your bangs to start. If you're not sure where to part your hair, try a few different options and see which one looks best.
  3. Comb your hair forward so that it covers your forehead, and use hair clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.
  4. Using sharp hair cutting scissors, start cutting your bangs in small sections. Cut each section a little longer than you want it to be, as curly hair will shrink up when it dries.
  5. Take your time and cut slowly, checking your progress as you go. It's better to cut a little at a time than to cut too much and have to start over.
  6. When you're finished cutting your bangs, shake your hair out and let it dry completely. This will give you a better idea of how your bangs will look once they're dry.
  7. If you need to make any adjustments, do so carefully and in small increments.
  8. When you're happy with your bangs, style them as you normally would and enjoy your new look!

Tips for Cutting Side Bangs on Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best results when cutting side bangs on curly hair:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as curly hair will shrink up as it dries.
  • Take your time and cut slowly to avoid making any mistakes.
  • Check your progress frequently to make sure you're on track.
  • If you're not sure about cutting your own bangs, consider going to a professional stylist.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about cutting side bangs on curly hair:

Q: Can I cut my own side bangs on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut your own side bangs on curly hair if you follow the right steps and take your time.

Q: Should I cut my bangs when my hair is wet or dry?

A: You should cut your bangs when your hair is about 80% dry, as curly hair will shrink up as it dries.

Q: How long should my side bangs be?

A: The length of your side bangs will depend on your personal preference and face shape. Try cutting them a little longer than you want them to be, as curly hair will shrink up when it dries.

Hair Care after Cutting Side Bangs on Curly Hair

After cutting side bangs on curly hair, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a diffuser when blow drying your hair to avoid frizz.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy your new side bangs on curly hair and look your best every day.
