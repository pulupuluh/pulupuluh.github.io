Should You Have A Fringe At 50

Written by Daniel Jun 24, 2023 · 7 min read

Welcome to our blog post about whether you should have a fringe at 50. This is a topic that many women in their 50s may be wondering about, and we’re here to provide some insight and guidance. In this article, we’ll be covering how to decide if a fringe is right for you, how to get the perfect fringe, and how to care for your new hairstyle. So, let’s dive in!

Table of Contents

  • How to Decide if a Fringe is Right for You
  • How to Get the Perfect Fringe
  • How to Care for Your New Hairstyle
  • FAQ

How to Decide if a Fringe is Right for You

Deciding whether or not to get a fringe can be a tough decision, especially if you’ve never had one before. Here are some things to consider when deciding if a fringe is right for you:

  • Your face shape: If you have a round face, a fringe can help to elongate it. If you have a square face, a soft, side-swept fringe can help to soften your features.
  • Your hair texture: If you have fine hair, a fringe can add some much-needed volume and texture. If you have thick hair, a fringe can help to break up your hair and add some dimension.
  • Your lifestyle: If you’re someone who likes to wash and go, a fringe may not be the best option for you. A fringe requires more maintenance and styling than other hairstyles.

Ultimately, the decision to get a fringe is a personal one. If you’re unsure, talk to your hairstylist and get their opinion.

How to Get the Perfect Fringe

Getting the perfect fringe is all about finding the right style for your face shape and hair texture. Here are some tips to help you get the perfect fringe:

  • Consult with your hairstylist: Your hairstylist can help you find the right style for your face shape and hair texture.
  • Consider a side-swept fringe: A side-swept fringe is a great option for women in their 50s as it’s a softer, more flattering look.
  • Don’t cut your fringe too short: A fringe that’s too short can make you look younger than you are.

Remember, it’s always better to start with a longer fringe and work your way shorter than to cut too much off and have to wait for it to grow out.

How to Care for Your New Hairstyle

Once you’ve got your new fringe, it’s important to take care of it. Here are some tips for caring for your new hairstyle:

  • Wash your fringe separately: Your fringe is more prone to getting oily and dirty than the rest of your hair, so it’s important to wash it separately and more frequently.
  • Blow dry your fringe: Use a round brush to blow dry your fringe and give it some volume.
  • Trim your fringe regularly: To keep your fringe looking fresh and neat, it’s important to trim it every 4-6 weeks.

FAQ

Q: Will a fringe make me look younger?

A: It depends on the style of fringe. A short, blunt fringe can make you look younger, while a longer, side-swept fringe can be more flattering and age-appropriate.

Q: Can I wear a fringe with glasses?

A: Yes, you can! Just make sure your fringe doesn’t interfere with your glasses and that it’s the right length to complement your frames.

Q: Can I style my fringe differently?

A: Yes, you can! Experiment with different styles, such as pinning your fringe back or sweeping it to the side.

Q: How do I know if a fringe is the right choice for me?

A: Talk to your hairstylist and consider your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle. Your hairstylist can help you find the right style for you.

Conclusion

Deciding whether or not to get a fringe can be a tough decision, but hopefully, this article has provided you with some guidance and insight. Remember to consider your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle when deciding if a fringe is right for you. And once you’ve got your new fringe, take care of it by washing it separately, blow drying it, and trimming it regularly. With the right care and styling, a fringe can be a great addition to your hairstyle!
