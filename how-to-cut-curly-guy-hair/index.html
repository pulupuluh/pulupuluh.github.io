How To Cut Curly Guy Hair A Complete Guide

Curly hair can be tricky to cut, but with the right technique and tools, it's possible to achieve a great result. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut curly guy hair step by step.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Curly Guy Hair

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a spray bottle filled with water, a comb, and clips to hold sections of hair in place.

It's important to cut curly hair when it's dry so you can see how the hair will fall naturally. Wet hair can stretch and look longer than it actually is, which can result in uneven cuts.

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair. Use the comb to divide the hair into four sections: the top, sides, and back. Clip each section in place.

Step 2: Cut the Sides

Start with the sides. Use the comb to pull the hair outwards and away from the head. Cut the hair at an angle, following the natural curve of the head. Cut in small sections, working your way around the head.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Next, move on to the back. Use the same technique as with the sides, cutting the hair at an angle and following the natural curve of the head.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Finally, move on to the top. Use the comb to pull the hair upwards and away from the head. Cut the hair at an angle, following the natural shape of the head. Cut in small sections, working your way around the head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to cutting curly guy hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Wash the hair and let it air dry completely. Cutting curly hair when it's wet can result in uneven cuts.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use the comb to divide the hair into four sections: the top, sides, and back. Clip each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Start with the sides. Use the comb to pull the hair outwards and away from the head. Cut the hair at an angle, following the natural curve of the head. Cut in small sections, working your way around the head.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Next, move on to the back. Use the same technique as with the sides, cutting the hair at an angle and following the natural curve of the head.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Finally, move on to the top. Use the comb to pull the hair upwards and away from the head. Cut the hair at an angle, following the natural shape of the head. Cut in small sections, working your way around the head.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure an even cut
  • Follow the natural shape of the head and hair
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp while cutting
  • Don't cut too much off at once

FAQ

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Can I cut curly hair with clippers?

While it's possible to cut curly hair with clippers, it's not recommended. Clippers can cause the hair to become frizzy and uneven.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

After cutting your curly hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and prevent frizz. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to cut curly guy hair with ease. Remember to take your time and follow the natural shape of the hair to achieve the best results.
