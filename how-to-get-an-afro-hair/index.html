How To Get An Afro Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Mar 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your straight hair and want to try something new? Have you always admired the beauty of afro hair and want to know how to get it? Look no further! In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to get an afro hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

An afro hair is a natural hair type that is characterized by tight curls or coils that grow outwards from the scalp. It is a popular hair type among people of African descent, but anyone can achieve an afro hair with the right techniques.

How to

If you want to get an afro hair, there are two main ways to do it: natural and relaxed. Natural afro hair is achieved by embracing your natural curls and using products to define and enhance them. Relaxed afro hair, on the other hand, involves chemically relaxing your hair to make it straight and then using techniques to create an afro texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to get an afro hair with the relaxed method:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup. Rinse thoroughly and then apply a deep conditioner to moisturize your hair. Leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 2: Apply a relaxer

Apply a relaxer to your hair, following the instructions on the package. Be sure to apply it evenly and avoid overlapping on previously relaxed hair. Leave the relaxer on for the recommended time, then rinse it out with warm water.

Step 3: Neutralize your hair

Apply a neutralizing shampoo to your hair to stop the chemical process of the relaxer. Leave it on for a few minutes and then rinse it out thoroughly with warm water.

Step 4: Create the afro texture

Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser attachment to create volume and texture. Use a pick comb to create the afro texture by picking the hair outwards from the scalp. You can also use a curling iron to create tighter curls or a flat iron to create looser curls.

Step 5: Style your hair

Style your hair as desired using a hair pick or your fingers. You can also use hair accessories such as headbands, scarves or hair clips to add some flair to your afro hair.

Tips

  • Use a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or using heat styling tools to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep it healthy and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using too much product on your afro hair as it can weigh it down and make it look greasy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Q: Can I get an afro hair if I have naturally straight hair?

A: Yes, you can achieve an afro hair by either using heat styling tools or getting a relaxer.

Q: How often should I wash my afro hair?

A: It is recommended to wash your afro hair once a week to prevent dryness and breakage.

Hair Care

Caring for your afro hair is essential to maintain its health and beauty. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush to prevent breakage.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or using a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair as it can strip it of its natural oils and cause dryness.
  • Use a deep conditioner at least once a week to moisturize your hair and prevent breakage.

Now that you know how to get an afro hair, go ahead and try it out! Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
