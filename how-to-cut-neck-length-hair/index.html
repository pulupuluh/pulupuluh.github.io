How To Cut Neck Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're tired of going to the salon every few weeks to get your neck length hair trimmed, then you may want to consider cutting it yourself. Cutting your own hair can be a bit daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without leaving your home. In this article, we'll provide you with a step by step guide on how to cut neck length hair.

Description

Neck length hair is a popular hairstyle among women, and it's easy to see why. It's versatile, easy to maintain, and looks great on almost anyone. However, getting regular trims at the salon can be expensive and time-consuming. By learning how to cut your own neck length hair, you can save money and time while still looking fabulous.

How to Cut Neck Length Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own neck length hair is to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. Make sure that your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's essential to wash and dry it thoroughly. This will remove any dirt or oil buildup and make it easier to cut. Once your hair is dry, use a comb to detangle any knots or tangles.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into four sections: two front sections and two back sections. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Trim the Back Sections

Starting with the back sections, remove one clip at a time and comb the hair straight down. Use your scissors to trim the ends of the hair, cutting off only a small amount at a time. Be sure to cut in a straight line and avoid cutting at an angle, as this can cause unevenness.

Step 5: Trim the Front Sections

Once you've finished trimming the back sections, move on to the front sections. Comb the hair straight down and trim the ends in the same way as the back sections. Be sure to cut the hair evenly on both sides.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

After trimming all four sections, remove the hair clips and use your comb to blend the layers together. Comb the hair straight down and trim any uneven pieces until everything is even.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, use a mirror to check the back of your head. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that everything is even and straight.

Tips for Cutting Neck Length Hair

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always trim more if needed, but you can't add hair back once it's cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure about anything.

FAQs

1. Can I cut my own neck length hair?

Yes, you can cut your own neck length hair with the right tools and techniques. It may take some practice, but it's definitely doable.

2. How often should I trim my neck length hair?

It's recommended to trim your neck length hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's essential to take care of it properly to ensure that it stays healthy and looks great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your hair's shape and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Learning how to cut your own neck length hair can save you time and money while still looking fabulous. By following our step by step guide and tips, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and be patient. With practice, you'll become a pro at cutting your own hair in no time.
