How To Get Rid Of Red Tint In Blonde Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Jul 30, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of your blonde hair having a red tint? Don’t worry, you’re not alone. Many people with blonde hair struggle with this issue. But the good news is, there are several ways to get rid of the red tint in your blonde hair. In this article, we’ll walk you through how to do it, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Rid of Red Tint in Blonde Hair
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Get Rid of Red Tint in Blonde Hair

The first thing you need to do is identify the cause of the red tint in your blonde hair. There are several reasons why this may be happening. It could be due to the type of hair dye you’re using, the type of water you’re washing your hair with, or even the products you’re using to style your hair.

Once you’ve identified the cause, you can take steps to eliminate it. Here are some of the most effective ways to get rid of red tint in blonde hair:

1. Use a Toning Shampoo

Toning shampoos are specifically designed to neutralize unwanted brassy and yellow tones in blonde hair. Look for a toning shampoo that has a blue or purple tint, as these colors are opposite to red on the color wheel and will cancel it out.

2. Try a Hair Gloss

A hair gloss is a semi-permanent treatment that adds shine and color to your hair. It can help to neutralize any red tones and leave your hair looking shiny and healthy.

3. Use a Hair Mask

A hair mask can help to restore your hair’s natural color and balance. Look for a mask that is specifically designed for blonde hair, and contains ingredients like chamomile and lavender to help neutralize red tones.

4. Get a Professional Color Correction

If your red tint is particularly stubborn, you may need to seek out a professional color correction. A stylist can help to neutralize the red tones in your hair and restore it to its natural blonde color.

Step-by-Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide to getting rid of the red tint in your blonde hair:

Step 1: Identify the Cause

As we mentioned earlier, the first step is to identify the cause of the red tint. Once you know what’s causing it, you can take steps to eliminate it.

Step 2: Use a Toning Shampoo

Start by using a toning shampoo to neutralize the red tones in your hair. Use it every time you wash your hair until you start to see a difference.

Step 3: Try a Hair Gloss

If the toning shampoo isn’t enough, try using a hair gloss to further neutralize the red tones.

Step 4: Use a Hair Mask

To restore your hair’s natural balance, use a hair mask designed for blonde hair. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 5: Get a Professional Color Correction

If all else fails, seek out a professional color correction to get rid of the red tint in your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get rid of the red tint in your blonde hair:

  • Use a shower filter to remove any minerals or impurities from the water you’re using to wash your hair.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make the red tint more noticeable.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help protect your hair from damage and keep it looking healthy.

FAQ

Can I get rid of red tint in my blonde hair without using hair dye?

Yes, there are several ways to get rid of red tint in blonde hair without using hair dye. Some of the most effective methods include using a toning shampoo, hair gloss, or hair mask.

What causes red tint in blonde hair?

Red tint in blonde hair can be caused by several factors, including the type of hair dye you’re using, the type of water you’re washing your hair with, or even the products you’re using to style your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your blonde hair looking its best, it’s important to take good care of it. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy, shiny hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can dry it out and make it more prone to damage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat-styling tools to help protect your hair from damage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using the methods we’ve outlined in this article, you can get rid of the red tint in your blonde hair and keep it looking its best.
