How To Cut Your Own Hair Medium Length Layers

If you're looking for a way to freshen up your look without splurging on a professional haircut, why not try cutting your own hair? With a little bit of practice and patience, you can achieve a stylish and trendy medium length layered haircut right in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own hair medium length layers step by step, as well as provide you with some tips, FAQ and hair care advice to ensure you get the best possible results.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Medium Length Layers

Cutting your own hair can seem daunting, but with a few simple steps, you can achieve salon-worthy results. Here's how to cut your own hair medium length layers:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. This will help you to see the natural fall of your hair and ensure that you're cutting it evenly.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Using a comb, divide your hair into four equal sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting at the back, remove the clip from the bottom section of your hair. Comb through the hair to ensure there are no tangles, then hold the hair between your fingers at the desired length and cut it straight across. Repeat this process for the other three sections of your hair.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you've cut the bottom layer, remove the clip from the middle section of your hair. Comb through the hair to ensure there are no tangles, then hold the hair between your fingers at the desired length and cut it at a slight angle. This will help to create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, remove the clip from the top section of your hair. Comb through the hair to ensure there are no tangles, then hold the hair between your fingers at the desired length and cut it at a slightly steeper angle than the middle layer. This will help to create more texture and movement in your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to cutting your own hair medium length layers:

  1. Wash and dry your hair
  2. Divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back
  3. Starting at the back, remove the clip from the bottom section of your hair
  4. Comb through the hair to ensure there are no tangles
  5. Hold the hair between your fingers at the desired length and cut it straight across
  6. Repeat this process for the other three sections of your hair
  7. Once you've cut the bottom layer, remove the clip from the middle section of your hair
  8. Comb through the hair to ensure there are no tangles
  9. Hold the hair between your fingers at the desired length and cut it at a slight angle
  10. Repeat this process for the top section of your hair, cutting it at a slightly steeper angle than the middle layer

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors - they'll make all the difference!
  • Take your time and don't rush the process
  • Start with small snips and work your way up - you can always cut more off, but you can't put it back on
  • Ask a friend or family member for help, especially when cutting the back of your hair
  • Watch YouTube tutorials to get a better understanding of the process

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended that you cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut your hair too short, don't panic. You can always style it differently or wear a hair accessory until it grows out.

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Cutting curly hair can be a bit trickier, so it's best to seek the help of a professional if you're not confident in your abilities.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've cut your own hair medium length layers, it's important to take care of it properly to ensure it stays healthy and looks great. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and nourished
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye, as they can cause damage

By following these tips and practicing your hair cutting skills, you can achieve a stylish and trendy medium length layered haircut right at home. Good luck!
