How Do I Find Out My Face Shape

Written by Daniel May 26, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you struggling to figure out your face shape? Don't worry, you're not alone. Knowing your face shape can help you choose the most flattering hairstyles, makeup looks, and glasses frames. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of determining your face shape, step by step. We'll also provide some tips and answer frequently asked questions to make sure you have all the information you need.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Measure Your Face
  • Step 2: Identify Your Face Shape
  • Tips for Choosing Flattering Hairstyles and Makeup
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Your Face Shape

Step 1: Measure Your Face

The first step in determining your face shape is to measure your face. Grab a tape measure and measure the following:

  • Forehead: Measure the width of your forehead from one hairline to the other.
  • Cheekbones: Measure the width of your cheekbones from the highest point of one cheekbone to the highest point of the other.
  • Jawline: Measure the width of your jawline from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jaw angles upward.
  • Face length: Measure the length of your face from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Write down these measurements so you can reference them later.

Step 2: Identify Your Face Shape

Now that you have your measurements, it's time to identify your face shape. Here are the most common face shapes:

Round Face Shape

A round face shape is characterized by a forehead and jawline that are similar in width, and full cheeks.

To confirm you have a round face shape, your face length measurement should be less than your cheekbones measurement, and your forehead and jawline measurements should be close to the same.

Oval Face Shape

An oval face shape is characterized by a face length that is greater than the width of the cheekbones, and a gently rounded hairline.

To confirm you have an oval face shape, your face length measurement should be greater than your cheekbones measurement, and your forehead measurement should be slightly wider than your jawline measurement.

Square Face Shape

A square face shape is characterized by a forehead, cheekbones, and jawline that are similar in width, and a strong, square jawline.

To confirm you have a square face shape, all four of your measurements should be similar in width.

Heart Face Shape

A heart face shape is characterized by a face shape that is wider at the temples and narrows at the jawline, and a pointed chin.

To confirm you have a heart face shape, your forehead measurement should be wider than your cheekbones measurement, and your jawline should be narrow and pointed.

Diamond Face Shape

A diamond face shape is characterized by a narrow forehead and jawline, and wide cheekbones.

To confirm you have a diamond face shape, your cheekbones measurement should be the widest, and your forehead and jawline measurements should be similar and narrower than your cheekbones measurement.

Tips for Choosing Flattering Hairstyles and Makeup

Now that you know your face shape, you can use this information to choose hairstyles and makeup that flatter your features. Here are some general tips:

  • Round face shape: Look for hairstyles that add height to the crown of your head, and avoid blunt bangs and chin-length cuts. Use contouring makeup techniques to create the illusion of a more angular jawline.
  • Oval face shape: Lucky you, almost every hairstyle and makeup look will flatter your face shape! Experiment with different styles to find your favorites.
  • Square face shape: Look for hairstyles that soften your strong jawline, such as layered cuts and side-swept bangs. Use blush and bronzer to soften the angles of your face.
  • Heart face shape: Look for hairstyles that add volume to the chin area, such as chin-length cuts and side-parted bangs. Use blush to soften the width of your forehead.
  • Diamond face shape: Look for hairstyles that create width at the forehead and jawline, such as bobs and pixie cuts with side-swept bangs. Use contouring makeup techniques to create the illusion of a more rounded forehead and jawline.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if my measurements don't match any of the face shapes?

It's possible that you have a combination of face shapes, or that your face shape doesn't fit into one of the traditional categories. Don't worry too much about labeling your face shape - use the general tips above to choose hairstyles and makeup that flatter your features.

Can my face shape change over time?

It's unlikely that your face shape will change significantly over time, but weight gain or loss can affect the appearance of your face shape.

Do face shapes matter for glasses frames?

Yes, certain glasses frames will flatter different face shapes. For example, round frames can complement a square face shape, while rectangular frames can complement a round face shape.

Hair Care for Your Face Shape

Once you've figured out your face shape and chosen a flattering hairstyle, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some general hair care tips:

  • Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and free of split ends.
  • Consider using hair masks or deep conditioning treatments once a week to keep your hair moisturized and shiny.

By following these tips, you can look and feel your best, no matter what your face shape may be. Remember, the most important thing is to feel confident and comfortable in your own skin!
