How To Cut Men s Short Haircuts A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 05, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the barber every few weeks for a simple haircut? You don't have to be a professional to give yourself or someone else a great short haircut. With a few tools and some practice, anyone can learn how to cut men's short haircuts.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather a few basic tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Water spray bottle
  • Small mirror

Make sure your clippers have different guard sizes to allow for varying lengths of hair.

Prepping the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. Wet hair is easier to cut, but make sure it's not dripping wet. Comb through the hair to remove any tangles and make sure it's evenly distributed. Drape the haircutting cape or towel around the person's shoulders and secure it with a clip or elastic band.

Choosing the Right Length

Decide on the length you want to cut the hair. Use the clippers with the desired guard size to go over the entire head, starting from the bottom and working your way up. This will create an even base for the haircut.

Cutting the Hair

Use the scissors to start snipping away at the hair. Start from the top and work your way down, using the comb to hold the hair in place and to guide your snips. Take small sections of hair at a time to ensure an even cut. Focus on one section at a time, making sure to check your work as you go.

Blending the Hair

Use the clippers without a guard to blend the hair on the sides and back of the head. Start from the bottom and work your way up, using short strokes to blend the hair together. Be careful not to cut too high up on the head, as this can create an uneven look.

Finishing Touches

Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. Check your work in the mirror from all angles to ensure an even cut. Use the clippers around the edges of the hairline and neck to clean up any stray hairs.

Tips for a Great Haircut

  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Use a spray bottle to mist the hair while cutting to keep it damp and easier to work with.
  • Watch tutorials or practice on a mannequin head before attempting to cut someone's hair.
  • Use a comb to help guide your scissors and clippers for an even cut.
  • Invest in high-quality tools to make the process easier and more effective.

FAQs

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you keep it. For a short haircut, plan on cutting it every 3-4 weeks to maintain the style.

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, with practice and the right tools, anyone can learn how to cut their own hair.

Hair Care After the Cut

After the haircut, wash and condition the hair to remove any loose hairs. Style as desired and use a product like pomade or gel to keep the hair in place.

Conclusion

Cutting men's short haircuts doesn't have to be intimidating. With a few basic tools and some practice, anyone can give a great haircut. Follow these steps and tips for a successful cut every time.
