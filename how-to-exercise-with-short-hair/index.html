How To Exercise With Short Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Apr 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Exercising with short hair can be a challenge, especially if you're used to long hair that can be tied up and out of the way. However, with a few simple tips and tricks, you can make your workouts more comfortable and keep your short hair looking great. In this article, we'll share our top tips for exercising with short hair, including how to keep your hair out of your face, how to prevent sweat from ruining your style, and how to care for your hair after your workout.

How to Keep Short Hair Out of Your Face During Exercise

One of the most annoying things about exercising with short hair is when it falls into your face and gets in the way of your workout. Here are some tips to help you keep your hair out of your eyes:

Use a Headband

A headband is a great way to keep your hair out of your face while you exercise. Choose a headband that's made from a moisture-wicking material, like cotton or spandex, so it won't slip off when you start to sweat. You can also choose a headband that matches your workout clothes for a stylish look.

Try a High Ponytail

If your hair is long enough, you can try pulling it back into a high ponytail. This will keep your hair off your face and neck, and you won't have to worry about it getting in your way. Use a hair tie that won't slip or break, and make sure to secure it tightly.

Use Bobby Pins

If you have shorter hair that won't stay in a ponytail, you can use bobby pins to keep it back. Part your hair down the middle and twist each section back, securing it with bobby pins. This will keep your hair out of your face and off your neck.

How to Prevent Sweat from Ruining Your Style

Another challenge of exercising with short hair is dealing with sweat. Here's how to prevent sweat from ruining your hairstyle:

Use Dry Shampoo

If you don't have time to wash your hair after your workout, use dry shampoo to soak up sweat and oil. Spray it on your roots and use your fingers to work it in. This will help your hair look fresh and clean, even if you're short on time.

Wrap Your Hair in a Towel

After your workout, wrap your hair in a towel to absorb sweat and moisture. This will help prevent your hair from becoming frizzy or flat. Leave the towel on for a few minutes, then take it off and let your hair air dry.

How to Care for Your Hair After Your Workout

Finally, it's important to take care of your hair after your workout to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips:

Don't Brush Your Hair When It's Wet

When your hair is wet, it's more fragile and prone to breakage. Instead of brushing it, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it gently. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, like braids or ponytails, can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles that won't strain your hair. If you do wear a ponytail, use a hair tie that won't damage your hair, like a scrunchie or a fabric hair tie.

Moisturize Your Hair

Exercising can dry out your hair, so it's important to moisturize it regularly. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. You can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about exercising with short hair:

Q: Can I wear a hat or a visor to keep my hair out of my face?

A: Yes, you can wear a hat or a visor to keep your hair out of your face. Just make sure it's made from a breathable material, like cotton, so it won't trap sweat on your head.

Q: Can I wash my hair every day?

A: It's not necessary to wash your hair every day, but if you do, make sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage. You can also use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.

Q: How can I prevent my hair from smelling bad after a workout?

A: To prevent your hair from smelling bad after a workout, use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil with a pleasant scent. You can also use a dry shampoo to absorb sweat and oil.

Conclusion

Exercising with short hair doesn't have to be a hassle. By using the tips and tricks we've shared in this article, you can keep your hair out of your face, prevent sweat from ruining your style, and care for your hair after your workout. With a little bit of effort, you can look and feel great while you exercise!
