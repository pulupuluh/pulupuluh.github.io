2. Parasites

Parasites such as fleas and ticks can cause your dog to lose hair. If you notice your dog scratching excessively, it might be a sign of a parasite infestation. Take your dog to the vet to get the right treatment and prevent further hair loss.

3. Hormonal Imbalance

A hormonal imbalance can cause hair loss in dogs, especially in female dogs. If you notice your dog losing hair around their tail or belly, it might be a sign of a hormonal imbalance. Take your dog to the vet to get the right treatment and prevent further hair loss.

4. Stress

Dogs can lose hair due to stress. If your dog has experienced a significant change in their environment, it might cause them to lose hair. Provide your dog with a calm and stable environment to reduce stress and prevent further hair loss.

Preventing excessive shedding in dogs requires a few simple steps:

1. Regular Grooming
