How To Achieve The Perfect 40S Hair Look

Written by Frank Jul 14, 2023 Â· 8 min read

The 1940s was a decade of glamour and sophistication, and one of the most iconic features of the era was the hairstyles. If you're looking to recreate the classic look of 40s hair, you've come to the right place! In this article, we'll guide you through the steps to achieve the perfect 40s hairstyle, from choosing the right cut to styling your hair.

How to Choose the Right Cut?

The first step in achieving the perfect 40s hair look is to choose the right cut. The most popular hairstyles of the era were the Victory Roll, the Pageboy, and the Pompadour. The Victory Roll was a signature look for women in the 1940s, with hair rolled up and away from the face. The Pageboy was a sleek, straight cut that was often paired with bangs. The Pompadour, on the other hand, was a more voluminous style that involved teasing and curling the hair.

Before you choose a cut, consider your face shape and hair texture. If you have a round face, a Pageboy cut with bangs may be more flattering. If you have thin or fine hair, a Pompadour may not be the best option, as it requires a lot of teasing and backcombing. Talk to your stylist about which cut would work best for you.

Step-by-Step Guide to Achieving 40s Hair

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are formulated for your hair type. Use a small amount of conditioner, focusing on the ends of your hair, and rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Before you begin styling your hair, apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from hot tools. This will also help your hairstyle last longer.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair until it is completely dry. If you have bangs, use a smaller brush to smooth them out.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting at the nape of your neck. Pin the top sections of your hair up, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 5: Curl Your Hair

Using a curling iron or hot rollers, curl the bottom section of your hair. Hold each curl for a few seconds before releasing it, and pin it in place to cool.

Step 6: Roll Your Hair

Once all of your hair is curled and cooled, remove the pins and start rolling your hair. For a Victory Roll, take a section of hair from the front of your head and roll it backwards towards the crown, securing it with bobby pins. For a Pompadour, tease the top section of your hair and roll it backwards, securing it with bobby pins.

Step 7: Smooth Out Your Hair

Use a comb or brush to smooth out any bumps or flyaways in your hairstyle. You can also use hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Achieving the Perfect 40s Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect 40s hair look:

  • Use hot rollers instead of a curling iron for a more voluminous look.
  • Tease your hair at the crown for a more dramatic Pompadour.
  • Experiment with different hair accessories, such as headbands or scarves.
  • Use a lightweight hairspray to hold your hairstyle in place.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I achieve the 40s hair look with short hair?

A: Yes! The Pageboy cut was a popular style for women with short hair in the 1940s.

Q: Do I need to use hot rollers to achieve the 40s hair look?

A: No, you can also use a curling iron or even pin curls to achieve the look.

Q: How do I keep my 40s hair style in place?

A: Use a lightweight hairspray to hold your hairstyle in place, and avoid touching or brushing your hair too much throughout the day.

Hair Care Tips for 40s Hair

To keep your 40s hair looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools too frequently, and always use a heat protectant spray.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it healthy and shiny.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve the perfect 40s hair look in no time. Whether you're going for a glamorous Victory Roll or a sleek Pageboy, these styles are timeless and always in fashion.
