What Dog Breeds Have Hair Instead Of Fur

Written by Eveline Apr 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking for a dog breed that doesn't shed as much? Or maybe you or someone in your family has allergies to dog fur? Look no further, because there are several dog breeds that have hair instead of fur! In this article, we will go over what dog breeds have hair instead of fur, how to take care of their hair, and some common questions you may have.

Table of Contents

  1. Dog Breeds with Hair
  2. How to Take Care of Their Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide to Grooming
  4. Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair
  5. FAQs

Dog Breeds with Hair

Here are some dog breeds that have hair instead of fur:

Poodle

The poodle is a popular breed known for its curly, hypoallergenic hair. They come in different sizes, including standard, miniature, and toy.

Yorkshire Terrier

The Yorkshire Terrier, or Yorkie, is a small breed that has silky hair that can grow up to 10 inches long. They are known for being hypoallergenic and low-shedding.

Maltese

The Maltese is another small breed with long, silky hair that is hypoallergenic and low-shedding. They require regular grooming to keep their hair from matting.

Afghan Hound

The Afghan Hound has long, flowing hair that requires a lot of maintenance. They are a large breed and are known for their regal appearance.

Shih Tzu

The Shih Tzu is a small breed with long, straight hair that is hypoallergenic and low-shedding. They are known for their friendly and playful personalities.

If you are interested in getting one of these breeds, make sure to do your research and find a reputable breeder.

How to Take Care of Their Hair

Dogs with hair require regular grooming to keep their coats healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

Brushing

Brush your dog's hair regularly to prevent matting and tangling. Use a slicker brush or comb specifically designed for their hair type.

Bathing

Bathe your dog as needed, but not too often as it can dry out their skin. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for dogs.

Trimming

Trim your dog's hair regularly to keep it at a manageable length. You can do this yourself or take them to a professional groomer.

Step-by-Step Guide to Grooming

Here is a step-by-step guide to grooming your dog with hair:

  1. Brush your dog's hair to remove any tangles or mats.
  2. Bathe your dog using a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  3. Towel dry your dog and then blow dry their hair on a low setting.
  4. Trim their hair to the desired length using scissors or clippers.
  5. Use a comb to check for any remaining tangles or mats.
  6. Finish by brushing your dog's hair again to give it a shiny appearance.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your dog's healthy hair:

Diet

Feed your dog a healthy diet that is rich in nutrients and vitamins. This will help keep their hair healthy and shiny.

Exercise

Make sure your dog gets enough exercise to keep their coat healthy and prevent obesity. A healthy dog will have healthier hair.

Regular Check-ups

Take your dog to the vet regularly to make sure they are healthy and to catch any potential health problems early on.

FAQs

Are dogs with hair hypoallergenic?

Some dog breeds with hair are hypoallergenic, but not all. It depends on the individual dog and person's allergies.

Do dogs with hair shed?

Dogs with hair do shed, but typically not as much as dogs with fur. However, they still require regular grooming to prevent matting and tangling.

Can I groom my dog with hair myself?

Yes, you can groom your dog with hair yourself, but it may be helpful to take them to a professional groomer for a more thorough grooming.

By following these tips, you can maintain your dog's healthy hair and keep them looking their best!
