The Best Flat Iron For Thick Coarse Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline May 26, 2023 · 10 min read

If you have thick, coarse, and curly hair, finding the right flat iron can be a challenge. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this guide, we'll share with you the best flat iron for thick coarse curly hair, along with tips on how to use it and take care of your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Best Flat Iron for Thick Coarse Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide on How to Use a Flat Iron on Thick Coarse Curly Hair
  • Tips for Using a Flat Iron on Thick Coarse Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Description

The best flat iron for thick coarse curly hair is one that can handle high heat and has wide plates to cover more hair in one pass. Ceramic or tourmaline plates are also ideal as they distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz. Look for a flat iron that has adjustable temperature settings so you can choose the right heat for your hair type.

How to Choose the Best Flat Iron for Thick Coarse Curly Hair

When choosing a flat iron for thick coarse curly hair, consider the following factors:

Plate Material

As mentioned earlier, ceramic or tourmaline plates are ideal for thick coarse curly hair. These materials help to distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz. Titanium plates can also work, but they tend to get hotter than ceramic or tourmaline, so you need to be careful not to damage your hair.

Plate Size

The wider the plates, the better for thick coarse curly hair. Look for plates that are at least 1.5 inches wide to cover more hair in one pass. This will save you time and minimize heat damage to your hair.

Temperature Settings

Thick coarse curly hair requires high heat to straighten. However, you don't want to use too much heat as it can damage your hair. Look for a flat iron that has adjustable temperature settings so you can choose the right heat for your hair type. Generally, a temperature range of 350°F to 450°F is ideal for thick coarse curly hair.

Step-by-Step Guide on How to Use a Flat Iron on Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Follow these steps to straighten your thick coarse curly hair using a flat iron:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are suitable for your hair type. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel-dry it. Don't use a blow dryer as it can cause more frizz.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to minimize heat damage. This will also help your hair to stay straight for longer.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable parts using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to straighten your hair and ensure that you cover every strand.

Step 4: Turn on Your Flat Iron

Turn on your flat iron and set it to the desired temperature. Wait for it to heat up before using it on your hair.

Step 5: Straighten Your Hair

Starting from the bottom section, take a small part of your hair and place it between the plates of the flat iron. Slowly glide the flat iron down to the ends of your hair. Repeat this process on each section of your hair until you've covered all parts.

Step 6: Finish with Hair Serum

Finish by applying a hair serum to add shine and reduce frizz. You can also use a hairspray to hold your hair in place.

Tips for Using a Flat Iron on Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your flat iron:

  • Always use a heat protectant to minimize heat damage.
  • Section your hair into manageable parts to ensure that you cover every strand.
  • Don't use too much heat as it can damage your hair. Choose the right temperature for your hair type.
  • Choose a flat iron with wide plates to cover more hair in one pass.
  • Clean your flat iron regularly to remove product buildup and maintain its performance.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a flat iron on wet hair?

A: No, you should never use a flat iron on wet hair. Wet hair is more prone to damage, and using a flat iron on wet hair can cause steam to escape, which can burn your scalp.

Q: How often should I use a flat iron on my hair?

A: It's best to limit the use of a flat iron to once or twice a week to prevent heat damage to your hair.

Q: How can I prevent heat damage when using a flat iron?

A: Always use a heat protectant, choose the right temperature for your hair type, and don't use too much heat. You can also limit the use of a flat iron to once or twice a week to prevent heat damage.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain your thick coarse curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often as they can cause heat damage to your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry as it can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the health of your hair.

With the right flat iron and proper hair care, you can achieve beautifully straight hair that's healthy and shiny. Follow the tips and steps outlined in this guide to get the best results. Happy straightening!
