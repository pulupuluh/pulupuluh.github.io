How To 50S Hairstyles For Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 19, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking to channel your inner Marilyn Monroe or Audrey Hepburn? Look no further than the iconic hairstyles of the 1950s. With their voluminous curls and elegant updos, 50s hairstyles are still popular today. In this step-by-step guide, we’ll show you how to achieve three classic 50s hairstyles for long hair.

Table of Contents

  • Pincurls
  • Victory Rolls
  • French Twist

How to: Pincurls

Pincurls were a popular hairstyle in the 50s, and they’re still a great way to add volume and texture to long hair. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Part your hair in the middle, and section it into small pieces.
  2. Take a small section of hair, and wrap it tightly around your index finger. Keep the tension tight, and slide the curl off your finger.
  3. Hold the curl in place with a bobby pin. Continue curling and pinning sections of hair until your entire head is covered.
  4. Let the pincurls set for several hours or overnight. Once they’re completely dry, remove the pins and gently brush out the curls.

Pincurls are a great base for a variety of 50s hairstyles, including half-up styles and updos.

How to: Victory Rolls

Victory rolls are a classic 50s hairstyle, and they’re surprisingly easy to do. Here’s how:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Part your hair on one side, and section it into two parts: the back section and the side section.
  2. Take the side section, and create a large, loose curl with a curling iron or hot rollers.
  3. Brush out the curl, and roll it forward towards your face. Pin the roll in place with bobby pins.
  4. Repeat on the other side.
  5. Take the back section of your hair, and tease it at the roots for extra volume. Smooth the top layer of hair, and roll it up towards the back of your head. Pin in place.
  6. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Victory rolls are a great option for special occasions or just for fun.

How to: French Twist

The French twist is a classic updo that’s perfect for long hair. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Brush it back into a low ponytail, and secure with an elastic band.
  2. Split the ponytail in half, and twist each section tightly.
  3. Cross the two twisted sections over each other, and tuck the ends under the twist. Pin in place.
  4. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

The French twist is a timeless and elegant hairstyle that’s perfect for any occasion.

Tips for 50s Hairstyles

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect 50s hairstyle:

  • Use hot rollers or a curling iron to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Tease your hair at the roots to add extra volume.
  • Use bobby pins to secure your hairstyles in place.
  • Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Frequently Asked Questions

What products do I need for 50s hairstyles?

You’ll need a few basic products for 50s hairstyles, including hot rollers or a curling iron, bobby pins, and hairspray.

Can I do 50s hairstyles on short hair?

While some 50s hairstyles are more suited to long hair, there are still plenty of options for short hair, including pixie cuts and bobs.

Hair Care for 50s Hairstyles

To keep your hair looking healthy and shiny, be sure to use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner. Regular trims will also help keep split ends at bay. And don’t forget to protect your hair from heat damage with a heat protectant spray.

With these tips and techniques, you’ll be able to achieve classic 50s hairstyles for long hair. Whether you’re going for a glamorous Hollywood look or just want to add some retro flair to your everyday style, these hairstyles are sure to turn heads.
