How to Cut My Own Curly Bangs - Step by Step Guide

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it looks stunning, it can be challenging to manage, especially when it comes to cutting your own curly bangs. But with a few tips and tricks, it is possible to cut your own curly bangs without any hassle.

How to Cut My Own Curly Bangs

Here we have a complete step-by-step guide on how to cut your own curly bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your curly bangs, it is essential to wash and dry your hair thoroughly. This is because curly hair can shrink when it dries, so it's crucial to cut it in its natural state.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into three parts: the top, middle, and bottom. Clip the top and middle sections away and work on the bottom section first.

Step 3: Comb and Straighten Your Bangs

Comb your bangs using a wide-tooth comb and straighten them with a flat iron. This will make them easier to cut.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Start by cutting your bangs longer than you want them to be. This will give you room for error. Then, using a pair of sharp scissors, cut your bangs slowly and gradually. Remember to cut them while they are still straight.

Step 5: Check the Length

After cutting your bangs, let them fall naturally and check their length. If they are too long, trim them slightly until they are the desired length.

Step 6: Style Your Bangs

Once you are satisfied with the length of your bangs, style them as desired. You can use a curling iron to create loose curls or leave them straight.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Curly Bangs

Here are some tips to help you cut your own curly bangs:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Take your time when cutting your bangs. Rushing can result in uneven bangs.
  • Start by cutting your bangs longer than you want them to be to avoid cutting them too short.
  • Don't cut your bangs when they are wet. This can cause shrinkage, making your bangs appear shorter than you intended.
  • Consider your face shape and hair type when deciding on the length and style of your bangs.

FAQs About Cutting Your Own Curly Bangs

Can I cut my curly bangs with regular scissors?

No. Regular scissors are not sharp enough to cut curly hair, and they can cause split ends.

How often should I cut my curly bangs?

We recommend cutting your curly bangs every 6-8 weeks to maintain their length and shape.

Should I use hair products after cutting my curly bangs?

Yes. After cutting your curly bangs, apply a styling product to keep them in place and prevent frizz.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your curly hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry as it can cause frizz.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to enhance your curls.

Now that you know how to cut your own curly bangs, it's time to give it a try. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and consider your face shape and hair type when deciding on the length and style of your bangs. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting your own curly bangs in no time!
