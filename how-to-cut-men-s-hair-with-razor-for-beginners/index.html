How To Cut Men s Hair With Razor For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 03, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking for a way to give your hair a sleek, stylish look, you may want to consider cutting it with a razor. Not only is this technique a great way to add texture and movement to your hair, but it's also relatively easy to learn, even if you're a beginner.

Description

Cutting men's hair with a razor involves using a special razor tool to create longer, textured layers in the hair. This technique is often used to create a messy, beachy look or to add volume and texture to fine or thinning hair.

How to Cut Men's Hair with Razor for Beginners

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a razor tool designed for cutting hair, a comb or brush, and a pair of scissors for trimming any stray hairs.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair. Wash your hair with your usual shampoo and conditioner, then dry it completely with a towel or blow dryer.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb or brush, divide your hair into sections. Start by sectioning off the top of your head, then work your way down to the sides and back of your head.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold your razor tool at a 45-degree angle to your hair and start cutting the first section. Use short, quick strokes to create longer, textured layers in your hair. Be sure to work slowly and carefully, and avoid cutting too much hair at once.

Step 5: Continue Cutting

Move on to the next section of hair and repeat the same cutting technique. Continue working your way around your head until all sections have been cut.

Step 6: Trim Any Stray Hairs

Once you've finished cutting your hair with the razor, use your scissors to trim any stray hairs that may be sticking out or look uneven. Be sure to work carefully and only trim a little bit at a time.

Tips for Cutting Men's Hair with Razor

  • Start with a longer blade to avoid cutting too much hair at once
  • Use a comb or brush to separate the sections of hair before cutting
  • Work slowly and carefully to avoid cutting too much hair
  • Use a mirror or ask a friend to help you see the back of your head
  • Practice on a mannequin head or a willing friend before cutting your own hair

FAQ

Q: Is it easy to cut men's hair with a razor?

A: Yes, cutting men's hair with a razor is relatively easy to learn, even if you're a beginner.

Q: Do I need any special tools to cut men's hair with a razor?

A: Yes, you'll need a razor tool designed for cutting hair, a comb or brush, and a pair of scissors for trimming any stray hairs.

Q: Will cutting my hair with a razor damage it?

A: Cutting your hair with a razor can cause some damage, but if done correctly and with the right tools, it should not cause significant damage or breakage.

Hair Care After Cutting with Razor

After cutting your hair with a razor, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, and use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help keep it moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid tangling or breakage.
