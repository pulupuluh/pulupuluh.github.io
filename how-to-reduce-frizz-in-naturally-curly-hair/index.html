How To Reduce Frizz In Naturally Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Jul 13, 2023 · 8 min read

Do you struggle with frizz in your naturally curly hair? Frizzy hair can be frustrating, but there are steps you can take to reduce it. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks to help you achieve smooth, defined curls without the frizz.

Table of Contents

  • How to Reduce Frizz in Naturally Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Frizz-Free Curls

How to Reduce Frizz in Naturally Curly Hair

Frizz is often caused by dryness and lack of moisture in the hair. To reduce frizz, you need to focus on keeping your hair hydrated and moisturized. Here are some steps to take:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Harsh shampoos can strip the hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won’t dry out your hair.

2. Condition, Condition, Condition

Curly hair needs a lot of moisture, so make sure to use a hydrating conditioner every time you wash your hair. Apply the conditioner from mid-length to the ends of your hair, and leave it in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to lock in moisture and prevent frizz. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your damp hair before styling.

4. Don’t Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy. Try to wash your hair no more than twice a week, and use dry shampoo in between washes to refresh your hair.

5. Use a Microfiber Towel or T-Shirt to Dry Your Hair

A regular towel can be too rough on curly hair, causing frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze excess water from your hair.

6. Use a Diffuser When Blow-Drying Your Hair

A diffuser attachment on your blow dryer can help to evenly distribute heat and prevent frizz. Use the diffuser on low heat to dry your hair, and scrunch your hair with your hands as you go.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to reducing frizz in naturally curly hair:

1. Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.

2. Apply a hydrating conditioner from mid-length to ends.

3. Rinse out the conditioner and gently squeeze excess water from your hair using a microfiber towel or t-shirt.

4. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair.

5. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

6. Apply a styling product, such as a curl cream or gel, to your hair.

7. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on low heat, scrunching your hair with your hands as you go.

8. Once your hair is dry, use a small amount of hair oil or serum to smooth down any frizz.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you reduce frizz in your curly hair:

1. Sleep on a satin pillowcase or use a satin hair wrap to prevent frizz while you sleep.

2. Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.

4. Try a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.

FAQs

Q: Can I brush my curly hair to reduce frizz?

A: Brushing curly hair can actually make frizz worse. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead.

Q: Can I use a hair dryer on my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use a hair dryer on your curly hair, but make sure to use a diffuser attachment and dry your hair on low heat.

Q: Should I use a straightening iron to reduce frizz?

A: No, using a straightening iron can damage your hair and make frizz worse in the long run. Embrace your natural curls instead!

Hair Care Tips for Frizz-Free Curls

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you achieve frizz-free curls:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and hydrating conditioner.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

4. Apply a styling product, such as a curl cream or gel, to your hair.

5. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on low heat.

6. Sleep on a satin pillowcase or use a satin hair wrap to prevent frizz while you sleep.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve smooth, defined curls without the frizz. Embrace your natural curls and enjoy beautiful, healthy hair!
