How To Find My Natural Hair Part A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 26, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you struggling to find your natural hair part? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people have trouble figuring out where their hair naturally falls, especially if they've been styling it a certain way for a long time. But finding your natural hair part is important if you want to achieve the best possible hairstyle. In this article, we'll show you how to find your natural hair part in just a few simple steps.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair
  • Step 2: Let Your Hair Air Dry
  • Step 3: Look for the Widest Point
  • Step 4: Use a Comb to Define Your Part
  • Tips for Finding Your Natural Hair Part
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Natural Hair Part

Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair

The first step in finding your natural hair part is to wash and detangle your hair. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner to cleanse and condition your hair. After rinsing out the conditioner, gently comb through your hair with a wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Once your hair is detangled, let it air dry naturally. Don't use a blow dryer or any other heating tools, as they can disrupt your hair's natural texture and make it harder to find your natural part. If you're short on time, you can use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess moisture, but avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel.

Step 3: Look for the Widest Point

Once your hair is dry, stand in front of a mirror and look for the widest point on your head. This is usually the point where your hair naturally falls to one side or the other. If you're not sure which side this is, try parting your hair in the middle and then shifting it to one side or the other. You should be able to feel where your hair naturally wants to fall.

Step 4: Use a Comb to Define Your Part

Now that you've found the widest point on your head, use a fine-tooth comb to define your part. Place the comb at the point where your hair naturally falls, and then gently comb your hair back on either side of the part. You can create a deep part, a middle part, or a shallow part, depending on your preference.

Tips for Finding Your Natural Hair Part

  • Don't try to force your hair to part a certain way, as this can damage your hair and make it harder to find your natural part.
  • Experiment with different parts to see which one looks best on you.
  • If you have trouble finding your natural part, try using a hair product like mousse or gel to add volume to your hair. This can help you see where your hair naturally falls.

FAQs

What if I can't find my natural hair part?

If you're having trouble finding your natural hair part, don't worry. It can take some time to figure out where your hair naturally falls, especially if you've been styling it a certain way for a long time. Keep experimenting with different parts, and try using hair products to add volume and texture to your hair.

Can I change my hair part?

Yes, you can definitely change your hair part if you want to. However, it's important to be gentle with your hair and avoid pulling it too tightly in one direction or the other. You can also try using a hair product like mousse or gel to help hold your part in place.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Natural Hair Part

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently comb through your hair each day.
  • Avoid using heating tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and disrupt your natural part.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Try using a hair oil or serum to add shine and smoothness to your hair.

By following these simple steps, you can find your natural hair part and achieve the best possible hairstyle for your hair type and face shape. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different parts to find the one that looks best on you.
