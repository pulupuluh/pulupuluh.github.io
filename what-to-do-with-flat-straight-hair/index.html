What To Do With Flat Straight Hair Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Oliver Mar 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of having flat, lifeless hair? Do you struggle to find hairstyles that work with your straight locks? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people with straight hair struggle to add volume and texture to their hair. But fear not, because there are plenty of things you can do to make the most out of your flat straight hair. In this article, we'll go over some of the best tips, tricks, and hair care practices for flat straight hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Add Volume to Flat Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Flat Straight Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Flat Straight Hair

How to Add Volume to Flat Straight Hair

If you have flat straight hair, adding volume is key to achieving a more voluminous and textured look. Here are some ways to add volume to your hair:

1. Blow-Dry Your Hair Upside Down

One of the easiest ways to add volume to flat straight hair is to blow-dry your hair upside down. This will lift your roots and create more volume. Simply flip your head over and blow-dry your hair in that position.

2. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner can help add volume to flat straight hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like hydrolyzed wheat protein, which can help plump up your hair strands.

3. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair can help create volume and texture. To do this, take a small section of hair and backcomb it with a teasing comb. Repeat this process throughout your hair.

4. Use a Root-Lifting Spray

A root-lifting spray can help add volume to your hair at the roots. Simply spray the product onto your roots and massage it in with your fingers.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Flat Straight Hair

Now that you know how to add volume to your hair, it's time to style it. Here's a step-by-step guide to styling flat straight hair:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before styling. This will help your hair hold its shape better.

2. Apply a Heat Protectant

Before using any heat tools, apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will help prevent damage and breakage.

3. Use a Curling Iron or Straightener

Depending on the style you're going for, you can use a curling iron or straightener to create waves or curls in your hair. For a more natural look, alternate the direction of the curls.

4. Add Texture with a Texturizing Spray

A texturizing spray can help add texture and hold to your hair. Spray the product onto your hair and scrunch it in with your fingers.

5. Finish with Hairspray

To set your style in place, finish with a light hold hairspray. This will help your style last all day.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Flat Straight Hair

Maintaining healthy hair is key to achieving a great hairstyle. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy flat straight hair:

1. Use a Clarifying Shampoo Once a Week

Using a clarifying shampoo once a week can help remove buildup from your hair. This will keep your hair looking and feeling healthy.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Every Day

Heat styling can damage your hair over time. Try to limit heat styling to a few times a week, and always use a heat protectant.

3. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can damage your hair just like it can damage your skin. Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

FAQs About Flat Straight Hair

Q. Can I still have long hair if I have flat straight hair?

A. Absolutely. Long hair can look beautiful on flat straight hair. Just remember to add volume and texture to your hair to keep it from looking too flat.

Q. Is it better to air dry or blow-dry flat straight hair?

A. It's up to personal preference. Air drying can be more gentle on your hair, but blow-drying can help add volume and texture.

Q. What hairstyles work best for flat straight hair?

A. Hairstyles with layers and texture work best for flat straight hair. Try a layered bob, beachy waves, or a messy bun.

Q. Can I color my flat straight hair?

A. Yes, but make sure to use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading and damage.

With these tips, tricks, and hair care practices, you can make the most out of your flat straight hair. Whether you're going for a natural look or a more styled look, these techniques will help you achieve the perfect hairstyle.
