Hairstyles For Over 60 Mid Length

Written by Bardi Jun 02, 2023 · 7 min read

Getting older doesn't mean that you have to sacrifice style. In fact, many women over 60 are still rocking their mid-length hair with confidence. If you're looking for some inspiration, read on for some of the best hairstyles for over 60 mid length.

Description

Mid-length hair is generally considered to be hair that falls between the chin and shoulder length. This length is great for those who want a versatile hairstyle that can be worn up or down, styled straight or curly, and can work for any occasion. As we age, our hair naturally loses some of its volume and thickness, so finding a hairstyle that can add some fullness and dimension is key.

How to Style Mid-Length Hair

Styling mid-length hair can seem overwhelming at first, but it's actually quite simple. Here are some basic steps to follow:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair
  3. Use a round brush to blow-dry your hair, focusing on the roots for volume
  4. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron or straightener to add some texture and definition
  5. Finish with a hairspray to hold your style in place

Step by Step Hairstyles for Over 60 Mid Length

1. Layered Bob

The layered bob is a classic hairstyle that never goes out of style. It's perfect for those with thin hair, as the layers can add some much-needed volume and movement. To achieve this look, ask your stylist for a chin-length bob with long layers. Style with a side part and some loose waves for a casual, effortless look.

2. Shaggy Layers

The shaggy layers hairstyle is a great option for those who want to add some texture and dimension to their mid-length hair. This style works best with a center part and some messy waves. To achieve this look, ask your stylist for choppy layers and some face-framing pieces. Use a texturizing spray to add some definition and hold.

3. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs are a great way to add some dimension and shape to your mid-length hair. They're also a great option for those with fine hair, as they can help to create the illusion of fuller hair. To achieve this look, ask your stylist for some side-swept bangs that fall just above your eyebrows. Style with some loose waves for a soft, romantic look.

Tips for Maintaining Mid-Length Hair

Here are some tips to help you maintain your mid-length hair:

  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush to help detangle your hair and distribute natural oils

FAQs

Q: What are some hairstyles for over 60s with thin hair?

A: Some great hairstyles for over 60s with thin hair include the layered bob, shaggy layers, and side-swept bangs.

Q: How can I add volume to my mid-length hair?

A: To add volume to your mid-length hair, try blow-drying your hair upside down, using a volumizing mousse or spray, or getting some layers cut into your hair.

Q: Do I have to cut my hair short as I get older?

A: No, you don't have to cut your hair short as you get older. Many women over 60 are still rocking their mid-length hair with confidence.

Hair Care for Mid-Length Hair

Mid-length hair requires some care and attention to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as it can cause damage and breakage
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal pieces, as they can damage your hair

With these tips and hairstyle ideas, you'll be able to rock your mid-length hair with confidence and style. Don't be afraid to try something new and experiment with different looks until you find the perfect hairstyle for you.
