Easy Updos For Long Hair Tutorial

Written by Daniel Jul 27, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have long hair, you know that styling it can be a challenge. It’s easy to get stuck in a rut of wearing the same hairstyle every day. But with a little creativity and some easy updos for long hair tutorial, you can switch up your look in no time. In this article, we’ll go over some simple hairstyles you can try at home, including a braided updo, a messy bun, and a sleek ponytail.

How to:

Before we get started, make sure you have some basic hair tools on hand. You’ll need a brush or comb, some hair ties, bobby pins, and possibly some hair spray. If you have thicker hair, you may also need a hair donut or sock bun to help create volume.

Braided Updo

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles. Then, create a deep side part and section off a small piece of hair near the part. Begin braiding this section, gradually adding in more hair as you go. Continue braiding until you reach the nape of your neck, then secure the braid with a hair tie.

Next, gather the rest of your hair into a low ponytail and secure it with a hair tie. Take the ponytail and wrap it around the base of the braid, pinning it in place with bobby pins. Finally, gently pull on the braid to loosen it and create a more voluminous look. Finish with some hairspray to hold everything in place.

Messy Bun

A messy bun is a classic hairstyle that’s perfect for when you’re short on time. Start by pulling your hair back into a high ponytail and securing it with a hair tie. Then, twist your hair around the hair tie, tucking the ends underneath. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place, and pull out a few strands to create a messy, undone look.

You can also add some texture to your bun by teasing your hair before you pull it back. This will create more volume and help the bun hold its shape. Finish with some hairspray to keep everything in place.

Sleek Ponytail

A sleek ponytail is a simple but elegant hairstyle that’s perfect for any occasion. Start by brushing your hair straight back and gathering it into a high ponytail. Use a hair tie to secure the ponytail in place, and wrap a small section of hair around the hair tie to conceal it.

For a more polished look, use a flat iron to smooth down any flyaways and create a sleek, straight finish. You can also add some shine to your hair by applying a small amount of hair oil or serum to the ends.

Step by Step:

1. Brush your hair to remove any tangles.

2. Decide which hairstyle you want to try.

3. Gather the necessary hair tools.

4. Follow the instructions for your chosen hairstyle.

5. Finish with hairspray or any other hair product you prefer.

Tips:

- If you have trouble braiding your own hair, try using a hairband to secure the braid and then wrapping it around the base of your ponytail.

- To add volume to your messy bun, tease your hair with a comb or brush before pulling it back.

- Use bobby pins that match your hair color to make them less noticeable.

FAQ:

Q: Can I try these hairstyles if I have shorter hair?

A: These hairstyles are designed for longer hair, but you can try adapting them for shorter hair by using hair extensions or adjusting the placement of the hairstyle.

Q: How do I prevent my updo from falling out?

A: Make sure to use enough bobby pins to secure your hairstyle in place. You can also use hairspray or hair gel to help hold everything in place.

Hair Care:

Remember to take care of your hair by using a good quality shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use hot tools. Regular trims can also help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these easy updos for long hair tutorial, you can switch up your hairstyle and add some variety to your look. Whether you’re headed to work, a special event, or just want to switch things up, these hairstyles are a great place to start. So grab your hair tools and get ready to try something new!
