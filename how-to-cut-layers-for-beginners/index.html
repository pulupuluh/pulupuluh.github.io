How To Cut Layers For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 07, 2023 · 8 min read

Cutting layers is a great way to add volume, movement, and texture to your hair. Whether you have long, medium, or short hair, layers can make a big difference in your overall look. However, if you've never cut layers before, it can be intimidating. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut layers for beginners.

How to Cut Layers for Beginners

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep the hair damp.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by dividing your hair into sections. Use the comb to create a part in the middle of your hair, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, divide each side of your hair into two sections, one in the front and one in the back. Clip each section to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin with one of the back sections. Take a small section of hair from the bottom, hold it between your fingers, and point-cut the ends. Point-cutting means cutting into the hair at an angle, rather than straight across. This will help create a softer, more natural look. Continue working your way up the section, taking small sections of hair and point-cutting the ends.

Repeat this process on the other back section, then move on to the front sections. Start with the section closest to your face and work your way back. Remember to point-cut the ends to create a softer look.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

After you've cut all the sections, it's time to blend the layers. Take a comb and hold it at a 45-degree angle to the hair. Slide the comb down each section of hair, starting at the roots and moving toward the ends. This will help blend the layers together and create a more seamless look.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting and blending your layers, take a look in the mirror. Check to make sure the layers are even and blended. If you notice any areas that need more attention, go back and trim them as needed.

Tips for Cutting Layers

Cutting layers can be tricky, especially if you're doing it for the first time. Here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's better to take your time and work in small sections than to try to cut too much at once.
  • Point-cut the ends for a softer, more natural look.
  • Use a comb to blend the layers together. This will help create a seamless look.
  • Check your work as you go. Don't be afraid to make adjustments as needed.

FAQ

How long should my layers be?

The length of your layers will depend on your hair type and personal preference. For shorter hair, layers can be as short as 1-2 inches. For longer hair, layers can be up to 6 inches long.

Can I cut layers in curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. However, it's important to be careful not to cut too much, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.

Should I cut my own layers or go to a stylist?

If you're comfortable with cutting your own hair and have the necessary tools, you can definitely cut your own layers. However, if you're unsure or have never cut your own hair before, it may be best to go to a stylist.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting layers for the first time can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look. Follow these steps, tips, and hair care advice to get the best results. Happy cutting!
