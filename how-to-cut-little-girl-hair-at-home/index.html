How To Cut Little Girl Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of paying a hefty sum every time you take your little girl to the hair salon? Do you want to learn how to cut little girl hair at home? If yes, then you've come to the right place. Cutting your little girl's hair at home can be a fun and cost-effective way to keep her hair looking great. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to cut little girl hair at home like a pro.

Description

Cutting little girl hair at home can be a daunting task, especially if you have no prior experience. However, with a little bit of practice and the right tools, you can give your little girl a stylish haircut that she'll love. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to cut your little girl's hair at home, including tips, step-by-step instructions, and hair care advice.

How to Cut Little Girl Hair at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step to cutting little girl hair at home is to gather all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Trimming scissors (optional)

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean before you start cutting.

Step 2: Choose the Right Style

Before you start cutting, it's important to choose the right style. You can find inspiration from magazines or online. Keep in mind your little girl's hair type and face shape when choosing a style.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Hair

Wash your little girl's hair with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Dry her hair thoroughly before you start cutting.

Step 4: Section the Hair

Section your little girl's hair into manageable parts. Use hair clips to secure the hair in place. Start by sectioning the hair on the crown of her head and then work your way down to the sides and back.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Start cutting the hair at the back of her head, working your way up to the top. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it in a straight line. Use the comb to keep the hair straight as you cut. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 6: Cut the Sides

Next, move on to the sides of her head. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length. Use the comb to keep the hair straight as you cut. Cut the hair in small sections to ensure an even cut.

Step 7: Trim the Bangs

If your little girl has bangs, trim them to the desired length. Make sure the bangs are straight and even.

Step 8: Check for Evenness

After you've finished cutting, check for evenness. Use the comb to make sure the hair is even all around. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 9: Style the Hair

Style your little girl's hair as desired. Use a hairbrush, curling iron or straightener to create the desired look. You can also use hair accessories like headbands and bows to add some flair.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to bigger cuts.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Take breaks if your little girl gets restless or uncomfortable.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Watch tutorials online to get a better understanding of how to cut hair.

FAQ

Can I cut my little girl's hair if she has curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your little girl's hair even if she has curly hair. However, it's important to be cautious while cutting curly hair as it can be difficult to get an even cut. Use trimming scissors to avoid cutting too much hair at once.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. Wait for the hair to grow back or take your little girl to a professional hairdresser to fix the mistake.

How often should I cut my little girl's hair?

It depends on how fast your little girl's hair grows. On average, you should cut your little girl's hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking healthy and stylish.

Hair Care

After cutting your little girl's hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking great. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to wash her hair. Avoid using too much heat while styling her hair as it can cause damage. Use a hairbrush to detangle her hair and avoid pulling too hard. Trim her hair regularly to prevent split ends.
