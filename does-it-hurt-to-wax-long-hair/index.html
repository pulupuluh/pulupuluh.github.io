Does It Hurt To Wax Long Hair

Written by Daniel Jun 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Waxing is a popular method of hair removal that involves applying hot wax to the skin and then pulling it off, taking the hair with it. While it's an effective way to get rid of unwanted hair, many people wonder if it hurts to wax long hair. In this blog post, we'll explore this question in detail and provide you with tips on how to make waxing less painful.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Long hair can be more painful to wax than shorter hair. This is because the longer the hair, the more difficult it is to remove from the root. Additionally, longer hair is often thicker, which can make the waxing process more uncomfortable. However, with the right techniques and products, you can reduce the pain and discomfort associated with waxing long hair.

How to

Here are some tips on how to wax long hair without experiencing too much pain:

Trim the Hair

Before waxing, trim your hair to about a quarter of an inch. This will make it easier to remove the hair from the root and reduce the pain associated with waxing.

Exfoliate

Exfoliate your skin before waxing to remove dead skin cells and promote a smoother waxing experience. Use a gentle exfoliating scrub or a dry brush to remove any impurities from your skin.

Apply Numbing Cream

If you're particularly sensitive to pain, you can apply a numbing cream to the area before waxing. This will help to reduce any discomfort you may experience during the waxing process.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to wax long hair:

Step 1: Prep the Skin

Cleanse the area of skin you want to wax with soap and water. Dry the area thoroughly.

Step 2: Apply Wax

Heat up the wax according to the instructions on the package. Apply the wax in the direction of hair growth, using a spatula or applicator stick.

Step 3: Place Strip

Place a waxing strip over the wax, pressing down firmly.

Step 4: Pull Strip

Hold the skin taut with one hand and use the other hand to pull the waxing strip off in the opposite direction of hair growth.

Step 5: Repeat

Repeat steps 2-4 until you have removed all the hair you want to remove.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help reduce pain and discomfort when waxing long hair:

  • Take a pain reliever before waxing to help reduce discomfort.
  • Don't wax over the same area more than once, as this can cause irritation and make the waxing process more painful.
  • Hold the skin taut when pulling off the waxing strip to reduce pain.
  • Apply a soothing lotion or oil to the skin after waxing to help reduce redness and irritation.

FAQ

Does waxing long hair hurt more than waxing short hair?

Yes, waxing long hair can be more painful than waxing short hair because it's more difficult to remove from the root.

How can I make waxing less painful?

You can make waxing less painful by trimming the hair to a quarter of an inch, exfoliating the skin, applying numbing cream, and using the right techniques.

Hair Care

After waxing, it's important to take care of your skin and hair to prevent irritation and promote healthy growth. Here are some tips for hair care after waxing:

  • Avoid sun exposure for at least 24 hours after waxing.
  • Don't use any harsh or irritating products on the waxed area, such as exfoliating scrubs or perfumed lotions.
  • Moisturize the area regularly to keep it soft and hydrated.
  • Avoid shaving or using any other hair removal methods for at least two weeks after waxing to allow the skin to heal.

In conclusion, waxing long hair can be more painful than waxing short hair, but with the right techniques and products, you can reduce the discomfort associated with it. Follow the tips and steps outlined in this blog post to make your waxing experience as painless as possible.
