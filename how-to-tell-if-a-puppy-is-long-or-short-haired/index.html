How To Tell If A Puppy Is Long Or Short Haired A Guide For New Pet Owners

Written by Aprilia Apr 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you about to welcome a new furry friend into your home? One of the first things you'll want to know is whether your puppy has long or short hair. This can affect how you care for them, what grooming tools you'll need, and even how often you'll need to bathe them. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to determine your puppy's hair type, as well as some tips for caring for their coat.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Look at the Breed
  • Step 2: Check the Fur Texture
  • Step 3: Look at the Parents
  • Tips for Caring for Your Puppy's Hair
  • FAQs

Step 1: Look at the Breed

The easiest way to tell whether your puppy has long or short hair is to look at their breed. Some breeds, like the Shih Tzu or Yorkshire Terrier, are known for their long, flowing locks. Others, like the Boxer or Dalmatian, typically have short hair. If you're unsure what breed your puppy is, do some research or ask your veterinarian.

Step 2: Check the Fur Texture

If your puppy's breed doesn't give you a clear answer, the next step is to check their fur texture. Long-haired dogs will have softer, silkier fur that falls in waves or curls. Short-haired dogs will have coarser fur that lays flat against their body. You can also try pulling up a section of fur to see how long it is.

Look at the Parents

If your puppy is a mix of two or more breeds, or if their breed isn't known, another way to determine their hair type is to look at their parents. If one parent has long hair and the other has short hair, there's a good chance your puppy will fall somewhere in between. If both parents have short hair, your puppy is likely to have short hair as well.

Tips for Caring for Your Puppy's Hair

Now that you know whether your puppy has long or short hair, it's important to care for their coat properly. Here are some tips:

  • Long-haired dogs will need to be brushed more often to prevent matting and tangling. A slicker brush or comb is best for this.
  • Short-haired dogs will still benefit from regular brushing to remove loose hair and distribute their natural oils. A rubber brush or grooming mitt works well for this.
  • Both long and short-haired dogs can benefit from a monthly bath using a gentle dog shampoo. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid any skin irritation.
  • If your puppy has a lot of hair around their ears, eyes, or paws, consider trimming it to prevent irritation and infection.

FAQs

What if my puppy's hair is somewhere in between long and short?

Some breeds, like the Australian Shepherd or Cocker Spaniel, have medium-length hair that falls somewhere in between long and short. In this case, you can use a combination of long-haired and short-haired grooming techniques to care for their coat.

How often should I brush my puppy?

This will depend on your puppy's hair type and length, as well as their activity level and environment. As a general rule, long-haired dogs should be brushed daily, while short-haired dogs can be brushed once or twice a week.

What if my puppy has skin irritation or a rash?

If your puppy is experiencing skin irritation or a rash, it's important to consult with your veterinarian. They can help diagnose the issue and recommend a treatment plan.

Can I use human shampoo on my puppy?

No, it's not recommended to use human shampoo on your puppy. Human shampoo can be too harsh for their sensitive skin and can strip their hair of natural oils. Use a gentle dog shampoo instead.

By following these tips and techniques, you can keep your puppy's coat looking and feeling healthy. Remember to always consult with your veterinarian if you have any concerns about your puppy's hair or skin.
