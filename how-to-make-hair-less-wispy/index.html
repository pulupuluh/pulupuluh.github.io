How To Make Hair Less Wispy Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Jul 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Wispy hair, characterized by fine, flyaway strands, can be frustrating to manage. It can also make your hair appear thinner and more fragile. Fortunately, there are several ways to make your hair less wispy and more voluminous. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you achieve the look you want.

How to Make Hair Less Wispy

1. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner

Look for hair products that are designed to add volume and thickness to your hair. These products contain ingredients that help to coat each strand of hair, making it appear fuller and more voluminous.

2. Avoid over-washing your hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and brittle. This can lead to breakage and flyaway strands. Try to limit washing your hair to every other day or every third day, and use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair than a brush, and it can help to detangle your hair without causing breakage. Use it to comb your hair after you've applied conditioner, and then rinse your hair thoroughly.

4. Blow-dry your hair upside down

Blow-drying your hair upside down can help to add volume and lift to your roots. Use a round brush to help create volume, and direct the air flow from your blow dryer upward to help create lift.

5. Use a volumizing mousse or hair spray

After blow-drying your hair, apply a volumizing mousse or hair spray to help keep your hair looking full and voluminous. These products can help to add texture and hold to your hair, making it less likely to fall flat or become wispy.

6. Get a layered haircut

A layered haircut can help to add volume and dimension to your hair, making it appear thicker and less wispy. Talk to your hairstylist about getting a layered cut that flatters your face shape and works with your hair texture.

7. Use a hair mask

Using a hair mask once a week can help to nourish and hydrate your hair, making it less prone to breakage and flyaway strands. Look for a mask that contains ingredients like coconut oil, argan oil, or shea butter, which can help to moisturize your hair and add shine.

Hair Care FAQ

Q: How often should I use a hair mask?

A: You should use a hair mask once a week to help nourish and hydrate your hair.

Q: Can I use a volumizing mousse and hair spray together?

A: Yes, you can use a volumizing mousse and hair spray together to help add volume and hold to your hair. Apply the mousse to your roots and mid-lengths, and then apply the hair spray to help set your style.

Q: Will blow-drying my hair upside down damage it?

A: No, blow-drying your hair upside down won't damage it as long as you use a heat protectant spray and don't use too much heat. Use a low or medium heat setting on your blow dryer, and hold it at least 6 inches away from your hair.

Tips for Making Hair Less Wispy

Here are some additional tips for making your hair less wispy:

  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners, which can damage your hair and cause it to become more wispy.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent breakage and flyaway strands.
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet to help promote healthy hair growth.
  • Consider taking a hair supplement like biotin or collagen to help improve the health and thickness of your hair.

Conclusion

Making your hair less wispy doesn't have to be difficult. By using the right products, tools, and techniques, you can achieve fuller, more voluminous hair that looks healthy and vibrant. Try incorporating these tips and tricks into your hair care routine, and see how they work for you.
