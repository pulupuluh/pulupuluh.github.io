Haircuts For Thin Straight Hair 2020 A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 29, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have thin straight hair, you know how challenging it can be to find the perfect haircut. Fortunately, 2020 has brought some exciting new haircut trends that can work wonders for thin straight hair. In this post, we’ll discuss some trendy haircuts for thin straight hair in 2020, how to style them, and hair care tips to keep your locks looking healthy and voluminous.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose a Haircut for Thin Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Haircuts for Thin Straight Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Thin Straight Hair
  • FAQs About Haircuts for Thin Straight Hair
  • Hair Care Routine for Thin Straight Hair

How to Choose a Haircut for Thin Straight Hair

Before we dive into specific haircuts, let’s talk about some general rules of thumb for choosing a haircut for thin straight hair:

  • Avoid heavy layers that can make hair look even thinner.
  • Opt for blunt cuts that create the illusion of thickness and fullness.
  • Short haircuts can work great for thin straight hair, as they add volume and texture.
  • Consider adding bangs to your haircut to frame your face and add dimension to your hair.

Now, let’s look at some specific haircuts that can work well for thin straight hair in 2020.

1. Bob Haircut

The bob haircut is a classic that has been around for decades. In 2020, there are several variations of the bob haircut that can work wonders for thin straight hair. The blunt bob is a great option, as it creates the illusion of thickness and fullness. You can also opt for an asymmetrical bob, which adds texture and movement to your hair.

2. Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a daring haircut that can work surprisingly well for thin straight hair. It adds volume and texture to your hair, and it’s also low-maintenance. Keep in mind that the pixie cut requires regular trims to maintain its shape.

3. Lob Haircut

The lob, or long bob, is a trendy haircut that has been popular in recent years. It’s a great option for thin straight hair, as it adds volume and texture without being too short. You can also add layers to your lob haircut to create the illusion of thickness and movement.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Haircuts for Thin Straight Hair

Now that you have some ideas for haircuts, let’s talk about how to style them for maximum volume and fullness:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products will help add volume and texture to your hair.
  2. Blow-dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  3. Use a round brush to curl the ends of your hair under, which will create the illusion of thickness.
  4. Finish with a volumizing spray or mousse to add even more fullness to your hair.

Remember that less is more when it comes to styling products. Too much product can weigh down your hair and make it look even thinner.

Tips for Maintaining Thin Straight Hair

Now that you have a haircut and styling routine that works for your thin straight hair, here are some tips for maintaining healthy locks:

  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as it’s gentler than a brush.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, which can make your hair look thinner.

FAQs About Haircuts for Thin Straight Hair

Q. Can bangs work for thin straight hair?

A. Yes, bangs can work great for thin straight hair. They can add dimension and texture to your hair, and they also frame your face.

Q. Should I avoid layers if I have thin straight hair?

A. Heavy layers can make thin straight hair look even thinner. Opt for blunt cuts or subtle layers to create the illusion of thickness and fullness.

Hair Care Routine for Thin Straight Hair

Here’s a simple hair care routine for thin straight hair:

  1. Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Blow-dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  3. Style your hair with a round brush to curl the ends under.
  4. Use a volumizing spray or mousse to add fullness to your hair.
  5. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With the right haircut, styling routine, and hair care tips, you can make the most of your thin straight hair in 2020. Try out some of these haircuts and techniques to see what works best for you!
