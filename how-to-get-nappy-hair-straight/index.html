How To Get Nappy Hair Straight A Complete Guide

If you have nappy hair, you might be wondering how to straighten it. Nappy hair is characterized by tight, coiled curls that can be difficult to manage. However, with the right techniques and products, you can achieve straight, sleek locks. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about how to get nappy hair straight.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Get Nappy Hair Straight

There are several methods you can use to straighten nappy hair. These include:

  • Using a flat iron
  • Blow drying with a round brush
  • Using a chemical relaxer
  • Using a keratin treatment

Each of these methods has its pros and cons, and the best method for you will depend on your hair type and personal preference. Let's take a closer look at each technique.

Using a Flat Iron

A flat iron is a simple and effective tool for straightening nappy hair. To use a flat iron, you'll need:

  • A flat iron
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hair clips
  • Comb

Here's how to straighten your nappy hair with a flat iron:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash and condition your hair. Apply a heat protectant spray to damp hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips.
  3. Starting with the bottom sections, use a comb to detangle your hair.
  4. Take a small section of hair and clamp the flat iron at the roots.
  5. Gently glide the flat iron down the length of your hair, pulling it taut as you go.
  6. Repeat this process with all sections of your hair.
  7. Use a comb to smooth down any frizz or flyaways.
  8. Finish with a light hair oil or serum to add shine and prevent frizz.

Blow Drying with a Round Brush

Blow drying with a round brush is another effective way to straighten nappy hair. To do this, you'll need:

  • A blow dryer
  • A round brush
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hair clips
  • Comb

Here's how to blow dry your nappy hair straight:

  1. Wash and condition your hair. Apply a heat protectant spray to damp hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips.
  3. Starting with the bottom sections, use a comb to detangle your hair.
  4. Take a small section of hair and place the round brush underneath it.
  5. Use the blow dryer to dry the hair, pulling it taut with the round brush as you go.
  6. Repeat this process with all sections of your hair.
  7. Use a comb to smooth down any frizz or flyaways.
  8. Finish with a light hair oil or serum to add shine and prevent frizz.

Using a Chemical Relaxer

A chemical relaxer is a permanent way to straighten nappy hair. This method involves applying a chemical solution to the hair that breaks down the bonds that give the hair its curl. To use a chemical relaxer, you'll need:

  • A chemical relaxer kit
  • Gloves
  • Applicator brush
  • Neutralizing shampoo
  • Conditioner

Here's how to use a chemical relaxer:

  1. Follow the instructions on the chemical relaxer kit carefully.
  2. Put on gloves to protect your hands.
  3. Apply the relaxer to your hair using the applicator brush.
  4. Leave the relaxer on your hair for the recommended amount of time.
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  6. Apply the neutralizing shampoo and rinse again.
  7. Apply conditioner and leave it on for several minutes before rinsing.

Using a Keratin Treatment

A keratin treatment is a semi-permanent way to straighten nappy hair. This treatment involves applying a keratin solution to the hair, which is then sealed in using heat. To use a keratin treatment, you'll need:

  • A keratin treatment kit
  • Gloves
  • Applicator brush
  • Flat iron

Here's how to use a keratin treatment:

  1. Follow the instructions on the keratin treatment kit carefully.
  2. Put on gloves to protect your hands.
  3. Apply the keratin solution to your hair using the applicator brush.
  4. Blow dry your hair until it is completely dry.
  5. Use a flat iron to seal in the keratin solution.
  6. Avoid washing your hair for the recommended amount of time.

Tips for Straightening Nappy Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when straightening nappy hair:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent heat damage.
  • Don't straighten your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to prevent breakage when detangling your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat, as this can cause damage and dryness.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to get nappy hair straight:

Can nappy hair be straightened?

Yes, nappy hair can be straightened using a variety of methods, including flat ironing, blow drying with a round brush, chemical relaxing, and keratin treatments.

Which method is best for straightening nappy hair?

The best method for straightening nappy hair depends on your hair type and personal preference. Flat ironing and blow drying are both effective and easy to do at home, while chemical relaxing and keratin treatments are more permanent but require professional application.

How often should I straighten my nappy hair?

You should avoid straightening your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage. Aim to straighten your hair no more than once a week.

Hair Care for Nappy Hair

To keep your nappy hair healthy and strong, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 1-2 weeks with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Condition your hair after every wash to keep it moisturized.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.

By following these tips and using the right techniques and products, you can achieve straight, sleek locks even if you have nappy hair.
