Tutorial How To Cut Hair In Layers

Written by Bardi Jun 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking flat and dull? Do you want to add some volume and texture to your locks? One way to achieve this is by cutting your hair in layers. Layered haircuts can give you a fresh, new look without sacrificing length. In this tutorial, we will show you how to cut hair in layers step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Hair in Layers
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Hair in Layers
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Layered Haircuts

How to Cut Hair in Layers

Before we get started, let's talk about the tools you'll need. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp. Now, let's get into the steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and style. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you begin cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into layers. Start at the bottom and work your way up. Use hair clips to secure each section as you go.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer and cut it to your desired length. Make sure to cut straight across and use the comb to keep the hair even. Once you've cut the bottom layer, release the next section and repeat the process.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, it's time to blend them together. Comb your hair straight down and use your scissors to blend the layers together. Make small, subtle cuts to avoid any harsh lines.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

After you've blended the layers, it's time for the finishing touches. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs and create face-framing layers if desired.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step by step guide to cutting hair in layers:

  1. Wash and dry your hair
  2. Section your hair into layers
  3. Cut the bottom layer to your desired length
  4. Release the next section and repeat the process
  5. Blend the layers together
  6. Trim any stray hairs and create face-framing layers if desired

Tips for Cutting Hair in Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once
  • Use a comb to keep the hair even and straight
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as this can cause it to look shorter when it dries
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Take your time and don't rush the process

FAQs

How often should I cut my hair in layers?

This will depend on how fast your hair grows and how long you want to keep your layers. Generally, you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layered haircut.

Can I cut my own hair in layers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair in layers. However, it's important to have the right tools and take your time to avoid any mistakes.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix any mistakes or uneven layers.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Haircuts

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to enhance your layers
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and healthy
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can cause frizz and breakage
  • Consider getting regular trims to maintain your layered haircut

That's it! You now know how to cut hair in layers. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a beautiful, layered haircut that will add volume and texture to your hair. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow these steps carefully.
