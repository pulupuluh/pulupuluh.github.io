Is Curly Hair Harder To Cut

Written by Robby Mar 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have curly hair and are planning to get a haircut, you might be wondering if it's harder to cut curly hair than straight hair. The answer is yes, curly hair can be harder to cut. This is because curly hair has a different texture and shape than straight hair, which can make it more difficult to achieve the desired look. In this article, we will discuss why curly hair is harder to cut and provide tips on how to cut curly hair.

Why is Curly Hair Harder to Cut?

Curly hair has a unique texture and shape that can make it harder to cut. The curls can be unpredictable and harder to control, which can make it difficult to achieve a specific hairstyle. Additionally, curly hair can shrink when it dries, which can make it harder to determine the length of the hair. This can result in a haircut that is shorter than you intended.

Another reason why curly hair is harder to cut is that it requires a different approach than straight hair. Cutting curly hair requires a special technique to ensure that the curls are not disrupted or damaged. This can involve cutting the hair while it's wet or using special scissors that are designed for curly hair.

How to Cut Curly Hair

If you're planning to cut curly hair, it's important to follow these steps to ensure that you achieve the desired look:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Before cutting curly hair, it's important to wash and condition the hair. This will remove any dirt or product buildup and make the hair easier to cut. It's also important to detangle the hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Once the hair is detangled, it's important to section the hair. This will make it easier to cut the hair and ensure that the hair is even. You can use hair clips or hair ties to section the hair.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

When cutting curly hair, it's important to use a special technique to ensure that the curls are not disrupted. You can either cut the hair while it's wet or dry. If you're cutting the hair while it's wet, make sure to use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp. If you're cutting the hair dry, make sure to use special scissors that are designed for curly hair.

Step 4: Style the Hair

After cutting the hair, it's important to style the hair to achieve the desired look. You can use a diffuser to dry the hair and enhance the curls. You can also use styling products to define the curls and add volume.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair.
  • Section the hair to ensure that the hair is even.
  • Use special scissors that are designed for curly hair.
  • Cut the hair while it's wet or use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp.
  • Style the hair to enhance the curls and achieve the desired look.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair:

Is it better to cut curly hair while it's wet or dry?

It depends on the person and the type of curls. Some people prefer to cut their hair while it's wet because it's easier to control the curls. Others prefer to cut their hair dry because they can see the shape of the hair better. Ultimately, it's up to personal preference.

Do I need special scissors to cut curly hair?

It's recommended to use special scissors that are designed for curly hair. These scissors have a serrated edge that can help prevent the hair from sliding out of the scissors and disrupting the curl pattern.

How often should I get my curly hair cut?

It's recommended to get your curly hair cut every 8-12 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep your curly hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair.
  • Use a diffuser to dry your hair and enhance the curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as it can disrupt the curl pattern.
  • Use styling products that are designed for curly hair to define the curls and add volume.

In conclusion, cutting curly hair can be harder than cutting straight hair due to its unique texture and shape. It's important to follow the proper steps and techniques to achieve the desired look. By following these tips and taking care of your curly hair, you can have healthy, beautiful curls.
