How Much Does It Cost To Dye Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline May 09, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you thinking about dyeing your curly hair but unsure about the cost? We’ve got you covered. In this guide, we’ll break down everything you need to know about the cost of dyeing curly hair. From how to prepare your hair for dyeing to the different types of dyes available, we’ll cover it all. So, let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare Your Hair for Dyeing
  • Types of Dye
  • Cost of Dyeing Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Dyeing Curly Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Dyed Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dyed Curly Hair

How to Prepare Your Hair for Dyeing

Before you dye your curly hair, it’s important to prepare it properly. Here’s how:

1. Wash your hair

Wash your hair a day or two before dyeing it. This will remove any product buildup and ensure that the dye adheres properly.

2. Deep condition your hair

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it’s important to deep condition your hair before dyeing it. This will help prevent damage and keep your curls looking healthy.

3. Avoid using heat styling tools

Heat styling tools can damage your hair, especially when it’s already dry from the dyeing process. Avoid using them for at least a few days before dyeing your hair.

Types of Dye

There are several types of dye available for curly hair:

Semi-permanent

Semi-permanent dyes are a great option if you’re looking for a temporary change. They typically last for 6-8 washes and don’t contain harsh chemicals.

Permanent

Permanent dyes are a good option if you’re looking for a long-lasting change. They contain harsh chemicals and can be damaging to your hair, so it’s important to use them sparingly.

Henna

Henna is a natural dye that’s made from the leaves of the henna plant. It’s a great option if you’re looking for a natural alternative to traditional dyes. However, it can be difficult to achieve certain colors with henna.

Cost of Dyeing Curly Hair

The cost of dyeing your curly hair will depend on several factors, including the length of your hair, the type of dye you choose, and the salon you go to. Here’s a breakdown of the average costs:

Semi-permanent

The cost of a semi-permanent dye job at a salon can range from $50-$100.

Permanent

The cost of a permanent dye job at a salon can range from $75-$150.

Henna

The cost of a henna dye job at a salon can range from $50-$100.

Step-by-Step Guide to Dyeing Curly Hair

1. Choose your dye

Choose the type of dye you want to use based on your desired outcome and the health of your hair.

2. Prepare your hair

Follow the steps outlined in the “How to Prepare Your Hair for Dyeing” section above.

3. Apply the dye

Follow the instructions on the dye package to apply the dye to your hair. Make sure to wear gloves to avoid staining your hands.

4. Rinse and condition your hair

Rinse the dye out of your hair thoroughly and apply a deep conditioner. Leave the conditioner in for at least 5 minutes before rinsing it out.

Tips for Maintaining Dyed Curly Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining dyed curly hair:

1. Use sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can cause dryness and damage. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your curls looking healthy.

2. Deep condition regularly

Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and prevent damage.

3. Avoid heat styling tools

Heat styling tools can cause damage to your hair, especially when it’s already dry from the dyeing process. Avoid using them as much as possible.

FAQs

1. Will dyeing my curly hair damage it?

Dyeing your curly hair can cause damage if you don’t take proper care of it. Follow the steps outlined in this guide to minimize damage.

2. How often should I dye my curly hair?

You should only dye your curly hair when necessary, as frequent dyeing can cause damage.

3. Can I dye my curly hair at home?

Yes, you can dye your curly hair at home. However, it’s important to follow the instructions carefully and take proper precautions to avoid damage.

Hair Care for Dyed Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your dyed curly hair:

1. Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as it’s less likely to cause damage than a brush.

2. Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers.

3. Get regular trims

Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

That’s everything you need to know about the cost of dyeing curly hair. With these tips and tricks, you can achieve the perfect color without damaging your curls. Happy dyeing!
