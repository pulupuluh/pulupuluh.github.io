How To Cut Your Own Hair Fade Undercut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 03, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of going to the barber every few weeks to get your hair cut? Why not try cutting your own hair? With a little practice and patience, you can easily learn how to cut your own hair fade undercut. In this post, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own hair fade undercut, step-by-step. We will also provide you with some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you achieve the perfect cut.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Fade Undercut

Cutting your own hair fade undercut can seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily. Here are the steps to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean and dry hair. It is easier to work with hair that is not greasy or wet.
  2. Choose the right tools. You will need a pair of clippers, a comb, and scissors. Make sure your clippers have different guards to achieve different lengths of hair.
  3. Decide on the length of your hair. The length of your hair will determine the guard you need to use. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one if needed.
  4. Use the clippers to cut the sides and back of your hair. Start from the bottom and work your way up, using the longer guard first. Use the shorter guard to create a fade effect by gradually decreasing the length of the hair as you move up the head.
  5. Cut the top of your hair using scissors. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to your desired length. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as you can always go back and trim later.
  6. Blend the sides and top of your hair using the clippers. Use the longer guard to blend the sides and top together, creating a seamless look.
  7. Trim the hair around your ears and the back of your neck. Use scissors to trim any excess hair around the ears and the back of your neck.
  8. Check your work in a mirror. Make sure your hair is even and the fade is smooth. If you notice any uneven spots or areas that need to be trimmed, go back and fix them.
  9. Clean up your workspace and tools. Use a brush or vacuum to clean up any loose hair and sanitize your tools for future use.
  10. Apply hair styling products. Style your hair as desired using hair gel, pomade or hairspray. This will help keep your hair in place and give it a polished look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with these tips, you can achieve a successful cut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one. It's easier to cut more hair than it is to add hair back.
  • Take your time and be patient. It's better to take your time and get it right than rush and end up with a bad cut.
  • Use a mirror to check your work. It's important to see your hair from different angles to ensure it is even.
  • Watch tutorial videos on YouTube. There are many tutorial videos available that can help you learn how to cut your own hair.
  • Invest in good quality tools. Good quality tools will make the process easier and give you better results.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair fade undercut:

  • Is it hard to cut your own hair?

    • It can be challenging at first, but with practice, it becomes easier.

  • What tools do I need to cut my own hair fade undercut?

    • You will need clippers, a comb, and scissors.

  • Can I achieve a professional-looking cut by cutting my own hair?

    • Yes, with practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking cut.

  • How often should I cut my hair?

    • It depends on your hair growth and personal preference. Generally, hair should be cut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly using a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Apply hair styling products to keep your hair in place and give it a polished look.
  • Trim any split ends regularly to prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair fade undercut, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help if needed. With practice, you can achieve a professional-looking cut.
