What Face Shape Can Wear A Pixie Cut A Comprehensive Guide

Are you considering a pixie cut but not sure if it will suit your face shape? In this guide, we'll explore the different face shapes and which ones can pull off a pixie cut. We'll also provide tips on how to style and care for your pixie cut, so you can rock this trendy hairstyle with confidence.

Different Face Shapes

Before we dive into which face shapes can wear a pixie cut, let's first determine what your face shape is. There are six common face shapes: Oval, Round, Square, Heart, Diamond, and Oblong. To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back away from your face. Take note of the widest part of your face, the length of your face, and the shape of your jawline.

Oval Face Shape

An oval face shape is longer than it is wide, with a rounded hairline and jawline. This face shape is considered the most versatile and can pull off nearly any hairstyle, including a pixie cut.

Round Face Shape

A round face shape is characterized by a circular shape, with a wide hairline and full cheeks. If you have a round face shape, you may want to avoid a pixie cut that is too short, as it can make your face look wider. Instead, opt for a longer pixie cut with layers that add volume and height at the crown.

Square Face Shape

A square face shape has a strong jawline and a squared hairline. If you have a square face shape, you can pull off a pixie cut with soft layers that frame your face and soften your features.

Heart Face Shape

A heart face shape has a wider forehead and a pointed chin. If you have a heart face shape, you can pull off a pixie cut that is longer on top and shorter on the sides, as it will balance out your features.

Diamond Face Shape

A diamond face shape is widest at the cheekbones and has a pointed chin and hairline. If you have a diamond face shape, you can pull off a pixie cut with a lot of texture and layers, as it will add volume to your cheekbones and balance out your features.

Oblong Face Shape

An oblong face shape is longer than it is wide, with a straight hairline and jawline. If you have an oblong face shape, you can pull off a pixie cut with volume at the crown and textured layers that add width to your face.

What is a Pixie Cut?

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that typically features short hair on the sides and back of the head, with longer hair on top that can be styled in various ways. Pixie cuts can be edgy, feminine, and versatile, and are popular among women of all ages.

Pixie Cut for Different Face Shapes

Now that you know your face shape and what a pixie cut is, let's explore which face shapes can pull off this trendy hairstyle.

Oval Face Shape: As mentioned earlier, an oval face shape can pull off nearly any pixie cut, from a super short crop to a longer, textured style.

Round Face Shape: If you have a round face shape, opt for a longer pixie cut with layers that add volume and height at the crown. This will elongate your face and balance out your features.

Square Face Shape: Soft layers are key for a square face shape, as they will frame your face and soften your features. Consider a pixie cut with side-swept bangs to add dimension to your face.

Heart Face Shape: A longer pixie cut that is shorter on the sides will balance out your features and draw attention to your eyes and cheekbones. Consider adding layers to the top of your head to add volume and texture.

Diamond Face Shape: A pixie cut with a lot of texture and layers will add volume to your cheekbones and soften your features. Consider a side-swept bang that will draw attention to your eyes.

Oblong Face Shape: A pixie cut with volume at the crown and textured layers that add width to your face will balance out your features. Consider a pixie cut with longer bangs that can be swept to the side.

How to Style a Pixie Cut

Styling a pixie cut is easy and versatile. Here are some tips on how to style your pixie cut:

  • Apply a styling product to damp hair, such as a mousse or gel.
  • Blow dry your hair with a round brush, lifting at the roots to add volume.
  • Use a flat iron or curling iron to add texture and dimension to your hair.
  • Finish with a hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Maintaining a Pixie Cut

Maintaining a pixie cut is easy and low maintenance. Here are some tips to keep your pixie cut looking fresh:

  • Visit your hairstylist every 4-6 weeks for a trim to maintain the shape of your pixie cut.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished.
  • Invest in a good dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find the ones that work best for your hair type.

FAQs

Q: Will a pixie cut make my face look too masculine?

A: Not at all! Pixie cuts can be feminine and edgy, and can suit any face shape with the right styling.

Q: How do I know if a pixie cut is right for me?

A: Consider your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle before deciding on a pixie cut. Consult with your hairstylist to determine if a pixie cut is right for you.

Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

Keeping your pixie cut looking fresh and healthy is easy with these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and frizz.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to keep them hydrated and shiny.

Now that you know which face shapes can pull off a pixie cut, it's time to book your appointment with your hairstylist and rock this trendy hairstyle with confidence. With the right styling and hair care, you can maintain a pixie cut that suits your face shape and personality.
