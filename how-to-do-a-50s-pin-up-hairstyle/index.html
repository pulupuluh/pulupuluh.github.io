How To Do A 50S Pin Up Hairstyle

Written by Eveline Mar 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking for a fun, retro hairstyle, look no further than the classic 50s pin up style. This look is all about big, voluminous curls and sleek, polished waves. Plus, it's surprisingly easy to achieve with a few simple steps!

What You'll Need

To get started, you'll need a few key tools and products:

  • Curling iron or hot rollers
  • Bobby pins
  • A teasing comb
  • Hairspray
  • Hair scarf or bandana (optional)

Step by Step

1. Prep Your Hair

To create the perfect pin up style, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. Apply a heat protectant spray to your locks to prevent damage from hot tools.

2. Curl Your Hair

Use a curling iron or hot rollers to create big, bouncy curls throughout your hair. Be sure to curl in different directions for a more natural look.

3. Tease Your Hair

To create the signature 50s volume, tease your hair at the roots using a teasing comb. Focus on the crown of your head and the areas you want to add height to.

4. Pin Your Hair

Once you've teased your hair to your liking, use bobby pins to secure the curls in place. Pin the curls up and back, leaving the ends loose for a more romantic look.

5. Add a Bandana (Optional)

For an extra touch of retro style, tie a bandana or scarf around your head. Fold the scarf into a triangle, then tie it at the front of your head and tuck the ends underneath.

6. Finish with Hairspray

To keep your pin up style in place all day, spray your hair with a strong hold hairspray.

Tips

  • Use a curling iron with a large barrel for bigger, more voluminous curls.
  • Don't be afraid to tease your hair a lot for maximum volume.
  • To make your curls last longer, apply a small amount of mousse or styling cream to your hair before curling.
  • For a more polished look, smooth down any flyaways with a light layer of hairspray.

FAQ

How long does a 50s pin up hairstyle take to create?

With a little practice, you can create a 50s pin up style in about 20-30 minutes.

Can I create a pin up style on short hair?

Absolutely! You may not be able to achieve the same level of volume as with longer hair, but you can still create a cute, retro style with short locks.

How can I make my pin up style last all day?

Use a strong hold hairspray and plenty of bobby pins to keep your style in place. You can also use a hair scarf to protect your curls from wind and humidity.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and strong, be sure to use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner. Avoid overusing heat styling tools, and use a heat protectant spray when you do. And don't forget to get regular trims to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends.
