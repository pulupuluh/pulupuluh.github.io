How To Style Your Hair To Make Your Face Look Thinner

Written by William Feb 23, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Do you feel like your face looks too round or chubby? One easy way to create the illusion of a slimmer face is by styling your hair in a way that flatters your face shape. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for how to style your hair to make your face look thinner.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step to styling your hair to make your face look thinner is to determine your face shape. There are six main face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and long. One way to figure out your face shape is to take a selfie and trace the outline of your face with a marker on the screen. Then, compare it to the different face shape diagrams online to see which one matches the closest.

Flattering Haircuts for Different Face Shapes

Once you've determined your face shape, you can choose a haircut that flatters your features. For round faces, try a long layered cut with side-swept bangs to create a slimming effect. Oval faces look great with blunt bobs or long, beachy waves. Square faces can benefit from a soft, layered cut with face-framing angles. Heart-shaped faces look best with a choppy pixie cut or long layers with side-swept bangs. Diamond-shaped faces can pull off a blunt bob or a shaggy, layered cut. For long faces, try a shoulder-length cut with blunt ends or a long, layered cut with side-swept bangs.

Bangs to Make Your Face Look Thinner

Bangs can be a great way to frame your face and create a slimmer look. Side-swept bangs are universally flattering and can help to soften a square jawline or angular features. Blunt bangs can add balance to a heart-shaped face or draw attention away from a prominent forehead. For round faces, try long, wispy bangs that graze the cheekbones to create a slimming effect.

Layering Your Hair to Create a Slimming Effect

Layering your hair can create the illusion of a slimmer face by adding movement and texture. For round faces, try long, face-framing layers that start at the chin or collarbone. For square faces, add layers around the cheekbones and jawline to soften the features. Heart-shaped faces can benefit from layers around the crown to add height and balance out a narrow chin. Diamond-shaped faces look great with layers that start at the cheekbones and taper down to the ends. For long faces, try layers that start at the chin or collarbone and add volume to the sides.

Highlights and Low Lights to Slim Your Face

Adding highlights and low lights to your hair can create depth and dimension, which can help to slim your face. For round faces, try adding highlights around the crown to create height and draw the eye upward. For square faces, add highlights around the cheekbones and jawline to soften the features. Heart-shaped faces can benefit from highlights around the crown and low lights around the chin to create balance. Diamond-shaped faces look great with highlights around the cheekbones and low lights around the chin. For long faces, try highlights around the crown and low lights around the sides to create volume.

Creating Slimming Updos

Updos can be a great way to create a slimming effect, especially if you have long hair. Try a high, messy bun at the crown of your head to elongate your face. A low, messy bun at the nape of your neck can also create the illusion of a longer face. For a more formal look, try a sleek chignon or French twist.

Accessories to Draw Attention Away from Your Face

If you're not ready for a drastic haircut or color change, try using accessories to draw attention away from your face. Statement earrings or a bold necklace can draw the eye downward, while a scarf or hat can add interest to your outfit without highlighting your face.

Products to Help Slim Your Hair and Face

There are a few products that can help to create a slimming effect on your hair and face. Volumizing mousse or spray can add height and movement to your hair, while a contouring powder or cream can help to define your cheekbones and jawline. A matte bronzer can also help to create the illusion of a slimmer face.

FAQs

Q: Can I still have long hair if I want to make my face look thinner?

A: Absolutely! Long hair can be slimming if it's layered and styled correctly. Try adding layers around the face to create movement and texture.

Q: Will bangs make my face look thinner?

A: Bangs can be a great way to frame your face and create a slimming effect, especially if they're side-swept or wispy.

Q: Do I have to change my hair color to make my face look thinner?

A: Not necessarily. Adding highlights or low lights can create depth and dimension, but you don't have to change your hair color completely.

Hair Care Tips for a Slimmer Look

Finally, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup and keep your hair feeling light. Avoid heavy products like hair oils or serums, which can weigh your hair down and make your face look fuller. And don't forget to get regular trims to keep your ends looking fresh and healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can easily style your hair to create the illusion of a slimmer face. Whether you prefer short or long hair, bangs or no bangs, there's a hairstyle that will flatter your face shape and make you feel confident and beautiful.
