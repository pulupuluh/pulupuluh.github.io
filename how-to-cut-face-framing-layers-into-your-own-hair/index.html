How To Cut Face Framing Layers Into Your Own Hair

Written by Eveline Jun 04, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're looking for a quick and easy way to update your hairstyle, cutting face framing layers into your own hair is a great option. Not only does it add dimension and movement to your locks, but it can also help to frame your face and highlight your best features. In this post, we'll walk you through the steps to cut face framing layers into your own hair, as well as provide some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect look.

Description

Face framing layers are a popular hairstyle trend that involves cutting shorter layers around the front of the face, typically starting around the cheekbones or jawline. This style can work for any hair length, from short pixie cuts to long flowing locks, and can help to bring attention to your eyes, cheekbones, and lips.

How to Cut Face Framing Layers into Your Own Hair

Before you begin, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a sharp pair of hair cutting scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have some hair clips or ties to help section off your hair as you work.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair when cutting face framing layers. This will help you to see the natural texture and shape of your hair, and make it easier to achieve the desired look. If you need to wash your hair, make sure it's completely dry before you begin.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Using your comb, section off the front portion of your hair that you want to cut into layers. This can vary depending on your hair length and face shape, but typically involves separating a section of hair from ear to ear, with the front section starting around the cheekbones or jawline.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Starting on one side of your hair, take a small section of hair from the front and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting shorter at the front and getting longer towards the back. This will create a soft, blended look that frames your face.

Step 4: Cut the Remaining Layers

Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle, working your way towards the back of your head. Remember to keep the front sections shorter and gradually lengthen the layers towards the back. Take your time and check your progress in the mirror as you go.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all of the layers, use your scissors to blend the ends together by lightly snipping into the hair. This will help to create a seamless, natural look.

Tips for Cutting Face Framing Layers into Your Own Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect face framing layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut gradually to avoid taking off too much at once.
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors and create a clean, even cut.
  • Check your progress in the mirror as you go to make sure the layers are even on both sides.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider practicing on a wig or mannequin head first.

FAQ

How often should I cut my face framing layers?

This can vary depending on your hair type and how quickly it grows, but generally, it's a good idea to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.

Can I cut face framing layers into curly hair?

Absolutely! Face framing layers can work well on curly hair, and can help to enhance your natural texture and shape. Just be sure to cut the layers while your hair is dry and in its natural state, to avoid any surprises once it dries.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you accidentally cut too much or make a mistake, don't panic! You can always go to a professional stylist to help fix the issue, or try blending the layers in with the rest of your hair for a more natural look.

Hair Care

After cutting your face framing layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat or styling products that can damage your hair. Regular trims can also help to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layers.

Overall, cutting face framing layers into your own hair can be a fun and exciting way to update your look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy style right at home. Happy cutting!
