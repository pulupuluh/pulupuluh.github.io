How To Stop Hair Shedding From Stress A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss due to stress? You're not alone. Stress-induced hair shedding is a common problem that affects many people, especially during times of high stress. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to stop hair shedding from stress. In this article, we'll provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to stop hair shedding from stress.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Stop Hair Shedding from Stress
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Shedding
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Description

Hair shedding is a natural process that occurs when old hairs fall out and new hairs grow in their place. On average, people lose between 50 and 100 hairs per day. However, stress can disrupt this process, causing more hairs to fall out than normal. This type of hair loss is known as telogen effluvium, and it is commonly caused by stress.

During times of high stress, your body enters a state of fight or flight. This response causes changes in your hormones, which can affect the growth cycle of your hair. The result is that more hairs enter the resting phase of the growth cycle and fall out prematurely, leading to hair shedding.

How to Stop Hair Shedding from Stress

Now that you understand the basics of hair shedding from stress, let's talk about how to stop it. Here are some steps you can take:

1. Manage Your Stress

The most important step in stopping hair shedding from stress is to manage your stress levels. This can be easier said than done, but it's essential for your overall health and well-being. Try to identify the sources of your stress and find ways to manage them. This might include exercise, meditation, therapy, or simply taking time for yourself to relax.

2. Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet. Some foods that are particularly good for hair health include eggs, nuts, leafy greens, and fatty fish.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids, buns, and ponytails, can put stress on your hair follicles and lead to hair shedding. Try to wear your hair down as much as possible, and avoid tight hairstyles when you do need to tie it back.

4. Be Gentle with Your Hair

When you do need to style your hair, be gentle. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to detangle your hair. Be gentle when towel-drying your hair, and avoid rubbing it vigorously.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to stop hair shedding from stress:

  1. Identify the sources of your stress
  2. Find ways to manage your stress, such as exercise, meditation, or therapy
  3. Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals
  4. Avoid tight hairstyles that can put stress on your hair follicles
  5. Be gentle with your hair when styling and drying it

Tips to Prevent Hair Shedding

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair shedding:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can damage your hair follicles
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, as they can contribute to hair shedding

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair shedding from stress:

Q: Is hair shedding from stress permanent?

A: No, hair shedding from stress is usually temporary. As you manage your stress levels and take care of your hair, your hair growth cycle should return to normal and your hair shedding should decrease.

Q: How long does it take to stop hair shedding from stress?

A: It can take several weeks to several months to stop hair shedding from stress, depending on the severity of your hair loss and how well you're managing your stress levels.

Q: Can hair shedding from stress be prevented?

A: While it's not always possible to prevent hair shedding from stress, taking steps to manage your stress levels and take care of your hair can help reduce the likelihood of hair shedding.

Hair Care for Stress-Induced Hair Shedding

If you're experiencing hair shedding from stress, it's important to take care of your hair to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Be gentle when towel-drying your hair
  • Consider using a hair growth supplement, such as biotin or vitamin D

Remember, managing your stress levels is the most important step in stopping hair shedding from stress. By taking care of your body and your hair, you can promote healthy growth and prevent further hair loss.
