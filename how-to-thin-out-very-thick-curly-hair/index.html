How To Thin Out Very Thick Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 27, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have very thick curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to style and manage. One solution to make your hair more manageable is to thin it out. In this guide, we'll show you how to thin out very thick curly hair without damaging it. We'll cover everything you need to know, from the tools you'll need to the step-by-step process, to common mistakes to avoid.

Description

Thinning out your hair means removing some of the bulk and weight from your hair, which can make it easier to style and manage. However, thinning out very thick curly hair can be challenging, especially if you don't know how to do it properly. In this guide, we'll show you how to thin out your hair without causing damage or creating uneven layers.

How to Thin Out Very Thick Curly Hair

Tools you'll need:

The tools you'll need to thin out your hair include:

  • Thinning shears
  • Regular hair cutting shears
  • Hair clips
  • Comb

Step-by-Step Process:

Follow these steps to thin out very thick curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair as usual.
  2. Part your hair into sections using hair clips.
  3. Take one section at a time and comb it straight.
  4. Use the regular hair cutting shears to trim the ends of the hair. This will remove any split ends and reduce the overall bulk of the hair.
  5. Take the thinning shears and start cutting the hair in small sections. Be sure to use a light hand and don't cut too much hair at once.
  6. Move the thinning shears up and down the hair shaft to create a feathered effect. This will thin out the hair without creating any harsh lines or uneven layers.
  7. Repeat this process on each section of hair, making sure to cut and thin the hair evenly.
  8. After you've thinned out all the sections, wash and condition your hair again.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips:

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out very thick curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections as you gain confidence.
  • Don't overdo it. It's better to thin out your hair gradually over time rather than all at once.
  • Be sure to use sharp, high-quality shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't thin out the hair too close to the roots, as this can create an uneven look.

FAQ

Can I thin out my own hair?

Yes, you can thin out your own hair. However, it's important to use the right tools and to be careful not to overdo it or create uneven layers.

How often should I thin out my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should aim to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain a healthy, manageable look.

Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

Thinning out your hair doesn't necessarily make it look thinner. Instead, it removes some of the bulk and weight from your hair, making it easier to manage and style.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, be sure to follow a regular hair care routine. This should include washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a high-quality hairbrush, and avoiding excessive heat styling or chemical treatments.

Thinning out very thick curly hair can be a great way to make it easier to manage and style. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can achieve a healthy, natural-looking hairstyle without damaging your hair.
