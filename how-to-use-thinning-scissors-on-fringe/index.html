Description

Thinning scissors are a great way to achieve a natural-looking and textured fringe. They are designed to remove some of the bulk from the hair, without leaving any noticeable lines or steps in the cut. Using thinning scissors on your fringe can help to soften your look and make your hair easier to style.

However, it's important to know how to use them properly to avoid any mishaps. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to use thinning scissors on fringe.

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Fringe

Before we get started, it's important to note that thinning scissors should only be used on dry hair. Wet hair can stretch and bounce back, which can affect the outcome of the cut. Make sure that your hair is clean, dry, and free from tangles before you begin.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to use thinning scissors on your fringe:

  1. Section your hair: Use a comb to section off your fringe from the rest of your hair. You can also use clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.
  2. Start from the middle: Hold the thinning scissors parallel to your fringe, and start cutting from the middle. This will help to create a natural-looking texture, rather than a blunt cut.
  3. Work your way outwards: Keep cutting small sections of hair, moving outwards towards the edges of your fringe. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven cut.
  4. Check your progress: As you go, make sure to check your progress in the mirror. This will help you to see if you need to make any adjustments.
  5. Blend the edges: Once you've finished cutting, use a comb to blend the edges of your fringe with the rest of your hair. This will help to create a seamless transition.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors on Fringe

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors on your fringe:

  • Go slow: Take your time when cutting your fringe, and don't rush the process. This will help you to achieve a more precise and even cut.
  • Don't cut too much: It's better to cut less hair at once, as you can always go back and cut more if needed. Cutting too much hair at once can result in an uneven cut.
  • Use the right scissors: Make sure that you're using thinning scissors specifically designed for hair. Using regular scissors can result in a choppy and uneven cut.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning scissors on fringe:

Can I use thinning scissors on wet hair?

No, thinning scissors should only be used on dry hair. Wet hair can stretch and bounce back, which can affect the outcome of the cut.

How often should I use thinning scissors on my fringe?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how you want your fringe to look. Some people may need to use thinning scissors every few weeks, while others may only need to use them once a month.

Can I use thinning scissors on curly hair?

Yes, you can use thinning scissors on curly hair. However, it's important to be careful not to thin out too much hair, as this can result in frizz.

Hair Care after Using Thinning Scissors on Fringe

After using thinning scissors on your fringe, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray if you must use them.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your fringe.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish and moisturize your hair.

Thinning scissors can be a great tool for achieving a natural-looking and textured fringe. By following these tips and tricks, you can use thinning scissors safely and effectively to achieve the look you want.
