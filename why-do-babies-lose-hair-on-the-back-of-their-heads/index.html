Why Do Babies Lose Hair On The Back Of Their Heads

Written by William Aug 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

As a new parent, you may be wondering why your baby is losing hair on the back of their head. This is a common phenomenon that affects many infants, but it can be concerning for parents who are not familiar with the reason behind it. In this article, we will explore why babies lose hair on the back of their heads, how to prevent it, and how to take care of your baby's hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Babies lose hair on the back of their heads for a variety of reasons, but the most common is due to friction. When babies spend a lot of time lying on their backs, their hair rubs against the surface they are lying on, causing it to fall out. This is especially true for infants who spend a lot of time in car seats or baby swings. In addition to friction, some babies may lose hair due to hormonal changes or nutrient deficiencies.

How to

The best way to prevent hair loss on the back of your baby's head is to ensure they spend less time lying on their backs. This means limiting the amount of time your baby spends in a car seat or baby swing, and encouraging tummy time during playtime. Additionally, you can use a soft pillow or blanket to cushion your baby's head and prevent friction against hard surfaces.

Step by step

  1. Limit time spent in car seats or baby swings.
  2. Encourage tummy time during playtime.
  3. Use a soft pillow or blanket to cushion your baby's head.
  4. Ensure your baby is getting enough nutrients through breast milk or formula.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for preventing hair loss on the back of your baby's head:

  • Change your baby's position frequently during the day.
  • Use a soft-bristled brush to gently comb your baby's hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your baby's hair.
  • Protect your baby's head from the sun with a hat or sunscreen.
  • Consult with your pediatrician if you are concerned about your baby's hair loss.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal for babies to lose hair on the back of their heads?

A: Yes, it is common for babies to lose hair on the back of their heads due to friction or hormonal changes.

Q: Will my baby's hair grow back?

A: Yes, your baby's hair will grow back over time. Most babies start to grow new hair within a few months.

Hair Care

While your baby's hair is growing back, it is important to take care of their scalp and hair. Use a mild baby shampoo to wash their hair, and avoid using adult hair products that may be too harsh for their delicate skin. Additionally, use a soft brush or comb to gently detangle your baby's hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on it. With proper care, your baby's hair will grow back healthy and full.
