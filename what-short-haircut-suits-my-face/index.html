What Short Haircut Suits My Face

Written by William Apr 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are considering getting a short haircut, you may be wondering what style would suit your face shape. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one. In this article, we will explore different short haircuts that complement different face shapes. Keep reading to find out which short haircut suits your face.

Description

A good haircut should enhance your features and complement your face shape. Short haircuts are a popular choice for those who want a low maintenance and chic look. However, not all short haircuts are created equal. The right haircut can make your face look more symmetrical and balanced. On the other hand, the wrong haircut can accentuate your flaws and make you look unflattering.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into the different short haircuts, you need to determine your face shape. You can do this by pulling your hair back and examining your face in the mirror. There are five main face shapes:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond

Each face shape has its own unique features and characteristics. Once you have determined your face shape, you can choose a short haircut that complements your features.

Short Haircuts for Round Faces

If you have a round face, you want to choose a short haircut that creates the illusion of length and angles. A layered bob or a pixie cut with side-swept bangs are great options. Avoid blunt cuts and center-parted styles, as they can make your face look wider.

Short Haircuts for Oval Faces

Oval faces are considered the most versatile face shape, as they can pull off almost any haircut. If you have an oval face, you can experiment with different short haircuts such as a blunt bob or a textured pixie cut. However, avoid hairstyles that cover your forehead, as they can make your face look longer.

Short Haircuts for Square Faces

If you have a square face, you want to soften your angles and create the illusion of length. A layered bob with side-swept bangs or a wavy pixie cut are great options. Avoid straight and blunt cuts, as they can make your face look boxy.

Short Haircuts for Heart Faces

Heart-shaped faces have a wider forehead and a narrow chin. If you have a heart-shaped face, you want to choose a short haircut that balances out your features. A chin-length bob or a messy pixie cut with bangs are great options. Avoid hairstyles that are too voluminous on top, as they can make your forehead look wider.

Short Haircuts for Diamond Faces

Diamond-shaped faces have a narrow forehead and a narrow chin. If you have a diamond-shaped face, you want to choose a short haircut that adds width to your forehead and jawline. A textured bob or a layered pixie cut with volume on top are great options. Avoid hairstyles that are too narrow or too voluminous on the sides, as they can make your face look unbalanced.

Step by Step Guide

1. Determine your face shape by pulling your hair back and examining your face in the mirror.

2. Choose a short haircut that complements your features and balances out your face shape.

3. Show your hair stylist a picture of the short haircut you want.

4. Discuss any concerns or questions you have with your hair stylist.

5. After your haircut, style your hair according to your face shape.

Tips

- Bring a picture of the short haircut you want to your hair appointment.

- Consider your hair texture and thickness when choosing a short haircut.

- Experiment with different styling techniques to find what works best for your face shape.

FAQ

Q: Can I pull off a short haircut if I have a round face?

A: Yes, you can. Choose a short haircut that creates the illusion of length and angles.

Q: What short haircut suits oval faces?

A: Oval faces can pull off almost any short haircut. Experiment with different styles and see what works best for you.

Q: How do I know if a short haircut suits my face?

A: A good haircut should enhance your features and complement your face shape. If you feel confident and comfortable with your new short haircut, then it suits your face.

Hair Care

Short hair requires less maintenance than long hair, but it still needs some TLC. Here are some tips for taking care of your short hair:

  • Wash your hair every other day to prevent dryness.
  • Use a lightweight conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage your strands.
  • Get regular trims to keep your haircut looking fresh and polished.

Now that you know what short haircut suits your face, it's time to book an appointment with your hair stylist. Remember, a good haircut can boost your confidence and make you feel like a million bucks.
