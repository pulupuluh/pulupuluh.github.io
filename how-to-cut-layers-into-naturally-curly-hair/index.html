How To Cut Layers Into Naturally Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 03, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of your curly hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers can give your curls more volume and shape, but cutting curly hair can be tricky. In this post, we’ll show you how to cut layers into naturally curly hair using simple techniques that anyone can master. By the end, you’ll have bouncy, beautiful curls that turn heads wherever you go.

Description

Layered haircuts are a great way to enhance the natural texture of curly hair. They add depth and dimension to your curls, making them look fuller and more defined. However, cutting curly hair can be tricky, especially if you’re new to it. Unlike straight hair, curly hair bounces up when cut, so it’s essential to know how to cut it properly.

In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut layers into naturally curly hair in a way that enhances your curls’ natural shape and texture. Whether you have loose waves or tight ringlets, these techniques will help you achieve the perfect layered look.

How to Cut Layers into Naturally Curly Hair

Before you start cutting, it’s essential to have the right tools. You’ll need a good pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Here’s how to cut layers into naturally curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help to keep your curls hydrated and prevent frizz. After washing, gently towel dry your hair and let it air dry until it’s about 80% dry. Don’t use a hairdryer, as this can cause frizz and damage your curls.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Clip each section up, leaving the bottom section loose. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and twist it tightly. Hold the twisted section between your fingers and cut off the ends. Repeat this process until you’ve cut all the hair in the bottom section. This will create a guide for the rest of your layers.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layers

Release the next section of hair and twist a small section tightly. Hold the twisted section between your fingers and cut off the ends, using the bottom layer as your guide. Continue this process until you’ve cut all the hair in the middle section.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layers

Release the top sections of hair and repeat the twisting and cutting process, using the bottom and middle layers as your guide. Be careful not to cut too much hair, as this can create a choppy, uneven look.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut all the layers, it’s time to blend them together. Take a small section of hair from each layer and twist it together. Use your scissors to snip off any stray ends. This will help to create a seamless, blended look.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect layered look:

  • Start with small sections of hair, as curly hair bounces up when cut.
  • Twist each section tightly before cutting to create a guide for the rest of your layers.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair, as this can create an uneven look.
  • Blend the layers together to create a seamless look.
  • If you’re unsure, start with a small trim and work your way up.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you cut curly hair when it’s wet or dry?

It’s best to cut curly hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can stretch and bounce up when cut. This can make it difficult to achieve an even look.

How often should you cut curly hair?

It’s recommended to cut curly hair every 8-12 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Should you use thinning shears on curly hair?

No, it’s not recommended to use thinning shears on curly hair. They can create a choppy, uneven look and cause frizz.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep your curly hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look for your naturally curly hair. Whether you have tight coils or loose waves, these tips will help you enhance your natural texture and keep your curls healthy and beautiful.
