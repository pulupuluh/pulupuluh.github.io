How To Decide What Color Blush To Wear A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Feb 21, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Blush is one of the most transformative makeup products out there. With just a few swipes, you can instantly add color and dimension to your face, making you look healthier, more vibrant, and more youthful. But with so many blush colors to choose from, how do you know which one is right for you? In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know to decide what color blush to wear.

Table of Contents

  • Determining Your Skin Tone
  • Choosing Blush Colors for Your Skin Tone
  • Application Tips
  • Choosing a Finish
  • Choosing a Texture
  • FAQs

Determining Your Skin Tone

The first step in deciding what color blush to wear is to determine your skin tone. There are three main categories: cool, warm, and neutral. Here's how to figure out which one you are:

  • Cool: If you have pink, red, or bluish undertones in your skin, you're cool-toned.
  • Warm: If you have yellow, peachy, or golden undertones in your skin, you're warm-toned.
  • Neutral: If you have a mix of both warm and cool undertones in your skin, you're neutral-toned.

Still not sure? Take a look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they look blue, you're cool-toned. If they look greenish, you're warm-toned. If they look like a mix of both, you're neutral-toned.

Choosing Blush Colors for Your Skin Tone

Once you know your skin tone, you can start to choose blush colors that will complement it. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Cool-toned skin: Stick to blushes that have cool undertones, like pink, rose, and mauve. Avoid anything too warm or orange-toned.
  • Warm-toned skin: Go for blushes that have warm undertones, like peach, coral, and bronze. Avoid anything too cool or pink-toned.
  • Neutral-toned skin: Lucky you! You can wear just about any blush color you want. Try experimenting with both warm and cool shades to see what looks best on you.

Remember, these are just guidelines. If you're drawn to a certain blush color that doesn't necessarily "match" your skin tone, go ahead and try it anyway! Makeup is all about having fun and expressing yourself.

Application Tips

Once you've chosen your blush color, it's time to apply it. Here are some tips to make sure you get the most natural-looking flush:

  • Use a fluffy, dome-shaped brush to apply your blush. This will help you blend it seamlessly into your skin.
  • Start with a light hand and build up the color gradually. It's easier to add more blush than it is to take it away!
  • Smile to find the apples of your cheeks, and apply your blush there. Then, blend it out along your cheekbones.
  • If you accidentally apply too much blush, don't panic! Use a clean brush to blend it out or tone it down with a bit of translucent powder.

Choosing a Finish

Blushes come in a variety of finishes, from matte to shimmer to satin. Here's how to choose the right one for you:

  • Matte: If you have oily skin or want a more natural-looking flush, go for a matte blush.
  • Shimmer: If you want a glowy, lit-from-within look, try a shimmer blush. Just be careful not to overdo it, or you might end up looking like a disco ball!
  • Satin: If you want the best of both worlds, opt for a satin blush. It has a subtle sheen that gives you a healthy glow without being too sparkly.

Choosing a Texture

Blushes also come in different textures, like powder, cream, and liquid. Here's how to choose the right one for you:

  • Powder: If you have oily skin or want a more natural-looking finish, go for a powder blush.
  • Cream: If you have dry or mature skin, try a cream blush. It will give you a dewy, youthful glow.
  • Liquid: If you want a long-lasting, natural-looking flush, try a liquid blush. It's also great for layering under powder blush for extra staying power.

FAQs

Q: Can I wear more than one blush color at once?

A: Absolutely! Layering different blush shades can create a unique, multidimensional look. Just make sure to blend them together well.

Q: What should I do if I have very fair or very deep skin?

A: Fair-skinned people should stick to light, sheer blushes in cool pink or peach shades. Deep-skinned people can go for bold, highly pigmented blushes in warm red or berry tones.

Q: Should I match my blush to my lip color?

A: It's not necessary, but it can create a cohesive, polished look. If you're wearing a bold lip color, try a more muted blush shade. If you're wearing a neutral lip color, you can amp up your blush for a pop of color.

Q: How often should I replace my blush?

A: Powder blushes can last up to two years, while cream and liquid blushes should be replaced every six to 12 months.

Choosing the right blush color can be intimidating, but it doesn't have to be. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to find the perfect shade for your skin tone and apply it like a pro. Happy blushing!
