Should I Cut Bangs If My Hair Is Thinning

Written by Christine Mar 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thinning hair, you may be considering a new hairstyle to help conceal the problem. One option that many people consider is cutting bangs. Bangs can be a great way to change up your look and make your hair appear fuller, but it's important to carefully consider whether or not they're the right choice for you.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Cut Bangs
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Cutting Bangs
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

Description

Bangs can be a great way to give your hair volume and make it look fuller. However, if you have thinning hair, cutting bangs may not be the best solution. Bangs require maintenance and styling to look their best, and if you're already struggling with thinning hair, you may not have the time or energy to devote to this. Additionally, cutting bangs can actually make thinning hair more obvious, as the hair on your forehead may be even thinner than the rest of your hair.

How to Cut Bangs

If you've decided to cut bangs despite the potential risks, there are a few things you should keep in mind. First, consider the type of bangs that would work best for your face shape and hair type. For example, if you have a round face, side-swept bangs may be more flattering than blunt bangs. Similarly, if you have curly hair, you may want to opt for longer, wispy bangs that blend into the rest of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by sectioning off the hair you want to cut into bangs. Use a comb to create a triangle-shaped section of hair at the front of your head.

2. Comb the hair straight down and hold it firmly between your fingers.

3. Cut the hair straight across, making sure to leave it longer than you actually want it to be. You can always trim it shorter later.

4. Use a comb to check for any uneven areas and trim as necessary.

5. Style your bangs as desired, using a blow dryer and round brush to create volume.

Tips for Cutting Bangs

If you're going to cut your own bangs, there are a few tips you should keep in mind:

  • Start with longer bangs than you actually want, as you can always trim them shorter later.
  • Use sharp scissors designed for cutting hair.
  • Don't cut your bangs straight across if you have curly hair, as this can make them look too short when they dry.
  • Consider asking a friend or family member to help you cut your bangs, as it can be difficult to do it yourself.

FAQs

Q: Will cutting bangs hide my thinning hair?

A: Bangs can be a great way to change up your look and give your hair volume, but they may not necessarily hide thinning hair. In fact, cutting bangs can sometimes make thinning hair more obvious, as the hair on your forehead may be even thinner than the rest of your hair.

Q: Can I cut my own bangs?

A: Yes, you can cut your own bangs, but it can be difficult to get them just right. Consider asking a friend or family member to help you, or visit a professional stylist to ensure you get the look you want.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

If you're struggling with thinning hair, there are a few things you can do to help make your hair look fuller:

  • Use volumizing shampoos and conditioners.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh your hair down.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to create volume.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it look thinner.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.

Ultimately, the decision to cut bangs if your hair is thinning is a personal one. Consider your face shape, hair type, and the amount of time and effort you're willing to put into styling your hair before making a decision. And if you're still unsure, consider consulting with a professional stylist who can help you choose a hairstyle that will flatter your face and hair type.
