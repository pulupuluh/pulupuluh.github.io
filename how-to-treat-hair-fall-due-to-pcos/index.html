How To Treat Hair Fall Due To Pcos A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Jun 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling with hair loss due to PCOS? You're not alone. Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) affects millions of women worldwide, and one of the most common symptoms is excessive hair fall. The good news is that there are several ways to treat hair loss due to PCOS. In this article, we'll discuss the causes of hair loss, how to prevent it, and how to treat it.

What Causes Hair Fall Due to PCOS?

PCOS is a hormonal disorder that affects the ovaries. Women with PCOS have higher levels of androgens, which are male hormones, than normal. This hormonal imbalance can lead to a variety of symptoms, including hair loss. Androgens can cause the hair follicles to shrink, resulting in weaker, thinner hair that falls out more easily.

How to Prevent Hair Fall Due to PCOS

Preventing hair loss due to PCOS requires a comprehensive approach that addresses the underlying hormonal imbalance. Here are some tips to help prevent hair fall:

Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and lean protein can help regulate insulin levels, which can in turn help regulate androgen levels. Avoid processed foods and sugary drinks, which can cause insulin spikes.

Exercise Regularly

Regular exercise can help regulate insulin and androgen levels. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise, such as brisk walking, most days of the week.

Manage Stress

Stress can increase insulin and androgen levels, so it's important to manage stress levels. Try relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.

How to Treat Hair Fall Due to PCOS

If you're already experiencing hair loss due to PCOS, there are several treatment options available. Here are some of the most effective treatments:

Medications

There are several medications that can help treat hair loss due to PCOS. These include:

  • Birth control pills: These can help regulate hormone levels and reduce androgen levels.
  • Anti-androgen medications: These can block the effects of androgens on the hair follicles.
  • Topical minoxidil: This medication can help stimulate hair growth.

Hair Transplant

If you have severe hair loss, a hair transplant may be a good option. This involves taking hair follicles from a donor area and transplanting them to the affected area.

Hair Replacement Systems

If you're not a candidate for a hair transplant, a hair replacement system may be a good option. These are custom-made hairpieces that are attached to the scalp with adhesive.

Tips for Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent further hair loss. Here are some tips:

Use Gentle Hair Care Products

Avoid harsh shampoos and conditioners that can strip the hair of its natural oils. Use products that are designed for your hair type and that are gentle on the scalp.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as straighteners and curling irons can damage the hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try air-drying your hair or using heat-protectant products before styling.

Be Gentle When Brushing

Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles to avoid pulling on the hair. Brush gently and avoid tugging or pulling.

FAQ

Can PCOS hair loss be reversed?

Yes, PCOS hair loss can be reversed with the right treatment. Medications, hair transplants, and hair replacement systems can all help regrow hair.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after PCOS?

It can take several months to a year for hair to grow back after PCOS. The timeline depends on the severity of the hair loss and the effectiveness of the treatment.

Can diet help prevent PCOS hair loss?

Yes, maintaining a healthy diet can help prevent PCOS hair loss. Eating a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and lean protein can help regulate insulin and androgen levels.

Conclusion

PCOS hair loss can be frustrating, but there are several effective treatments available. By maintaining a healthy lifestyle, seeking treatment, and taking good care of your hair, you can prevent further hair loss and regrow your hair. If you're struggling with hair loss due to PCOS, talk to your doctor about the best treatment options for you.
