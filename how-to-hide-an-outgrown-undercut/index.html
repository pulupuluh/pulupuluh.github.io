How To Hide An Outgrown Undercut Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Aug 05, 2023 ¬∑ 7 min read

If you've had an undercut hairstyle for a while, you may have noticed that it can be difficult to hide as it grows out. But don't fret! There are plenty of ways to style your hair to hide an outgrown undercut. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for hiding an outgrown undercut so you can feel confident and stylish.

How to Hide an Outgrown Undercut

First things first, it's important to understand what an undercut is and why it can be tricky to hide. An undercut is a hairstyle where the sides and/or back of the head are shaved or cut very short, while the top is left longer. This creates a dramatic contrast between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.

As the undercut grows out, the longer hair on top can become heavy and difficult to style. Additionally, the short hair on the sides and back can start to stick out and look unkempt. To hide an outgrown undercut, you'll need to find ways to balance out the longer and shorter sections of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for hiding an outgrown undercut:

Step 1: Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it until it's damp.

Step 2: Apply a styling product to your hair. This could be a mousse, gel, or pomade, depending on your hair type and the look you're going for.

Step 3: Use a blow dryer to dry your hair while using a round brush to add volume and shape to the longer sections on top.

Step 4: Use a comb or brush to smooth down the shorter sections on the sides and back. You may need to use some hairspray or other product to keep these sections in place.

Step 5: Style your hair as desired. You could try a slicked-back look, a messy bun, or a half-up, half-down style to hide the outgrown undercut.

Tips for Hiding an Outgrown Undercut

Here are some additional tips for hiding an outgrown undercut:

  • Experiment with different styles: Don't be afraid to try out new styles and see what works best for you. You may find that a certain style or product works better than others.
  • Use accessories: Headbands, scarves, and hats can all be great accessories for hiding an outgrown undercut. They can also add some extra style to your look!
  • Get regular haircuts: Even if you're growing out your hair, it's important to get regular haircuts to keep the shorter sections looking neat and tidy.
  • Consider a new haircut: If you're really struggling to hide your outgrown undercut, you may want to consider getting a new haircut altogether. Talk to your hairstylist about options that would work well with your hair type and face shape.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about hiding an outgrown undercut:

Q: How long does it take to grow out an undercut?

A: It depends on how short the undercut was and how fast your hair grows. Generally, it can take anywhere from a few months to a year to fully grow out an undercut.

Q: Can I still style my hair while growing out an undercut?

A: Absolutely! You may just need to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep in mind while growing out an undercut:

  • Use a conditioner: Conditioner can help keep your hair healthy and nourished as it grows out.
  • Avoid heat damage: Try to avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons as much as possible. If you do use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet: Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to hide your outgrown undercut with confidence and style. Remember to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you, and don't forget to take good care of your hair as it grows out. Happy styling!
