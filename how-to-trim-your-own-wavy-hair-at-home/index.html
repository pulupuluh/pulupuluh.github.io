How To Trim Your Own Wavy Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 14, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon for a simple trim? With a bit of practice and patience, you can learn how to trim your own wavy hair at home. Not only will it save you money, but it will also give you more control over your hairstyle and allow you to experiment with different looks. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process step-by-step, share some tips and tricks, and answer some frequently asked questions.

3. How to Trim Your Hair

How to Prepare

Before you start trimming your wavy hair, it’s important to prepare your tools and workspace. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Sectioning clips or rubber bands
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • A large mirror or two mirrors
  • A clean towel or cape to catch hair clippings

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you begin. It’s also a good idea to wear a shirt that you don’t mind getting hair on.

How to Section Your Hair

Once you have everything you need, it’s time to section your hair. This will help you achieve an even trim and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much hair. Here’s how:

  1. Start by parting your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck.
  2. Clip or tie one side of your hair out of the way.
  3. Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down.
  4. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about an inch or two from the ends.
  5. Use your scissors to snip off the tips of the hair, about a quarter of an inch at a time.
  6. Repeat this process with small sections of hair all around your head, working your way from the back to the front.
  7. Once you’ve trimmed one side of your head, unclip the other side and repeat the process.

How to Trim Your Hair

When it comes to actually trimming your hair, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Always start with small snips. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.
  • Hold your scissors vertically, not horizontally. This will help you achieve a more natural-looking edge.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Trimming your own hair can be tiring, so don’t be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.

How to Check for Evenness

Once you’ve finished trimming your hair, it’s important to check for evenness. Here’s how:

  1. Stand in front of a mirror and comb your hair straight down.
  2. Look for any sections that appear longer or shorter than the rest.
  3. Use your scissors to make any necessary adjustments, taking care to make small snips.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect trim:

  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help. It can be difficult to trim the back of your head, so ask a friend or family member for assistance.
  • Invest in a good pair of haircutting scissors. They’ll make the process much easier and give you better results.
  • Take your time. Rushing through the process can lead to mistakes and uneven cuts.
  • Experiment with different styles. Trimming your own hair gives you more control over your hairstyle, so don’t be afraid to try something new.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about trimming wavy hair:

How often should I trim my wavy hair?

It’s generally recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Can I trim my hair when it’s wet?

It’s best to trim your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer and make it difficult to achieve an even cut.

Should I use thinning shears?

Thinning shears can be useful for removing bulk and creating texture, but they should be used sparingly and only on specific sections of hair.

Hair Care Tips

After you’ve finished trimming your wavy hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause breakage and split ends.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to nourish and moisturize it.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as it can cause breakage and damage.

Trimming your own wavy hair at home can be a bit intimidating at first, but with practice and patience, you can master the technique and achieve salon-worthy results. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and check for evenness throughout the process. With these tips and tricks, you’ll be a pro in no time!
