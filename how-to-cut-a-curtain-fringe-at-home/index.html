How To Cut A Curtain Fringe At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for an easy way to update your hairstyle, cutting a curtain fringe at home is a great option. Not only is it a low-maintenance look, but it can also be customized to suit your face shape and personal style. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut a curtain fringe at home, as well as provide some tips and hair care advice to keep your new look looking fresh and fabulous.

How to Cut a Curtain Fringe at Home

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting your own fringe can be tricky, especially if you've never done it before. If you're hesitant, it may be best to seek the help of a professional stylist. However, if you're feeling confident and want to give it a try, here's how to cut a curtain fringe at home:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp hair-cutting scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Start by sectioning off the hair you want to cut. For a curtain fringe, you'll want to create a triangular section of hair that starts at the top of your head and ends at your eyebrows. Use your comb to create the section, and clip the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Determine the Length

With your hair sectioned off, you can now determine the length of your fringe. Use your fingers to pinch the hair where you want it to fall, and cut straight across with your scissors. It's important to take it slow and cut small amounts at a time, as you can always trim more later.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Once you've cut the length of your fringe, it's time to cut the sides. Take small sections of hair on either side of your fringe and angle your scissors downwards towards your face. This will create a soft, blended look that frames your face.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, it's important to check your work. Make sure your fringe is even and symmetrical, and trim any stray hairs as needed.

Tips for Cutting a Curtain Fringe at Home

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect curtain fringe:

  • Start with dry hair. Wet hair can be harder to cut, as it tends to shrink as it dries.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can create uneven, jagged cuts.
  • Take it slow. It's better to cut small amounts at a time than to cut too much off at once.
  • Don't cut too short. Remember that hair will bounce back, so it's better to err on the side of caution.
  • Style your fringe. Once you've cut your fringe, style it with a blow dryer and round brush for a polished look.

FAQ: Cutting a Curtain Fringe at Home

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a curtain fringe:

Can I cut a curtain fringe if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut a curtain fringe if you have curly hair. However, it's important to cut it when your hair is dry and styled in its natural state, as curly hair can shrink when it dries.

How often should I trim my fringe?

It's best to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh and polished.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your fringe, don't panic. You can always pin it back or seek the help of a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care for Your Curtain Fringe

Now that you've cut your curtain fringe, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your fringe clean and oil-free.
  • Blow dry your fringe with a round brush to create volume and shape.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your fringe to prevent damage.
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your fringe between washes.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut a gorgeous curtain fringe at home. Just remember to take it slow and be patient, and you'll have a chic and stylish new look in no time.
