How To Fix Snapped Hair From Bleach

Written by Eveline Jun 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you dealing with snapped hair from bleach? If so, don't panic. There are several ways to fix it, and we've got all the details below. In this blog post, we'll cover everything from how to fix snapped hair from bleach to hair care tips that can help prevent it from happening in the first place. So, let's get started!

Description

Bleaching your hair can give you a whole new look, but it can also cause some serious damage. One of the most common problems people face after bleaching their hair is snapped hair. This happens when the hair becomes so weak that it snaps off, leaving you with uneven, broken hair. It's a frustrating problem, but it's not impossible to fix.

How to Fix Snapped Hair from Bleach

There are several ways to fix snapped hair from bleach, and the best method depends on the severity of the damage. Here are a few options:

1. Trim Your Hair

If your hair is severely damaged, the best thing you can do is trim it. This will get rid of the snapped ends and give your hair a fresh start. It may be hard to let go of some length, but it's worth it to have healthy hair again.

2. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help strengthen your hair and prevent further damage. Look for a product that contains keratin or collagen, as these are both effective at repairing damaged hair. Follow the instructions on the product and use it regularly to see results.

3. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning can also help repair snapped hair. Look for a product that contains natural oils, such as coconut or argan oil. Apply the conditioner to your hair and let it sit for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step by Step

1. Trim Your Hair

Step 1: Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

Step 2: Comb through your hair to remove any tangles.

Step 3: Use hair cutting scissors to trim off the snapped ends. Start with a small amount and work your way up until you're satisfied with the length.

Step 4: Style your hair as usual.

2. Use a Protein Treatment

Step 1: Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and towel dry.

Step 2: Apply the protein treatment to your hair, focusing on the areas that are most damaged.

Step 3: Leave the treatment on for the recommended amount of time.

Step 4: Rinse the treatment out of your hair and style as usual.

3. Deep Condition Your Hair

Step 1: Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and towel dry.

Step 2: Apply the deep conditioner to your hair, focusing on the areas that are most damaged.

Step 3: Cover your hair with a shower cap and let the conditioner sit for at least 30 minutes.

Step 4: Rinse the conditioner out of your hair and style as usual.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help prevent snapped hair from bleach:

  • Don't bleach your hair too often.
  • Use a lower volume developer.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly.

FAQ

Can you fix snapped hair from bleach?

Yes, snapped hair from bleach can be fixed. Try trimming your hair, using a protein treatment, or deep conditioning your hair.

How do you prevent snapped hair from bleach?

To prevent snapped hair from bleach, avoid bleaching your hair too often, use a lower volume developer, avoid using heat styling tools, and deep condition your hair regularly.

Hair Care

It's important to take good care of your hair, especially if it's been damaged by bleach. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Don't brush your hair when it's wet
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends

By following these tips and using the methods we've outlined above, you can fix snapped hair from bleach and prevent it from happening in the future. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair as it recovers, and you'll have healthy, beautiful locks once again.
