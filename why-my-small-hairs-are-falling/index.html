Why Are My Small Hairs Falling Possible Causes And Solutions

Written by William Aug 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you noticing small hairs falling out when you comb or wash your hair? This can be a common issue that affects both men and women, and it can be due to various reasons. In this blog post, we will explore the possible causes and solutions to why your small hairs are falling.

Table of Content

  • Hair Care

  • Tips to Prevent Small Hair Loss

Genetics

One of the most common reasons for small hair loss is genetics. If your family members have a history of hair loss or baldness, then it is likely that you are genetically predisposed to it. It is important to understand that genetic hair loss cannot be prevented entirely, but there are treatments available to slow down the process and stimulate hair growth.

Hair Care

Over-styling, using too much heat, and chemical treatments such as coloring and perming can damage your hair and cause small hairs to fall out. To prevent this, it is essential to take good care of your hair. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner, avoid using too much heat, and limit hair treatments to once in a while.

Stress

Stress can cause a lot of physical and mental health issues, including hair loss. When you are stressed, your body produces hormones that can damage hair follicles and cause hair loss. To prevent this, it is essential to manage your stress levels through exercise, relaxation techniques, and getting enough rest.

Nutrition

Your diet plays a significant role in the health of your hair. If your diet lacks essential nutrients such as protein, iron, and vitamins, then it can lead to hair loss. Make sure to include a balanced diet that includes fruits, vegetables, lean meats, and whole grains.

Hormones

Hormonal changes in the body can also cause hair loss. This is especially true for women who experience hormonal changes during pregnancy, menopause, and menstruation. If you notice small hair loss during these times, then it is likely due to hormonal changes.

Scalp Issues

Issues with your scalp such as dandruff, fungal infections, and psoriasis can also cause small hair loss. Make sure to keep your scalp clean and healthy by washing it regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner. If you notice any issues with your scalp, then it is essential to seek medical attention.

Medication

Some medications such as chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and antidepressants can cause hair loss. If you notice small hair loss after starting any medication, then it is essential to speak to your doctor. They may be able to suggest alternative medications or treatments to prevent hair loss.

FAQ

How much hair loss is normal?

It is normal to lose between 50 to 100 hairs per day. If you notice more hair loss than this, then it is essential to speak to your doctor.

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss. When you are stressed, your body produces hormones that can damage hair follicles and cause hair loss.

Can diet affect hair loss?

Yes, a lack of essential nutrients such as protein, iron, and vitamins can lead to hair loss.

Tips to Prevent Small Hair Loss

  • Take good care of your hair by using mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid over-styling, using too much heat, and chemical treatments.
  • Manage your stress levels through exercise, relaxation techniques, and getting enough rest.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes fruits, vegetables, lean meats, and whole grains.
  • Seek medical attention if you notice any issues with your scalp or hair loss.

By following these tips and understanding the possible causes of small hair loss, you can prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. If you are concerned about your hair loss, then it is essential to speak to your doctor to determine the underlying cause and the best course of treatment.
