How To Cut Layers In Medium Hair Yourself

Written by Christine Mar 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for an easy and affordable way to spruce up your medium-length hair, adding layers is a great option. Not only does it create movement and texture, but it also adds volume and dimension to your locks. And the best part? You can easily do it yourself at home! In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps on how to cut layers in medium hair yourself, so you can achieve the perfect salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home.

How to Cut Layers in Medium Hair Yourself

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, it's important to gather the necessary tools. You'll need:

  • Sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • Clips to section your hair
  • A spray bottle with water

Now that you have everything you need, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair, so it's clean and tangle-free. Brush through it with a comb to ensure there are no knots or snarls.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to create a clean part down the middle of your head, dividing your hair into two sections. Clip one side out of the way and focus on the other side.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. Do you want them to start at your chin, collarbone, or lower? Use your fingers as a guide and mark off where you want the bottom layer to fall.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Using your scissors, start cutting your hair at the bottom layer, holding your hair at a 45-degree angle away from your head. Cut straight across and continue working your way up to the top layer, cutting small sections at a time. Make sure to keep the sections even and use a comb to guide you as you cut.

Step 5: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you've finished one side, unclip the other side and repeat the process. Make sure the layers are symmetrical and even on both sides.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

To blend the layers and create a seamless look, take a small section of hair from the top layer and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to cut into the hair, creating small snips. This will create texture and blend the layers together. Repeat this process throughout your hair, being careful not to overdo it.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you're finished cutting, style your hair as desired. You can blow-dry it straight or add some waves with a curling iron. The layers will add volume and dimension to your hair, making it look fuller and more polished.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Medium Hair Yourself

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in medium hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections as you gain confidence.
  • Don't cut too much at once. You can always go back and trim more if needed.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. It's better to cut slowly and carefully than to rush and make a mistake.

FAQs

Can I Cut Layers in Curly Hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. Just make sure to cut your hair when it's dry and use your curls as a guide. Cut at an angle to avoid creating a blunt look.

How Often Should I Cut My Layers?

It's recommended to cut your layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and avoid split ends. However, if you're happy with the length and shape of your layers, you can wait longer between cuts.

Can I Cut Layers in Thin Hair?

Yes, layers can actually add volume and texture to thin hair. Just be careful not to overdo it and create too many layers, as this can make your hair look even thinner.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Now that you've successfully cut layers in your medium hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to replenish moisture and nourish your strands.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your layers.

Cutting layers in medium hair yourself is easier than you might think. With the right tools, a little patience, and our step-by-step guide, you can achieve the perfect layered look at home. Just remember to take your time, start with small sections, and use a mirror to check your progress. Happy cutting!
