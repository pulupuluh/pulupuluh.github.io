How To Cure Bleach Damaged Hair The Ultimate Guide

Written by William Apr 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you've ever bleached your hair, you know the feeling of excitement and anticipation that comes with seeing your new, lighter locks. However, you may also be familiar with the aftermath of bleach damage, which can leave your hair looking and feeling dry, brittle, and damaged. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to repair and restore your hair to its former glory. Keep reading for our ultimate guide on how to cure bleach damaged hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cure Bleach Damaged Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Bleach Damage
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Bleach-Damaged Hair

How to Cure Bleach Damaged Hair

The first step in curing bleach damaged hair is to assess the extent of the damage. If your hair feels dry and brittle, you can start by deep conditioning your hair once or twice a week. If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to cut off the damaged ends and start fresh.

Here are some other tips for curing bleach damaged hair:

1. Use a protein treatment

Protein is essential for healthy hair, and a protein treatment can help to repair the damage caused by bleach. Look for a treatment that contains hydrolyzed keratin, collagen, or silk protein.

2. Apply a hair mask

A hair mask can provide deep conditioning and nourishment to your hair. Look for a mask that contains natural ingredients like avocado, coconut oil, or honey.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can further damage already weakened hair. Try to avoid using hot tools like straighteners, curling irons, and hair dryers. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and set the temperature to a lower heat setting.

4. Use a leave-in conditioner

Leave-in conditioners can provide extra moisture and protection to your hair. Look for a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down.

5. Limit washing your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to further dryness and damage. Try to limit washing your hair to once or twice a week.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to curing bleach damaged hair:

1. Assess the extent of the damage

Take a close look at your hair and determine how much damage has been done. If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to cut off the damaged ends and start fresh.

2. Deep condition your hair

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair once or twice a week. Leave the treatment on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Use a protein treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair once a month to help repair the damage.

4. Apply a hair mask

Apply a hair mask once a week to provide extra nourishment and hydration to your hair.

5. Avoid heat styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, which can further damage your hair.

6. Use a leave-in conditioner

Apply a lightweight leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing and conditioning it.

Tips for Preventing Bleach Damage

Prevention is key when it comes to bleach damage. Here are some tips to help prevent bleach damage:

1. Don't bleach your hair too often

Avoid bleaching your hair too often. Aim for no more than once every 6-8 weeks.

2. Use a lower volume developer

Use a lower volume developer to help reduce the amount of damage caused by bleach.

3. Deep condition your hair regularly

Deep condition your hair once or twice a week to help keep it healthy and hydrated.

4. Use a heat protectant spray

If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to help reduce the amount of damage caused by heat.

5. Limit washing your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to further dryness and damage. Try to limit washing your hair to once or twice a week.

FAQs

Can bleach damage be reversed?

While bleach damage can't be fully reversed, it can be treated and managed with the right hair care routine.

How long does it take to cure bleach damaged hair?

The time it takes to cure bleach damaged hair depends on the extent of the damage. Minor damage can be resolved in a few weeks, while more severe damage may take several months to fully heal.

Can I bleach my hair again after it has been damaged?

It's not recommended to bleach hair that has already been damaged. Wait until your hair has fully recovered before bleaching it again.

Hair Care for Bleach-Damaged Hair

After curing your bleach damaged hair, it's important to maintain a healthy hair care routine to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to help prevent further damage to your hair.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair from the damaging effects of the sun.

4. Trim your hair regularly

Trim your hair every 8-12 weeks to get rid of split ends and prevent further damage.

5. Eat a healthy diet

Eat a diet rich in vitamins and minerals to help promote healthy hair growth and prevent further damage.

By following these tips and incorporating them into your hair care routine, you can help to cure and prevent bleach damaged hair. Remember to be patient and consistent, and your hair will thank you for it!
