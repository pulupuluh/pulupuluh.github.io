How To Sleep With Short Hair So It Looks Good In The Morning

Written by Robby Jun 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of waking up with messy, unmanageable short hair? Do you want to know how to sleep with short hair so it looks good in the morning without spending hours styling it? Look no further than this guide, which will provide you with tips and tricks to keep your short hair looking great while you sleep.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Sleep with Short Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Sleeping with Short Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Short Hair

How to Sleep with Short Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's take a look at some general tips for sleeping with short hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair, as it can cause breakage and damage.
  • Avoid using hair ties or clips, as they can cause creases and kinks in your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to moisturize and protect your hair while you sleep.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know some general tips, let's get into the step-by-step guide:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair before bed. Make sure to use a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Part your hair in a way that feels comfortable for you. You can either part it down the middle, on the side, or create a loose braid or bun.
  4. Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands, but avoid using elastic bands or hair ties.
  5. If you're worried about your hair getting tangled while you sleep, you can use a silk or satin hair scarf to protect it.
  6. Go to bed and get a good night's sleep!
  7. In the morning, remove any bobby pins or hair scarf and gently shake out your hair.
  8. Use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to style your hair as desired.
  9. If your hair feels dry or frizzy, you can apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to tame it.

Tips for Sleeping with Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid sleeping on your back, as it can cause flat hair. Instead, try sleeping on your side or stomach.
  • If you have bangs, consider pinning them to the side to prevent them from falling in your face while you sleep.
  • Use a small amount of dry shampoo in the morning to refresh your hair and add volume.
  • Invest in a good quality haircut to ensure that your short hair is easy to style and looks great even when you wake up.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about sleeping with short hair:

Q: Can I sleep with wet hair?

A: It's best to avoid sleeping with wet hair, as it can cause breakage and damage. If you must sleep with wet hair, use a microfiber towel to gently squeeze out excess water and avoid rubbing your hair with a towel.

Q: Should I use a hair tie to keep my hair in place while I sleep?

A: No, it's best to avoid using hair ties or clips, as they can cause creases and kinks in your hair. Instead, use bobby pins to secure any loose strands.

Q: How often should I wash my short hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with short hair may need to wash it every day, while others can go a few days between washes. Experiment and see what works best for you.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Here are some general hair care tips for short hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and moisturizing conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools, or use them sparingly and always use a heat protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid over-brushing your hair, as it can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

By following these tips and tricks, you can sleep with short hair and wake up with a stylish, manageable look every morning.
