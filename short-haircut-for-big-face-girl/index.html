Short Haircut For Big Face Girl A Guide To Flattering Cuts In 2023

Written by William Sep 02, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you a big face girl looking for a fresh, new haircut? Short haircuts can be a great option for those with larger facial features, as they can help balance out the proportions of the face. In this article, we’ll explore some of the best short haircuts for big face girls, as well as provide tips on how to care for and style your new ‘do.

How to Choose the Right Short Haircut for Your Face Shape

Before you head to the salon, it’s important to consider your face shape when selecting a short haircut. While there are no hard and fast rules, there are certain styles that tend to flatter certain face shapes more than others. Here are some general guidelines:

Round Face

If you have a round face, you’ll want to avoid haircuts that add volume to the sides of your head. Instead, opt for a cut that is shorter on the sides and longer on top, which can help elongate your face. A side-swept bang can also help create the illusion of a longer face.

Square Face

A square face is characterized by a strong jawline and forehead. To balance out these features, you’ll want a haircut that softens the angles of your face. Try a short bob or a layered pixie cut, which can help add movement and texture to your hair.

Oval Face

If you have an oval face, you’re in luck – most short haircuts will flatter your face shape. Consider a sleek, angled bob or a textured pixie cut to show off your features.

Heart-Shaped Face

Heart-shaped faces tend to be wider at the forehead and taper down to a narrower chin. To balance out these proportions, consider a short haircut with volume at the top and a tapered back. A side-parted bob can also help draw attention away from the forehead.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Short Haircut

Once you’ve selected your perfect short haircut, it’s important to know how to style it. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair until it’s damp.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product, such as mousse or gel, to your hair. This will help add volume and texture to your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair in sections. Focus on lifting the roots to add volume to your hair.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, style it as desired. Use a flat iron or curling iron to add texture, or slick it back with some pomade for a sleek, sophisticated look.

Tips for Maintaining Your Short Haircut

Short haircuts require less maintenance than long hair, but there are still a few things you’ll want to keep in mind to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Visit the salon regularly for trims to keep your haircut looking fresh
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair
  • Consider using dry shampoo to extend the time between washes

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I’m not sure which short haircut is right for me?

Your stylist can help you determine which short haircut will flatter your face shape and hair type. Bring in some photos for inspiration, and don’t be afraid to ask for their opinion.

Will a short haircut make my face look even bigger?

Not necessarily – in fact, a well-chosen short haircut can help balance out the proportions of your face. Be sure to choose a cut that flatters your face shape and hair type.

How often should I wash my short hair?

This depends on your hair type and personal preferences. Some people may need to wash their hair every day, while others can go a few days between washes. Experiment to find the washing schedule that works best for you.

Conclusion

A short haircut can be a great way to flatter a big face and show off your features. With the right cut and styling techniques, you can rock a short ‘do with confidence. Remember to visit the salon regularly for trims, use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and avoid overusing heat styling tools to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.
