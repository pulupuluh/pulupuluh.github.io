How To Thin Thick Hair Without Thinning Scissors

Written by Daniel Feb 17, 2023 · 10 min read

Are you tired of your thick and heavy hair weighing you down? Do you want to achieve a lighter and more manageable look without resorting to thinning scissors? You’ve come to the right place! In this article, we’ll share with you some tips and tricks on how to thin thick hair without thinning scissors.

Description: What Are Thinning Scissors?

Thinning scissors, also known as texturizing shears, are specialized scissors designed to remove bulk from hair. They have teeth-like blades that cut some hair strands while leaving others intact, creating a layered and feathered effect. While thinning scissors can be useful in creating volume and texture, they can also cause damage and unevenness if not used properly.

How to Thin Thick Hair Without Thinning Scissors

Now, let’s get to the fun part. Here are some ways to thin thick hair without thinning scissors:

1. Use a Razor Comb

A razor comb is a comb with a razor blade attached to it. It can be used to thin out thick hair by cutting some strands while leaving others untouched. To use a razor comb, comb your hair first to remove any knots and tangles. Then, take a small section of hair and comb it downwards. Hold the razor comb at a 45-degree angle and run it through the section of hair, starting from the middle and working your way to the ends. Repeat on other sections until you achieve your desired level of thinness.

2. Try Point Cutting

Point cutting is a hair cutting technique where the scissors are held vertically and cuts are made into the ends of the hair, creating texture and removing bulk. To point cut, take a small section of hair and hold it upwards. Use the tips of the scissors to make small, diagonal cuts into the ends of the hair. Repeat on other sections until you achieve your desired level of thinness.

3. Opt for Layering

Layering is a hair cutting technique that involves cutting different lengths of hair to create movement and volume. It can also help thin out thick hair by removing some of the weight. To layer your hair, section it into different layers and cut each layer at a different length. This will create a natural-looking, textured effect that will make your hair appear thinner.

Step by Step Guide

To help you visualize the process, here’s a step by step guide on how to thin thick hair without thinning scissors:

Step 1: Clean and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and allow it to dry completely. Thinning thick hair when it’s wet can make it difficult to see the results, so make sure your hair is completely dry.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don’t miss any spots.

Step 3: Choose Your Method

Choose the method that you prefer from the ones we’ve mentioned above: razor comb, point cutting, or layering.

Step 4: Thin Out Your Hair

Start working on one section at a time. Use the method of your choice to thin out your hair, making sure to work slowly and carefully. It’s better to start with less hair and work your way up, rather than cutting too much at once.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you’ve finished thinning out one section, check your progress by comparing it to the other sections. If you’re satisfied with the level of thinness, move on to the next section.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you’ve thinned out your hair to your liking, style it as usual. You’ll notice that your hair feels lighter and more manageable!

Tips for Thinning Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for thinning thick hair:

  • Use sharp scissors or a razor comb to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Start with small sections and work your way up to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Be patient and take your time. Thinning out thick hair can be a time-consuming process, but the results are worth it.
  • Consult a professional stylist if you’re unsure about how to thin out your hair.

FAQs

Can I thin out my hair without cutting it?

Yes, you can thin out your hair without cutting it by using hair products that are designed to add volume and texture. Look for volumizing shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that can give your hair a lighter feel.

What are the benefits of thinning out thick hair?

Thinning out thick hair can make it more manageable, easier to style, and less heavy. It can also create a more natural-looking and textured effect, which can be flattering for different face shapes.

Is it safe to thin out my hair at home?

Thinning out your hair at home can be safe as long as you use the right tools and techniques. However, if you’re unsure about how to do it properly, it’s recommended to consult a professional stylist to avoid any damage or unevenness.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for taking care of your thick hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and dryness. If you must use heat styling tools, apply a heat protectant beforehand.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as it can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and health.

Conclusion

Thinning out thick hair can be a challenge, but it’s not impossible. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a lighter and more manageable look without resorting to thinning scissors. Remember to be patient, take your time, and consult a professional stylist if needed. Follow our tips for hair care to keep your thick hair healthy and shiny.
