How To Prevent Hair From Falling Out After Bleaching

Written by Frank Aug 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of losing your hair after bleaching? Bleaching can be a great way to achieve a new look, but it can also be damaging to your hair. In this article, we will provide you with some tips and tricks on how to stop hair from falling out after bleaching.

Description

Bleaching your hair can cause a lot of damage, and one of the most common side effects is hair loss. This can be a frustrating and discouraging experience, but there are several things you can do to prevent it. By taking good care of your hair and using the right products, you can minimize the damage and keep your hair healthy and strong.

How to Stop Hair from Falling Out After Bleaching

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

One of the best things you can do for your hair after bleaching is to use a deep conditioning treatment. This will help to restore moisture and nutrients to your hair, which will help to prevent breakage and hair loss. Look for a treatment that contains ingredients like keratin, argan oil, or shea butter, which are all great for repairing damaged hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be very damaging to bleached hair, so it's important to avoid it as much as possible. If you must use heat, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low. Try to air dry your hair as much as possible and use a wide-toothed comb to detangle it.

3. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments are another great way to strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Look for a treatment that contains hydrolyzed protein, which can help to rebuild the structure of your hair. Be careful not to overdo it, however, as too much protein can actually make your hair more brittle.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Bleached hair is more prone to split ends and breakage, so it's important to trim it regularly. This will help to get rid of any damaged ends and prevent them from splitting further up the hair shaft. Try to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash Your Hair with a Gentle Shampoo

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo that is designed for damaged or bleached hair. This will help to remove any buildup and prepare your hair for conditioning.

Step 2: Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, making sure to focus on the ends. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time (usually 10-15 minutes) and then rinse it out thoroughly.

Step 3: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair, following the instructions on the product. Be careful not to leave it on for too long, as this can make your hair brittle.

Step 4: Air Dry Your Hair

Avoid heat styling and let your hair air dry as much as possible. If you must use heat, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Step 5: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Get a trim every 6-8 weeks to get rid of any split ends and prevent further breakage.

Tips

- Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or relaxers.

- Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles.

- Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, such as tight ponytails or braids.

FAQ

Q: Can I bleach my hair if it's already damaged?

A: It's not recommended to bleach already damaged hair, as this can cause further breakage and hair loss. It's best to wait until your hair is healthy before bleaching it.

Q: How long should I wait before bleaching my hair again?

A: It's best to wait at least 4-6 weeks between bleaching sessions to give your hair time to recover.

Hair Care

Remember to take good care of your hair to prevent damage and hair loss. This includes using gentle products, avoiding heat styling, and getting regular trims. With the right care, you can keep your hair healthy and strong, even after bleaching.
