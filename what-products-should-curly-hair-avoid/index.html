What Products Should Curly Hair Avoid

Written by William Aug 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Curly hair can be challenging to manage, but with the right products and techniques, it can look stunning. However, not all hair products are created equal, and some can damage your curly locks. In this article, we'll explore what products curly hair should avoid and why.

Table of Contents

  1. Silicones
  2. Sulfates
  3. Alcohol
  4. Heat
  5. Brushes
  6. Tips for Curly Hair Care
  7. FAQs

Silicones

Silicones are commonly found in hair products due to their ability to provide shine and smoothness. However, for curly hair, they can be damaging. Silicones coat the hair shaft, which prevents moisture from penetrating and can cause build-up. This build-up can weigh down your curls and make them look limp and lifeless. Look for hair products without silicones or with water-soluble silicones that won't build up on your hair.

Sulfates

Sulfates are cleaning agents found in many shampoos, and while they are effective at removing dirt and oil, they can also strip your hair of its natural oils. This can be especially damaging for curly hair, which already tends to be dry. Look for sulfate-free shampoos that are gentle on your curls.

Alcohol

Alcohol is often found in styling products and can be drying to curly hair. It can also cause frizz and damage. Look for products that are alcohol-free or contain fatty alcohols like cetyl and stearyl alcohol, which are moisturizing and won't dry out your curls.

Heat

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can be damaging to curly hair, causing breakage, split ends, and dryness. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant product and keep the heat setting low. Alternatively, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer.

Brushes

Brushes can be damaging to curly hair, especially if you use them when your hair is dry. Brushes can cause breakage, frizz, and damage to your curls. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet and use a detangling spray or conditioner to make it easier.

Tips for Curly Hair Care

Here are some tips for curly hair care:

  • Wash your hair less frequently to prevent dryness.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry to prevent damage.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair without causing frizz.
  • Apply styling products to wet hair to help define your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or use them sparingly and with a heat protectant product.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on curly hair?

A: It's best to use a sulfate-free shampoo that's gentle on curly hair. Regular shampoos with sulfates can be drying and damaging to your curls.

Q: Can I use a hairbrush on my curly hair?

A: It's best to avoid using a hairbrush on curly hair, especially when it's dry. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers instead.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It's best to wash your curly hair less frequently to prevent dryness. Aim for once or twice a week, depending on your hair type and how active you are.

By avoiding products that can be damaging to curly hair and following proper hair care techniques, you can keep your curls healthy and beautiful. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.
