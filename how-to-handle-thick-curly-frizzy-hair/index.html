How To Handle Thick Curly Frizzy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 30, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have thick curly frizzy hair, you know how challenging it can be to manage. It can be frustrating to deal with tangles, knots, and frizz on a daily basis. But with the right techniques and products, you can keep your hair healthy, shiny, and under control. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to handle thick curly frizzy hair, step by step.

Description

Thick curly frizzy hair is a unique hair type that requires special care and attention. It’s characterized by tight curls, thick strands, and a tendency to frizz. This hair type can be challenging to manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.

How to Handle Thick Curly Frizzy Hair

Step 1: Start with a Good Shampoo and Conditioner

The foundation of any good hair care routine is a quality shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are formulated specifically for curly hair, as they will help to nourish and define your curls. Avoid products that contain sulfates or alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and make your frizz worse.

Step 2: Use a Deep Conditioner Weekly

Thick curly frizzy hair can be prone to dryness, so it’s important to keep it moisturized. Once a week, use a deep conditioner to hydrate your hair and nourish your curls. Apply the conditioner to your hair and leave it on for at least 10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair Gently

Tangled hair can lead to breakage and damage, so it’s important to detangle your hair gently. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to work through any knots or tangles, starting at the ends and working your way up. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage.

Step 4: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep your curls defined and hydrated throughout the day. Apply a small amount to your hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths. Be careful not to use too much, as this can weigh down your curls and make them look limp.

Step 5: Use a Diffuser to Dry Your Hair

A diffuser attachment for your hair dryer can help to dry your hair without causing frizz. Use the diffuser on a low heat setting and gently scrunch your hair as you dry it. This will help to define your curls and prevent frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for handling thick curly frizzy hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make your frizz worse.
  • Experiment with different hair care products to find what works best for your hair.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my thick curly frizzy hair?

A: It’s best to wash your hair every 2-3 days, as this will help to keep it clean without stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I use a hair mask on my thick curly frizzy hair?

A: Yes, a hair mask can be a great way to deep-condition your hair and keep it hydrated. Look for a mask that is specifically formulated for curly hair.

Q: How can I reduce frizz in my thick curly hair?

A: To reduce frizz, avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, use a diffuser to dry your hair, and apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated.

Hair Care

Taking care of your thick curly frizzy hair requires a bit of effort, but the results are worth it. With the right techniques and products, you can keep your hair healthy, shiny, and under control. Be patient, experiment with different products, and don’t be afraid to ask for help from a professional stylist if you need it.
