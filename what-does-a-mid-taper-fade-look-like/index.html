What Does A Mid Taper Fade Look Like

Written by Eveline Jun 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, you might want to try a mid taper fade. This haircut is a classic style that has been popular for many years. It's a versatile cut that can be worn by men of all ages and hair types. In this blog post, we'll be discussing what a mid taper fade looks like, how to get it, and how to maintain it.

Description

A mid taper fade is a type of fade that starts halfway up the sides and back of the head. This means that the hair is longer at the top and gradually gets shorter towards the neckline. The fade is usually done with clippers, and the length of the hair can vary depending on personal preference.

How to Get a Mid Taper Fade

If you're interested in getting a mid taper fade, you should go to a professional barber or hairstylist. They will be able to create the fade using clippers and scissors. You should also bring a picture of the style you want to show the barber. This will give them a clear idea of what you're looking for.

The first step in getting a mid taper fade is to decide how long you want your hair to be at the top. The barber will then use clippers to create a gradual fade from the top to the sides and back of the head. They will also use scissors to blend the hair for a natural look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to get a mid taper fade:

  1. Decide on the length of hair you want at the top.
  2. Choose a barber or hairstylist that you trust.
  3. Show them a picture of the style you want.
  4. The barber will start by cutting the top of your hair to the desired length.
  5. They will then use clippers to create a gradual fade from the top to the sides and back of the head.
  6. The barber will use scissors to blend the hair for a natural look.
  7. They will repeat the process until the fade is smooth and even.
  8. The barber will then style the hair to your liking.

Tips for Maintaining a Mid Taper Fade

Here are some tips for maintaining a mid taper fade:

  • Visit a barber or hairstylist every 2-3 weeks to maintain the fade.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Use a comb or brush to style your hair, and avoid using your fingers.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about mid taper fades:

What's the difference between a mid taper fade and a high taper fade?

A mid taper fade starts halfway up the sides and back of the head, while a high taper fade starts closer to the crown of the head. The length of the hair gradually gets shorter towards the neckline for both styles.

Can I get a mid taper fade if I have curly hair?

Yes, a mid taper fade works well with curly hair. Your barber or hairstylist will be able to create the fade using clippers and scissors, and will be able to adjust the length of the hair to suit your curls.

Hair Care

Maintaining a mid taper fade requires proper hair care. You should use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it. Use a comb or brush to style your hair, and avoid using your fingers. Visit a barber or hairstylist every 2-3 weeks to maintain the fade.

In conclusion, a mid taper fade is a classic and versatile haircut that can be worn by men of all ages and hair types. It's a trendy and stylish haircut that requires proper maintenance and care. If you're interested in getting a mid taper fade, make sure to go to a professional barber or hairstylist, and bring a picture of the style you want. With proper care and maintenance, you can enjoy the benefits of this stylish haircut for years to come.
