Is Hair Loss A Symptom Of Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome

Written by Aprilia Aug 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects millions of women worldwide. It is a condition where a woman's body produces too much androgen hormone, which results in several symptoms that can affect their physical and emotional well-being. One of the most common symptoms of PCOS is hair loss. In this article, we will explore the relationship between PCOS and hair loss and discuss the ways to manage this condition.

What is Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome?

Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects the ovaries, the reproductive organs responsible for producing eggs and hormones. Women with PCOS produce higher-than-normal levels of androgen hormone, which can cause several symptoms, including:

  • Irregular periods
  • Heavy bleeding during periods
  • Acne
  • Weight gain
  • Hair loss
  • Hirsutism (excessive hair growth on the face, chest, and back)

Is Hair Loss a Symptom of PCOS?

Yes, hair loss is a common symptom of PCOS. Women with PCOS may experience hair loss due to the increased production of androgen hormone. Androgen hormone can cause hair loss by shrinking the hair follicles, resulting in thinning hair or baldness in some cases. Hair loss due to PCOS is usually gradual and may affect the scalp, face, and body.

How to Manage Hair Loss Due to PCOS?

If you are experiencing hair loss due to PCOS, there are several ways to manage this condition:

1. Medications

Your doctor may prescribe medications to help manage the symptoms of PCOS, including hair loss. Medications such as spironolactone, finasteride, and minoxidil can help reduce hair loss and promote hair growth.

2. Lifestyle Changes

Adopting a healthy lifestyle can also help manage the symptoms of PCOS, including hair loss. Eating a balanced diet, exercising regularly, and managing stress can help reduce the production of androgen hormone and promote hair growth.

3. Hair Care

Taking care of your hair can also help manage hair loss due to PCOS. Use gentle shampoos and conditioners, avoid heating tools and chemical treatments, and avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can hair loss due to PCOS be reversed?

A: Yes, hair loss due to PCOS can be reversed with the right treatment. Medications, lifestyle changes, and hair care can help promote hair growth and reduce hair loss.

Q: Can hair loss due to PCOS cause permanent baldness?

A: In some cases, hair loss due to PCOS can cause permanent baldness. It is important to seek treatment as soon as possible to prevent further hair loss.

Q: Can PCOS cause hair growth in unwanted places?

A: Yes, PCOS can cause hirsutism, which is the excessive growth of hair on the face, chest, and back.

Conclusion

PCOS is a hormonal disorder that can cause several symptoms, including hair loss. If you are experiencing hair loss due to PCOS, it is important to seek treatment as soon as possible. Medications, lifestyle changes, and hair care can help manage this condition and promote hair growth. Talk to your doctor to learn more about your treatment options.
