How To Cut Layers Into Hair Extensions A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Mar 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having flat and lifeless hair extensions? Adding layers to your hair extensions can give your locks more volume, texture, and movement. The good news is that you don't have to go to a salon to achieve this look. With the right tools and techniques, you can learn how to cut layers into hair extensions at home. In this blog post, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve the perfect layered look for your hair extensions.

How to Cut Layers into Hair Extensions

Before we get started, let's review the tools you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clips to section off your hair
  • Comb
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Extensions

The first step to cutting layers into your hair extensions is to wash and dry them. This will make it easier to see the natural layers of the hair. Once they are clean and dry, comb through the extensions to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section off your hair into layers. Pin the top layer of your hair up and out of the way. This will make it easier to see and cut the layers at the bottom. You can also use clips to section off the hair extensions.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. If you're not sure, start with longer layers and work your way up. You can always cut more hair off, but you can't add it back on.

Step 4: Cut the First Layer

Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair extensions and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers at the length you want your first layer to be. Use your scissors to cut the hair straight across.

It's important to only cut a little bit of hair at a time. This will help you avoid cutting too much and creating uneven layers.

Step 5: Continue Cutting Layers

Once you've cut the first layer, let down the next section of hair extensions and repeat the process until you've cut all the layers you want.

Remember to keep the sections even and to cut small amounts of hair at a time. This will ensure that your layers are even and well-blended.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

After you've cut all the layers, use your comb to blend them together. Comb through your hair extensions to make sure the layers are even and well-blended.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Hair Extensions

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting layers into your hair extensions:

  • Start with longer layers and work your way up.
  • Always cut small amounts of hair at a time.
  • Make sure the sections are even.
  • Blend the layers together with a comb.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut layers into any type of hair extensions?

A: Yes, you can cut layers into any type of hair extensions, whether they are synthetic or human hair.

Q: Can I cut layers into curly hair extensions?

A: Yes, you can cut layers into curly hair extensions. However, keep in mind that the curls will be shorter and tighter once you cut the hair, so start with longer layers and work your way up.

Q: Do I need to use special scissors to cut hair extensions?

A: It's recommended to use sharp scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Regular scissors may damage the hair extensions and create split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers into Hair Extensions

After you've cut layers into your hair extensions, it's important to take care of them properly to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Don't wash your hair extensions for 24 hours after cutting them.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair soft and shiny.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on the hair extensions.
  • Store your hair extensions in a cool, dry place when you're not wearing them.

Now that you know how to cut layers into hair extensions, you can achieve the perfect layered look at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow these tips for the best results.
