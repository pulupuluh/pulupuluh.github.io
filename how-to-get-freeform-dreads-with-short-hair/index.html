How To Get Freeform Dreads With Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

With the right technique and a bit of patience, you can get the same unique and stylish look as those with longer hair. In this guide, we will show you how to get freeform dreads with short hair, including tips and hair care advice.

How to Get Freeform Dreads with Short Hair

Freeform dreads are a popular hairstyle choice for those who want a unique and edgy look. Unlike traditional dreadlocks, freeform dreads are created without any styling or maintenance, resulting in a natural and organic look. Here's how you can achieve the same look with short hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup from your scalp and hair.

Step 2: Towel dry your hair until it is slightly damp. Avoid using a blow dryer, as this can cause damage to your hair and scalp.

Step 3: Section your hair into small, equal-sized parts using a rat-tail comb. Make sure that each section is about the same size as a dreadlock you want to achieve.

Step 4: Take one section of hair and start twisting it in a clockwise direction. Keep twisting until the hair starts to form a tight coil.

Step 5: Once the coil is formed, use a small hair tie or rubber band to secure the end of the dread. Repeat this process for each section of hair until you have achieved the desired number of dreads.

Step 6: Leave your hair alone for the next few weeks. Do not wash or style your hair during this time. As your hair grows, the dreads will start to form and tighten on their own.

Tips for Maintaining Freeform Dreads

Once your dreads have formed, there are a few things you can do to maintain them:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair. This will prevent any buildup from forming on your scalp and hair.
  • Avoid using any hair products, as this can cause the dreads to unravel or become loose.
  • Use a fine-tooth comb to gently separate any hair that has become matted or tangled.
  • Trim any loose or frayed ends to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to get freeform dreads with short hair?

A: It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for your dreads to fully form and mature.

Q: Can I still wash my hair with freeform dreads?

A: Yes, you can wash your hair, but it's important to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using any hair products.

Q: Can I still style my hair with freeform dreads?

A: It's best to avoid styling your hair with freeform dreads, as this can cause the dreads to unravel or become loose.

Hair Care for Freeform Dreads

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and beautiful freeform dreads. Here are a few tips to keep your hair and scalp in top condition:

  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and stimulate hair growth.
  • Use a natural oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, to moisturize your scalp and hair.
  • Avoid using any harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as this can damage your scalp and hair follicles.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to ensure that your hair receives all the necessary nutrients it needs to grow strong and healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve beautiful and natural freeform dreads with short hair. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair, and you'll be rocking your new look in no time!
