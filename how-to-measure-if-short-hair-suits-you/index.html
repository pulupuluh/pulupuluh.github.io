How To Measure If Short Hair Suits You

Written by Christine Jul 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Short hair can be a bold and daring choice for anyone, but it is important to know if it is the right choice for you. Not everyone can pull off short hair, but with the right measurements, anyone can find a style that suits them perfectly. In this article, we will discuss how to measure if short hair suits you and provide tips for hair care and styling.

Table of Contents

  • How to Take Measurements
  • Consider Your Face Shape
  • Consider Your Hair Texture
  • Styling Tips for Short Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Take Measurements

The first step in determining if short hair suits you is to take accurate measurements of your face shape and hair texture.

Start by measuring the distance between your hairline and your eyebrows. This will give you an idea of how much forehead you have to work with. If you have a shorter distance, you may want to avoid a very short pixie cut that exposes too much of your forehead.

Next, measure the distance from your chin to your ear. This will give you an idea of how wide your jawline is. If you have a wider jawline, you may want to avoid a blunt bob that ends at the jawline and opt for a longer, layered style that softens the jawline.

Finally, measure the distance from the top of your head to the nape of your neck. This will give you an idea of how much volume you can work with. If you have a shorter distance, you may want to avoid very voluminous styles that may overwhelm your face.

Consider Your Face Shape

While measurements are important, your face shape will also play a role in determining if short hair suits you. Here are some general guidelines:

  • If you have a round face, a short bob or pixie cut with volume on top can help elongate your face.
  • If you have a square face, a longer bob or layered cut can soften your jawline.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, a pixie cut with volume on top can help balance out a wider forehead.
  • If you have an oval face, you can pull off almost any short hairstyle.

Consider Your Hair Texture

Another factor to consider when determining if short hair suits you is your hair texture. Here are some general guidelines:

  • If you have straight hair, a blunt bob or pixie cut can work well.
  • If you have curly hair, a layered cut can help define your curls.
  • If you have fine hair, a short bob or pixie cut with volume on top can add the illusion of thickness.
  • If you have thick hair, a layered cut can help thin out your hair and make it more manageable.

Styling Tips for Short Hair

Now that you have determined if short hair suits you, it's time to consider some styling tips:

  • Invest in a good texturizing spray to add volume and definition to your short hair.
  • Consider using a flat iron or curling wand to add some waves or curls to your short hair.
  • Play around with different parts to find what suits you best.
  • Consider getting regular trims to maintain your short style.

FAQ

How short is too short?

It depends on your face shape and hair texture, but generally speaking, a very short pixie cut may not be the best choice for everyone. Consider your forehead size and how much volume you can work with.

What if I don't like my short hair?

Remember that hair grows back! If you don't like your short hair, you can always grow it out or try a different style.

Can I still wear hair accessories with short hair?

Absolutely! Consider trying hair clips, headbands, and even hats to accessorize your short hair.

Hair Care Tips

Short hair requires some maintenance to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Shampoo and condition regularly to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your short hair to prevent damage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your short style.

With these tips and guidelines, you can determine if short hair suits you and find a style that you love. Remember to take measurements, consider your face shape and hair texture, and experiment with different styles and accessories to find what works best for you.
