How To Give Short Thin Hair Volume Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Apr 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

In today's world, having voluminous hair is a must-have. But what if you have short, thin hair? Don't worry, there are many ways to give your hair volume and make it look fuller. In this blog post, we will discuss some tips and tricks to help you get the volume you want.

How to Give Short Thin Hair Volume

If you have short, thin hair, there are many ways to give it volume. Here are some tips and tricks:

1. Use the Right Products

The right products can make a huge difference in giving your hair volume. Look for products that are specifically designed for fine hair. These products will add volume without weighing your hair down. Some products to consider include volumizing shampoos, conditioners, mousses, and sprays.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help give it volume. Use a round brush and blow dry your hair in sections, lifting each section as you dry it. This will create volume and lift at the roots.

3. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair is an easy way to add volume. Take small sections of hair and backcomb them at the roots. This will create volume and lift at the crown of your head.

4. Use Hot Rollers

Hot rollers are a great way to add volume to short, thin hair. Roll your hair in small sections and leave the rollers in for at least 20 minutes. This will create volume and lift at the roots.

5. Get a Layered Haircut

A layered haircut can help give your hair volume. Layers will create movement and texture, making your hair appear fuller. Talk to your hairstylist about getting a layered haircut that will work for your hair type.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know some tips and tricks to give your short, thin hair volume, here is a step by step guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products will help add volume without weighing your hair down.

Step 2: Apply Volumizing Mousse

Apply a volumizing mousse to your hair, focusing on the roots. This will help add volume and lift to your hair.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush. Lift each section as you dry it to create volume and lift at the roots.

Step 4: Tease Your Hair

Take small sections of hair and backcomb them at the roots. This will create volume and lift at the crown of your head.

Step 5: Use Hot Rollers

Roll your hair in small sections using hot rollers. Leave the rollers in for at least 20 minutes to create volume and lift at the roots.

Tips for Maintaining Volume

Here are some tips for maintaining volume in your short, thin hair:

1. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent your hair from getting flat while you sleep. Silk is gentle on your hair and won't cause friction, which can flatten your hair.

2. Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo is a great way to refresh your hair and add volume between washes. Apply it to your roots and massage it in to create volume and lift.

3. Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, which can cause it to become flat and lifeless. Try to wash your hair every other day, or even every three days, to help maintain volume.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a volumizing spray instead of a mousse?

A: Yes, you can use a volumizing spray instead of a mousse. Look for a spray that is specifically designed for fine hair.

Q: How often should I use hot rollers?

A: You can use hot rollers as often as you like. However, be careful not to damage your hair by using them too frequently.

Q: Will a layered haircut work for all hair types?

A: No, a layered haircut may not work for all hair types. Talk to your hairstylist about whether a layered haircut will work for your hair type.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to help keep your short, thin hair healthy and looking its best:

1. Use a Heat Protectant

Before using hot tools on your hair, be sure to use a heat protectant. This will help prevent damage and breakage.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles, such as tight ponytails and braids, as these can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

3. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

With these tips and tricks, you can give your short, thin hair volume and make it look fuller and more voluminous. Try them out and see what works best for your hair type.
