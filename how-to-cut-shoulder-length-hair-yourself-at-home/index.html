How To Cut Shoulder Length Hair Yourself At Home A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 04, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of spending ridiculous amounts of money at the salon for a simple haircut? Do you want to try your hand at cutting your own hair at home? Cutting shoulder length hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done successfully. In this article, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut shoulder length hair yourself at home.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Shoulder Length Hair Yourself at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Shoulder Length Hair Yourself
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Shoulder Length Hair

Description

Shoulder length hair is a versatile length that can be styled in many ways. Whether you want to trim your hair, add layers, or just give yourself a fresh new look, cutting your own hair at home is a cost-effective and convenient option. However, it’s important to have the right tools and techniques to ensure that your haircut turns out the way you want it to.

How to Cut Shoulder Length Hair Yourself at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, it’s important to have the right tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. Once you have these, you’re ready to get started.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it’s clean and dry. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you get an accurate length. Once your hair is clean and dry, comb it out to remove any tangles.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using the comb, section your hair into four parts – two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with the back sections, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the scissors to cut the hair to your desired length. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve cut all of the back sections.

Once you’ve finished cutting the back sections, move on to the front sections. Take a small section of hair and pull it down in front of your face. Use the scissors to cut the hair to your desired length. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve cut all of the front sections.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

If you want to add layers to your hair, use the scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle to create a layered effect.

Step 5: Check Your Hair

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, use the mirror to check that it’s even. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair is the length and style you want it to be.

Tips for Cutting Shoulder Length Hair Yourself

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean and accurate cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting off too much at once.
  • Don’t cut your hair too short – you can always cut more later.
  • Always cut your hair when it’s dry to avoid any surprises when it dries.
  • Take your time and be patient – cutting your own hair can be tricky, but with practice, you’ll get better.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair if it’s curly?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. However, it’s important to be extra careful and take small sections of hair to ensure an accurate cut.

How often should I cut my shoulder length hair?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my hair wet or dry?

You should always cut your hair when it’s dry to avoid any surprises when it dries.

Hair Care After Cutting Shoulder Length Hair

After you’ve cut your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and style. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using heat-styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. Instead, let your hair air-dry or use a diffuser to dry it.

Cutting your own shoulder length hair at home can be a great way to save money and give yourself a fresh new look. By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut right in the comfort of your own home.
