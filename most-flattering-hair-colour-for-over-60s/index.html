Most Flattering Hair Colour For Over 60S

Written by Luffy Aug 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you over 60 and looking for a hair colour that will enhance your natural beauty? Look no further! In this article, we will discuss the most flattering hair colours for over 60s. From warm blondes to rich brunettes, we have got you covered. Let's dive in!

Description

As we age, our skin tone and hair texture change, making it important to choose a hair colour that complements our features. The right hair colour can brighten your complexion, minimize fine lines, and make you feel confident.

How To Choose the Right Hair Colour

Choosing the right hair colour can be overwhelming, but it doesn't have to be. Here are some tips to help you find the perfect shade:

Consider Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone plays a crucial role in finding the right hair colour. If you have cool undertones, opt for shades with blue or violet undertones, such as ash blonde or cool brown. If you have warm undertones, choose shades with red or gold undertones, such as honey blonde or chestnut brown.

Think About Your Natural Colour

Your natural colour should also be taken into consideration. If you have naturally light hair, consider going a shade or two darker. If you have naturally dark hair, try going lighter with highlights or balayage.

Look at Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle should also be considered. If you are low maintenance, choose a hair colour that requires minimal upkeep, such as a natural-looking balayage or highlights. If you enjoy going to the salon, try something bold and trendy, such as a bright red or platinum blonde.

Step by Step Guide to Choosing the Right Hair Colour

Follow these steps to find the perfect hair colour:

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

Hold a piece of white paper next to your face. If your skin looks pink or rosy, you have cool undertones. If your skin looks yellow or golden, you have warm undertones.

Step 2: Consider Your Natural Hair Colour

Think about your natural hair colour and how it will affect the final result. If you have grey hair, consider going blonde or adding highlights.

Step 3: Look at Your Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle and how much maintenance you are willing to do. If you are low maintenance, opt for a natural-looking balayage or highlights. If you enjoy going to the salon, try something bold and trendy.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Colour

Once you have found the perfect hair colour, it is important to maintain it. Here are some tips:

Use Colour-Safe Shampoo and Conditioner

Colour-safe shampoo and conditioner can help prolong the life of your hair colour. Avoid products with harsh sulfates and opt for gentle formulas.

Avoid Hot Tools

Hot tools can cause damage to your hair and fade your colour. Try air-drying your hair or using a low-heat setting on your styling tools.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays can cause your hair colour to fade. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF when you are in the sun.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most flattering hair colour for over 60s?

The most flattering hair colour for over 60s depends on your skin tone, natural hair colour, and lifestyle. Warm blondes, rich brunettes, and natural-looking balayage are all great options.

Can I dye my hair at home?

You can dye your hair at home, but it is recommended that you seek the advice of a professional stylist. They can help you choose the right shade and provide tips for application and maintenance.

How often should I touch up my hair colour?

It depends on the type of hair colour and how fast your hair grows. Generally, touch-ups are needed every 4-6 weeks for permanent hair colour and every 6-8 weeks for semi-permanent hair colour.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining your hair colour. Here are some tips:

Use a Heat Protectant

Before using hot tools, apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will help prevent damage and keep your colour looking vibrant.

Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture to your hair and keep it healthy. Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair looking and feeling its best.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can dull your hair colour. Visit your stylist every 6-8 weeks for a trim.

Now that you know the most flattering hair colours for over 60s and how to maintain them, it's time to book an appointment with your stylist. Remember to choose a colour that makes you feel confident and beautiful!
