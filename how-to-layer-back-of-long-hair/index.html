How To Layer Back Of Long Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Long hair is beautiful, but sometimes it can be challenging to manage. One of the best ways to add dimension and movement to long hair is by layering it. In this article, we will discuss how to layer the back of long hair, step-by-step. Whether you have straight, wavy, or curly hair, this guide will help you achieve the perfect layered look.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Prepare Your Hair
  • Step 2: Determine the Length of Your Layers
  • Step 3: Section Your Hair
  • Step 4: Start Cutting
  • Step 5: Blend the Layers
  • Step 6: Style Your Hair
  • Tips for Layering Back of Long Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

The first step is to prepare your hair by washing and conditioning it with products that suit your hair type. Dry your hair thoroughly and detangle it using a wide-toothed comb. If you have curly hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help define your curls.

Step 2: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Before you start cutting your hair, determine the length of your layers. Use a comb to create a part in the middle of your head, and then divide your hair into two sections. Decide on the length of your layers by using the bottom of your ear as a guide. If you want shorter layers, use the top of your ear as a guide.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections by clipping the top sections away. Use hair clips to secure each section of hair, starting from the bottom. Work on one section at a time to ensure that you cut each layer evenly.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair by trimming the bottom layer to the desired length. Use a sharp pair of scissors and cut the hair at an angle. Cut the hair in a straight line if you prefer a blunt look. Work your way up to the next layer, using the same technique.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all the layers, blend them together to create a seamless look. Use your scissors to trim any uneven ends and to create a natural-looking finish.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Now that you have layered your hair, it's time to style it. You can keep it straight, curl it, or create waves. Experiment with different styles to find the one that suits you best.

Tips for Layering Back of Long Hair

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure that you cut each layer evenly.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut a little at a time than to cut too much and regret it.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Layering hair can be time-consuming, so don't rush the process.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're not confident in your cutting skills, ask a friend or a hairstylist to help you.

FAQs

1. Can I layer my hair if it's thin?

Yes, layering can add volume and dimension to thin hair. However, be careful not to cut too much hair as it can make your hair appear even thinner.

2. How often should I layer my hair?

It depends on your hair growth rate and how quickly you want to change your look. Typically, layering hair every six to eight weeks is recommended.

3. Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

Yes, layering can enhance the natural curls of your hair. However, be careful not to cut off too much hair as it can alter the curl pattern.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair to avoid breakage.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent split ends.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain the shape of your layers.

Layering the back of long hair can add movement, volume, and dimension to your hair. Follow these steps and tips to achieve the perfect layered look. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at layering your hair in no time.
