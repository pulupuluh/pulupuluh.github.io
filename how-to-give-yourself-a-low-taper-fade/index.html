How to Give Yourself a Low Taper Fade

A low taper fade is a haircut that gradually fades from short to long, starting above the ears and ending at the nape of the neck. It's a versatile style that can be worn in a variety of ways, from a classic look to a more modern, edgy style. Here's how you can achieve a low taper fade:

Tools You'll Need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Brush
  • Mirror
  • Haircutting cape or towel

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to give yourself a low taper fade:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before starting, wash your hair thoroughly to remove any dirt or product buildup. This will make it easier to cut and style your hair.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want for your hair. Use the clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of your head to the desired length. You can start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one for a gradual fade.

Step 3: Create the Fade

Use the clippers to create a fade by gradually decreasing the length of the hair as you move up the sides and back of your head. Use the comb to blend the shorter and longer sections of hair together for a smooth transition.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Use scissors to cut the hair on top of your head to the desired length. You can leave it longer for a more classic look, or shorter for a more modern style.

Step 5: Blend the Top and Sides

Use the scissors to blend the hair on top of your head with the shorter hair on the sides and back. This will create a seamless transition between the two sections of hair.

Step 6: Clean Up the Edges

Use the clippers to clean up the edges around your ears and neckline. You can use a shorter guard for a more defined look, or a longer one for a softer finish.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Use a brush or comb to style your hair as desired. You can use products like gel, pomade, or wax to add texture and hold.

Tips for a Perfect Low Taper Fade

Here are some tips to help you achieve a perfect low taper fade:

  • Practice on a mannequin head or a friend before attempting to cut your own hair.
  • Invest in quality hair clippers and scissors for a more precise cut.
  • Take your time and make sure you're cutting the hair evenly on both sides.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head as you're cutting.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're having trouble with a particular section of hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I give myself a low taper fade with long hair?

A: Yes, but it may be more difficult to create a clean fade. It's best to start with shorter hair and work your way up to longer hair as you gain more experience.

Q: How often should I trim my hair to maintain a low taper fade?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade begins to grow out. On average, you should trim your hair every 2-4 weeks to maintain the look.

Q: Can I use a razor to create a low taper fade?

A: It's not recommended to use a razor as it can cause skin irritation and uneven cutting.

Hair Care for a Low Taper Fade

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair after giving yourself a low taper fade:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your low taper fade.

Now that you know how to give yourself a low taper fade, you can save money and create a stylish look in the comfort of your own home. With a little practice and patience, you'll be able to master this haircut and feel confident in your new style!
