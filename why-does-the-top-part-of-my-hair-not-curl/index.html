Why Does The Top Part Of My Hair Not Curl

Written by Daniel May 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've ever had trouble getting the top part of your hair to curl, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this issue, and it can be frustrating to spend time styling your hair only to have it fall flat in certain areas. In this article, we'll explore some of the reasons why the top part of your hair may not be curling and provide some tips for getting the curls you want.

Description

The top part of your hair not curling can be caused by a variety of factors, including hair length, hair texture, and the products you use. It's important to understand what's causing the issue so that you can take steps to address it.

How to

If you're struggling to get the top part of your hair to curl, there are a few things you can try:

1. Use the right products:

The products you use can have a big impact on how your hair curls. Make sure you're using products that are designed for your hair type and that provide the right amount of hold. If your hair is particularly resistant to curling, you may want to try using a stronger hold product or a curl-enhancing spray.

2. Change your technique:

If you're not getting the results you want with your current curling technique, try changing things up. For example, you could try using a different size curling iron or wrapping your hair around the iron in a different way.

3. Get a haircut:

If your hair is too long or too heavy, it may be difficult to get it to curl. Consider getting a haircut to remove some of the weight and make it easier to style.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting the top part of your hair to curl:

1. Prep your hair:

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Apply a heat protectant product to prevent damage from heat styling.

2. Section your hair:

Separate the top part of your hair from the rest of your hair and clip it up. This will make it easier to curl the top part without getting in the way.

3. Start curling:

Using a curling iron, take small sections of hair and wrap them around the iron. Hold the iron in place for a few seconds, then release the curl.

4. Repeat:

Continue curling small sections of hair until you've curled the entire top part of your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for getting the top part of your hair to curl:

1. Don't overdo it:

Be careful not to over-curl your hair, as this can make it look unnatural and damage your hair over time.

2. Use a diffuser:

If you're using a hair dryer to style your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the heat more evenly and prevent frizz.

3. Experiment with different products:

There are a wide variety of hair products available, so don't be afraid to experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair.

FAQ

Q: Why won't the top part of my hair curl?

A: There are many reasons why the top part of your hair may not be curling, including hair length, hair texture, and the products you use. Try changing your technique or using different products to see if that helps.

Q: What should I do if my hair won't hold a curl?

A: If your hair is particularly resistant to curling, try using a stronger hold product or a curl-enhancing spray. You may also want to consider getting a haircut to remove some of the weight and make it easier to style.

Q: How often should I curl my hair?

A: It's best to avoid curling your hair every day, as this can damage your hair over time. Try to limit heat styling to once or twice a week, and always use a heat protectant product to minimize damage.

Hair Care

Remember, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best. This includes washing and conditioning regularly, using heat protectant products when heat styling, and avoiding over-styling or using too many products. By taking good care of your hair, you'll be able to achieve the curls you want without causing damage or frustration.
