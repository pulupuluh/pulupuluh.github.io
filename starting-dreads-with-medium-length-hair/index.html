Starting Dreads With Medium Length Hair

Are you thinking of starting your dreadlock journey but have no idea how to do it with medium-length hair? Fear not, because in this guide, we'll show you how! From the basics of how to start them to hair care tips, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Start Dreads with Medium Length Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are formed by twisting or matting hair together to create a rope-like appearance. Starting dreads with medium-length hair can be challenging, but it's not impossible. With the right techniques and a little patience, you can achieve the dreadlock look you desire.

How to Start Dreads with Medium Length Hair

Before you start your dreadlock journey, it's essential to have healthy hair. Make sure to wash your hair regularly and avoid using any products that contain wax or heavy oils as they can prevent your hair from locking.

Next, decide on the method you want to use to start your dreads. Some popular methods include:

  • Twist and Rip
  • Backcombing
  • Crochet
  • Natural

Step-by-Step Guide

For this guide, we'll be using the twist and rip method. Here's how to start your dreads:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry.
  2. Section your hair into small, even parts.
  3. Take one section of hair and divide it into two parts.
  4. Twist the two parts of hair together, starting at the roots and going down to the ends.
  5. Hold the twisted hair in place and rip it apart. Repeat this process until the entire section of hair is twisted and ripped.
  6. Continue this process with the rest of your hair, making sure to keep each section even in size.
  7. Once you've twisted and ripped all of your hair, let it sit for a few days to start locking.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Here are some tips to help you maintain your dreads:

  • Protect your dreads while sleeping by wearing a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase.
  • Keep your dreads clean by washing them regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using products that contain wax or heavy oils as they can prevent your hair from locking.
  • Retwist your roots every 4-6 weeks to keep your dreads looking neat.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads with medium length hair:

  • Can I start dreads with relaxed hair?
  • Yes, you can start dreads with relaxed hair, but it may take longer for them to lock.
  • How long does it take for dreads to form?
  • It can take anywhere from 3-6 months for dreads to start forming.
  • Can I wash my dreads?
  • Yes, you can wash your dreads, but make sure to use a residue-free shampoo.

Hair Care for Your Dreads

Here are some tips for taking care of your dreads:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can clog your pores.
  • Retwist your roots every 4-6 weeks to keep your dreads looking neat.
  • Protect your dreads from the sun to prevent fading.

Starting dreads with medium-length hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and care, you can achieve the look you desire. Remember to be patient and take care of your dreads to keep them looking healthy and neat.
