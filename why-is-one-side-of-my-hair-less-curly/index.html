Why Is One Side Of My Hair Less Curly

Written by Oliver Aug 17, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly hair, you may have noticed that one side of your hair is less curly than the other. This can be frustrating and make it difficult to achieve an even look. In this blog post, we will discuss the reasons why one side of your hair may be less curly and provide tips on how to fix it.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of One Side of Hair Being Less Curly
  • How to Fix Uneven Curls
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Fixing Uneven Curls

Causes of One Side of Hair Being Less Curly

There are several reasons why one side of your hair may be less curly than the other:

Heredity

One of the most common causes of uneven curls is heredity. If your parents or grandparents have uneven curls, there is a good chance that you will too. This is because curly hair is determined by genetics, and some people have more curly genes on one side of their head than the other.

Haircut

The way your hair is cut can also affect how curly it appears. If your hair is cut unevenly, it may look less curly on one side than the other. This is because the weight of the hair is distributed differently on each side of your head.

Styling Habits

If you style your hair differently on each side of your head, this can also cause uneven curls. For example, if you always sleep on one side of your head, the weight of your head may cause the curls on that side to flatten over time.

How to Fix Uneven Curls

If you are unhappy with the uneven curls on your head, there are several things you can do to fix them:

Get a Haircut

If your hair is unevenly cut, getting a haircut can help to even out your curls. Make sure to go to a stylist who is experienced with cutting curly hair, as they will know how to distribute the weight of your hair evenly.

Change Your Styling Habits

If you always style your hair the same way, try switching things up to see if it makes a difference. For example, if you always part your hair on the same side, try parting it on the other side for a few days to see if this helps to even out your curls.

Use the Right Products

Using the right products for your hair can also make a big difference in how curly it appears. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, as these will help to enhance your curls and make them more uniform. Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.

Step-by-Step Guide to Fixing Uneven Curls

Here is a step-by-step guide to fixing uneven curls:

  1. Start by getting a haircut from a stylist who is experienced with cutting curly hair. Make sure to ask them to distribute the weight of your hair evenly.
  2. Switch up your styling habits to see if this helps to even out your curls. For example, try parting your hair on the other side or sleeping on a different side of your head.
  3. Use products that are specifically designed for curly hair to enhance your curls and make them more uniform.
  4. Consider using a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help enhance your curls.

Tips for Maintaining Even Curls

Here are some tips for maintaining even curls:

  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to enhance your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb when detangling your hair to minimize breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair and make it look less curly.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a straightener to make my hair more even?

A: While using a straightener may make your hair more even, it can also cause damage to your hair and make it look less healthy. We recommend using the tips and techniques outlined in this blog post to achieve more even curls.

Q: How long will it take to fix my uneven curls?

A: The time it takes to fix your uneven curls will depend on the severity of the issue and the techniques you use to address it. However, with consistent effort, you should start to see improvement within a few weeks.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of curly hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it look dry and frizzy.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can cause damage and make your curls look less defined.
  • Avoid using towels to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to blot your hair dry.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve more even, defined curls and maintain healthy, beautiful hair.
