Why Is The Top Layer Of My Hair Less Curly

Written by Robby Mar 15, 2023 · 9 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can look absolutely stunning, it can also be difficult to maintain. One common issue that people with curly hair face is that the top layer of their hair is less curly than the rest. This can be frustrating and leave you wondering why this is happening. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind this issue and provide tips on how to deal with it.

Description

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. People with curly hair have follicles that are more oval-shaped, which causes the hair to grow in a spiral. However, the shape of the hair follicle can vary from person to person, which can cause differences in curl patterns. This is why some people have looser curls while others have tighter curls.

When the top layer of your hair is less curly than the rest, it can make your hair look uneven and frizzy. This can be caused by a number of factors, including genetics, damage, and styling habits.

How to Identify if the Top Layer of Your Hair is Less Curly

Before we dive into the reasons why the top layer of your hair is less curly, it's important to know how to identify if this is an issue for you. Here are some signs to look out for:

  • The top layer of your hair looks frizzy and undefined
  • The curls on the top layer of your hair are looser than the rest of your hair
  • The top layer of your hair is straighter than the rest of your hair

Reasons Why the Top Layer of Your Hair is Less Curly

Genetics

One of the main reasons why the top layer of your hair is less curly than the rest could be due to genetics. As mentioned earlier, the shape of your hair follicles can vary from person to person, which can cause differences in curl patterns. If your parents or grandparents have a similar hair pattern, it's likely that genetics are playing a role in your hair's curl pattern.

Damage

Another reason why the top layer of your hair is less curly could be due to damage. Heat styling, chemical treatments, and even brushing can cause damage to your hair, which can affect its curl pattern. The top layer of your hair is more exposed to these types of damage, which can cause it to be less curly than the rest of your hair.

Styling Habits

The way you style your hair can also affect its curl pattern. If you're constantly pulling your hair back into a tight ponytail or bun, it can cause the top layer of your hair to become straighter over time. Similarly, if you're using products that are too heavy for your hair type, it can weigh down the top layer of your hair and make it less curly.

How to Deal with the Top Layer of Your Hair Being Less Curly

Use the Right Products

Using the right products for your hair type can make a huge difference in the curl pattern of your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair and avoid anything that is too heavy or contains drying ingredients like sulfates.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair, which can affect its curl pattern. If you're looking to enhance your curls, try air-drying your hair instead of using a blow dryer or diffuser. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant and keep the temperature on the lowest setting possible.

Experiment with Styling Techniques

The way you style your hair can also affect its curl pattern. Experiment with different techniques like plopping, diffusing, and twisting to see what works best for your hair. You may find that a certain technique helps to enhance the curls on the top layer of your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

While dealing with the top layer of your hair being less curly can be frustrating, there are things you can do to maintain healthy, beautiful curls. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can cause breakage
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing to help keep it hydrated
  • Consider getting a trim every few months to prevent split ends

FAQ

Q: Can the top layer of my hair become more curly?

A: Yes, with the right care and styling techniques, it's possible to enhance the curls on the top layer of your hair.

Q: Is it normal for the top layer of my hair to be less curly?

A: Yes, it's not uncommon for the top layer of curly hair to be less curly than the rest. This can be due to genetics, damage, or styling habits.

Q: Will cutting my hair make it more curly?

A: Cutting your hair won't necessarily make it more curly, but it can help to remove damaged ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care

Taking care of curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can maintain healthy, beautiful curls. Remember to avoid heat styling, experiment with styling techniques, and use the right products for your hair type. With a little bit of effort, you can enhance the curls on the top layer of your hair and achieve the perfect curly look.
