How To Wash Thick Wavy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter May 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

When you have thick wavy hair, washing it can be quite an ordeal. With so many tangles and knots, it can be difficult to get your hair squeaky clean without damaging it. However, with the right technique and a few helpful tips, washing your thick wavy hair can be a breeze. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to wash thick wavy hair.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

  4. FAQs

How to Wash Thick Wavy Hair

Washing thick wavy hair requires a gentle touch and a few specialized techniques. Here are the basics:

  • Use lukewarm water to wet your hair thoroughly. Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out your hair and scalp.
  • Apply a quarter-sized amount of shampoo to your hair. Focus on your scalp and roots, using your fingertips to gently massage the shampoo into your scalp.
  • Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water.
  • Apply conditioner to the lengths and ends of your hair, avoiding the scalp. Use a wide-toothed comb to distribute the conditioner evenly through your hair.
  • Leave the conditioner in your hair for a few minutes before rinsing it out with lukewarm water.
  • Wrap your hair in a microfiber towel or a t-shirt to absorb excess water. Avoid using a regular towel, as it can cause frizz and breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to wash thick wavy hair:

Step 1: Brush your hair

Before getting in the shower, use a wide-toothed comb or a detangling brush to remove any tangles and knots from your hair. This will make it easier to wash and prevent further tangles.

Step 2: Wet your hair

Use lukewarm water to wet your hair thoroughly. Make sure that the water is not too hot, as this can damage your hair and scalp.

Step 3: Apply shampoo

Apply a quarter-sized amount of shampoo to your hair, focusing on your scalp and roots. Use your fingertips to gently massage the shampoo into your scalp, being careful not to scrub too hard.

Step 4: Rinse your hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water, making sure to remove all of the shampoo. Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out your hair and scalp.

Step 5: Apply conditioner

Apply conditioner to the lengths and ends of your hair, avoiding the scalp. Use a wide-toothed comb to distribute the conditioner evenly through your hair. Leave the conditioner in your hair for a few minutes before rinsing it out with lukewarm water.

Step 6: Dry your hair

Wrap your hair in a microfiber towel or a t-shirt to absorb excess water. Avoid using a regular towel, as it can cause frizz and breakage. Let your hair air-dry or use a diffuser to dry it on a low heat setting.

Tips for Washing Thick Wavy Hair

Here are a few helpful tips for washing thick wavy hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply conditioner to the lengths and ends of your hair, as these areas are typically drier and more prone to breakage.
  • Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out your hair and scalp.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as it is less likely to cause breakage than a regular brush.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or a t-shirt.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my thick wavy hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or work out frequently, you may need to wash your hair more often. However, if you have dry or curly hair, you may be able to go longer between washes.

Q: Can I use regular towels to dry my hair?

A: It is best to avoid using regular towels to dry your hair, as they can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a microfiber towel or a t-shirt to absorb excess water.

Q: Should I brush my hair when it's wet or dry?

A: It is best to avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.

Hair Care for Thick Wavy Hair

Here are a few tips for taking care of your thick wavy hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal parts, as they can cause breakage. Use fabric hair ties instead.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can keep your thick wavy hair looking healthy and beautiful.
