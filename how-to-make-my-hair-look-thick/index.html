How To Make My Hair Look Thick

Written by Luffy Mar 22, 2023 · 7 min read
DescriptionHow to Make your Hair Look ThickStep by Step GuideTips to Keep in MindFAQsHair Care for Thick Hair

Description

Many people dream of having thick and voluminous hair. However, not everyone is blessed with naturally thick hair. But don't worry, there are ways to make your hair look thick and full of volume without having to resort to hair extensions or hair thickeners. In this article, we will guide you on how to make your hair look thick and full of volume. We will also provide you with tips on how to take care of your hair to maintain its thickness.

How to Make Your Hair Look Thick

If you want to make your hair look thick and full of volume, there are several things you can do. Here are some tips:

1. Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Using the right shampoo and conditioner can make a big difference in how your hair looks and feels. Look for products that are designed for fine or thin hair. These products are formulated to add volume and thickness to your hair. Avoid heavy products that can weigh your hair down and make it look flat.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow-drying your hair upside down can add volume to your roots, making your hair look thicker. Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots while blow-drying. This will create a natural lift and add volume to your hair.

3. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair can give it an instant lift and make it look thicker. Use a fine-toothed comb to gently tease your hair at the roots. Be careful not to tease too aggressively, as this can cause damage to your hair.

4. Use Hair Extensions

If you want to add instant thickness to your hair, consider using hair extensions. There are many types of hair extensions available, including clip-ins, tape-ins, and sew-ins. Choose the type of extension that suits your needs and budget.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to make your hair look thick:

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry it gently.

Step 2: Apply a Volumizing Mousse

Apply a volumizing mousse to your damp hair. Use your fingers to work the mousse through your hair, focusing on the roots.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow-dry your hair upside down, using a round brush to lift your hair at the roots. Concentrate on the roots to create volume and lift.

Step 4: Tease Your Hair

Use a fine-toothed comb to tease your hair at the roots. Gently backcomb your hair to create volume and lift.

Step 5: Use Hair Extensions (Optional)

If you want to add more thickness to your hair, you can use hair extensions. Choose the type of extension that suits your needs and budget.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some tips to keep in mind when trying to make your hair look thick:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heavy hair products that can weigh your hair down
  • Blow-dry your hair upside down
  • Tease your hair at the roots
  • Use hair extensions if needed

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about making hair look thicker:

Q: Can hair thickeners make my hair look thicker?

A: Yes, hair thickeners can make your hair look thicker, but they can also make your hair look greasy and weigh it down. It's best to use them sparingly.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every day. If you have dry hair, you can wash it less often. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

If you have naturally thick hair, you need to take care of it to maintain its thickness. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using heating tools too often
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh your hair down

By following these tips, you can make your hair look thick and full of volume. Remember to take care of your hair to maintain its thickness and health.
