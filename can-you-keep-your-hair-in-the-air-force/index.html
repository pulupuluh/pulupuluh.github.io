Can You Keep Your Hair In The Air Force

Written by William Apr 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are planning to join the Air Force, you might be wondering whether you can keep your hair. As with any military branch, there are grooming standards that you need to follow. In this article, we will explore the answer to the question, "Can You Keep Your Hair in the Air Force?" and provide you with helpful tips and advice on how to maintain your hair while serving in the Air Force.

Description

The Air Force has strict grooming standards for both men and women. These standards are in place to ensure that all service members maintain a professional appearance and adhere to military discipline. The Air Force's grooming standards are designed to promote uniformity and discipline while also maintaining a professional image.

How to Keep Your Hair in the Air Force

Keeping your hair in the Air Force will depend on your gender and the length of your hair. Here are some tips on how to maintain your hair while serving in the Air Force:

For Men

Male service members are required to have short, well-groomed hair. Hair should not extend beyond the collar of the shirt, and it should not touch the ears or eyebrows. If you have longer hair, you will need to cut it before joining the Air Force. You will also need to keep your hair clean and neatly trimmed at all times.

For Women

Female service members are allowed to have longer hair than male service members. However, the hair must be neatly groomed and must not interfere with the proper wearing of military headgear. Hair should be worn up and off the collar of the shirt. If your hair is longer than your collar, you will need to wear it in a bun or a neat braid.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to maintain your hair while serving in the Air Force:

Step 1: Know the Grooming Standards

Before joining the Air Force, you should familiarize yourself with the grooming standards. This will help you understand what is expected of you and how to maintain your hair properly.

Step 2: Cut Your Hair Before Joining

If you have long hair, you will need to cut it before joining the Air Force. This will ensure that you meet the grooming standards from day one.

Step 3: Keep Your Hair Clean and Neatly Trimmed

Regardless of your gender, you will need to keep your hair clean and neatly trimmed at all times. This will help you maintain a professional appearance and avoid disciplinary action.

Step 4: Wear Your Hair Up and Off the Collar

Female service members should wear their hair up and off the collar of the shirt. If your hair is longer than your collar, you can wear it in a bun or a neat braid.

Step 5: Use Hair Products Sparingly

Avoid using too many hair products, such as gels or hairsprays. These products can cause buildup and make your hair look greasy.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair in the Air Force

Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair in the Air Force:

  • Keep a comb or brush with you at all times to maintain a neat appearance.
  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain a neat appearance.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, as these can damage your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I have colored hair in the Air Force?

A: Hair color must be natural and conservative. Unusual or bright colors are not permitted.

Q: Can I wear a wig or hairpiece in the Air Force?

A: Wigs or hairpieces are not allowed, except for medical reasons.

Q: Can I have dreadlocks in the Air Force?

A: Dreadlocks are not permitted in the Air Force.

Q: Can I have a shaved head in the Air Force?

A: Yes, male service members are allowed to have a shaved head as long as it meets the grooming standards.

Hair Care in the Air Force

Maintaining your hair while serving in the Air Force is important for both your appearance and your overall health. By following the grooming standards and taking care of your hair, you can maintain a professional appearance and avoid disciplinary action. Remember to keep your hair clean, neatly trimmed, and styled according to the Air Force's grooming standards.
