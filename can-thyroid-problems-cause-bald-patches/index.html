Can Thyroid Problems Cause Bald Patches

Written by Frank May 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thyroid problems are more common than people realize. They can cause a range of symptoms that affect a person's overall health and well-being. One of the most notable symptoms of thyroid problems is hair loss, which can include bald patches. In this article, we will explore whether or not thyroid problems can cause bald patches, what to do if you are experiencing hair loss, and how to care for your hair if you have a thyroid condition.

Table of Contents

  • What are Thyroid Problems?
  • Can Thyroid Problems Cause Hair Loss?
  • What to Do If You Are Experiencing Hair Loss
  • Hair Care for Thyroid Conditions
  • Frequently Asked Questions

What are Thyroid Problems?

The thyroid is a small gland located in the neck that produces hormones that regulate the body's metabolism. When the thyroid is not functioning properly, it can cause a range of symptoms, including weight gain or loss, fatigue, and hair loss. There are two main types of thyroid problems: hypothyroidism (an underactive thyroid) and hyperthyroidism (an overactive thyroid).

Can Thyroid Problems Cause Hair Loss?

Yes, thyroid problems can cause hair loss. When the thyroid gland is not producing enough hormones (hypothyroidism), it can cause hair to become thin and brittle. In some cases, it can cause bald patches. On the other hand, when the thyroid gland is producing too much hormone (hyperthyroidism), it can cause hair to become fine and fragile, which can also lead to hair loss.

If you are experiencing hair loss, it is important to speak with your healthcare provider to determine the underlying cause. They may run blood tests to check your thyroid function or refer you to a dermatologist for further evaluation.

What to Do If You Are Experiencing Hair Loss

If you are experiencing hair loss due to a thyroid problem, the first step is to treat the underlying condition. Your healthcare provider may prescribe medication to regulate your thyroid function. It is important to follow their instructions and take your medication as directed.

In addition to treating your thyroid problem, there are other steps you can take to promote hair growth and prevent further loss. These include:

  • Eating a healthy, balanced diet
  • Taking supplements, such as biotin or iron, as recommended by your healthcare provider
  • Avoiding harsh hair treatments, such as perms or coloring
  • Gently washing and brushing your hair

Hair Care for Thyroid Conditions

If you have a thyroid condition, it is important to take extra care of your hair. This includes:

  • Using gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoiding excessive heat styling
  • Using a wide-tooth comb or brush to avoid pulling on hair
  • Avoiding tight hairstyles that can cause breakage
  • Protecting your hair from the sun and chlorine

Frequently Asked Questions

Can thyroid problems cause bald patches in women?

Yes, thyroid problems can cause bald patches in women. When the thyroid gland is not functioning properly, it can cause hair to become thin and brittle, which can lead to bald patches.

Can hair grow back after thyroid treatment?

Yes, hair can grow back after thyroid treatment. Once the underlying thyroid problem is treated, hair can begin to grow back. However, it may take several months for hair to return to its normal thickness and texture.

Can stress cause thyroid problems?

While stress can affect the body in many ways, it is not a direct cause of thyroid problems. However, stress can exacerbate existing thyroid problems and make symptoms worse.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after thyroid treatment?

The amount of time it takes for hair to grow back after thyroid treatment can vary. In some cases, it may take several months for hair to grow back to its normal thickness and texture.

Can thyroid problems cause hair thinning?

Yes, thyroid problems can cause hair thinning. When the thyroid gland is not producing enough hormones (hypothyroidism), it can cause hair to become thin and brittle.
