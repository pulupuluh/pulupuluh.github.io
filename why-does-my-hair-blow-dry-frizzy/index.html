Why Does My Hair Blow Dry Frizzy

Written by William Mar 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're someone who struggles with frizzy hair, you're not alone. Many of us have experienced the frustration of trying to achieve sleek, smooth locks only to end up with a poofy mess. But why does this happen, especially when blow drying? In this article, we'll dive into the science behind frizz and share some tips on how to tame it.

What is Frizz?

Frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of your hair becomes raised, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft. This causes the hair to swell, resulting in a fluffy, undefined appearance. Humidity and dryness can make frizz worse, which is why blow drying can be a tricky task.

How Does Blow Drying Affect Frizz?

When you blow dry your hair, you're exposing it to heat and air flow. This can cause the cuticle layer to become even more raised, leading to more frizz. Additionally, if you're using a brush or comb to style your hair, you could be causing friction that further exacerbates frizz.

How to Prevent Frizz When Blow Drying

Preventing frizz when blow drying starts with the products you use. Look for a heat protectant spray that can help shield your hair from damage. Additionally, using a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum can help to smooth the cuticle layer and prevent moisture from entering your hair shaft.

When it comes to blow drying technique, start by using a lower heat setting and holding the dryer at a distance from your hair to avoid direct heat. Use a round brush to gently guide your hair while blow drying, but avoid pulling or tugging on your strands. You can also try using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer, which can help to distribute air flow more evenly and prevent frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to blow dry your hair without causing frizz:

  1. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair.
  2. Section your hair and use clips to keep each section separate.
  3. Start blow drying on a low heat setting, holding the dryer about 6 inches away from your hair.
  4. Use a round brush to guide your hair while blow drying, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and be gentle when using the brush.
  6. If you have curly hair, consider using a diffuser attachment to help define your curls while blow drying.
  7. Once your hair is dry, use a cool shot to set the style and smooth the cuticle layer.
  8. Finish with a light mist of hairspray or anti-frizz serum to help lock in your style.

Tips for Preventing Frizz

Here are some additional tips for preventing frizz:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, which can strip it of its natural oils and lead to dryness and frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel after washing, as this can cause friction and lead to frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase, which can help to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid touching your hair throughout the day, as this can transfer oils from your hands and lead to frizz.

FAQ

Is frizz only caused by blow drying?

No, frizz can be caused by a variety of factors including humidity, dryness, and certain hair products. Blow drying can exacerbate frizz, but it's not the only cause.

What's the best way to style frizzy hair?

The best way to style frizzy hair is to use products that help to smooth the cuticle layer and prevent moisture from entering the hair shaft. You can also try using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer, or embracing your natural texture with a curly or wavy style.

Hair Care for Frizz-Prone Hair

Finally, here are some general hair care tips for those with frizz-prone hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling wands too frequently, as these can cause damage and lead to frizz.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove buildup from your hair and scalp.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals, which can help to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, which can lead to frizz and breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help to prevent frizz when blow drying your hair and keep your locks looking sleek and smooth.
