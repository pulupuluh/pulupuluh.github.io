Does Cancer Stop Hair Growth

Written by Luffy May 30, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cancer is a disease that affects the body in many ways. One of the most common side effects of cancer treatment is hair loss. Many patients wonder if cancer not only causes hair loss but if it also stops hair growth. In this article, we will explore this question and provide some tips on how to care for your hair during cancer treatment.

How to Care for Your Hair During Cancer Treatment

Chemotherapy and radiation therapy are two of the most common cancer treatments that can cause hair loss. Chemotherapy works by targeting rapidly dividing cells, which includes cancer cells but also hair follicle cells. Radiation therapy can also damage hair follicles by targeting the hair shaft directly. Hair loss can occur on the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and other parts of the body.

While cancer treatment can be tough, there are steps you can take to care for your hair during this time:

1. Be Gentle

Avoid using harsh shampoos or styling products that can damage your hair. Use a soft-bristled brush or a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, curling irons, or straighteners.

2. Protect Your Scalp

Your scalp may become sensitive during cancer treatment, so it's important to protect it from the sun and cold weather. Wear a hat or scarf when going outside to prevent sunburn or heat loss.

3. Moisturize Your Hair

Chemotherapy and radiation therapy can dry out your hair and scalp, so it's important to moisturize regularly. Use a gentle, moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair hydrated.

4. Consider a Wig or Hairpiece

If you're uncomfortable with hair loss, you may want to consider wearing a wig or hairpiece. Talk to your doctor or a hair specialist about your options.

Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Your Hair During Cancer Treatment

Here's a step-by-step guide to caring for your hair during cancer treatment:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Use a gentle, moisturizing shampoo to wash your hair. Avoid using hot water, and be gentle when washing and rinsing your hair.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Apply a moisturizing conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes before rinsing it out with lukewarm water.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Gently pat your hair dry with a soft towel. Avoid rubbing or pulling your hair, as this can cause breakage.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, curling irons, or straighteners. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a soft, flexible hair roller.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair During Cancer Treatment

Here are some additional tips for caring for your hair during cancer treatment:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet can help promote hair growth. Make sure to include plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet.

2. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair and scalp hydrated.

3. Avoid Chemical Treatments

Avoid dyeing, perming, or relaxing your hair during cancer treatment, as these chemical treatments can damage your hair further.

4. Be Patient

Remember that hair growth is a slow process, and it may take several months for your hair to grow back after cancer treatment. Be patient and kind to yourself during this time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does cancer always cause hair loss?

A: No, not all cancer treatments cause hair loss. However, chemotherapy and radiation therapy are the two most common treatments that can cause hair loss.

Q: Will my hair grow back after cancer treatment?

A: In most cases, yes. Hair growth may take several months, and the texture and color of your hair may be different than before. Talk to your doctor or a hair specialist for more information.

Conclusion

Cancer treatment can be a difficult time, but it's important to care for your hair and scalp during this time. By being gentle, moisturizing, and protecting your hair, you can help promote hair growth and maintain healthy hair. Remember to be patient and kind to yourself during this time, and don't hesitate to reach out to your doctor or a hair specialist for advice.
