What To Do When Your Hair Is Falling Out After Dying It

Written by Robby Aug 12, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Have you recently dyed your hair and noticed that it's falling out? This can be a scary experience, but don't worry, there are steps you can take to prevent further damage and promote hair growth. In this article, we'll go over what to do when your hair is falling out after dying it.

Table of Contents

  1. Causes of Hair Loss After Dyeing
  2. How to Stop Hair Loss After Dyeing
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Hair Care After Dyeing
  5. FAQs

Causes of Hair Loss After Dyeing

Hair loss after dyeing can be caused by a number of factors, including:

  • Chemicals in the dye
  • Over-processing or leaving the dye on for too long
  • Using too much heat when styling
  • Not rinsing the dye out properly
  • Genetics or underlying health conditions

If you're experiencing hair loss after dyeing, it's important to identify the cause and take steps to prevent further damage.

How to Stop Hair Loss After Dyeing

The first step in stopping hair loss after dyeing is to stop using harsh chemicals on your hair. This means avoiding any further dyeing or chemical treatments until your hair has had a chance to recover.

You should also avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as much as possible. If you do need to use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Another important step is to use gentle, nourishing hair products that promote hair growth. Look for shampoos and conditioners that contain biotin, keratin, and other vitamins and minerals that support healthy hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to stopping hair loss after dyeing:

  1. Stop using harsh chemicals on your hair
  2. Avoid heat styling tools
  3. Use gentle, nourishing hair products
  4. Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow
  5. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  6. Consider taking a hair growth supplement
  7. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  8. Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  9. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet
  10. Protect your hair from the sun and wind

Tips for Hair Care After Dyeing

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your hair after dyeing:

  • Wait at least a week after dyeing before washing your hair
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel to dry it
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair

FAQs

Q: Will my hair grow back after falling out from dyeing?

A: In most cases, yes. As long as you take steps to promote hair growth and avoid further damage, your hair should eventually grow back.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after falling out from dyeing?

A: Hair growth varies from person to person, but it typically takes several months for hair to grow back after falling out.

Q: Can I dye my hair again after it falls out from dyeing?

A: It's best to wait until your hair has fully recovered before dyeing it again. This can take several months or longer, depending on the extent of the damage.

In conclusion, if you're experiencing hair loss after dyeing, it's important to take steps to prevent further damage and promote hair growth. By following the tips and guidelines outlined in this article, you can help your hair recover and grow back stronger and healthier than ever.
