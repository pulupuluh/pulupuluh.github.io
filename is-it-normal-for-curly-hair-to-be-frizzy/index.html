Is It Normal For Curly Hair To Be Frizzy

Written by Christine Mar 10, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can be a challenge to manage. One of the biggest issues that people with curly hair face is frizz. It can be frustrating to spend time styling your hair, only to have it turn into a frizzy mess within a few hours. If you're wondering whether it's normal for curly hair to be frizzy, the answer is yes. But why does it happen, and what can you do about it? Let's find out.

Description

Frizz is caused by the outer layer of your hair, called the cuticle, becoming raised and allowing moisture to penetrate into the hair shaft. This causes the hair to swell and become frizzy. Curly hair is more prone to frizz because the cuticle is naturally more raised than on straight hair. Additionally, curly hair tends to be dryer than straight hair because the natural oils from the scalp have a harder time traveling down the hair shaft. When hair is dry, it's more prone to frizz.

How to

If you're dealing with frizzy curly hair, there are a few things you can do to help manage it:

1. Use the Right Products

Curly hair needs moisture, so make sure you're using products that are designed for your hair type. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, and glycerin. These ingredients will help hydrate your hair and keep it from becoming too dry.

2. Don't Over-Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day, and consider using a co-wash (a conditioner that cleanses your hair) instead of shampoo on some days.

3. Use a Diffuser

If you're using a hair dryer, make sure to use a diffuser attachment. This will help distribute the heat more evenly and reduce the amount of frizz. You can also air-dry your hair, which is the healthiest option for curly hair.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Curling irons and flat irons can be damaging to curly hair, and can also cause frizz. Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and if you do use them, make sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

5. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Regular cotton pillowcases can cause friction and lead to frizz. Sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase can help reduce friction and keep your hair looking smoother.

Step by Step

If you're looking for a step-by-step routine for managing frizzy curly hair, here's what we recommend:

1. Shampoo and Condition

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that are designed for curly hair. Make sure to rinse thoroughly and gently squeeze out excess water.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. This will help keep your hair hydrated and reduce frizz. You can also apply a curl-enhancing cream or gel at this point if you'd like.

3. Use a Microfiber Towel or T-Shirt to Dry Your Hair

Avoid using a regular towel to dry your hair, as the rough texture can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or a t-shirt to gently blot your hair and remove excess water.

4. Use a Diffuser to Dry Your Hair

If you're using a hair dryer, attach a diffuser and use a low heat setting. Dry your hair in sections, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Avoid touching your hair too much while you're drying it.

5. Finish with a Hair Oil or Serum

Once your hair is dry, apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to the ends to help smooth out any frizz.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips for managing frizzy curly hair:

- Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair hydrated.

- Try using a hair mask made with avocado, honey, or coconut oil to help nourish your hair.

- Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal for curly hair to be frizzy?

A: Yes, it's normal for curly hair to be frizzy. Curly hair is more prone to frizz because the cuticle is naturally more raised than on straight hair. Additionally, curly hair tends to be dryer than straight hair because the natural oils from the scalp have a harder time traveling down the hair shaft. When hair is dry, it's more prone to frizz.

Q: How can I prevent frizz in my curly hair?

A: To prevent frizz in curly hair, make sure to use products that are designed for your hair type, wash your hair no more than every other day, use a diffuser when blow-drying, avoid heat styling as much as possible, and sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase.

Q: What's the best way to style curly hair?

A: The best way to style curly hair is to embrace its natural texture. Use products that are designed for your hair type, and avoid heat styling as much as possible. You can also try different techniques like plopping, diffusing, or air-drying your hair to enhance your curls.

Hair Care

Managing frizzy curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, it's possible to keep your hair looking smooth and defined. Remember to use products that are designed for curly hair, avoid over-washing and heat styling, and be gentle when drying and detangling your hair. By following these tips, you can embrace your natural curls and keep them looking their best.
