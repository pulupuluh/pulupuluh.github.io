How To Cut Long Thick Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Apr 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have long, thick hair, you may be tempted to go to a professional stylist to get a haircut. However, with the right tools and technique, cutting your own hair at home can be a simple and cost-effective solution. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to cut long thick hair at home, including tips on hair care and frequently asked questions.

  • How to Cut Long Thick Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Long Thick Hair at Home

How to Cut Long Thick Hair at Home

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Sharp hairdressing scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Once you have your tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and allowing it to air dry or blow dry. This will help you see the natural texture and length of your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into four equal sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Unclip one of the back sections and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to trim any split ends or uneven lengths. Repeat with the other back section.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Unclip one of the front sections and comb it straight down. Use the bottom layer as a guide and trim any hairs that are longer than the rest. Repeat with the other front section.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Unclip the top layer of your hair and comb it straight down. Use the middle layer as a guide and trim any hairs that are longer than the rest.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut each layer, it's time to blend them together. Comb your hair straight down and check for any uneven lengths. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs until all the layers are even.

Step 7: Trim Your Bangs (Optional)

If you have bangs, unclip them and trim them to the desired length. Make sure they are even on both sides.

Step 8: Use Clippers (Optional)

If you want a shorter hairstyle, you can use clippers to trim the sides and back. Start with the longest guard and work your way down to the shortest until you reach your desired length.

Step 9: Check Your Work

After you have finished cutting your hair, comb it out and check for any uneven lengths or missed spots. Make any final trims as needed.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron. Apply any styling products you like, such as mousse or hairspray.

Tips for Cutting Long Thick Hair at Home

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use sharp, high-quality scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Always cut your hair when it's dry to prevent any surprises when it dries.
  • Start with small trims and work your way up to more significant changes.
  • Don't be afraid to seek advice from a professional stylist if you're unsure about anything.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair without professional training?

Yes, you can cut your own hair at home with the right tools and technique. However, it's always a good idea to seek advice from a professional stylist if you're unsure about anything.

How often should I cut my long thick hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

No, it's not recommended to cut your hair when it's wet because it can be difficult to see the natural texture and length of your hair. Always cut your hair when it's dry.

Hair Care After Cutting

After you have cut your hair, it's essential to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends.

Cutting long thick hair at home can be a simple and cost-effective solution. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home.
