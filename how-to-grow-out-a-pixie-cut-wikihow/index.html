How to Grow Out a Pixie Cut Wikihow

Growing out a pixie cut can be challenging, but with some patience and care, you can do it. The key is to let your hair grow out naturally and avoid cutting it too short. Here are some tips to help you grow out your pixie cut:

1. Be patient

It takes time for hair to grow out, so be patient. Your hair will grow about half an inch per month on average, so it may take several months to grow out a pixie cut.

2. Avoid cutting your hair too short

It's important to avoid cutting your hair too short while you're growing it out. This will only prolong the process and make it more difficult to style your hair. Instead, ask your stylist to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

3. Experiment with different hairstyles

While you're growing out your hair, experiment with different hairstyles to keep your look fresh and new. Try braids, buns, and ponytails to change up your style.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of growing out a pixie cut, let's dive into the step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Start with a clean, dry head of hair

Wash and dry your hair before starting the growing out process. This will help you see the natural growth pattern of your hair.

Step 2: Determine your hair's natural part

Using a comb, determine your hair's natural part. This will help you style your hair as it grows out.

Step 3: Use hair accessories

Use hair clips, headbands, and other accessories to keep your hair out of your face as it grows out. This will also help you experiment with different hairstyles.

Step 4: Trim your hair regularly

Visit your stylist every 6-8 weeks for a trim. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Step 5: Be patient

Be patient and let your hair grow out naturally. It may take several months to grow out a pixie cut, but the end result will be worth it.

Tips for Growing Out a Pixie Cut

Here are some additional tips for growing out a pixie cut:

1. Use hair masks

Use hair masks once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong. Look for masks that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, and avocado oil.

2. Try different haircuts

Try different haircuts to help you transition from a pixie cut to a longer hairstyle. Ask your stylist for suggestions on what will work best for your hair type and face shape.

3. Use heat protectant

Use heat protectant when styling your hair with hot tools like curling irons and straighteners. This will help prevent damage to your hair.

4. Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid harsh chemicals like bleach and hair dyes while you're growing out your hair. These can damage your hair and slow down the growing process.

FAQs

How long does it take to grow out a pixie cut?

It takes about 6-12 months to grow out a pixie cut, depending on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your hair to be.

Can I dye my hair while growing out a pixie cut?

Avoid harsh chemicals like bleach and hair dyes while you're growing out your hair. These can damage your hair and slow down the growing process.

What are some easy hairstyles for growing out a pixie cut?

Try braids, buns, and ponytails to change up your style while you're growing out your hair.

Hair Care for Growing Out a Pixie Cut

Here are some additional hair care tips for growing out a pixie cut:

1. Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush. This will help prevent breakage.

2. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner

Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair and keep it hydrated throughout the day.

4. Avoid heat

Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners as much as possible. This will help prevent damage to your hair.

Overall, growing out a pixie cut takes time and patience. Follow these tips and tricks to help make the process easier and more enjoyable. Remember, healthy hair is happy hair!
