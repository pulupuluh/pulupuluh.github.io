How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair Male A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having to go to the barbershop every time you need a haircut? Learning how to cut your own hair can be a great way to save time and money. However, cutting the back of your hair can be a little tricky, especially if you're a male. In this post, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut the back of your hair male.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're a beginner. But with a little practice and the right tools, you can achieve a great-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. The key to getting a good haircut is to take your time and follow the proper steps.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair Male

Cutting the back of your hair can be challenging, but with the right technique, you can achieve a great result. Here's how:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a good pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror.

Step 2: Start with the Sides

Begin by cutting the sides of your hair. Use the clippers to create an even length on both sides. Make sure you use a comb to guide the clippers and create a smooth transition between the top and sides of your hair.

Step 3: Trim the Top

Once you've finished the sides, it's time to trim the top. Use scissors to cut the hair on top of your head to the desired length. Use a comb to guide the scissors and create an even cut.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Cutting the back of your hair can be tricky, but it's not impossible. Use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head. Use the clippers or scissors to create an even cut. Start at the bottom of your hairline and work your way up.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, take a look in the mirror to make sure it looks even. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with a longer length and cut gradually. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use a comb to guide your clippers or scissors and create an even cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated.
