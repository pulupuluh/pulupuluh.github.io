How To Manage Coarse Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Mar 03, 2023 · 7 min read

Do you have coarse curly hair that’s prone to dryness and frizz? If so, you’re not alone. Many people with this hair type struggle to find the right products and techniques to keep their locks looking healthy and defined. But fear not – with the right care and attention, you can manage your coarse curly hair and achieve the look you’ve always wanted. Here are our top tips and tricks for managing coarse curly hair.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Manage Coarse Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Routine

How to Manage Coarse Curly Hair

The first step in managing coarse curly hair is to find the right products. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as shampoos, conditioners, and styling products. These products will help to nourish and define your curls, while also reducing frizz and dryness.

When washing your hair, be gentle. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner, and avoid scrubbing your scalp too vigorously. After washing, gently blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt – never rub your hair with a regular towel, as this can cause frizz and damage.

Next, it’s time to style your hair. For coarse curly hair, it’s best to use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz. Apply the product to damp hair, then use your fingers or a wide-toothed comb to distribute it evenly. Once your hair is styled, avoid touching it too much – this can disrupt the curls and cause frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to managing coarse curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner.
  2. Gently blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair.
  4. Use your fingers or a wide-toothed comb to distribute the product evenly.
  5. Avoid touching your hair too much once it’s styled.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for managing coarse curly hair:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and dryness.
  • Sleep on a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce frizz and protect your curls while you sleep.
  • Try the “pineapple” method – gather your hair into a loose ponytail on top of your head before bed to preserve your curls.
  • Consider getting a silk or satin hair bonnet to wear while you sleep.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about managing coarse curly hair:

  • Should I brush my hair when it’s wet or dry? It’s best to brush your hair when it’s wet, using a wide-toothed comb or a detangling brush. Brushing dry curly hair can cause breakage and frizz.
  • How often should I wash my hair? This depends on your hair type and personal preferences. Coarse curly hair tends to be dry, so you may not need to wash it as often as other hair types. Try washing every 2-3 days to start, and adjust as needed.
  • What’s the best way to dry my hair? Gently blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt, then let it air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting.
  • Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my curly hair? It’s best to use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, as these products will help to nourish and define your curls while also reducing frizz and dryness.

Hair Care Routine

Here’s a sample hair care routine for managing coarse curly hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner every 2-3 days.
  • Gently blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair.
  • Use your fingers or a wide-toothed comb to distribute the product evenly.
  • Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting.
  • Sleep on a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce frizz and protect your curls.

Managing coarse curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your locks looking healthy and defined. Try these tips and tricks, and don’t be afraid to experiment to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
