How To Cut Long Side Swept Bangs At Home Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

In this year 2023, it's no secret that long side swept bangs are all the rage. They're chic, stylish, and can be worn by just about anyone. However, getting your bangs cut at a salon can be expensive and time-consuming. That's why we've put together this step-by-step guide on how to cut long side swept bangs at home.

Description

Long side swept bangs are a great way to change up your look without committing to a drastic haircut. They're also versatile and can be styled in different ways to suit your face shape and personal style. Cutting long side swept bangs at home can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it's definitely doable. Plus, it can save you money and time spent at the salon.

How to Cut Long Side Swept Bangs at Home

Before we get started, there are a few things you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle
  • Flat iron or curling iron (optional)

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting your bangs.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to part your hair down the middle. Then, take the section of hair that you want to cut into bangs and clip it away from the rest of your hair.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Bangs

Decide on the length of your bangs. Remember, it's better to start longer and gradually cut more if needed. Use the comb to pull the section of hair straight down in front of your face.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Hold your scissors vertically and cut into the hair at an angle. Start at the center of your bangs and work your way out towards the edges. Make sure you cut the hair at an angle for a more natural and blended look.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Release the clipped section of hair and check your work. If needed, trim any stray hairs or adjust the length of your bangs. Remember, it's always better to cut less than to cut too much.

Step 6: Style Your Bangs

You can style your bangs however you like. Use a flat iron or curling iron to add some texture or leave them straight. You can also tuck them behind your ear or pin them back for a different look.

Tips for Cutting Long Side Swept Bangs at Home

  • Start with dry hair to ensure a more accurate cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to cut less and adjust as needed.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and require some trial and error.
  • Be patient with yourself. It may take a few tries to get your bangs exactly how you want them.

FAQ

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your bangs if you have curly hair. Just make sure to cut them when your hair is dry and in its natural curl pattern.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look overgrown. As a general rule, trimming your bangs every 4-6 weeks should keep them looking fresh.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, you can always adjust the length of your bangs or style them differently to hide any unevenness.

Hair Care Tips for Long Side Swept Bangs

  • Keep your bangs clean and oil-free to prevent them from looking greasy.
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your bangs between washes.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can transfer oils from your hands to your hair.
  • When blow drying your hair, use a round brush to smooth out your bangs and create a more polished look.
  • Consider using a heat protectant spray if you frequently style your bangs with a flat iron or curling iron.

Now that you have a step-by-step guide on how to cut long side swept bangs at home, you can try it out for yourself. Remember to be patient, take breaks if you need to, and be careful not to cut too much at once. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting your own bangs in no time!
