How To Make Frizzy Hair Lay Flat Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Mar 11, 2023 · 8 min read

Frizzy hair can be a nightmare to deal with, especially when you're trying to achieve a sleek and polished look. Fortunately, there are several ways to make frizzy hair lay flat and stay that way throughout the day. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to tame frizz and achieve smooth, silky locks.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Frizzy Hair Lay Flat
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Frizzy Hair

How to Make Frizzy Hair Lay Flat

Before we get into the specifics, let's first understand what causes frizz. Frizz occurs when the hair cuticle is raised, allowing moisture to penetrate the hair shaft and causing it to swell. This can be due to a variety of factors, including humidity, heat damage, over-processing, and genetics.

To make frizzy hair lay flat, you need to smooth down the hair cuticle and lock in moisture. Here are some ways to do that:

1. Use a Smoothing Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by using a smoothing shampoo and conditioner that are designed for frizzy hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, argan oil, and coconut oil, which can help to nourish and strengthen the hair.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help smooth down the hair cuticle and prevent frizz. Be sure to apply it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help to smooth down the cuticle and prevent frizz. Use a high-quality hair dryer with a diffuser attachment to evenly distribute the heat. Be sure to dry your hair in sections, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.

4. Use a Flat Iron

If you have particularly stubborn frizz, you may need to use a flat iron to smooth down your hair. Choose a high-quality flat iron with ceramic plates and adjustable temperature settings. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using the flat iron to prevent damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make frizzy hair lay flat:

  1. Wash your hair with a smoothing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub it too vigorously.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.
  4. Blow dry your hair in sections, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Use a flat iron to smooth down any remaining frizz.
  6. Finish with a light hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help make frizzy hair lay flat:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Invest in a high-quality hairbrush with natural bristles, which can help to distribute the natural oils in your hair and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy.
  • Try using a silk or satin pillowcase, which can help to prevent friction and keep your hair smooth.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

FAQs

Q: Can I make frizzy hair lay flat without using heat styling tools?

A: Yes, you can make frizzy hair lay flat without using heat styling tools. One way to do this is to apply a smoothing serum to your hair before blow drying it. You can also try air-drying your hair and using a leave-in conditioner to smooth down the cuticle.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I have frizzy hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every two to three days if you have frizzy hair. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy.

Hair Care for Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for frizzy hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as this can cause heat damage and make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends, which can make your hair look frizzy and damaged.

With these tips and tricks, you can make frizzy hair lay flat and achieve the sleek, polished look you've always wanted. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques until you find what works best for you.
