How To Style Thinning Hair On Crown Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline May 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're experiencing thinning hair on your crown, you might feel self-conscious about styling your hair. However, there are many ways to work with your hair to make it look fuller and more voluminous. In this guide, we'll show you how to style thinning hair on your crown.

How to

Here are some tips and tricks for styling thinning hair on your crown:

1. Use volumizing hair products

To add volume to your hair, use volumizing hair products such as mousse, root-lifting spray, or hair powder. Apply these products to your roots and massage them into your scalp to create lift and volume.

2. Try a new haircut

A new haircut can make a big difference in the way your hair looks. Consider a shorter haircut that adds texture and volume to your hair. A layered haircut can also create the illusion of fuller hair.

3. Use a round brush

When blow-drying your hair, use a round brush to lift your roots and create volume. Brush your hair from the roots to the ends, and focus on the crown area to create lift.

4. Embrace your natural texture

If you have wavy or curly hair, embrace your natural texture. Use a curl-enhancing product to define your curls and create volume.

5. Avoid heavy products

Heavy hair products can weigh your hair down and make it look flat. Avoid using heavy oils and creams, and opt for lighter products such as serums or leave-in conditioners.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style thinning hair on your crown:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.

Step 2: Apply volumizing product

Apply a volumizing product such as mousse, root-lifting spray, or hair powder to your roots.

Step 3: Blow-dry your hair

Use a round brush to lift your roots and blow-dry your hair. Focus on the crown area to create lift and volume.

Step 4: Style your hair

Style your hair as desired. Consider a shorter haircut or a layered haircut for added texture and volume.

Step 5: Finish with a light-hold hairspray

Finish your hairstyle with a light-hold hairspray to keep your hair in place without weighing it down.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for styling thinning hair on your crown:

1. Use a volumizing dry shampoo

If you don't have time to wash your hair, use a volumizing dry shampoo to add lift and texture to your roots.

2. Consider a hair topper

If you're experiencing significant hair loss on your crown, consider using a hair topper. A hair topper is a hairpiece that covers the crown area and adds volume and fullness to your hair.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling and damaging your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I still dye my hair if I have thinning hair on my crown?

A: Yes, you can still dye your hair if you have thinning hair on your crown. However, be sure to use a gentle, non-damaging hair dye and avoid over-processing your hair.

Q: Will wearing a hat make my hair loss worse?

A: No, wearing a hat won't make your hair loss worse. However, be sure to choose hats that fit properly and aren't too tight.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important, especially if you're experiencing hair loss. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Condition regularly

Use a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and soft.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Avoid using hot tools such as curling irons and straighteners, or use a heat protectant spray before styling.

4. Eat a healthy diet

A healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can promote healthy hair growth. Be sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean protein.

In conclusion, styling thinning hair on your crown may seem challenging, but with the right techniques and products, you can create a fuller, more voluminous hairstyle. Remember to take care of your hair by using gentle products and maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
