How To Cut Inverted Stacked Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 11, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to give your hair a fresh and modern update, the inverted stacked bob is a great option. This style is characterized by shorter layers in the back that gradually get longer towards the front, creating a dramatic and edgy look. But how do you achieve this haircut? Follow our step-by-step guide below to learn how to cut an inverted stacked bob.

Description

The inverted stacked bob is a versatile haircut that can work on a variety of hair types and face shapes. It features shorter layers at the nape of the neck that gradually get longer towards the front, creating a stacked effect. The front pieces are typically chin-length or longer and can be styled in a variety of ways, including straight, wavy, or curled.

How to Cut Inverted Stacked Bob

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into four parts: the back, left side, right side, and top. Use clips to secure each section in place.

Step 2: Cut the Back

Begin with the back section. Take a horizontal section of hair at the nape of the neck and comb it straight down. Using a pair of sharp scissors, cut a straight line across the section, creating the shortest layer of the haircut. Continue working your way up the back section, taking horizontal sections and cutting them at a slightly longer length each time, until you reach the crown of the head.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Move on to the left and right side sections. Take a diagonal section of hair from the back of the head towards the front, and comb it straight down. Use the shortest layer in the back as a guide and cut the hair at the same length. Continue working your way up the sides, taking diagonal sections and cutting them at a slightly longer length each time, until you reach the crown of the head.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Finally, move on to the top section. Take a horizontal section of hair from the crown of the head and comb it straight up. Use the previous layers as a guide and cut the hair at a slightly longer length. Continue working your way towards the front of the head, taking horizontal sections and cutting them at a slightly longer length each time, until you reach the desired length in the front.

Tips

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to ensure an even and balanced haircut.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can the inverted stacked bob work on any hair type?

Yes, the inverted stacked bob can work on straight, wavy, and curly hair types. However, the length and layering may need to be adjusted based on the hair's natural texture.

How often should I get my inverted stacked bob trimmed?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of the inverted stacked bob.

Hair Care

To keep your inverted stacked bob looking fresh and healthy, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type and texture, and avoid over-washing to prevent dryness and damage. Use a heat protectant before using any hot styling tools, and avoid using too much product that can weigh down the hair.

Overall, the inverted stacked bob is a stylish and bold haircut that can give your hair a fresh update. By following our step-by-step guide and taking care of your hair, you can achieve a beautiful and modern look.
