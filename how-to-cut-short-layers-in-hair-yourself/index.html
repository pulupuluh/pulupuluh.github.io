How To Cut Short Layers In Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 05, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your long, boring hair and looking for a way to spice things up? Short layers might be just what you need! Not only will they add volume and texture to your hair, but they’re also easy to maintain. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut short layers in hair yourself, so you can save money and feel confident in your new ‘do.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Cut Short Layers in Hair Yourself

Before we get started, there are a few things you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Water spray bottle

Once you have all of these items, you’re ready to start cutting!

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut short layers in hair yourself:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair, making it easier to cut.

Step 2: Separate Your Hair

Using your comb, separate your hair into three sections: top, middle, and bottom. Clip or tie the top two sections out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer. Take a small section of hair from the back, and comb it straight down. Holding the scissors at a slight angle, cut into the hair to create a soft, feathered effect. Continue doing this all around the back of your head.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the middle section of your hair, and repeat the same process as step three. This time, start with the middle-back section and work your way around the sides.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, release the top section of your hair, and repeat the same process as step four. Be careful not to cut too much, as this layer is the most visible.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting short layers in hair yourself:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut a little at a time. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can result in uneven or jagged cuts.
  • Use a water spray bottle to dampen your hair if it’s too dry or difficult to comb.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help if you’re not sure what you’re doing. Cutting your own hair can be tricky, and it’s always better to be safe than sorry.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short layers in hair yourself:

How often should I cut my hair?

It’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

You can cut your hair wet or dry, but it’s generally easier to see the shape and texture of your hair when it’s dry.

How much hair should I cut?

This depends on your personal preference and the style you’re going for. Start with small sections and cut a little at a time.

Hair Care

After you’ve cut your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when it’s wet.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut short layers in hair yourself, you can experiment with different styles and feel confident in your ability to maintain them. Happy cutting!
