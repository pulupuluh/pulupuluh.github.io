How To Make Thick Hair Look Thin Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Robby Jun 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thick hair, you know the struggle of dealing with a full head of hair. While it can be a blessing in some ways, it can also be a curse, especially when it comes to styling. Thick hair is heavy, voluminous, and can be difficult to manage, which is why many people with thick hair look for ways to make it look thinner. If you're one of them, you're in luck because we've got some tips, tricks, and hair care advice to help you achieve the look you want.

How to Make Thick Hair Look Thin

If you want to make your thick hair look thinner, there are a few things you can do:

  • Cut your hair: This may seem counterintuitive, but cutting your hair can actually make it look thinner. Shorter hair is lighter and has less volume, so it doesn't look as thick.
  • Layer your hair: Layers can help to lighten up your hair and create movement, which can make it look less dense.
  • Straighten your hair: Straightening your hair can help to elongate it and make it look thinner. You can use a straightening iron or blow dryer to achieve this look.
  • Use thinning scissors: Thinning scissors are designed to remove weight from your hair, which can make it look thinner.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're not sure how to make your thick hair look thinner, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Blow dry your hair straight using a round brush.
  3. Straighten your hair using a straightening iron or blow dryer.
  4. Apply a hair serum or oil to smooth down any flyaways.
  5. Use thinning scissors to remove weight from your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up.
  6. Finish with a light hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Making Thick Hair Look Thin

Here are some additional tips to help you make your thick hair look thinner:

  • Don't use heavy products: Heavy products can weigh down your hair and make it look even thicker. Instead, opt for lightweight products that won't add too much volume.
  • Avoid backcombing: Backcombing can add volume to your hair, which is the opposite of what you want. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids can make your hair look even thicker. Instead, try loose waves or a messy bun.
  • Try a hair mask: A hair mask can help to nourish your hair and make it more manageable. Look for a mask that's designed for thick hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about making thick hair look thin:

  • Can thinning scissors damage my hair? Thinning scissors can be damaging if they're not used properly. Make sure to have them used by a professional stylist.
  • How often should I use a hair mask? You can use a hair mask once a week or as needed, depending on the condition of your hair.
  • What's the best way to blow dry thick hair? Use a round brush and blow dry your hair in sections to avoid tangles and ensure even drying.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

If you have thick hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for thick hair.
  • Don't wash your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your thick hair look thinner and more manageable. Remember to take care of your hair properly and use the right products and tools to achieve the look you want.
