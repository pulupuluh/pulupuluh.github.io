How To Freeform Dreads With Straight Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your straight hair and want to try something new? Do you want to embrace a new hairstyle that is unique and trendy? If yes, then freeform dreads might be just the thing for you. In this article, we will guide you on how to freeform dreads with straight hair. We will cover everything you need to know, from preparation to maintenance.

Table of Content

  • How to Freeform Dreads with Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Preparation

Before you start freeforming your dreads, there are a few things you need to keep in mind. First, it is essential to understand that freeform dreads require patience, dedication, and time. It is a process that can take several months or even years to achieve the desired results. Second, you need to have healthy hair and scalp. If you have any scalp or hair conditions, it is best to consult a dermatologist or a trichologist before starting the process.

Next, you need to gather the necessary tools, including a wide-tooth comb, a natural oil or butter, and a hair tie. You may also want to invest in a good shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for dreadlocks.

How to Freeform Dreads with Straight Hair

Freeforming dreads with straight hair is a process that involves allowing your hair to naturally lock and form into dreadlocks without manipulating or twisting them. This process can take time and requires patience, but the results are worth it. Here is how to freeform dreads with straight hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Stop using combs and brushes
The first step to freeform dreads is to stop using combs and brushes. These tools can cause your hair to become straight and untangled, which is not what you want. Instead, use your fingers to separate and gently twist your hair.

Step 2: Wash your hair regularly
Wash your hair regularly with a good shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for dreadlocks. Avoid using products that contain sulfates or alcohol as they can dry out your hair and scalp.

Step 3: Moisturize your hair and scalp
Moisturize your hair and scalp regularly with a natural oil or butter. This will help to keep your hair and scalp healthy and prevent your hair from becoming dry and brittle.

Step 4: Allow your hair to lock naturally
Allow your hair to lock naturally by not manipulating or twisting it. You can gently separate your hair with your fingers to prevent it from matting or tangling.

Step 5: Wear a hair tie
As your hair starts to form into dreads, it is essential to wear a hair tie to keep them from unraveling. You can use a soft hair tie that won't damage your hair, such as a silk scrunchie.

Tips for Maintaining Freeform Dreads

Maintaining freeform dreads requires patience and dedication. Here are some tips to help you maintain your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good shampoo and conditioner.
  • Moisturize your hair and scalp regularly with a natural oil or butter.
  • Avoid using products that contain sulfates or alcohol.
  • Wear a hair tie to keep your dreads from unraveling.
  • Separate your hair gently with your fingers to prevent it from matting or tangling.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to freeform dreads with straight hair?
A: Freeforming dreads can take several months or even years to achieve the desired results. It all depends on your hair type, length, and texture.

Q: Do I need to cut off my hair to freeform dreads?
A: No, you do not need to cut off your hair to freeform dreads. You can start the process with any hair length.

Hair Care for Freeform Dreads

Freeform dreads require special care to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good shampoo and conditioner.
  • Moisturize your hair and scalp regularly with a natural oil or butter.
  • Avoid using products that contain sulfates or alcohol.
  • Wear a hair tie to keep your dreads from unraveling.
  • Separate your hair gently with your fingers to prevent it from matting or tangling.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons or curling irons.
  • Avoid using hair dyes or bleaches as they can damage your hair and scalp.

Freeforming dreads with straight hair can be a rewarding experience. It is a unique and trendy hairstyle that requires patience and dedication. By following these tips, you can achieve beautiful and healthy dreadlocks that will make you stand out from the crowd.
