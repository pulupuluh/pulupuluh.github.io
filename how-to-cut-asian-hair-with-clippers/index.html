How To Cut Asian Hair With Clippers Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are an Asian man with thick, coarse hair, you know how challenging it can be to find the right hairstyle that looks good on you. Going to a salon or barbershop can be expensive and time-consuming, and sometimes you might not get the results you were hoping for. That's why learning how to cut your own hair with clippers can be a game-changer. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your Asian hair with clippers, step by step.

Description

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's talk about why cutting Asian hair with clippers is different from cutting other types of hair. Asian hair is typically thicker, coarser, and more resistant to movement. This can make it challenging to achieve the desired hairstyle. Clippers can be a great tool because they are designed to cut through thick hair quickly and efficiently.

How to Cut Asian Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

The first step to cutting your Asian hair with clippers is to gather the right tools. You will need a good quality pair of clippers, a comb, scissors, and a mirror. Make sure your clippers come with different guards or attachments that allow you to adjust the length of the cut.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more challenging to cut, and it's harder to see the length of the hair when it's wet. Comb your hair to remove any tangles and make sure your hair is free of any hair products that can make it sticky or greasy.

Step 3: Choose Your Hairstyle

Decide on the hairstyle you want before you start cutting. Look for inspiration online or in magazines to get an idea of what you want. Keep in mind your hair type and face shape when choosing a hairstyle.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair with the longest guard or attachment first. This will help you avoid cutting too much hair off at once. Use the clippers to cut the hair in the direction of the hair growth, starting at the nape of your neck and moving upwards. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut your hair with the longest guard, switch to a shorter one to blend the hair. Use the clippers to cut the sides and back of your hair, blending it with the top. Make sure you don't create any harsh lines or uneven spots.

Step 6: Trim the Edges

Use scissors to trim the edges and create a clean, finished look. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs around your ears and the back of your neck. Make sure you don't cut too much hair off and ruin the shape of your hairstyle.

Tips for Cutting Asian Hair with Clippers

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting your Asian hair with clippers:

  • Start with a longer guard or attachment to avoid cutting too much hair off at once.
  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Move the clippers in the direction of the hair growth for a more even cut.
  • Blend the hair with different guards or attachments to avoid harsh lines and uneven spots.
  • Trim the edges with scissors for a clean, finished look.

FAQ

What type of clippers are best for cutting Asian hair?

Clippers with adjustable guards or attachments are best for cutting Asian hair because they allow you to adjust the length of the cut. Look for clippers with a powerful motor that can cut through thick hair quickly and efficiently.

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with clippers if you have the right tools and follow the proper technique. Cutting your own hair can save you time and money, but it can be challenging if you're not experienced.

Hair Care

After cutting your Asian hair with clippers, it's essential to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type. Avoid using too many hair products that can make your hair greasy or heavy. Visit a salon or barbershop every few months to get a professional trim and maintain the shape of your hairstyle.

Learning how to cut your own Asian hair with clippers can be a game-changer. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve the hairstyle you want without spending a lot of money or time. Follow the step-by-step guide and tips in this article to get the best results.
