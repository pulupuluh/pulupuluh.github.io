How To Cut Curly Kid Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 22, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're a parent of a curly-haired kid, you know how tricky it can be to cut their hair. Curly hair has a mind of its own and can easily turn into a frizzy mess if not cut properly. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut curly kid hair in a relaxed and stress-free way.

How to Cut Curly Kid Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, here are some things you'll need:

  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A comb
  • Haircutting scissors
  • A mirror

Step 1: Wash and Detangle

Start by washing your child's hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Once their hair is clean, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle their curls.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide your child's hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to cut each section and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the ends of each section. Remember to cut less than you think you need to since curly hair tends to shrink up after it dries.

Use the comb to guide you as you cut, and make sure to cut at an angle to create layers.

Step 4: Check the Length

Once you've finished cutting all the sections, check the length of your child's hair. If it's still too long, go back and trim some more.

Step 5: Style and Finish

After you've finished cutting your child's hair, style it as desired. You can use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help define their curls and keep their hair looking healthy.

Tips for Cutting Curly Kid Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your child's curly hair:

  • Cut their hair when it's dry, not wet. This will help you see the true length of their hair and prevent any surprises.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging their hair or creating split ends.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. It can be challenging to cut curly hair on your own, so consider bringing in a friend or family member to assist you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly kid hair:

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how fast their hair grows and how quickly it becomes tangled. As a general rule, aim to cut their hair every 8-12 weeks.

Should I cut my child's hair myself or take them to a salon?

It's up to you and your child's preferences. If you feel comfortable cutting their hair yourself, go for it. If you're not confident in your abilities, consider taking them to a salon that specializes in curly hair.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my child's hair?

Don't worry – hair grows back! If you make a mistake, try to fix it as best you can or take your child to a professional.

Hair Care for Curly Kids

Cutting your child's curly hair is just one part of their hair care routine. Here are some additional tips to keep their curls looking healthy:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair
  • Avoid brushing their hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle their curls
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help define their curls

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to cut your child's curly hair with confidence and ease. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
