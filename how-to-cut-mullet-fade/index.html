How To Cut Mullet Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Apr 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

The mullet fade is a popular men's haircut that combines the classic mullet with a modern fade. This hairstyle is perfect for those who want a bold, edgy, and low-maintenance look. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting a mullet fade, from preparation to aftercare.

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Tools You'll Need
  • Step 1: Start with the Sides
  • Step 2: Create the Mullet
  • Step 3: Blend the Fade
  • Step 4: Refine the Cut
  • Tips for Cutting a Mullet Fade
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for a Mullet Fade

Preparation

Before you start cutting, it's essential to prepare your workspace and your client. Make sure you have a clean and well-lit area to work in. Ask your client how they want their mullet fade to look and discuss any concerns they may have. It's also a good idea to show them some reference images to ensure you're both on the same page.

Tools You'll Need

To cut a mullet fade, you'll need the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Trimmers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle
  • Mirror

Step 1: Start with the Sides

Begin by using clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back of the head to a uniform length. Depending on the desired length, start with a higher guard and work your way down. Use a comb to lift the hair and trim any stray hairs with scissors.

Step 2: Create the Mullet

Now it's time to create the mullet. Use the comb to section off the hair on top of the head from the hair on the sides and back. Cut the hair on top to the desired length, leaving it longer in the back. Use scissors to create layers and texture.

Step 3: Blend the Fade

To blend the fade, use clippers to create a fade line around the ears and the nape of the neck. Start with a higher guard and gradually work your way down. Use the trimmers to create a clean and precise line.

Step 4: Refine the Cut

Finally, use scissors to refine the cut and make any necessary adjustments. Check the symmetry of the cut and ensure that it's even on both sides. Use a mirror to check the back of the head.

Tips for Cutting a Mullet Fade

  • Always start with a higher guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair too quickly
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and create a natural-looking blend
  • Take your time and make small adjustments as you go
  • Don't be afraid to ask your client for feedback throughout the process
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp and easier to cut

FAQs

How long does a mullet fade last?

A mullet fade can last anywhere from 2-4 weeks, depending on how quickly the hair grows. It's recommended to get a trim every 3-4 weeks to maintain the look.

Can I cut a mullet fade on my own?

While it's possible to cut a mullet fade on your own, it's recommended to have a professional do it. A stylist will have the tools and expertise to create a precise and even cut.

Hair Care for a Mullet Fade

To maintain a mullet fade, it's important to keep the hair clean and well-conditioned. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat when styling. A leave-in conditioner or oil can help keep the hair soft and moisturized.

Now that you know how to cut a mullet fade, you can confidently create this edgy and stylish look for your clients. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and ask for feedback throughout the process. With a little practice, you'll be a mullet fade pro in no time!
