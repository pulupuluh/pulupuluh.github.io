How To Cut Your Own Hair In Layers Medium Length

Written by Oliver Aug 17, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting experience, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut your own hair in layers medium length. Layers are a great way to add texture and volume to your hair, and they work well for all hair types. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Own Hair in Layers Medium Length
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Before we dive into the steps on how to cut your own hair in layers medium length, let's talk about what layers are. Layers are different lengths of hair that are cut into the hair to create volume, texture, and movement. Medium length hair is hair that falls between your shoulders and your collarbone. It is a versatile length that can be worn in many styles.

How to Cut Your Own Hair in Layers Medium Length

Here are the tools you will need for cutting your own hair in layers medium length:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section your hair

Now, let's get started on how to cut your own hair in layers medium length:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair, so you can see exactly how much you are cutting. If your hair is wet, it may appear longer than it actually is.

2. Section your hair

Separate your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use clips to hold each section in place.

3. Cut the first layer

Start with the bottom layer. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. This will create a subtle layer.

4. Cut the second layer

Release the next section of hair, and use the first layer as a guide. Cut the second layer at a slightly steeper angle than the first layer.

5. Cut the third layer

Release the third section of hair and use the second layer as a guide. Cut the third layer at a steeper angle than the second layer.

6. Cut the fourth layer

Finally, release the top section of hair and use the third layer as a guide. Cut the top layer at the steepest angle to create the most volume.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair in layers medium length:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle.
  4. Release the next section of hair, and use the first layer as a guide. Cut the second layer at a slightly steeper angle than the first layer.
  5. Release the third section of hair and use the second layer as a guide. Cut the third layer at a steeper angle than the second layer.
  6. Release the top section of hair and use the third layer as a guide. Cut the top layer at the steepest angle to create the most volume.
  7. Blend the layers together by cutting into them at an angle.
  8. Check your work by running your fingers through your hair and looking in the mirror.
  9. Trim any uneven pieces or flyaways.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair in layers medium length:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little bit at a time. You can always go back and cut more.
  • Use a mirror to check your work as you go.
  • Don't cut your hair when you're in a rush or feeling stressed.
  • If you're not confident in your ability to cut your own hair, consider getting a professional haircut.

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair?

You should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

It's not recommended to cut your hair when it's wet because it will appear longer than it actually is. This can lead to uneven cutting.

Is it possible to cut your own hair in layers with curly hair?

Yes, it's possible to cut your own hair in layers with curly hair. However, it may be more difficult to see the layers as you're cutting. Consider cutting your hair when it's dry to see the true length.

Hair Care

Now that you know how to cut your own hair in layers medium length, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Now that you have the tools and knowledge on how to cut your own hair in layers medium length, give it a try! Remember to take your time, start small, and have fun.
