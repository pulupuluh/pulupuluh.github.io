How To Style Naturally Curly Frizzy Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you know that it can be a blessing and a curse. While curls can be beautiful and unique, they can also be difficult to manage, especially if your hair is prone to frizz. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll show you how to style naturally curly frizzy hair so that you can embrace your curls and rock your best hair yet.

Description

Naturally curly hair is beautiful, but it can also be difficult to manage. If your curls are prone to frizz, you may feel like you're fighting a losing battle. But with the right styling techniques and hair care routine, you can tame your frizzy curls and show them off with confidence.

How to Style Naturally Curly Frizzy Hair

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

The first step to styling naturally curly frizzy hair is to start with clean hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage to your hair. Rinse your hair with cool water to help seal the hair cuticles and reduce frizz.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help moisturize and detangle your curls. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Use a Curl-Defining Cream

To define your curls and reduce frizz, use a curl-defining cream. Apply the cream to your hair in sections, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Use your fingers to scrunch your curls and encourage them to form their natural shape.

Step 4: Diffuse Your Hair

To dry your hair without causing frizz, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. Set the dryer to low heat and low speed, and use the diffuser to gently dry your curls. Avoid touching your hair with your hands while it's drying, as this can cause frizz.

Step 5: Finish with a Hair Oil

To add shine and reduce frizz, finish your styling routine with a hair oil. Apply a small amount of oil to your palms and run it through your hair, focusing on the ends. This will help seal the hair cuticles and prevent frizz.

Tips for Styling Naturally Curly Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling naturally curly frizzy hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz
  • Use a microfiber towel or a t-shirt to dry your hair, as regular towels can cause frizz
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often, as this can damage your hair and cause frizz
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my naturally curly frizzy hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp is. Some people with curly hair only need to wash their hair once a week, while others may need to wash it every other day. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener on my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use a hair straightener on your curly hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant first. Straightening your hair too often can damage your hair and cause frizz.

Hair Care for Naturally Curly Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your naturally curly frizzy hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid washing your hair with hot water, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often, as this can damage your hair and cause frizz
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

With these tips and techniques, you can embrace your naturally curly frizzy hair and show it off with confidence. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type.
