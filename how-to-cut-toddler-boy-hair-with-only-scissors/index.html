How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair With Only Scissors

Written by Aprilia Jun 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a parent of a toddler boy, you know that haircuts are a necessary part of life. Whether you're looking to save some money or simply prefer to cut your child's hair at home, using only scissors can be a practical and easy solution. In this article, we'll walk you through how to cut toddler boy hair with only scissors, including step by step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice. Let's get started!

How to Cut Toddler Boy Hair with Only Scissors

Cutting a toddler's hair with only scissors can seem intimidating, but it's actually quite simple if you follow these steps:

Step 1: Gather your supplies

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary supplies. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb or brush, and a towel to keep your child's neck clean.

Step 2: Prep your child's hair

Start by wetting your child's hair with a spray bottle or by dipping their head in a sink. This will make the hair easier to cut and will help you see any unevenness or cowlicks.

Step 3: Comb and section the hair

Use a comb or brush to detangle your child's hair and section it off into manageable pieces. You may find it helpful to use clips to hold the hair in place.

Step 4: Start cutting

Using your scissors, begin cutting the hair in small sections. Start at the back of the head and work your way forward. Use your comb to hold the hair in place and to guide your scissors. Take your time and be patient.

Step 5: Trim around the ears and neckline

When you get to the ears and neckline, use your comb to lift the hair and cut around the edges. Be careful not to cut too much or too close to the skin. You can always go back and trim more if necessary.

Step 6: Check for evenness

After you've finished cutting, check for any uneven spots or areas that need touching up. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections.

Step 7: Finish up

When you're done cutting, use a damp towel to wipe any hair off your child's neck and shoulders. Comb their hair and style as desired.

Tips for Cutting Toddler Boy Hair with Only Scissors

Here are some tips to help make the process of cutting your toddler's hair with only scissors go smoothly:

  • Use sharp scissors to make clean cuts and avoid pulling or tugging on the hair.
  • Cut the hair when it's slightly damp to make it easier to manage.
  • Use a comb as a guide to help you cut straight lines.
  • Take breaks if your child gets antsy or starts to lose patience.

FAQs about Cutting Toddler Boy Hair with Only Scissors

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting toddler boy hair with only scissors:

Do I need special scissors to cut my child's hair?

No, you don't need special scissors. However, it's important to use sharp scissors to make clean cuts and avoid pulling or tugging on the hair.

How often should I cut my child's hair?

This depends on how fast your child's hair grows and how short you want to keep it. Some parents cut their child's hair every month, while others wait a few months between haircuts.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. Remember that hair grows back and you can always go back and fix any uneven spots or mistakes.

Hair Care Tips for Toddler Boys

Here are some tips for caring for your toddler boy's hair:

  • Wash their hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle their hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Trim their hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, cutting your toddler boy's hair with only scissors can be a stress-free and enjoyable experience. Happy cutting!
