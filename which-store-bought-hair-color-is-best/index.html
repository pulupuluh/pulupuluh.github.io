Description

Choosing the best store bought hair color can be a daunting task. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one for your hair type and desired look. In this blog post, we will discuss which store bought hair color is best and provide tips on how to choose the right one for you. We will also give you a step-by-step guide on how to color your hair at home and provide tips on hair care after coloring.

How to Choose the Best Store Bought Hair Color

When choosing a store bought hair color, there are a few things to consider:

1. Hair Type

Consider your hair type when choosing a store bought hair color. If you have dry or damaged hair, look for a formula that is specifically designed for damaged hair. If you have fine hair, look for a formula that is lightweight and won't weigh your hair down.

2. Desired Look

Consider the look you want to achieve. Do you want to cover gray hair or add highlights? Do you want a bold or subtle change? Look for a color that will help you achieve your desired look.

3. Brand Reputation

Choose a brand that has a good reputation for producing high-quality hair color. Look for reviews online or ask friends and family for recommendations.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to color your hair at home:

1. Prep Your Hair

Before coloring your hair, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Don't use any styling products or conditioner before coloring.

2. Mix the Color

Follow the instructions on the box to mix the hair color. Wear gloves to prevent staining.

3. Apply the Color

Apply the color to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down. Use a brush or applicator to ensure even coverage. Leave the color on for the recommended time, usually between 20-30 minutes.

4. Rinse and Condition

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water until the water runs clear. Apply the conditioner that comes with the hair color and leave it on for 2-3 minutes before rinsing.

Tips for Coloring Your Hair at Home

  • Always do a patch test before coloring your hair to avoid an allergic reaction.
  • Don't wash your hair for at least 24 hours after coloring to allow the color to set.
  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to keep your color looking vibrant.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools on your hair for a few days after coloring to prevent fading.

FAQs

1. Can I color my hair if it's damaged?

If your hair is severely damaged, it's best to wait until it's healthy before coloring. Coloring damaged hair can cause further damage and breakage.

2. How often should I color my hair?

It's best to wait at least 4-6 weeks between coloring to allow your hair to recover and prevent damage.

Hair Care After Coloring

After coloring your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain the color and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent drying it out.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools on your hair too often to prevent further damage.

Overall, the best store bought hair color depends on your hair type and desired look. Consider these factors when choosing a color and follow the step-by-step guide and tips for coloring and hair care to achieve the best results.
