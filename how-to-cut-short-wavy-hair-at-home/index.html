How To Cut Short Wavy Hair At Home The Ultimate Guide

Written by Luffy May 10, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have wavy hair. But with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to cut short wavy hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Short Wavy Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Short Wavy Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

How to Cut Short Wavy Hair at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, it’s important to have the right tools on hand. You’ll need a pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

When it comes to cutting wavy hair, it’s important to cut it when it’s dry. This will allow you to see the natural movement of your hair and avoid cutting too much off. Wet hair can also shrink when it dries, making it difficult to achieve the desired length.

It’s also important to cut your hair in sections. This will make it easier to achieve an even cut and avoid any choppy or uneven layers. Start by sectioning off the top of your hair, then work your way down to the sides and back.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut short wavy hair at home:

  1. Start by sectioning off the top of your hair. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and comb it straight up.
  3. Use your scissors to snip off the desired length, cutting straight across. Be sure to only cut a little at a time.
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 for the rest of the top section of your hair.
  5. Continue working your way down to the sides and back, sectioning off small sections of hair and cutting them to the desired length.
  6. Once you’ve cut all of your hair, use your scissors to add texture by cutting into the ends of your hair. This will help create movement and prevent any blunt lines.

Tips for Cutting Short Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short wavy hair:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can’t put it back once it’s cut.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make sure it’s even.
  • Don’t cut too much off at once. Wavy hair tends to bounce up when it dries, so it’s better to cut less than you think you need.
  • If you’re unsure about cutting your hair, start with a small section and see how it turns out before moving on to the rest of your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short wavy hair:

Can I cut my wavy hair when it’s wet?

No, it’s best to cut wavy hair when it’s dry. Wet hair can shrink when it dries, making it difficult to achieve the desired length.

How often should I cut my wavy hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to maintain the shape of your cut. Generally, it’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

What’s the best way to add texture to wavy hair?

The best way to add texture to wavy hair is by cutting into the ends with scissors. This will create movement and prevent any blunt lines.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to keep your wavy hair looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as they can damage the natural curl pattern.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your waves.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it’s wet.

Now that you know how to cut short wavy hair at home, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. Just remember to start with small snips, work in sections, and cut your hair when it’s dry. Happy cutting!
