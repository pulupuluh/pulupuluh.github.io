How To Layer Cut Your Own Hair Short

Written by William Apr 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle or trim your locks, layer cutting your own hair short can be a great option. Layer cutting creates movement and texture in your hair, making it look fuller and more voluminous. In this article, we'll go through step-by-step instructions on how to layer cut your own hair short, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

  2. Step-by-Step Instructions

How to Layer Cut Your Own Hair Short

Before we get started, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Comb out any tangles and part your hair the way you normally wear it.

Step 2: Section your hair into layers. Start by clipping the top layer of your hair up, leaving the bottom layer down.

Step 3: Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting at the length you want your layers to begin.

Step 4: Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle, moving from the back of your head to the front.

Step 5: Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, unclip the next layer and repeat the same process.

Step 6: When you reach the top layer of your hair, comb it straight up and cut it straight across. This will give you a layered look without taking off too much length.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here's a more detailed breakdown of the layer cutting process:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Comb out any tangles and part your hair the way you normally wear it.

Step 2: Section your hair into layers. Start by clipping the top layer of your hair up, leaving the bottom layer down.

Step 3: Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting at the length you want your layers to begin.

Step 4: Use the first cut section as a guide for the rest of your hair. Take a small section next to the first cut section and cut it at the same angle.

Step 5: Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle, moving from the back of your head to the front. Make sure to keep each section the same length and angle to maintain a uniform look.

Step 6: Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, unclip the next layer and repeat the same process.

Step 7: When you reach the top layer of your hair, comb it straight up and cut it straight across. This will give you a layered look without taking off too much length.

Tips for Layer Cutting Your Own Hair Short

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layer cutting your own hair short:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid taking off too much length at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.
  • Keep your hair wet while cutting to make it easier to manage.
  • Check your layers in a mirror to make sure they're even on both sides.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's tangled or knotted.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about layer cutting your own hair short:

Can I layer cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can layer cut your own hair if it's curly. Just be sure to cut it when it's dry and in its natural state, since curly hair can shrink up when it dries.

How often should I layer cut my own hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how much maintenance you want to do. Generally, layer cutting every 6-8 weeks can help maintain your hairstyle and keep your hair healthy.

What should I do if I accidentally cut too much off?

If you accidentally cut too much off, don't panic! Wait a few days for your hair to settle and then go to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care Tips

After layer cutting your own hair short, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools like flat irons and curling irons.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or applying a leave-in conditioner before swimming.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth and health.

Layer cutting your own hair short can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice, you'll be able to achieve a stylish and healthy look. Happy cutting!
