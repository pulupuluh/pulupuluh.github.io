How Do I Know If Blonde Will Suit Me

Written by Oliver Apr 03, 2023 Â· 2 min read

Blonde hair is a classic and timeless look that many people aspire to achieve. However, before taking the plunge and dyeing your hair blonde, it's important to consider whether or not it will suit you. In this article, we'll go over how to determine if blonde hair will work for you and what you can do to ensure that your hair looks its best.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Going Blonde
  3. Tips for Maintaining Blonde Hair

How to Determine If Blonde Hair Will Suit You

Determining whether or not blonde hair will suit you largely depends on your skin tone and natural hair color. Here are a few things to consider:

  • Skin Tone: If you have fair skin with cool undertones, blonde hair is likely to suit you well. Warm-toned skin may clash with blonde hair, so it's best to avoid it if your skin has yellow or olive undertones.
  • Natural Hair Color: If you have naturally light hair, going blonde will be easier and less damaging to your hair. However, if you have dark hair, you may need to have your hair bleached multiple times to achieve the desired shade, which can be damaging to your hair.
  • If you have a classic, preppy style or prefer a more natural look, blonde hair may work well for you. However, if you have an edgier or more alternative style, blonde hair may not be the best fit.

Ultimately, whether or not blonde hair will suit you is a personal choice. If you're unsure, consider trying a temporary wig or hair extensions to see how you feel before committing to a permanent dye job.

Step-by-Step Guide to Going Blonde

If you've decided to take the plunge and go blonde, here's a step-by-step guide to achieving the perfect shade:

  1. Choose a shade of blonde that will complement your skin tone and natural hair color.
  2. Prepare your hair by washing it with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.
  3. Protect your hair by applying a deep conditioning treatment or hair mask before bleaching.
  4. Bleach your hair using a high-quality bleach and developer, following the instructions carefully.
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly and apply a toner to achieve the desired shade of blonde.
  6. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your new look!

Remember to take care of your newly blonde hair by using sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, deep conditioning treatments, and avoiding heat styling tools whenever possible.

Tips for Maintaining Blonde Hair

Maintaining blonde hair can be a challenge, but with a little extra care, you can keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant:

  • Use a purple shampoo once a week to neutralize any yellow or brassy tones.
  • Avoid swimming in chlorinated water, as it can cause your hair to turn green.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Avoid heat styling tools whenever possible, and use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

FAQs About Going Blonde

Q: Can I dye my hair blonde at home?

A: It is possible to dye your hair blonde at home, but it can be tricky and may result in uneven or damaged hair. It's best to visit a professional stylist who can help you achieve the perfect shade of blonde.

Q: Will going blonde damage my hair?

A: Bleaching your hair can be damaging, but with proper care and maintenance, you can keep your hair healthy and looking its best. Be sure to use a high-quality bleach and developer, and avoid over-processing your hair.

Q: How often will I need to touch up my roots?

A: The frequency of root touch-ups will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how noticeable your roots are. Most people need to touch up their roots every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Blonde Hair

Blonde hair requires special care to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out and becoming brittle.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

By following these tips and taking extra care of your blonde hair, you can keep it looking healthy and vibrant for years to come.
