How To Say Barber In Italian

Written by Bardi Sep 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're planning a trip to Italy and need a haircut, it's important to know how to communicate with your hairdresser. In this article, we'll be discussing how to say barber in Italian and other related terms to help you navigate your next visit to the salon.

What is a Barber?

A barber is a professional who specializes in cutting, styling, and grooming men's hair. Barbershops are often seen as a place where men can relax, socialize, and receive a quality haircut.

How to Say Barber in Italian

The word for barber in Italian is "barbiere". It's pronounced "bar-bee-air-eh".

Other Related Terms

Here are some other related terms that you might find useful:

  • Haircut: Taglio di capelli
  • Shave: Rasatura
  • Beard: Barba
  • Moustache: Baffi
  • Hair salon: Salone di bellezza

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to communicate with your Italian barber:

  1. Greet your barber with a friendly "Buongiorno" (good morning) or "Buonasera" (good evening).
  2. Explain what you want. For example, "Vorrei un taglio di capelli corto" (I would like a short haircut).
  3. If you have a picture of the style you want, show it to your barber.
  4. If you need a shave or beard trim, use the terms "rasatura" or "barba".
  5. If you're not sure what style you want, ask for a recommendation. Your barber will be happy to help.
  6. During the haircut, your barber may ask you questions about your hair or offer suggestions. Feel free to engage in conversation.
  7. When the haircut is finished, thank your barber with a polite "Grazie".
  8. If you're happy with the result, you can say "Ã perfetto" (It's perfect).
  9. If you're not satisfied, politely explain what changes you would like.
  10. Paying for your haircut is usually done at the front desk. You can ask your barber "Dove si paga?" (Where do I pay?)

Tips for a Great Haircut

Here are some tips to ensure you leave the salon with a great haircut:

  • Bring a picture of the style you want.
  • Be specific about how much hair you want cut off.
  • Listen to your barber's suggestions. They're the experts!
  • Don't be afraid to ask questions.
  • Relax and enjoy the experience.

FAQs

What's the difference between a barber and a hairdresser?

A barber specializes in cutting men's hair and often provides other grooming services such as shaves and beard trims. A hairdresser, on the other hand, is trained in cutting, styling, and coloring both men and women's hair.

How much should I tip my Italian barber?

In Italy, it's not customary to tip at a barbershop. However, if you're happy with the service, you can round up the bill or leave a small tip as a gesture of appreciation.

What's the best time to visit an Italian barbershop?

Barbershops in Italy are often busiest on weekends and evenings. If you want to avoid the crowds, try visiting during the week or in the morning.

Hair Care in Italy

Italian barbershops often offer a range of hair care services such as haircuts, shaves, and beard trims. They may also sell hair care products such as shampoos, conditioners, and styling products.

If you're looking for a quality haircut in Italy, visiting a local barbershop is a great option. With the right communication and a little bit of trust, you're sure to leave with a great new look.
