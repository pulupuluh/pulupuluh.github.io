What Bathing Suits Flatter Your Body Type A Guide To Finding The Perfect Fit

Written by Frank Apr 09, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Welcome to our guide on what bathing suits flatter your body type! With so many different styles, cuts, and colors available, it can be overwhelming to find the perfect swimsuit that complements your unique figure. But fear not, we've got you covered (pun intended)! In this article, we'll take you through the different body types and suggest the best bathing suits to enhance your natural curves and make you feel confident and beautiful.

  • Pear Body Type
  • Apple Body Type
  • Athletic Body Type

Hourglass Body Type

If you have an hourglass figure, you have a well-defined waist and curves in all the right places. To accentuate your figure, choose bikini tops and bottoms that have a high waist to show off your hourglass shape. A halter top will also look great on you as it provides support and lift to your bust.

When it comes to one-piece swimsuits, go for styles that have a V-neckline to elongate your torso and show off your curves. Try a swimsuit with ruching on the sides to give your waistline an extra boost. Avoid swimsuits with too much coverage, as they can hide your curves and make you look boxy.

Pear Body Type

If you have a pear-shaped body, you have wider hips and thighs compared to your upper body. The goal is to draw attention to your upper body to balance out your proportions. A good way to achieve this is by wearing a bikini top that has a bright color or print. Details like ruffles, frills, and embellishments can also help draw the eye upwards.

When it comes to bikini bottoms, opt for styles that have a higher waist and provide more coverage for your hips and thighs. Avoid styles that are too skimpy or have a lot of detailing on the hips as they can accentuate your lower half.

One-piece swimsuits can also work well for pear-shaped bodies. Look for styles that have cutouts or detailing on the upper body to draw attention upwards.

Apple Body Type

If you have an apple-shaped body, you carry more weight around your midsection. The key is to create the illusion of a smaller waist while providing support for your bust. A tankini is a great option for apple-shaped bodies as it provides coverage for the midsection while still showing off your curves.

When it comes to one-piece swimsuits, look for styles that have ruching or shirring around the waist to create a more defined shape. A swimsuit with a deep V-neckline or a plunging neckline can also help elongate your torso and create the illusion of a smaller waist.

Athletic Body Type

If you have an athletic body type, you have a straighter figure with fewer curves. The goal is to create the illusion of curves and femininity. Look for swimsuits that have ruffles, frills, or cutouts to add volume and texture to your curves.

Bikinis with triangle tops and tie-side bottoms can work well for athletic body types as they create the illusion of curves. High-waisted bikini bottoms can also help create the illusion of a smaller waist and wider hips.

One-piece swimsuits with cutouts or detailing on the sides can add curves to your figure and create a more defined shape.

Plus Size Body Type

If you have a plus size body type, the key is to find swimsuits that provide support, comfort, and confidence. Look for swimsuits that have built-in bras, thick straps, and a high back to provide support for your bust and back.

One-piece swimsuits with ruching or shirring can help create a more defined waistline and hide any areas you may be self-conscious about. High-waisted bikini bottoms can also work well for plus size body types as they provide coverage for the midsection and create the illusion of a smaller waist.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I wear a bikini if I have a larger bust?

A: Yes, you can! Look for bikini tops with underwire or thicker straps for support. Halter tops can also provide lift and support for larger busts.

Q: Can I wear a one-piece swimsuit if I have a shorter torso?

A: Yes, you can! Look for one-piece swimsuits with high-cut legs to elongate your torso and create the illusion of a longer silhouette.

Q: Can I wear a strapless bikini if I have a smaller bust?

A: Yes, you can! Look for bikini tops with ruffles or detailing on the bust to create the illusion of volume.

Hair Care Tips for the Beach

Now that you've found the perfect swimsuit to flatter your body type, it's time to think about your hair. The beach can be tough on your locks, so here are some hair care tips to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny:

  • Apply a leave-in conditioner before heading to the beach to protect your hair from the sun, salt, and chlorine.
  • Wear a hat or a scarf to protect your scalp and hair from the sun.
  • Rinse your hair with fresh water after swimming to get rid of any salt or chlorine.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair as they can further damage your locks.
  • Try a beachy wave spray to create effortless, beachy waves without the damage.

With these tips, you'll be able to enjoy the sun, sand, and surf while looking and feeling your best in your new swimsuit!
