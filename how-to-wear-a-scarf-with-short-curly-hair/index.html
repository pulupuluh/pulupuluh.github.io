How To Wear A Scarf With Short Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Mar 23, 2023 · 8 min read

Scarves are a versatile and stylish accessory that can add a pop of color and texture to any outfit. However, if you have short curly hair, you may be hesitant to wear a scarf for fear of it overwhelming your hairstyle. Fear not! With a few simple tips and tricks, you can easily incorporate a scarf into your look without sacrificing your curls. Here’s how:

Description

This blog post is a step-by-step guide on how to wear a scarf with short curly hair. It includes tips and tricks on how to choose the right scarf, tie it in different ways, and incorporate it into your hairstyle. Whether you’re looking to add a pop of color to your outfit, protect your curls from the elements, or just switch up your look, this post has got you covered.

How to Choose the Right Scarf

When it comes to choosing a scarf for short curly hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, opt for a lightweight fabric like silk or cotton that won’t weigh down your curls. Second, choose a scarf that complements your skin tone and hair color. If you have warm undertones, go for scarves in earthy tones like rust, mustard, and olive. If you have cool undertones, opt for scarves in jewel tones like emerald, sapphire, and amethyst. Finally, consider the size of the scarf. A smaller scarf will be easier to tie and won’t overwhelm your hairstyle.

Step-by-Step Guide to Tying a Scarf

There are countless ways to tie a scarf, but here are a few of our favorites:

The Classic Knot

Start by folding the scarf in half to create a loop. Drape the scarf around your neck so that the loop is on one side and the loose ends are on the other. Take one loose end and wrap it over the loop and through the hole. Repeat on the other side. Adjust the scarf so that it sits comfortably around your neck.

The Headband

Start by folding the scarf in half to create a triangle. Place the scarf at the nape of your neck with the point of the triangle facing forward. Tie the loose ends at the top of your head, just above your forehead. Tuck the point of the triangle under the knot for a seamless look.

The Bow

Start by folding the scarf in half to create a loop. Drape the scarf around your neck so that the loop is on one side and the loose ends are on the other. Tie the loose ends together in a bow. Adjust the bow so that it sits off-center for a playful look.

Tips for Incorporating a Scarf into Your Hairstyle

Now that you know how to tie a scarf, it’s time to incorporate it into your hairstyle. Here are a few ideas:

- Use a scarf as a headband to keep your curls out of your face.

- Tie a scarf around a low ponytail or bun for a chic look.

- Wrap a scarf around a top knot for a boho vibe.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear a scarf if I have short hair?

A: Absolutely! Short hair looks great with a scarf tied around the neck or as a headband.

Q: How can I keep my curls from getting flattened by a scarf?

A: Opt for a lightweight scarf that won’t weigh down your curls, and tie it loosely so that it doesn’t compress your curls.

Hair Care Tips for Short Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit of extra TLC to stay healthy and defined. Here are a few tips:

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

- Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair to help define your curls.

- Use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry, rather than rubbing it with a towel.

- Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid frizz and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you’re ready to rock a scarf with your short curly hair. Whether you’re running errands or attending a fancy event, a scarf is a versatile and stylish accessory that can elevate any look. So go ahead and experiment – you might just discover a new favorite hairstyle!
