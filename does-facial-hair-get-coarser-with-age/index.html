Does Facial Hair Get Coarser With Age

Written by Bardi Apr 24, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're a man who's been growing facial hair for years, you may have noticed that your beard and mustache have become coarser over time. This is a natural part of the aging process, but many men wonder why it happens and what they can do about it. In this article, we'll explore whether facial hair does, in fact, get coarser with age and how you can keep your beard and mustache looking and feeling their best.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How-To
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Facial hair can become coarser with age due to a variety of factors. One of the main reasons is that as men age, their testosterone levels decrease. Testosterone is the hormone responsible for hair growth, and when levels drop, hair can become thinner and coarser. Additionally, as hair follicles age, they can become less efficient at producing hair, leading to a coarser texture.

Other factors that can contribute to coarser facial hair include genetics, lifestyle choices, and environmental factors. For example, if you smoke or drink heavily, you may notice that your beard and mustache are coarser than those of non-smokers and moderate drinkers. Similarly, if you live in a dry or polluted area, your facial hair may be more prone to dryness and damage.

How-To

If you're wondering how to keep your facial hair looking and feeling its best as you age, there are several things you can do:

1. Keep it Clean

Wash your beard and mustache regularly with a gentle, moisturizing shampoo. This will help to remove dirt and debris that can make your facial hair feel coarse and dry. Avoid using harsh soaps or shampoos that can strip the natural oils from your hair.

2. Moisturize

Use a good quality beard oil or balm to moisturize your facial hair and the skin underneath. This will help to keep your beard and mustache soft, shiny, and healthy-looking. Look for products that contain natural ingredients like jojoba oil, argan oil, and shea butter.

3. Brush and Comb

Brush and comb your facial hair regularly to distribute the natural oils and prevent tangles. This will also help to exfoliate the skin underneath, promoting healthy hair growth. Use a boar bristle brush or a wide-tooth comb for best results.

4. Trim Regularly

Trim your beard and mustache regularly to keep them looking neat and tidy. Use sharp scissors or a beard trimmer to remove split ends and shape your facial hair. When trimming, be sure to follow the natural contours of your face for a natural-looking result.

Step-by-Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your facial hair looking and feeling its best:

1. Wash your beard and mustache

Use a gentle, moisturizing shampoo to wash your facial hair, massaging it into the skin underneath. Rinse thoroughly and pat dry with a towel.

2. Apply beard oil or balm

Take a small amount of beard oil or balm and rub it between your palms. Apply it to your facial hair, starting at the roots and working your way to the tips. Use a comb or brush to distribute the product evenly.

3. Brush and comb your facial hair

Use a boar bristle brush or a wide-tooth comb to brush and comb your facial hair, starting at the roots and working your way to the tips. This will help to distribute the natural oils and prevent tangles.

4. Trim your beard and mustache

Use sharp scissors or a beard trimmer to trim your facial hair, following the natural contours of your face. Remove any split ends and shape your beard and mustache as desired.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for keeping your facial hair looking and feeling its best:

  • Avoid using hot water on your beard and mustache, as this can dry out the hair and skin.
  • Be patient - it can take time for your facial hair to grow and fill in.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your facial hair, such as bleach or hair dye.
  • Consider using a beard conditioner to soften and moisturize your facial hair.

FAQ

Q: Does facial hair get coarser with age?

A: Yes, it's common for facial hair to become coarser as men age due to a variety of factors, including decreased testosterone levels and aging hair follicles.

Q: Can I prevent my facial hair from becoming coarse?

A: While you can't completely prevent your facial hair from becoming coarse, you can take steps to keep it looking and feeling its best as you age. This includes using a gentle, moisturizing shampoo, applying a good quality beard oil or balm, and trimming your beard and mustache regularly.

Q: Will shaving make my facial hair grow back coarser?

A: No, shaving does not make facial hair grow back coarser. This is a common myth - in reality, the regrowth may feel coarser because it hasn't been softened by natural oils and has a blunt edge from the razor.

Hair Care

Facial hair requires special care to keep it looking and feeling its best. By following the tips and techniques outlined in this article, you can keep your beard and mustache soft, shiny, and healthy-looking as you age.

Remember to wash, moisturize, brush, and trim your facial hair regularly, and avoid using harsh chemicals or hot water. With a little bit of care and attention, you can maintain a handsome beard and mustache for years to come.
