Why Is My Hair Shorter After Braids

Written by Frank Mar 31, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have ever gotten braids, you may have noticed that your hair seems shorter once you take them out. This is a common concern among people who wear braids, and there are several reasons why this happens. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your hair may be shorter after braids and provide you with tips on how to prevent this from happening.

Description

Braids are a popular protective hairstyle that involves weaving hair into a series of interlockings. They are often used to protect hair from damage and promote hair growth. However, many people notice that their hair seems shorter after getting braids. This can be frustrating, especially if you are trying to grow your hair out.

How to

If you want to prevent your hair from becoming shorter after braids, there are several steps you can take:

1. Moisturize Your Hair

Before getting braids, make sure your hair is well moisturized. This will help prevent breakage and damage when you take out your braids. You can use a deep conditioner or oil treatment to moisturize your hair.

2. Avoid Tight Braids

Tight braids can cause tension on your hairline and lead to breakage. Make sure your braids are not too tight and avoid styles that pull on your hairline.

3. Take Breaks Between Braids

Give your hair a break between braiding sessions. This will allow your hair to rest and recover from any damage caused by braids.

4. Use a Silk or Satin Scarf

When you sleep, use a silk or satin scarf to protect your hair from friction and breakage. This will help your hair stay healthy and prevent any further damage.

Step by Step

If you have already gotten braids and notice that your hair is shorter, there are several steps you can take to promote hair growth:

1. Trim Your Ends

Trimming your ends can help prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth. This will also help your hair look and feel healthier.

2. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help increase blood flow to your hair follicles and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

3. Use a Hair Growth Oil

There are many hair growth oils available that can help promote hair growth. Look for oils that contain ingredients like peppermint, rosemary, and tea tree oil.

4. Be Patient

Remember that hair growth takes time. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you will see results over time.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent your hair from becoming shorter after braids:

1. Avoid Hair Products with Harsh Chemicals

Harsh chemicals can damage your hair and lead to breakage. Use hair products that are free of sulfates, parabens, and other harmful chemicals.

2. Drink Plenty of Water

Drinking water can help keep your hair and scalp hydrated, which is essential for healthy hair growth.

3. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Q: Why does my hair seem shorter after braids?

A: Braids can cause tension on your hairline and lead to breakage. When you take out your braids, you may notice that your hair is shorter due to breakage.

Q: How can I prevent my hair from becoming shorter after braids?

A: You can prevent your hair from becoming shorter after braids by moisturizing your hair, avoiding tight braids, taking breaks between braids, and using a silk or satin scarf when you sleep.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after braids?

A: Hair grows at a rate of about half an inch per month. It may take several months for your hair to grow back after braids, depending on how much breakage occurred.

Hair Care

Overall, taking care of your hair is essential for preventing breakage and promoting healthy hair growth. Make sure to moisturize your hair, avoid tight hairstyles, and be patient with your hair care routine. With time and consistency, you can achieve healthy, long hair.
