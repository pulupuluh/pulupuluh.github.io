How To Cut A Pixie Cut Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 07, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're looking for a low-maintenance haircut that's both fashionable and practical, a pixie cut might be just what you need. And the good news is, you don't have to spend a fortune at a salon to get one. With a little bit of courage and the right tools, you can cut a pixie cut yourself at home. In this article, we'll show you how.

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that's typically cut above the ears and around the nape of the neck. It's a popular choice for women who want a low-maintenance haircut that's easy to style and can be dressed up or down. Pixie cuts are also great for women with fine hair, as they create the illusion of more volume and thickness.

How to Cut a Pixie Cut Yourself: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, you'll need to gather the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. You want your hair to be clean and free of any styling products.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, part your hair into sections. Start by creating a part down the middle of your head, then create a part from the middle of your forehead to the nape of your neck. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting at the back of your head, use your scissors to cut your hair in small sections. Cut at an angle to create layers and texture. Use your clippers to trim the hair around your ears and the nape of your neck.

Step 5: Move to the Sides

Once you've finished the back, move to the sides of your head. Use your scissors to cut your hair in small sections, pulling the hair outwards to create a textured look. Use your clippers to trim the hair around your ears.

Step 6: Cut the Top Section

Unclip the top section of your hair and comb it forward. Use your scissors to cut the hair in small sections, creating layers and texture. Use your clippers to trim the hair around your ears.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, comb your hair and check for any uneven spots. Use your scissors to make any necessary adjustments.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

You can style your pixie cut in a variety of ways, from slicking it back with gel to adding texture with a texturizing spray. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Tips for Cutting a Pixie Cut Yourself

  • Start with longer hair and gradually cut it shorter. You can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • Take your time and don't rush. It's better to make small cuts and adjustments than to make big mistakes.
  • Use your fingers to gauge how much hair to cut, rather than relying solely on your eyes.

FAQ

Can I cut a pixie cut myself?

Yes, with the right tools and a little bit of confidence, you can cut a pixie cut yourself.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always make adjustments or go to a salon to have it fixed.

How often do I need to cut my pixie cut?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want to keep your hair. On average, you'll need to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your pixie cut.

Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

Once you've cut your pixie cut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.

Now that you know how to cut a pixie cut yourself, you can save money and have fun experimenting with different styles. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and have fun!
