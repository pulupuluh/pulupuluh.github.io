How To Make Waves Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 12, 2023 · 9 min read

If you're looking to add some texture and movement to your hair, making waves is a great option. Whether you have naturally straight or curly hair, creating waves is a versatile style that can work for any occasion. In this guide, we'll go over how to make waves hair in a relaxed, easy-to-understand language. From the tools you'll need to the techniques you'll use, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Preparation
  3. Techniques for Making Waves Hair
  4. Tips for Getting the Perfect Waves
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care for Waves

Tools You'll Need

Before we get started, you'll need to gather a few tools to help you make waves in your hair. Here's what you'll need:

  • Heat protectant spray
  • Curling iron or wand
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Hairspray
  • Texture spray or sea salt spray (optional)

Make sure to choose a curling iron or wand that's the right size for the waves you want to create. A smaller barrel will give you tighter waves, while a larger barrel will give you looser waves.

Preparation

Before you start making waves, it's important to prepare your hair. Here's what you should do:

  1. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the curling iron or wand.
  3. Brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  4. Separate your hair into sections using clips or hair ties.

Now you're ready to start making waves!

Techniques for Making Waves Hair

There are a few different techniques you can use to create waves in your hair. Here are three of the most popular:

The Curling Iron Technique

To make waves using a curling iron, follow these steps:

  1. Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron.
  2. Hold the curling iron in place for a few seconds, then release the hair.
  3. Repeat this process with all sections of your hair, alternating the direction of the curls.

Once you've curled all of your hair, use your fingers to gently separate the curls into waves.

The Wand Technique

The wand technique is similar to the curling iron technique, but instead of wrapping your hair around a barrel, you wrap it around a wand. Here's how to do it:

  1. Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the wand.
  2. Hold the wand in place for a few seconds, then release the hair.
  3. Repeat this process with all sections of your hair, alternating the direction of the curls.

Once you've curled all of your hair, use your fingers to gently separate the curls into waves.

The Twist and Pin Technique

The twist and pin technique is a no-heat way to make waves in your hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Take a small section of hair and twist it away from your face.
  2. Pin the twisted section of hair to your head using a clip or bobby pin.
  3. Repeat this process with all sections of your hair, twisting in the same direction.
  4. Leave the pins in your hair for a few hours or overnight.
  5. Remove the pins and use your fingers to gently separate the twists into waves.

Tips for Getting the Perfect Waves

Here are a few tips to help you get the perfect waves:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Alternate the direction of the curls or twists to create a more natural look.
  • Use a texture spray or sea salt spray to add more volume and texture to your waves.
  • Don't over-curl your hair – less is more when it comes to creating waves.
  • Use your fingers to gently separate and shape your waves once you're done curling or twisting.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about making waves in your hair:

Can I make waves in my hair if it's naturally curly?

Yes, you can! Just use a curling iron or wand to create more defined waves.

How long will my waves last?

It depends on your hair type and how well you maintain your waves. Generally, waves will last anywhere from a few hours to a few days.

How can I make my waves last longer?

Use a hairspray or texture spray to help hold the waves in place. You can also sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braid to help maintain the waves overnight.

Hair Care for Waves

Now that you know how to make waves in your hair, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help prevent your waves from becoming frizzy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage your waves and make them look limp.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your waves moisturized and healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to make waves in your hair like a pro. Whether you're going for a beachy, tousled look or a more polished, defined wave, you can customize your waves to suit your style. Have fun experimenting with different techniques, and remember to take good care of your waves to keep them looking their best.
