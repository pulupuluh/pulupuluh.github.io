How To Treat Frizzy Wavy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel May 28, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Frizzy wavy hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can transform your unruly locks into beautiful waves that are easy to style and maintain. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to treat frizzy wavy hair, including how to care for your hair, style it, and prevent frizz. Read on for our top tips and tricks!

Table of Contents

  2. How To
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ

Hair Care

The first step to treating frizzy wavy hair is to get a good hair care routine in place. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

Cleanse and Condition Regularly

Wavy hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it's important to keep it hydrated with a good shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are designed for wavy hair, and avoid shampoos that contain sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils. Condition your hair every time you shampoo, and consider using a deep conditioner once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can be damaging to wavy hair, causing it to become dry and frizzy. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray first, and try to limit your use to once a week.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Wavy hair can easily become tangled, which can lead to breakage and frizz. To avoid this, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, being gentle to avoid pulling or tearing your hair.

How To

Now that you know how to care for your hair, let's move on to how to treat it to prevent frizz and enhance your waves.

Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After you've washed and conditioned your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help lock in moisture and prevent frizz. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, shea butter, or coconut oil, which can help nourish your hair and keep it soft and shiny.

Don't Dry Your Hair with a Towel

When you're ready to dry your hair, avoid rubbing it vigorously with a towel, which can cause frizz. Instead, gently blot your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt, which will absorb moisture without roughing up the hair cuticle.

Avoid Brushing Your Hair

Brushing your hair can cause it to become frizzy, especially if you have wavy hair. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair. If you must use a brush, use a boar bristle brush, which can help distribute your hair's natural oils and prevent frizz.

Step by Step

Now that you know the basics, here's a step-by-step guide to treating frizzy wavy hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for wavy hair, and avoid sulfates. Condition your hair every time you shampoo, and use a deep conditioner once a week.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help lock in moisture and prevent frizz.

Step 3: Blot Your Hair Dry

Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry, avoiding rubbing or twisting your hair.

Step 4: Avoid Brushing Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

You can enhance your waves by using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer, scrunching your hair as you dry it. You can also use a curling iron or hot rollers to add definition to your waves.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for treating frizzy wavy hair:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent hair breakage and frizz.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use hair products sparingly, as too much can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about treating frizzy wavy hair:

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how much oil your scalp produces. If you have wavy hair, you may be able to go longer between washes, as wavy hair tends to be drier than straight hair. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or as needed.

What should I do if my hair is still frizzy after following these steps?

You may need to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair. Consider trying a hair serum or oil to help tame frizz, or consult with a hair stylist for personalized advice.

Can I straighten my hair if I have wavy hair?

Yes, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and limit your use of heat styling tools to prevent damage to your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you can transform your frizzy wavy hair into beautiful waves that are easy to manage and style. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid heat styling as much as possible, and use the right products to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Happy styling!
