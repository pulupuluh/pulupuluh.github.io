What To Use To Slick Natural Hair Back Your Ultimate Guide In 2023

Are you looking for a sleek and sophisticated hairstyle that can make you stand out from the crowd? Slicked-back hair is a timeless look that can be achieved with the right products and techniques. In this guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to know about what to use to slick natural hair back.

  • How To Slick Natural Hair Back

Description

Slicked-back hair is a classic hairstyle that has been popular for decades. It involves combing the hair straight back from the forehead, using a styling product to hold it in place. This look can be achieved with any hair type, including natural hair.

How To Slick Natural Hair Back

To achieve a sleek and structured look, you need the right products and techniques. Here are the key steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before slicking your hair back, it's important to start with clean and conditioned hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a nourishing conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel-dry it gently.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

To slick your hair back, you need a strong-hold styling product that can keep your hair in place all day. You can choose from a variety of products, including pomades, gels, and waxes. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, using your fingers or a comb to distribute it evenly.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair Back

Using a fine-tooth comb, start combing your hair straight back from your forehead. Make sure to comb your hair smoothly and evenly, without creating any bumps or tangles. Use the comb to shape your hair into the desired style, whether you prefer a sleek and polished look or a more relaxed and tousled vibe.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

To keep your hair in place all day, finish with a light mist of hairspray. This will help to lock in the style and prevent any frizz or flyaways. Choose a hairspray that provides a flexible hold, so that your hair doesn't feel stiff or crunchy.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're new to slicking your hair back, here's a detailed guide to follow:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and a nourishing conditioner. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel-dry it gently.
  2. Apply a strong-hold styling product, such as a pomade or gel, evenly throughout your hair. Use your fingers or a comb to distribute the product evenly.
  3. Using a fine-tooth comb, start combing your hair straight back from your forehead. Make sure to comb your hair smoothly and evenly, without creating any bumps or tangles.
  4. If you have any stray hairs or flyaways, use a small amount of product to tame them and keep them in place.
  5. Finish with a light mist of flexible-hold hairspray to lock in the style and prevent any frizz or flyaways.

Tips for Slicking Natural Hair Back

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when slicking your natural hair back:

  • Choose a styling product that works well with your hair type. If you have thick or coarse hair, you may need a stronger hold product, while those with fine or thin hair may prefer a lighter product.
  • Use a fine-tooth comb to comb your hair back, rather than a brush. This will help to create a smooth and polished look.
  • Experiment with different styles and textures to find the look that works best for you. You can slick your hair back tightly for a sleek and structured look, or leave it a bit looser for a more relaxed and tousled vibe.
  • Don't be afraid to add some volume to your hair by backcombing or teasing the roots. This can help to create a more dramatic and eye-catching style.

FAQs

Q: Can I slick my natural hair back without using product?

A: While it is possible to slick your hair back without using product, you may find that your hair doesn't stay in place as well. Using a styling product can help to provide a stronger hold and prevent your hair from slipping or falling out of place.

Q: How often should I wash my hair when slicking it back?

A: It's important to keep your hair clean and conditioned when slicking it back. However, washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils and lead to dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or as needed based on your hair type and lifestyle.

Q: Can I slick my hair back if it's curly or wavy?

A: Absolutely! Slicked-back hair can work well with any hair type, including curly and wavy hair. You may need to use a bit more product or spend more time combing your hair to achieve the desired look, but it is definitely possible.

Hair Care Tips for Slicked-Back Hair

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, here are some key hair care tips to follow:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner that is free from sulfates and harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too frequently. These tools can cause damage to your hair and lead to breakage and split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy or dirty.

By following these tips and using the right products, you can achieve a sleek and polished look that will turn heads wherever you go. Happy styling!
