How To Soften Coarse African American Hair

Written by Frank Aug 15, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you struggling with coarse and unmanageable hair? Do you want to achieve soft and silky hair? If you are an African American, you might experience this hair problem due to the unique texture of your hair. But don't worry, there are ways to soften and improve the quality of your hair. In this article, we will discuss the steps on how to soften coarse African American hair.

How to Soften Coarse African American Hair

The first step in softening coarse African American hair is to determine the cause of the dryness and coarseness. Typically, coarse hair is caused by a lack of moisture and natural oils. This can be due to overuse of hair products, exposure to heat and chemicals, and even genetics. Here are some steps to soften your hair:

1. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner

One of the easiest ways to soften your hair is to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically formulated for dry and coarse hair. These products contain natural oils and ingredients that can help hydrate and soften your hair.

2. Use a deep conditioner

Deep conditioning is a crucial step in softening coarse African American hair. Look for a deep conditioner that contains natural oils, such as coconut oil, olive oil, or argan oil. Apply the deep conditioner to your hair once a week and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it off.

3. Limit the use of heat styling tools

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can damage your hair and make it even more coarse and dry. Limit the use of these tools and always use a heat protectant spray before applying heat to your hair.

4. Use natural oils

Natural oils, such as coconut oil, olive oil, and argan oil, can help soften your hair and add moisture. Apply a small amount of oil to your hair before styling or as a leave-in treatment. These oils can also help reduce breakage and improve the overall health of your hair.

5. Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as relaxers and dyes. These chemicals can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage. Instead, opt for natural hair care products that are gentle and free of harsh chemicals.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to soften coarse African American hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo that is specifically formulated for dry and coarse hair. Massage the shampoo into your scalp and hair, and rinse it off with lukewarm water.

Step 2: Apply a deep conditioner

Apply a deep conditioner to your hair once a week. Look for a product that contains natural oils, such as coconut oil, olive oil, or argan oil. Leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it off with lukewarm water.

Step 3: Use natural oils

Apply a small amount of natural oil to your hair before styling or as a leave-in treatment. Natural oils, such as coconut oil, olive oil, and argan oil, can help soften your hair and add moisture.

Step 4: Avoid heat styling tools

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as much as possible. If you must use these tools, always use a heat protectant spray before applying heat to your hair.

Tips for Softening Coarse African American Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you soften your hair:

  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using tight hairstyles, such as braids and weaves, as these can cause damage to your hair.

FAQs

Q: How often should I deep condition my hair?

A: It's recommended to deep condition your hair once a week to help soften and hydrate your hair.

Q: Can I use natural oils on my scalp?

A: Yes, you can use natural oils on your scalp. However, it's important to use a small amount and massage it into your scalp gently to avoid clogging your pores.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you maintain healthy and soft hair:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using harsh hair care products that contain sulfates and alcohol.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh environmental factors by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to provide your hair with the essential nutrients it needs to thrive.
  • Get enough sleep to reduce stress and promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and steps, you can soften your coarse African American hair and achieve the soft and silky hair you desire. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you'll see results in no time!
