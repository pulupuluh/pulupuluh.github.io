How To Protect Natural Hair From Cold Weather

Written by Eveline Jul 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Winter can be a tough season for those who have natural hair. The cold weather can make your hair dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. But don't worry, there are steps you can take to protect your natural hair from the harsh winter weather. In this article, we'll show you how to keep your hair healthy and moisturized all winter long.

How Does Cold Weather Affect Natural Hair?

Cold weather can be harmful to natural hair in a number of ways. The low humidity levels can cause the hair to become dry and brittle, which can lead to breakage. The cold air can also cause the hair cuticles to lift, making the hair more susceptible to damage. In addition, wearing hats, scarves, and other winter gear can cause friction and breakage, especially if the hair is not properly moisturized.

How to Protect Natural Hair from Cold Weather

The key to protecting natural hair from cold weather is to keep it moisturized. This means using products that are specifically designed for natural hair, and avoiding products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol, which can dry out the hair. It's also important to wear protective styles, such as braids or twists, to minimize the amount of exposure your hair has to the cold air. And, of course, wearing a hat or scarf can help to keep your hair protected from the elements.

Step by Step Guide to Protecting Your Natural Hair

Here are some steps you can take to protect your natural hair from cold weather:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  4. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can further dry out the hair.
  5. Wear protective styles, such as braids or twists, to minimize exposure to the cold air.
  6. Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the elements.
  7. Keep your hair covered at night with a satin scarf or bonnet to prevent breakage.
  8. Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated.
  9. Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip the hair of its natural oils.
  10. Use a hair oil or serum to seal in moisture and protect your hair from the cold air.

Tips for Protecting Your Natural Hair from Cold Weather

Here are some additional tips for protecting your natural hair from cold weather:

  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can further dry out the hair.
  • Use a humidifier to add moisture to the air in your home.
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or scarves, as they can cause friction and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the wind by wearing a hood or head wrap.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out the hair.

FAQs

What type of products should I use to protect my natural hair from cold weather?

You should use products that are specifically designed for natural hair, and that are moisturizing and free from harsh chemicals or alcohol. Look for products that contain natural oils, such as jojoba oil, coconut oil, or argan oil.

How often should I deep condition my hair in the winter?

You should deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.

What is the best way to protect my hair when wearing a hat or scarf?

You should wear a satin or silk scarf or bonnet underneath your hat or scarf to protect your hair from friction and breakage.

Hair Care in Winter

In addition to protecting your natural hair from cold weather, there are other steps you can take to keep your hair healthy and moisturized in the winter. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair less frequently to prevent it from drying out.
  • Use a hair oil or serum to seal in moisture and protect your hair from the cold air.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can further dry out the hair.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated.
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or scarves, as they can cause friction and breakage.
  • Use a humidifier to add moisture to the air in your home.

By following these tips and taking good care of your natural hair, you can keep it healthy and moisturized all winter long.
