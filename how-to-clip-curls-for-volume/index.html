How To Clip Curls For Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver May 22, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that sometimes your curls can fall flat and lose their volume. But don't worry, there's a simple solution to this problem: clipping your curls! In this blog post, we'll show you how to clip curls for volume in a relaxed English language. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips to get the bouncy curls you've always wanted.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Clip Curls for Volume
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Clipping Curls
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Clip Curls for Volume

Clipping your curls is a simple process that can help add volume and definition to your curls. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clips (duckbill or butterfly clips work best)
  • Leave-in conditioner or curl cream
  • Hair spray (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to clip your curls for volume:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair to help define your curls.
  2. Section your hair into small sections (about 1-2 inches wide). Use a clip to hold the rest of your hair out of the way.
  3. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly. This will help define your curl and make it easier to clip.
  4. Place the clip at the base of the twist, close to your scalp. Make sure the clip is secure but not too tight.
  5. Continue twisting and clipping small sections of hair until you've completed your whole head.
  6. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser to dry your curls. Do not remove the clips until your hair is completely dry.
  7. Once your hair is dry, remove the clips and gently shake out your curls. You should notice more volume and definition in your curls.
  8. Optional: If you want extra hold, spray your hair with a light hairspray.

Tips for Clipping Curls

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best results when clipping your curls:

  • Use clips that won't leave a crease in your hair, like duckbill or butterfly clips.
  • Don't twist your hair too tightly or you may damage your curls.
  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before removing the clips. Wet hair can cause your curls to fall flat.
  • Experiment with different section sizes to find what works best for your hair.
  • Clip your curls before bed and sleep with them in for extra hold and definition.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about clipping curls for volume:

Q: How long should I leave my hair clipped?

A: You should leave your hair clipped until it is completely dry. This could take several hours or overnight.

Q: Will clipping my curls damage my hair?

A: No, clipping your curls will not damage your hair as long as you don't twist your hair too tightly or use too much tension when clipping.

Q: Can I clip my curls every day?

A: Yes, you can clip your curls every day if you want. Just make sure to use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to keep your hair moisturized.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some general tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools (like flat irons or curling irons) too often, as they can damage your curls.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair.
  • Don't brush your hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they're wet.

Now that you know how to clip curls for volume, you can add some extra bounce and definition to your curls. Remember to take care of your hair and experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
