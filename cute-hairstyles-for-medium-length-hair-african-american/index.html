Cute Hairstyles For Medium Length Hair African American

Written by Luffy Jul 30, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Medium length hair is versatile and can be styled in different ways. African American women have unique hair texture and require hairstyles that suit their hair type. In this article, we will discuss some cute hairstyles for medium length hair African American that you can try out.

Table of Content

  1. Description
  2. How to Style
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Styling
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care

Description

Medium length hair African American is hair that falls between the chin and shoulders. This hair type is thick, curly, and requires a lot of moisture to prevent breakage. The cute hairstyles for medium length hair African American include bobs, pixies, braids, twists, and updos. These hairstyles are easy to maintain and can be worn to different occasions.

How to Style

The first step to styling medium length hair African American is to ensure that your hair is clean and moisturized. You can use a leave-in conditioner and a curl defining cream to enhance your curls. The next step is to detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. After detangling, you can proceed to style your hair using any of the hairstyles discussed below.

Bobs

Bobs are a classic hairstyle that never goes out of style. You can wear a bob with or without bangs depending on your preference. To style a bob, you can use a flat iron to straighten your hair or a curling iron to add some curls. You can also add some volume to your bob by teasing the roots of your hair.

Pixies

Pixies are short hairstyles that work well for medium length hair African American. Pixies are low maintenance and can be styled in different ways. You can add some texture to your pixie by using a styling gel or wax. You can also add some color to your pixie to make it more interesting.

Braids

Braids are a protective hairstyle that can be worn for a long time. You can style your hair in different types of braids including box braids, cornrows, and twists. Braids are versatile and can be styled in different ways. You can also add some accessories to your braids to make them more interesting.

Twists

Twists are a simple hairstyle that can be done at home. To style twists, you can divide your hair into small sections and twist each section. You can also add some extensions to your twists to make them longer. Twists are low maintenance and can be worn for a long time.

Updos

Updos are elegant hairstyles that work well for special occasions. You can style your hair in a bun, a chignon, or a French twist. Updos require some skill to style, and you may need the help of a hairstylist to achieve the perfect look.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to Style a Bob

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Blow-dry your hair
  3. Straighten your hair using a flat iron
  4. Tease the roots of your hair to add volume
  5. Apply some hairspray to hold the style in place

How to Style a Pixie

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Blow-dry your hair
  3. Apply some styling gel or wax to your hair
  4. Use your fingers to create texture in your hair
  5. Apply some hairspray to hold the style in place

How to Style Braids

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Divide your hair into small sections
  3. Braid each section using your preferred braid style
  4. Apply some oil to your scalp to moisturize it
  5. Add some accessories to your braids

How to Style Twists

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Divide your hair into small sections
  3. Twist each section using your fingers
  4. Apply some oil to your scalp to moisturize it
  5. Add some extensions to your twists if desired

How to Style an Updo

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Blow-dry your hair
  3. Create a bun, chignon, or French twist
  4. Secure your hair with bobby pins or a hair tie
  5. Apply some hairspray to hold the style in place

Tips for Styling

  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools
  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent breakage
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent hair breakage
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: You should wash your hair once a week or every two weeks to prevent dryness.

Q: How do I prevent breakage?

A: You can prevent breakage by moisturizing your hair regularly and avoiding tight hairstyles.

Q: Can I use extensions in my hair?

A: Yes, you can use extensions to add length and volume to your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy, you should moisturize it regularly and avoid using harsh chemicals. You should also trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends. If you are experiencing hair loss or other hair problems, you should consult a hairstylist or a dermatologist for advice.

Cute hairstyles for medium length hair African American can be fun and versatile. You can experiment with different styles and accessories to find the perfect look for you. With proper care, your hair can look healthy and beautiful.
