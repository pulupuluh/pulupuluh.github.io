How Can I Get Rid Of Brassy Hair At Home

Written by Frank Mar 05, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of dealing with brassy hair? Whether you have blonde, brown or red hair, it’s common to experience brassiness at some point. Brassy hair is caused by the oxidation process which occurs when your hair is exposed to sunlight or chemicals. Luckily, there are ways to get rid of brassy hair at home without spending a fortune at the salon. In this article, we’ll discuss some effective methods that you can use to get rid of brassy hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Rid of Brassy Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Brassy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products for Brassy Hair

How to Get Rid of Brassy Hair at Home

If you’re looking to get rid of brassy hair at home, you have a few options. Here are some methods that you can use:

1. Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a must-have for anyone with blonde hair. It contains purple pigments that neutralize the yellow and orange tones in your hair, leaving you with a cooler, more neutral tone. Simply swap out your regular shampoo for a purple shampoo and use it once or twice a week.

2. Apple Cider Vinegar Rinse

Apple cider vinegar is a natural hair clarifier that can help to remove buildup and restore your hair’s natural shine. Mix 1 part apple cider vinegar with 2 parts water and apply it to your hair after shampooing. Leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Baking Soda and Shampoo

Baking soda is a natural exfoliant that can help to remove buildup from your hair. Mix a tablespoon of baking soda with your shampoo and apply it to your hair. Leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

4. Lemon Juice

Lemon juice is a natural hair lightener that can help to remove brassiness from your hair. Squeeze some lemon juice into a spray bottle and spritz it onto your hair. Leave it on for a few hours before washing it out.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of brassy hair using purple shampoo:

  1. Wet your hair and apply a generous amount of purple shampoo.
  2. Massage the shampoo into your hair and scalp, focusing on the areas with the most brassiness.
  3. Leave the shampoo on for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  5. Follow up with a conditioner.
  6. Repeat once or twice a week, depending on how brassy your hair is.

Tips for Preventing Brassy Hair

Here are some tips for preventing brassiness in your hair:

  • Avoid overexposure to sunlight and chlorine.
  • Use a heat protectant when styling your hair.
  • Wash your hair with cool water instead of hot water.
  • Avoid using products that contain sulfates.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

FAQs

Q. Can I use purple shampoo on brown hair?

A. Yes, you can use purple shampoo on brown hair to neutralize any brassiness.

Q. How often should I use apple cider vinegar on my hair?

A. You should use apple cider vinegar on your hair once a week to remove buildup and restore shine.

Q. Will lemon juice damage my hair?

A. Lemon juice can be drying to your hair, so it’s important to dilute it with water before using it.

Hair Care Products for Brassy Hair

If you’re looking for hair care products to help combat brassiness, here are some options:

  • Purple shampoo
  • Blue shampoo (for brown hair with brassiness)
  • Color depositing conditioner
  • Hair toner
  • Hair mask

With these tips and tricks, you can say goodbye to brassy hair and hello to a beautiful, shiny mane. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid overexposure to sunlight and chemicals. With a little bit of effort, you can maintain healthy, beautiful hair all year round.
