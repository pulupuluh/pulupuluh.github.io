How To Cut Thinning Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel May 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

As men age, it's common for their hair to start thinning. And while some men choose to embrace their new look, others prefer to keep their hair as full as possible. If you're in the latter group, you may be wondering how to cut thinning men's hair without making it look even thinner. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps you need to take to give your client a great haircut that makes the most of their hair.

Description

Thinning hair can be a challenge to work with, but with the right techniques and tools, you can make it look fuller and more voluminous. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut thinning men's hair in a way that enhances its natural texture and creates the illusion of thickness. We'll cover everything from choosing the right haircut to using the right tools and products to style your client's hair.

How to Cut Thinning Men's Hair

Step 1: Choose the Right Haircut

The first step in cutting thinning men's hair is to choose the right haircut. A good haircut can make a big difference in how full and voluminous the hair looks. Generally, shorter haircuts work better for thinning hair because they create the illusion of thickness. However, you don't want to go too short or you'll end up emphasizing the thinning areas. A good rule of thumb is to keep the hair between 1 and 3 inches long, depending on your client's preferences.

Step 2: Use Thinners and Texturizers

Once you've chosen the right haircut, it's time to start cutting. To create the illusion of thickness, you'll want to use thinning shears and texturizing scissors. These tools can help you blend the hair and create a more natural, layered look. Be careful not to overdo it, though, or you'll end up making the hair look even thinner.

Step 3: Cut in Layers

One of the best ways to make thinning hair look fuller is to cut it in layers. Layered hair creates the illusion of thickness and volume, especially when it's styled properly. When cutting in layers, start at the top of the head and work your way down. Use your thinning shears and texturizing scissors to create a natural, layered look.

Step 4: Style the Hair

Once you've finished cutting the hair, it's time to style it. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to give the hair some extra lift, then blow dry it with a round brush. You can also use a styling product like mousse or gel to create more volume and hold. Be sure to use products that are designed for thinning hair, as they'll be gentler and less likely to damage the hair.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when cutting thinning men's hair:

  • Don't cut the hair too short or it will emphasize the thinning areas
  • Use thinning shears and texturizing scissors to create a more natural, layered look
  • Cut the hair in layers to create the illusion of thickness and volume
  • Use styling products that are designed for thinning hair
  • Be gentle when styling the hair to avoid damaging it further

FAQ

Q: Can you use clippers on thinning hair?

A: Yes, you can use clippers on thinning hair, but be careful not to cut the hair too short or you'll end up emphasizing the thinning areas. Clippers can be a good option for creating a more uniform look, but you'll still want to use thinning shears and texturizing scissors to create layers and texture.

Q: What's the best way to style thinning hair?

A: The best way to style thinning hair is to use products that are designed for thinning hair, like volumizing shampoos and conditioners. You can also use mousse or gel to create more volume and hold. Be gentle when styling the hair and avoid using heat tools too often, as they can damage the hair and make it look even thinner.

Q: How often should you cut thinning men's hair?

A: It's best to cut thinning men's hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking its best. Regular haircuts can help to remove split ends and keep the hair healthy, which can make it look fuller and more voluminous.

Hair Care

To keep thinning hair looking great, it's important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for thinning hair
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as they can damage the hair
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Take supplements like biotin and vitamin D to support hair health
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can put stress on the hair and cause breakage

By following these tips and using the right techniques and tools, you can create a great haircut for your clients with thinning hair. Remember to be gentle and take your time, and your clients will leave your chair with a fuller, more voluminous look.
