Is Short Hair Easier To Bleach

Written by Petter Mar 16, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're thinking about changing up your hair color, you might be wondering whether short hair is easier to bleach. Bleaching hair can be a tricky process, and the length of your hair can certainly affect how easy or difficult it is to achieve your desired results. In this article, we'll explore whether short hair is indeed easier to bleach, and offer some tips for achieving the perfect bleached look.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Bleach Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Bleaching Short Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Bleaching

Description

Short hair can be easier to bleach than longer hair, simply because there is less hair to work with. This means that the bleach can be applied more evenly and quickly, and there is less risk of missing spots or leaving bleach on for too long. However, shorter hair can also be more porous, which means it can absorb bleach more quickly and become damaged more easily. Additionally, short hair can be more prone to breakage, so it's important to take extra care when bleaching.

How to Bleach Short Hair

Bleaching short hair can be done at home or in a salon, depending on your comfort level and experience. If you're new to bleaching, it might be best to start with a professional stylist who can guide you through the process and ensure that your hair stays healthy. However, if you're feeling confident, here are some steps to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Choose a bleach kit that is specifically designed for short hair.
  2. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or product residue.
  3. Section your hair into four parts, using hair clips to keep each section separate.
  4. Mix the bleach according to the instructions on the kit.
  5. Starting at the back of your head, apply the bleach to your hair, making sure to cover each section evenly.
  6. Leave the bleach on for the recommended time (usually 20-30 minutes).
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.
  8. Apply a toner to neutralize any yellow or brassy tones.
  9. Rinse your hair again, and then condition it with a deep conditioner.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Bleaching Short Hair

Bleaching short hair can be a bit more challenging than bleaching longer hair, but there are some things you can do to make the process smoother and more successful:

  • Choose a bleach kit that is specifically designed for short hair, as these tend to be gentler and less damaging.
  • Don't leave the bleach on for too long, as short hair can become damaged more easily.
  • Use a toner to neutralize any yellow or brassy tones that may appear after bleaching.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly, as bleaching can be very drying and damaging.
  • Avoid heat styling your hair for a few days after bleaching, as this can cause further damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about bleaching short hair:

How long should I leave bleach on my short hair?

It depends on the bleach kit you're using, but most recommend leaving the bleach on for 20-30 minutes. Check the instructions on the kit to be sure.

Can I bleach my short hair at home?

Yes, you can bleach your short hair at home, but it's important to choose a kit that is specifically designed for short hair and to follow the instructions carefully.

Will bleaching my short hair damage it?

Bleaching can be very damaging to hair, especially if it is done incorrectly or too frequently. Short hair is more prone to breakage, so it's important to take extra care when bleaching.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your short hair, it's important to take extra care of it to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Deep condition your hair regularly.
  • Avoid heat styling your hair for a few days after bleaching.
  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can cause it to become dry and brittle.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

In conclusion, short hair can be easier to bleach than longer hair, but it's important to take extra care when bleaching short hair. Choose a kit that is specifically designed for short hair, follow the instructions carefully, and take extra care to deep condition and protect your hair after bleaching.
