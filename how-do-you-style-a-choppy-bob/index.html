How Do You Style A Choppy Bob

Written by Bardi Jul 29, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're looking for a low-maintenance, edgy haircut that's easy to style, a choppy bob might be right for you. This versatile cut can be worn straight, curled, or even messy, making it perfect for any occasion. But how do you style a choppy bob? In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect look.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Style a Choppy Bob
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Maintaining Your Choppy Bob
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Your Choppy Bob

How to Style a Choppy Bob

The key to styling a choppy bob is to keep it simple. The beauty of this haircut is its effortless, edgy vibe, so don't overthink it. You can wear it straight, wavy, or curly – it's up to you!

When it comes to styling products, less is more. You don't want to weigh down your hair or make it look greasy. A lightweight styling cream or mousse is all you need to add some texture and hold.

Step 1: Wash and Dry

Start with clean, towel-dried hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, then apply a dime-sized amount of styling cream or mousse to your palms. Rub your hands together to distribute the product evenly.

Step 2: Add Texture

Flip your head upside down and scrunch your hair with your hands, working from the ends to the roots. This will help create some texture and volume. If you want a more defined look, you can twist small sections of hair around your fingers as you scrunch.

Step 3: Style

Flip your head back up and use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. This will help enhance your natural texture and add some volume. Once your hair is dry, you can use a curling iron or flat iron to create some waves or curls, or you can leave it straight for a more casual look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling your choppy bob:

  1. Wash and towel-dry your hair.
  2. Apply a dime-sized amount of styling cream or mousse to your palms and rub them together.
  3. Flip your head upside down and scrunch your hair with your hands, working from the ends to the roots.
  4. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron or flat iron to create waves or curls, or leave it straight.
  6. Finish with a light-hold hairspray if desired.

Tips for Maintaining Your Choppy Bob

Here are a few tips to keep your choppy bob looking its best:

  • Get regular trims to keep your ends from looking too blunt.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to prevent damage.
  • To create a messy, bedhead look, use a salt spray on damp hair and let it air dry.
  • To add some shine, apply a small amount of hair oil to your ends.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about styling a choppy bob:

Q: Can I wear a choppy bob if I have curly hair?

A: Absolutely! A choppy bob can be worn by anyone, regardless of hair type. If you have curly hair, you can enhance your natural texture by using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer or applying a curl-enhancing cream.

Q: How often should I wash my choppy bob?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or work out frequently, you may need to wash your hair every day or every other day. If your hair is dry or you don't sweat much, you can probably get away with washing it every 2-3 days.

Q: Can I wear a choppy bob to a formal event?

A: Absolutely! A choppy bob can be dressed up or down, depending on the occasion. To make it more formal, you can add some curls or waves and tuck one side behind your ear.

Hair Care for Your Choppy Bob

To keep your choppy bob looking its best, be sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type. If you have oily hair, use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup. If your hair is dry, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.

When styling your hair, be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle, and avoid using a brush on wet hair, as it can cause breakage.

Finally, protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools. And don't forget to get regular trims to keep your ends looking fresh!
