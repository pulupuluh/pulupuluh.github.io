How Can I Check If A Hairstyle Suits Me Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jun 28, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you tired of trying new hairstyles that always look great on someone else but never seem to suit you? Fret not, because we've got you covered. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how you can check if a hairstyle suits you. From face shape to hair texture, we'll cover everything you need to know to find your perfect hairstyle. So, let's get started.

Know Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the right hairstyle for you is to know your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Each face shape has unique features and requires a specific type of hairstyle to balance them out. For instance, if you have a round face, you should go for a hairstyle that adds height and length to your face, such as a long bob or high ponytail.

Here are the hairstyles that suit each face shape:

  • Round face: Long bob, high ponytail, side-swept bangs
  • Oval face: Pixie cut, blunt bob, middle parting
  • Square face: Soft curls, layered bob, side parting
  • Heart face: Side-swept bangs, chin-length bob, messy updo
  • Diamond face: Pixie cut, side-swept bangs, chin-length bob
  • Oblong face: Long layers, side-swept bangs, messy bun

Consider Your Hair Texture

Your hair texture is another important factor to consider when choosing a hairstyle. If you have fine hair, you should opt for a hairstyle that adds volume and texture, such as a layered bob or beachy waves. On the other hand, if you have thick hair, you should go for a hairstyle that thins out your hair, such as a blunt cut or a pixie cut.

Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle also plays a crucial role in determining your hairstyle. If you have a busy schedule and don't have much time to style your hair, you should go for a low-maintenance hairstyle, such as a messy bun or a ponytail. If you have an active lifestyle and love to exercise, you should go for a hairstyle that stays in place, such as a braided updo or a top knot.

Try On Haircuts Virtually

If you're not sure which hairstyle suits you, you can try on haircuts virtually before committing to one. There are many online tools and apps that allow you to upload your photo and try on different hairstyles. This can give you a good idea of how a particular hairstyle will look on you before you get it done.

Consult with a Hairstylist

If you're still unsure which hairstyle suits you, you can always consult with a hairstylist. They can analyze your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle and recommend a hairstyle that suits you best. They can also suggest the right hair color that complements your skin tone and eye color.

Consider Maintenance

Before getting a new haircut, you should consider how much maintenance it requires. If you're not willing to spend a lot of time and money on styling your hair, you should go for a low-maintenance hairstyle that requires minimal upkeep. On the other hand, if you love to experiment with different hairstyles and don't mind spending time and money on them, you can go for a high-maintenance hairstyle.

Experiment with Hair Color

Changing your hair color can also make a big difference in how a hairstyle suits you. If you have warm skin tones, you should go for warm hair colors, such as golden blonde or auburn. If you have cool skin tones, you should go for cool hair colors, such as ash blonde or silver. You can also experiment with highlights and lowlights to add depth and dimension to your hair.

Take Care of Your Hair

Lastly, taking care of your hair is essential to maintaining a hairstyle that suits you. You should use the right hair products for your hair type, such as shampoo, conditioner, and styling products. You should also avoid using heating tools too often and protect your hair from the sun and pollution. Regular trims and hair treatments can also keep your hair healthy and shiny.

FAQs

1. How do I know my face shape?

You can determine your face shape by measuring the width and length of your face and looking at the proportions. You can also use a face shape calculator online.

2. Can I change my face shape with a hairstyle?

You can't change your face shape, but you can create an illusion of a different face shape with a hairstyle that complements your features.

3. Should I go for a hairstyle that's trending?

You can go for a trending hairstyle if it suits your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle, but you shouldn't choose a hairstyle just because it's popular.

Conclusion

Choosing a hairstyle that suits you can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. By considering your face shape, hair texture, lifestyle, maintenance, and hair color, you can find a hairstyle that enhances your features and makes you feel confident. Don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles and consult with a hairstylist if you're not sure which one suits you best. Remember, your hair is your crowning glory, so take care of it and wear it with pride.
