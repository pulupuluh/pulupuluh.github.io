How to Part Hair for Diamond Shaped Face

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's first discuss what a diamond shaped face looks like. This face shape has a narrow forehead, prominent cheekbones, and a pointed chin. The goal of parting your hair for this face shape is to create balance and soften the angles.

The best way to do this is to part your hair off-center, creating a diagonal line that starts at the center of your forehead and ends at the crown of your head. This will create the illusion of a wider forehead and a softer jawline.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by brushing your hair straight back to get rid of any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Using a tail comb, create a diagonal part starting at the center of your forehead and ending at the crown of your head. The part should be slightly off-center.

Step 3: Use clips to secure the hair on both sides of the part.

Step 4: Style your hair as desired. You can wear it down or put it up in a ponytail or bun.

Tips for Parting Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when parting your hair:

  • Experiment with different parting positions to find the one that flatters your face shape the most.
  • Use a tail comb to create a precise part.
  • Apply a small amount of hair gel or hairspray to keep your hair in place.
  • Accessorize with a headband or hair clip to add some flair to your hairstyle.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I still part my hair in the middle if I have a diamond shaped face?

A: While an off-center part is generally more flattering for this face shape, you can still part your hair in the middle if you prefer. Just make sure to add some volume to the top of your hair to balance out your cheekbones.

Q: Should I part my hair on the left or right side?

A: It depends on which side of your face you want to emphasize. If you want to draw attention to your left cheekbone, part your hair on the right side. If you want to highlight your right cheekbone, part your hair on the left side.

Hair Care for Diamond Shaped Face

Aside from parting your hair, there are other ways to flatter your diamond shaped face through your hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Opt for hairstyles that add volume to the top of your head, such as a layered haircut or a volumizing mousse.
  • Avoid hairstyles that are too slicked back or flat, as they can accentuate your angular features.
  • Consider adding bangs to soften your forehead.
  • Experiment with different hair lengths to find the one that works best for you.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be able to find the perfect hair part for your diamond shaped face and create a hairstyle that flatters your unique features.
