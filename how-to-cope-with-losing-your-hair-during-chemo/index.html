How To Cope With Losing Your Hair During Chemo

Written by Frank Jul 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

For cancer patients undergoing chemotherapy, hair loss is a common side effect that can be emotionally and mentally challenging. Losing hair can be a visible sign of illness and can impact self-esteem and body image. Coping with hair loss during chemotherapy can be difficult, but there are strategies and tips that can help. In this article, we will discuss how to cope with losing your hair during chemo, including how to prepare for hair loss, hair care tips, and ways to boost self-confidence.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Prepare for Hair Loss
  2. Hair Care Tips During Chemo
  3. Boosting Self-Confidence
  4. FAQ

How to Prepare for Hair Loss

It is important to prepare for hair loss before it starts. Talk to your doctor or nurse about when you can expect to start losing hair and how much hair loss you should expect. This can help you mentally prepare for the changes to come.

You may want to consider cutting your hair short or shaving your head before hair loss starts. This can help you feel more in control of the situation and make the transition less abrupt. You may also want to experiment with wigs, hats, scarves, or other head coverings before hair loss starts to find what makes you feel comfortable.

It is also important to take care of your scalp during chemotherapy. Use gentle shampoos and avoid harsh chemicals or treatments that can irritate the scalp. You may also want to consider using a scalp cooling system during chemotherapy, which can help reduce hair loss.

Hair Care Tips During Chemo

When hair loss starts, it is important to take care of your scalp and any remaining hair. Use a gentle shampoo and avoid using hot tools or harsh chemicals on your hair. You may also want to consider using a satin pillowcase or head covering to reduce hair breakage.

Scalp massages can also be helpful during this time. Massaging your scalp can increase blood flow and help promote hair growth. You may also want to consider using natural oils such as coconut or jojoba oil to moisturize your scalp.

Step-by-Step Guide to Scalp Massage:

  1. Sit comfortably in a chair or on a bed.
  2. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp in circular motions.
  3. Apply gentle pressure and move your fingers in a clockwise direction.
  4. Continue massaging for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Rinse your scalp with warm water and a gentle shampoo.

Boosting Self-Confidence

Losing hair can be emotionally challenging, but there are ways to boost self-confidence during this time. Consider experimenting with different head coverings such as hats, scarves, or wigs. You may also want to try different makeup looks or accessories to draw attention away from your hair.

Remember that hair loss is temporary and that your hair will likely grow back after chemotherapy ends. Focus on self-care and doing things that make you feel good about yourself. This can include exercise, spending time with loved ones, or pursuing a hobby.

FAQ

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

A: Yes, hair loss during chemotherapy is usually temporary and hair will typically grow back after treatment ends.

Q: Is it safe to color or perm my hair during chemotherapy?

A: It is not recommended to color or perm your hair during chemotherapy, as these treatments can damage hair that is already weakened by chemotherapy.

Q: Can scalp cooling systems reduce hair loss during chemotherapy?

A: Yes, scalp cooling systems can help reduce hair loss during chemotherapy by reducing blood flow to the scalp and therefore reducing the amount of chemotherapy that reaches the hair follicles.

Coping with hair loss during chemotherapy can be challenging, but there are strategies and tips that can help make the process easier. By preparing for hair loss, taking care of your scalp and remaining hair, and focusing on self-care and self-confidence, you can feel more in control during this difficult time.
