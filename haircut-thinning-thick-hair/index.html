Haircut Thinning Thick Hair How To Get The Perfect Cut

Written by Christine Apr 29, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have thick hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. While it's great for creating voluminous hairstyles, it can also be a challenge to manage. One way to make your thick hair more manageable is by getting a haircut that thins out the bulk. In this blog post, we'll show you how to get the perfect haircut for thinning thick hair.

Description

Thick hair can be difficult to manage, especially if you have a lot of it. It can also be heavy and uncomfortable, especially during the summer months. By getting a haircut that thins out the bulk, you can make your hair more manageable and comfortable.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair

Thinning out thick hair is a process that should be done by a professional hairstylist. Here are the steps they will typically follow:

Step 1: Consultation

The hairstylist will start by consulting with you about the look you want to achieve. They will ask questions about the length, style, and overall appearance you're going for.

Step 2: Sectioning

The hairstylist will then section your hair and clip it up. This allows them to work on one section at a time, ensuring that each section is thinned out evenly.

Step 3: Thinning Shears

The hairstylist will use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk from each section. Thinning shears are specialized scissors that have teeth or notches on one blade. When the blade is closed, the teeth or notches remove some of the hair, leaving the remaining hair thinner and more manageable.

Step 4: Blending

Once each section has been thinned out, the hairstylist will use regular scissors to blend the layers together. This creates a seamless transition from the thinned-out sections to the rest of your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Thinned-Out Hair

Once you've gotten your haircut, there are several things you can do to maintain the look:

  • Use a lightweight shampoo and conditioner to prevent weighing down your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can make your hair look greasy and flat.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush designed for thick hair to prevent breakage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.

FAQ

Q: Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

A: Thinning out your hair will remove some of the bulk, but it won't necessarily make your hair look thinner overall. The goal is to make your hair more manageable, not to make it look less voluminous.

Q: Will thinning out my hair damage it?

A: Thinning out your hair shouldn't cause any damage if it's done correctly by a professional hairstylist. However, if the hairstylist uses the wrong technique or cuts too much hair, it could lead to damage.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

By following these tips and getting a haircut that thins out your hair, you can enjoy healthy, manageable, and voluminous locks.
