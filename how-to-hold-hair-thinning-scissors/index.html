How To Hold Hair Thinning Scissors - Tips, Steps & FAQ

Thinning scissors are an essential tool for hairstylists who want to create texture, volume, and shape in their clients' hair. However, it's important to know how to hold hair thinning scissors properly to avoid any accidents or unwanted results. In this guide, we'll show you the right way to hold hair thinning scissors, tips for hair care, and answer some frequently asked questions.

How To Hold Hair Thinning Scissors

Before we dive into the steps, let's first discuss the different types of hair thinning scissors. There are two main types: the single-bladed and the double-bladed. The single-bladed is ideal for light to medium hair, while the double-bladed is perfect for thick and coarse hair.

Step 1: Choose The Right Scissors

As mentioned, the type of scissors you use will depend on the type of hair you're thinning out. Make sure you have the right scissors on hand before starting.

Step 2: Hold The Scissors Properly

Hold the scissors with your thumb in one hole and your middle and ring fingers in the other. Your index finger should be on top of the scissors, resting on the tang, while your pinky should be tucked into your palm.

Step 3: Position The Scissors

Position the scissors so that the blades are pointing upwards and away from the hair. This will help you avoid accidentally cutting too much hair.

Step 4: Begin Thinning The Hair

Start thinning the hair by taking small sections and running the scissors through the hair in a zigzag motion. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can create an uneven look.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After thinning out a section of hair, comb through it to check your progress. If necessary, go back and thin out any areas that still look too thick.

Tips For Hair Care

Now that you know how to hold hair thinning scissors, here are some tips to keep in mind when using them:

  • Never thin out wet hair, as this can cause the hair to look uneven when it dries.
  • Be gentle when using thinning scissors, as they can easily damage the hair if used too aggressively.
  • Always start with small sections of hair, and work your way up to larger sections as you become more comfortable.
  • Make sure to clean and sanitize your scissors after each use to avoid any infections or bacteria.

FAQ

Q: Can I Use Thinning Scissors On My Own Hair?

A: While it's possible to use thinning scissors on your own hair, it's not recommended. It's best to have a professional hairstylist do it for you to avoid any mistakes or accidents.

Q: How Often Should I Thin Out My Hair?

A: It really depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and texture.

Q: Do Thinning Scissors Damage Hair?

A: Thinning scissors can damage hair if used too aggressively or if the hair is already weak and brittle. It's important to use them gently and only on healthy hair.

Now that you know how to hold hair thinning scissors properly, you can create beautiful, textured hairstyles for your clients with confidence. Remember to always prioritize hair care and follow the tips and steps we've outlined in this guide.
