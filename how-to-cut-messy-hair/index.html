How To Cut Messy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 02, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your messy hair? Do you want to know how to cut it yourself? Cutting your own hair may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a breeze. In this article, we'll show you how to cut messy hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. We'll cover everything from the tools you'll need to the step-by-step process, tips, FAQs, and hair care. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • How to Cut Messy Hair
  • Tips for Cutting Messy Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to have the right tools. Here are the tools you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair ties (optional)
  • Water spray bottle (optional)

It's essential to have sharp scissors because dull ones will damage your hair and make it look uneven. The comb will help you section your hair, and the clippers can help you trim the sides and back of your head. Hair ties and a water spray bottle are optional but can be helpful when cutting long hair.

How to Cut Messy Hair

Now that you have the tools, it's time to start cutting your messy hair. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to do it:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, wash and dry it thoroughly. Clean hair is easier to cut and will give you a better idea of how it will look after you're done. If you have curly or wavy hair, let it dry naturally, so you can see the natural texture.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use a comb to make clean parts and secure each section with hair ties or clips. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 3: Cut the Top Section

Start with the top section. Use your comb to lift the hair straight up and cut it straight across. You can use your fingers as a guide to keep the length consistent. If you want to add layers, cut the top layer shorter than the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Side Sections

Next, move on to the side sections. Comb the hair straight down and cut it to your desired length. If you want a layered look, cut the top layer shorter than the bottom layer.

Step 5: Cut the Back Section

Finally, move on to the back section. Use a hand mirror to see the back of your head and cut the hair to your desired length. You can also use clippers to trim the sides and back of your head for a more even look.

Tips for Cutting Messy Hair

Here are some tips to make cutting your messy hair easier:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Use a water spray bottle to dampen your hair if it's too dry or frizzy.
  • Trim your hair every four to six weeks to maintain the length and shape.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your hair, start with small trims and work your way up to a full cut.
  • If you want a drastic change, it's best to go to a professional stylist.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about cutting messy hair:

Can I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

You can cut your hair when it's wet or dry, but wet hair is easier to cut because it's more manageable. If you're cutting curly or wavy hair, it's best to cut it when it's dry, so you can see the natural texture.

How do I cut layers in my hair?

To cut layers in your hair, lift the top layer and cut it shorter than the bottom layer. If you want more layers, repeat the process on the next layer until you achieve your desired look.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim more hair to even it out. If it's a significant mistake, go to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's essential to take care of it to maintain the length and shape. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling wands, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair every four to six weeks to maintain the length and shape.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Cutting your messy hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. Remember to start with small cuts and work your way up, and if you're not confident, it's always best to go to a professional stylist. Happy cutting!
