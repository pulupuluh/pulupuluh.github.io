How To Style Thick Coarse Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Aug 24, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have thick, coarse, and curly hair, you know that styling it can be quite the challenge. But don’t worry, you don’t have to settle for a frizzy mess or a boring ponytail. With the right products and techniques, you can transform your hair into beautiful, defined curls. In this blog post, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to style thick coarse curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Style Thick Coarse Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thick Coarse Curly Hair

How to Style Thick Coarse Curly Hair

The first step in styling thick, coarse, curly hair is to choose the right products. Look for products that are specifically formulated for curly hair, such as curl-defining creams, gels, and mousses. These products will help define your curls and reduce frizz.

Another important factor in styling curly hair is to avoid brushing or combing it when it’s dry. This can cause your curls to become frizzy and lose their shape. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it’s wet.

When styling your hair, try to avoid using heat tools as much as possible. Heat can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and set your tools to a low or medium heat setting.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to style thick, coarse, curly hair:

  1. Start with clean, wet hair. Apply a curl-defining cream or mousse to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use clips to hold each section in place.
  3. Take a small section of hair and apply a curl-enhancing product, such as a gel or mousse.
  4. Twist the section of hair around your finger to define the curl. Repeat with each section of hair.
  5. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.
  6. Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to gently separate and fluff your curls.
  7. Finish with a hairspray or finishing spray to hold your curls in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for styling thick, coarse, curly hair:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Try not to wash your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.
  • When applying products to your hair, use a praying hands technique to distribute the product evenly.
  • Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.

FAQs

Q: How can I reduce frizz in my curly hair?

A: To reduce frizz in curly hair, avoid brushing or combing it when it’s dry, use a satin or silk pillowcase, and use products specifically formulated for curly hair.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and set your tools to a low or medium heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care for Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Finally, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful. Here are some hair care tips for thick, coarse, curly hair:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using shampoos that contain sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help detangle your hair and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using heat tools as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you can style your thick, coarse, curly hair with confidence. Don’t be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair. With a little patience and practice, you can rock beautiful, defined curls!
