How To Cut Your Own Short Hair In Layers Step By Step

Written by Bardi May 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on getting your hair cut every few weeks? Why not try cutting your own hair in layers? It may sound daunting, but with the right tools and a little guidance, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps to cutting your own short hair in layers. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. How To
  2. Step by Step
  3. Tips
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care

How To

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Scissors (preferably hair cutting scissors)
  • A comb
  • Clips to section off your hair
  • A mirror

It's important to note that cutting your own hair in layers can be a bit tricky, especially if you've never done it before. It's always a good idea to start with a simple cut and work your way up to more complicated styles.

Step by Step

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it. You want your hair to be clean and free of any products. Once your hair is dry, comb it out so it's free of tangles.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using clips, section off your hair into manageable sections. Start with the bottom section and work your way up. This will make it easier to cut your hair in layers.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Using your scissors, cut straight across. Make sure to keep your scissors parallel to the floor to ensure an even cut.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you've cut the bottom layer, release the next section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers and cut at an angle. This will create a layered effect.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, release the top layer of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers and cut at an angle, just like you did with the middle layer.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections of your hair, it's time to blend the layers. Comb your hair straight down and use your scissors to blend any uneven areas.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you're done cutting, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure your hair is even and the layers are blended.

Tips

  • Always use hair cutting scissors. Regular scissors can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.
  • Start with a simple cut and work your way up to more complicated styles.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting your own hair in layers can be tricky, but it's worth it in the end.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can stretch, and you may end up cutting off more than you intended.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair in layers if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair in layers if you have curly hair. However, it may be more difficult to achieve an even cut. Consider seeking the help of a professional if you're unsure.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always make adjustments or seek the help of a professional to fix any uneven areas.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny. Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and always use a heat protectant when you do.

Cutting your own short hair in layers can be a great way to save money and achieve a professional-looking haircut. With the right tools and a little guidance, you can do it yourself from the comfort of your own home.
