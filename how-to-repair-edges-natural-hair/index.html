How To Repair Edges Natural Hair Tips Faq And Hair Care

Written by Bardi Apr 12, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you experiencing thinning or balding edges? You’re not alone. Many women, especially those with natural hair, face this issue. The good news is that there are ways to repair edges natural hair. In this article, we’ll share some tips, step-by-step instructions, and hair care advice to help you regain your edges.

Table of Contents

  • Step 2: Change Your Hairstyle
  • Step 3: Use Natural Oils and Ingredients
  • Step 4: Massage Your Scalp
  • Step 5: Take Supplements
  • FAQ

Step 1: Determine the Cause of Your Thinning Edges

Thinning edges can be caused by a variety of factors, such as tight hairstyles, heat damage, genetics, hormonal changes, and health conditions. It’s important to identify the cause of your thinning edges before starting any treatment. This will help you determine the best course of action.

Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids, weaves, and ponytails, can cause tension on your edges and lead to hair loss. If you wear these styles frequently, consider giving your edges a break and opting for looser styles.

Heat Damage

Using heat tools, such as flat irons and blow dryers, can damage your hair and cause breakage. If you must use heat, make sure to use a heat protectant and keep the temperature low.

Genetics

Genetics can play a role in thinning edges. If your family members also have thinning edges, it may be hereditary.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes, such as pregnancy and menopause, can cause hair loss and thinning edges. Talk to your doctor if you suspect hormonal changes are the cause of your thinning edges.

Health Conditions

Health conditions, such as alopecia and thyroid disorders, can cause hair loss. If you suspect a health condition is the cause of your thinning edges, consult a doctor.

Step 2: Change Your Hairstyle

If tight hairstyles are the cause of your thinning edges, it’s important to change your hairstyle. Opt for looser styles, such as twists or bantu knots. Avoid styles that pull on your edges, such as high ponytails or cornrows.

Step 3: Use Natural Oils and Ingredients

Using natural oils and ingredients can help repair your edges. Some popular options include castor oil, peppermint oil, aloe vera, and onion juice. These ingredients can help promote hair growth and strengthen your hair follicles.

Step 4: Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help increase blood flow to your hair follicles and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

Step 5: Take Supplements

Taking supplements, such as biotin and vitamin D, can help promote hair growth and strengthen your hair. Talk to your doctor before starting any new supplements.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to repair thinning edges?

A: It can take several months to a year to see significant improvement in your thinning edges. Consistency is key.

Q: Can I use hair growth products to repair my edges?

A: Hair growth products, such as Rogaine, may be effective in promoting hair growth on your edges. However, it’s important to talk to your doctor before using any new products.

Q: Can I wear wigs and weaves while repairing my edges?

A: Yes, you can wear wigs and weaves while repairing your edges. However, make sure to avoid tight styles that can pull on your edges.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help repair your edges:

  • Avoid using heat tools too frequently
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin or silk scarf
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients

With these tips and steps, you can repair your edges natural hair and regain your confidence. Remember to be patient and consistent in your hair care routine.
