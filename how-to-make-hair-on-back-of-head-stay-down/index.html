How To Make Hair On Back Of Head Stay Down A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Mar 14, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you struggling with unruly hair on the back of your head? Do you find yourself constantly trying to smooth it down, only to have it pop back up again? It can be frustrating, but don't worry - we've got you covered. In this article, we'll show you how to make hair on the back of your head stay down, using simple tips and techniques that anyone can follow. So let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tip 1: Use the Right Products
  • Tip 2: Blow-Dry Your Hair
  • Tip 3: Use a Flat Iron
  • Tip 4: Try a Hair Gel or Wax
  • Tip 5: Use a Hair Spray
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Tip 1: Use the Right Products

The first step to making your hair stay down is to use the right products. Look for a styling product that is designed to hold hair in place, such as a pomade or hair wax. These products are designed to add texture and grip to your hair, which will help it stay in place. Apply the product to the back of your head, working it in with your fingers until your hair feels evenly coated.

Another option is to use a hair gel, which can help to tame flyaways and keep your hair in place. However, be careful not to use too much, as this can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.

Tip 2: Blow-Dry Your Hair

Blow-drying your hair can also help to keep it in place. Use a round brush to smooth your hair down as you dry it, directing the airflow from the dryer downwards towards the back of your head. This will help to set your hair in place and create a sleek, smooth look.

If you have particularly stubborn hair, try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. This will help to distribute the heat more evenly, which can help to prevent frizz and flyaways.

Tip 3: Use a Flat Iron

If your hair is very curly or wavy, a flat iron can be a great tool for smoothing it down. Use the flat iron to straighten your hair, working in small sections and pulling the iron downwards towards the back of your head. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using the flat iron, as this will help to prevent damage to your hair.

Tip 4: Try a Hair Gel or Wax

If you're still struggling to keep your hair down, try using a hair gel or wax. Look for a product that is designed to provide a strong hold, and apply it to the back of your head, working it in with your fingers. This will help to keep your hair in place throughout the day, even in windy or humid conditions.

Tip 5: Use a Hair Spray

Finally, if all else fails, a hair spray can be a great tool for keeping your hair in place. Look for a hairspray that provides a strong hold, and spray it onto the back of your head from a distance of about 6-8 inches. Be sure to use the hairspray sparingly, as too much can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or work out frequently, you may need to wash your hair every day. However, if your hair is dry or curly, you may be able to go longer between washes. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, and use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy.

Q: Can I use hairspray every day?

A: While hairspray can be a great tool for keeping your hair in place, using it every day can be damaging to your hair. Hairspray contains alcohol, which can dry out your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try to use hairspray sparingly, and opt for a high-quality, alcohol-free formula.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage

By following these tips and techniques, you can make your hair on the back of your head stay down and achieve the sleek, stylish look you're after. So give them a try, and see what works best for you!
