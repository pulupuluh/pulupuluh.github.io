How To Make Your Hair Not Frizzy After You Straighten It

Written by Oliver Jul 14, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of spending hours straightening your hair, only to end up with frizzy locks just a few hours later? Don't worry, you're not alone. Frizzy hair after straightening can be frustrating, but there are ways to combat it. In this article, we'll give you some tips on how to make your hair not frizzy after you straighten it.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The key to preventing frizz after straightening your hair is to keep it hydrated and protected. Here are some tips:

Use a Heat Protectant

Before you start straightening your hair, make sure you apply a heat protectant spray or cream. This will help prevent damage to your hair and keep it looking smooth and shiny. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Don't Straighten Wet Hair

Straightening wet hair can cause damage and frizz. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start straightening it. Use a blow dryer to dry your hair completely or let it air dry naturally.

Use the Right Straightener

Using the right straightener can make a big difference in preventing frizz. Look for a straightener with ceramic or tourmaline plates, which will help distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz. Avoid using metal plates, as they can cause damage and make your hair more prone to frizz.

Step by Step

Now that you know the basics, here's a step-by-step guide on how to make your hair not frizzy after you straighten it:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray or cream.
  3. Blow dry your hair completely or let it air dry naturally.
  4. Section your hair and start straightening it with a ceramic or tourmaline straightener.
  5. Work in small sections and make sure you don't go over the same section multiple times.
  6. Once you've straightened all of your hair, apply a smoothing serum or oil to the ends.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent frizz:

  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis, as this can cause damage and make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

FAQ

Q: How can I prevent frizz if I have naturally curly hair?

A: If you have naturally curly hair, it can be more prone to frizz. To prevent frizz after straightening, make sure you use a straightener with ceramic or tourmaline plates and apply a smoothing serum or oil to the ends of your hair.

Q: Can I use a leave-in conditioner to prevent frizz?

A: Yes, a leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz. Look for a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair healthy is key to preventing frizz. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers.
  • Get regular haircuts to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using tight hair ties or hairstyles that pull on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can prevent frizz and keep your locks looking smooth and shiny.
