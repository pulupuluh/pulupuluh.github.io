1960 Hairstyles Information A Guide To Classic Hairdos

Welcome to our guide on 1960 hairstyles information! The 1960s were a time of change and innovation in fashion and beauty, and hairstyles were no exception. From the iconic beehive to the sleek and sophisticated bob, there are many classic hairstyles from this era that are still popular today. In this article, we will explore the different types of 1960 hairstyles, how to create them, and tips for maintaining them.

Table of Contents

  • Types of 1960 Hairstyles
  • How to Create 1960 Hairstyles
  • Tips for Maintaining 1960 Hairstyles

Types of 1960 Hairstyles

The 1960s saw a variety of hairstyles that were both glamorous and practical. Here are some of the most popular styles:

Beehive

The beehive is perhaps the most iconic hairstyle of the 1960s, popularized by celebrities like Audrey Hepburn and Brigitte Bardot. This voluminous updo is created by teasing the hair at the crown of the head and pinning it into place, creating a dome-like shape.

Bouffant

The bouffant is another popular updo from the 1960s, characterized by its high volume and backcombed crown. This style is often paired with a headband or scarf for added glamour.

Bob

The bob is a classic hairstyle that has been popular for decades, but it had a resurgence of popularity in the 1960s. This short, sleek style is characterized by its blunt cut and straight lines.

Flip

The flip is a playful hairstyle that was popularized by actress Mary Tyler Moore. This style is created by flipping the ends of the hair outward, giving it a bouncy, retro look.

Twiggy Cut

The Twiggy cut is a short, boyish hairstyle that was made popular by the famous model Twiggy. This style is characterized by its short, choppy layers and side-swept bangs.

How to Create 1960 Hairstyles

Creating a 1960s hairstyle may seem daunting, but with a little practice and the right tools, it can be easy to achieve. Here are some steps to follow:

Beehive

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Tease the hair at the crown of the head using a fine-tooth comb.
  3. Gently smooth the teased section with a soft-bristled brush, shaping it into a dome-like shape.
  4. Secure the hair in place with bobby pins or hair clips.
  5. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Bouffant

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Backcomb the hair at the crown of the head using a fine-tooth comb.
  3. Gently smooth the backcombed section with a soft-bristled brush, creating a high, voluminous shape.
  4. Secure the hair in place with bobby pins or hair clips.
  5. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Bob

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use a straightening iron to create a sleek, straight look.
  3. Cut the hair into a blunt, straight line.
  4. Style with a center or side part, depending on your preference.
  5. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Flip

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use a round brush to blow-dry the hair, flipping the ends outward as you go.
  3. Use a straightening iron to touch up any areas that need smoothing.
  4. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Twiggy Cut

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Cut the hair into short, choppy layers.
  3. Create side-swept bangs by cutting the hair at an angle.
  4. Style the hair using a small amount of pomade or wax, focusing on the ends for a choppy, textured look.
  5. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Maintaining 1960 Hairstyles

Once you've created your 1960s hairstyle, there are some tips to follow to keep it looking great:

  • Use a hairspray or styling product to hold the style in place.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it harder to style.
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.
  • Invest in a high-quality hairbrush and comb to keep your hair looking smooth and tangle-free.
  • Consider using heat protectant products when using hot tools like straightening irons or curling wands.

FAQs About 1960 Hairstyles

What products were popular for styling hair in the 1960s?

Hairspray and mousse were popular styling products in the 1960s, as they helped to hold hairstyles in place and add volume.

Were hair accessories popular in the 1960s?

Yes, hair accessories like headbands, scarves, and hairpins were popular in the 1960s and often used to add a touch of glamour to hairstyles.

What celebrities popularized 1960 hairstyles?

Celebrities like Audrey Hepburn, Brigitte Bardot, and Mary Tyler Moore were known for their iconic 1960s hairstyles.

Hair Care Tips for 1960 Hairstyles

When it comes to caring for 1960s hairstyles, there are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can damage it over time.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach or hair dye, as these can cause damage and breakage.
  • Be gentle when brushing and styling your hair, especially if you have a beehive or bouffant that requires teasing.

We hope that this guide on 1960 hairstyles information has been helpful! Whether you're looking to recreate an iconic hairstyle from this era or simply want to add a retro touch to your look, there are many options to choose from. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a classic, timeless look that never goes out of style.
