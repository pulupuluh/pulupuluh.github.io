How To Fade An Undercut Step By Step

Written by Petter Sep 09, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to give your undercut a fresh look, fading is a great way to do it. Fading is a technique that involves gradually cutting the hair shorter from the top to the bottom. This creates a smooth transition from the longer hair on top to the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair
  • Step 2: Determine the Length of Your Undercut
  • Step 3: Use Clippers to Create the Fade
  • Step 4: Blend the Fade with Scissors
  • Step 5: Clean Up the Neckline and Sideburns
  • Tips for Fading an Undercut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Undercut

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start fading your undercut, make sure that your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that the cut is even.

Step 2: Determine the Length of Your Undercut

Decide on the length of your undercut. This will determine where you start the fade. If you want a shorter undercut, you can start the fade higher up on the sides and back. If you want a longer undercut, you can start the fade lower down.

Step 3: Use Clippers to Create the Fade

Start with the longest guard on your clippers and work your way down to the shortest guard. Use the clippers to create a fade from the longer hair on top to the shorter hair on the sides and back. Start at the bottom and work your way up, making sure to blend the hair as you go.

Step 4: Blend the Fade with Scissors

Once you've created the basic fade with clippers, use scissors to blend the hair even more. Use the scissors to remove any obvious lines and to create a smooth transition between the different lengths of hair.

Step 5: Clean Up the Neckline and Sideburns

Use a trimmer to clean up the neckline and sideburns. This will give your undercut a clean and polished look.

Tips for Fading an Undercut

  • Start with the longest guard on your clippers and work your way down gradually.
  • Make sure to blend the hair as you go to create a smooth transition.
  • Use scissors to remove any obvious lines and to create a more natural look.
  • Use a trimmer to clean up the neckline and sideburns for a polished finish.

FAQs

How often should I get my undercut faded?

You should get your undercut faded every 2-3 weeks to maintain the look.

What if I mess up the fade?

If you mess up the fade, don't panic. You can always go shorter or try again in a few days.

Can I fade my undercut myself?

Yes, you can fade your undercut yourself. However, it's always a good idea to go to a professional stylist if you're not comfortable doing it yourself.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Undercut

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Use a styling product, such as pomade or wax, to keep your undercut in place.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your undercut.

Now that you know how to fade an undercut step by step, you can give your hair a fresh new look. Just remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have a great looking undercut in no time!
