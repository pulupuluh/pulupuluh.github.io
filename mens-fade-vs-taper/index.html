Mens Fade Vs Taper What Is The Difference

Written by Frank May 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a man looking to switch up your hairstyle, you may have heard of the terms "fade" and "taper." While they may sound similar, they actually refer to two different haircuts. In this article, we'll break down the differences between mens fade vs taper haircuts.

What is a Fade?

A fade is a type of haircut where the hair gradually becomes shorter from the top of the head down to the sides and back. The fade can be high or low, which refers to how high up on the head the shortest part of the hair is cut. A high fade will have very short hair up to the temple, while a low fade will have longer hair up to the ear.

The fade is a popular haircut for men of all ages and can be worn with a variety of styles, including buzz cuts, crew cuts, and pompadours. It's a versatile haircut that can be dressed up or down, depending on the occasion.

What is a Taper?

A taper is a type of haircut where the hair gradually becomes shorter from the top of the head down to the nape of the neck. Unlike a fade, which has a very short length at the sides and back, a taper leaves some length at the bottom. Tapers can be short or long, and can be worn with a variety of styles.

Tapers are a great option for men who want a more natural-looking haircut that is easy to maintain. They're also a good choice for men who want to keep some length at the bottom of their hair but still have a clean, polished look.

How to Get a Fade

If you're interested in getting a fade, it's important to find a stylist who has experience with this type of haircut. To get a fade, your stylist will use clippers to gradually shorten the length of your hair from the top down to the sides and back. They'll blend the different lengths together to create a smooth, seamless transition.

When getting a fade, it's important to communicate with your stylist about the length you want and the style you're going for. This will help ensure that you get a haircut that you're happy with.

How to Get a Taper

To get a taper, your stylist will use clippers to gradually shorten the length of your hair from the top down to the nape of your neck. They'll leave some length at the bottom to create a tapered effect.

Like with a fade, it's important to communicate with your stylist about the length you want and the style you're going for. This will help ensure that you get a haircut that you're happy with.

Tips for Maintaining Your Haircut

Whether you opt for a fade or a taper, there are a few things you can do to keep your haircut looking fresh and stylish:

  • Get regular trims: To keep your haircut looking neat, it's important to get regular trims every 4-6 weeks.
  • Use the right products: Using styling products like pomades, gels, and waxes can help you achieve the look you want and keep your hair in place throughout the day.
  • Wash your hair regularly: Keeping your hair clean and free of buildup can help prevent dandruff and other scalp issues.

FAQ

Q: What's the difference between a fade and a taper?

A: A fade is a type of haircut where the hair gradually becomes shorter from the top of the head down to the sides and back. A taper is a type of haircut where the hair gradually becomes shorter from the top of the head down to the nape of the neck.

Q: How do I know which haircut is right for me?

A: The best way to determine which haircut is right for you is to consult with a stylist. They can help you choose a haircut that complements your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.

Hair Care

Regardless of whether you choose a fade or a taper, it's important to take good care of your hair. This means using the right products, washing your hair regularly, and getting regular trims. With the right care, you can keep your haircut looking fresh and stylish for weeks to come.
