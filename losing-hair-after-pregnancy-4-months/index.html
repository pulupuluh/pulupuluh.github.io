Losing Hair After Pregnancy 4 Months Causes Tips And Hair Care

Written by Luffy Aug 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are a new mom, you may have noticed that your hair is falling out more than usual. Losing hair after pregnancy 4 months is quite common, and it's nothing to worry about. In fact, around 50% of women experience postpartum hair loss. In this article, we will discuss the causes of hair loss after pregnancy, tips to reduce hair loss, and hair care practices to keep your hair healthy.

Causes of Hair Loss After Pregnancy

During pregnancy, your body goes through a lot of hormonal changes. One of the side effects of these changes is that your hair may grow thicker and fuller. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels start to normalize, and this can cause your hair to fall out. Hair loss after pregnancy is usually temporary, and your hair should start to grow back within a few months.

Another cause of hair loss after pregnancy is stress. Taking care of a newborn can be stressful, and stress can cause your hair to fall out. Lack of sleep and poor nutrition can also contribute to hair loss.

Tips to Reduce Hair Loss

While you can't prevent hair loss after pregnancy, there are some things you can do to reduce it. Here are some tips:

1. Take Care of Your Hair

Be gentle with your hair and avoid harsh treatments like hot oil treatments, perms, or coloring. Brush your hair gently and avoid using a hairbrush with hard bristles. Use a wide-toothed comb instead.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help reduce hair loss. Make sure you are getting enough iron, vitamin D, and B vitamins. Foods like spinach, lentils, eggs, and salmon are all good sources of these nutrients.

3. Get Enough Sleep

Getting enough sleep is essential for your overall health and can help reduce stress levels. Try to get as much sleep as possible, and take naps when you can.

Hair Care Practices to Keep Your Hair Healthy

While you are experiencing hair loss after pregnancy, it's essential to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy. Here are some hair care practices to keep in mind:

1. Keep Your Hair Clean

Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy. Use a mild shampoo, and avoid using too much conditioner, as this can weigh down your hair.

2. Use a Gentle Hairbrush

Use a gentle hairbrush with soft bristles to avoid damaging your hair. Brush your hair gently, and avoid pulling or tugging on it.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, or blow dryers, as these can damage your hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use them sparingly and use a heat protectant spray.

FAQs

1. How long does hair loss after pregnancy last?

Hair loss after pregnancy is usually temporary and should stop within a few months. Your hair should start to grow back within six to twelve months.

2. Can I prevent hair loss after pregnancy?

You can't prevent hair loss after pregnancy, but you can reduce it by taking good care of your hair, eating a healthy diet, and reducing stress.

3. Is hair loss after pregnancy normal?

Yes, hair loss after pregnancy is normal and affects around 50% of women. It's a temporary side effect of hormonal changes in the body.

Conclusion

While losing hair after pregnancy 4 months can be concerning, it's a normal side effect of hormonal changes in the body. By taking good care of your hair, eating a healthy diet, and reducing stress, you can reduce hair loss and keep your hair healthy. Remember, hair loss after pregnancy is temporary, and your hair should start to grow back within a few months.
