How To Make Messy Hair Guys A Guide To Effortless Style

Written by Aprilia Feb 19, 2023 · 11 min read

If you’re looking for a hairstyle that’s both stylish and low-maintenance, messy hair might just be the look for you. Messy hair guys are known for their effortless style, and the good news is that achieving this look is easier than you might think. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to make messy hair guys work for you, from choosing the right cut to styling and maintenance.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Right Haircut for Messy Hair Guys
  • How to Style Messy Hair Guys: Step by Step
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Messy Hair Look
  • FAQ About Messy Hair Guys
  • Hair Care for Messy Hair Guys

How to Choose the Right Haircut for Messy Hair Guys

Before you can start styling your messy hair, you need to choose the right cut. The key to a good messy hair look is to have enough length to create texture and movement, but not so much length that your hair becomes unmanageable. Here are a few options to consider:

Short and Textured

A short, textured cut is a great option for guys who want a low-maintenance look. This style typically involves shorter hair on the sides and back, with longer hair on top that can be styled with a bit of product for a messy, tousled look.

Medium Length and Layered

A medium-length cut with layers is another great option for messy hair guys. This style typically involves longer hair on top that can be swept to the side or styled with product for a messy look, with shorter layers on the sides and back to add texture and movement.

Long and Shaggy

If you’re willing to commit to longer hair, a shaggy cut can be a great option for achieving a messy, relaxed look. This style typically involves longer hair all over, with layers and choppy ends to add texture and movement.

How to Style Messy Hair Guys: Step by Step

Once you’ve chosen the right cut for your messy hair, it’s time to start styling. Here’s a step-by-step guide to achieving the look:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo. Avoid using hot water, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz. Instead, use lukewarm water and gently massage the shampoo into your scalp, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

After washing, gently towel dry your hair to remove excess moisture. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with the towel, which can cause damage and breakage. Instead, gently pat your hair dry.

Step 3: Apply Styling Product

Next, apply a styling product to your hair to create texture and hold. A texturizing spray or sea salt spray can be a great option for messy hair, as it adds grip and volume without weighing your hair down. You can also use a matte pomade or wax for a more structured look.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once you’ve applied your styling product, it’s time to start styling. Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create volume and texture. You can also use a comb or brush to create a more structured look, but be careful not to overdo it – the key to messy hair is to look effortless and natural.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray (Optional)

If you want to add extra hold and texture to your hair, you can finish with a light mist of hairspray. Look for a product that’s designed to add texture and hold without making your hair look stiff or crunchy.

Tips for Maintaining Your Messy Hair Look

Messy hair might be low-maintenance, but that doesn’t mean it doesn’t require some upkeep. Here are a few tips for maintaining your messy hair look:

  • Visit your stylist regularly to keep your hair trimmed and healthy. This will help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking full and voluminous.
  • Invest in high-quality styling products that are designed to add texture and hold without weighing your hair down.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz. Instead, aim to wash your hair every other day or every few days.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a natural, messy look.

FAQ About Messy Hair Guys

What if I have fine or thinning hair?

Messy hair can work for guys with all hair types, including fine or thinning hair. Just be sure to choose a cut that’s tailored to your hair type, and use styling products that are designed to add volume and texture.

How do I know if messy hair will suit my face shape?

Messy hair can work for guys with a variety of face shapes, but it’s important to choose a cut that complements your features. If you’re not sure which cut will suit you best, ask your stylist for advice.

Can I achieve a messy hair look without using styling products?

While styling products can help create texture and hold, it is possible to achieve a messy look without using them. Try using a wide-toothed comb to create volume and movement, or simply tousle your hair with your fingers for a natural, relaxed look.

Hair Care for Messy Hair Guys

Finally, it’s important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips for maintaining healthy, vibrant hair:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that’s designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can dry it out and make it harder to style.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a natural, messy look.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to achieve the perfect messy hair look in no time. Whether you opt for a short, textured cut or a long, shaggy style, messy hair is all about effortless style and relaxed charm. So go ahead – embrace your inner messiness and rock that tousled, textured look with confidence!
