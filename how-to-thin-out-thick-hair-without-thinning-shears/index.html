How To Thin Out Thick Hair Without Thinning Shears

Written by Petter Sep 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you have thick hair that can be a bit too much to handle? Thinning out thick hair is a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. However, not everyone has thinning shears or wants to use them. Luckily, there are other ways to thin out your hair without needing specialized tools. Keep reading for our step-by-step guide on how to thin out thick hair without thinning shears.

Description

Thick hair can be difficult to style and manage, which is why many people choose to thin it out. Thinning out your hair can make it easier to style, reduce the amount of volume, and make it more manageable. While thinning shears are commonly used to thin out hair, they are not the only option. There are several ways to thin out your hair without thinning shears, including using scissors, razors, and even a comb.

How To Thin Out Thick Hair Without Thinning Shears

Follow these simple steps to thin out your hair without thinning shears:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you have a clean canvas to work on. Avoid using any styling products or serums as they can make your hair more difficult to cut.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections to make it easier to work with. Use clips to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take your scissors and start cutting small sections of hair. Cut at an angle to ensure that you are not cutting straight across. This will help to blend the hair and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 4: Use a Razor

If you have a razor, you can use it to thin out your hair. This technique is best for people with wavy or curly hair as it can help to enhance the natural texture. Simply run the razor through small sections of hair to thin it out.

Step 5: Use a Comb

If you don't have scissors or a razor, you can use a comb to thin out your hair. This technique is best for people with straight hair. Take your comb and comb through small sections of hair. Use your scissors to cut the ends of the hair that stick out of the comb. This will help to create a more layered look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once
  • Cut at an angle to prevent any harsh lines
  • Use a razor for wavy or curly hair
  • Use a comb for straight hair
  • Don't cut too much at once, you can always go back and cut more if needed
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out thick hair:

1. How often should I thin out my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, most people need to thin out their hair every 6-8 weeks.

2. Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

Thinning out your hair will not make it look thinner, but it will make it more manageable and easier to style.

3. Can I thin out my hair at home?

Yes, you can thin out your hair at home. Just make sure to use the right tools and follow the proper techniques.

Hair Care

Thinning out your hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. However, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent any damage. Make sure to use a heat protectant when using hot tools and avoid using too much product. Additionally, make sure to get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Thinning out thick hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. While thinning shears are commonly used, there are other ways to thin out your hair without needing specialized tools. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips to thin out your hair at home.
