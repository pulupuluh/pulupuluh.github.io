Does Rubbing Nails Really Grow Hair?

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips

Description

Many people believe that rubbing nails can help in hair growth. This practice, also known as Balayam, is said to increase blood circulation in the scalp and stimulate hair growth. But does it really work?

There is no scientific evidence to support the claim that rubbing nails can promote hair growth. However, many people have reported positive results after practicing Balayam regularly.

How to

To practice Balayam, sit in a comfortable position and bring your hands in front of your chest. Rub the nails of each hand against each other vigorously for 5-10 minutes. You can do this several times a day, but make sure to take breaks in between.

Step by step

  1. Sit in a comfortable position.
  2. Bring your hands in front of your chest.
  3. Interlock your fingers.
  4. Start rubbing the nails of each hand against each other vigorously.
  5. Continue for 5-10 minutes.
  6. Take breaks in between.

Tips

Here are some tips to make your Balayam practice more effective:

  • Practice on an empty stomach.
  • Use the fingers of both hands to rub the nails.
  • Do it regularly for 3-4 months to see results.

FAQ

1. Can rubbing nails cause any harm?

No, rubbing nails is a safe practice and does not cause any harm.

2. Can Balayam help in reducing hair fall?

Yes, many people have reported a reduction in hair fall after practicing Balayam regularly.

3. Can Balayam help in regrowing hair?

There is no scientific evidence to support this claim. However, many people have reported positive results after practicing Balayam regularly.

Hair Care

While Balayam can help in promoting hair growth, it is important to take care of your hair in other ways as well. Here are some tips:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair.
  • Do not comb or brush your hair when it is wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet.
