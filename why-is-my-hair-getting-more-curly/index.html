Why Is My Hair Getting More Curly

Written by Eveline Jul 31, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you've noticed that your hair is getting more curly, you're not alone. Many people experience changes in their hair texture over time, and it's not always clear why. In this article, we'll explore some of the reasons why your hair may be getting curlier, as well as some tips for taking care of your newfound curls.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

As we age, our hair can change in many ways. For some people, their hair may become thinner, while others may experience more frizz or breakage. One common change that many people experience is an increase in curliness. This can be especially noticeable for people who have naturally straight hair, as the curls may become more pronounced and defined over time.

There are a few reasons why your hair may be getting curlier. One is simply genetics. If you have a family history of curly hair, it's possible that your hair is becoming more curly as you get older. Another factor is hormonal changes. During puberty, pregnancy, and menopause, hormone levels can fluctuate, which can impact the texture of your hair. Finally, environmental factors like humidity and heat can also affect the curl pattern of your hair.

How to

If you're not used to having curly hair, you may be wondering how to best care for it. Here are some tips:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that's designed for curly hair.

2. Condition regularly

Conditioning is key for keeping curly hair hydrated and healthy. Use a conditioner that's designed for curly hair, and consider using a deep conditioner once a week.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

Curly hair is prone to tangling, so it's important to use a comb that won't snag or pull your hair. A wide-tooth comb is a great choice, as it will gently detangle your hair without causing damage.

4. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can damage curly hair and disrupt the natural curl pattern. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to caring for your curly hair:

1. Shampoo your hair

Wet your hair and apply a small amount of shampoo to your scalp. Massage the shampoo into your scalp, and then rinse thoroughly with warm water.

2. Condition your hair

Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and then let the conditioner sit for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Blot your hair dry

After you've rinsed out the conditioner, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, as this can cause frizz.

4. Apply styling products

Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing cream to your hair, using your fingers to distribute the product evenly. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help enhance your curls.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips for caring for curly hair:

1. Sleep on a satin pillowcase

Satin pillowcases are gentle on curly hair, and can help prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

2. Avoid brushing your hair

Brushing can disrupt the natural curl pattern of your hair, so it's best to avoid it. Instead, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

3. Experiment with different products

Every curly head is different, so it may take some trial and error to find the right products for your hair. Don't be afraid to experiment with different brands and formulas to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Q: Will my hair stay curly?

A: It's impossible to say for sure. Your hair may continue to get curlier over time, or it may eventually return to its original texture.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use heat styling tools to straighten your hair. Just make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low to prevent damage.

Q: Can I color my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can color your curly hair. Just make sure to use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be a bit of a learning curve, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and styling techniques until you find what works best for you.
