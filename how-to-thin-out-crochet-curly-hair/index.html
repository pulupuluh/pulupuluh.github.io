How To Thin Out Crochet Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of your crochet curly hair being too thick and bulky? Don't worry, thinning out your crochet hair is an easy process that can give you the perfect look you desire. In this blog post, we will discuss how to thin out crochet curly hair in a relaxed English language. We'll also provide you with tips and hair care advice to keep your hair looking fabulous.

How to Thin Out Crochet Curly Hair

Before we begin, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a rat-tail comb, and a mirror.

Thinning out your crochet curly hair involves removing some of the hair from the weft, creating a more natural and less bulky look. Here's how to do it.

Step 1: Separate the Hair

Separate the hair into sections, leaving one small section at the back. This section will be your guide for how much hair to thin out from each section.

Step 2: Thin Out the Hair

Take a small section of hair and hold it away from your head. Hold the scissors at an angle and cut into the hair, creating small snips. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. You can always go back and thin out more hair if needed.

Repeat the process for each section of hair, using the small section at the back as your guide for how much hair to remove.

Step 3: Blend the Hair

After thinning out each section, use your rat-tail comb to blend the hair together. Comb through each section of hair, making sure it looks natural and not too bulky.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're a visual learner, here's a step-by-step guide on how to thin out crochet curly hair:

  1. Separate the hair into sections, leaving one small section at the back.
  2. Take a small section of hair and hold it away from your head.
  3. Hold the scissors at an angle and cut into the hair, creating small snips.
  4. Repeat the process for each section of hair, using the small section at the back as your guide for how much hair to remove.
  5. Use your rat-tail comb to blend the hair together.

Tips for Thinning Out Crochet Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your crochet curly hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and gradually thin out more if needed.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • If you're unsure about how much hair to remove, consult a hairstylist.

FAQs

Can you thin out crochet hair after installation?

Yes, you can thin out crochet hair after installation. However, it's easier to thin out the hair before installation.

Is it better to thin out crochet hair when wet or dry?

It's better to thin out crochet hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to work with and may not give you the desired results.

How often should you thin out crochet hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it thickens. You may need to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care Advice

After thinning out your crochet curly hair, it's important to take care of it to avoid damaging the hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Protect your hair while sleeping by wearing a satin bonnet or using a satin pillowcase.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, which can damage your hair.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and looking fabulous.

Conclusion

Thinning out your crochet curly hair is an easy process that can give you the natural, less bulky look you desire. Remember to use the right tools, start with small snips, and blend the hair together for a natural look. Don't forget to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
