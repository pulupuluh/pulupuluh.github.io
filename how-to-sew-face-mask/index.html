How To Sew Face Mask A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Apr 20, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking for a way to protect yourself and others during the ongoing pandemic, sewing your own face mask is a great option. Not only is it cost-effective, but you can also customize it to your liking. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of sewing your very own face mask.

How to Sew Face Mask: Step by Step

Materials Needed:

  • Cotton fabric (two pieces, 10 x 6 inches each)
  • Elastic bands (two pieces, 6 inches each)
  • Scissors
  • Pins
  • Sewing machine or needle and thread

Instructions:

Step 1: Cut two pieces of cotton fabric, each measuring 10 x 6 inches. These will be the main pieces of your face mask.

Step 2: Cut two pieces of elastic, each measuring 6 inches. These will be used to secure your mask around your ears.

Step 3: Place the two pieces of fabric on top of each other, with the right sides facing each other. Pin them together along the shorter edges.

Step 4: Sew along the pinned edges using a 1/4 inch seam allowance.

Step 5: Turn the fabric right side out and press it flat using an iron.

Step 6: Fold the fabric in half, with the shorter edges meeting. Pin the elastic bands to each corner of the mask, on the outside of the fabric.

Step 7: Sew the elastic bands onto the mask using a zigzag stitch.

Step 8: Fold the mask in half again, with the shorter edges meeting. Pin the longer edges together, leaving a small opening in the center of one of the edges.

Step 9: Sew along the pinned edges using a 1/4 inch seam allowance. Be sure to leave the small opening unsewn.

Step 10: Turn the mask right side out through the small opening. Press it flat using an iron.

Step 11: Sew the small opening closed using a slip stitch.

Tips:

  • Be sure to wash your fabric before using it to make your mask.
  • Choose a fabric that is tightly woven and breathable, such as cotton. Avoid using stretchy or slippery fabrics.
  • Make sure your elastic bands are securely sewn onto your mask.
  • Wash your mask regularly, and avoid touching your face while wearing it.

FAQ:

Q: Can I use a different type of fabric to make my mask?

A: While cotton is the recommended fabric for making face masks, other tightly woven fabrics such as linen or flannel can also be used.

Q: How often should I wash my face mask?

A: It is recommended to wash your face mask after every use.

Hair Care:

While wearing a face mask, it's important to take care of your hair as well. Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that may cause breakage or damage to your hair. Opt for loose styles such as a messy bun or braid. You can also use a hair scarf or headband to keep your hair out of your face.

Now that you know how to sew your own face mask, you can stay safe and stylish during these challenging times. Remember to follow all recommended guidelines for wearing and caring for your face mask.
