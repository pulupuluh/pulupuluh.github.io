How To Know What Type Of Glasses Fit Your Face

Written by Bardi Mar 09, 2023 · 10 min read

Are you tired of trying on countless pairs of glasses only to find that none of them seem to suit your face? Choosing the right type of glasses can be a daunting task, but it’s important to find a pair that not only looks great but also fits comfortably. In this article, we’ll provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to know what type of glasses fit your face.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Step-by-Step Guide on Choosing Glasses for Your Face Shape
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Glasses
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Glasses Wearers

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the perfect pair of glasses is determining your face shape. This will help you narrow down the options and find a pair that flatters your features. Here are the most common face shapes and how to identify them:

Round

A round face is characterized by soft curves and a lack of angles. Your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your forehead and jawline are equally rounded. To determine if you have a round face, measure the width and length of your face. If they’re roughly the same, you have a round face.

Square

A square face is defined by a strong jawline and a broad forehead. The length and width of your face are almost equal, and your chin is usually flat or slightly squared off. To determine if you have a square face, measure the width of your forehead, jawline, and cheekbones. If they’re all roughly the same, you have a square face.

Heart

A heart-shaped face has a wider forehead and cheekbones that taper down to a narrow chin. To determine if you have a heart-shaped face, measure the width of your forehead and compare it to the width of your jawline. If your forehead is wider, you have a heart-shaped face.

Oval

An oval face is longer than it is wide, and your forehead is slightly wider than your jawline. Your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your chin is rounded. To determine if you have an oval face, measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. If your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, you have an oval face.

Step-by-Step Guide on Choosing Glasses for Your Face Shape

Now that you’ve determined your face shape, it’s time to find the perfect pair of glasses. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Consider Your Face Shape

Use the guide above to determine your face shape and identify the most flattering styles for your features.

Step 2: Look for Contrasting Shapes

Choose glasses with a shape that contrasts with your face shape. For example, if you have a round face, choose glasses with angular frames to create balance and definition.

Step 3: Consider Your Skin Tone

Choose glasses with a color that complements your skin tone. If you have a warm skin tone, choose frames in shades of brown, gold, or honey. If you have a cool skin tone, choose frames in shades of silver, black, or blue.

Step 4: Choose the Right Size

Make sure the glasses are the right size for your face. The frame should be proportional to your features and not too big or too small. The temples should fit comfortably behind your ears, and the bridge should fit snugly on your nose without pinching.

Step 5: Try Them On

Always try on glasses before you buy them. This will give you an idea of how they look and feel on your face. If you’re shopping online, look for virtual try-on tools that allow you to see how the glasses look on your face.

Tips for Choosing the Right Glasses

Here are some additional tips to help you choose the right glasses:

  • Consider your lifestyle – if you’re active, choose glasses that are durable and lightweight.
  • Try on different styles – even if you think you know what you want, try on a variety of styles to see what looks best.
  • Choose a reputable brand – quality matters when it comes to glasses, so choose a brand that’s known for its craftsmanship and durability.
  • Ask for help – don’t be afraid to ask a salesperson or optometrist for help in choosing the right glasses.

FAQs

Q: Can I wear any style of glasses regardless of my face shape?

A: While you can wear any style of glasses you like, certain styles will be more flattering and complement your features better. It’s important to choose glasses that balance and enhance your natural features.

Q: How do I know if glasses fit properly?

A: Glasses should fit comfortably and securely on your face without slipping or pinching. The temples should fit snugly behind your ears, and the bridge should fit comfortably on your nose without leaving marks.

Q: Can I wear glasses with bangs?

A: Yes, you can wear glasses with bangs. However, it’s important to choose glasses that don’t clash with your bangs. For example, if you have thick bangs, choose glasses with a thin frame or no frame at all to avoid overwhelming your face.

Hair Care Tips for Glasses Wearers

If you wear glasses, it’s important to take care of your hair to avoid tangling or snagging your frames. Here are some hair care tips for glasses wearers:

  • Avoid wearing hair accessories that can get caught in your glasses, such as bulky headbands or hair clips.
  • Brush your hair regularly to prevent tangles and snags.
  • Choose hairstyles that work well with your glasses, such as a low ponytail or a side braid.
  • Use a hair serum or oil to smooth down flyaways and keep your hair looking sleek.

By following these tips and using our step-by-step guide, you’ll be able to find the perfect pair of glasses that flatters your features and fits comfortably. Happy shopping!
