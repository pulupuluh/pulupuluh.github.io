What Deficiency Makes Your Hair Fall Out

Written by Oliver Mar 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss? There are several factors that can contribute to hair loss, and one of them is a deficiency in certain vitamins and minerals. In this article, we will discuss what deficiency makes your hair fall out and how to address it.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Identify the Deficiency
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Addressing the Deficiency
  • Tips for Promoting Hair Growth
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Healthy Hair

Description

When your body lacks certain vitamins and minerals, it can lead to hair loss. The most common deficiencies that cause hair loss include:

  • Vitamin D
  • Vitamin B12
  • Iron
  • Zinc

If you are experiencing hair loss, it's important to identify which deficiency is causing it so that you can address it properly.

How to Identify the Deficiency

If you suspect that a deficiency is causing your hair loss, the first step is to get a blood test. A blood test can identify which vitamins and minerals your body is lacking. Once you know which deficiency is causing your hair loss, you can take steps to address it.

Step-by-Step Guide to Addressing the Deficiency

If your blood test reveals that you are deficient in a certain vitamin or mineral, here are the steps you can take to address it:

  1. Take supplements: If you are deficient in vitamin D, B12, iron, or zinc, your doctor may recommend taking supplements to address the deficiency. Make sure to follow your doctor's instructions and take the supplements as directed.
  2. Eat a balanced diet: Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help address deficiencies. Make sure to eat a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
  3. Get more sunlight: If you are deficient in vitamin D, spending more time in the sun can help your body produce more vitamin D.

Tips for Promoting Hair Growth

In addition to addressing the deficiency, there are steps you can take to promote hair growth:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles like braids, weaves, and ponytails can put stress on your hair follicles and lead to hair loss.
  • Be gentle when styling: When styling your hair, be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and lead to hair loss. Try to avoid using them as much as possible.
  • Use a gentle shampoo: Harsh shampoos can strip your hair of its natural oils and lead to hair loss. Use a gentle shampoo that is formulated for your hair type.

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can be a contributing factor to hair loss. When you are stressed, your body produces hormones that can affect your hair growth cycle.

Q: Is it possible to reverse hair loss?

A: In some cases, hair loss can be reversed. If the hair loss is caused by a deficiency, addressing the deficiency can help promote hair growth. However, in some cases, hair loss may be permanent.

Hair Care for Healthy Hair

In addition to addressing deficiencies and promoting hair growth, taking care of your hair is important for maintaining healthy hair. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Washing your hair regularly can help keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid over-washing: Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils and lead to dry, brittle hair.
  • Use conditioner: Using a conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair: Wet hair is more fragile and prone to breakage. Wait until your hair is dry to brush it.

By addressing deficiencies, promoting hair growth, and taking care of your hair, you can maintain healthy hair and prevent hair loss.
