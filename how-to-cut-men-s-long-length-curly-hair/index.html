How to Cut Men's Long Length Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. Curly hair tends to be more voluminous and can hide uneven cuts. Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools and a plan in mind.

Tools You'll Need

  • Shears
  • Clipper
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Styling products

Plan Your Cut

Decide on the length you want and where you want to cut your hair. Do you want an even length all around, or do you want layers? Take into account your hair's natural curl pattern and how it will fall when cut. It's always a good idea to have a picture or two of a style you like to show your stylist or use as a reference when cutting your hair yourself.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Wash and condition your hair thoroughly before cutting it. This will make it easier to detangle and cut. Apply a leave-in conditioner to help your hair stay hydrated and smooth.

2. Detangle Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers, detangle your hair to remove any knots or tangles. This will make it easier to cut and ensure an even cut.

3. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair ties or clips. This will make it easier to cut your hair in layers or even lengths.

4. Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting at the back of your head, cut the bottom layer of your hair to your desired length. Use the comb to comb the hair straight down and cut in a straight line. Work your way up to the top layer, cutting each section to the same length.

5. Cut the Top Layer

Cut the top layer of your hair to your desired length. This layer should be slightly shorter than the bottom layer to create layers and texture.

6. Cut Face-Framing Layers

If you want face-framing layers, take a small section of hair at the front of your head and cut it to your desired length. Repeat on the other side.

7. Blend the Layers

Using the shears, blend the layers together to create a seamless, natural look. Be careful not to cut too much or create any unevenness.

8. Trim the Ends

Trim the ends of your hair to remove any split ends or damage. This will help your hair look healthy and neat.

9. Style Your Hair

Apply styling products to your hair to enhance your natural curls and hold your style. You can use a diffuser to dry your hair or let it air dry.

Tips for Cutting Men's Long Length Curly Hair

  • Invest in high-quality shears and clippers to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • If you're not confident cutting your hair yourself, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQs

1. How often should I cut my long length curly hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

2. Can I cut my curly hair when it's dry?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's wet, as it's easier to detangle and cut evenly.

3. How do I know if I've cut too much?

Take small cuts at a time and check your progress in the mirror. If you're not sure, it's better to cut less and go back for more rather than cutting too much at once.

Hair Care Tips for Men's Long Length Curly Hair

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

By following these tips and steps, you can achieve a great cut for your long length curly hair. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a stylish and manageable look that you'll love.
