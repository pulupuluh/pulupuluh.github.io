Barber Thinned Out Hair Too Much What To Do When Your Hair Is Over Thinned

Written by Petter Jul 18, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you struggling with hair that has been over-thinned by your barber? If so, you're not alone. Over-thinning is a common problem that can leave your hair looking limp and lifeless. In this article, we'll discuss what you can do when your barber thinned out hair too much. We'll cover how to fix the problem, steps to take, tips to prevent over-thinning, and more.

How to Know If Your Hair Has Been Over-Thinned?

Over-thinning can happen when your barber uses thinning shears or razors too aggressively. Your hair may look fine immediately after the cut, but over time, you may notice that it's lost volume and thickness. Some signs that your barber thinned out hair too much include:

  • Your hair feels thin and wispy
  • You can see scalp through your hair
  • Your hair tangles easily
  • You have trouble styling your hair

Steps to Take When Your Hair is Over-Thinned

If you've realized that your hair has been over-thinned, don't panic. There are steps you can take to fix the problem. Here's what to do:

Step 1: Talk to Your Barber

The first step is to talk to your barber. Explain how you're feeling about the cut and the problems you're experiencing. A good barber will listen to your concerns and work with you to find a solution.

Step 2: Give Your Hair Some Time

Sometimes, over-thinning is a temporary problem. Your hair may need some time to grow out and regain its volume and thickness. Be patient and give your hair some time before trying any drastic measures.

Step 3: Get a Haircut

If you're still unhappy with your hair after talking to your barber and giving it some time, it may be time to get a haircut. Look for a stylist who specializes in cutting thick hair and ask for their advice on the best style for your hair.

Tips to Prevent Over-Thinning

The best way to deal with over-thinning is to prevent it from happening in the first place. Here are some tips to help you avoid over-thinning:

  • Choose a barber who has experience cutting thick hair
  • Avoid using thinning shears or razors on your hair
  • Opt for a blunt cut or a layered cut that doesn't rely on thinning to create volume
  • Use hair products that add volume and thickness to your hair

FAQ

Can Over-Thinning Cause Hair Loss?

Over-thinning can damage your hair and cause it to break, but it won't cause permanent hair loss. If you're concerned about hair loss, talk to your doctor or a dermatologist.

How Long Does It Take for Over-Thinned Hair to Grow Back?

The length of time it takes for over-thinned hair to grow back varies depending on the individual. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for your hair to regain its volume and thickness.

Hair Care

Taking good care of your hair is essential for preventing over-thinning and keeping it healthy. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Don't wash your hair too often
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week

In conclusion, over-thinning can be a frustrating problem, but it's not the end of the world. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can fix the problem and prevent it from happening in the future. Remember to talk to your barber, be patient, and take good care of your hair.
