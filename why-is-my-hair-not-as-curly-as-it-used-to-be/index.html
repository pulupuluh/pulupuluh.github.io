Why Is My Hair Not As Curly As It Used To Be

Written by Bardi Feb 27, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're someone who has naturally curly hair, you may have noticed that your curls are not as bouncy or defined as they used to be. This can be frustrating, especially if you've always loved your curly locks. There are several reasons why your hair may not be as curly as it used to be, and in this article, we'll explore some of the most common causes and what you can do to get your curls back.

Table of Contents

  • Cause #1: Heat Damage
  • Cause #2: Chemical Damage
  • Cause #3: Hormonal Changes
  • Cause #4: Age
  • Tips for Restoring Your Curls
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Cause #1: Heat Damage

One of the most common reasons why curly hair loses its curl pattern is heat damage. If you're someone who frequently uses hot tools like straighteners or curling irons, the high heat can damage your hair's natural curl pattern. Over time, this can lead to looser curls or even straight hair.

To prevent heat damage, try to limit your use of hot tools. If you do need to use them, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible. You can also try using alternative styling methods like twist-outs or braid-outs to achieve a curly look without heat.

Cause #2: Chemical Damage

Chemical treatments like relaxers or perms can also damage your hair's natural curl pattern. If you've recently had a chemical treatment, you may notice that your curls are looser or less defined than they used to be.

To restore your curls, you may need to grow out the damaged hair and cut it off. In the meantime, try to avoid any further chemical treatments and focus on nourishing your hair with moisturizing products.

Cause #3: Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also affect your hair's curl pattern. For example, women may notice changes in their hair during pregnancy or menopause.

Unfortunately, there's not much you can do to prevent hormonal changes from affecting your hair. However, you can focus on keeping your hair healthy with nourishing products and a healthy diet.

Cause #4: Age

As we age, our hair naturally loses some of its elasticity and volume. This can lead to looser curls or even straight hair.

To keep your curls looking their best as you age, focus on keeping your hair healthy with nourishing products and a healthy diet. You may also want to consider getting regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh.

Tips for Restoring Your Curls

If you're looking to restore your curls, there are several things you can try:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can lead to frizz
  • Try using a diffuser when blow drying your hair to enhance your curls
  • Consider using a curl-enhancing product like a curl cream or gel
  • Try sleeping on a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz

FAQs

Q: Can genetics affect my curl pattern?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in your hair's natural curl pattern. If your parents have curly hair, it's likely that you will too.

Q: Can stress affect my curls?

A: Yes, stress can affect your hair's curl pattern. When you're stressed, your body produces more cortisol, which can lead to hair loss and changes in texture.

Q: Can diet affect my curls?

A: Yes, a healthy diet can help keep your hair looking its best. Make sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein to nourish your hair from the inside out.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

If you have naturally curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons too frequently
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can lead to frizz
  • Try using a diffuser when blow drying your hair to enhance your curls
  • Consider using a curl-enhancing product like a curl cream or gel
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and protein to nourish your hair from the inside out

By following these tips and taking care of your hair properly, you can help restore your curls and keep them looking their best.
