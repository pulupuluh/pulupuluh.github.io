How To Lighten Dyed Red Hair Without Bleach A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 16, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you've dyed your hair red and are now looking to lighten it without using bleach, you're in luck. It's possible to achieve a lighter shade of red or even move towards blonde without damaging your hair with harsh chemicals like bleach. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to lighten dyed red hair without bleach. Read on for step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice.

How to Lighten Dyed Red Hair Without Bleach

Before we dive into the steps for lightening dyed red hair without bleach, it's important to understand the process. When you dye your hair, the color penetrates the hair shaft and replaces your natural hair color. To lighten dyed hair, you'll need to remove some of the pigment to reveal a lighter shade. This can be done with natural ingredients, hair dye removers, or color correctors.

Using Natural Ingredients

Lemon juice, honey, and chamomile tea are natural ingredients that can help lighten dyed red hair without bleach. Lemon juice is acidic and can help break down the red color molecules in your hair. Honey contains small amounts of hydrogen peroxide, which can lighten hair. Chamomile tea has a natural yellow pigment that can counteract red tones.

Using Hair Dye Removers

Hair dye removers are products that remove artificial hair color from your hair. They work by breaking down the dye molecules and allowing them to be washed out. Hair dye removers can be found at most drugstores and beauty supply stores.

Using Color Correctors

Color correctors are products that neutralize unwanted tones in your hair. For example, a green color corrector can be used to counteract red tones. Color correctors can be found at most beauty supply stores.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you understand the process of lightening dyed red hair without bleach, let's dive into the steps:

Step 1: Choose a Method

Decide which method you want to use to lighten your dyed red hair. You can use natural ingredients, hair dye removers, or color correctors.

Step 2: Gather Your Supplies

Depending on the method you choose, you'll need different supplies. If you're using natural ingredients, you'll need lemon juice, honey, or chamomile tea. If you're using a hair dye remover, you'll need the product and gloves. If you're using a color corrector, you'll need the corrector and gloves.

Step 3: Test the Product

Before applying any product to your hair, it's important to test it on a small section first. This will help you determine if you're allergic to any of the ingredients and will also give you an idea of how the product will work on your hair.

Step 4: Apply the Product

Follow the instructions on your chosen product and apply it to your hair. Be sure to apply it evenly and thoroughly, covering all of your hair.

Step 5: Rinse and Condition

After the recommended processing time, rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to help restore moisture to your hair.

Tips for Lightening Dyed Red Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when lightening dyed red hair:

  • Be patient. Lightening hair can be a slow process, especially without bleach.
  • Avoid heat styling tools. Heat can cause damage to already processed hair.
  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your new shade.
  • Avoid over-processing your hair. Lightening hair too often can cause damage and breakage.

FAQs

Can I lighten dyed red hair without bleach?

Yes, it's possible to achieve a lighter shade of red or even move towards blonde without using bleach. You can use natural ingredients, hair dye removers, or color correctors to lighten your hair.

Will lightening my hair damage it?

Lightening hair can cause damage if it's done too frequently or with harsh chemicals like bleach. However, using natural ingredients or gentle hair dye removers is less likely to cause damage.

Hair Care Advice for Lightened Hair

Once you've lightened your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain its health and color. Here are a few hair care tips:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your new shade.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can cause the color to fade more quickly.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, as these can cause damage to already processed hair.

By following these tips and using natural ingredients, hair dye removers, or color correctors, you can lighten your dyed red hair without bleach. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair to maintain its health and color.
