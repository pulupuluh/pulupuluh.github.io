How to Slick Back Coarse Hair

Slicking back coarse hair requires a bit of extra effort compared to other hair types. Coarse hair tends to be thicker, denser, and more resistant to styling. However, with the right products and techniques, you can achieve a polished look that lasts all day. Here are the basic steps to slick back coarse hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Coarse hair is prone to dryness, so it's important to use products that hydrate and nourish your strands. Avoid using hot water, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more difficult to style.

Step 2: Apply a Styling Product

Next, apply a styling product that works well with coarse hair. A pomade or wax can provide the hold you need without weighing your hair down. Rub a small amount of product between your palms and apply it evenly throughout your hair. Be sure to focus on the roots and work your way down to the tips.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair Back

Using a wide-tooth comb, start combing your hair back from your forehead. Be sure to comb your hair in the direction you want it to stay. If you have a side part, make sure to comb the hair on the side with less hair first before combing the other side. Use the comb to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

To ensure your style stays in place all day, finish with a light mist of hairspray. Hold the can about 8-10 inches away from your head and spray evenly over your hair. Avoid using too much hairspray, as it can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basic steps, let's dive into a more detailed step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse with cool water to seal the cuticle and prevent frizz. Towel dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub or twist it, as this can cause breakage.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

If you plan to use a hairdryer or flat iron, apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will help prevent heat damage and keep your hair looking healthy. Spray the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush and a hairdryer, blow dry your hair in the direction you want it to go. Be sure to use the nozzle attachment on your hairdryer to focus the airflow and reduce frizz. Use a high heat setting for maximum control and a low speed to avoid tangling your hair.

Step 4: Apply a Styling Product

Once your hair is dry, apply a styling product that works well with coarse hair. A hair wax or pomade can provide the hold you need without weighing your hair down. Rub a small amount of product between your palms and apply it evenly throughout your hair.

Step 5: Comb Your Hair Back

Using a wide-tooth comb, start combing your hair back from your forehead. Be sure to comb your hair in the direction you want it to stay. Use the comb to create a smooth, even look that frames your face. If you have a side part, comb the hair on the side with less hair first before combing the other side.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

To ensure your style stays in place all day, finish with a light mist of hairspray. Hold the can about 8-10 inches away from your head and spray evenly over your hair. Avoid using too much hairspray, as it can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

Tips for Slicking Back Coarse Hair

Slicking back coarse hair can be a challenge, but with the right tips and techniques, you can achieve a polished look. Here are a few tips to help you slick back your coarse hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot water, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more difficult to style.
  • Start with a small amount of styling product and add more as needed. Too much product can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and minimize frizz.
  • Blow dry your hair in the direction you want it to go to create a smooth, even look.
  • Finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold your style in place.

FAQs

Q: Can I slick back my coarse hair without using styling products?

A: While it's possible to slick back coarse hair without using styling products, it can be more difficult to achieve a polished look. Styling products like pomade or wax can provide the hold you need to keep your hair in place all day.

Q: How often should I wash my coarse hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently. If you have dry hair, you may be able to go longer between washes. Generally, it's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I slick back my coarse hair if I have a receding hairline?

A: Yes, you can still slick back your hair if you have a receding hairline. However, you may need to adjust your style to avoid emphasizing the hairline. Talk to your stylist about the best way to slick back your hair based on your hairline and face shape.

Hair Care for Coarse Hair

If you have coarse hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are a few tips for caring for your coarse hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and minimize frizz.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back too tightly, as it can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add moisture and shine to your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can slick back your coarse hair and achieve a sleek and polished look. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and manageable.
