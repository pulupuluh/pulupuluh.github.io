How To Cut Medium Wavy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 15, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of your medium wavy hair looking unkempt and unruly? Whether you want to maintain your current style or try something new, cutting your hair at home can save you time and money. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut medium wavy hair like a pro. Keep reading for tips, tricks, and step-by-step instructions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Medium Wavy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Medium Wavy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

How to Cut Medium Wavy Hair

Cutting medium wavy hair requires a bit of skill and patience, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut at home. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Optional: thinning shears or razor

Before you begin, it's important to have a clear idea of what you want your hair to look like. Look for inspiration pictures online or in magazines, and consider your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle. If you're unsure, start with a simple trim and adjust from there.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your medium wavy hair:

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before cutting. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, and cutting damp hair can lead to uneven results.

2. Section your hair.

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then divide it into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.

3. Cut the back section.

Start with the back section of your hair. Comb or brush it straight down, then trim the ends in a straight line. Take small sections at a time, and use your fingers to measure the length you want.

4. Move to the front section.

Release the front section of your hair, and comb it forward. Use the back section as a guide, and trim the ends in a straight line. Be careful not to cut too much, as the front section tends to be shorter than the back.

5. Layer your hair (optional).

If you want to add layers to your hair, use thinning shears or a razor to create texture and movement. Take small sections at a time, and cut at an angle to create a soft, layered look.

6. Blend the layers.

After you’ve added layers, blend them into the rest of your hair by gently combing through with your fingers. This will create a seamless transition from the shorter layers to the longer ones.

7. Check for symmetry.

Make sure both sides of your hair are even by checking in a mirror. If you notice any unevenness, trim the longer side until they match.

8. Finish with styling.

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, style it as desired. Use a curling iron or diffuser to enhance your natural waves, or straighten it for a sleeker look.

Tips for Cutting Medium Wavy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect cut:

  • Start with small cuts, and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can’t add length back once it’s gone.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it will appear longer than it actually is.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your hair can be tiring, so don’t rush it.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you if you’re not confident cutting your own hair.

FAQs

Can I cut my wavy hair when it’s wet?

No. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, and cutting damp hair can lead to uneven results. Always cut your hair when it’s dry.

How often should I cut my medium wavy hair?

It depends on your hair texture, but most people should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain their style.

Do I need to use thinning shears or a razor to add layers?

No, you can use regular scissors to add layers. However, thinning shears and razors create a softer, more natural look.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

Now that you’ve cut your medium wavy hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance your waves and keep them defined.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. If you do use them, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.

Now that you know how to cut medium wavy hair, you can maintain your style at home and save money on salon visits. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow our step-by-step guide.
