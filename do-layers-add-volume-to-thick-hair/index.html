Do Layers Add Volume To Thick Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 17, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of your thick hair weighing you down? Do you want to add some volume and bounce to your locks? Look no further than layers! Layers can be a game-changer for those with thick hair, but you may be wondering: do layers really add volume to thick hair? Let's dive in.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To Add Layers
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Layering Thick Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Layered Hair

Description

Layers are a haircutting technique that involves cutting the hair at different lengths, creating different "layers" that add dimension and movement to the hair. For those with thick hair, layers can help to remove some of the weight and bulk, allowing the hair to have more volume and shape. So, the short answer is yes, layers can add volume to thick hair!

How To Add Layers

If you're ready to add some layers to your thick hair, the first step is to find a stylist who is experienced in layering thick hair. They will be able to assess your hair's texture, density, and natural movement to determine the best placement and length of layers for your specific needs.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a general step-by-step guide for adding layers to thick hair:

  1. Wash and blow-dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair into manageable parts.
  3. Start with the bottom layer and cut it to the desired length.
  4. Move up to the next layer and cut it slightly shorter than the layer below.
  5. Repeat this process until you reach the top layer.
  6. Blend the layers together by using thinning shears or a razor.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Layering Thick Hair

When it comes to layering thick hair, there are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a stylist who has experience with thick hair and layering techniques.
  • Consider your face shape and hair texture when deciding on the length and placement of your layers.
  • Opt for longer layers to avoid creating too much volume and frizz.
  • Don't over-layer your hair, as this can lead to thinning and breakage.

FAQs

Still have questions about layers and thick hair? Here are some frequently asked questions:

Will layers make my hair look thinner?

No, layers should not make your hair look thinner. In fact, layers can actually make your hair look fuller and more voluminous by removing some of the weight and bulk.

Can layers work for all hair types?

Yes, layers can work for all hair types, but the placement and length of the layers may vary depending on your hair's texture, density, and natural movement.

How often should I get my layers trimmed?

It's recommended to get your layers trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and volume of your haircut.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

Once you have your new layered haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its volume and shape. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add even more volume to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow-dry your hair upside down to add extra volume.
  • Consider using a texturizing spray to enhance the layers and add more movement to your hair.

So, there you have it – layers can definitely add volume to thick hair! If you're ready to try out this haircutting technique, be sure to find a stylist who specializes in layering thick hair and follow these tips for the best results.
