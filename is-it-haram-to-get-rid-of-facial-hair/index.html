Is It Haram To Get Rid Of Facial Hair Exploring The Islamic Perspective

Written by William Jun 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Facial hair is a prominent feature in men's appearance. However, some men prefer to get rid of facial hair for various reasons, such as hygiene, aesthetics, or personal preferences. But, is it haram to get rid of facial hair? Let's explore the Islamic perspective on this matter.

What Does Islam Say About Facial Hair?

In Islam, facial hair is considered a natural and essential feature of men's appearance. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) encouraged men to grow beards and trim their mustaches. He also warned against shaving the beard or letting it grow too long without trimming.

Moreover, scholars have interpreted the Quranic verse that says, "And follow the way of him who turns to me in repentance" (Quran 31:15) as a reference to following the Prophet's example in growing a beard.

Is It Haram to Shave or Trim Facial Hair?

Based on the Islamic teachings mentioned above, shaving or trimming facial hair completely is considered haram (forbidden) for men. However, trimming the mustache to keep it neat and not covering the upper lip is permissible.

Some scholars also allow trimming the beard to maintain a certain length or shape, but not to the extent of shaving it completely. However, there is no consensus on the maximum or minimum length of the beard, and it varies among different schools of thought.

How to Care for Facial Hair According to Islam?

Since facial hair is an essential feature in Islam, it is recommended to take care of it properly. Here are some tips to follow:

1. Wash Your Beard Regularly

Keeping your beard clean is essential for hygiene and appearance. Wash it regularly with mild shampoo, and dry it gently with a towel. Avoid using harsh chemicals or hot water that can damage your skin or hair.

2. Comb Your Beard Daily

Combing your beard regularly helps to remove tangles and knots, and keeps it neat and tidy. Use a soft-bristled brush or comb, and start from the roots to the tips.

3. Apply Beard Oil or Balm

Using beard oil or balm helps to moisturize your skin and hair, and prevent dryness, itchiness, or dandruff. Choose a natural and fragrance-free product that suits your skin type and beard length.

FAQ About Facial Hair in Islam

1. Is it mandatory for men to grow a beard in Islam?

No, it is not mandatory, but it is highly recommended to follow the Prophet's example, and it is considered a sunnah.

2. Can women remove facial hair in Islam?

Yes, women can remove facial hair that is considered excessive or causes harm or discomfort, such as upper lip hair or chin hair. However, they should avoid shaping or styling their eyebrows or imitating men's appearance.

3. What is the ruling on trimming the beard shorter than a fist-length?

There is no consensus on the exact length of the beard, but some scholars allow trimming it shorter than a fist-length if it is for hygiene or necessity, such as if it gets dirty or unmanageable.

Conclusion

In conclusion, getting rid of facial hair completely is considered haram in Islam, but trimming it to maintain a neat and tidy appearance is permissible. It is recommended to take care of your facial hair properly by washing, combing, and applying beard oil or balm. Remember to follow the Islamic teachings and the Prophet's example in your appearance and behavior.
