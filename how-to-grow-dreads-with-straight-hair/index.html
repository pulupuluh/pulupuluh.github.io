How To Grow Dreads With Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dreadlocks, or dreads, are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are a symbol of cultural identity, spirituality, and rebellion. Many people with straight hair may think that they cannot achieve dreadlocks, but that is not true. With some patience, dedication, and proper care, you can grow dreads with straight hair. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to grow dreads with straight hair.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Decide on the Type of Dreads
  • Step 2: Start with Clean Hair
  • Step 3: Section Your Hair
  • Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair
  • Step 5: Twist and Secure Your Hair
  • Step 6: Maintain Your Dreads

  • FAQs About Growing Dreads with Straight Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Dreadlocks
  • Conclusion

Step 1: Decide on the Type of Dreads

Before you start growing dreads, you need to decide on the type of dreads you want. There are several types of dreadlocks, including:

  • Freeform dreads
  • Crochet dreads
  • Twist and rip dreads
  • Backcombed dreads

The method you choose will depend on your hair type, lifestyle, and personal preference. For straight hair, backcombing is the most popular method.

Step 2: Start with Clean Hair

It's important to start with clean hair before you start the dreadlocking process. Shampoo your hair thoroughly to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup. Avoid using conditioner as it can make your hair too soft and slippery.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small squares using a comb. The size of the sections will depend on the size of the dreads you want. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Backcombing involves teasing the hair backwards towards the roots to create knots. Take one section of hair and backcomb it from the tips to the roots using a fine-toothed comb. Repeat this process until all sections of hair are backcombed.

Step 5: Twist and Secure Your Hair

After backcombing, twist each section of hair tightly in the same direction until it forms a dreadlock. Use a rubber band or hair tie to secure the end of each dread. Be careful not to make the rubber band too tight as it can damage your hair.

Step 6: Maintain Your Dreads

To maintain your dreads, you need to take good care of them. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using conditioner or heavy oils
  • Retwist your dreads regularly to prevent them from unraveling
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz
  • Avoid using heat styling tools

Tips for Growing Dreads with Straight Hair

Here are some additional tips for growing dreads with straight hair:

  • Be patient. It can take several months or even years for your dreads to fully mature.
  • Avoid using wax as it can cause buildup and attract dirt.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a dreadlock accelerator to speed up the locking process.

FAQs About Growing Dreads with Straight Hair

Can I grow dreads with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can grow dreads with relaxed hair. However, it may take longer for your hair to lock due to the chemical processing.

Do I need to shave my head to start growing dreads?

No, you don't need to shave your head to start growing dreads. Backcombing is a popular method for starting dreads on all hair types.

Can I wash my dreads?

Yes, you can wash your dreads. It's important to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using too much water as it can take a long time to dry.

Hair Care Tips for Dreadlocks

To keep your dreads healthy and looking their best, here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using conditioner or heavy oils
  • Retwist your dreads regularly
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Get regular trims

Conclusion

Growing dreads with straight hair may take some time and effort, but it is possible. With the right method and proper care, you can achieve the hairstyle you desire. Remember to be patient, take good care of your dreads, and enjoy the journey!
