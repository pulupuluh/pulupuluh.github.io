How Do You Know If Short Hair Will Look Good On You

Written by William Jun 16, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Short hair has been trending for years and it can be a bold and freeing move for anyone. However, chopping off your locks can be a big step and can be intimidating to some people. You might be thinking, "will short hair look good on me?" and that's a valid concern. In this article, we'll discuss how to determine if short hair will suit you and give you some tips on how to care for your new style.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Determine if Short Hair Will Suit You
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing Short Hair
  • Tips for Short Hair Care
  • FAQs about Short Hair

How to Determine if Short Hair Will Suit You

Before you take the plunge, there are a few things you should consider to know if short hair will look good on you.

1. Face Shape

Your face shape is one of the most important factors to consider when choosing a short hairstyle. Different face shapes suit different haircuts, so it's important to know your face shape to determine which short hairstyles will suit you.

2. Hair Texture

Your hair texture can also affect how short hair will look on you. If you have thick, curly hair, you may want to consider a longer short style to avoid a triangle-shaped head. On the other hand, if you have fine, straight hair, a shorter cut might work better for you.

3. Skin Tone

Your skin tone can also play a role in how well short hair will look on you. If you have a warm skin tone, you may want to consider warmer hair colors like golden blondes or rich browns. If you have a cool skin tone, you may want to try cooler hair colors like platinum blonde or ash brown.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing Short Hair

Now that you know what to consider, here's a step-by-step guide to choosing short hair:

1. Research Short Hairstyles

Look up short hairstyles on social media, magazines, and blogs to get an idea of what you like. Save pictures of hairstyles that catch your eye and show them to your stylist.

2. Consider Your Lifestyle

Think about your daily routine and whether a short hairstyle will fit into your lifestyle. Short hair can be low maintenance, but it may require more frequent trips to the salon for trims.

3. Consult with Your Stylist

Once you've gathered some ideas, schedule a consultation with your stylist to discuss your options. They can help you determine which styles will suit you and your hair type.

4. Take the Plunge

If you're feeling confident and excited about your new short hairstyle, go for it! Remember that hair grows back, so if you don't love it, you can always try something new.

Tips for Short Hair Care

Now that you've got your new short hairstyle, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

1. Use the Right Products

Use products specifically designed for short hair, like texturizing sprays and molding pastes. These products can help add volume and texture to your hair.

2. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Short hair can get greasy quickly, so it's important to wash it regularly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.

3. Get Regular Trims

To keep your short hairstyle looking fresh, get regular trims every 4-6 weeks. This will keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs about Short Hair

1. Will short hair make my face look rounder?

Not necessarily. There are short hairstyles that can actually make your face look slimmer, like a pixie cut with longer bangs.

2. Can I still style my short hair?

Yes! Short hair can be styled in many different ways, like slicked back, textured, or with curls.

3. Will short hair be too cold in the winter?

Not if you wear a hat! Short hair can actually be easier to manage in the winter since you don't have to worry about tangles and knots from scarves and coats.

At the end of the day, the decision to cut your hair short is a personal one. By considering your face shape, hair texture, and skin tone, you can determine if short hair will look good on you. Remember to consult with your stylist and take care of your new style with the right products and regular trims. And most importantly, embrace your new look with confidence!
