How To Start Dreads On Long Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to start dreads on your long curly hair, you've come to the right place. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to get you started. Dreads can be a great way to express yourself, and they can also be low-maintenance. Whether you're starting from scratch or transitioning from loose curls to dreads, this guide will help you achieve your desired look.

Description

Dreads are a hairstyle that involves matting and twisting sections of hair to create a rope-like appearance. They can be done on any hair texture, including long curly hair. Starting dreads on curly hair requires a bit more patience and care than other hair textures, but the end result is worth the effort.

How to Start Dreads on Long Curly Hair: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start your dread journey, make sure your hair is clean. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or residue. This will ensure that your hair is as healthy as possible before you begin the locking process.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small, equal sections using hair ties or clips. The size of the sections will depend on your hair texture and desired size of your dreads.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Using a metal comb, backcomb each section of hair starting from the root and working your way down to the tip. This will create a knotted effect, which is the foundation of your dreadlocks. Make sure to backcomb each section thoroughly to ensure that it locks properly.

Step 4: Twist Your Hair

After backcombing, twist each section of hair in a clockwise direction. This will help to tighten the knots and begin the locking process. Make sure to twist each section tightly to ensure that it stays in place.

Step 5: Secure Your Hair

Once you've twisted each section, secure it with a hair tie or rubber band. This will keep the twists in place and help them to lock over time. Make sure to use a non-damaging hair tie to avoid breakage or damage to your hair.

Step 6: Maintain Your Dreads

Now that you've started your dreads, it's important to maintain them properly. This includes washing your hair regularly, avoiding products that contain silicone or other harsh chemicals, and retwisting your dreads periodically to keep them tight and neat.

Tips for Starting Dreads on Long Curly Hair

Starting dreads on long curly hair can be a bit more challenging than other hair textures, but with these tips, you'll be well on your way to achieving your desired look:

  • Be patient - dreads take time to form, so don't get discouraged if your hair doesn't lock up right away.
  • Avoid using wax or other heavy products, as they can cause buildup and prevent your hair from locking.
  • Retwist your hair regularly to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer, as the heat can cause damage to your hair.
  • Invest in a good shampoo and conditioner designed for dreadlocks to keep your hair healthy and clean.

FAQs

1. How long does it take to start dreads on long curly hair?

The length of time it takes to start dreads on long curly hair can vary depending on your hair texture and how well you maintain them. Generally, it can take anywhere from a few months to a year to fully lock your hair.

2. Can I start dreads on long curly hair without using a metal comb?

While a metal comb is the most effective tool for backcombing your hair, you can also use a brush or your fingers to create the knotted effect. Keep in mind that using a brush or your fingers may take longer to achieve the desired result.

3. How often should I wash my hair after starting dreads?

You should wash your hair regularly to keep your dreads clean and healthy. Aim to wash your hair at least once a week, but you can wash it more often if needed.

Hair Care for Dreads on Long Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and neat-looking dreads. Here are some tips to keep your dreadlocks looking their best:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner designed for dreadlocks.
  • Avoid using products that contain silicone or other heavy chemicals, as they can cause buildup and prevent your hair from locking.
  • Retwist your hair regularly to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer, as the heat can cause damage to your hair.
  • Avoid sleeping on wet hair, as this can cause mildew and unpleasant odors.

Starting dreads on long curly hair can be a fun and exciting journey. With patience and care, you can achieve the look you desire and enjoy your new style for years to come.
