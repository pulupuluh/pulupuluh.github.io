How To Cut Your Own Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 27, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the salon every time you need a trim or a new hairstyle? Cutting your own natural hair can be intimidating, but with a little patience and practice, it can be done. In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to cut your own natural hair. We will cover everything from how to prepare your hair, tools you will need, and step-by-step instructions on how to cut your hair. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

How to Cut Your Own Natural Hair

The first thing you need to do is determine what type of cut you want. Do you want a trim, a new style, or just a touch-up? Once you have decided on the type of cut you want, it's time to get started.

Tools You Will Need

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the necessary tools. Here are some of the basic tools you will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Mirror

Once you have gathered all the tools, it's time to prepare your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Dry your hair using a towel, and then detangle it using a wide-tooth comb. Divide your hair into sections, and clip or tie each section to keep it out of the way.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want to cut your hair. If you're not sure, start with a small trim and work your way up.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take one section of your hair, and comb it straight. Hold the section of hair between your fingers, and cut off the desired length. Make sure you cut straight across, and avoid cutting at an angle.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After cutting a section of hair, release it and check your progress. If you need to cut more, repeat the previous step until you achieve the desired length.

Step 5: Repeat the Process

Repeat the cutting process on all sections of your hair until you have achieved your desired style.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you cut your own natural hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as it can be difficult to fix mistakes.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up.
  • Take breaks if you need to.

FAQ

Can I Cut My Own Natural Hair?

Yes, you can cut your own natural hair. It may take some practice, but with patience and the right tools, anyone can do it.

How Often Do I Need to Cut My Hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks, but it ultimately depends on your hair's growth rate and the style you desire.

Can I Cut My Hair While It's Wet?

It's better to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it is.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for hair care after cutting your hair:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Cutting your own natural hair can be a rewarding experience. With the right tools, preparation, and patience, you can achieve a beautiful hairstyle from the comfort of your own home. Happy cutting!
