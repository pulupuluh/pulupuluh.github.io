How To Wash Permed Hair Male A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Aug 28, 2023 · 7 min read

If you are a male with permed hair, you may be wondering how to properly wash and care for your hair. Permed hair requires special attention and care, and it is important to know the right techniques to keep your hair healthy and looking great. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to wash permed hair male, as well as some tips and FAQ to help you maintain your hair’s health and appearance.

How to Wash Permed Hair Male: Step-by-Step Guide

Washing permed hair requires a gentle and careful approach to prevent damage to the hair. Here are the steps to follow when washing your permed hair:

Step 1: Pre-wash Preparation

Before washing your permed hair, it is important to prepare it first. Start by brushing your hair gently to remove any tangles or knots. This will help prevent any further damage to your hair during washing.

Step 2: Wet Your Hair

Once your hair is tangle-free, wet your hair with warm water. Make sure that your hair is fully wet from root to tip.

Step 3: Apply Shampoo

Apply a small amount of shampoo to your palm and massage it onto your scalp gently. Do not rub or tug on your hair, as this can cause damage to the perm.

Step 4: Rinse Thoroughly

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water until all the shampoo is removed. Make sure that there is no shampoo residue left in your hair.

Step 5: Apply Conditioner

Apply a small amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use your fingers or a wide-toothed comb to distribute the conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

Step 6: Leave the Conditioner In

Leave the conditioner in your hair for a few minutes to allow it to penetrate your hair shaft.

Step 7: Rinse with Cold Water

Rinse your hair with cold water to seal the cuticles and prevent frizz. Make sure that all the conditioner is removed.

Step 8: Dry Your Hair

Gently pat your hair with a towel to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, as this can cause damage to the perm.

Step 9: Apply Styling Products

Apply your preferred styling products to your hair, such as mousse or gel. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Washing Permed Hair Male

Here are some tips to help you maintain your permed hair’s health and appearance:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage to the perm.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can cause the perm to loosen or fall out.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools, such as blow dryers or flat irons, as these can damage the perm.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and damage to your hair while sleeping.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I wash my permed hair?

A: It is recommended to wash permed hair once or twice a week to prevent the perm from loosening or falling out.

Q: Can I use a deep conditioner on my permed hair?

A: Yes, you can use a deep conditioner on your permed hair once a week to keep it healthy and moisturized.

Q: How do I prevent my perm from becoming frizzy?

A: To prevent your perm from becoming frizzy, use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, avoid using hot water, use a wide-toothed comb, and use a silk or satin pillowcase.

Hair Care for Permed Hair Male

To maintain your permed hair’s health and appearance, it is important to follow a regular hair care routine. Here are some tips to help you care for your permed hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase
  • Apply a deep conditioner once a week
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized

By following these tips and techniques, you can keep your permed hair healthy, strong, and looking great.
