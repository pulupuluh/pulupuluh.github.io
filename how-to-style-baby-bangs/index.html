How To Style Baby Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Mar 05, 2023 · 8 min read
If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, baby bangs are a great way to do so. These short, wispy bangs can give your look an edgy and youthful vibe. But styling baby bangs can be tricky – they require a bit more maintenance than regular bangs. In this article, we'll go over how to style baby bangs, including tips and hair care advice.

How to Style Baby Bangs

Styling baby bangs can be a bit more challenging than regular bangs, but with the right technique, it can be done. Here are the steps to styling baby bangs: 1. Start with clean, dry hair. Baby bangs tend to look best when they're freshly washed and blow-dried. 2. Section off your hair. Use a comb to create a section at the front of your hairline – this will be where your baby bangs will be. 3. Cut your baby bangs. If you haven't already cut your baby bangs, now is the time to do so. Use sharp scissors and cut your bangs to the desired length, keeping in mind that they will likely shrink up a bit once styled. 4. Style your baby bangs. There are a few different ways to style baby bangs, including using a round brush and blow dryer, a flat iron, or simply air-drying. We'll go over each method in more detail in the next section.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for styling your baby bangs using each of the different methods: 1. Round Brush and Blow Dryer: - Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair - Take a small section of your baby bangs and wrap them around a round brush - Use a blow dryer on high heat to dry the section while still wrapped around the brush - Repeat with the rest of your baby bangs, making sure to wrap them in the same direction for a uniform look - Finish with a light hairspray to keep in place 2. Flat Iron: - Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair - Take a small section of your baby bangs and clamp them with a flat iron at the roots - Slowly glide the flat iron down the length of the section - Repeat with the rest of your baby bangs, making sure to clamp them all in the same direction for a uniform look - Finish with a light hairspray to keep in place 3. Air-Drying: - Apply a styling cream or mousse to your hair - Use your fingers to gently tousle your baby bangs and let them air-dry naturally - Once dry, use a light hairspray to keep in place

Tips for Styling Baby Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when styling your baby bangs: - Use a light hand when applying products to your baby bangs – too much can weigh them down and make them look greasy - Avoid cutting your baby bangs too short – they may shrink up even more once styled - If you're using a round brush and blow dryer, make sure to wrap your baby bangs tightly around the brush to create a smooth, curled look - If you're using a flat iron, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to avoid damage to your hair - Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles – baby bangs can be versatile and look great with updos, ponytails, and more

FAQs

Q: Can I style my baby bangs with a curling iron? A: You can, but it may be more difficult to get a uniform look since baby bangs are so short. It's best to stick with a round brush and blow dryer or a flat iron. Q: How often do I need to trim my baby bangs? A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows, but you'll likely need to trim your baby bangs every 2-3 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Hair Care for Baby Bangs

To keep your baby bangs looking their best, here are some hair care tips: - Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid over-drying your scalp - Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils - Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized - Avoid using too much heat on your baby bangs – use a heat protectant spray and try to limit heat styling to once or twice a week - Trim your baby bangs regularly to keep them looking fresh Styling baby bangs can take some practice, but with the right technique and hair care routine, you can rock this edgy look. Try out different styling methods and find what works best for you – and don't forget to have fun with it!
