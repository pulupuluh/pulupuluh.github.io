What Style Of Glasses Look Best On Me A Guide To Finding The Perfect Pair

Written by Eveline Mar 02, 2023 · 10 min read

Are you in the market for a new pair of glasses but unsure of what style would suit you best? Look no further! In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about finding the perfect pair of glasses that not only complements your face shape but also fits your personal style.

Description

Glasses can be both practical and fashionable, but it's important to choose a style that flatters your face. The right pair of glasses can enhance your features and elevate your overall look. With so many styles to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide which one is right for you. This guide will help you navigate the world of eyewear and find the perfect pair for your face shape, skin tone, and personal style.

How to

The first step in finding the perfect pair of glasses is determining your face shape. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Once you've identified your face shape, you can start looking for frames that will complement and balance your features.

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you're in luck! Almost any style of glasses will look great on you. Oval faces are well-proportioned and balanced, so you can choose frames that are wide or narrow, round or square, and still look great. Just make sure the frames are not too big or too small for your face.

Round Face Shape

Round faces have soft, curvy lines with no angles. To balance out the roundness, choose frames that are wider than your face and have angular lines, such as rectangular or square frames. Avoid small frames or frames with rounded edges, as they will only emphasize the roundness of your face.

Square Face Shape

Square faces have strong, angular features with a prominent jawline and forehead. To soften your features, choose frames that are round or oval-shaped. Look for frames that are slightly wider than your face and have a curved design. Avoid square or rectangular frames, as they will only make your face look more angular.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart-shaped face, your forehead is wider than your jawline, and your chin is pointy. To balance out your features, choose frames that are wider at the bottom than the top. Look for frames that are round, oval, or cat-eye shaped. Avoid frames that are too heavy or have a lot of detail on the top, as they will draw attention to your forehead.

Diamond Face Shape

Diamond faces have narrow foreheads and jawlines, with wide cheekbones. To enhance your cheekbones and balance out your features, choose frames that have detailing or accents on the browline. Look for frames that are oval or cat-eye shaped. Avoid frames that are too narrow or too wide for your face.

Oblong Face Shape

Oblong faces have long and narrow features, with a high forehead and narrow chin. To make your face appear shorter, choose frames that have a deep, square shape. Look for frames that have a bold design or detailing on the temples. Avoid frames that are too small or too narrow, as they will only elongate your face.

Step by Step

Now that you know your face shape and which frames will suit you best, it's time to choose a style that fits your personal taste. Here are some popular styles to consider:

Classic

Classic frames are timeless and versatile, perfect for everyday wear. They usually have a simple design with a neutral color, such as black, brown, or tortoise shell.

Modern

Modern frames have a sleek and minimalist design, often with a metal or plastic frame. They come in a variety of colors and shapes, from round to square to hexagonal.

Retro

Retro frames have a vintage-inspired design, often with bold colors or patterns. They come in a variety of shapes, from round to cat-eye to square.

Sporty

Sporty frames are designed for active lifestyles, with durable materials and a comfortable fit. They often have a wraparound design and come in bright colors.

Tips

When choosing a pair of glasses, keep these tips in mind:

  • Choose a frame that fits your face correctly. The glasses should not slide down your nose or pinch your temples.
  • Consider your skin tone when choosing the color of the frames. Warm skin tones look best with frames in brown, gold, or red, while cool skin tones look best with frames in black, silver, or blue.
  • Choose frames that complement your personal style, whether that's classic, modern, retro, or sporty.
  • If you're unsure about which frames to choose, ask a friend or family member for their opinion.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing glasses:

Can I wear glasses with any face shape?

No, different face shapes require different styles of glasses to flatter and balance their features.

Can I wear glasses with any outfit?

Yes, glasses can complement any outfit and add a stylish touch to your look.

How do I clean my glasses?

Use a microfiber cloth and lens cleaner to clean your glasses. Avoid using paper towels or clothing, as they can scratch the lenses.

Hair Care

When choosing glasses, it's important to consider how they will look with your hair. If you have long hair, choose frames that won't get tangled or caught in your hair. If you have short hair, choose frames that will complement your hairstyle and not overpower it. Consider the color of your hair when choosing the color of the frames. Frames in a similar color to your hair will create a cohesive look.

Now that you know what style of glasses look best on you, it's time to start shopping! Remember to choose frames that flatter your face shape, complement your personal style, and fit correctly. With the right pair of glasses, you can enhance your features and elevate your overall look.
