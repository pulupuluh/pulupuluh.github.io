How To Start Dreadlocks With Short Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Mar 30, 2023 · 8 min read

If you’ve always wanted to rock a head full of dreadlocks, but you have short hair, then you’re in luck because, in this article, we’re going to teach you how to start dreadlocks with short hair. Whether you’re going for a neat, uniform look or you prefer the organic, freeform style, we’ve got you covered. Read on to find out how to get started with your dreadlock journey.

Description

Dreadlocks are a hairstyle that has been around for centuries and is deeply rooted in African culture. They are formed by matting or braiding hair and allowing it to lock together over time. The process of starting dreadlocks can be a bit intimidating, especially if you have short hair, but with patience and dedication, anyone can achieve their desired look.

How to Start Dreadlocks with Short Hair

Step 1: Decide Your Dreadlock Style

The first step in starting dreadlocks with short hair is to decide on the style you want to achieve. You can opt for traditional, uniform locks, or you can go for the freeform, organic look. Knowing what you want will help guide you through the process.

Step 2: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start, you’ll need to gather all the supplies you’ll need. This includes a comb, hair clips, rubber bands, a locking agent, and a dreadlock shampoo. Make sure you have everything you need before you begin.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

The next step is to section your hair. Use the comb to create even, small sections of hair. You can use hair clips to keep the sections separate.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Take one section of hair and backcomb it with the comb. Start at the roots and work your way down to the tips. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Roll Your Hair

After backcombing, you’ll need to roll your hair into a tight cylinder shape. Use your fingers to roll the hair, or you can use a locking tool to help create the shape.

Step 6: Use Rubber Bands

Use rubber bands to keep the rolled hair in place. Make sure the rubber bands are tight enough to hold the hair in place but not so tight that they cause discomfort.

Step 7: Apply Locking Agent

Apply a locking agent to each section of hair to help hold the dreadlocks in place. You can use a gel-based locking agent or a wax-based locking agent, depending on your preference.

Step 8: Be Patient

Starting dreadlocks with short hair requires patience. You’ll need to wait for your hair to grow and for the locks to form naturally. Avoid using a lot of products or manipulating your hair too much, as this can cause damage and slow down the locking process.

Tips for Starting Dreadlocks with Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you start your dreadlock journey:

  • Be patient and don’t rush the process
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol or other harsh chemicals
  • Wash your hair regularly with a dreadlock shampoo
  • Avoid sleeping on wet hair
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz
  • Consider wearing a hat or head wrap to protect your hair from the elements

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with short hair:

Can I start dreadlocks with very short hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with very short hair. The process may take longer, but with patience and dedication, you can achieve your desired look.

Do I need to cut my hair to start dreadlocks?

No, you do not need to cut your hair to start dreadlocks. In fact, it’s better to let your hair grow out a bit before starting the process.

How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

Dreadlocks can take anywhere from a few months to a few years to form, depending on your hair type, length, and the method you use to start them.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once your dreadlocks have formed, it’s important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a dreadlock shampoo
  • Avoid using conditioner or other products that can loosen the locks
  • Use a hairdryer or sit in the sun to dry your hair thoroughly
  • Avoid using tight hair ties, as they can cause breakage
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized

Starting dreadlocks with short hair may seem daunting, but with the right tools, techniques, and mindset, anyone can achieve their desired look. Follow these steps, be patient, and above all, have fun with your new hairstyle.
