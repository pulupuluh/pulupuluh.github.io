Best Hair Color For 50 Year Olds A Guide For Refreshing Your Hairstyle

Written by Aprilia Jun 07, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're in your 50s and looking for a way to refresh your hairstyle, changing your hair color can be a great option. However, with so many hair colors to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide which one is right for you. In this article, we'll discuss the best hair color for 50 year olds, as well as how to choose the right shade, tips for maintaining your color, and more.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Choose the Best Hair Color for 50 Year Olds
  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Coloring Your Hair at Home
  3. Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Color
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips for Colored Hair

How to Choose the Best Hair Color for 50 Year Olds

When it comes to choosing the best hair color for 50 year olds, there are a few things to keep in mind:

Consider Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone can have a big impact on how certain hair colors look on you. Generally, people with warm skin tones look best in warm hair colors like golden blonde, honey brown, and copper, while those with cool skin tones should opt for cooler shades like ash blonde, platinum, and burgundy.

Think About Your Natural Hair Color

While you don't necessarily need to stick to your natural hair color, it's a good idea to consider it when choosing a new shade. If you have dark hair, going too light can be damaging and require a lot of maintenance, while going too dark can be too dramatic. If you have light hair, going too dark can also be a big change.

Consider Your Lifestyle

When choosing a new hair color, it's important to consider your lifestyle. If you have a busy schedule and don't have a lot of time to spend on maintenance, you may want to opt for a low-maintenance shade like a natural-looking balayage or highlights. If you're retired and have more time to devote to your hair, you may be able to consider a more high-maintenance shade.

Step-by-Step Guide to Coloring Your Hair at Home

If you're looking to save money and color your hair at home, here's a step-by-step guide to getting the best results:

Step 1: Choose Your Color

Choose the shade of hair color that you would like to use. Take into consideration your natural hair color, skin tone, and lifestyle.

Step 2: Gather Your Supplies

Make sure you have all the necessary supplies, including the hair color, gloves, a brush, a bowl, a comb, and a timer.

Step 3: Do a Strand Test

Before applying the hair color all over your head, do a strand test to make sure you like the color and to check for any allergic reactions.

Step 4: Apply the Color

Section your hair and apply the hair color to each section, starting at the roots and working your way down. Make sure to follow the instructions on the hair color box.

Step 5: Rinse and Condition

Once the color has set, rinse it out thoroughly and apply the conditioner that came with the hair color to help seal in the color.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Color

To keep your new hair color looking fresh and vibrant, follow these tips:

Use Color-Safe Shampoo and Conditioner

Regular shampoos and conditioners can strip your hair of color and cause it to fade faster. Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your color.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can cause your hair color to fade faster. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can also cause your hair color to fade. If you're going to be spending time outdoors, wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray.

FAQs

What is the best hair color for 50 year olds?

The best hair color for 50 year olds depends on your skin tone, natural hair color, and lifestyle. Generally, warm hair colors like golden blonde, honey brown, and copper look best on those with warm skin tones, while cool hair colors like ash blonde, platinum, and burgundy look best on those with cool skin tones.

Can I color my hair at home?

Yes, you can color your hair at home. Just make sure to follow the instructions on the hair color box and do a strand test beforehand.

How often should I touch up my hair color?

How often you touch up your hair color depends on the shade you chose and how fast your hair grows. Generally, you should touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Colored Hair

Colored hair requires special care to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Colored hair can become dry and brittle, so it's important to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep it moisturized.

Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause your color to fade faster. Try to wash your hair every other day or every few days.

Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair look dull.

By following these tips, you can choose the best hair color for 50 year olds, color your hair at home, and maintain your new color to keep it looking fresh and vibrant for longer.
