Why Is My Curly Hair Going Away

Written by Oliver Jul 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you may have noticed that it's not as curly as it used to be. There are many reasons why your curly hair may be going away, including age, hormonal changes, heat styling, and chemical treatments. In this blog post, we'll discuss the causes of curly hair loss and provide tips on how to prevent it.

Description

Curly hair is characterized by its unique shape and texture. The hair shaft is not straight, but rather has a spiral or wave pattern. This texture is created by the shape of the hair follicle, which is more oval than round. The natural oils produced by the scalp have a harder time traveling down the hair shaft in curly hair, which can make it more prone to dryness and damage.

How to

If you're experiencing curly hair loss, there are a few things you can do to prevent it. One of the most important things is to avoid heat styling as much as possible. Heat can damage the hair shaft and cause it to lose its natural curl. If you must use heat, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Another way to prevent curly hair loss is to avoid chemical treatments such as relaxers and perms. These treatments can permanently alter the shape of the hair follicle, making it impossible to regain your natural curl. If you've already had a chemical treatment, you may need to cut off the damaged hair and start fresh.

Finally, it's important to take good care of your curly hair by using products that are specifically designed for your hair type. Look for products that are moisturizing and contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil. Avoid products that contain alcohol or sulfates, as these can further dry out your hair.

Step by Step

If you're looking to maintain your curly hair, here are some steps you can follow:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Massage the shampoo into your scalp and rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Condition your hair

Apply a moisturizing conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Apply a leave-in conditioner

After rinsing out the conditioner, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair. This will help to moisturize your hair and prevent frizz.

Step 4: Style your hair

If you're looking to style your hair, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair. This will help to prevent frizz and maintain your natural curl.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent curly hair loss:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as ponytails and braids.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

FAQ

Q: Can hormonal changes cause curly hair loss?

A: Yes, hormonal changes such as pregnancy, menopause, and thyroid disorders can all cause changes in hair texture and lead to curly hair loss.

Q: Is it possible to regain my natural curl after chemical treatments?

A: In some cases, it may be possible to regain your natural curl after chemical treatments, but it will depend on the severity of the damage. You may need to cut off the damaged hair and wait for it to grow back.

Hair Care

Overall, it's important to take good care of your curly hair to prevent loss and damage. Avoid heat styling and chemical treatments as much as possible, and use products that are specifically designed for curly hair. With the right care, you can maintain your natural curl and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
