How To Find The Most Flattering Bathing Suit In 2023

Written by Petter Jun 03, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Summer is just around the corner, and it's time to start thinking about beach days and pool parties. But if you're like most people, the thought of shopping for a bathing suit can be daunting. With so many styles and designs to choose from, it can be hard to know where to start. In this article, we'll cover how to find the most flattering bathing suit for your body type, so you can feel confident and beautiful all summer long.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Know Your Body Type
  • Step 2: Choose the Right Style
  • Step 3: Consider the Fabric and Pattern
  • Step 4: Try Before You Buy
  • Tips for Finding the Most Flattering Bathing Suit
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for the Beach

Step 1: Know Your Body Type

The first step in finding the most flattering bathing suit is to know your body type. There are four main body types: apple, pear, hourglass, and rectangle. Knowing which one you have can help you choose a bathing suit that accentuates your best features and downplays any areas you're not as confident about.

If you're not sure what your body type is, stand in front of a mirror and take a good look at yourself. Are your hips wider than your shoulders? You might be a pear shape. Do you have a defined waist and curvy hips? That's the hourglass shape. Once you know your body type, you can move on to choosing the right style.

Step 2: Choose the Right Style

Now that you know your body type, it's time to choose a bathing suit style that flatters your figure. Here are some tips:

For Apple Shapes:

Choose a one-piece bathing suit with ruching or gathering around the waist to create the illusion of a smaller midsection. V-necklines and halter tops can also help elongate the torso.

For Pear Shapes:

Look for a bathing suit with a high-cut leg to make your legs look longer and more slender. A top with ruffles or embellishments can draw attention to your upper body and balance out your proportions.

For Hourglass Shapes:

Choose a bathing suit that accentuates your waist, such as a belted one-piece or a bikini with a high-waisted bottom. Avoid anything too loose or baggy, as it can hide your curves.

For Rectangle Shapes:

Look for a bathing suit that creates curves, such as a bikini with ruffles or a one-piece with cutouts at the waist. You can also try a monokini, which has the appearance of a one-piece from the back but is cut out at the sides to create the illusion of a smaller waist.

Step 3: Consider the Fabric and Pattern

The fabric and pattern of your bathing suit can also make a difference in how flattering it looks. Here are some tips:

Dark Colors: Dark colors, such as black, navy, and dark green, can be slimming and make you look more toned.

Vertical Stripes: Vertical stripes can elongate the body and make you look taller and slimmer.

Small Patterns: Small patterns can be more flattering than large ones, as they can make you look smaller overall.

Matte Fabrics: Matte fabrics can be more slimming than shiny ones, as they don't reflect as much light.

Step 4: Try Before You Buy

Before you buy a bathing suit, make sure to try it on and move around in it. Bend over, jump up and down, and walk around to make sure it stays in place and feels comfortable. If you're buying online, make sure to check the size chart and read reviews from other customers to get a better idea of how it fits.

Tips for Finding the Most Flattering Bathing Suit

  • Don't be afraid to mix and match tops and bottoms to find the perfect fit.
  • If you're self-conscious about your midsection, try a one-piece with a tummy control panel.
  • If you're busty, look for a bathing suit with underwire or molded cups for support.
  • If you're petite, avoid bathing suits with too much fabric or ruffles, as they can overwhelm your frame.
  • If you're tall, look for bathing suits with adjustable straps or longer torso options.

FAQs

What if I don't know my body type?

It's okay if you're not sure what your body type is. You can always try on different bathing suit styles and see what looks best on you. Don't be afraid to ask a sales associate for help, as they can often offer valuable advice.

What if I'm plus-sized?

There are plenty of stylish and flattering bathing suits available in plus sizes. Look for styles with ruching or shirring around the midsection, as they can help create a more hourglass shape. High-waisted bottoms and one-pieces with cutouts can also be very flattering.

What if I have a small bust?

If you have a small bust, look for bathing suits with ruffles or embellishments on the top to create the illusion of more volume. You can also try a push-up or padded top for added lift.

Hair Care Tips for the Beach

Now that you've found the perfect bathing suit, it's time to think about your hair. Here are some tips for keeping your locks looking great at the beach:

  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil before you hit the sand to help protect your hair from the sun and salt water.
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun.
  • Rinse your hair with fresh water after swimming to remove any salt or chlorine.
  • Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and straighteners, as they can damage your hair in the heat.
  • Try a messy bun or braid to keep your hair out of your face and prevent tangles.

With these tips, you're sure to find the most flattering bathing suit for your body type and feel confident and beautiful all summer long. Happy shopping!
