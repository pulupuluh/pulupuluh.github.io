How To Cut Straight Male Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cutting straight male hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're new to cutting hair. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut that will make your client look and feel great. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to cut straight male hair, from preparation to finishing touches.

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Sectioning the Hair
  • Cutting the Hair
  • Blending and Texturizing
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Straight Male Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and clippers with guards of various sizes. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water and a towel handy.

Next, have your client sit in a comfortable chair, and drape a cape or towel over them to catch any hair. It's also a good idea to ask your client what kind of haircut they want, so you have a clear idea of what they're looking for.

Sectioning the Hair

The first step in cutting straight male hair is to section the hair. Start by combing the hair straight down, and then part it down the middle from the forehead to the nape of the neck. Clip one side of the hair out of the way, and start cutting the other side.

Take a small section of hair from the top of the head, and use your fingers or a comb to hold it straight out from the head. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, working from the tips of the hair inward.

Continue cutting small sections of hair, moving from the top of the head to the sides and back. Make sure to keep your scissors angled downward, and take care not to cut too much off at once.

Cutting the Hair

Once you've cut the top and sides of the hair, it's time to move on to the back. Comb the hair straight down, and use your clippers to trim the hair to the desired length. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard, depending on the length of hair you want to leave.

Work your way up the back of the head, using your clippers to create a smooth, even cut. Take care not to cut too high up the back of the head, as this can create an uneven line.

Blending and Texturizing

Once you've cut the hair to the desired length, it's time to blend and texturize the hair. Use your scissors to cut into the hair, creating texture and movement. You can also use thinning shears to remove bulk and create a more natural look.

Blend the hair on the sides and back of the head by using your clippers without a guard. Move the clippers upward from the neck to the hairline, creating a smooth transition between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Finishing Touches

Once you've blended and texturized the hair, it's time for the finishing touches. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs, and check for any uneven spots or lines. Use your clippers to clean up the hairline around the ears and neck.

Finally, spray the hair with water and comb it into place. Style the hair as desired, and your client is ready to go!

Tips for Cutting Straight Male Hair

  • Start with a longer guard on your clippers, and work your way down to a shorter guard as you go.
  • Take your time, and don't rush the process.
  • Use your fingers or a comb to hold the hair in place as you cut it.
  • Keep your scissors angled downward to create a natural, blended look.
  • Use thinning shears to remove bulk and create texture.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut straight male hair?

A: It depends on the length of the hair and the style the client is going for. Generally, hair should be cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Q: Can I cut straight male hair with just scissors?

A: Yes, you can cut straight male hair with just scissors, but it's easier to use clippers to create an even, blended look.

Hair Care

After the haircut, it's important to take care of the hair to keep it looking great. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your client's hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling the hair. Regular trims will also help to keep the hair looking healthy and well-groomed.

Cutting straight male hair can be a challenging task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great result. Follow these steps, and your client will leave your chair looking and feeling great.
