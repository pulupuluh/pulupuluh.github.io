How To Get Dreads With Straight Hair Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Mar 24, 2023 · 10 min read

Are you tired of your straight, short hair and looking for a change? Dreadlocks are a great way to switch up your style and add some edge to your look. However, many people with straight, short hair assume that they can't achieve dreadlocks. The good news is that it's possible, and we're here to show you how.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Dreads with Straight Hair Short
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Dreadlocks are a hairstyle that involves twisting and matting hair together to form ropes or cords. They can be achieved on all hair types, including straight and short hair. The process of getting dreads with straight hair short is different from getting dreads with curly or afro-textured hair, but it's not impossible.

How to Get Dreads with Straight Hair Short

If you have straight, short hair and want to get dreadlocks, you have a few options:

  • Backcombing: This involves teasing your hair backwards to create knots that eventually form into dreadlocks.
  • Twist and Rip: This involves sectioning your hair and twisting each section before ripping it apart to create knots that eventually form into dreadlocks.
  • Crochet: This involves using a crochet hook to pull hair through itself to create knots that eventually form into dreadlocks.

The method you choose will depend on your hair type, personal preference, and the time you have available. Backcombing and twist and rip are the most popular methods for getting dreadlocks with straight hair short.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for getting dreadlocks with backcombing:

  1. Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Towel dry your hair until it's damp, but not dripping wet.
  3. Section your hair into small, even sections using clips or hair ties.
  4. Take one section of hair and backcomb it using a fine-tooth comb. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up towards the roots, teasing the hair backwards to create knots.
  5. Repeat this process for each section of hair.
  6. Once all sections of hair have been backcombed, use a dread wax or locking gel to help the knots stay in place.
  7. Twist each section of hair clockwise until it starts to form a dreadlock.
  8. Continue twisting until the dreadlock is tight and secure.
  9. Repeat this process for each section of hair.
  10. Use a hair tie or rubber band to secure the ends of each dreadlock.
  11. Leave the rubber bands in for a few days to help the dreadlocks set.
  12. After a few days, remove the rubber bands and use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair.

Here's a step-by-step guide for getting dreadlocks with twist and rip:

  5. Hold the twisted section of hair at the root and pull it apart to create knots.
  6. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

  13. After a few days, remove the rubber bands and use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Once you've achieved dreadlocks, it's important to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair every 2-3 days.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or other drying agents.
  • Avoid using a conditioner on your scalp, as it can cause buildup and mildew.
  • Use a special dreadlock wax or locking gel to help keep your dreads in place.
  • Avoid using a hairdryer, as it can cause your dreads to unravel.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to avoid snagging your dreads while you sleep.
  • Be patient – it can take several months for your dreads to fully mature.

FAQs

Can I get dreads with straight hair short?

Yes, you can. Backcombing and twist and rip are popular methods for getting dreadlocks with straight hair short.

How long does it take to get dreadlocks with straight hair short?

It can take several hours to get dreadlocks with backcombing or twist and rip, and several months for your dreads to fully mature.

How do I maintain my dreadlocks?

Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair every 2-3 days, avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or other drying agents, and use a special dreadlock wax or locking gel to help keep your dreads in place.
