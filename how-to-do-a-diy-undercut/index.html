How To Do A Diy Undercut A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 15, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of your same old hairstyle and want to switch things up? The undercut is a trendy and edgy hairstyle that has been popular among both men and women for years. With a little bit of patience and practice, you can easily achieve a DIY undercut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we’ll take you through the steps on how to do a DIY undercut, along with some tips and FAQs to help you achieve the perfect look.

What is an Undercut?

Before diving into the instructions for the DIY undercut, let’s first define what an undercut is. An undercut is a hairstyle where the hair on the sides and back of the head is shaved or cut very short, while the hair on top is left longer. It’s a versatile hairstyle that can be styled in different ways and suits many face shapes and hair types.

How to Do a DIY Undercut

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before starting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need clippers, scissors, a comb, a mirror, and hair clips. It’s also a good idea to have a friend or family member to help you with the back of your head.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It’s easier to work with clean and dry hair, and it will also give you a better idea of your hair’s natural texture and shape.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair from the crown of your head to the back of your ear on both sides. Clip the hair on top of your head out of the way and leave the hair on the sides and back loose.

Step 4: Start with the Sides

Using your clippers, start with a longer guard and work your way down until you get the desired length. Make sure to use the same guard on both sides to keep it even. You can also use scissors to clean up the edges and blend the hair between the top and sides.

Step 5: Move to the Back

Next, move to the back of your head and repeat the same process. It’s easier if you have someone help you to ensure you get an even cut.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

Using your scissors, blend the hair between the top and sides to create a seamless transition. It’s best to use point cutting to achieve a natural look.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you have the desired length and shape, style your hair as desired. You can use a hair dryer, straightener, or curling iron to achieve different looks.

Tips for a Perfect DIY Undercut

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect DIY undercut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use scissors to blend the hair between the top and sides for a natural look.
  • Take your time and be patient. It’s better to cut a little at a time than to cut too much and regret it later.
  • Experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for your hair type.

FAQs About DIY Undercuts

Can I do a DIY undercut on curly hair?

Yes, you can do a DIY undercut on curly hair. However, it’s important to work with your hair’s natural texture and shape to achieve the best results. It’s also a good idea to get advice from a professional stylist before attempting it on your own.

How often should I trim my undercut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain it. On average, it’s recommended to trim an undercut every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Hair Care Tips for Undercuts

After you’ve achieved the perfect DIY undercut, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your undercut looking fresh.

With these tips and instructions, you can easily achieve a DIY undercut at home. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don’t be afraid to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
