What Is Considered A Pixie Cut

Written by Daniel May 12, 2023 Â· 9 min read

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that is typically cropped close to the head and features short, choppy layers. This style became popular in the 1950s, when women began to rebel against traditional gender roles and embrace more daring and unconventional looks. Today, the pixie cut is still a popular choice for women who want a low-maintenance, edgy hairstyle that shows off their confidence and individuality.

Description

The typical pixie cut is cut short around the sides and back, with longer layers on top that can be styled in a number of different ways. This style is often worn with a side-swept fringe or bangs, which can be cut at various lengths to flatter different face shapes. The overall effect is a bold, striking look that is both feminine and modern.

How to Get a Pixie Cut

If you're thinking about getting a pixie cut, there are a few things to consider before you take the plunge. First, it's important to find a stylist who is experienced in cutting short hair, as this can be a tricky and precise process. You should also take the time to research different pixie cut styles and find one that will flatter your face shape and personal style.

When you're ready to get your pixie cut, your stylist will begin by cutting the hair on the sides and back of your head very short, usually with clippers. They will then use scissors to create choppy layers on top, and may add a side-swept fringe or bangs if desired. The whole process can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the complexity of the style.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're interested in trying out a pixie cut for yourself, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step 1: Find a Stylist

Look for a stylist who has experience cutting short hair, and who can help you choose a pixie cut style that will flatter your face shape and personal style.

Step 2: Choose Your Style

Research different pixie cut styles and find one that you love. Consider factors like length, texture, and whether you want a side-swept fringe or bangs.

Step 3: Prepare Your Hair

Before your appointment, make sure your hair is clean and free of any styling products. Your stylist may also ask you to come in with dry hair, as this can make it easier to cut.

Step 4: Get the Cut

During your appointment, your stylist will begin by cutting the hair on the sides and back of your head very short, usually with clippers. They will then use scissors to create choppy layers on top, and may add a side-swept fringe or bangs if desired.

Step 5: Style Your New Cut

Once your cut is complete, your stylist may recommend styling products or techniques to help you achieve your desired look. Experiment with different styles until you find one that you love!

Tips for Maintaining Your Pixie Cut

Once you've got your new pixie cut, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking sharp and stylish. Here are a few tips to help you maintain your new cut:

  • Visit your stylist regularly for touch-ups and trims.
  • Use high-quality styling products that are designed for short hair.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques to find what works best for your hair texture and personal style.
  • Consider investing in a good quality hair dryer or straightener to help you achieve your desired look.
  • Be prepared to spend a little extra time on styling and maintenance, as short hair can require more frequent attention than longer styles.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about pixie cuts:

What face shapes are best suited for a pixie cut?

Pixie cuts can flatter a range of face shapes, but they tend to look best on those with oval or heart-shaped faces. If you have a round or square face, you may want to steer clear of very short cuts that could exaggerate your face shape.

Can I still style my hair if I have a pixie cut?

Absolutely! There are plenty of styling options for pixie cuts, from sleek and polished to messy and tousled. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you.

Will a pixie cut work for all hair types?

Pixie cuts can work for a range of hair types, from fine and straight to thick and curly. However, it's important to work with a stylist who is experienced in cutting short hair, as the process can be different depending on your hair texture.

Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

With a pixie cut, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips to help you care for your new cut:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before styling with hot tools.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain your style.

With the right care and maintenance, a pixie cut can be a bold and beautiful hairstyle that shows off your unique sense of style and confidence. Whether you're looking for a low-maintenance, edgy look or a chic and polished style, a pixie cut might be just what you need to take your hair game to the next level!
