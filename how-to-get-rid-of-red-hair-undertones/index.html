How To Get Rid Of Red Hair Undertones Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jul 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling with red undertones in your hair? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people experience this issue, whether they have naturally red hair or have dyed it that way. Fortunately, there are several ways to get rid of red hair undertones and achieve the color you desire. In this post, we'll explore some tips and tricks for banishing red tones from your hair.

  2. How to Get Rid of Red Hair Undertones: Step by Step

What Causes Red Undertones in Hair?

Red undertones in hair can be caused by a variety of factors. One of the most common is exposure to the sun, which can cause hair to lighten and take on a reddish hue. Another factor is genetics; if you have a family history of red hair, you may be more likely to have red undertones in your own hair.

Dyeing your hair can also contribute to red undertones. If you use a hair dye that contains red pigments, these pigments can linger even after the dye has faded. This can result in an unwanted reddish tint to your hair.

How to Get Rid of Red Hair Undertones: Step by Step

If you're ready to banish red undertones from your hair, follow these steps:

  1. Choose a hair color that is ashy or cool-toned. Look for colors that have a blue or green base, as these will help cancel out any red tones in your hair.
  2. Apply the hair color according to the instructions on the box. Make sure you're using a permanent hair color, as semi-permanent or temporary dyes may not be strong enough to cover red undertones.
  3. Leave the hair color on for the recommended amount of time. Rinse it out thoroughly and condition your hair as usual.
  4. If you still notice red undertones after coloring your hair, you may need to repeat the process or seek professional help from a hair stylist.

Tips for Getting Rid of Red Hair Undertones

Here are some additional tips for banishing red tones from your hair:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can cause your hair color to fade and take on a reddish tint.
  • Use a purple shampoo or conditioner to neutralize any remaining red tones in your hair.
  • Avoid exposure to chlorine and salt water, as these can also cause your hair color to fade and become reddish.
  • Consider getting a color correction treatment from a professional stylist, especially if you have stubborn red undertones that won't go away.

FAQs

Here are some commonly asked questions about getting rid of red hair undertones:

Q: Will using a toner help get rid of red undertones in my hair?

A: Yes, using a toner can help cancel out red tones in your hair. Look for a toner that is specifically designed for cool-toned hair colors.

Q: How often should I use purple shampoo or conditioner to get rid of red undertones?

A: It depends on your hair type and how often you wash your hair. As a general rule, you can use purple shampoo or conditioner once or twice a week to maintain your hair color and neutralize any remaining red tones.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Red Undertones

Here are some tips to help prevent red undertones from appearing in your hair:

  • Wear a hat or use a UV-protective hair product when spending time in the sun.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause color fading.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to protect your hair color and keep it looking vibrant.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can cause your hair color to fade more quickly.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair color appear dull and faded.

By following these tips and tricks, you can get rid of red hair undertones and achieve the hair color you've always wanted. Whether you're a natural redhead or have dyed your hair that way, these techniques can help you achieve a cool-toned, beautiful hair color.
