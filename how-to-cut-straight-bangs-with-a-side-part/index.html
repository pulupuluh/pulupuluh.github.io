How To Cut Straight Bangs With A Side Part

Written by Luffy Apr 03, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking to switch up your hairstyle and add some bangs with a side part? Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve the look you want. In this article, we’ll go over step-by-step instructions on how to cut straight bangs with a side part.

Description

Straight bangs with a side part is a trendy and versatile hairstyle that can suit any facial shape. This style adds dimension to your hair and can make your face look slimmer. However, cutting bangs can be a daunting task, especially if you’ve never done it before. But don’t worry, we’ve got you covered.

How to

Before you begin, make sure you have a pair of sharp haircutting scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and a mirror. It’s also helpful to have a spray bottle with water to keep your hair damp while cutting.

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off the hair you want to cut. Use a fine-tooth comb to create a straight part on the side of your head where you want your bangs to fall. Use the comb to separate the front section of your hair from the rest of your hair.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length of your bangs. It’s better to start with a longer length and gradually trim more if needed. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can’t add hair back once it’s cut.

Step 3: Cut the Bangs

Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across using the sharp scissors. Make sure to cut slowly and carefully, checking the length as you go. It’s best to cut your bangs when your hair is damp, as it’s easier to control and cut.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After cutting, check your bangs for evenness. If you notice any unevenness, trim the longer sections until they’re even. Be careful not to over-cut, as this can result in uneven and choppy bangs.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Once you’re happy with the length and evenness of your bangs, it’s time to style them. Use a blow dryer and a round brush to style your bangs. Brush your bangs to the side of your head where you’ve created the part. Use a little bit of hairspray to keep your bangs in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting straight bangs with a side part:

  • Start with longer bangs and gradually trim more as needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid uneven and choppy bangs.
  • Cut your hair when it’s damp to make it easier to control and cut.
  • Check for evenness after cutting and trim any longer sections to make them even.
  • Style your bangs using a round brush and blow dryer to add volume.

FAQ

Can I cut my bangs when my hair is dry?

It’s best to cut your bangs when your hair is damp, as it’s easier to control and cut. If you cut your bangs when your hair is dry, it can result in uneven and choppy bangs.

How do I create a side part?

Use a fine-tooth comb to create a straight part on the side of your head where you want your bangs to fall. Use the comb to separate the front section of your hair from the rest of your hair.

What if I cut my bangs too short?

If you cut your bangs too short, don’t panic. They’ll grow back eventually. In the meantime, you can style your bangs to the side or use hair accessories to disguise them.

Hair Care

After cutting your bangs, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut straight bangs with a side part, you can experiment with your hairstyle and create a new look. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and follow the steps carefully. Happy cutting!
