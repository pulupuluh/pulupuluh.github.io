How To Layer Medium Hair Yourself Diy Guide

Written by Christine Jun 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle to freshen up your look? Layering medium hair is a great option that can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair. While visiting a salon is one way to achieve this style, it can be expensive. Fortunately, it's possible to layer medium hair yourself at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to create a layered look that suits your face shape and hair type. Let's get started!

How to Layer Medium Hair Yourself

Before you begin, gather the necessary tools: sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also helpful to have a mirror and good lighting. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the natural shape and texture of your hair. If you have curly hair, consider blow-drying it straight to make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Use the comb to part your hair in the middle or on one side, depending on your preference. Clip the top section of your hair up and out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends at an angle. This creates a soft, feathered effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way from the back of your head to the front.

Step 4: Work on the Middle Layer

Release the top section of your hair and clip the bottom layer up. Repeat the same cutting technique on the middle layer of your hair. This will help create more volume and texture.

Step 5: Finish with the Top Layer

Finally, release the last section of your hair and clip the rest up. Cut the top layer of your hair in the same way as the previous layers. Make sure the layers blend seamlessly together.

Tips for Layering Medium Hair Yourself

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small, cautious cuts. You can always cut more if needed, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut. It can be helpful to ask a friend to assist you, especially for hard-to-reach areas.
  • Don't cut too much at once. Take breaks and step back to look at your progress.
  • If you have thick hair, consider thinning shears to remove some of the bulk.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about layering medium hair:

Can I layer my hair if it's already short?

Yes, you can layer hair of any length. However, keep in mind that the shorter your hair, the more dramatic the layers will be.

How often should I layer my hair?

It's recommended to layer your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the style and prevent split ends.

Can I layer my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, but it may be more challenging. Consider straightening your hair first to make it easier to see and cut.

Hair Care for Layered Medium Hair

After you've layered your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Don't wash your hair too frequently, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

Layering medium hair yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience. By following these steps and tips, you can create a look that's unique to you. Good luck!
