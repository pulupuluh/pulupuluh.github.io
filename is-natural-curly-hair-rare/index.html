Is Natural Curly Hair Rare A Comprehensive Guide

Introduction

Curly hair is unique and can be found in different textures, shapes, and colors. Some people are born with natural curly hair, while others might have to resort to hair treatments to obtain this look. However, the question that arises is whether natural curly hair is rare or not. In this comprehensive guide, we will answer this question and provide you with tips on how to take care of your natural curly hair.

Description of Natural Curly Hair

Natural curly hair is characterized by its corkscrew shape and its tendency to frizz. It is often found in people with African, Mediterranean, or Hispanic ancestry, but can also be found in other ethnicities. Natural curly hair can range from loose waves to tight coils, and its texture can vary from fine to coarse.

How Rare is Natural Curly Hair?

It is estimated that only 15-20% of the world's population has natural curly hair. Therefore, it can be considered rare. However, natural curly hair is becoming more common due to the rise of the natural hair movement, which encourages people to embrace their natural hair texture.

How to Identify Natural Curly Hair?

Identifying natural curly hair can be tricky, especially if the hair has been treated with chemicals or heat. However, here are a few tips that can help:

  • Look for a corkscrew shape in the hair strands.
  • Check for frizz and volume in the hair.
  • Observe the hair's elasticity. Natural curly hair will bounce back when pulled.
  • Ask the person if they have ever chemically treated their hair.

Tips for Taking Care of Natural Curly Hair

Taking care of natural curly hair requires a gentle approach. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition the hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair.
  • Detangle the hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.
  • Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to dry the hair, as regular towels can cause frizz.

FAQs

1. Can you get natural curly hair if you have straight hair?

No, you cannot get natural curly hair if you have straight hair. However, you can use hair treatments such as perms or curling irons to achieve a curly look.

2. Is natural curly hair more prone to breakage?

Natural curly hair can be more prone to breakage if not properly taken care of. This is because curly hair tends to be drier and more fragile than straight hair. Therefore, it is important to keep natural curly hair moisturized and avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.

3. Can natural curly hair be straightened?

Yes, natural curly hair can be straightened with a flat iron or a chemical treatment such as a keratin treatment. However, it is important to use heat protectant products and to avoid over-processing the hair.

4. How often should you wash natural curly hair?

It is recommended to wash natural curly hair once or twice a week to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils. However, this can vary depending on the person's hair type and lifestyle.

With these tips and information, you can now identify whether natural curly hair is rare or not and how to take care of it properly. Embrace your natural hair texture and rock those curls!
