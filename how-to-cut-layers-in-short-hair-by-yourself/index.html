How To Cut Layers In Short Hair By Yourself

Written by Petter Aug 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of the same old haircut and want to switch things up? Cutting layers into your short hair can add volume and texture, giving you a fresh new look. And the best part? You can do it yourself at home! In this article, we'll walk you through the steps on how to cut layers in short hair by yourself.

How to Cut Layers in Short Hair by Yourself

Before we get started, there are a few things you'll need:

  • Sharp hair scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Mirror

Once you have all of your supplies ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that your hair is clean and free of any product buildup. Once your hair is dry, comb it through to remove any tangles.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips or ties to keep each section separate. Start by creating a part down the middle of your head, then make another part from ear to ear. You should now have four sections of hair.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin cutting the back section of your hair first. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and use your scissors to cut into the ends at an angle. This will create a layered effect.

Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up to the top of your head. Be sure to cut at an angle to create a gradual and natural-looking effect.

Step 4: Move to the Front

Once you've finished the back section, move on to the front. Take small sections of hair and cut at an angle, following the shape of your face. Be sure to keep the layers longer towards the front of your face for a framing effect.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've finished cutting, use a mirror to check your progress. Look for any uneven areas or spots that need more layering. If you see any, go back and trim them until you're happy with the result.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Short Hair

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with these tips, you'll be a pro in no time:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Cut at an angle to create a natural-looking effect.
  • Be patient and take your time.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress frequently.
  • If you're unsure, start with a longer layer and work your way up to a shorter one.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers in short hair:

Can I cut layers into my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can! However, keep in mind that curly hair can be more difficult to cut evenly. Consider going to a professional stylist if you're unsure.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut layers into my hair if it's thin?

Absolutely! In fact, cutting layers into thin hair can add volume and make it appear fuller.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers in Short Hair

After cutting layers into your short hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a heat protectant when styling with hot tools.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Consider using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to enhance the layered effect.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Now that you know how to cut layers in short hair by yourself, you can experiment with different lengths and styles. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
