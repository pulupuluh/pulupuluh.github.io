Can You Get Faux Locs With Relaxed Hair

Description

Faux locs are a popular protective hairstyle that gives a bohemian and exotic vibe. They are a great alternative to traditional locs, and they can be achieved with different hair types. However, many people wonder if it is possible to get faux locs with relaxed hair. The answer is yes, you can get faux locs with relaxed hair, but there are some things to consider.

How To

The process of getting faux locs with relaxed hair is similar to getting them with natural hair. However, some adjustments may be necessary to ensure that the relaxed hair does not get damaged or break. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before getting faux locs, it is essential to prepare your hair. For relaxed hair, it is recommended to do a protein treatment to strengthen the hair and minimize breakage. You should also deep condition your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent dryness.

Step 2: Choose Your Hair Extensions

For faux locs, you can use different types of hair extensions, such as synthetic hair, human hair, or yarn. It is essential to choose hair extensions that match your hair's texture and color for a natural look.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small parts using a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections will depend on how thick or thin you want your locs to be. Make sure that the sections are even for a neat look.

Step 4: Install the Hair Extensions

Take a strand of hair extension and fold it in half. Place the folded end of the hair extension under your natural hair, and then bring the two ends of the extension over your hair. Twist the hair extension with your natural hair until it forms a loc. Repeat this process for each section of your hair.

Step 5: Seal the Ends

To prevent the hair extensions from unraveling, you can seal the ends with boiling water or a lighter. Be careful not to burn your hair or scalp.

Tips

Here are some tips to ensure that your faux locs with relaxed hair look great and last longer:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid pulling your hair too tight when installing the hair extensions to prevent tension and breakage.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a silk or satin bonnet or scarf.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils that can weigh down your hair and cause buildup.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting faux locs with relaxed hair:

Can relaxed hair handle the weight of faux locs?

Yes, relaxed hair can handle the weight of faux locs if the hair is healthy and strong. However, you should avoid installing heavy extensions or pulling your hair too tight to prevent breakage.

How long can I keep my faux locs with relaxed hair?

You can keep your faux locs with relaxed hair for up to six weeks, depending on how well you take care of them. You should also avoid leaving them in for too long to prevent hair damage.

Hair Care

After getting faux locs with relaxed hair, it is essential to take care of your hair to prevent breakage and maintain healthy hair. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil to prevent dryness.
  • Wash your hair extensions once a week with a mild shampoo and conditioner to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or excessive brushing or combing to prevent breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and maintain healthy hair.

In conclusion, getting faux locs with relaxed hair is possible, but it requires proper preparation and maintenance. Follow the steps and tips provided in this article to achieve a beautiful and long-lasting protective hairstyle.
