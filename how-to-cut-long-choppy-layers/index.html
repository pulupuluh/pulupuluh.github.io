How To Cut Long Choppy Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 21, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Cutting your hair can be a daunting task, especially if you want to achieve a choppy layered look. But fear not, because we have got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to cut long choppy layers. Whether you have long, medium, or short hair, this guide is perfect for achieving that edgy and stylish look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Long Choppy Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Long Choppy Layers

How to Cut Long Choppy Layers

Long choppy layers are a great way to add volume and texture to your hair. To achieve this look, you will need to follow these simple steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural fall and shape of your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into four sections, two in the front and two in the back.
  3. Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers.
  4. Point your scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair, moving your fingers down as you cut.
  5. Repeat this process for the rest of the sections, making sure to cut at the same angle.
  6. Once you have finished the back, move on to the front sections of your hair.
  7. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers, making sure to incorporate some of the hair from the back sections to create a seamless look.
  8. Cut the hair at the same angle as before, but this time make sure to point the scissors upwards for a more choppy look.
  9. Continue cutting the rest of the front sections, making sure to incorporate the hair from the back sections as you go.
  10. Once you have finished cutting all the sections, check for any unevenness and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Long Choppy Layers

Cutting your own hair can be tricky, so here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect long choppy layers:

  • Make sure to use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Take small sections of hair and cut slowly to avoid mistakes.
  • If you want a more dramatic look, angle your scissors upwards when cutting the front sections of your hair.
  • Always start with a little bit of hair and work your way up to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Make sure to cut in a well-lit area and use a mirror to check for any unevenness.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut long choppy layers on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut long choppy layers on curly hair. However, it's important to be extra careful when cutting curly hair because it can be difficult to see the natural fall and shape of your hair.

Q: Can I achieve long choppy layers with short hair?

A: No, long choppy layers are specifically for long hair. However, you can achieve a similar look with short hair by asking your stylist for a choppy pixie cut.

Hair Care After Cutting Long Choppy Layers

Once you have finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain your new look. Here are some tips for hair care after cutting long choppy layers:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your choppy layers.

And there you have it, a step-by-step guide on how to cut long choppy layers. Follow these tips and you'll be rocking an edgy and stylish look in no time!
