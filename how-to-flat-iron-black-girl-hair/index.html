How To Flat Iron Black Girl Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 08, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of struggling to flat iron your black girl hair? Do you want to achieve a sleek and straight look without damaging your hair? Look no further, because we’ve got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to flat iron black girl hair.

How to Flat Iron Black Girl Hair

Flat ironing black girl hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have thick or coarse hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a smooth and straight look without causing damage to your hair.

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let’s go over some essential tools and products you’ll need:

  • Flat iron
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Hair serum or oil

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have all the necessary tools and products, let’s get started with the step-by-step guide on how to flat iron black girl hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to hydrate your hair and prevent it from becoming dry and brittle.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant Spray

Once you’ve washed and conditioned your hair, apply a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage. Be sure to apply the spray evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. This will help to prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to flat iron your hair and ensure that you don’t miss any sections.

Step 5: Flat Iron Your Hair

Starting with the bottom sections of your hair, use your flat iron to straighten your hair. Be sure to use a low to medium heat setting to avoid causing heat damage to your hair. Slowly glide the flat iron down each section of hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.

Step 6: Apply Hair Serum or Oil

Once you’ve flat ironed all of your hair, apply a hair serum or oil to add shine and prevent frizz.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You can leave it straight or add some curls or waves for a different look.

Tips for Flat Ironing Black Girl Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when flat ironing black girl hair:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.
  • Section your hair into small sections to ensure that you don’t miss any sections.
  • Use a low to medium heat setting to avoid causing heat damage to your hair.
  • Slowly glide the flat iron down each section of hair to avoid causing damage or breakage.
  • Apply a hair serum or oil to add shine and prevent frizz.

FAQs

Can I flat iron my hair every day?

No, it’s not recommended to flat iron your hair every day. Flat ironing your hair too frequently can cause heat damage and breakage.

What heat setting should I use on my flat iron?

For black girl hair, it’s recommended to use a low to medium heat setting to avoid causing heat damage to your hair.

How can I prevent heat damage when flat ironing my hair?

You can prevent heat damage by using a heat protectant spray, using a low to medium heat setting, and slowly gliding the flat iron down each section of hair.

Hair Care Tips for Flat Ironed Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind after flat ironing your hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 2-3 days after flat ironing to maintain the straightness.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while sleeping.
  • Avoid using hair ties or clips that can cause creases in your hair.
  • Apply a hair serum or oil to your ends daily to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a sleek and straight look without causing damage to your black girl hair. Remember to always use a heat protectant spray and a low to medium heat setting to avoid heat damage. Happy flat ironing!
