What Vitamins Can You Take To Get Thicker Hair

Written by Robby May 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to get thicker hair, you're not alone. Many people struggle with hair thinning and hair loss, and it can be frustrating to find a solution that works. Fortunately, there are several vitamins and supplements that can help improve the health of your hair and promote growth. In this article, we'll explore what vitamins you can take to get thicker hair and how you can incorporate them into your daily routine.

Why Do We Lose Hair?

Before we dive into the vitamins that can help with hair growth, it's important to understand why we lose hair in the first place. Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and poor nutrition. In some cases, medical conditions or medications can also contribute to hair loss.

How Do Vitamins Help with Hair Growth?

Vitamins and minerals play a crucial role in the health of our hair, as they provide the nutrients that our hair follicles need to grow and thrive. When we don't get enough of these nutrients, our hair can become weak and brittle, leading to hair loss and thinning. By taking the right vitamins and supplements, we can help support the health of our hair and encourage growth.

What Vitamins Can You Take to Get Thicker Hair?

Vitamin A

Vitamin A is essential for the growth and development of all cells in our body, including our hair follicles. It helps produce sebum, which is a natural oil that lubricates the scalp and keeps our hair healthy. However, it's important not to overdo it with vitamin A, as too much can actually lead to hair loss. The recommended daily intake of vitamin A is 700-900 mcg for adults.

Vitamin B7 (Biotin)

Biotin is a B-vitamin that's often referred to as the "hair vitamin" because of its role in promoting hair growth. It helps produce keratin, which is a protein that makes up the structure of our hair. Biotin also helps improve the elasticity of our hair, reducing the risk of breakage. The recommended daily intake of biotin is 30 mcg for adults.

Vitamin C

Vitamin C is an antioxidant that helps protect our hair from damage caused by free radicals. It also helps produce collagen, which is a protein that's essential for the health of our hair and skin. The recommended daily intake of vitamin C is 75-90 mg for adults.

Vitamin D

Vitamin D is important for the growth and maintenance of our hair follicles. It also helps regulate the production of hormones that can contribute to hair loss. The recommended daily intake of vitamin D is 600-800 IU for adults.

Vitamin E

Vitamin E is another antioxidant that helps protect our hair from damage. It also helps improve the circulation of blood and oxygen to our scalp, which is important for hair growth. The recommended daily intake of vitamin E is 15 mg for adults.

How to Incorporate These Vitamins into Your Daily Routine

The best way to get these vitamins is through a healthy and balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. However, if you're not getting enough of these vitamins through your diet, you can also take supplements. Talk to your doctor or a registered dietitian to determine the right dosage and form of these supplements for you.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

In addition to taking vitamins and supplements, there are several other things you can do to promote the health of your hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to detangle your hair
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly into a ponytail or bun
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet
  • Get enough sleep and manage your stress levels

FAQs

Q: Can vitamins really help with hair growth?

A: Yes, vitamins and minerals play a crucial role in the health of our hair, and taking the right supplements can help support hair growth.

Q: How long does it take to see results from taking vitamins for hair growth?

A: It can take several months to see noticeable results from taking vitamins and supplements for hair growth. Be patient and consistent with your routine.

Q: Are there any side effects to taking vitamins for hair growth?

A: In general, taking vitamins and supplements in recommended doses is safe for most people. However, too much of certain vitamins can lead to side effects. Talk to your doctor or a registered dietitian before starting any new supplements.

Conclusion

If you're struggling with hair loss or thinning, incorporating the right vitamins and supplements into your daily routine can help support the health of your hair and promote growth. Remember to eat a healthy and balanced diet, manage your stress levels, and take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
