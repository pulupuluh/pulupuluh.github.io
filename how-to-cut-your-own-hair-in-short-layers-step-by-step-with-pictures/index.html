How To Cut Your Own Hair In Short Layers Step By Step With Pictures

Written by Frank Mar 04, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you ready to take the plunge and cut your own hair in short layers? With the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we will go through the process step by step, providing you with tips and tricks along the way.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Will Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Tools You Will Need

Before we get started, make sure you have these tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

Preparing Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. Comb through any tangles and make sure your hair is completely detangled. If you have curly hair, consider straightening it before you start cutting. This will help you get a more accurate cut.

Sectioning Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair in the middle and then section it into two parts. Clip the top part of your hair up and away from the bottom part. Take the bottom section and divide it into two parts. Clip the top of the bottom section up and out of the way.

Cutting Your Hair

Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length. Repeat this process for each section until you have cut all of the hair in the bottom section.

Once you have finished cutting the bottom section, unclip the top section and repeat the process. Take small sections of hair and cut off the desired length. Make sure to cut at an angle for a layered look.

Finishing Touches

Once you have cut all of your hair, take a close look at the layers. If you see any uneven parts, trim them until they are even. If you want to add more layers, repeat the cutting process until you are happy with the results.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and damage your hair.
  • Take your time. Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, so don't rush the process.
  • Check your progress frequently. Use a mirror to make sure your layers are even.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair in short layers if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair in short layers if you have curly hair. Just make sure to straighten your hair before you start cutting so you can get a more accurate cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

You should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care After Cutting

After you have finished cutting your own hair in short layers, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. And don't forget to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair in short layers step by step with pictures, you can save money and achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow these tips for the best results.
