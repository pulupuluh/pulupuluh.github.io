How To Cut Face Framing Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Sep 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to add some dimension and style to your hair, face framing layers are a great way to do it. This technique involves cutting shorter layers around the face to accentuate your features and add movement to your hair. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of how to cut face framing layers at home.

Table of Contents:

  1. What You'll Need
  2. How to Cut Face Framing Layers
  3. Tips for Cutting Face Framing Layers
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting Face Framing Layers

What You'll Need

Before you start cutting, make sure you have everything you need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips to section off your hair
  • A mirror

How to Cut Face Framing Layers

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Separate the hair around your face into two sections. Clip the rest of your hair back so it's out of the way.

Step 3: Determine How Much Hair to Cut

Decide how short you want your face framing layers to be. It's always best to start with a little and cut more if necessary.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Starting with one side, comb the hair straight down and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the chin and working your way down to the desired length.

Repeat on the other side, making sure the layers are even.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Using your scissors, blend the layers so they look natural and seamless.

Tips for Cutting Face Framing Layers

  • Start with a little hair and cut more if necessary. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take your time and cut a little bit at a time.
  • Make sure the layers are even on both sides.
  • Blending is key. Use your scissors to blend the layers so they look natural and seamless.

FAQs

How often should I cut my face framing layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how much maintenance you're willing to do. Generally, every 6-8 weeks is a good timeframe for a trim.

Can I cut my face framing layers myself?

Yes, you can cut your face framing layers yourself. However, it's always best to seek the help of a professional stylist if you're unsure.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Hair grows back. If you make a mistake, wait a couple of weeks and try again.

Hair Care After Cutting Face Framing Layers

After cutting your face framing layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

Congratulations, you've now learned how to cut face framing layers! With a little practice, you'll be a pro in no time.
