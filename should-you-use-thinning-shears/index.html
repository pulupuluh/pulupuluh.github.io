Should You Use Thinning Shears

Written by Petter Mar 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Thinning shears are a type of hair cutting tool that can be used to remove bulk from thick hair, add texture to fine hair, and create soft layers. However, there are varying opinions on whether or not you should use thinning shears on your hair. In this article, we will explore the benefits and drawbacks of using thinning shears, and provide some tips on how to use them properly.

Table of Contents

  2. Drawbacks of Using Thinning Shears
  3. How to Use Thinning Shears

  6. Hair Care Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Benefits of Using Thinning Shears

One of the main benefits of using thinning shears is that they can help to remove bulk from thick hair. This can make the hair more manageable and easier to style. Thinning shears can also be used to create soft layers and add texture to fine hair. Additionally, thinning shears can be a good option for people with curly hair, as they can help to reduce frizz and create more defined curls.

Thinning shears can also be a good option for people who want to maintain the length of their hair while still removing some of the weight. This can be particularly useful for people with long hair who want to keep their hair looking healthy and full.

Drawbacks of Using Thinning Shears

While there are benefits to using thinning shears, there are also some drawbacks to consider. One of the main drawbacks is that if thinning shears are used incorrectly, they can create uneven layers and choppy ends. This can be difficult to fix and may require a professional haircut to correct.

Additionally, thinning shears should not be used on already thin or damaged hair, as they can cause further damage and breakage. If you have thin or damaged hair, it is best to consult with a professional stylist before using thinning shears.

How to Use Thinning Shears

If you have decided to use thinning shears on your hair, it is important to use them correctly to avoid any unwanted results. Here are some steps to follow:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off the hair you want to thin out.
  3. Hold the thinning shears horizontally and make small, snipping motions along the length of the hair.
  4. Continue snipping until you have achieved the desired level of thinning.
  5. Repeat on other sections of hair as needed.

It is important to use caution when using thinning shears, as they can easily create a choppy or uneven look if used incorrectly.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use thinning shears sparingly to avoid over-thinning the hair.
  • Do not use thinning shears on wet hair, as this can result in uneven cutting.
  • Consider using a comb to help guide the thinning shears and create a more even cut.

FAQs About Using Thinning Shears

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears can be used on most hair types, but it is important to use caution when using them on already thin or damaged hair.

Can thinning shears damage hair?

Thinning shears can cause damage if used incorrectly or on already thin or damaged hair. It is important to use them sparingly and to consult with a professional stylist if you are unsure about how to use them.

Can thinning shears be used to create bangs?

Thinning shears can be used to create bangs, but it is important to use them carefully to avoid creating an uneven or choppy look.

Hair Care Tips for Using Thinning Shears

After using thinning shears on your hair, it is important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis, as this can cause damage to the hair.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and repair the hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a protective styling product.

Overall, thinning shears can be a useful tool for creating texture and removing bulk from the hair. However, it is important to use them correctly and to take good care of your hair to avoid any damage or unwanted results.
