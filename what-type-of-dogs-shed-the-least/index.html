What Type Of Dogs Shed The Least

Written by Frank May 01, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you're looking for a furry companion that doesn't leave a trail of hair everywhere they go, you're in luck! There are several dog breeds that shed the least. In this article, we'll go through the different dog breeds that are known for low shedding and provide tips on how to care for their hair.

Table of Contents

  • Breeds That Shed the Least
  • How to Care for Low-Shedding Dog Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Grooming Low-Shedding Dogs

Breeds That Shed the Least

Before we dive into the breeds, it's important to note that all dogs shed to some extent. However, there are certain breeds that shed less than others.

Poodle

Poodles are known for their hypoallergenic coat that sheds minimally. They come in three sizes: standard, miniature, and toy. Poodles require regular grooming to maintain their curly coat, but they're a great option for those with allergies or who don't want to deal with excessive shedding.

Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise is a small breed with a curly, fluffy coat that sheds very little. They require regular grooming to prevent matting and keep their coat looking its best. Bichons are known for their friendly, playful personalities, making them a great choice for families.

Portuguese Water Dog

The Portuguese Water Dog has a non-shedding coat that requires regular grooming to prevent matting. They were originally bred to help fishermen in Portugal, so they're great swimmers and love being in the water. They're also very loyal and make great family pets.

Shih Tzu

The Shih Tzu is a small, affectionate breed with a long, silky coat that sheds very little. They require daily grooming to keep their coat tangle-free and looking its best. Shih Tzus are known for their friendly, outgoing personalities and make great lap dogs.

Maltese

The Maltese is a small breed with a long, silky coat that sheds very little. They require daily grooming to prevent matting and keep their coat looking its best. Maltese dogs are known for their sweet, affectionate personalities and make great companions.

How to Care for Low-Shedding Dog Hair

Caring for a low-shedding dog's hair is important to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

Regular Grooming

Regular grooming is essential for low-shedding dogs. Brushing their coat daily or a few times a week will prevent matting and keep their coat looking shiny and healthy. You should also trim their hair regularly to prevent it from getting too long.

Bathing

Low-shedding dogs don't need to be bathed as often as other breeds. You should only bathe them when they're dirty or have a strong odor. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for dogs to avoid drying out their skin.

Diet

A healthy diet is important for a dog's overall health, including their hair. Make sure your dog is eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein and nutrients to promote healthy hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide to Grooming Low-Shedding Dogs

Grooming a low-shedding dog can be a bit more time-consuming than grooming other breeds. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Brush their coat

Start by brushing your dog's coat to remove any tangles or mats. Use a slicker brush or comb to gently work through any knots. You can also use a detangling spray to make the process easier.

Step 2: Trim their hair

Use a pair of scissors or clippers to trim your dog's hair. Make sure to only cut a small amount at a time to avoid cutting too much. You can also take your dog to a professional groomer if you're not comfortable doing it yourself.

Step 3: Bathe your dog

If your dog needs a bath, use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for dogs. Make sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid leaving any residue on their coat.

Step 4: Dry their coat

Use a towel or blow dryer to dry your dog's coat. Make sure to dry them thoroughly to avoid any moisture getting trapped in their coat.

Tips for Reducing Dog Hair in Your Home

Even low-shedding dogs will leave some hair around your home. Here are some tips for reducing dog hair:

Regular Grooming

The more you groom your dog, the less hair they'll leave around your home. Brush their coat regularly and trim their hair as needed.

Vacuum Regularly

Invest in a good vacuum cleaner and use it regularly to keep your home free of dog hair. You can also use a lint roller or pet hair remover to get rid of any stray hairs.

Wash Dog Bedding

Wash your dog's bedding regularly to remove any hair and keep it smelling fresh. You can also use a washable dog bed that's easy to clean.

FAQs about Low-Shedding Dogs

Q: Do low-shedding dogs require less grooming?

A: No, low-shedding dogs require regular grooming to maintain their coat and prevent matting.

Q: Are low-shedding dogs hypoallergenic?

A: Some low-shedding dogs are considered hypoallergenic, but not all. If you have allergies, it's important to spend time with a dog before adopting to see if you have a reaction.

Q: Can low-shedding dogs be left alone for long periods?

A: No, all dogs require socialization and exercise. Low-shedding dogs are no exception and should not be left alone for long periods of time.

Q: Can low-shedding dogs be trained?

A: Yes, all dogs can be trained regardless of their shedding level. Low-shedding dogs are often very intelligent and eager to please, making them great candidates for training.

Conclusion

If you're looking for a dog that sheds the least, there are several breeds to choose from. Remember that all dogs require regular grooming and care, even low-shedding breeds. By following the tips in this article, you can keep your dog's hair healthy and reduce the amount of hair in your home.
