How To Cut Your Hair By Yourself In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 17, 2023 · 7 min read

If you've been wanting to switch up your hairstyle or simply need a trim, you might be hesitant to visit a hair salon due to the pandemic. But fear not, cutting your own hair at home is possible, especially if you're interested in creating layers. In this blog post, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your hair by yourself in layers in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Description

Layered hair is a popular style that can add texture, movement, and volume to your hair. The layers are created by cutting different lengths of hair, which can be done by a professional or by yourself at home. There are a few things you'll need before you start cutting, such as sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips.

How to

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and prevent any surprises when it dries. Once your hair is dry, brush or comb it to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair in half, from ear to ear. Then, use hair clips to secure the top section of your hair on top of your head. This will help you focus on cutting the bottom section first.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Section

Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight. Hold the scissors perpendicular to the hair and cut up towards the ends. This will create a soft, feathered effect. Repeat this process for each section, making sure to cut at an angle to create layers.

Step 4: Cut the Top Section

Release the top section of your hair and repeat the same process as the bottom section. Make sure to match the length of the layers to the bottom section. You can also add more layers to the top section for a more dramatic effect.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you're done cutting, release all the sections of your hair and check your work in a mirror. If you notice any uneven sections, you can trim them with your scissors. Remember to take your time and be patient, as cutting your hair can be a time-consuming process.

Tips

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger ones.
  • Be careful not to cut too much off at once.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can lead to a shorter cut when it dries.
  • Consider watching tutorials or asking a friend for help if you're unsure about cutting your hair.

FAQ

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

While it's technically possible to use regular scissors to cut your hair, it's not recommended. Regular scissors can be dull and may result in uneven cuts, which can be difficult to fix later on.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. However, you can cut your hair at home in between salon visits if you need a quick trim.

Can I still style my hair after cutting it in layers?

Yes, layered hair can be styled in a variety of ways, such as straight, wavy, or curly. The layers can add volume and texture to your hair, making it easier to style.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy and shiny. This includes washing your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, using a heat protectant when styling with heat tools, and avoiding harsh chemicals or dyes. You can also use hair masks or oils to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.

Overall, cutting your hair by yourself in layers can be a fun and rewarding experience. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and follow these steps for a successful cut. Happy styling!
