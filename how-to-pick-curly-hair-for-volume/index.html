How To Pick Curly Hair For Volume A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of limp, lifeless curls? Do you want to add some volume and bounce to your curly hair? If so, you've come to the right place! In this article, we'll show you how to pick curly hair for volume. We'll cover everything from how to choose the right products to how to style your curls for maximum volume. So, let's get started!

How to Pick Curly Hair for Volume

The first step in adding volume to your curly hair is to choose the right type of hair. Here are some tips:

1. Look for Hair with a Tight Curl Pattern

Curly hair with a tight curl pattern tends to have more volume than hair with looser curls. Look for hair that has a defined S-shaped curl pattern.

2. Choose Hair with a Coarser Texture

Hair with a coarser texture tends to be thicker and fuller, which can add volume to your curls. Look for hair that is labeled as "coarse" or "thick."

3. Avoid Hair with a Silky Texture

Hair that is too silky or smooth can be difficult to style and may not hold curls well. Look for hair that has a slightly rough texture, which will help your curls hold their shape.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know how to choose the right type of curly hair, it's time to style your curls for maximum volume. Here's how:

1. Start with Clean, Detangled Hair

Wash and condition your hair using products that are designed for curly hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while it's still wet.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it moisturized and to prevent frizz. Be sure to apply the conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

3. Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product that is designed for adding volume to curly hair. This could be a mousse, a gel, or a curl cream. Be sure to apply the product evenly throughout your hair.

4. Use a Diffuser

Attach a diffuser to your hair dryer and use it to dry your hair. This will help to enhance your curls and add volume.

5. Scrunch Your Hair

While your hair is still damp, use your hands to scrunch your curls upward. This will help to enhance the curl pattern and add volume.

6. Finish with Hairspray

Finish off your look with a light mist of hairspray. This will help to hold your curls in place and add some extra volume.

Tips for Adding Volume to Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for adding volume to your curly hair:

1. Don't Overload Your Hair with Products

Using too much product can weigh down your curls and make them look limp. Use a small amount of product and apply it evenly throughout your hair.

2. Don't Touch Your Hair Too Much

Touching your hair too much can cause frizz and make your curls lose their shape. Try to avoid touching your hair as much as possible.

3. Sleep on a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and preserve your curls overnight.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how often you style your hair. Generally, curly hair should be washed every 2-3 days.

Q: Can I add volume to my curly hair without using heat?

A: Yes! You can add volume to your curly hair by using a diffuser or by air-drying your curls.

Q: What should I do if my curls are too tight?

A: You can loosen your curls by using a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

1. Use Products Designed for Curly Hair

Choose products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your curls and make them look frizzy. Try to avoid using heat as much as possible.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can cause damage to your hair and make your curls look dull. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

With these tips, you can add volume and bounce to your curly hair. Remember to choose the right type of curly hair, use the right products, and style your curls with care. Happy styling!
