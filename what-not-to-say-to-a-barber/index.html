What Not To Say To A Barber A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia May 29, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Getting a haircut can be a nerve-wracking experience, especially if you're not sure what to say to your barber. While it's important to communicate your desired style and preferences, there are some things you should never say to your barber. In this article, we'll cover what not to say to a barber and provide tips on how to communicate effectively to ensure you get the haircut you want.

Why It's Important to Be Mindful of What You Say to Your Barber

Your barber is a professional who is there to help you look your best. However, just like any other profession, there are certain things that can be offensive or unprofessional to say. Being mindful of what you say to your barber can help ensure that you have a positive experience and get the haircut you want.

What Not to Say to Your Barber

Here are some things you should never say to your barber:

1. "I don't know what I want"

While it's okay to not have a specific style in mind, saying "I don't know what I want" can be frustrating for your barber. Instead, try to have a general idea of the length and style you're looking for.

2. "Just make me look like [insert celebrity name]"

Your barber can't magically turn you into your favorite celebrity. While it's okay to bring in a photo for reference, don't expect your barber to create an exact replica of someone else's hairstyle.

3. "I want it really short"

Without specifying a length, saying "really short" can mean different things to different people. Be specific about how short you want your hair to be.

4. "I hate my hair"

While it's okay to express dissatisfaction with your current hairstyle, saying "I hate my hair" can be demoralizing for your barber. Instead, try to provide constructive feedback on what you'd like to change.

5. "Can you fix this horrible haircut I got somewhere else?"

Insulting someone else's work is unprofessional and can make your barber feel uncomfortable. Instead, focus on what you'd like to change about your current haircut.

How to Communicate Effectively with Your Barber

Now that you know what not to say to your barber, here are some tips on how to communicate effectively:

1. Bring in a photo for reference

Bringing in a photo of a hairstyle you like can help your barber understand what you're looking for.

2. Be specific about the length and style you want

Don't be afraid to use specific terms like "taper fade" or "side part" to describe the style you're looking for. Be specific about how short or long you want your hair to be.

3. Provide feedback during the haircut

If your barber is going in a direction you don't like, speak up! Providing feedback during the haircut can help ensure that you get the haircut you want.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting the Haircut You Want

Follow these steps to ensure you get the haircut you want:

1. Research different styles

Look online or in magazines for hairstyles you like. Bring in a photo for reference.

2. Find a reputable barber

Ask friends or family for recommendations or read reviews online to find a barber with a good reputation.

3. Communicate effectively

Be specific about the length and style you want. Provide feedback during the haircut if necessary.

4. Maintain your haircut

Follow your barber's advice on how to maintain your haircut at home. Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh.

Tips for Maintaining Your Haircut

Here are some tips for maintaining your haircut:

1. Wash and condition your hair regularly

Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type to keep your hair healthy and clean.

2. Style your hair with the right products

Use products like hair gel, pomade, or wax to style your hair. Ask your barber for recommendations.

3. Get regular trims

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking neat.

FAQs

1. How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on your hair type and style, but most people need a haircut every 4-8 weeks.

2. How can I find a good barber?

Ask friends or family for recommendations or read reviews online to find a barber with a good reputation.

3. What should I do if I don't like my haircut?

Speak up during the haircut if you don't like the direction it's going in. If you're still not satisfied, talk to your barber about how to fix it.

Conclusion

Communicating effectively with your barber is key to getting the haircut you want. By avoiding certain phrases and being specific about what you want, you can ensure a positive experience and a great haircut. Remember to maintain your haircut with regular trims and proper hair care.
