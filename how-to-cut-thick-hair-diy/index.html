How To Cut Thick Hair Diy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful haircut at home. In this article, we will show you how to cut thick hair DIY, step-by-step. From preparing your hair to finishing your cut, we've got you covered. Read on to learn more!

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Here are some steps you should follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup. This will make your hair easier to cut and style.

Step 2: Dry Your Hair

Blow-dry your hair until it's completely dry. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will also help you see the natural shape of your hair.

Tools You'll Need

Here are the tools you'll need to cut your thick hair DIY:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is important when cutting thick hair DIY. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 2: Section the Front

Section the front of your hair by parting it down the middle. Clip each side back.

Step 3: Section the Back

Section the back of your hair by parting it horizontally at the nape of your neck. Clip the top section up.

Cutting Your Hair

Now that your hair is prepared and sectioned, it's time to start cutting. Here's how:

Step 1: Cut the Back

Starting in the back, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and trim the ends with scissors. Continue this process, working from the back to the front on each section.

Step 2: Cut the Sides

Unclip one side of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and trim the ends with scissors. Repeat on the other side.

Trimming Your Hair

Now that your hair is cut, it's time to trim any uneven ends. Here's how:

Step 1: Comb Your Hair

Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will help you see any uneven ends.

Step 2: Trim the Ends

Take small sections of hair and trim any uneven ends with scissors. Be careful not to cut too much off.

Blow-Drying Your Hair

Blow-drying your hair is important when cutting thick hair DIY. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant to your hair to prevent damage from the blow dryer.

Step 2: Blow-Dry Your Hair

Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to add volume and shape. Work from the roots to the ends.

Styling Your Hair

Now that your hair is cut and blow-dried, it's time to style it. Here's how:

Step 1: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your hair to add texture and hold.

Step 2: Style Your Hair

Style your hair using a curling iron or straightener. Finish with hairspray.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair DIY

Here are some tips to help you cut your thick hair DIY:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much off
  • Trim your hair when it's dry to avoid any surprises once it's dry
  • Take breaks if you need them to avoid fatigue or cramps

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own hair if it's very thick?

A: Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's very thick. Just be sure to use sharp scissors and take your time.

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using hot tools too often to prevent damage
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Don't wash your hair every day to avoid stripping it of natural oils

Now that you know how to cut thick hair DIY, you can achieve a beautiful haircut at home. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow these steps. With practice, you'll become a pro at cutting your own hair!
