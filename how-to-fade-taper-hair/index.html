How To Fade Taper Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, then a taper fade might be just what you need. This haircut has been gaining popularity in recent years, and for good reason. It's versatile, low-maintenance, and looks great on almost anyone. However, getting a fade taper haircut can be intimidating, especially if you're not sure how to do it. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to fade taper hair, along with some helpful tips and FAQs to make the process as easy as possible.

How to Fade Taper Hair

The first step in fading taper hair is to understand what a taper fade is. A taper fade is a haircut that gradually fades from long to short, usually from the top of the head to the sides and back. This creates a clean and sharp look that is easy to maintain.

To get a taper fade haircut, you'll need to use clippers with different guard sizes. The guard size you use will determine how short or long the hair will be. You'll also need some scissors and a comb to blend the different lengths of hair together.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fade taper hair:

Step 1: Determine the Length

The first step is to determine the length you want for your hair. You can start with a longer guard size and work your way down until you reach your desired length. It's important to remember that the hair on the top of your head should be longer than the hair on the sides and back.

Step 2: Start with the Sides and Back

Once you've determined the length you want, start with the sides and back of your head. Use a shorter guard size and slowly work your way up, blending the hair as you go. Use a comb to keep the hair even and make sure you don't miss any spots.

Step 3: Move to the Top of the Head

Once you've finished the sides and back, move to the top of the head. Use a longer guard size and blend the hair into the shorter hair on the sides and back. Make sure you don't create any harsh lines or uneven spots.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After you've finished the top of the head, blend the hair together using scissors and a comb. Make sure you don't cut too much hair and create any uneven spots. It's important to take your time and blend the hair together until it looks even and clean.

Step 5: Clean Up the Hairline

The final step is to clean up the hairline around the ears and neck. Use a trimmer to create a clean and sharp line. Make sure the line is even and symmetrical on both sides.

Tips for Fading Taper Hair

Here are some tips to make the process of fading taper hair easier:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a comb to keep the hair even and create a seamless blend.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Don't create any harsh lines or uneven spots.
  • Clean up the hairline for a clean and polished look.

FAQs about Fading Taper Hair

1. What length should I start with?

You should start with a longer guard size and work your way down until you reach your desired length. The hair on the top of your head should be longer than the hair on the sides and back.

2. How often should I get a taper fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your haircut loses its shape. Generally, you should get a taper fade every 2-4 weeks to maintain its shape and clean look.

3. Can I do a taper fade at home?

Yes, you can do a taper fade at home if you have the right tools and knowledge. However, it's always best to consult with a professional barber or stylist if you're unsure about the process.

Hair Care after Fading Taper Hair

After you've faded your taper hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and style. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Use a hair styling product to maintain its shape and style.
  • Get regular trims to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or straighteners.

With these tips and a little practice, you'll be able to fade taper hair like a pro. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and styles. Good luck!
