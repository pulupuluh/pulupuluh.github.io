Why Does My Baby s Head Feel Uneven

Written by Bardi Apr 16, 2023 · 8 min read

As a parent, you may have noticed that your baby's head feels uneven or misshapen. This can be a concerning discovery, but it is actually quite common in infants. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your baby's head may feel uneven, how to check for it, and what you can do to help.

Table of Contents

  • Reasons Why Baby's Head Feels Uneven
  • How to Check for Unevenness
  • Tips to Help Your Baby's Head Shape
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Your Baby's Head

Reasons Why Baby's Head Feels Uneven

There are several reasons why your baby's head may feel uneven:

Positional Plagiocephaly

Positional plagiocephaly, also known as flat head syndrome, is a common condition that occurs when a baby's head develops a flat spot due to lying in the same position for an extended period of time. This can happen when a baby is placed in the same position to sleep or when they spend too much time in a car seat or bouncer.

Cephalohematoma

Cephalohematoma is a condition where there is bleeding between the skull and the periosteum (the membrane that covers the bone) of a newborn baby's skull. It is usually caused by trauma during delivery, such as the use of forceps or a vacuum extractor. Cephalohematoma can cause a lump on the baby's head that may feel uneven.

Craniostenosis

Craniostenosis is a rare condition where the bones in a baby's skull fuse together too early, before the brain has finished growing. This can lead to an abnormal head shape and may cause the head to feel uneven.

How to Check for Unevenness

If you suspect that your baby's head is uneven, there are several things you can do to check:

Visual Inspection

Take a good look at your baby's head from different angles. Does it appear asymmetrical or misshapen? Does one side of the head look flatter than the other?

Palpation

Run your hands over your baby's head, feeling for any lumps or irregularities. You may want to do this while your baby is sleeping or nursing, as they will be more relaxed.

Measurement

You can also measure your baby's head circumference using a soft tape measure. This can help you keep track of any changes over time.

Tips to Help Your Baby's Head Shape

If you have determined that your baby's head is uneven, there are several things you can do to help:

Tummy Time

Encourage your baby to spend time on their tummy during supervised playtime. This can help strengthen their neck muscles and prevent flat head syndrome.

Frequent Repositioning

Make sure to change your baby's position frequently during the day to avoid prolonged pressure on any one spot. This can include changing the direction your baby faces in their crib or switching up the side of the car seat they sit in.

Use a Special Pillow

There are special pillows available that can help prevent flat head syndrome by providing support and cushioning for your baby's head. However, it is important to follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully and not to leave your baby unattended while using one of these pillows.

FAQs

Is it normal for a baby's head to feel uneven?

Yes, it is quite common for a baby's head to feel uneven or misshapen, especially in the early months of life. This is usually due to positional plagiocephaly and can be improved with simple interventions such as tummy time and repositioning.

When should I be concerned about my baby's head shape?

You should consult your pediatrician if you notice any sudden changes in your baby's head shape, or if you have concerns about their development. Your pediatrician can rule out any underlying medical conditions and provide guidance on how to help your baby's head shape.

Hair Care for Your Baby's Head

Finally, it is important to take care of your baby's hair and scalp, especially if they have an uneven head shape. Here are some tips:

Be Gentle

Use a soft brush or comb to gently detangle your baby's hair, being careful not to pull or tug on any lumps or bumps.

Cleanse Regularly

Wash your baby's scalp with a mild, baby-friendly shampoo to remove any dirt or oil buildup. Be sure to rinse thoroughly and avoid getting soap in their eyes.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles that can put pressure on your baby's scalp or pull on their hair. Instead, opt for loose styles or let their hair air-dry naturally.

In conclusion, if you notice that your baby's head feels uneven, it is important to stay calm and take action. By understanding the reasons why your baby's head may feel uneven, checking for any irregularities, and taking steps to help improve their head shape, you can ensure that your baby is healthy and happy.
