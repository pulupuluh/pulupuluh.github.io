Which Cutting Is Best For Curly Hair

Written by Robby May 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it can look stunning and voluminous, it can also be challenging to manage, especially when it comes to getting the right haircut. The right haircut can make all the difference in how your curly hair looks and feels. In this post, we'll explore which cutting is best for curly hair and some tips to help you maintain your curls.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Curly hair can be categorized into three different types - wavy, curly, and coily. Each of these types requires a different type of cut to make the most of the curls. For wavy hair, a long layered cut works best, while for curly hair, a medium-length cut with layers is ideal. For coily hair, a short cut with layers is perfect. The goal is to create a shape that complements the natural curl pattern and makes styling easier.

How to

The first step in getting the right cut for your curly hair is finding a stylist who specializes in curly hair. Look for someone who understands the unique needs of curly hair and has experience in cutting it.

When you go for your appointment, be sure to bring pictures of haircuts you like and ones you don't like. This will help your stylist get a better idea of what you're looking for.

Step by step

Here are the steps to follow when getting a haircut for curly hair:

  1. Book an appointment with a stylist who specializes in curly hair.
  2. Bring pictures of haircuts you like and don't like to your appointment.
  3. Discuss your hair type with your stylist and what you hope to achieve with your cut.
  4. Let your stylist wash and condition your hair and dry it with a diffuser.
  5. Have your stylist cut your hair into the desired shape, taking into account your natural curl pattern.
  6. Style your hair with the products recommended by your stylist.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to avoid frizz.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your curls hydrated.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about haircuts for curly hair:

Q: How often should I get my curly hair cut?

A: It's recommended to get your curly hair cut every 8-12 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Q: Is it better to cut curly hair wet or dry?

A: It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry as this will allow the stylist to see the natural curl pattern and shape the hair accordingly.

Q: Can I still straighten my curly hair after getting a curly haircut?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your curly hair after getting a curly haircut, but it's recommended to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care

Curly hair requires a little extra care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

Overall, the key to getting the right haircut for curly hair is finding a stylist who understands your hair type and can work with you to achieve the look you want. Use these tips and take good care of your curls to keep them looking their best.
