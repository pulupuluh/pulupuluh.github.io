Is Facial Hair Allowed In The Us Military

Written by Frank Apr 16, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Facial hair has always been a topic of discussion when it comes to the military. While some countries allow their soldiers to grow beards and mustaches, others enforce strict grooming standards. In the United States, the rules regarding facial hair in the military have changed over the years. In this article, we will discuss whether or not facial hair is allowed in the US military and what the rules are.

Description

Facial hair has been a part of military culture for centuries. In the US, soldiers used to grow beards and mustaches as a sign of masculinity and strength. However, in recent years, the military has enforced stricter grooming standards, and soldiers are required to maintain a clean-shaven appearance. This article will explore why the rules have changed and what the current regulations are.

How to

If you are considering joining the military and want to know the rules about facial hair, there are a few things you can do. First, you can check with your local recruiter or military base to find out what the current regulations are. You can also look up the regulations online on the official military website.

Step by Step

The rules regarding facial hair in the military are as follows:

  • Beards are not allowed except for certain medical conditions.
  • Mustaches are allowed, but they must be well-groomed and not extend past the corners of the mouth.
  • Sideburns are allowed, but they must be neatly trimmed and not extend past the bottom of the earlobe.
  • Goatees are not allowed.

Tips

If you are joining the military, it is important to be aware of the grooming standards and regulations. Make sure to maintain a clean-shaven appearance and keep your hair and nails well-groomed. This will not only help you comply with the rules but will also show that you are disciplined and take your job seriously.

FAQ

Q. Why are beards not allowed in the military?

A. Beards are not allowed in the military because they can interfere with the proper fit of gas masks and other protective equipment. In addition, beards can also make it difficult to maintain a proper seal around the face, which can compromise the soldier's safety.

Q. Can soldiers with certain medical conditions be allowed to grow beards?

A. Yes, soldiers with certain medical conditions, such as severe skin allergies or dermatitis, may be allowed to grow beards if it is deemed necessary for their health and well-being.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair and facial hair well-groomed is an important part of military life. Here are some tips to help you maintain a clean and professional appearance:

  • Shave regularly with a sharp razor.
  • Keep your hair trimmed and neatly styled.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or other harsh chemicals.
  • Keep your facial hair well-groomed and trimmed.

Overall, it is important to remember that the military has strict standards when it comes to grooming and appearance. By following the rules and maintaining a clean and professional appearance, you can show that you are dedicated to your job and committed to serving your country.
