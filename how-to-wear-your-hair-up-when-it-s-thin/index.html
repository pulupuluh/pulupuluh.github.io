How To Wear Your Hair Up When It s Thin Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Apr 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling with thin hair? Do you want to wear your hair up but feel self-conscious about its lack of volume? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many women with thin hair face the same challenge. However, there are ways to style your hair up that will make it look fuller and more voluminous. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to wear your hair up when it's thin.

How to:

1. Choose the Right Hairstyle

The first step to wearing your hair up when it's thin is to choose the right hairstyle. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull your hair back tightly, such as a high ponytail or bun. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles that create volume, such as a messy bun or a low ponytail. You can also try a half-up, half-down hairstyle, which will add volume to the top of your head.

2. Use Hair Extensions

If you want to add more volume to your hair, consider using hair extensions. You can clip them in to add length and volume to your hair. Choose extensions that match your hair color and texture to create a seamless look.

3. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair can create volume and texture, making it easier to wear your hair up. Use a fine-tooth comb to backcomb your hair at the crown of your head. Then, smooth the top layer of your hair over the teased section and secure it with bobby pins.

4. Use Hair Accessories

Hair accessories can add volume and texture to your hair. Try using a headband, hair scarf, or hair clip to create a stylish updo. You can also use a volumizing spray or mousse to add texture and hold to your hair.

Step by Step:

1. Messy Bun

Start by creating a loose ponytail at the nape of your neck. Twist the ponytail and wrap it around the base of the ponytail to create a messy bun. Secure the bun with bobby pins. Use your fingers to loosen the bun and create a messy, voluminous look.

2. Half-Up, Half-Down

Start by creating a small ponytail at the crown of your head. Tease the ponytail to create volume, then smooth the top layer of your hair over the teased section and secure it with bobby pins. Leave the rest of your hair down for a half-up, half-down look.

3. Low Ponytail

Start by creating a low ponytail at the nape of your neck. Use a fine-tooth comb to tease the hair at the crown of your head to create volume. Smooth the top layer of your hair over the teased section and secure it with bobby pins. Use a hair tie to secure the ponytail.

Tips:

  • Use dry shampoo to add texture and volume to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy hair products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find the one that works best for you.
  • Don't be afraid to use accessories to add volume and texture to your hair.

FAQ:

Q: Can I still wear a high ponytail if my hair is thin?

A: It's best to avoid high ponytails if your hair is thin, as they can pull your hair back tightly and create a flat, lifeless look. Opt for a lower ponytail or a messy bun instead.

Q: How can I add volume to my hair without using hair extensions?

A: Try teasing your hair at the crown of your head, using a volumizing spray, or using a hair accessory to create volume and texture.

Hair Care:

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, voluminous hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for thin hair. Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, as they can damage your hair. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on your blow dryer. Lastly, get regular haircuts to prevent split ends and promote hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently wear your hair up when it's thin. Remember to choose a hairstyle that creates volume and texture, use hair accessories, and take good care of your hair to maintain its health and vitality.
