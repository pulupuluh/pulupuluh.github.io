How To Cut Curly Hair For Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 22, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how frustrating it can be to achieve volume and bounce. But fear not, because with the right technique, you can cut your curly hair for maximum volume. In this guide, we’ll take you through the steps to achieve the perfect haircut for curly hair that will make your locks bounce and shine.

Description

Cutting curly hair is not rocket science, but it requires a bit of skill and knowledge. The key to achieving volume in curly hair is to cut it when it’s dry and to avoid using thinning shears or razors. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut your curly hair for volume in a way that will make it easier to style and maintain.

How to Cut Curly Hair for Volume: Step by Step

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry. Do not use a blow dryer, as this can cause frizz and damage to your hair. Once your hair is dry, comb through it to remove any tangles.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair Into Sections

Use a comb to divide your hair into four sections: two on the sides, one in the back, and one on top. Clip each section to keep them separate.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Begin by cutting the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers, pulling it straight down. Use sharp scissors to cut the ends in a straight line. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working from the back to the sides and then to the top.

Step 4: Check for Balance

After you’ve cut all sections of your hair, check for balance. Make sure each section is the same length and that the layers are even.

Step 5: Add Layers for Volume

To add volume to your hair, use sharp scissors to cut into the ends of your hair. Make small, angled cuts into the hair, working from the bottom to the top. This will create layers that add volume and bounce to your curls.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

After you’ve cut your hair, style it as desired. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair, or let it air dry. Use a curling iron or wand to define your curls and add extra bounce.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair for Volume

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your curly hair for volume:

  • Always cut your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is
  • Avoid using thinning shears or razors, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz
  • Use sharp scissors to create clean cuts
  • Cut your hair in small sections to ensure evenness
  • Add layers to create volume and bounce

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair for volume?

A: It’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and encourage growth.

Q: Can I cut my curly hair by myself?

A: It’s recommended to go to a professional hair stylist who has experience with cutting curly hair. However, if you choose to cut your hair at home, make sure you have sharp scissors and follow the steps outlined in this guide.

Hair Care

To keep your curly hair healthy and vibrant, it’s important to follow a proper hair care routine. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized, and use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray when you do.

Cutting curly hair for volume can be intimidating, but with the right technique and tools, it’s easy to achieve a gorgeous, bouncy look. Follow the steps in this guide and remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and vibrant.
