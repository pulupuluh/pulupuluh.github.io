How To Cut Fringe Around Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 12, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle, cutting fringe around your face can be a great way to do it. Fringe, also known as bangs, can help frame your face, add texture, and give you a fresh new look. However, cutting fringe can be intimidating if you've never done it before. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieving the perfect fringe.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Fringe Around Your Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Fringe
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Fringe

How to Cut Fringe Around Your Face

The first step is to decide what type of fringe you want. There are several different styles to choose from, including blunt, side-swept, and choppy. Once you've decided on a style, it's important to gather the right tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Fringe is easier to cut when your hair is clean and dry.

2. Use your comb to section off the hair you want to cut into fringe. This can be as narrow or as wide as you want, depending on the style you're going for.

3. Use your fingers to hold the hair taut, and then cut across the section with your scissors. Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length.

4. Repeat the process on the other side, making sure to keep the sections even.

5. If you're going for a side-swept fringe, angle your scissors diagonally as you cut to create a softer look.

6. Once you've finished cutting, use your comb to blend the fringe into the rest of your hair.

Tips for Cutting Fringe

- Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length. It's easier to take more off than it is to put it back on!

- Don't cut your fringe too short. It's better to err on the side of caution and leave it a little longer than you think you want.

- If you're nervous about cutting your own fringe, consider going to a professional stylist for help.

- Remember that fringe can take some getting used to. It may look different than you expected at first, but give it a few days to settle in before making any changes.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut fringe on curly hair?

A: Yes, but it can be a little trickier. Make sure your hair is dry and straightened before cutting, and consider cutting it a little longer than you think you want to account for shrinkage.

Q: How often do I need to trim my fringe?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your fringe starts to look uneven. As a general rule, plan on trimming it every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care for Fringe

Now that you've cut your fringe, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

- Wash your fringe every few days to keep it clean and fresh.

- Blow-dry your fringe with a round brush to give it volume and shape.

- Avoid using heavy styling products on your fringe, as they can weigh it down and make it look greasy.

- If you're struggling with oily fringe, consider using dry shampoo between washes.

With these tips and tricks, you're ready to cut your own fringe and enjoy a fresh new look. Good luck!
