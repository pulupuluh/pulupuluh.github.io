How Should A Yorkie Hair Be Cut A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Apr 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a proud owner of a Yorkshire Terrier or Yorkie, you know that their long, silky hair is a big part of their charm. However, keeping their hair healthy and well-groomed can be a challenge. One of the most important aspects of Yorkie hair care is regular haircuts. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how should a Yorkie hair be cut.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut a Yorkie's Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Yorkie Haircut
  3. Tips for Cutting Yorkie Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Yorkie Hair Care

How to Cut a Yorkie's Hair

The first step in cutting a Yorkie's hair is determining the desired length. Yorkie hair can be cut anywhere from half an inch to several inches long. Once you've decided on the length, gather the necessary tools: a pair of sharp scissors, thinning shears, and a comb.

It's important to note that Yorkie hair is delicate and prone to matting, so it's best to cut their hair when it's dry and tangle-free. Begin by combing through the hair to remove any knots or tangles. Then, use the scissors to trim the hair around the ears, paws, and tail. Use the thinning shears to blend the hair and create a natural look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Yorkie Haircut

1. Start by combing through your Yorkie's hair to remove any tangles or mats.

2. Trim the hair around the ears, paws, and tail with sharp scissors.

3. Use thinning shears to blend the hair and create a natural look.

4. Trim the hair on the body to the desired length, using scissors or clippers.

5. Pay special attention to the hair around the anus, as it can become matted and cause discomfort.

6. Trim the hair on the legs to match the length of the body hair.

7. Use thinning shears to blend the leg hair and create a natural look.

8. Use a comb to check for any remaining tangles or mats and trim them as necessary.

9. Finish the haircut by trimming any stray hairs and giving your Yorkie a final comb through.

Tips for Cutting Yorkie Hair

1. Use sharp scissors and thinning shears to avoid pulling or tearing the hair.

2. Cut the hair when it's dry and tangle-free to avoid matting.

3. Take breaks during the haircut to give your Yorkie a chance to stretch and relax.

4. Use treats or toys to distract your Yorkie and make the haircut more enjoyable.

5. Consider taking your Yorkie to a professional groomer for their first few haircuts.

FAQs

How often should you cut a Yorkie's hair?

Yorkie hair should be cut every 6-8 weeks to maintain its health and appearance.

Can you use clippers to cut a Yorkie's hair?

Yes, clippers can be used to cut Yorkie hair, but it's important to use a guard to avoid cutting the hair too short or unevenly.

What should you do if you accidentally cut your Yorkie's skin?

If you accidentally cut your Yorkie's skin, apply a small amount of cornstarch or styptic powder to stop the bleeding. If the cut is deep or doesn't stop bleeding, seek veterinary care.

Yorkie Hair Care

In addition to regular haircuts, there are several things you can do to keep your Yorkie's hair healthy and shiny. Brush your Yorkie's hair daily to prevent tangles and mats. Use a detangling spray or conditioner to make brushing easier. Bathe your Yorkie every 4-6 weeks with a gentle shampoo formulated for dogs. Avoid using human shampoo, as it can dry out their skin and hair.

By following these tips and guidelines, you can ensure that your Yorkie's hair looks and feels its best. Remember to always use caution when cutting their hair, and seek professional help if you're unsure about anything.
