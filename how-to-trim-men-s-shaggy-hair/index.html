How To Trim Men s Shaggy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine May 14, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Shaggy hair has been a popular men's hairstyle for years, and it's not going away anytime soon. However, maintaining that shaggy look can be a bit tricky, especially if you don't want to go to the salon every few weeks. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to trim men's shaggy hair at home, so you can keep your hair looking great without breaking the bank.

  • How to Trim Men's Shaggy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Trimming Shaggy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Shaggy Hair

How to Trim Men's Shaggy Hair

Trimming shaggy hair is all about creating layers and texture. The goal is to keep the length, but remove any split ends or damaged hair. Here are the basic steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash your hair and towel dry it until it's damp, but not soaking wet.

Step 2: Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 3: Start by trimming the ends of your hair. Take small sections of hair and trim only the tips, making sure to cut straight across.

Step 4: Move on to creating layers. Take a section of hair and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to cut into the hair at a slight angle, removing a small amount of hair. Repeat this process throughout your hair, focusing on the areas that need more texture.

Step 5: Blend the layers together by taking small sections of hair and cutting into them at a slight angle. This will help create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 6: Use thinning shears to remove any bulk or thickness. Take small sections of hair and use the shears to remove some of the hair, creating a more natural look.

Step 7: Finish by trimming any stray hairs or split ends.

Tips for Trimming Shaggy Hair

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors and thinning shears. This will make the process much easier and prevent damage to your hair.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to trim a little bit at a time and check your progress along the way.
  • Keep your hair dry while trimming. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven cuts.
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors. This will help you create even layers and prevent any accidental choppy cuts.

FAQs

Q: How often should I trim my shaggy hair?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much maintenance you want to do. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good timeline for trimming shaggy hair.

Q: Can I trim my shaggy hair myself?

A: Yes, with the right tools and technique, you can definitely trim your shaggy hair at home.

Q: Do I need to use any products after trimming my shaggy hair?

A: It's always a good idea to use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil after trimming to help keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Hair Care for Shaggy Hair

Maintaining shaggy hair requires a little bit of extra care. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause breakage and split ends.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to avoid pulling and damaging your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily trim your shaggy hair at home and keep it looking great between salon visits. Just remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have a stylish, low-maintenance look in no time.
