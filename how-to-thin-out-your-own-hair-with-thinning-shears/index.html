How To Thin Out Your Own Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Petter Aug 02, 2023 · 6 min read

Thinning out your hair with thinning shears is a great way to add some texture and volume to your hair. It’s also a great way to remove some of the bulk if your hair is too thick. If you’re wondering how to thin out your own hair with thinning shears, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we’ll be discussing everything you need to know about thinning your own hair with thinning shears.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth on one of the blades. These teeth are designed to remove some of the hair with each cut, creating a more textured and less bulky look. Thinning shears are great for people with thick or heavy hair, as they can help to remove some of the weight and create more movement in the hair.

How To

Thinning out your own hair with thinning shears is a fairly easy process. However, it’s important to be careful and take your time, as you don’t want to accidentally cut too much hair or create an uneven look.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you begin, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see where you need to thin out your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don’t miss any areas.

Step 3: Start Thinning Your Hair

Begin by taking a small section of hair and holding it between your fingers. Use the thinning shears to make small cuts along the length of the hair. Make sure to only cut a little bit of hair with each cut, as you can always go back and cut more if needed.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After you’ve thinned out a section of hair, let it fall naturally and check your progress. You may need to thin out more or less depending on how your hair looks.

Step 5: Repeat

Continue thinning out your hair section by section until you’ve achieved the desired look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when thinning out your own hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and gradually work your way up
  • Don’t cut too much hair at once, as you can always go back and cut more if needed
  • Be careful not to cut yourself with the thinning shears
  • Don’t thin out your hair too much, as it can lead to a frizzy or uneven look

FAQ

Can I use regular scissors to thin out my hair?

No, regular scissors are not designed for thinning out hair. Thinning shears have teeth on one of the blades that help to remove some of the hair with each cut, creating a more textured look.

How often should I thin out my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it’s recommended to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks or as needed.

Hair Care

After thinning out your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized

Thinning out your own hair with thinning shears is a great way to add some texture and volume to your hair. By following the steps outlined in this article and using some of the tips and tricks, you can achieve a beautiful, textured look that’s perfect for any occasion.
