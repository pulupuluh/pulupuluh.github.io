How To Determine Your Black Natural Hair Type

Having natural black hair can be both beautiful and challenging. One of the first steps in taking care of your hair is to determine your hair type. Knowing your hair type can help you choose the right products and styling techniques that work best for your hair. In this article, we'll walk you through how to determine your black natural hair type.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Determine Your Hair Type

There are four main hair types for black natural hair: Type 1 (straight), Type 2 (wavy), Type 3 (curly), and Type 4 (coily/kinky). Each type has subcategories depending on the texture and thickness of your hair. To determine your hair type, follow these simple steps:

Step 1: Look at Your Hair's Shape

The shape of your hair can tell you a lot about your hair type. If your hair is straight with no curl pattern, you likely have Type 1 hair. If your hair has a slight wave or curl, you may have Type 2 hair. If your hair has a defined curl pattern, you may have Type 3 hair. If your hair has a tight coil pattern, you likely have Type 4 hair.

Step 2: Check Your Hair's Texture

The texture of your hair refers to the thickness of each individual strand. If your hair is fine and thin, you have a finer hair texture. If your hair is thick and coarse, you have a coarser hair texture. This can affect how your hair responds to different products and styling techniques.

Step 3: Determine Your Hair's Porosity

Porosity refers to how easily your hair absorbs and retains moisture. If your hair absorbs moisture quickly and retains it well, you have high porosity hair. If your hair takes longer to absorb moisture and doesn't retain it well, you have low porosity hair. Understanding your hair's porosity can help you choose the right products and techniques to hydrate and moisturize your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to determining your black natural hair type:

  1. Examine the shape of your hair to determine if it is straight, wavy, curly, or coily/kinky.
  2. Check the texture of your hair to determine if it is fine or coarse.
  3. Determine your hair's porosity by taking a strand of hair and placing it in a bowl of water for a few minutes. If it sinks quickly, you have high porosity hair. If it floats to the top, you have low porosity hair.
  4. Use the chart below to determine your hair type based on the shape, texture, and porosity of your hair:
Hair TypeShapeTexturePorosity
Type 1StraightFine or coarseHigh or low
Type 2WavyFine or coarseHigh or low
Type 3CurlyFine or coarseHigh or low
Type 4Coily/kinkyFine or coarseHigh or low

Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type

Once you have determined your hair type, it's important to care for your hair accordingly. Here are some tips for caring for each hair type:

Type 1

Type 1 hair is the easiest to manage and style. Avoid heavy products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy. Use a light, moisturizing shampoo and conditioner and avoid heat styling as much as possible.

Type 2

Type 2 hair can be styled in a variety of ways, from straight to wavy to curly. Use a curl-enhancing product to define your waves and avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.

Type 3

Type 3 hair requires more moisture than Type 1 or Type 2 hair. Use a leave-in conditioner and a curl defining product to help define your curls. Avoid heat styling and harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

Type 4

Type 4 hair is the most delicate and requires the most moisture. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner and a leave-in conditioner to help hydrate your curls. Avoid heat styling and harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, your hair type can change over time. Factors like age, hormonal changes, and styling techniques can all affect your hair's texture and curl pattern.

Q: Can I have more than one hair type?

A: Yes, it's possible to have more than one hair type. For example, you may have Type 3 hair on the top of your head and Type 4 hair on the back and sides.

Hair Care for Your Hair Type

Now that you know your hair type, it's important to take care of your hair accordingly. Here are some general hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid heat styling and harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using cotton pillowcases as they can absorb moisture from your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Determining your black natural hair type can be a helpful first step in taking care of your hair. By understanding your hair's shape, texture, and porosity, you can choose the right products and styling techniques that work best for your hair. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and techniques until you find what works best for you.
