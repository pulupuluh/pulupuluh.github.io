How To Stop Losing Hair After Having A Baby Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Aug 13, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're a new mom, you may be experiencing hair loss after having a baby. This can be distressing, but don't worry – it's a common issue that affects many women. In this article, we'll discuss the causes of postpartum hair loss and provide tips on how to stop losing hair after having a baby.

Causes of Postpartum Hair Loss

During pregnancy, your body experiences a surge in hormones, which can cause your hair to grow thicker and longer than usual. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels start to return to normal. This can trigger a shedding phase, where you lose more hair than usual. This phase typically occurs around 3-6 months after giving birth, and can last up to a year.

Other factors that can contribute to postpartum hair loss include stress, lack of sleep, and nutritional deficiencies. If you're experiencing excessive hair loss or other symptoms, it's important to talk to your healthcare provider to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Hair Care Tips

While postpartum hair loss is a natural process, there are things you can do to minimize the amount of hair you lose. Here are some tips:

1. Be Gentle

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, especially when it's wet. Use a wide-toothed comb or a soft-bristled brush to detangle your hair, and avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hairline.

2. Avoid Heat

Limit your use of hot styling tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, as these can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature on the lowest setting.

3. Wash Less Frequently

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can make it more brittle and prone to breakage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, and use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid further damage.

4. Use a Hair Mask

Applying a nourishing hair mask once a week can help to strengthen your hair and prevent it from breaking. Look for masks that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

Diet and Supplements

Eating a healthy diet and taking supplements can also help to support healthy hair growth. Here are some nutrients to focus on:

1. Protein

Your hair is made up of protein, so it's important to make sure you're getting enough in your diet. Good sources of protein include lean meats, fish, eggs, and beans.

2. Iron

Iron is essential for healthy hair growth, as it helps to carry oxygen to your hair follicles. Good sources of iron include red meat, spinach, and lentils.

3. Biotin

Biotin is a B-vitamin that is essential for healthy hair, skin, and nails. You can find biotin in foods like eggs, nuts, and whole grains, or take a biotin supplement.

Stress Reduction

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it's important to find ways to manage your stress levels. Here are some ideas:

1. Exercise

Regular exercise can help to reduce stress and improve your overall health. Aim to get at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise most days of the week.

2. Practice Relaxation Techniques

Yoga, meditation, and deep breathing exercises can all help to reduce stress and promote relaxation. Try incorporating these practices into your daily routine.

Hairstyle and Accessories

The way you style your hair can also make a difference in how much hair you lose. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can pull on your hairline and cause breakage. Opt for loose hairstyles, such as a messy bun or a loose braid, instead.

2. Use Hair Accessories Carefully

Avoid using hair accessories that can pull on your hair, such as elastic bands or barrettes. Instead, use soft, fabric hair ties or hairpins to secure your hair.

FAQ

1. Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is usually temporary and should resolve on its own within a year of giving birth.

2. Can hair loss be a sign of a medical problem?

Yes, excessive hair loss or other symptoms can be a sign of an underlying medical condition. It's important to talk to your healthcare provider if you're concerned.

3. Can I color my hair while experiencing postpartum hair loss?

It's generally safe to color your hair while experiencing postpartum hair loss, but it's best to avoid harsh chemicals and opt for a gentle, ammonia-free hair dye.

By following these tips, you can help to minimize postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient – it may take some time for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state, but with proper care, it will get there!
