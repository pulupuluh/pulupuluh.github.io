Why Does My Hair Curl More Underneath

Written by Frank Mar 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you one of those people whose hair curls more underneath than on top? If so, you're not alone. Many people experience this phenomenon, and it can be frustrating to try and style your hair in a way that looks even. In this article, we'll explore some of the reasons why your hair might curl more underneath, as well as some tips and tricks to help you manage it.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Identify Your Hair Type
  • Why Does Hair Curl More Underneath?
  • Tips and Tricks for Managing Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Identify Your Hair Type

Before we dive into why your hair might curl more underneath, it's important to identify your hair type. There are four main types of hair:

  • Straight
  • Wavy
  • Curly
  • Coily/Kinky

Each hair type has different needs and requires different care. If you're not sure what your hair type is, try the hair typing system created by Andre Walker, Oprah Winfrey's former hairstylist:

  • Type 1: Straight
  • Type 2: Wavy
  • Type 3: Curly
  • Type 4: Coily/Kinky

Once you know your hair type, you can better understand how to care for your hair and style it.

Why Does Hair Curl More Underneath?

There are several reasons why your hair might curl more underneath:

1. Genetics

One of the main reasons why your hair might curl more underneath is genetics. If your parents have curly hair, there's a good chance that you inherited the gene for curly hair as well. Genetics can also determine the texture and thickness of your hair, which can affect how it curls.

2. Heat Damage

If you use heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons frequently, you may be causing heat damage to your hair. Heat damage can make your hair more prone to curling, especially underneath where it's closer to the scalp.

3. Moisture Imbalance

Another reason why your hair might curl more underneath is a moisture imbalance. When your hair is dry, it can become more prone to curling, especially in areas where it's closer to the scalp. On the other hand, if your hair is too moisturized, it can become limp and lose its curl.

Tips and Tricks for Managing Curly Hair

If you're struggling with curly hair that curls more underneath, here are some tips and tricks to help you manage it:

1. Use the Right Products

Using the right products for your hair type can make a huge difference in how your hair looks and feels. Look for products specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl defining creams or gels, to help enhance your natural curls.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

To prevent further heat damage, try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible.

3. Embrace Your Curls

Instead of trying to fight your curls, embrace them! Experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you. Curly hair can be beautiful and unique, so don't be afraid to show it off.

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but be aware that heat styling can cause damage and make your hair more prone to curling. If you must straighten your hair, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much oil your scalp produces. Some people with curly hair can go several days without washing, while others may need to wash every other day. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

To keep your curly hair healthy and manageable, follow these tips:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Cleanse your hair with a gentle shampoo that won't strip away natural oils. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that's specifically designed for curly hair.

2. Condition Regularly

Use a conditioner that's designed for curly hair to help detangle and moisturize your hair. Consider using a leave-in conditioner for extra moisture.

3. Avoid Brushing Wet Hair

Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so avoid brushing it when it's wet. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.

4. Protect Your Hair While You Sleep

Try sleeping on a satin pillowcase or using a satin bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep. This can help prevent frizz and breakage.

In conclusion, there are several reasons why your hair might curl more underneath, including genetics, heat damage, and moisture imbalance. By using the right products and techniques, you can manage your curly hair and embrace your natural curls.
