How To Take Care Of Big Chop Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 24, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you thinking of doing the big chop? Or have you already done it and need some tips on how to take care of your new hair? Well, you’ve come to the right place! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to take care of big chop hair.

Description

The big chop is a drastic haircut that involves cutting off all of your relaxed or damaged hair, leaving only your natural hair. This is a big decision for many women, as it can be a major change in their appearance. However, it can also be liberating and empowering, as you embrace your natural hair texture.

How to Take Care of Big Chop Hair

Step 1: Moisturize

One of the most important things you can do for your big chop hair is to keep it moisturized. Natural hair tends to be drier than relaxed hair, so it’s important to use products that will help to lock in moisture. Look for products that contain natural oils like coconut oil, olive oil, and jojoba oil.

Step 2: Detangle

Detangling your hair is crucial to prevent breakage and damage. Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently work through any knots or tangles. Start from the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Protect

Protective styles like braids, twists, and buns can help to protect your hair from damage and breakage. These styles also help to keep your hair moisturized, as you can apply products to your hair before styling it.

Step 4: Trim

Regular trims are important to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. It’s recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you take care of your big chop hair:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and tangling
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, or use them sparingly
  • Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates
  • Wash your hair every 1-2 weeks, depending on your hair type and texture
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to take care of big chop hair:

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It’s recommended to wash your hair every 1-2 weeks, depending on your hair type and texture. If your hair is very dry, you may want to wash it less frequently.

Q: Can I use heat styling tools on my big chop hair?

A: It’s best to avoid using heat styling tools, or use them sparingly. Heat can cause damage and breakage, especially on natural hair.

Q: How do I prevent breakage and damage?

A: To prevent breakage and damage, make sure to keep your hair moisturized, detangle your hair gently, and use protective styles like braids and twists.

Hair Care

Taking care of your big chop hair can seem daunting at first, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your hair healthy and looking its best. Remember to moisturize, detangle, protect, and trim your hair regularly, and follow our additional tips to keep your hair in top condition.

With these tips, you can rock your big chop hair with confidence and style!
