Does Jet Black Hair Make You Look Older

Written by Aprilia Apr 09, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have jet black hair, you may be wondering if it makes you look older. There is a common misconception that dark hair makes you look more mature, but is this really true? In this article, we will explore whether or not jet black hair makes you look older and provide some tips on how to care for your hair if you decide to go for the darker look.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Jet black hair can be a stunning and dramatic look, but many people are concerned that it can make them look older than they really are. This concern is understandable, as darker hair can create a more severe look, especially when paired with pale skin. However, the truth is that whether or not jet black hair makes you look older depends on several factors, including your skin tone and the overall style of your hair.

How to

If you're considering dyeing your hair jet black, there are a few things you should keep in mind to ensure that the color looks great on you:

Consider your skin tone

One of the most important things to consider when choosing a hair color is your skin tone. If you have fair or pale skin, jet black hair can create a stark contrast that may make you look older. However, if you have a warmer or darker skin tone, jet black hair can create a beautiful, bold look that complements your complexion.

Choose a flattering cut and style

The way you style your hair can also affect how old or young you look. If you're concerned about looking too mature with jet black hair, consider choosing a cut and style that is more youthful and playful. For example, you might opt for a layered bob or a messy updo rather than a sleek, straight style.

Use the right products

To keep your jet black hair looking its best, you'll need to use products that are specifically designed for color-treated hair. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are formulated to protect and prolong the life of your color, and use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Step by step

If you're ready to dye your hair jet black, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get the best results:

Step 1: Prepare your hair

Before you dye your hair, you'll need to prepare it by washing and drying it thoroughly. You should also apply a protective barrier cream around your hairline and ears to prevent the dye from staining your skin.

Step 2: Mix your dye

Follow the instructions on your hair dye to mix it properly, and be sure to wear gloves to protect your hands from staining. If you're not sure how to mix your dye, consult a professional or watch a tutorial online.

Step 3: Apply the dye

Starting at the roots, apply the dye to your hair in small sections, making sure to saturate each strand evenly. Be careful not to get any dye on your skin or clothing, and avoid overlapping sections of hair to prevent uneven color.

Step 4: Wait and rinse

After you've applied the dye, wait the recommended amount of time before rinsing it out. Be sure to use cool water and a color-safe shampoo to rinse your hair thoroughly, and avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners for at least a few days after dyeing your hair.

Tips

  • If you're not sure whether jet black hair is right for you, try a semi-permanent dye first to see how you like the color.
  • Consider adding highlights or lowlights to your jet black hair to create a more dimensional look.
  • Use a color-safe dry shampoo to extend the life of your color between washes.

FAQ

Does jet black hair make you look older?

Not necessarily. Whether or not jet black hair makes you look older depends on several factors, including your skin tone and the overall style of your hair.

What should I consider before dyeing my hair jet black?

You should consider your skin tone, the style of your hair, and the products you'll need to care for your jet black hair before dyeing your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your jet black hair looking its best, you'll need to take good care of it. Here are some tips to help you care for your color-treated hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent color fading
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner

In conclusion, jet black hair can be a stunning and dramatic look, but whether or not it makes you look older depends on several factors. By considering your skin tone, the style of your hair, and the products you use to care for your hair, you can ensure that your jet black hair looks great and complements your overall look.
