How To Stop Hair Fall Postpartum

Written by Bardi Mar 26, 2023 · 7 min read

Postpartum hair loss is a normal phenomenon that affects many new mothers. The hair fall can be alarming for new moms, but it is a natural process that occurs after giving birth. The good news is that there are ways to minimize hair fall and promote healthy hair growth postpartum.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Stop Hair Fall Postpartum
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQ

Description

Postpartum hair loss is a temporary hair loss that occurs after giving birth. During pregnancy, hormones cause the hair to remain in the growth phase, resulting in fuller and thicker hair. After delivery, the hormones drop, and the hair enters the resting phase, leading to hair fall. The hair fall can start a few weeks after delivery and can last up to six months. The hair loss can be more pronounced if you had a C-section or experienced a lot of stress during and after delivery.

How to Stop Hair Fall Postpartum

Here are some ways to minimize hair fall and promote hair growth postpartum:

Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth. Include foods that are high in protein, iron, and vitamin C to promote healthy hair growth. Leafy greens, eggs, nuts, and berries are great options to add to your diet.

Reduce Stress

Stress can contribute to hair fall, so it's essential to take care of your mental health. Engage in activities that promote relaxation, such as yoga or meditation. Also, try to get enough sleep and take breaks whenever possible.

Use Gentle Hair Care Products

Use gentle hair care products that do not contain harsh chemicals that can damage your hair. Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and hair dryers, as they can cause hair breakage. Instead, air-dry your hair or use a cool setting on your hair dryer.

Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help increase blood flow to your hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp gently for a few minutes every day.

Take Supplements

Supplements can help provide your body with the essential vitamins and minerals it needs for healthy hair growth. Biotin, vitamin D, and iron are some of the supplements that can help promote hair growth.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to stop hair fall postpartum:

  1. Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  2. Reduce stress by engaging in relaxation activities.
  3. Use gentle hair care products that do not contain harsh chemicals.
  4. Massage your scalp gently every day.
  5. Take supplements to provide your body with essential vitamins and minerals.

Tips for Hair Care

Here are some additional tips to promote healthy hair growth:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause hair breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your body hydrated.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

FAQ

What causes postpartum hair loss?

Postpartum hair loss is caused by hormonal changes that occur after giving birth. During pregnancy, hormones cause the hair to remain in the growth phase, resulting in fuller and thicker hair. After delivery, the hormones drop, and the hair enters the resting phase, leading to hair fall.

When does postpartum hair loss start?

Postpartum hair loss can start a few weeks after delivery and can last up to six months.

Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition. Hair growth typically returns to normal six to twelve months after delivery.

Can stress cause postpartum hair loss?

Yes, stress can contribute to hair fall, so it's essential to take care of your mental health.

What can I do to promote healthy hair growth postpartum?

Eat a balanced diet, reduce stress, use gentle hair care products, massage your scalp, and take supplements to promote healthy hair growth postpartum.

In conclusion, postpartum hair loss is a natural process that occurs after giving birth. While hair fall can be alarming, there are ways to minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth postpartum. Follow these steps, take care of your hair, and remember that the hair fall is temporary.
