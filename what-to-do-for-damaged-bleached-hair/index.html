What To Do For Damaged Bleached Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Apr 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have bleached your hair and are now experiencing damage and breakage, don't worry! There are things you can do to help repair and strengthen your hair. In this article, we will go over some tips and tricks on what to do for damaged bleached hair.

How to Repair Damaged Bleached Hair

Firstly, it is important to understand why bleached hair is prone to damage. Bleach strips your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. The process also weakens the hair's structure, making it more susceptible to breakage. Here are some things you can do to repair your damaged bleached hair:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

One of the most effective ways to repair damaged bleached hair is by using a deep conditioning treatment. Look for a product that contains ingredients like keratin, argan oil, or shea butter. Apply the treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends, and leave it on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing it out.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can further damage your already weakened hair. Try to avoid using them altogether, or at least use them sparingly. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to help minimize damage.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Split ends can cause further breakage and damage to your hair. Regular trims can help prevent this by removing the damaged ends of your hair. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

Step-by-Step Guide to Repairing Damaged Bleached Hair

Now that you know what to do to repair your damaged bleached hair, here is a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo. Avoid using a clarifying shampoo, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step 2: Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub it too hard. Avoid using a hair dryer, as the heat can cause further damage.

Step 4: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it moisturized throughout the day.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.

Step 6: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Get regular trims to remove split ends and prevent further breakage.

Tips for Preventing Damaged Bleached Hair

Prevention is key when it comes to keeping your hair healthy and strong. Here are some tips for preventing damaged bleached hair:

1. Avoid Over-Processing Your Hair

Don't bleach your hair too often. Give your hair time to recover between treatments.

2. Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfates can be harsh on your hair, so switch to a sulfate-free shampoo to help keep your hair healthy.

3. Use a Heat Protectant Spray

If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

4. Wear a Hat in the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun's harmful UV rays by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

FAQs About Damaged Bleached Hair

1. Can damaged bleached hair be repaired?

Yes, damaged bleached hair can be repaired with the right products and treatments.

2. How long does it take to repair damaged bleached hair?

It depends on the extent of the damage. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to see significant improvement.

3. Can I still bleach my hair if it is damaged?

It is not recommended to bleach your hair if it is already damaged. Give your hair time to recover before bleaching it again.

Hair Care for Damaged Bleached Hair

Finally, here are some general hair care tips for keeping your bleached hair healthy:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

2. Don't Brush Wet Hair

Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so avoid brushing it when it is wet.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids can cause breakage, so avoid them if possible.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help repair and strengthen your damaged bleached hair. Remember to be patient, as it can take time to see significant improvement. With the right care, your hair can be healthy and beautiful once again!
