How To Cut A Choppy Pixie Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 14, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Pixie cuts are a popular hairstyle that never seems to go out of style. It's a low-maintenance haircut that can make you look chic and edgy. If you're looking for a new look, why not try a choppy pixie cut? This haircut is perfect for those who want a drastic change in their hairstyle. In this article, we'll show you how to cut a choppy pixie cut.

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that is usually cut above the ears and at the nape of the neck. It's a low-maintenance hairstyle that is perfect for those who don't have a lot of time to style their hair. A choppy pixie cut is a variation of the pixie cut that is cut unevenly to create a textured and edgy look.

How to Cut a Choppy Pixie Cut

Step 1: Prepare your tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle with water to keep the hair damp.

Step 2: Section the hair

Start by sectioning the hair into four parts: the front, the sides, and the back. Clip the sections of hair that you're not working on to keep them out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the back

Start at the back of the head and work your way up. Use your comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle. Cut small sections of hair at a time to create a choppy look. Make sure to keep the hair damp so that it's easier to cut.

Step 4: Cut the sides

Once you've finished cutting the back, move on to the sides. Start at the bottom and work your way up. Cut the hair at an angle to create a choppy look. Keep the hair damp so that it's easier to cut.

Step 5: Cut the front

Finally, move on to the front. Cut the hair at an angle to create a choppy look. Make sure to cut the hair around the face so that it frames it nicely. Keep the hair damp so that it's easier to cut.

Step 6: Style the hair

Once you've finished cutting, style the hair to your liking. You can use a wax or pomade to create a textured and edgy look. You can also use a straightener to create a sleek and polished look.

Tips

  • Start with dry hair so that you can see how the hair falls naturally.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Don't be afraid to cut more hair if you want a more dramatic look.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, seek the help of a professional.

FAQ

Can I cut a choppy pixie cut on my own?

Yes, you can cut a choppy pixie cut on your own, but it's important to be careful and take your time. If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, seek the help of a professional.

How often do I need to trim my choppy pixie cut?

You'll need to trim your choppy pixie cut every four to six weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Hair Care

To keep your choppy pixie cut looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and use a heat protectant spray when using styling tools. It's also important to get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.
