What Does A 7 Guard Haircut Look Like A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William May 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you considering getting a haircut with a 7 guard but don't know what it looks like? Look no further! In this article, we'll break down everything you need to know about a 7 guard haircut. From the description, how-to, step-by-step guide, tips, FAQ, and hair care, we've got you covered. So, let's dive in!

Description

A 7 guard haircut is a hairstyle that is achieved by using a clipper guard number 7. This guard leaves hair 7/8 inch or 22mm long. It's a popular choice for men because it's short enough to be low maintenance but long enough to style. The 7 guard is perfect for those who want to keep their hair short but not too short.

How To

To achieve a 7 guard haircut, you'll need to have a clipper with a number 7 guard attachment. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Attach the number 7 guard to your clipper.
  3. Begin at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown of the head, using the clipper in an upward motion.
  4. Move to the sides and use the clipper in a downward motion.
  5. Blend the sides and back into the top by using a shorter guard, such as a 6 or 5, and fade the hair gradually.
  6. Trim the top of the hair with scissors or a comb and clipper.
  7. Style as desired.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to achieve a 7 guard haircut:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start cutting it. Wet hair can be harder to cut, and it's easier to see the length of your hair when it's dry.

Step 2: Attach the Number 7 Guard to Your Clipper

Make sure the clipper is clean and oiled before attaching the guard. This will ensure a smooth and even cut.

Step 3: Start at the Nape of the Neck

Begin at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown of the head, using the clipper in an upward motion. Make sure to keep the clipper level and move it slowly to ensure an even cut.

Step 4: Move to the Sides

Move to the sides of the head and use the clipper in a downward motion. Again, make sure to keep the clipper level and move it slowly to ensure an even cut.

Step 5: Blend the Sides and Back into the Top

Use a shorter guard, such as a 6 or 5, to blend the sides and back into the top of the hair. Gradually fade the hair to create a smooth transition.

Step 6: Trim the Top of the Hair

Use scissors or a comb and clipper to trim the top of the hair to the desired length. Make sure to cut evenly and check for any stray hairs.

Step 7: Style as Desired

Once you're done cutting your hair, style it as desired. You can use a hair product to add texture and hold.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect 7 guard haircut:

  • Start with a longer guard and gradually work your way down to ensure you don't cut your hair too short.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make sure your cut is even.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time to ensure an even cut.
  • Experiment with different hair products to find one that works for you.

FAQ

What Face Shapes Suit a 7 Guard Haircut?

A 7 guard haircut is versatile and suits most face shapes. It's a great option for those with a round or square face because it can help to add length and balance out the face.

How Often Should You Get a 7 Guard Haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. Generally, a 7 guard haircut can last up to 6 weeks before it needs to be trimmed again.

Can You Achieve a 7 Guard Haircut with Scissors?

Technically, yes. However, it's much easier and more precise to achieve a 7 guard haircut with clippers. Using scissors can result in an uneven cut and take longer to achieve the desired length.

Hair Care

To maintain your 7 guard haircut, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as blow dryers and straighteners, too often.
  • Use a hair product to add texture and hold.
  • Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the length and shape.

Now that you know everything there is to know about a 7 guard haircut, you can confidently head to your barber or DIY it at home. Just remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully for the best results!
