Can Males Have Braids In The Army

Table of Contents:

Description

Many men and women serving in the military may want to wear braids in their hair, whether for practical or cultural reasons. However, there have been some questions regarding whether or not males can have braids in the Army. In short, the answer is yes, but there are some regulations that must be followed.

How to

Before deciding to wear braids in the Army, it is important to ensure that they comply with the Army's grooming standards. According to Army Regulation 670-1, hair must be neat and conservative in style. Braids must be small in diameter (no larger than a quarter of an inch) and must be worn in a straight line. Hair must be pulled back and not touch the collar of the uniform.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to wear braids in the Army:

  1. Ensure that your hair is within regulation length and style.
  2. Part your hair into small sections.
  3. Braid each section tightly, making sure each braid is no larger than a quarter of an inch in diameter.
  4. Secure the ends of each braid with a rubber band or hair tie.
  5. Pull all braids back and secure them in a straight line, ensuring that they do not touch the collar of the uniform.
  6. Use hairspray or gel to keep any flyaways in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to ensure that your braids are within Army regulations:

  • Use a ruler or measuring tape to ensure that each braid is no larger than a quarter of an inch in diameter.
  • If you have longer hair, consider wearing a hairnet or bun to keep hair from touching the collar of the uniform.
  • Apply hairspray or gel to keep braids in place and prevent flyaways.

FAQ

Q: Can males have cornrows in the Army?

A: Yes, males can have cornrows in the Army as long as they are within regulation diameter and style.

Q: Can males have dreadlocks in the Army?

A: Yes, males can have dreadlocks in the Army as long as they are within regulation diameter and style. However, new recruits may not have dreadlocks until they have completed basic training.

Hair Care

Wearing braids in the Army can cause hair breakage and dryness if not properly cared for. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy while wearing braids:

  • Moisturize hair regularly with leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Do not leave braids in for too long. Braids should be taken down after 6-8 weeks to prevent hair breakage.
  • Wash braids regularly to prevent buildup of oil and dirt.

In conclusion, males can have braids in the Army as long as they comply with Army grooming regulations. It is important to properly care for hair while wearing braids to prevent breakage and dryness.
