How To Cut Your Own Long Hair Male A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 06, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're a man with long hair, you know how important it is to keep it looking neat and presentable. However, regular trips to the hair salon can be expensive, and sometimes you just don't have the time or energy to go. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your own long hair male in a relaxed English language, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Long Hair Male

Description

Cutting your own long hair male can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Not only will you save money on salon visits, but you'll also have the satisfaction of knowing you did it yourself. However, it's important to note that cutting your own hair requires patience, practice, and a steady hand.

How to Cut Your Own Long Hair Male

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's first talk about the tools you'll need. Here are some essential items:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers with guard attachments
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape to protect your clothes

Step-by-Step Guide

Now, let's get into the nitty-gritty of how to cut your own long hair male:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. It's easier to cut hair that isn't wet or tangled.

  2. Section your hair into manageable parts. Use hair ties or clips to keep the sections separate.

  3. Use clippers to trim the sides and back of your head. Start with a longer guard attachment and work your way down to a shorter one if necessary. Be careful not to go too short too quickly.

  4. Use scissors to trim the top and front of your head. Comb the hair forward and cut small sections at a time. Don't cut too much at once, as you can always go back and trim more.

  5. Blend the sides and top together. Use the clippers without a guard to blend the sides into the top. Be careful not to create a visible line.

  6. Trim your bangs, if you have them. Comb them forward and cut small sections at a time. Be careful not to cut them too short.

  7. Check the back of your head with a mirror. Use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments.

  8. Refine your cut. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. Take your time and be careful not to overdo it.

  9. Clean up. Use a vacuum or broom to clean up any hair clippings. Take a shower to wash away any loose hairs.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Long Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind while cutting your own long hair male:

  • Practice makes perfect. Don't expect to get it right on the first try.
  • Be patient. Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Start with a longer guard attachment on the clippers and work your way down if necessary.
  • Use scissors to trim the top and front of your head. Comb the hair forward and cut small sections at a time.
  • Blend the sides and top together to create a seamless look.
  • Check the back of your head with a handheld mirror to make any necessary adjustments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting your own long hair male:

Can I cut my own long hair with regular scissors?

While you technically can, it's not recommended. Regular scissors aren't sharp enough to cut hair cleanly and can cause split ends.

How often should I cut my own long hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it gets unruly. On average, men with long hair should get a trim every 8-12 weeks.

How can I fix a bad haircut?

If you're not happy with your haircut, the best thing to do is wait a few days for the hair to settle and then try to fix it. If you're still not happy, it's best to go to a professional stylist to get it fixed.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own long hair male, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own long hair male can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to make mistakes. With practice, you'll be able to give yourself a great haircut every time.
