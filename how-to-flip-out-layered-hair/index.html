Description

If you're looking for a new hairstyle that's easy to maintain and adds volume to your hair, flipping out your layered hair is a great option. This style involves curling the ends of your hair outwards, creating a bouncy and playful look. Whether you have short or long layered hair, you can achieve this look with the right tools and techniques. In this blog post, we'll show you how to flip out layered hair step by step and share some tips for maintaining this style.

How to Flip Out Layered Hair

Before we get started, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a curling iron with a barrel size of 1 to 1 ½ inches, heat protectant spray, and a comb. Here's how to flip out layered hair:

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the curling iron.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into small sections. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the barrel of the curling iron. Hold the curling iron in place for about 10 seconds, then release the curl. Repeat this process for each section of hair, curling the ends of your hair outwards.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

Once you've curled all of your hair, finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Flipping Out Layered Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when flipping out your layered hair:

  • Use a curling iron with a small barrel size to create tight curls.
  • Don't curl your hair too tightly or it will look unnatural.
  • Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair before using a curling iron.
  • Use hairspray to hold the style in place.
  • Don't overdo it with hairspray or your hair will look stiff and unnatural.

FAQs

How long does this style last?

This style can last up to 2-3 days with proper maintenance.

Can I flip out my layered hair if it's short?

Yes, you can still achieve this look with short layered hair. Use a smaller curling iron barrel size and start at the back of your head.

Can I use a hair straightener instead of a curling iron?

While you can use a hair straightener to flip out your hair, a curling iron will give you better results.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

To keep your layered hair healthy and looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

Now that you know how to flip out layered hair and how to care for your hair, you're ready to rock this playful and bouncy style. Follow our step-by-step guide and use our tips to achieve the best results.
