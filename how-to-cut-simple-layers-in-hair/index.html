How To Cut Simple Layers In Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 22, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers to your hair can give it more volume and texture, and it's easier than you might think to do it yourself. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut simple layers in hair, including tips and tricks for getting the perfect look.

Table of Contents:

Section 1: How to Prepare Your Hair

Section 2: How to Cut Simple Layers in Hair

Section 3: Tips for Cutting Simple Layers

Section 4: FAQ

Section 5: Hair Care After Cutting Layers

Section 1: How to Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to make sure it's clean and dry. Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner, and then blow dry it straight. If you have curly hair, straighten it with a flat iron.

Next, comb your hair straight down and divide it into three sections: left, right, and back. Clip the left and right sections out of the way, leaving the back section loose.

Section 2: How to Cut Simple Layers in Hair

Now it's time to start cutting! Begin by taking a small section of hair from the back, holding it straight up with your fingers, and cutting it straight across at the desired length. This will be your guide for the rest of the layers.

Next, take another small section of hair from the back, and hold it up at a 45-degree angle. Cut it at the same length as your guide, creating a layer. Continue cutting small sections of hair at this angle, working your way up to the crown of your head.

Once you've finished the back section, unclip the left and right sections, and repeat the process on each side, cutting at the same angle.

Finally, trim any uneven ends and check that the layers are even on both sides.

Section 3: Tips for Cutting Simple Layers

Here are some additional tips to help you get the perfect layered look:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and jagged cuts.
  • Don't cut too much at once - it's easier to take off more later than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Keep your sections small and even for a more polished look.
  • Don't cut too close to your face - leave at least an inch of hair in front.

Section 4: FAQ

Q: Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

A: It's best to use hair cutting scissors, which are sharper and designed for cutting hair. Regular scissors can cause split ends and damage to your hair.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Section 5: Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a heat protectant when styling with heat tools to prevent damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much product, which can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Don't wash your hair every day - try to wash it every 2-3 days to prevent stripping it of its natural oils.

By following these tips, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look that will make your hair look more voluminous and full of life. Happy cutting!
