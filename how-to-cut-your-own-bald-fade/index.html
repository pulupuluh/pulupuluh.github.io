How To Cut Your Own Bald Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Mar 22, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking for a sleek and stylish haircut, a bald fade may be just what you need. However, going to the barbershop can be expensive and time-consuming. Fortunately, with the right tools and techniques, you can learn how to cut your own bald fade at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process and offer tips and tricks to help you achieve a professional-looking haircut.

How to Cut Your Own Bald Fade

Before you start cutting, gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. You may also want to invest in a few different clipper guards, which will help you achieve different fade lengths.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before you start cutting. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that your hair is free of any product buildup or dirt.

Step 2: Create a Guideline

Using your clippers, create a guideline around the sides and back of your head. This will mark the point where your hair will start to fade. Use a longer clipper guard for this step.

Step 3: Blend the Hair

Using shorter clipper guards, blend the hair around the guideline, moving from the bottom up. Use a smooth, even motion to create a gradual fade. Be patient and take your time, as this step can be tricky.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Using your scissors, cut the hair on top of your head to your desired length. Be sure to use a comb to keep the hair even and avoid any jagged edges.

Step 5: Refine the Fade

Go back over the fade with a shorter clipper guard, refining any areas that need it. Pay close attention to the transition between the sides and the top of your head, as this can be a tricky area to blend.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Using a handheld mirror, check your work from all angles. Make any necessary adjustments until you're happy with the result.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Bald Fade

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect bald fade:

  • Start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down gradually.
  • Use a comb to keep the hair even and avoid any jagged edges.
  • Be patient and take your time. Fading can be tricky!
  • Check your work from all angles using a handheld mirror.
  • Ask a friend or family member for help if you're having trouble.

FAQ

Can I cut my own bald fade?

Yes! With the right tools and techniques, anyone can learn how to cut their own bald fade.

What tools do I need to cut a bald fade?

You'll need clippers, scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. You may also want to invest in different clipper guards to achieve different fade lengths.

How long does a bald fade last?

A bald fade typically lasts 2-3 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Bald Fade

After you've finished cutting your own bald fade, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Visit a professional stylist every few months to keep your haircut looking fresh.

By following these tips and techniques, you can learn how to cut your own bald fade and achieve a stylish, professional-looking haircut at home. Good luck!
