How To Take Care Of Wavy Frizzy Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Apr 04, 2023 · 9 min read

Wavy frizzy hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right hair care routine and products, you can keep your locks looking their best. In this article, we’ll give you some tips and tricks for taking care of wavy frizzy hair, as well as answer some frequently asked questions about this hair type.

Table of Contents

  • How to Take Care of Wavy Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Hair Care Routine
  • Tips for Managing Wavy Frizzy Hair
  • FAQs About Wavy Frizzy Hair

How to Take Care of Wavy Frizzy Hair

Wavy frizzy hair tends to be dry and prone to breakage, so it’s important to use products that nourish and hydrate your hair. Here are some steps to help you take care of your wavy frizzy hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Look for a shampoo that’s designed for wavy or curly hair. These shampoos are usually sulfate-free and won’t strip your hair of its natural oils. Avoid shampoos that contain harsh ingredients like sulfates, alcohol, or fragrances, as these can dry out your hair and make it even more frizzy.

2. Condition regularly

Conditioning is essential for wavy frizzy hair, as it helps to hydrate and nourish your locks. Look for a conditioner that’s designed for wavy or curly hair, and leave it in for a few minutes to help it penetrate your hair shaft. You can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture.

3. Don’t over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it even more prone to frizz. Try to wash your hair every other day, or even less frequently if possible. If your hair gets oily in between washes, you can use a dry shampoo to absorb the excess oil.

4. Use a wide-tooth comb

Wavy frizzy hair is prone to tangles, so it’s important to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your locks. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull or tug on your hair.

5. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it even more frizzy. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and if you do use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step-by-Step Hair Care Routine

Here’s a step-by-step hair care routine for wavy frizzy hair:

1. Shampoo your hair

Use a gentle shampoo that’s designed for wavy or curly hair, and massage it into your scalp to remove dirt and oil.

2. Condition your hair

Apply conditioner to the ends of your hair and work it up to the roots. Leave it in for a few minutes to allow it to penetrate your hair shaft.

3. Detangle your hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

4. Rinse your hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water to help seal your hair cuticles and reduce frizz.

5. Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair to help hydrate and nourish your locks.

6. Let your hair air dry

Avoid using heat styling tools and let your hair air dry instead. If you need to speed up the drying process, you can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer.

7. Style your hair

Once your hair is dry, you can style it using a light hold styling product like a mousse or curl cream. Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can create frizz.

Tips for Managing Wavy Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing wavy frizzy hair:

1. Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Silk pillowcases are gentler on your hair and can help reduce frizz and breakage.

2. Use a satin hair cap

If you sleep with your hair in a bun or braids, use a satin hair cap to protect your hair and prevent frizz.

3. Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry

Brushing your hair when it’s dry can create frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

4. Use a hair mask once a week

Using a hair mask once a week can help hydrate and nourish your hair, leaving it soft and shiny.

FAQs About Wavy Frizzy Hair

Q: What causes wavy frizzy hair?

A: Wavy frizzy hair is often caused by genetics, but it can also be caused by environmental factors like humidity, heat styling, or chemical treatments.

Q: Can I straighten my wavy frizzy hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your wavy frizzy hair using a flat iron, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using the highest heat setting to minimize damage.

Q: How can I prevent frizz in my wavy hair?

A: You can prevent frizz in your wavy hair by using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and using a leave-in conditioner or serum to help hydrate your hair.

Q: What’s the best haircut for wavy frizzy hair?

A: The best haircut for wavy frizzy hair is one that’s layered and textured, as this can help reduce bulk and make your hair more manageable.

In conclusion, taking care of wavy frizzy hair requires a gentle hair care routine and the right products. By following the tips and tricks in this article, you can keep your locks looking their best and minimize frizz and breakage.
