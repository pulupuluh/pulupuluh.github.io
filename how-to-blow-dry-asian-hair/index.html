How To Blow Dry Asian Hair

Written by Robby Aug 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

In this article, we'll be discussing how to blow dry Asian hair. As we know, Asian hair can be challenging to manage and style, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a beautiful blowout. Read on for step-by-step instructions, tips, and FAQs on how to blow dry Asian hair.

Description

Blow drying your hair is an essential step in your hair care routine. It helps to style your hair, add volume, and remove excess moisture. However, blowing drying Asian hair can be tricky as it tends to be thicker, coarser, and more resistant to heat styling. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a smooth, frizz-free, and bouncy blowout.

How to Blow Dry Asian Hair

Follow these steps to blow dry your Asian hair:

Step 1: Prep your hair

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner suitable for your hair type. Towel-dry your hair and apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the blow dryer. Comb your hair to detangle and divide it into sections.

Step 2: Choose the right blow dryer

Invest in a high-quality blow dryer with different heat and speed settings. Look for a dryer with a diffuser attachment, which can help to evenly distribute heat and reduce frizz.

Step 3: Start blow drying

Start by drying the roots of your hair first, using a low-heat and high-speed setting. Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots and direct the airflow downwards. Once the roots are dry, move on to the mid-lengths and ends of your hair, using a medium-heat and medium-speed setting. Use the round brush to smooth out your hair and add volume.

Step 4: Finish with a cool shot

Once you've dried all sections of your hair, switch to the cool shot setting and blast your hair with cold air. This will help to set the style and add shine to your hair.

Tips for Blow Drying Asian Hair

Here are some tips for blow drying Asian hair:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the blow dryer.
  • Divide your hair into sections to make the blow drying process more manageable.
  • Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots and add volume.
  • Blow dry your hair in a downward direction to prevent frizz.
  • Finish with a cool shot to set the style and add shine.

FAQs about Blow Drying Asian Hair

Q: How often should I blow dry my Asian hair?

A: It's best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: Can I blow dry my Asian hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to towel-dry your hair first and apply a heat protectant spray before blow drying.

Q: What's the best way to avoid frizz when blow drying my Asian hair?

A: Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer and blow dry your hair in a downward direction.

Hair Care for Asian Hair

Aside from blow drying your hair, here are some tips for caring for your Asian hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair and use a heat protectant spray.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet to prevent breakage.

Blow drying Asian hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a beautiful blowout. Remember to use a heat protectant spray, divide your hair into sections, and use a round brush to add volume. Finish with a cool shot to set the style and add shine. With these tips, you'll be able to blow dry your Asian hair like a pro.
