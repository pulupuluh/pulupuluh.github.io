How To Make Thick Hair Less Heavy A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Mar 01, 2023 · 7 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can look voluminous and luxurious, it can also feel heavy and unmanageable. If you're struggling with your thick hair, you're not alone. In this article, we'll explore how to make thick hair less heavy with easy-to-follow tips and tricks.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Make Thick Hair Less Heavy
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Managing Thick Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Thick Hair

Description

Thick hair is characterized by its large number of hair strands, which can make it feel heavy and difficult to style. However, there are several ways to make thick hair less heavy, including using the right products, getting regular haircuts, and avoiding certain styling techniques.

How to Make Thick Hair Less Heavy

Here are some ways to make thick hair less heavy:

Cut Your Hair

Getting regular haircuts is the best way to make thick hair less heavy. When you get your hair cut, your stylist will remove some of the weight from your hair, making it easier to manage. Be sure to ask for layers or thinning to remove some of the bulk from your hair.

Use the Right Products

Using the right products is key when it comes to managing thick hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for thick hair, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners. Avoid heavy styling products, such as waxes and pomades, which can weigh down your hair.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can make your hair feel even heavier. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature at a low to medium heat.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to make thick hair less heavy:

  1. Get regular haircuts to remove some of the weight from your hair.
  2. Use volumizing shampoos and conditioners to add volume to your hair.
  3. Avoid heavy styling products, such as waxes and pomades.
  4. Avoid heat styling tools, or use them sparingly.
  5. Consider getting a keratin treatment to help smooth and soften your hair.

Tips for Managing Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing thick hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently detangle your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles, such as ponytails and braids, which can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Consider using a hair oil or serum to add shine and smoothness to your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can thinning shears help make thick hair less heavy?

A: Yes, thinning shears can help remove some of the weight from your hair. However, be sure to have a professional stylist use them, as using them incorrectly can lead to uneven or choppy hair.

Q: Can using too much conditioner make thick hair feel heavy?

A: Yes, using too much conditioner can weigh down your hair. Be sure to use only a small amount of conditioner and focus it on the ends of your hair.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for thick hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup from your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can dry out your hair and make it feel even heavier.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal clasps, as these can cause breakage.
  • Be gentle when towel-drying your hair, and avoid rubbing it vigorously with a towel.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your thick hair feel less heavy and more manageable. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you.
