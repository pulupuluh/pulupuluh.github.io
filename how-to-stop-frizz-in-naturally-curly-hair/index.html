How To Stop Frizz In Naturally Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby May 20, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizz in your naturally curly hair? Frizzy hair can be a major pain, making it difficult to style and leaving you feeling frustrated. Fortunately, there are several steps you can take to prevent frizz and keep your curls looking their best. In this article, we’ll share some expert tips and tricks for taming frizz in naturally curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Frizz in Naturally Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Stop Frizz in Naturally Curly Hair

The first step in preventing frizz is to understand what causes it. Frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of your hair is raised, allowing moisture to enter and causing the hair to swell. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, dryness, and damage from heat styling or chemical treatments.

To prevent frizz, you’ll need to take steps to keep your hair moisturized, protect it from damage, and style it in a way that minimizes frizz. Here are some tips to get you started:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

One of the best ways to prevent frizz is to keep your hair well-moisturized. Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair that contains moisturizing ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil. These products will help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent dryness, which can lead to frizz.

2. Don’t Over-Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. Try to limit washing your hair to once or twice a week, and use a dry shampoo in between washes if necessary.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can be a great way to add extra moisture to your hair and prevent frizz. Look for a lightweight formula that won’t weigh your curls down, and apply it to damp hair before styling.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can be damaging to curly hair, causing it to become dry and frizzy. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and let your hair air-dry instead of using a blow dryer.

5. Use a Diffuser Attachment

If you do need to use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the air evenly and prevent frizz. Hold the diffuser close to your hair and use a low or medium heat setting.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to preventing frizz in naturally curly hair:

1. Start with Clean, Moisturized Hair

Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair. Gently towel dry your hair and apply a leave-in conditioner.

2. Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product like a curl cream or gel to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to comb through your hair and define your curls.

3. Avoid Touching Your Hair While It Dries

Touching your hair while it dries can cause frizz. Once you’ve applied your styling product, let your hair air-dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. Don’t touch your hair until it’s completely dry.

4. Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, you can style it as desired. Avoid using heat styling tools if possible, and use a lightweight hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few more tips and tricks for preventing frizz in naturally curly hair:

  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel, as these materials are gentler on curly hair.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I use oil to prevent frizz in curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use a lightweight oil like argan or jojoba oil to help prevent frizz. Apply a small amount to your hair after styling to add shine and control frizz.

Q: How often should I trim my curly hair to prevent frizz?

A: It’s recommended to get a trim every 8-12 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage, which can lead to frizz.
