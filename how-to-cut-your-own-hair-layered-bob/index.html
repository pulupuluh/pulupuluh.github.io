How To Cut Your Own Hair Layered Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the salon for a layered bob haircut? Do you want to learn how to cut your own hair layered bob? Look no further! In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to achieving a layered bob haircut from the comfort of your own home.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Layered Bob
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Description

A layered bob is a versatile and trendy hairstyle that can be customized to suit any face shape and hair type. It is characterized by shorter layers at the back and longer layers at the front, creating a flattering and face-framing effect. Cutting your own hair layered bob can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it is achievable.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Layered Bob

The first step in cutting your own hair layered bob is to gather the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. It is also recommended to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp and easy to cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and make it easier to cut.
  2. Divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Clip each section up and out of the way.
  3. Starting with the back section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to cut the ends of the hair at a slight angle, creating a graduated effect. Repeat this step for all sections of the back of your hair.
  4. Unclip one of the front sections of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut the ends at the same angle as the back section. Repeat this step for the other front section of your hair.
  5. Release the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Take small sections and repeat the same cutting technique as before, making sure to blend the layers into the rest of your hair.
  6. Check your work in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more, but it is difficult to fix a haircut that is too short.
  • Invest in a pair of high-quality scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can cause split ends and make it difficult to achieve a clean cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.
  • Ask a friend or family member for help if you are unsure about any steps.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair layered bob if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can! However, it is recommended to cut curly hair when it is dry, as wet curly hair can appear longer than it actually is.

How often should I cut my own hair layered bob?

It is recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, it is best to leave it and have a professional stylist fix it. Trying to fix it yourself can often make the situation worse.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair layered bob, it is important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do. Trimming the ends of your hair every 6-8 weeks will also help maintain the shape of your haircut.

Cutting your own hair layered bob can be a fun and cost-effective way to switch up your hairstyle. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and always ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
