How Long Should Straight Hair Be For Dreads A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 09, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Dreadlocks, or simply dreads, are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are often associated with the Rastafari movement, but they have also been worn by many cultures around the world. Nowadays, dreads are not only a fashion statement but also a way to express one's cultural and spiritual identity. If you have straight hair and are considering getting dreads, you may wonder how long your hair should be before you start. In this article, we will answer this question and provide you with some tips and tricks for maintaining healthy dreads.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Dreads on Straight Hair
  • How Long Should Straight Hair Be for Dreads?
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Dreads
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Dreads

How to Get Dreads on Straight Hair

Before we dive into how long your hair should be for dreads, let's briefly go over how to get them on straight hair. The process of creating dreads involves knotting and matting your hair until it forms into locks. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean and dry hair, as this will make the process easier and less painful. You can use a residue-free shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils from your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using a comb or your fingers. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be. Generally, smaller sections will result in thinner dreads, while larger sections will give you thicker ones.

Step 3: Backcomb the Sections

Take one section of hair and backcomb it using a dread comb or a fine-toothed comb. This involves combing the hair towards the scalp, creating a knotted and tangled mass of hair. Repeat this process for each section of hair until you have achieved the desired number of dreads.

Step 4: Maintain Your Dreads

Once you have your dreads, you will need to maintain them to keep them looking neat and tidy. This involves palm rolling, which is rolling each dread between your palms in a circular motion to tighten them up. You can also use a residue-free locking gel or wax to help the dreads stay in place.

How Long Should Straight Hair Be for Dreads?

Now that you know how to get dreads on straight hair, let's answer the question of how long your hair should be before you start. The short answer is that your hair should be at least 6 inches long to form dreads. However, the ideal length for dreads is around 10-12 inches. This is because longer hair is easier to knot and mat, resulting in stronger and more durable dreads.

Keep in mind that the length of your hair will also affect the thickness of your dreads. If your hair is shorter, your dreads will be thinner, while longer hair will result in thicker dreads. Additionally, the texture of your hair will also play a role in how your dreads turn out. Curly and kinky hair will form dreads more easily than straight hair, but with some patience and perseverance, anyone can achieve the dread look.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Dreads

Now that you have your dreads, it's important to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo to remove buildup and prevent mold and mildew.
  • Avoid using conditioner or any other heavy products that can weigh down your dreads or cause buildup.
  • Keep your dreads dry, as wet hair can lead to mold and mildew growth.
  • Avoid using a hairdryer, as the heat can damage your dreads and cause them to unravel.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your dreads from getting tangled while you sleep.

FAQ

Q: Can I get dreads if my hair is naturally straight?

A: Yes, you can. However, it may take longer for your hair to form dreads compared to someone with curly or kinky hair.

Q: Can I get dreads if my hair is chemically relaxed?

A: Yes, you can. However, you should wait at least six months after getting a relaxer before attempting to dread your hair. This will give your hair enough time to recover from the chemical treatment.

Q: Do I need to cut off my hair to get dreads?

A: No, you don't. You can start with any length of hair, as long as it's at least 6 inches long.

Hair Care for Dreads

Dreadlocks require a different type of hair care compared to other hairstyles. Here are some tips:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo specifically designed for dreads.
  • Do not comb or brush your dreads, as this can cause them to unravel.
  • Use a locking gel or wax to help your dreads stay in place.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair, as this can lead to mold and mildew growth.
  • Get regular maintenance from a professional dread stylist to keep your dreads looking neat and healthy.

By following these tips and taking good care of your dreads, you can enjoy this unique and stylish hairstyle for years to come.
