How To Cut Thick Straight Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 21, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of shelling out big bucks at the salon every time you need a haircut? Cutting your own hair at home can save you time and money, but it can be intimidating if you've never done it before. If you have thick, straight hair, don't worry – we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own hair at home, step by step.

How to Cut Thick Straight Hair at Home

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can achieve salon-quality results at home. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • Clips to separate your hair into sections
  • A handheld mirror to check the back of your head

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and free of any styling products. Blow dry your hair straight, using a round brush if necessary to smooth out any kinks or bumps.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using clips, divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Start with one of the back sections.

Step 3: Begin cutting

Take a small section of hair from the back, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across, making sure to keep the scissors perpendicular to the hair. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you've cut all of the hair in that section.

Step 4: Move on to the sides

Once you've finished the back sections, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair from the front of one of the side sections, and hold it straight up. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the length you want your hair to be and angling the scissors downward. This will create a layered effect.

Step 5: Check your work

After you've finished cutting your hair, use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure your hair is even.

Tips for Cutting Thick Straight Hair

Cutting thick straight hair can be challenging, but these tips can help make the process easier:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure evenness
  • Don't cut your hair too short – you can always go back and trim more if necessary
  • If you're unsure about how much hair to cut, start with a small amount and work your way up

FAQs

Can you cut thick straight hair with regular scissors?

You can, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors are often dull and can create uneven, jagged cuts. Invest in a pair of sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair.

How often should you cut thick straight hair?

It depends on your hair and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and free of split ends.

Hair Care for Thick Straight Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, thick straight hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle wet hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can confidently cut your own thick straight hair at home. Remember to take your time and be patient – practice makes perfect!
