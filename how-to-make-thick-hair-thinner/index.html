How To Make Thick Hair Thinner A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Jul 22, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of dealing with unruly thick hair? While having thick hair can be a blessing, it can also be a curse, especially when it comes to styling and managing. In this comprehensive guide, we will show you how to make thick hair thinner so that you can achieve the hairstyle of your dreams. From tips to FAQs, we've got you covered.

What Causes Thick Hair?

Before we dive into how to make thick hair thinner, let's first understand what causes thick hair. Genetics play a significant role in determining hair thickness. If your parents or grandparents have thick hair, there's a good chance you will too. Additionally, hormones can also affect hair thickness as well as nutrition and hair care habits.

How to Make Thick Hair Thinner

1. Get a Haircut

The quickest way to make thick hair thinner is by getting a haircut. Ask your hairstylist for layers, which will help thin out your hair and give it more movement. Avoid blunt cuts or heavy layers, as these can make your hair look even thicker.

2. Use Thinners or Thinning Scissors

If you want to thin out your hair without losing too much length, you can use thinning scissors or thinning shears. These special scissors have teeth that help remove some of the bulk from your hair without cutting off too much length. You can also use thinners, which are similar to scissors but have a razor blade that cuts the hair at an angle.

3. Try Chemical Straightening

If you have curly or wavy hair, chemical straightening can help make your hair appear thinner. This process involves applying chemicals to your hair to break down the protein bonds that give your hair its shape. While this can be an effective way to make your hair thinner, it can also be damaging to your hair, so it's essential to consult with a professional hairstylist before attempting it.

4. Use Hair Products

Using the right hair products can also help make your hair appear thinner. Look for products that are designed specifically for thick hair, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners. You can also use styling products like mousse or gel to create the illusion of thinner hair.

Tips for Managing Thick Hair

While these techniques can help make your thick hair appear thinner, it's also essential to learn how to manage your hair properly. Here are some tips to help you:

1. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Thick hair can be very difficult to comb, especially when it's wet. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair gently, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it look even thicker. Try to avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners whenever possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

3. Opt for Updos

Updos are a great way to manage thick hair, especially during the summer months. Try braids or buns to keep your hair out of your face and reduce the appearance of thickness.

FAQs About Making Thick Hair Thinner

1. Is it possible to make thick hair permanently thinner?

While you can make your hair appear thinner using the techniques we've outlined, there's no way to permanently change the thickness of your hair. It's important to learn how to manage your hair properly instead of trying to change it.

2. Will thinning my hair damage it?

Using thinning scissors or thinners can be damaging to your hair if not done properly. It's essential to consult with a professional hairstylist before attempting to thin your hair on your own.

3. Can I use regular hair products on thick hair?

While you can use regular hair products on thick hair, it's best to use products specifically designed for thick hair. These products have formulas that can help manage and style thick hair more effectively.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Proper hair care is essential for managing thick hair. Here are some tips:

1. Wash Your Hair Less Frequently

Thick hair doesn't need to be washed as often as other hair types. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it look dull and lifeless. Try washing your hair every two to three days instead.

2. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Deep conditioning treatments can help keep your hair healthy and moisturized, reducing the appearance of thickness. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin or argan oil, which can help strengthen and nourish your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can damage your hair and make it look even thicker. Wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray to shield your hair from harmful rays.

Now that you know how to make thick hair thinner, you can rock any hairstyle with confidence. Remember to take good care of your hair and consult with a professional hairstylist if you're unsure about any of these techniques. With a little patience and the right tools, you can achieve the hair of your dreams.
