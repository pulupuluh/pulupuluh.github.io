How Long Does Fade Last A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Mar 22, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're someone who loves to experiment with your hair, you might have come across the term "fade" hairstyle. Fades have been in trend for quite some time now and are still going strong. But, have you ever wondered how long does fade last? If yes, then you're in the right place. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about fade hairstyles, including how long they last, how to maintain them, and much more.

What is a Fade Hairstyle?

A fade hairstyle is a popular hair trend that involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back and gradually fading it into longer hair on the top. Fades can be high, low, or mid, depending on the length of the hair left on the sides and back. This hairstyle is versatile and can be customized according to your preferences and face shape.

How Long Does Fade Last?

The duration of a fade hairstyle depends on various factors, such as the type of fade, hair texture, and maintenance. On average, a fade hairstyle lasts between two to four weeks. However, if you take proper care of your hair and avoid exposing it to harsh chemicals or heat, you can extend the life of your fade by a few more weeks.

Factors Affecting Fade Duration

As mentioned earlier, several factors can impact how long a fade lasts. Here are some of the most common factors:

  • Hair Texture: People with coarse or curly hair tend to have longer-lasting fades than those with straight hair.
  • Length of Hair: The longer the hair on the top, the more prominent the fade, and the shorter its lifespan.
  • Type of Fade: High fades tend to last longer than low fades.
  • Maintenance: Proper maintenance, including regular washing, conditioning, and trimming, can significantly extend the life of your fade.

How to Maintain a Fade Hairstyle?

Proper maintenance is crucial for any hairstyle, and fade is no exception. Here are some tips to help you maintain your fade:

  • Wash Regularly: Regular washing helps keep your hair and scalp clean and healthy. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Trim Regularly: Trimming your hair every two to four weeks can help maintain the shape and length of your fade.
  • Avoid Heat: Excessive heat styling can damage your hair and shorten the life of your fade. Try to avoid blow-drying, straightening, or curling your hair as much as possible.
  • Protect from Sun: Excessive exposure to the sun's UV rays can cause hair damage and fade color. Wear a hat or use a hair protectant spray when you're out in the sun.

Tips for Longer-Lasting Fade

Here are some additional tips to help you extend the life of your fade:

  • Avoid Harsh Chemicals: Chemical treatments such as coloring, perming, or relaxing can weaken your hair and cause breakage. Avoid these treatments if possible.
  • Use Quality Hair Products: Use high-quality hair products that are specifically designed for your hair type and texture. Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol.
  • Don't Overwash: Overwashing can strip your hair of natural oils and cause dryness and breakage. Wash your hair every two to three days, or as needed.

Common Questions about Fade

Q. Can I do a fade hairstyle at home?

A. Yes, you can do a fade hairstyle at home, but it's recommended to visit a professional hairstylist for best results.

Q. Can I style my hair differently with a fade?

A. Yes, you can style your hair differently with a fade. Fades are versatile and can be customized to suit your preferences and face shape.

Q. How often should I trim my hair with a fade?

A. It's recommended to trim your hair every two to four weeks to maintain the shape and length of your fade.

Hair Care Tips for Fade Hairstyle

To keep your fade looking fresh and stylish, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a Quality Shampoo and Conditioner: Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for your hair type and texture. This will help keep your hair clean, healthy, and free from dandruff and other scalp conditions.
  • Avoid Harsh Chemicals: Avoid using harsh chemicals such as bleach, hair dye, and relaxer, as they can damage your hair and shorten the life of your fade.
  • Protect Your Hair from Heat: Excessive heat can damage your hair and make it prone to breakage. Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers as much as possible.
  • Eat a Balanced Diet: Eating a balanced diet that's rich in vitamins, minerals, and protein can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss.

In conclusion, fade hairstyles are stylish, versatile, and can be customized to suit your preferences and face shape. They typically last between two to four weeks, but with proper maintenance, you can extend their lifespan by a few more weeks. Follow the tips and hair care advice mentioned in this article to keep your fade looking fresh and stylish for longer.
