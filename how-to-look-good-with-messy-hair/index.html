How To Look Good With Messy Hair

Written by Bardi May 04, 2023 · 9 min read

If you're tired of spending hours trying to perfect your hair, it's time to embrace the messy hair trend. Not only is it effortless, but it's also incredibly stylish. In this article, we'll show you how to look good with messy hair in relaxed English.

Table of Contents

  • How to Look Good with Messy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Messy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Messy Hair

How to Look Good with Messy Hair

Messy hair doesn't mean you have to look unkempt. With the right techniques and styling products, you can achieve a chic and effortless look. Here's how:

1. Embrace Your Natural Texture

The key to achieving messy hair is to embrace your natural texture. Whether you have straight, wavy, or curly hair, work with what you have. Use a texturizing spray to enhance your natural texture and create a messy look.

2. Use the Right Styling Products

When it comes to styling messy hair, less is more. Use a lightweight styling product like a sea salt spray or a dry shampoo to achieve a tousled look. Avoid heavy products like gels and mousses, as they can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.

3. Play with Layers

Layers can add depth and dimension to your hair, making it look effortless and messy. Ask your hairstylist for choppy layers that can be styled in different ways. Use a texturizing spray to separate the layers and create a tousled look.

4. Try a Messy Bun or Ponytail

A messy bun or ponytail is a great way to achieve a messy yet chic look. Pull your hair back into a loose bun or ponytail, leaving a few strands out to frame your face. Use bobby pins to secure the hairstyle in place, and use a texturizing spray to add volume and texture.

5. Experiment with Braids

Braids are a great way to add texture and dimension to your hair. Try a messy fishtail braid or a messy French braid for a bohemian-inspired look. Use a texturizing spray to create a tousled look and pull out a few strands to frame your face.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving messy hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help add volume and texture to your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Texturizing Spray

Spray a texturizing spray onto your hair, focusing on the roots and mid-lengths. Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create a messy look.

Step 3: Use a Lightweight Styling Product

Use a lightweight styling product like a sea salt spray or a dry shampoo to add texture and volume to your hair. Avoid heavy products like gels and mousses, as they can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.

Step 4: Play with Layers

If you have layers in your hair, use a texturizing spray to separate them and create a tousled look.

Step 5: Try a Messy Bun or Ponytail

Pull your hair back into a loose bun or ponytail, leaving a few strands out to frame your face. Use bobby pins to secure the hairstyle in place, and use a texturizing spray to add volume and texture.

Tips for Maintaining Messy Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining messy hair:

1. Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo is a lifesaver for messy hair. It can absorb excess oil and add volume to your hair, making it look fresh and tousled.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it look dry and frizzy. Try to wash your hair every other day or use a dry shampoo to refresh it in between washes.

3. Sleep in a Messy Bun

Sleeping in a messy bun can help maintain your tousled look. Simply pull your hair back into a loose bun before going to bed, and use a scrunchie to secure it in place.

FAQs

Q: Can messy hair be professional?

A: Yes, messy hair can be professional if styled correctly. Opt for a messy bun or ponytail instead of leaving your hair down, and avoid using heavy styling products.

Q: Can you achieve messy hair with short hair?

A: Yes, you can achieve messy hair with short hair. Use a texturizing spray to add volume and texture, and play with layers to create a tousled look.

Hair Care for Messy Hair

Messy hair requires minimal maintenance, but it's still important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Use a Heat Protectant

If you use heat styling tools like a flat iron or a curling iron, use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and shiny. Visit your hairstylist every 6-8 weeks for a trim.

3. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair. This will keep it looking healthy and shiny.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a chic and effortless look with messy hair. Remember to embrace your natural texture and use lightweight styling products to create a tousled look. Don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles and have fun with your hair!
