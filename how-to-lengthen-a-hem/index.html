How To Lengthen A Hem A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 30, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you've ever found a perfect dress or pair of pants that fit you perfectly except for the fact that they're just a bit too short, don't worry! You don't have to give up on them just yet. With a few simple steps, you can easily lengthen the hem yourself and save yourself the trouble of finding a new piece of clothing to fit you. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of lengthening a hem and provide you with some helpful tips along the way.

How to Lengthen a Hem

The process of lengthening a hem can vary depending on the type of garment you're working with, but here are some general steps that you can follow:

Step 1: Choose Your Fabric

The first thing you'll want to do is choose a fabric that matches the original garment as closely as possible. This will help the hem look more natural and seamless once it's been lengthened. You can either purchase fabric that matches the original garment or use fabric from another piece of clothing that you no longer wear.

Step 2: Pre-Wash the Fabric

Before you start working with the fabric, be sure to pre-wash it to prevent any shrinkage or color bleeding in the future. Follow the care instructions on the fabric label to ensure that you're washing it properly.

Step 3: Cut the Fabric

Using a pair of fabric scissors, cut the fabric to the desired length for the hem. Be sure to add an extra inch or two to allow for hemming and folding.

Step 4: Pin the Fabric

Pin the fabric to the inside of the garment, making sure that it's lined up evenly with the original hem. Use as many pins as you need to keep the fabric in place.

Step 5: Sew the Fabric

Using a sewing machine or needle and thread, sew the fabric in place along the bottom of the garment. Be sure to sew as close to the edge of the fabric as possible to create a clean, even hem.

Step 6: Iron the Hem

Once you've finished sewing the hem, iron it to help it lay flat and look more natural. Be sure to use the appropriate heat setting for the fabric you're working with.

Tips for Lengthening a Hem

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when lengthening a hem:

  • Choose a fabric that matches the original garment as closely as possible
  • Be sure to pre-wash the fabric to prevent shrinkage or color bleeding
  • Use as many pins as you need to keep the fabric in place while sewing
  • Sew as close to the edge of the fabric as possible to create a clean, even hem
  • Iron the hem to help it lay flat and look more natural

FAQ

Can I lengthen a hem on any type of garment?

While you can technically lengthen a hem on any type of garment, some may be more difficult or time-consuming than others. For example, lengthening a hem on a pair of pants may require more work than lengthening a hem on a simple skirt.

Do I need to have sewing experience to lengthen a hem?

While some sewing experience can be helpful, it's not necessary to lengthen a hem. As long as you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully, you should be able to lengthen a hem successfully.

Can I lengthen a hem on a garment that has a pattern or design?

Yes, you can lengthen a hem on a garment that has a pattern or design, but you'll need to be careful to match up the pattern as closely as possible when sewing the new fabric in place.

Hair Care

While you're working on lengthening your hem, why not take some time to give your hair a little extra care? Try using a deep conditioning treatment or hair mask to help nourish and hydrate your hair. You can also try using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help smooth and protect your hair from damage.

By following these simple steps, you can easily lengthen a hem on any piece of clothing and give it a new lease on life. With a little bit of patience and the right tools, you can save yourself the trouble of finding a new piece of clothing and create a customized fit that's perfect for you.
