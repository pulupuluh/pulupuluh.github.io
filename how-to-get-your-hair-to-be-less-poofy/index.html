How To Get Your Hair To Be Less Poofy Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jun 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have naturally thick, curly or wavy hair, you know the struggle of dealing with poofy hair. It can be frustrating to style and manage, and it can make you feel self-conscious about your appearance. Fortunately, there are ways to get your hair to be less poofy without damaging it or spending hours on styling.

How to

1. Use a good quality conditioner

One of the most important things you can do to reduce poofiness in your hair is to use a good quality conditioner. This will help to moisturize and nourish your hair, making it easier to manage and style. Look for a conditioner that is formulated for your hair type, and use it every time you wash your hair.

2. Avoid using hot tools too often

Another reason why your hair might be poofy is because you are using hot tools too often. This can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz and poofiness. Try to limit your use of hot tools, and when you do use them, make sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can be a great way to reduce poofiness in your hair. Look for a lightweight formula that wonât weigh down your hair, and apply it to damp hair before styling. This will help to moisturize your hair and prevent frizz.

4. Try a hair mask

Using a hair mask once a week can also be a great way to reduce poofiness in your hair. Look for a mask that is formulated for your hair type, and apply it to your hair for 20-30 minutes before washing it out. This will help to nourish and moisturize your hair, making it easier to manage.

5. Use a diffuser

If you have curly or wavy hair, using a diffuser can be a great way to reduce poofiness. A diffuser will help to distribute the heat from your hair dryer evenly, which can help to define your curls and reduce frizz.

Step by Step

1. Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any product buildup.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair, focusing on the ends. This will help to moisturize your hair and reduce frizz.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up. This will help to prevent breakage and reduce poofiness.

4. Use a diffuser

Attach a diffuser to your hair dryer and use it to dry your hair. Start by using a low heat setting, and then gradually increase the heat as needed. Use your hands to scrunch your hair as you dry it, which will help to define your curls and reduce frizz.

5. Finish with a serum

Finish by applying a serum to your hair to add shine and reduce frizz. Use a small amount, and apply it to the ends of your hair.

Tips

  • Try to avoid brushing your hair too often, as this can lead to frizz and poofiness.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Consider getting a haircut that is designed to reduce poofiness, such as a layered haircut or a long bob.

FAQ

What causes poofy hair?

There are several factors that can contribute to poofy hair, including humidity, damaged hair, and using hot tools too often.

Can I reduce poofiness without using products?

While using products can be helpful, there are other things you can do to reduce poofiness, such as using a wide-tooth comb, avoiding brushing your hair too often, and avoiding hot tools.

Can I use a hair mask if I have fine hair?

Yes, you can use a hair mask even if you have fine hair. Just make sure to choose a mask that is formulated for your hair type, and donât leave it on for too long.

Hair Care

Remember, taking good care of your hair is the best way to keep it healthy and manageable. Make sure to use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and try to avoid using hot tools too often. By following these tips and tricks, you can get your hair to be less poofy and more manageable.
