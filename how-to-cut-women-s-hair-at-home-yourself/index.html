How To Cut Women s Hair At Home Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to trim your locks or give yourself a completely new hairstyle, you might be wondering how to cut women's hair at home yourself. Cutting your own hair can be daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to achieve a professional-looking cut without having to go to a salon. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut your own hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • Materials You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Styling Your Hair
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

Materials You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right materials. Here's what you'll need:

  • Haircutting scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror
  • Towel (to drape over your shoulders)
  • Spray bottle filled with water (optional)

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare your hair and your workspace. Here's what you should do:

  • Wash and dry your hair: Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start cutting it. Wet hair is harder to cut and can lead to uneven results.
  • Choose the right location: Find a well-lit room with a large mirror. Make sure you have enough space to move around and that you're comfortable.
  • Wear the right clothes: Put on a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on. You may also want to wear a cape or towel to protect your clothes.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is an important step in cutting your own hair. It will help you achieve a more even cut and make it easier to work with your hair. Here's how to section your hair:

  1. Start by dividing your hair into four sections: front, back, and two sides.
  2. Clip or tie each section so that it stays in place.
  3. Take one section at a time and divide it into smaller sections, depending on how thick your hair is. The smaller the section, the more precise your cuts will be.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start with the back section: Unclip the back section of your hair and comb it straight down. Then, cut the hair straight across, using your fingers as a guide. You can also use clippers to trim the back of your hair.
  2. Move on to the side sections: Unclip one of the side sections and comb it straight down. Use the back section as a guide and cut the hair at the same length. Repeat on the other side.
  3. Finish with the front section: This is the trickiest part. Comb your front hair straight down and make sure it's even on both sides. Use your fingers to create a diagonal line from your chin to the back of your ear. Cut the hair at an angle, following the line. Repeat on the other side.

Styling Your Hair

After you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. Here are some tips:

  • If you want a blunt cut, blow-dry your hair straight and flat iron it.
  • If you want a layered cut, blow-dry your hair with a round brush to add volume.
  • If you want to add texture, use a texturizing spray or pomade.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up.
  • Use hair clips to keep your hair in place.
  • Don't cut your hair too short. You can always cut more later.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair without scissors?

No, you need scissors to cut your hair. Clippers can be used for trimming, but they're not suitable for cutting long hair.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want it to be. As a general rule, you should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't worry if you make a mistake. Hair grows back, and you can always go to a salon to fix it if needed.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without having to go to a salon. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
