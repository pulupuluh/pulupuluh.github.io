Description

Non-Newtonian fluids are fascinating to experiment with, and they are easy to make. Cornstarch is often used as a thickening agent, but it's not always available or desirable. In this article, we will show you how to make non-Newtonian fluid without cornstarch. This recipe is safe, easy to follow, and can be used for a wide range of activities, such as science projects, art, and sensory play. Let's get started!

How to

Making non-Newtonian fluid without cornstarch is simple. You only need two ingredients:

  • Corn syrup
  • Water

Step by Step

Step 1: Mix Corn Syrup and Water

In a bowl, mix 1 part corn syrup with 1 part water. For example, if you use 1 cup of corn syrup, add 1 cup of water. Stir the mixture until it's well combined.

Step 2: Test the Consistency

If you want a thicker consistency, add more corn syrup. If you want a thinner consistency, add more water. Keep in mind that the amount of corn syrup you add will affect the fluid's viscosity, which is the measure of a fluid's resistance to flow.

Step 3: Experiment with the Fluid

Pour the fluid into a container and experiment with its properties. You can try to poke it, squeeze it, or even walk on it. You'll notice that the fluid behaves differently depending on the amount of force you apply. It will become more solid if you apply more force, and more liquid if you apply less force.

Tips

  • Use a clear container to see the fluid's behavior more clearly.
  • Add food coloring to the mixture to make it more visually appealing.
  • Store the fluid in an airtight container to prevent it from drying out.
  • Use a spoon or other utensil to mix the fluid if you don't want to get your hands dirty.

FAQ

Q: Is non-Newtonian fluid safe?

A: Yes, non-Newtonian fluid is safe. However, it's not recommended to ingest it.

Q: Can I use other types of syrup instead of corn syrup?

A: Yes, you can use other types of syrup, such as maple syrup or agave nectar. Keep in mind that the fluid's properties may differ depending on the type of syrup you use.

Q: Can I reuse the fluid?

A: Yes, you can reuse the fluid as long as it's stored in an airtight container. However, keep in mind that the fluid's properties may change over time.

Hair Care

While making non-Newtonian fluid is fun, it can be messy. If the fluid gets on your hair, don't panic. Here's how to remove it:

  1. Wash your hair with warm water.
  2. Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair.
  3. Comb your hair gently to remove the fluid.
  4. Rinse your hair with warm water.
  5. Repeat if necessary.

Now that you know how to make non-Newtonian fluid without cornstarch, go ahead and try it out. It's a great activity for kids and adults alike, and you're sure to have fun with it. Happy experimenting!
