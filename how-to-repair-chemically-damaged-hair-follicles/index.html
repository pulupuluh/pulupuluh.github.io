How To Repair Chemically Damaged Hair Follicles

Written by Petter Apr 26, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you've been using chemical treatments on your hair for a while, you may have noticed that your hair isn't as healthy as it used to be. Chemical treatments like hair dye, relaxers, and perms can damage the hair follicles and cause breakage, split ends, and hair loss. But don't worry! There are things you can do to repair chemically damaged hair follicles.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Repair Chemically Damaged Hair Follicles
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Chemical treatments can damage the hair follicles by breaking down the proteins in the hair shaft and weakening the hair's natural structure. This can lead to a variety of hair problems, including breakage, split ends, thinning hair, and hair loss. Repairing chemically damaged hair follicles requires a combination of treatments that address the root cause of the damage and help to strengthen and nourish the hair from the inside out.

How to Repair Chemically Damaged Hair Follicles

There are several things you can do to repair chemically damaged hair follicles:

1. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help to repair the hair's natural structure by filling in gaps in the hair shaft and strengthening the hair from the inside out. Look for a protein treatment that contains hydrolyzed protein, which is a form of protein that has been broken down into smaller molecules that can penetrate the hair shaft more easily.

2. Use a Moisturizing Treatment

Moisturizing treatments can help to restore the hair's natural moisture balance and prevent further damage. Look for a moisturizing treatment that contains ingredients like glycerin, honey, or aloe vera, which are all natural humectants that can help to attract moisture to the hair.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can further damage chemically treated hair by stripping away moisture and weakening the hair's natural structure. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and when you do use them, use a heat protectant spray to help minimize damage.

4. Trim Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can make chemically damaged hair look even worse. Aim to trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it looking healthy and prevent further damage.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to repairing chemically damaged hair follicles:

Step 1: Use a Clarifying Shampoo

Start by using a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup from styling products or hard water. This will help to prepare your hair for the treatments you'll be using.

Step 2: Apply a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas that are particularly damaged. Leave the treatment on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse it out thoroughly.

Step 3: Apply a Moisturizing Treatment

Apply a moisturizing treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas that are particularly dry or damaged. Leave the treatment on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse it out thoroughly.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and when you do use them, use a heat protectant spray to help minimize damage.

Step 5: Trim Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for repairing chemically damaged hair follicles:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to help prevent further damage.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip away moisture and make your hair more prone to damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and be gentle when brushing or combing to avoid further damage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

FAQ

How long does it take to repair chemically damaged hair follicles?

The amount of time it takes to repair chemically damaged hair follicles will vary depending on the severity of the damage and the treatments you're using. In general, you should start to see improvements in the condition of your hair within a few weeks of starting a repair regimen.

Can chemically damaged hair be repaired?

Yes, chemically damaged hair can be repaired with the right treatments and care. It's important to address the root cause of the damage and use treatments that help to strengthen and nourish the hair from the inside out.

Hair Care

Maintaining healthy hair is important to prevent damage from happening in the first place. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals that are essential for healthy hair, such as biotin, vitamin D, and iron.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated from the inside out.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip away natural oils and make your hair more prone to damage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dye or bleach, as these can damage the hair follicles and cause breakage and hair loss.

By following these tips and using the right treatments, you can repair chemically damaged hair follicles and restore your hair to its natural beauty.
