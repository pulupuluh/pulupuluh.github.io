How To Prevent Hair Fall During Chemotherapy

Written by Bardi Apr 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dealing with cancer can be tough, but going through chemotherapy can be even tougher. One of the most common side effects of chemotherapy is hair loss. Losing your hair can be distressing and can affect your self-esteem. But, there are things you can do to prevent or reduce hair fall during chemotherapy. Here are some tips that can help.

Table of Content

  1. How to Prevent Hair Fall During Chemotherapy
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips to Prevent Hair Fall During Chemotherapy
  4. FAQs about Hair Fall During Chemotherapy
  5. Hair Care During Chemotherapy

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to prevent hair fall during chemotherapy:

Step 1: Talk to Your Doctor

Before starting chemotherapy, talk to your doctor about the potential side effects, including hair loss. Your doctor can give you advice on how to minimize hair fall.

Step 2: Use a Cold Cap

A cold cap is a scalp cooling system that can reduce hair fall during chemotherapy. The cap is placed on your head before, during, and after chemotherapy. The cold temperature constricts blood vessels in the scalp, reducing the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach the hair follicles.

Step 3: Use Minoxidil

Minoxidil is a medication that can stimulate hair growth. It is available over-the-counter and can be used during chemotherapy to prevent hair fall. However, you should talk to your doctor before using minoxidil to make sure it is safe for you.

Step 4: Be Gentle with Your Hair

During chemotherapy, your hair may become more fragile and prone to breakage. Be gentle with your hair when washing, combing, or styling. Use a wide-toothed comb and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

Tips to Prevent Hair Fall During Chemotherapy

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair fall during chemotherapy:

Tip 1: Get a Short Haircut

Getting a short haircut before starting chemotherapy can reduce the amount of hair that falls out. Short hair is also easier to manage, and you can experiment with wigs, hats, or scarves to cover your head.

Tip 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools during chemotherapy.

Tip 3: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet can help promote hair growth. Include plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables in your diet. You can also take supplements like biotin or vitamin D to support healthy hair growth.

FAQs about Hair Fall During Chemotherapy

Q: Is hair fall during chemotherapy permanent?

A: Hair fall during chemotherapy may be temporary or permanent. In most cases, hair will grow back after chemotherapy is finished. However, some people may experience permanent hair loss.

Q: Can I dye my hair during chemotherapy?

A: It is not recommended to dye your hair during chemotherapy. Chemicals in hair dye can damage your hair and scalp, making it more prone to hair fall.

Q: Can stress cause hair fall during chemotherapy?

A: Stress can contribute to hair fall, but it is not the main cause of hair loss during chemotherapy. Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells, including hair follicles, which can lead to hair loss.

Hair Care During Chemotherapy

It is important to take care of your hair during chemotherapy, even if it is falling out. Here are some tips for hair care during chemotherapy:

Tip 1: Use a Mild Shampoo

Use a mild, fragrance-free shampoo to wash your hair. Avoid harsh chemicals or ingredients that can irritate your scalp.

Tip 2: Moisturize Your Scalp

Chemotherapy can make your scalp dry and itchy. Use a moisturizer specifically designed for the scalp to keep it hydrated.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Chemotherapy can make your scalp more sensitive to the sun. Wear a hat or scarf when you go outside to protect your hair and scalp from the sun's harmful rays.

Remember, losing your hair during chemotherapy is a temporary side effect. With proper care and treatment, your hair will grow back. In the meantime, try to stay positive and focus on your recovery.
