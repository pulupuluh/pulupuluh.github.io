How To Cut Layers In Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Bardi May 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to find the right haircut that flatters your curls. One popular haircut for curly hair is layered hair. Layers add volume, definition, and movement to curly hair. In this article, we will show you how to cut layers in naturally curly hair.

Description

Layered hair is a popular haircut for curly hair because it adds movement, volume, and definition to your curls. Layered haircuts involve cutting your hair at different lengths, with shorter layers at the top and longer layers at the bottom. This creates a natural-looking flow and adds dimension to your curls. There are different types of layered haircuts, such as long layers, choppy layers, and face-framing layers.

How to Cut Layers in Naturally Curly Hair

Here are the steps to cut layers in naturally curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and detangle your hair

Start by washing and detangling your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair. Detangle your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. Do not brush your hair when it is wet, as it can cause breakage and frizz.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Separate the top section from the bottom section. You can also create side sections if you want face-framing layers.

Step 3: Cut the bottom layer

Start with the bottom layer. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it at the desired length. Use the first section as a guide for the rest of the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the middle layer

Move on to the middle layer. Take a small section of hair from the middle layer and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers, and cut it slightly shorter than the bottom layer. Use the bottom layer as a guide for the length.

Step 5: Cut the top layer

Finally, cut the top layer. Take a small section of hair from the top layer and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers, and cut it slightly shorter than the middle layer. Use the middle layer as a guide for the length.

Step 6: Blend the layers

After cutting all the layers, blend them together by combing your hair with your fingers. Check for any uneven sections and trim them if necessary.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in naturally curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Cut your hair when it is dry, as curly hair can shrink when it dries.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Do not cut your hair straight across, as it can create a blunt look.
  • Experiment with different types of layers to find the best style for your curls.

FAQ

Can I cut layers in my curly hair myself?

It is possible to cut layers in your curly hair yourself, but it is recommended to go to a professional hairstylist to avoid any mistakes. If you decide to do it yourself, make sure to follow the steps carefully and use sharp hair cutting scissors.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It is recommended to cut your curly hair every 3-4 months to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Here are some hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to avoid frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage and frizz.

By following these tips, you can keep your naturally curly hair healthy and beautiful.
