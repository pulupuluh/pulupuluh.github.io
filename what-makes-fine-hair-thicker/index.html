What Makes Fine Hair Thicker Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jun 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of having fine, limp hair that just won't hold a style? Do you wish your hair was thicker and fuller? Well, you're not alone. Many people with fine hair struggle to make it look thicker and more voluminous. But don't worry, there are things you can do to give your fine hair a boost. In this article, we'll go over what makes fine hair thicker and share some tips and tricks to help you achieve the fuller hair you've always wanted.

How to Make Fine Hair Thicker

1. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

The right shampoo and conditioner can make all the difference when it comes to making fine hair look thicker. Look for products that are specifically formulated to add volume and body to hair. These products usually contain ingredients like proteins, vitamins, and minerals that help to strengthen and plump up each hair strand.

2. Add Volume with Styling Products

There are a variety of styling products that can help to add volume and thickness to fine hair. Mousse, for example, is a lightweight foam that can be applied to damp hair before blow-drying to create volume and body. Root-lifting sprays and volumizing powders can also be used to add lift and texture to fine hair.

3. Blow-Dry Your Hair Upside Down

One of the easiest ways to add volume to fine hair is to blow-dry it upside down. This technique helps to lift the roots and create a fuller, more voluminous look. Start by flipping your hair over and blow-drying the roots while using your fingers to lift and separate the strands. Once the roots are dry, flip your hair back over and use a brush to smooth out the ends.

4. Avoid Heavy Oils and Conditioners

While it's important to keep your hair moisturized, using heavy oils and conditioners can weigh down fine hair and make it look even thinner. Instead, opt for lightweight, leave-in conditioners and hair oils that won't leave your hair feeling greasy or weighed down.

5. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When it comes to detangling fine hair, it's best to use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. Brushes can pull and break fine hair, while a wide-tooth comb will gently detangle without causing damage. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to tug or pull on the hair.

Tips for Thicker Hair

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

What you eat can have a big impact on the health and thickness of your hair. Eating a diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals is essential for strong, healthy hair. Some of the best foods for hair health include eggs, nuts, leafy greens, and salmon.

2. Get Regular Haircuts

Getting regular haircuts can help to keep your hair looking its best. Even if you're trying to grow your hair out, regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can make hair look thinner.

3. Don't Overdo It with Heat Styling

Using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can cause damage to fine hair, making it look thinner and more fragile. If you do use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible.

4. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Finally, be gentle with your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on it, and don't brush or comb it too aggressively. Treat your hair with care, and it will look healthier and more voluminous.

Avoid heavy oils and conditioners, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle, and be gentle with your hair.
