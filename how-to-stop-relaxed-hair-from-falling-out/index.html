How To Stop Relaxed Hair From Falling Out Tips And Tricks

Relaxed hair can be beautiful and easy to manage, but it can also be prone to breakage and shedding. If you're struggling with hair loss, it's important to take steps to protect your strands and promote healthy growth. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for stopping relaxed hair from falling out.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Stop Relaxed Hair from Falling Out

The first step to preventing hair loss is to identify the cause. There are many factors that can contribute to shedding, including:

  • Over-processing with relaxers or other chemicals
  • Tight braids or weaves
  • Heat damage from styling tools
  • Poor diet or vitamin deficiencies
  • Stress and hormonal changes

Once you've pinpointed the source of your hair loss, you can take steps to address it. Here are some strategies that can help:

1. Reduce Chemical Processing

If you're experiencing breakage from relaxers, consider spacing out your appointments or switching to a milder formula. You can also try alternative methods like stretching or texlaxing, which involve leaving the relaxer on for less time to achieve a looser curl pattern.

2. Avoid Tight Styles

Braids and weaves can be convenient and low-maintenance, but they can also put stress on your hairline and cause breakage. If you must wear these styles, make sure they're not too tight and avoid leaving them in for extended periods.

3. Use Heat Sparingly

Flat irons and curling wands can be damaging to relaxed hair, especially if you use them frequently or at high temperatures. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools and always use a heat protectant spray or serum.

4. Eat a Balanced Diet

Your hair needs vitamins and nutrients to grow strong and healthy. Make sure you're getting plenty of protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and E in your diet. You can also take supplements or use hair growth products that contain these ingredients.

5. Manage Stress

Stress can cause hair loss by disrupting the natural growth cycle. Try to reduce stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques. You can also talk to a therapist or counselor if you're struggling with anxiety or depression.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're not sure where to start with your hair care routine, try following these steps:

1. Wash and Condition Regularly

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. You can also try co-washing (using conditioner instead of shampoo) to reduce stripping.

2. Detangle with Care

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it's wet and coated with conditioner. Start at the ends and work your way up to avoid pulling or breaking your strands.

3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing and detangling, apply a leave-in conditioner or moisturizing cream to help seal in moisture and prevent breakage.

4. Style with Caution

Avoid tight ponytails, braids, or other styles that put tension on your hair. Use soft scrunchies or hair ties and avoid elastics with metal clasps that can snag your strands.

5. Protect Your Hair at Night

Wrap your hair in a satin scarf or bonnet before bed to prevent friction and breakage. You can also try sleeping on a satin pillowcase to reduce damage.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few additional tips to help you keep your relaxed hair healthy:

  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot water or harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.
  • Protect your hair from the sun with a hat or scarf.
  • Avoid over-manipulating your hair (brushing, combing, etc.) to reduce damage.

FAQs

Q: Can I still relax my hair if it's falling out?

A: It's generally not recommended to continue using relaxers if you're experiencing hair loss or breakage. You may want to switch to a milder formula or try alternative methods like stretching or texlaxing.

Q: Will vitamins or supplements help with hair loss?

A: Vitamins and supplements can be helpful for promoting healthy hair growth, but they're not a cure-all for hair loss. It's important to address the underlying cause of your shedding (such as stress or poor diet) as well.

Hair Care Routine

Here's an example hair care routine for relaxed hair:

  • Wash hair once a week with a gentle shampoo
  • Condition hair after washing and detangle with a wide-tooth comb
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or moisturizing cream
  • Style hair with a soft scrunchie or hair tie
  • Wrap hair in a satin scarf or bonnet at night
  • Trim ends every 6-8 weeks

By following these tips and tricks, you can help prevent relaxed hair from falling out and promote healthy growth. Remember to be patient and take care of your hair, and you'll soon see the results you're looking for!
