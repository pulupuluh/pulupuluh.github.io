How To Know If Birth Control Is Causing Hair Loss

Written by Eveline Aug 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Birth control pills are a widely used form of contraception. However, many women experience hair loss as a side effect of using birth control pills. In this article, we will discuss how to know if birth control is causing hair loss and what you can do to prevent it.

Table of Contents

  2. How to Know if Birth Control is Causing Hair Loss

  4. Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

Description of Hair Loss

Hair loss is a common problem that affects millions of people worldwide. It can be caused by various factors such as genetics, stress, poor nutrition, and hormonal changes. Birth control pills contain hormones that can affect the body's hormonal balance, leading to hair loss. Hair loss due to birth control pills can be temporary or permanent, depending on the individual's body chemistry and the type of birth control used.

How to Know if Birth Control is Causing Hair Loss

If you are experiencing hair loss and suspect that it may be due to birth control pills, there are a few signs to look out for:

  • Increased shedding of hair
  • Thinning of hair on the scalp
  • Bald spots on the scalp
  • Changes in hair texture or quality

If you are experiencing any of these symptoms, it is important to speak to your doctor to determine if birth control is the cause of your hair loss.

Step by Step Guide

If you suspect that birth control is causing your hair loss, follow these steps:

  1. Speak to your doctor: Your doctor will be able to determine if birth control is the cause of your hair loss and may recommend a change in birth control or other treatment options.
  2. Stop using birth control: If your doctor determines that birth control is the cause of your hair loss, they may recommend that you stop using birth control altogether.
  3. Consider other forms of contraception: If you stop using birth control, it is important to consider other forms of contraception to prevent unwanted pregnancy.
  4. Consult a hair specialist: If your hair loss is severe, you may want to consult a hair specialist for treatment options such as hair transplants or other hair restoration treatments.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

If you are using birth control and want to prevent hair loss, there are a few tips to follow:

  • Switch to a low-androgen index birth control pill: Birth control pills with a low-androgen index are less likely to cause hair loss.
  • Take hair supplements: Supplements such as biotin, iron, and vitamin D can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss.
  • Eat a healthy diet: Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss.
  • Reduce stress: Stress can cause hair loss. Engage in stress-reducing activities such as yoga or meditation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss and birth control:

Can all birth control pills cause hair loss?

No, not all birth control pills cause hair loss. Pills with a high androgen index are more likely to cause hair loss.

Is hair loss due to birth control permanent?

It depends on the individual's body chemistry and the type of birth control used. Hair loss may be temporary or permanent.

Can hair loss due to birth control be prevented?

Yes, hair loss due to birth control can be prevented by switching to a low-androgen index birth control pill, taking hair supplements, eating a healthy diet, and reducing stress.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to the tips mentioned above, here are some additional hair care tips:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as braids or ponytails.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools such as flat irons or curling irons.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals such as hair dyes or relaxers.

In conclusion, if you suspect that birth control is causing your hair loss, it is important to speak to your doctor to determine the cause and appropriate treatment options. By following the tips mentioned in this article, you can prevent hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
