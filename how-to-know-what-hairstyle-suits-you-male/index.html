How To Know What Hairstyle Suits You Male A Comprehensive Guide

Choosing the right hairstyle can make a huge difference in your overall appearance. It can boost your confidence, enhance your features, and even project a certain image or personality. But with so many options out there, how do you know which one is right for you? In this guide, we'll give you tips and tricks to help you figure out what hairstyle suits you best.

Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the right hairstyle is to determine your face shape. This will help you identify which styles will complement your features and balance out any disproportionate areas. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, look in the mirror and measure the length and width of your face, then compare it to the guidelines for each shape.

Oval Face Shape

An oval face is longer than it is wide, with a rounded hairline and jawline. This face shape is considered the most versatile, as it can pull off a wide range of hairstyles, from short to long.

Round Face Shape

A round face has soft, curved lines with a width and length that are almost equal. The cheeks are typically full, and the jawline is rounded. To elongate the face, opt for hairstyles with volume on top and length on the sides.

Square Face Shape

A square face has a strong, angular jawline and forehead, with a width and length that are roughly equal. Hairstyles that soften the corners of the face, like textured layers or side-swept bangs, are ideal for this shape.

Heart Face Shape

A heart-shaped face has a wide forehead and cheekbones, with a narrow chin. Hairstyles that add volume at the chin, like a chin-length bob or shaggy layers, can help balance out the face.

Diamond Face Shape

A diamond face shape has a narrow forehead and jawline, with wide cheekbones. To create balance, try hairstyles that add volume at the forehead and chin, like a messy quiff or side-parted bangs.

Oblong Face Shape

An oblong face is longer than it is wide, with a straight hairline and jawline. Hairstyles that add width to the sides of the face, like a layered cut or side-swept bangs, can help balance out this shape.

Consider Your Hair Type

In addition to your face shape, your hair type also plays a role in determining which hairstyles will work best for you. There are four main hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each type has its own unique texture, volume, and styling options.

Straight Hair

Straight hair is naturally smooth and shiny, with little to no texture or volume. This hair type can pull off sleek, polished hairstyles, like a classic side-part or slicked-back look.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair has a slight texture and volume, with loose, natural-looking waves. This hair type looks great with layered cuts or messy, textured styles.

Curly Hair

Curly hair has a distinct texture and volume, with tight, defined curls or waves. To enhance this hair type, try a curly undercut or a messy, voluminous style.

Coily Hair

Coily hair has a very tight, springy texture, with small, defined curls or coils. This hair type looks great with short, textured cuts or longer, natural styles.

Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle and daily routine can also influence which hairstyle is right for you. If you have a busy schedule or prefer a low-maintenance look, you may want to opt for a shorter, easier-to-style cut. If you have a more flexible schedule or enjoy experimenting with different styles, you may want to try a longer, more versatile cut.

Reflect on Your Personality

Your hairstyle can also reflect your personality and personal style. If you're outgoing and adventurous, you may want to try a bold, edgy cut or color. If you're more reserved or professional, you may prefer a classic, understated style.

Consider Your Professionalism

If you work in a professional setting, you may need to consider your hairstyle's level of professionalism. Generally, shorter, neater cuts are more appropriate for formal settings, while longer, more relaxed styles are better suited for casual settings.

Look at Current Fashion Trends

Finally, it's always a good idea to stay up-to-date on current fashion trends and hairstyles. This can give you inspiration and ideas for new looks to try. However, it's important to remember that not all trends will work for everyone, so always choose a style that complements your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

Explore Hairstyle Options

Once you've considered all of these factors, it's time to start exploring different hairstyle options. You can do this by browsing online galleries, flipping through magazines, or consulting with a hairstylist. Some popular hairstyle options for men include:

  • Short back and sides
  • Crew cut
  • Buzz cut
  • Undercut
  • Pompadour
  • Quiff
  • Slicked-back
  • Side part
  • Textured crop

Hair Care Tips

Once you've found the right hairstyle, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, like blow-drying or straightening, to prevent damage.
  • Use styling products, like mousse or wax, to enhance your hairstyle and hold it in place.
  • Visit a hairstylist regularly for trims and maintenance.

FAQ

How do I know if a hairstyle will work for me?

Consider your face shape, hair type, lifestyle, personality, professionalism, and current fashion trends when choosing a hairstyle. Consult with a hairstylist for personalized advice.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle, but most people should aim to wash their hair every 2-3 days.

What can I do if my hair is thinning or receding?

There are several options for men with thinning or receding hair, including hair transplants, scalp micropigmentation, and hair loss medications. Consult with a hair specialist for personalized advice.

In conclusion, finding the right hairstyle can take some time and effort, but it's worth it to achieve a look that makes you feel confident and stylish. Consider your face shape, hair type, lifestyle, personality, professionalism, and current fashion trends when choosing a hairstyle, and don't forget to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
