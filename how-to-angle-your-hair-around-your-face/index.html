How to Angle Your Hair Around Your Face: Step-by-Step Guide

When it comes to styling hair, one of the most important things to consider is the shape of your face. Angling your hair around your face can help to accentuate your features and create a flattering look. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to angle your hair around your face.

How to Angle Your Hair Around Your Face

Angling your hair around your face involves creating layers that frame your face and highlight your best features. Here are the basic steps:

1. Determine Your Face Shape

The first step is to determine your face shape. This will help you to figure out which angles will be most flattering for you. There are six basic face shapes:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Rectangle
  • Heart
  • Diamond

Once you know your face shape, you can start to consider which angles will work best for you.

2. Choose Your Angles

Next, you'll need to decide where to angle your hair. This will depend on your face shape and the features you want to highlight. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Round face: Angle hair at the cheekbones to add definition
  • Oval face: Angle hair at the jawline to create a more angular look
  • Square face: Angle hair at the temples to soften the jawline
  • Rectangle face: Angle hair at the chin to create balance
  • Heart face: Angle hair at the chin to soften a pointy chin
  • Diamond face: Angle hair at the cheekbones to soften a wide forehead

3. Determine the Length

The final step is to determine the length of your angled hair. This will depend on your personal style and the look you want to achieve. Some people prefer shorter, more dramatic angles, while others prefer longer, subtler angles.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, here's a step-by-step guide to angling your hair around your face:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Begin by washing and drying your hair. You want your hair to be clean and free of any product buildup.

2. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

3. Angle the Front Sections

Start with the front sections. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers at the angle you want. Use scissors to cut the hair at the angle you're holding it. Continue cutting small sections of hair at the same angle until you've cut all the hair in that section.

4. Angle the Back Sections

Repeat the same process on the back sections, being careful to match the angle of the front sections.

5. Blend the Layers

Once you've angled all four sections, use scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a more natural, cohesive look.

6. Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron, straightener, or other styling tools to create the look you want.

Tips for Angling Your Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when angling your hair around your face:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure a more precise cut
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you're not confident in your cutting skills
  • Experiment with different angles and lengths to find the look that works best for you

FAQs

How often should I angle my hair?

This will depend on your hair type and personal preference. Some people prefer to angle their hair every six to eight weeks, while others can go longer between cuts.

What if I don't like my angled hair?

If you're not happy with your angled hair, don't worry. Hair grows back! You can always get a different cut or style.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are some tips for keeping your angled hair healthy and looking great:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can damage it
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and to prevent split ends
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth and health

Now that you know how to angle your hair around your face, give it a try! With a little practice and experimentation, you can create a flattering, stylish look that highlights your best features.
