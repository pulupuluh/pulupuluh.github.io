My Curly Hair Goes Frizzy When I Brush It Understanding The Problem And Finding Solutions

Written by Oliver Apr 04, 2023 ¬∑ 9 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On the one hand, it has a unique texture and volume that many people envy. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style, especially when it comes to brushing. If you have curly hair, you may have noticed that brushing can cause your hair to go frizzy and lose its shape. This can be frustrating and make it difficult to achieve the look you want. In this article, we will explore the reasons why curly hair goes frizzy when brushed and provide tips and solutions to help you manage your locks.

Why Does Curly Hair Go Frizzy When Brushed?

The main reason why curly hair goes frizzy when brushed is that brushing disrupts the natural curl pattern. Curly hair is naturally more fragile than straight hair, and brushing can cause the hair to break, tangle, and lose its shape. When the hair is brushed, the curls become stretched out, which can lead to frizz and flyaways. Additionally, the friction caused by brushing can create static electricity, which further exacerbates the problem.

How to Brush Curly Hair Without Causing Frizz

If you want to brush your curly hair without causing frizz, there are several steps you can take:

Step 1: Use the Right Brush

When it comes to brushing curly hair, not all brushes are created equal. You should avoid using brushes with hard bristles or metal prongs, as these can damage the hair and cause frizz. Instead, opt for a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles, such as a boar bristle brush. These brushes will help to detangle your hair without disrupting the natural curl pattern.

Step 2: Brush When Wet

Curly hair is more manageable when it is wet, so it's best to brush your hair after you've washed it. Apply a leave-in conditioner or detangling spray to your hair, and use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your curls. Be sure to start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, as this will help to prevent breakage.

Step 3: Don't Overbrush

One of the biggest mistakes people with curly hair make is overbrushing. You should only brush your hair when it's necessary, and never brush your hair when it's dry. Overbrushing can cause your hair to become frizzy and lose its natural curl pattern.

Tips for Managing Frizzy Curly Hair

If you're struggling with frizzy curly hair, there are several things you can do to manage the problem:

Tip 1: Use a Diffuser

A diffuser is a great tool for managing frizzy curly hair. It helps to distribute heat evenly and reduce the amount of frizz caused by blow-drying. To use a diffuser, attach it to your blow dryer and use it on a low heat setting. Place your curls into the diffuser and gently move it around your head, being careful not to disturb the natural curl pattern.

Tip 2: Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and breakage. Silk is a smooth fabric that won't tug on your hair or disrupt the natural curl pattern. Additionally, it helps to prevent static electricity, which can cause frizz.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be very damaging to curly hair, as it can cause the hair to become dry and brittle. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low. Additionally, you should limit your use of heat styling tools to once a week or less.

FAQ About Curly Hair and Frizz

Q: Is it bad to brush curly hair?

A: Brushing curly hair can be damaging if it's done incorrectly. You should avoid using brushes with hard bristles or metal prongs, and never brush your hair when it's dry. Additionally, you should only brush your hair when it's necessary.

Q: Can I use hairspray to control frizz?

A: Hairspray can be helpful for controlling frizz, but it should be used sparingly. Too much hairspray can make your hair look stiff and unnatural. Instead, opt for a lightweight hair oil or serum to help smooth your curls.

How to Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

Tip 1: Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil, which can help to nourish and hydrate your curls.

Tip 2: Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning is essential for keeping curly hair healthy and hydrated. You should deep condition your hair at least once a week, using a product that's designed for curly hair. Apply the conditioner to your hair and leave it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.

Tip 3: Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Harsh chemicals, such as sulfates and parabens, can be very damaging to curly hair. Look for products that are sulfate-free and paraben-free, and avoid using hair dyes or relaxers, which can cause your hair to become dry and brittle.

By following these tips and techniques, you can manage frizz and keep your curly hair looking beautiful and healthy.
