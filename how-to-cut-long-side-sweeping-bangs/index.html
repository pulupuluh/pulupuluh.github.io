How To Cut Long Side Sweeping Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 09, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you thinking of getting long side sweeping bangs? They can add a touch of glamor to your hairstyle and frame your face beautifully. However, cutting your own bangs can be a daunting task, especially if you're not used to handling scissors. But don't worry – with the right tools and technique, you can easily cut your own long side sweeping bangs at home. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut long side sweeping bangs.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Long Side Sweeping Bangs
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Cut Long Side Sweeping Bangs

Before we get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or bobby pins
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Now, let's get started on how to cut long side sweeping bangs.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. Your hair should be completely dry before you start cutting your bangs.
  2. Section your hair. Use a comb to section off the hair that you want to cut into bangs. This should be a triangle section of hair at the front of your head.
  3. Clip the rest of your hair away. Use hair clips or bobby pins to clip the rest of your hair away from your face, leaving only the section of hair you want to cut.
  4. Choose your desired length. Hold the hair in your fingers and decide how long you want your bangs to be. Remember to take into account that your hair will shrink up slightly when it dries.
  5. Cut your hair. Use your scissors to cut your hair in a straight line. Start slowly and cut a little bit at a time, checking the length in the mirror as you go.
  6. Blend your bangs. Once you've cut your hair to the desired length, point cut into the ends of your hair to blend it with the rest of your hair.
  7. Create your side sweep. Use your scissors to cut your bangs at an angle, creating a side sweep. Cut more hair on the side you want your bangs to sweep towards.
  8. Check your work. Use a mirror to check your bangs and make any final adjustments.
  9. Style your hair. Style your hair as desired, using a hair straightener or curling iron to create loose waves or straighten your hair.
  10. Enjoy your new style. You have successfully cut your own long side sweeping bangs!

Tips for Cutting Bangs:

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own bangs:

  • Start slowly and cut a little bit at a time. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut!
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can shrink up and make your bangs look shorter than you intended.
  • Use a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair if you need to.
  • Point cut into the ends of your hair to blend it with the rest of your hair.

FAQs:

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long side sweeping bangs:

  • How do I know if long side sweeping bangs will suit me?
    Long side sweeping bangs can suit most face shapes, but it's always best to consult with a hairstylist. They can give you a professional opinion on what will work best for your face shape and hair type.
  • How often should I trim my bangs?
    Bangs grow quickly, so it's best to trim them every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.
  • Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?
    Yes, but keep in mind that curly hair shrinks up when it dries. Cut your hair a little longer than your desired length to account for this.

Hair Care Tips:

Here are some tips to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools on your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos and conditioners, as they can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Now that you know how to cut long side sweeping bangs, it's time to give it a try! Remember to take it slow and be patient. With practice, you'll be able to cut your own bangs like a pro.
