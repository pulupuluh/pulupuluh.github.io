How To Tie Long Hair For Sports Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Mar 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Sports can be a lot of fun, but if you have long hair, it can be a challenge to keep it out of your face and away from your eyes while you're playing. That's why it's important to know how to tie your hair properly for sports. In this article, we'll show you how to tie long hair for sports and provide you with some helpful tips and tricks to keep your hair in place.

How to Tie Long Hair for Sports

Before we dive into the steps for tying your long hair for sports, it's important to note that the type of sport you're playing may influence how you tie your hair. For example, if you're playing a contact sport like football or rugby, you may need to tie your hair differently than if you're playing a non-contact sport like tennis or volleyball. With that said, here are the general steps for tying long hair for sports:

Step 1: Brush Your Hair

Before you start tying your hair, make sure it's brushed and free of tangles. This will make it easier to manage and prevent knots from forming while you're playing.

Step 2: Choose Your Tie

There are several options for tying long hair for sports, including ponytails, braids, and buns. Choose the tie that feels most comfortable and secure for you.

Step 3: Secure Your Hair

Once you've chosen your tie, gather your hair and secure it with an elastic band. Make sure the band is tight enough to keep your hair in place, but not so tight that it causes discomfort or headaches.

Step 4: Add Extra Security

If you're playing a contact sport or your hair tends to slip out of its tie, you may want to add extra security. One way to do this is to wrap a second elastic band around your ponytail or braid, about an inch or two below the first band. This will help keep your hair in place and prevent it from falling out.

Step 5: Use Hairpins

If you're tying your hair in a bun, you may want to use hairpins to secure it in place. Insert the hairpins through the bun and into your hair, making sure they're hidden from view.

Tips for Tying Long Hair for Sports

Here are some additional tips to help you tie your long hair for sports:

  • Use hair ties that are specifically designed for sports, as they tend to be more durable and less likely to break or slip.
  • Try different tying techniques to find the one that works best for you.
  • Consider using a headband or sweatband to keep your hair out of your face and absorb sweat.
  • Avoid tying your hair too tightly, as this can cause headaches and damage to your hair.
  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup of sweat and dirt that can make it harder to tie.

FAQs

Q: Can I tie my long hair in a bun for sports?

A: Yes, you can tie your long hair in a bun for sports. Just make sure it's secured with hairpins and an elastic band to prevent it from falling out.

Q: Should I use a hair tie or a scrunchie?

A: It's generally better to use a hair tie for sports, as it's more secure and less likely to slip. However, if you prefer using a scrunchie, make sure it's tight enough to keep your hair in place.

Q: How can I prevent my hair from getting tangled while playing sports?

A: Brush your hair before and after playing sports to prevent tangles. You can also use a detangling spray or conditioner to make it easier to manage.

Hair Care for Sports

Playing sports can be tough on your hair, so it's important to take care of it to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for hair care for sports:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to remove sweat and dirt.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and damage.
  • Avoid tying your hair in the same way every day, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to tie your long hair for sports like a pro. Just remember to choose a tie that feels comfortable and secure, and take good care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage.
