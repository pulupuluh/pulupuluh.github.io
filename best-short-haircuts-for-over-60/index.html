Best Short Haircuts For Over 60 A Guide To The Most Flattering Styles

If you're over 60 and looking for a new haircut, you're in luck! There are plenty of short hairstyles that can help you look and feel your best. In this guide, we'll go over some of the best short haircuts for over 60, including how to style them and care for your hair.

How to Choose the Right Short Haircut

When it comes to choosing a short haircut, there are a few things to consider. First, think about your face shape. Some styles may be more flattering on certain face shapes than others. Second, consider your hair type. If you have thick hair, you may want to opt for a layered cut to help thin it out. If you have fine hair, a shorter cut can help add volume. Lastly, think about your personal style and how much time you want to spend styling your hair each day.

The Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic short haircut that can be both chic and low-maintenance. This style works well for those with a more angular face shape, as it can help soften the features. It's also a great option for those with fine hair, as it can help add volume.

To style a pixie cut, simply apply a styling product, such as a wax or pomade, to damp hair and use your fingers to create texture. You can also use a round brush and blow dryer to add volume to the crown of your head.

The Bob

The bob is a versatile short haircut that can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type. For those with a round face shape, a longer bob that hits just below the chin can help elongate the face. Those with a square face shape may want to opt for a shorter bob with layers to soften the features.

To style a bob, blow dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and create a sleek look. You can also add soft waves using a curling iron or wand.

The Shag

The shag is a layered haircut that can add texture and movement to your hair. It's a great option for those with thick or curly hair, as it can help thin it out and make it more manageable.

To style a shag, apply a styling product, such as a mousse or texturizing spray, to damp hair and use your fingers to create texture. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your natural curls.

Layered Haircuts

Layered haircuts are a great option for those with thick hair, as they can help thin it out and make it more manageable. They can also help add volume to fine hair.

To style a layered haircut, blow dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and create a sleek look. You can also add soft waves using a curling iron or wand.

Tips for Styling Short Hair

  • Invest in quality styling products, such as a wax or pomade, to help create texture and hold your style.
  • Use a round brush and blow dryer to add volume and create a sleek look.
  • For a more casual look, use your fingers to create texture and enhance your natural waves.
  • Add soft waves using a curling iron or wand for a more polished look.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.

FAQ

What short haircut is best for a round face?

A longer bob that hits just below the chin can help elongate a round face.

What short haircut is best for thin hair?

A shorter cut can help add volume to thin hair.

What short haircut is best for thick hair?

A layered haircut can help thin out thick hair and make it more manageable.

Hair Care Tips for Short Haircuts

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help prevent damage and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, too often to prevent heat damage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut and prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and elements by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to help nourish and hydrate your hair.

With these tips and some inspiration from the best short haircuts for over 60, you'll be able to find a style that works for you and makes you feel confident and beautiful.
