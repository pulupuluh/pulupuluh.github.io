How To Cut Layers In Shoulder Length Curly Hair

Written by Robby Jul 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your same old boring shoulder-length curly hair? Do you want to add some volume and movement to your hair? Then, you should consider getting layers. Adding layers to your hair can help enhance your natural curls and give it a more textured and defined look. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut layers in shoulder length curly hair.

Description

Shoulder length curly hair can be beautiful, but it can also be challenging to style. Without the right cut, it can look flat and lifeless. Adding layers to your hair can help add volume and movement to your curls. Layers work by removing some of the weight from your hair, allowing your curls to bounce and move more freely. The layers can be cut at different lengths to create a textured look, making it easier to style and manage.

How to Cut Layers in Shoulder Length Curly Hair

If you are planning to cut layers in your shoulder length curly hair, it is best to visit a professional stylist. However, if you prefer to do it yourself, here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help remove any dirt, oil, or styling products that may be on your hair. Also, it will make it easier to work with your hair.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair. This will help create an even base for your layers.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections. You can use hair clips to separate your hair. This will help you cut your hair evenly.

Step 4: Cut the First Layer

Start by cutting the first layer. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use a pair of sharp scissors to cut the hair at an angle. The angle should be about 45 degrees. This will create a soft, textured look.

Step 5: Cut the Remaining Layers

Continue cutting the remaining layers. Make sure that the layers are even and blend well with the previous layer. If you want more layers, repeat steps 4 and 5 until you achieve the desired look.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you have cut your layers, style your hair as desired. You can use a diffuser to enhance your curls or use a curling iron to create loose waves.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in shoulder length curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Start with a small section of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Do not cut your hair when it is wet, as it can lead to uneven cutting.
  • Consult a professional stylist if you are not confident in cutting your hair.

FAQ

Is it better to cut curly hair dry or wet?

It is better to cut curly hair dry. This is because curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, which can result in uneven cutting if you cut it when it is wet.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

You should cut your curly hair every 6 to 8 weeks. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it is essential to take care of it. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as it can cause frizz.

With these tips, you can maintain your layered shoulder length curly hair and keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
