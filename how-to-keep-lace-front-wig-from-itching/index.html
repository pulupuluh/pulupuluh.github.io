How To Keep Lace Front Wig From Itching In 2023

Written by Aprilia Apr 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are wearing lace front wigs, you know how comfortable and versatile they are. However, one of the most common problems with lace front wigs is itching. It can be a frustrating and uncomfortable experience, but it doesn't have to be. In this article, we will discuss how to keep lace front wig from itching in 2023.

  1. How to Keep Lace Front Wig from Itching

How to Keep Lace Front Wig from Itching

The first step in keeping your lace front wig from itching is to ensure that it fits correctly. A loose wig can move around on your head and cause irritation. Make sure to measure your head and choose the right size wig. Additionally, you can adjust the straps and combs inside the wig to customize the fit.

Another way to prevent itching is to wear a wig cap underneath your lace front wig. A wig cap creates a barrier between your scalp and the wig, reducing friction and irritation. Choose a wig cap that is made of a breathable material such as cotton or bamboo to prevent sweating and itching.

You can also use a scalp protector before wearing your lace front wig. A scalp protector is a product that creates a barrier between your scalp and the wig adhesive. It can help to reduce itching and irritation.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to keep lace front wig from itching:

  1. Ensure that the wig fits correctly and adjust the straps and combs inside the wig as needed.
  2. Wear a wig cap made of a breathable material such as cotton or bamboo.
  3. Apply a scalp protector before wearing the wig.
  4. Wash the wig regularly to prevent buildup of oils and dirt.
  5. Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out the scalp and cause itching.
  6. Use a wig brush or comb to prevent tangles and matting, which can cause itching.
  7. Avoid scratching your scalp, as this can cause further irritation.
  8. Take breaks from wearing your wig to allow your scalp to breathe.
  9. Consider using a cooling spray or gel to soothe itching.
  10. If the itching persists, consult with a dermatologist or hair specialist.

Tips for Keeping Your Lace Front Wig from Itching

Here are some additional tips for keeping your lace front wig from itching:

  • Choose a wig made of high-quality, breathable materials.
  • Avoid wearing your wig for extended periods of time.
  • Keep your scalp and wig clean and free of dirt and oils.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your wig.
  • Consider using a wig grip to keep your wig in place without causing irritation.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my lace front wig?

A: It is recommended to use products specifically designed for synthetic or human hair wigs to preserve their quality and prevent damage.

Q: How often should I wash my lace front wig?

A: It depends on how often you wear your wig and how much product you use. Generally, it is recommended to wash your wig every 6-8 wears.

Q: Can I swim or shower with my lace front wig?

A: It is not recommended to wear your wig while swimming or showering, as the water can cause damage to the wig and adhesive.

Hair Care for Lace Front Wigs

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your lace front wig looking and feeling great. Here are some tips:

  • Brush or comb your wig gently using a wide-toothed comb or wig brush.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling wands on synthetic wigs.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand to maintain its shape.
  • Avoid exposing your wig to direct sunlight or heat sources.
  • Use a detangling spray to prevent tangles and matting.

By following these tips and using the right products, you can keep your lace front wig from itching and looking great for years to come.
