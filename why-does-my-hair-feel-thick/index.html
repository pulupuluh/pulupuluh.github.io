Why Does My Hair Feel Thick

Written by Petter Jun 08, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you wondering why your hair feels thick? There could be several reasons for this, including genetics, product buildup, and hair damage. In this article, we will explore the various causes of thick hair and how to manage it.

Description

Thick hair can be a blessing or a curse, depending on how you look at it. While it can give you a full, voluminous look, it can also be difficult to manage. If you have thick hair, you may find that it takes longer to dry, style, and even brush. In some cases, thick hair can also be a sign of an underlying health condition.

How to Determine If Your Hair is Thick

The best way to determine if your hair is thick is to look at the individual strands. If your hair strands are wider than a piece of sewing thread, then you have thick hair. Another way to tell is by how much hair you have on your head. If you have a lot of hair, it is likely thick.

Causes of Thick Hair

Genetics

One of the most common causes of thick hair is genetics. If your parents have thick hair, then it is likely that you will too. This is because hair thickness is determined by the number of hair follicles on your scalp.

Product Buildup

Another reason why your hair may feel thick is because of product buildup. Over time, the use of styling products, such as hairspray and gel, can build up on your hair and scalp. This can cause your hair to feel heavy and thick. To avoid product buildup, try using a clarifying shampoo once a week.

Hair Damage

If you have damaged hair, it may feel thicker than usual. This is because damaged hair has a rough texture and can become tangled easily. To avoid hair damage, be gentle when brushing and styling your hair. Also, avoid using heat styling tools too often.

How to Manage Thick Hair

Use the Right Products

If you have thick hair, it is important to use the right products. Look for products that are designed for thick hair, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners. These products can help to add volume to your hair and make it easier to manage.

Get Regular Haircuts

Getting regular haircuts can also help to manage thick hair. By getting rid of split ends and other damage, your hair will be easier to manage and style. It is recommended to get a haircut every six to eight weeks.

Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can damage your hair and make it feel thicker. Try to avoid using these tools too often. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Tips for Maintaining Thick Hair

Brush Your Hair Regularly

Brushing your hair regularly can help to distribute natural oils and prevent tangles. Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to avoid damaging your hair.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can put stress on your hair and make it feel thicker. Opt for looser hairstyles instead.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and make it feel thicker. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair.

FAQ

Can I Thin Out My Thick Hair?

Yes, you can thin out your thick hair. This can be done by a professional hairstylist using thinning shears or by using a razor comb at home. However, it is important to be careful not to overdo it, as thinning your hair too much can cause damage.

Can Thick Hair Fall Out?

Yes, thick hair can fall out just like any other hair type. Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, such as genetics, stress, and health conditions.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly can help to remove dirt and oil buildup. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for thick hair.

Avoid Overwashing

While it is important to wash your hair regularly, overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it feel thicker. Try to wash your hair every two to three days.

Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to keep your hair soft and manageable. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

Conclusion

Thick hair can be beautiful, but it can also be difficult to manage. By using the right products, getting regular haircuts, and avoiding heat styling tools, you can keep your thick hair healthy and manageable. Remember to be gentle when brushing and styling your hair, and protect it from the sun and other environmental factors.
