How To Cut My Boyfriend s Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Mar 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you looking to save some money on your boyfriend's haircuts? Or maybe you just want to try your hand at cutting hair? Whatever your reason, cutting curly hair can be a bit tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know to give your boyfriend a great curly haircut at home.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping for the Cut
  • Cutting the Hair
  • Tips for a Great Cut
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care after the Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Cape or towel to protect clothing

Prepping for the Cut

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to prep your boyfriend's hair for the cut:

  1. Wash and dry the hair as you normally would.
  2. Comb through the hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Section the hair into manageable parts using hair ties or clips.
  4. Spray each section of hair with water until it's damp but not soaking wet.

Cutting the Hair

Now that the hair is prepped, it's time to start cutting:

  1. Start with the back of the head and work your way forward. Use your scissors to trim the ends of each section of hair. Be sure to cut straight across and avoid snipping any individual curls.
  2. If you're using clippers, attach a guard that will leave the hair at the desired length. Use the clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of the head.
  3. When you get to the top of the head, use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. You can either cut straight across or use a technique called "point cutting" to create more texture.
  4. Blend the hair on the sides and back with the hair on top using scissors or clippers.

Tips for a Great Cut

Here are some tips to keep in mind as you're cutting your boyfriend's curly hair:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. It's always easier to take off more hair than to add it back on.
  • Don't pull the hair too tight as you're cutting it. This can lead to uneven cuts and an awkward shape.
  • Use point cutting to create more texture and movement in the hair.
  • Check your work as you go. Step back from the hair every so often to make sure everything looks even.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about cutting curly hair:

Should I cut curly hair dry or wet?

It's generally best to cut curly hair when it's wet. This makes it easier to see the shape of the hair and ensures a more even cut. However, if your boyfriend's hair is particularly curly, you may want to cut it dry so you can see how each curl will fall.

How often should I cut curly hair?

Curly hair can be prone to split ends, so it's important to get regular trims. Depending on how fast your boyfriend's hair grows, you may want to cut it every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care after the Cut

Once you've finished cutting your boyfriend's curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage the hair and cause frizz.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance the curls.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to give your boyfriend a great curly haircut at home. Just remember to take your time, start with small snips, and check your work often. Good luck!
