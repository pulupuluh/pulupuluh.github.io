How To Cut Short Hair Step By Step Yourself

Written by Robby Aug 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every time you need a haircut? Do you want to save money and time while still achieving a fresh and stylish look? Then, you've come to the right place! In this article, we will guide you on how to cut short hair step by step yourself. From preparation to aftercare, we've got you covered. So, grab your scissors and let's get started!

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare your workspace. Find a well-lit area with a large mirror, and make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wear comfortable clothing and tie a towel or cape around your neck to catch any loose hair. It's also helpful to have someone to assist you with the cutting process, especially for hard-to-reach areas.

Tools Needed

To cut short hair, you will need the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle
  • Thinning shears (optional)

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean to ensure a smooth and precise cut. Clippers are useful for trimming the sides and back of your head, while a comb and sectioning clips will help you divide your hair into manageable sections. A spray bottle filled with water can help you wet your hair as needed, and thinning shears can be used to remove excess bulk.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is an essential step in cutting short hair. It helps you achieve a more even and balanced cut, especially around the crown and nape areas. Start by dividing your hair into four sections: top, sides, back, and nape. Use sectioning clips to hold each section in place as you work on the others.

Cutting Your Hair

Now that you've sectioned your hair, it's time to start cutting. Start with the sides and back, using the clippers to trim the hair to your desired length. Use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to trim evenly. Move on to the top section, and use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Use the comb to lift the hair and the scissors to cut straight across. Use the thinning shears to remove any excess bulk, if needed. Finally, move on to the nape area, and use the clippers to trim the hair at the base of your neck.

Checking for Symmetry

After you've cut your hair, it's important to check for symmetry. Use the mirror to view your hair from different angles, and make sure each section is cut evenly. Check the length of your hair and make any necessary adjustments. Use the thinning shears to blend any harsh lines or unevenness.

Styling Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. Use a blow dryer and a round brush to add volume and texture to your hair. Apply a styling product, such as gel or mousse, to hold your hair in place. Experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use the mirror to view your hair from different angles and check for symmetry.
  • Watch tutorial videos or ask a friend for help if you're unsure about the cutting process.
  • Be patient and take your time to achieve the desired results.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my hair if it's wet?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry to avoid any shrinkage or unevenness.

Q: How often should I cut my short hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how you want it to look. On average, short hair should be trimmed every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather. Schedule regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your short hair.

Conclusion

Cutting short hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, especially when you do it yourself. By following these step-by-step instructions and using the right tools, you can achieve a fresh and stylish look without breaking the bank. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
