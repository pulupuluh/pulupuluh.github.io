Can You Sleep With Wet Tape In Hair Extensions

Written by Luffy Apr 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a fan of hair extensions, you must have heard of tape-in hair extensions. They're easy to install and provide a natural-looking result. But what happens if you have to go to bed with wet tape-in hair extensions? In this article, we'll answer the question, "Can you sleep with wet tape in hair extensions?" and give you tips on how to care for your hair extensions.

  • Can You Sleep with Wet Tape-In Hair Extensions?
  • How to Dry Wet Tape-In Hair Extensions

  • FAQ

How to Install Tape-In Hair Extensions

Before we talk about sleeping with wet tape-in hair extensions, let's quickly go through the installation process. Tape-in hair extensions are installed by sandwiching a thin strip of your natural hair between two adhesive tape strips. Here's how to install tape-in hair extensions:

  1. Start by parting your hair horizontally where you want to install the hair extensions.
  2. Take a thin section of your hair and attach the top adhesive tape strip to it.
  3. Take another thin section of your hair and sandwich it with the bottom adhesive tape strip.
  4. Repeat the process until you've installed all the hair extensions.

It's essential to follow the manufacturer's instructions for installation to prevent damage to your natural hair.

Can You Sleep with Wet Tape-In Hair Extensions?

The answer is NO. You shouldn't sleep with wet tape-in hair extensions. Sleeping with wet hair extensions can cause tangling, matting, and breakage. The adhesive tape used to install the hair extensions needs to be dry to hold the extensions securely in place. Wet tape can come loose, causing the extensions to fall out or slip, damaging your natural hair.

How to Dry Wet Tape-In Hair Extensions

If you've accidentally gone to bed with wet tape-in hair extensions, don't panic. Here's how you can dry them:

  1. Gently towel-dry your hair extensions to remove excess water.
  2. Use a hairdryer on a cool setting to dry the hair extensions.
  3. Once they're dry, gently brush them to remove any tangles.

Don't use a hairdryer on a hot setting as it can damage the adhesive tape and cause the hair extensions to slip or fall out.

Tips for Sleeping with Tape-In Hair Extensions

Here are some tips for sleeping with tape-in hair extensions:

  • Always make sure your hair extensions are dry before going to bed.
  • Tie your hair extensions in a loose braid or ponytail to prevent tangling.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid sleeping on wet hair as it can cause damage to your natural hair and hair extensions.

How to Care for Tape-In Hair Extensions

Caring for your tape-in hair extensions is crucial to ensure they last longer and look better. Here are some tips for caring for your tape-in hair extensions:

  • Brush your hair extensions gently with a soft-bristled brush to prevent tangling.
  • Wash your hair extensions with sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Don't use hot styling tools on your hair extensions as they can damage the adhesive tape.
  • Don't pull or tug on your hair extensions as it can cause breakage.

FAQ

Q: How long do tape-in hair extensions last?

A: Tape-in hair extensions can last up to six to eight weeks with proper care.

Q: Can I swim with tape-in hair extensions?

A: Yes, you can swim with tape-in hair extensions, but it's best to wear a swim cap to protect them from chlorine and saltwater. After swimming, rinse your hair extensions with clean water and gently towel-dry them.

Q: Can I dye my tape-in hair extensions?

A: Yes, you can dye your tape-in hair extensions, but it's best to consult a professional stylist to ensure the dye is compatible with the adhesive tape.

In conclusion, sleeping with wet tape-in hair extensions can cause damage to your natural hair and hair extensions. Always make sure your hair extensions are dry before going to bed and follow the care instructions for longer-lasting and healthier-looking hair extensions.
