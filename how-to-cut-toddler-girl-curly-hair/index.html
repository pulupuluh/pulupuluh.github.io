How To Cut Toddler Girl Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver May 29, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting your toddler girl’s curly hair can be a daunting task, especially if you’ve never done it before. But with the right tools and techniques, you can give your little one a beautiful haircut that she’ll love. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to cut toddler girl curly hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Toddler Girl Curly Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Toddler Girl Curly Hair
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care for Toddler Girl Curly Hair

How to Cut Toddler Girl Curly Hair

Before you get started, make sure you have the right tools. You’ll need a good pair of scissors, a fine-tooth comb, a spray bottle filled with water, and a mirror. You may also want to have some hair clips on hand to help keep your toddler’s hair in place.

When it comes to cutting curly hair, it’s important to remember that curls will bounce up once they’re dry. This means you’ll need to cut the hair a little longer than you want it to be when it’s dry.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your toddler girl’s curly hair:

  1. Wet your toddler’s hair with the spray bottle.
  2. Comb through the hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Part the hair down the middle and clip one side out of the way.
  4. Starting at the back of the head, take a small section of hair between your fingers and cut it to the desired length.
  5. Continue working your way around the head, taking small sections of hair and cutting them to the same length.
  6. Once you’ve finished one side, unclip the other side and repeat the process.
  7. When you’re finished cutting, comb through the hair again to make sure it’s even.
  8. If you notice any uneven spots, trim them with your scissors.
  9. Style the hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Toddler Girl Curly Hair

Here are some tips to make the process easier:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Work slowly and carefully to avoid cutting unevenly.
  • Use hair clips to keep the hair in place while you’re working.
  • If your toddler is wiggly, consider having someone else hold her still while you cut her hair.
  • If you’re not sure how short to cut the hair, start with a longer length and trim it gradually until you reach the desired length.

FAQ

Can I cut my toddler’s curly hair when it’s dry?

It’s best to cut curly hair when it’s wet so you can see the natural curl pattern and cut accordingly.

What if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut too much hair, it’s best to stop cutting and wait for it to grow out. If you notice any uneven spots, trim them with your scissors.

Hair Care for Toddler Girl Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair, as it can cause frizz.
  • Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to help define the curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair.
  • Instead, let the hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer.

By following these tips, you can help your toddler girl’s curly hair look its best!
