How To Clean Thinning Scissors A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Mar 09, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Thinning scissors are an essential tool for any hairdresser or stylist. They are used to create texture and movement in haircuts and are an essential part of any hair salon. However, like any tool, they need to be cleaned and maintained regularly to ensure they work properly and last a long time. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cleaning thinning scissors, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Clean Thinning Scissors
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cleaning Thinning Scissors
  • FAQs

How to Clean Thinning Scissors

Cleaning thinning scissors is a simple process that can be done with a few basic tools. Here are the things you will need:

  • Warm water
  • Dish soap or shampoo
  • A soft-bristled brush
  • A clean, dry towel
  • Rubbing alcohol

Before you begin cleaning your thinning scissors, make sure they are cool and dry. Never attempt to clean them when they are still hot. This can damage the blades and cause injury.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to clean your thinning scissors:

  1. Mix warm water and dish soap or shampoo in a bowl.
  2. Dip the soft-bristled brush into the soapy water and gently scrub the blades of the scissors.
  3. Rinse the blades under warm running water to remove any soap residue.
  4. Dry the blades with a clean, dry towel.
  5. Apply a small amount of rubbing alcohol to a cotton ball or pad.
  6. Wipe the blades with the cotton ball or pad, making sure to remove any debris or buildup.
  7. Allow the rubbing alcohol to evaporate completely before using the scissors again.

Tips for Cleaning Thinning Scissors

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cleaning your thinning scissors:

  • Never use harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners on your thinning scissors.
  • Always dry the blades thoroughly before storing the scissors.
  • Store your thinning scissors in a dry, cool place to prevent rust and corrosion.
  • Oil your thinning scissors regularly to keep them working smoothly.
  • Consider investing in a professional sharpening service to keep your scissors in top condition.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cleaning thinning scissors:

How often should I clean my thinning scissors?

You should clean your thinning scissors after every use to prevent buildup and maintain their performance.

Can I use regular soap to clean my thinning scissors?

Yes, you can use dish soap or shampoo to clean your thinning scissors. Just make sure to rinse them thoroughly afterward.

Should I oil my thinning scissors after cleaning them?

Yes, it's a good idea to oil your thinning scissors after cleaning them to keep them working smoothly.

Hair Care Tips

Cleaning your thinning scissors is just one part of maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair in top condition:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental stressors.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients to promote strong, healthy hair.
  • Consider using hair treatments, such as deep conditioning masks and oils, to nourish and repair your hair.

By following these tips and regularly cleaning your thinning scissors, you can keep your hair and your tools in top condition.
