What Causes A Lot Of Hair Shedding Understanding The Factors

Written by Christine Mar 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you experiencing hair shedding more than the usual? Hair shedding is a common phenomenon that happens to everyone, but excessive hair shedding can be concerning. There are various reasons why you might be experiencing a lot of hair shedding, and it is essential to understand the factors that contribute to it. In this article, we will discuss the causes of hair shedding and provide tips on how to manage it.

What is Hair Shedding?

Hair shedding is a natural process where hair falls out from the scalp. Typically, a person loses around 50-100 strands of hair per day, which is considered normal. Hair shedding occurs in different phases, and each hair strand has its growth cycle. Hair growth and shedding are normal biological processes that happen to everyone.

What Causes a Lot of Hair Shedding?

Experiencing a lot of hair shedding can be due to various reasons. Here are some common factors that contribute to excessive hair shedding:

1. Hormonal Changes

Hormonal imbalances can cause hair shedding. For instance, women who have just given birth or have gone through menopause may experience hair shedding due to changes in hormone levels. Hormonal changes can affect the hair growth cycle, leading to more hair shedding than usual.

2. Nutritional Deficiencies

Nutritional deficiencies, such as iron, vitamin D, and protein, can cause hair shedding. Lack of these essential nutrients can weaken hair follicles, leading to hair loss. It is essential to have a balanced diet to maintain healthy hair.

3. Stress

Stress can cause hair shedding. Physical or emotional stress can lead to hair loss. The stress hormone cortisol can affect the hair growth cycle, leading to more hair shedding than usual.

4. Medical Conditions

Medical conditions, such as thyroid disorders, autoimmune diseases, and scalp infections, can cause hair shedding. These conditions can affect the hair growth cycle and lead to hair loss.

5. Hairstyling Practices

Hairstyling practices, such as tight braids, weaves, and ponytails, can cause hair shedding. These styles pull on the hair, causing tension on the hair follicles, leading to hair loss.

How to Manage Hair Shedding?

Here are some tips on how to manage hair shedding:

1. Maintain a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet with essential nutrients, such as iron, vitamin D, and protein, can help manage hair shedding. These nutrients can strengthen hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth.

2. Practice Stress Management Techniques

Practicing stress management techniques, such as meditation, yoga, and deep breathing, can help manage hair shedding. These techniques can reduce stress levels and prevent hair loss.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoiding tight hairstyles, such as braids, weaves, and ponytails, can help manage hair shedding. These styles can cause tension on the hair follicles, leading to hair loss.

4. Use Gentle Hair Products

Using gentle hair products and avoiding harsh chemicals can help manage hair shedding. Harsh chemicals can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is hair shedding normal?

Yes, hair shedding is a normal biological process that happens to everyone. A person typically loses around 50-100 strands of hair per day, which is considered normal.

2. How much hair shedding is considered excessive?

Excessive hair shedding is when a person loses more than 100 strands of hair per day. If you notice more hair shedding than usual, it is essential to identify the underlying cause.

3. Can hair shedding be reversed?

Depending on the underlying cause, hair shedding can be reversed. If it is due to nutritional deficiencies, maintaining a balanced diet can help manage hair shedding. If it is due to medical conditions, seeking medical treatment can help manage hair shedding.

Hair Care: Conclusion

Experiencing hair shedding can be concerning, but it is essential to understand the factors that contribute to it. By maintaining a balanced diet, practicing stress management techniques, avoiding tight hairstyles, and using gentle hair products, you can manage hair shedding. If you notice more hair shedding than usual, it is essential to identify the underlying cause and seek medical treatment if necessary.
