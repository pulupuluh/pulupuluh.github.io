Is It Haram To Get A Haircut During Ramadan

Written by William Jun 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

During Ramadan, Muslims observe fasting and engage in spiritual activities to purify their souls. Many people wonder whether it is permissible to get a haircut during this holy month. In this article, we will explore the answer to the question "is it haram to get a haircut during Ramadan?"

Description

In Islam, Ramadan is a time of spiritual reflection, self-improvement, and heightened devotion and worship. Fasting is a fundamental aspect of Ramadan, and Muslims are required to abstain from food, drink, and other physical needs from dawn until sunset. During this time, Muslims are encouraged to engage in prayer, read the Quran, and perform charitable acts.

Many people have questioned whether it is permissible to get a haircut during Ramadan. The short answer is yes, it is permissible. However, there are certain conditions that must be met.

How to

If you are planning to get a haircut during Ramadan, there are a few things to keep in mind:

Choose a suitable time

It is important to choose a suitable time for your haircut. It is not recommended to get a haircut during the last ten days of Ramadan, as this is a time of heightened spiritual activity and devotion.

Observe cleanliness

Before getting a haircut, make sure you are clean and have performed ablution. This is a form of purification and is required before engaging in any form of worship or spiritual activity.

Be respectful

When getting a haircut, be respectful to your barber or hairdresser. Do not engage in idle talk or gossip, and maintain a respectful demeanor at all times.

Step by step

Here are some step-by-step guidelines to follow when getting a haircut during Ramadan:

Step 1: Choose a suitable time

Choose a time that is suitable for you and your barber or hairdresser. Avoid getting a haircut during the last ten days of Ramadan, as this is a time of heightened spiritual activity and devotion.

Step 2: Observe cleanliness

Before getting a haircut, make sure you are clean and have performed ablution. This is a form of purification and is required before engaging in any form of worship or spiritual activity.

Step 3: Be respectful

When getting a haircut, be respectful to your barber or hairdresser. Do not engage in idle talk or gossip, and maintain a respectful demeanor at all times.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a haircut during Ramadan:

Avoid extravagant hairstyles

It is recommended to avoid extravagant hairstyles during Ramadan, as this goes against the spirit of modesty and humility that is encouraged during this month.

Be mindful of your intentions

When getting a haircut, be mindful of your intentions. Make sure you are getting a haircut for the right reasons, and not out of vanity or pride.

FAQ

Is it haram to get a haircut during Ramadan?

No, it is not haram to get a haircut during Ramadan. However, certain conditions must be met, such as observing cleanliness and being respectful.

Can I get a haircut during the last ten days of Ramadan?

It is not recommended to get a haircut during the last ten days of Ramadan, as this is a time of heightened spiritual activity and devotion.

Hair Care

During Ramadan, it is important to take care of your hair, especially if you are fasting. Here are some tips:

Stay hydrated

Drink plenty of water during non-fasting hours to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Eat a balanced diet

Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals to keep your hair strong and healthy.

Avoid excessive styling

Avoid excessive styling, as this can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

In conclusion, getting a haircut during Ramadan is permissible as long as certain conditions are met. It is important to be mindful of your intentions and to observe cleanliness and respect when getting a haircut. Taking care of your hair during Ramadan is also important to maintain healthy and strong hair.
