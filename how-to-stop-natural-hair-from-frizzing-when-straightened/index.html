How To Stop Natural Hair From Frizzing When Straightened

Frizzing is a common problem that people with natural hair face when they straighten their hair. It can be frustrating to spend so much time and effort straightening your hair only to have it frizz up shortly after. In this article, we'll give you tips on how to stop natural hair from frizzing when straightened. Here's what we'll cover:

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Stop Natural Hair from Frizzing When Straightened
  2. Step by Step
  3. Tips
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care

How to Stop Natural Hair from Frizzing When Straightened

The first step to preventing your hair from frizzing when straightened is to make sure that it is properly moisturized. Dry hair is more prone to frizzing, so you want to make sure that your hair is well hydrated before you straighten it. You can use a deep conditioner or a leave-in conditioner to help moisturize your hair.

Another important step is to use a heat protectant before you straighten your hair. Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizzing. A heat protectant will help to protect your hair from the heat and keep it smooth and shiny.

When you straighten your hair, make sure that you use a high-quality flat iron. A cheap flat iron can cause more damage to your hair and make it more prone to frizzing. Look for a flat iron with ceramic plates, as these are less damaging to your hair.

Finally, avoid using too much product on your hair. Overloading your hair with product can weigh it down and make it more prone to frizzing. Use a small amount of product, such as a serum or oil, to keep your hair smooth and shiny.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to stop natural hair from frizzing when straightened:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair and apply a deep conditioner or leave-in conditioner.
  3. Blow dry your hair on a low heat setting until it is about 80% dry.
  4. Apply a heat protectant to your hair.
  5. Divide your hair into sections and clip them up.
  6. Using a high-quality flat iron, straighten each section of hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  7. Apply a small amount of serum or oil to your hair to keep it smooth and shiny.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent your natural hair from frizzing when straightened:

  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair. Use a lower heat setting on your flat iron.
  • Don't straighten your hair too often. Give your hair a break between straightening sessions.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much after you've straightened it. The oils from your hands can make your hair more prone to frizzing.
  • Use a satin pillowcase or bonnet to prevent your hair from rubbing against cotton and causing frizz.

FAQ

How often should I straighten my natural hair?

You should avoid straightening your natural hair too often, as it can cause damage and make your hair more prone to frizzing. Try to limit your straightening sessions to once a month or less, and make sure to use a heat protectant.

What kind of flat iron should I use?

You should look for a flat iron with ceramic plates, as these are less damaging to your hair. Avoid using a cheap flat iron, as it can cause more damage to your hair and make it more prone to frizzing.

What kind of product should I use on my hair?

Use a small amount of product, such as a serum or oil, to keep your hair smooth and shiny. Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh your hair down and make it more prone to frizzing.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for preventing frizzing when straightening natural hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it well moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair.
  • Limit your use of chemical treatments, such as relaxers and perms.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as it can cause breakage and frizzing.

By following these tips, you can prevent your natural hair from frizzing when straightened. Remember to keep your hair well moisturized, use a heat protectant, and avoid using too much product on your hair. With the right care and attention, you can keep your natural hair looking smooth and shiny.
