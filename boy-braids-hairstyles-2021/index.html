Boy Braids Hairstyles 2021 How To Rock The Trend

Written by Christine Aug 28, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you looking for a fresh and trendy hairstyle for your little boy? Look no further than boy braids hairstyles! This versatile hairstyle has been gaining popularity in recent years and continues to be a hit in 2021. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about boy braids hairstyles, including how to achieve them, tips for maintenance, and more.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Achieve Boy Braids Hairstyles
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Braiding Your Boy's Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Boy Braids Hairstyles
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Keeping Your Boy's Braids Healthy

How to Achieve Boy Braids Hairstyles

Boy braids hairstyles consist of braided sections of hair that are either left loose or gathered into a ponytail or bun. There are several different styles to choose from, including cornrows, box braids, and twists. To achieve this hairstyle, you'll need to have at least shoulder-length hair. If your boy's hair is shorter, you may need to wait a few months before attempting boy braids hairstyles.

1. Prepare Your Boy's Hair

Before you start braiding, you'll need to make sure your boy's hair is clean and free of tangles. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to wash his hair, and detangle it with a wide-tooth comb. If your boy has thick or curly hair, you may want to consider using a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner to make the process easier.

2. Section the Hair

Once your boy's hair is detangled, you'll need to section it into small sections using clips or hair ties. The size of the sections will depend on the style you're going for. For cornrows, you'll need to section the hair into small, narrow sections. For box braids or twists, you'll need larger sections.

3. Start Braiding

Now it's time to start braiding! Begin at the front of the head and work your way back, adding sections of hair as you go. You can do a simple three-strand braid, or get more creative with French braids, Dutch braids, or fishtail braids. Make sure to braid tightly and evenly, and secure the ends with a small hair tie or elastic band.

Step-by-Step Guide to Braiding Your Boy's Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve boy braids hairstyles:

Step 1:

Wash and detangle your boy's hair.

Step 2:

Section the hair into small or large sections, depending on the style you want.

Step 3:

Start at the front of the head and work your way back, adding sections of hair as you go.

Step 4:

Braid tightly and evenly, using a three-strand braid, French braid, Dutch braid, or fishtail braid.

Step 5:

Secure the ends with a small hair tie or elastic band.

Tips for Maintaining Boy Braids Hairstyles

Boy braids hairstyles can last for several weeks with proper maintenance. Here are some tips to help you keep your boy's braids looking fresh:

1. Cover the Hair at Night

Use a satin or silk pillowcase, or a satin/silk scarf, to protect your boy's braids while he sleeps. This will help prevent frizz and keep the braids looking neat.

2. Moisturize the Scalp

Apply a light oil, such as coconut oil or jojoba oil, to your boy's scalp every few days to keep it moisturized. This will help prevent itching and dryness.

3. Avoid Pulling or Tugging

Avoid pulling or tugging on your boy's braids, as this can cause them to loosen or fall out. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle any knots or tangles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take to braid a boy's hair?

A: The amount of time it takes to braid a boy's hair will depend on the style you're going for and the length and thickness of his hair. Expect to spend anywhere from 1-3 hours on the process.

Q: How often should I wash my boy's hair when he has braids?

A: You should aim to wash your boy's hair every 1-2 weeks when he has braids. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner and rinse thoroughly to prevent build-up.

Q: Can boys with short hair get braids?

A: Boys with short hair may not be able to achieve some of the more complex braided styles, but they can still have braids. Consider doing small, simple braids on the top of the head, or adding braids to the sides or back of the hair.

Hair Care Tips for Keeping Your Boy's Braids Healthy

In addition to the tips mentioned above, there are a few other things you can do to keep your boy's braids healthy and looking great:

1. Avoid Chemical Treatments

Avoid using chemical treatments, such as relaxers or perms, on your boy's hair while he has braids. These treatments can weaken the hair and cause breakage.

2. Trim the Ends

Trim the ends of your boy's hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage. Use sharp scissors and cut off no more than 1/4 inch at a time.

3. Protect from the Sun

When your boy is outside, protect his braids from the sun and wind by using a hat or scarf. This will help prevent dryness and damage.

Boy braids hairstyles are a fun and stylish way to switch up your little one's look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a variety of styles that will keep your boy looking cool and confident. Use the tips and tricks in this article to keep your boy's braids looking fresh for weeks on end.
