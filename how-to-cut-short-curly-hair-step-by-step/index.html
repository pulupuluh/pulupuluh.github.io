How To Cut Short Curly Hair Step By Step

Written by Petter Apr 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Short curly hair can be tricky to cut, as the curls can bounce up and change the length of the hair. However, with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve the perfect short curly haircut. In this article, we will guide you through how to cut short curly hair step by step and provide some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Short Curly Hair
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips and Tricks
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After the Cut

How to Cut Short Curly Hair

Cutting short curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. You need to consider the curl pattern, the length, and the shape of the hair. Here are some general tips to keep in mind:

  • Use sharp scissors to make clean cuts and avoid frizz.
  • Cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and look longer than it is.
  • Start with small sections and cut less than you think you need to. You can always go back and cut more later.

Step by Step Guide

Now let's dive into the step by step process of cutting short curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning the hair. Comb through the hair to remove any tangles and let it air dry.
  2. Section the hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to keep the sections separate.
  3. Take a small section of hair from one of the front sections. Hold it between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting from the bottom and moving upwards. Don't cut straight across, as this can create a blunt look.
  4. Continue cutting small sections from the front sections, moving from one side of the head to the other. Use the first section as a guide for the length of the rest of the sections.
  5. Move to the back sections and repeat the same process. Make sure to cut the hair at an angle and not straight across.
  6. Blend the front and back sections together by taking small sections of hair from the sides and cutting them at an angle to match the length of the rest of the hair.
  7. Check the length and shape of the hair from all angles. Make any necessary adjustments by cutting small sections at a time.
  8. Use thinning shears to remove any bulk or create texture in the hair.
  9. Style the hair as desired using a diffuser or air-drying for a natural look.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips to make cutting short curly hair easier:

  • Use a spray bottle to mist the hair with water if it starts to dry out during the cutting process.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting curly hair can be time-consuming, but the results are worth it.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and shapes to find what works best for your hair.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting short curly hair:

Can you cut curly hair with regular scissors?

You can, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors can create blunt ends and cause frizz. It's best to use sharp, professional hair cutting scissors.

How often should you cut short curly hair?

It depends on the individual's hair growth and style preferences. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good time to get a trim to maintain the shape of the haircut.

Can you cut curly hair when it's wet?

You can, but it's not recommended. Wet hair can stretch and look longer than it is, which can result in uneven cuts. It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry.

Hair Care After the Cut

After cutting short curly hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep the hair moisturized and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of the haircut.

By following these steps and tips, you can cut short curly hair with confidence and achieve the perfect look. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and shapes to find what works best for you.
