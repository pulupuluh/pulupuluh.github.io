How To Make Your Dog Stop Shedding So Much Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Apr 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a dog owner, you know that shedding is a fact of life. But what if your dog sheds excessively? All that fur can be a nuisance, not to mention a potential allergen for some people. In this guide, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to make your dog stop shedding so much.

Table of Contents

  • Reasons Why Dogs Shed
  • Diet and Nutrition
  • Grooming Habits
  • Supplements and Vitamins
  • Medical Causes of Excessive Shedding
  • FAQs

Reasons Why Dogs Shed

Before we dive into the tips, it's important to understand why dogs shed in the first place. Most dogs shed as a natural part of their growth cycle. As old fur falls out, new fur grows in its place. Some breeds shed more than others, such as Huskies and Golden Retrievers. Other reasons for shedding can include:

  • Seasonal changes (ex: spring and fall)
  • Stress or anxiety
  • Poor diet
  • Pregnancy or lactation
  • Medical conditions (ex: thyroid issues or allergies)

Now that we know why dogs shed, let's talk about what we can do to reduce shedding.

Diet and Nutrition

One of the most important factors in reducing shedding is a healthy diet. A diet that is high in protein and healthy fats can help improve your dog's coat and reduce shedding. Make sure your dog is getting enough omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids, which can be found in fish and flaxseed oil. Avoid foods with fillers and artificial preservatives, as these can contribute to poor coat health.

Grooming Habits

Regular grooming is another important factor in reducing shedding. Brushing your dog's coat regularly can help remove dead fur and prevent matting. Use a deshedding tool to remove loose fur and stimulate oil production in the skin. Bathe your dog regularly, but not too often, as this can strip the coat of natural oils. Use a gentle shampoo that is specifically formulated for dogs.

When grooming, make sure to pay attention to your dog's ears, nails, and teeth as well. Regular cleaning and maintenance can help prevent infections and other health issues that can contribute to shedding.

Supplements and Vitamins

In addition to a healthy diet, your dog may benefit from supplements and vitamins that promote coat health. Omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids can be found in fish oil supplements. Biotin and vitamin E can also help improve coat quality. Talk to your vet about the best supplements for your dog's specific needs.

Medical Causes of Excessive Shedding

If your dog is shedding excessively despite your efforts to improve their diet and grooming habits, there may be an underlying medical condition. Thyroid issues, allergies, and other health problems can all cause excessive shedding. Talk to your vet about any concerns you have about your dog's shedding.

FAQs

How often should I brush my dog?

This depends on your dog's breed and coat type. Long-haired dogs may need to be brushed daily, while short-haired dogs may only need to be brushed once a week. Talk to your vet or a professional groomer for advice on how often to groom your specific dog.

Can I use human shampoo on my dog?

No, human shampoo is not formulated for use on dogs and can be too harsh for their skin and coat. Use a shampoo that is specifically formulated for dogs.

What if my dog hates being groomed?

Some dogs may not enjoy being groomed, but it's important to make it a positive experience for them. Use treats and praise to reward good behavior, and start with short grooming sessions that gradually increase in length. If your dog has a particularly difficult time, consider taking them to a professional groomer.

Conclusion

Reducing shedding in dogs requires a combination of healthy diet, regular grooming, and potentially supplements or medical treatment. By following the tips in this guide, you can help improve your dog's coat health and reduce shedding.
