Description

Cutting hair on the sides is an essential part of any haircut. It helps to create a balanced look, highlights the features of the face, and adds texture to the hair. However, it can be challenging to know how high to cut hair on sides, especially for those who are new to cutting hair.

Whether you are a professional hairstylist or a beginner, this guide will provide you with all the information you need to know about cutting hair on the sides.

How to Cut Hair on Sides

Before cutting hair on the sides, it is essential to have the right tools, including scissors, clippers, and a comb. It is also crucial to have a clear understanding of the client's hair type, face shape, and desired hairstyle.

When it comes to cutting hair on the sides, there are two main techniques: using clippers or scissors. Clippers are best for creating a shorter, more precise cut, while scissors are perfect for creating a more textured, layered look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut hair on the sides using scissors:

  1. Start by sectioning off the hair on the top of the head.
  2. Take a small section of hair on one side of the head and comb it straight down.
  3. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it to the desired length.
  4. Repeat this process on the other side of the head.
  5. Blend the hair on the sides with the hair on the top of the head using scissors or thinning shears.
  6. Check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Finish the haircut by trimming the hair on the back of the head and around the ears.

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut hair on the sides using clippers:

  1. Start by selecting the right clipper guard size for the desired length.
  2. Section off the hair on the top of the head.
  3. Begin cutting the hair on one side of the head by starting at the bottom and working your way up.
  4. Repeat this process on the other side of the head.
  5. Blend the hair on the sides with the hair on the top of the head using scissors or thinning shears.
  6. Check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Finish the haircut by trimming the hair on the back of the head and around the ears.

Tips for Cutting Hair on Sides

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting hair on the sides:

  • Always start with a clean and dry head of hair.
  • Use a comb to keep the hair in place while cutting.
  • Take your time and work slowly to avoid any mistakes.
  • Blend the hair on the sides with the hair on the top of the head to create a seamless look.
  • Use thinning shears to create a more textured, layered look.
  • Check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments before finishing the haircut.

FAQs

Q: How high should I cut my hair on the sides?

A: The height you should cut your hair on the sides depends on the desired hairstyle and face shape. It is best to consult with a professional hairstylist for personalized advice.

Q: Can I cut my own hair on the sides?

A: While it is possible to cut your own hair on the sides, it is recommended to seek the help of a professional hairstylist to avoid any mistakes.

Hair Care After Cutting Hair on Sides

After cutting hair on the sides, it is essential to take care of the hair to maintain its health and appearance. This includes washing the hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and trimming the hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a perfectly balanced and stylish haircut that highlights your best features and complements your face shape.
