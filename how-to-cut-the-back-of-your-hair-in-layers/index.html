How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair In Layers Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 19, 2023 · 9 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll guide you through how to cut the back of your hair in layers, step by step. Whether you're looking to freshen up your current style or try something new, layering your hair can add texture and movement to your locks.

Description

Layering is a popular hair cutting technique that involves cutting different lengths of hair throughout the hair shaft, creating a layered effect. This technique can be used to add volume, texture, and movement to your hair. While layering the front of your hair is relatively easy, cutting the back of your hair in layers can be more challenging. However, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking layered cut.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair in Layers: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also helpful to have a handheld mirror to check the back of your hair as you cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off your hair. Use the comb to create a straight part down the middle of your head, dividing your hair into two sections. Then, use the comb to create another part from ear to ear, separating the top section of your hair from the bottom section. Clip the top section of your hair up and out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer of your hair, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length, angling the scissors slightly upward to create a soft, blended look. Repeat this process, working your way around the back of your head.

Step 4: Layer the Bottom Section

Once you've cut the bottom layer, it's time to layer the hair. Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors parallel to the hair shaft and make small, diagonal cuts into the hair, creating a textured effect. Repeat this process, working your way around the back of your head.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Once you've finished layering the bottom section, release the top section of your hair and repeat the process. Comb a small section of hair straight down and cut off the desired length, angling the scissors slightly upward. Work your way around the back of your head, cutting the top layer of your hair.

Step 6: Layer the Top Section

Once you've cut the top layer, it's time to layer the hair. Take a small section of hair from the top layer and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors parallel to the hair shaft and make small, diagonal cuts into the hair, creating a textured effect. Repeat this process, working your way around the back of your head.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure your layers are even and balanced.

Tips for Cutting the Back of Your Hair in Layers

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect layered cut:

  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Make sure your hair is dry and straight before you start cutting.
  • Work in a well-lit area with a clean surface to avoid any accidents.
  • Take your time and work slowly to avoid mistakes.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair in layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair in layers. However, it's important to remember that curly hair tends to bounce up when it dries, so you may need to cut more length than you think you need to achieve the desired look.

How often should I cut my hair in layers?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the health and shape of your hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair in Layers

After cutting your hair in layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your layers.
  • Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

Cutting the back of your hair in layers can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps and tips to achieve the perfect layered cut and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
