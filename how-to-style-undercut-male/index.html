How To Style Undercut Male A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are looking to switch up your hairstyle, the undercut is a great option for men. This style has been popular for years and shows no signs of slowing down. In this guide, we will show you how to style undercut male hair in a relaxed English language. We will cover everything from how to get the right cut to the best products to use for styling.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get the Undercut
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling
  • Tips for Maintaining the Undercut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Undercut Male

How to Get the Undercut

The undercut is a hairstyle where the hair on the sides and back is shaved or cut short, while the hair on top is left longer. To get this style, you will need to visit a barber or stylist. Here are some tips to help you get the perfect undercut:

  • Bring a picture of the style you want to show your barber or stylist
  • Discuss the length and style you want for the top and sides of your hair
  • Consider the shape of your face when deciding on the length of your hair
  • Choose a barber or stylist who has experience with the undercut hairstyle

Once you have the right cut, you can start styling your undercut male hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling

Styling an undercut male hair is easy and requires just a few simple steps. Here is a step-by-step guide to help you:

  1. Start with clean, towel-dried hair
  2. Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair, such as a pomade or wax
  3. Use a comb or your fingers to style your hair to the desired shape and height
  4. Blow dry your hair using a round brush to add volume and shape
  5. Finish with a light hairspray to hold the style in place

It's important to note that the amount of product you use will depend on the length and thickness of your hair. You may need to experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type.

Tips for Maintaining the Undercut

Now that you know how to style undercut male hair, it's important to maintain the style to keep it looking fresh. Here are some tips to help you:

  • Get regular trims to keep the hair on the sides and back short
  • Wash your hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it greasy
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before styling
  • Experiment with different styles to keep your look fresh and interesting

FAQs

Can I style an undercut male hair without using any product?

While it is possible to style an undercut without using any product, it may not hold its shape for very long. Using a styling product can help to add volume, shape, and hold to your hair.

Do I need to blow dry my hair every time I style it?

No, you don't need to blow dry your hair every time you style it. However, blow drying can help to add volume and shape to your hair, which can make it easier to style.

Can I style an undercut with curly hair?

Yes, you can style an undercut with curly hair. However, you may need to use a different styling product that is designed for curly hair.

Hair Care for Undercut Male

Maintaining the health of your hair is important when styling an undercut male hair. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools too often
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy
  • Avoid pulling on your hair too much, as this can cause breakage and damage

By following these tips, you can keep your undercut male hair looking great and feeling healthy.

Conclusion

The undercut male style is a classic look that never goes out of fashion. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can learn how to style undercut male hair and keep it looking great. Remember to experiment with different products and styles to find the one that works best for you. With a little practice, you'll be able to rock the undercut like a pro.
