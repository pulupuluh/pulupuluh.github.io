How To Hide An Undercut Growing Out Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Aug 28, 2023 Â· 11 min read

If you're growing out an undercut, you know how frustrating it can be when your hair starts to look uneven and awkward. Fortunately, there are several ways to hide an undercut while it grows out. In this article, we'll discuss some simple tips and tricks to help you conceal your undercut and maintain a polished look.

How to Hide an Undercut Growing Out

First, let's talk about the basics. An undercut is a hairstyle where the sides and/or back of the head are shaved or cut very short, while the top is left longer. If you're growing out an undercut, it means that you're trying to let the shaved or short hair grow in, so that it's more even with the longer hair on top.

To conceal an undercut while it grows out, you can try one or more of the following techniques:

1. Part your hair differently

One simple way to hide an undercut is to part your hair differently. If your undercut is on one side of your head, try parting your hair on the other side. This will help cover up the shaved or short hair, and create the illusion of a more even hairline.

2. Use hair accessories

Hair accessories like headbands, clips, and bobby pins can be a lifesaver when you're growing out an undercut. Use them to pin back any shorter or uneven sections of hair, or to create a more polished and put-together look.

3. Try a new hairstyle

If you're feeling adventurous, try experimenting with new hairstyles to help conceal your undercut. For example, you could try a messy bun, a low ponytail, or a side braid. Play around with different styles and see what works best for you.

4. Get regular trims

While you're growing out your undercut, it's important to get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy. Your stylist can help you blend in the shorter sections of hair and maintain a more even length as your hair grows.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Hide an Undercut Growing Out

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you hide an undercut while it grows out:

Step 1: Wash and condition your hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a gentle, nourishing shampoo and conditioner. This will help keep your hair healthy and strong as it grows out.

Step 2: Towel-dry your hair

Gently towel-dry your hair to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Step 3: Apply a styling product

Apply a styling product like mousse or gel to help tame any flyaways or frizz. This will help create a more polished and put-together look.

Step 4: Part your hair

Part your hair in a way that helps conceal your undercut. For example, if your undercut is on one side of your head, try parting your hair on the other side.

Step 5: Use hair accessories

Use hair accessories like headbands, clips, and bobby pins to help conceal any shorter or uneven sections of hair. Play around with different styles and see what works best for you.

Step 6: Get regular trims

Visit your stylist regularly to get trims and maintain a more even hairline. Your stylist can help you blend in the shorter sections of hair and keep your hair looking neat and tidy as it grows out.

Tips for Hiding an Undercut Growing Out

Here are some additional tips to help you conceal your undercut while it grows out:

1. Be patient

Growing out an undercut takes time, so be patient and don't get discouraged. With a little bit of patience and some creative styling, you'll be able to hide your undercut and maintain a polished look.

2. Use heat protectant

When styling your hair, be sure to use a heat protectant to help prevent damage and breakage. This will help keep your hair healthy and strong as it grows out.

3. Don't be afraid to experiment

Experiment with different hairstyles, hair accessories, and products to find what works best for you. Don't be afraid to try something new!

4. Take good care of your hair

Finally, be sure to take good care of your hair by eating a healthy diet, using gentle products, and avoiding excessive heat and chemical treatments. This will help keep your hair healthy and strong as it grows out.

FAQ: How to Hide an Undercut Growing Out

Q: How long does it take to grow out an undercut?

A: The length of time it takes to grow out an undercut depends on several factors, including the length of your hair, the rate of your hair growth, and the style of your undercut. Generally, it can take several months to a year or more to fully grow out an undercut.

Q: Can I dye my hair while growing out an undercut?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair while growing out an undercut. However, it's important to be gentle with your hair and avoid excessive chemical treatments, as these can cause damage and breakage.

Q: Will my hair look uneven while growing out an undercut?

A: Yes, your hair may look uneven while growing out an undercut. However, with regular trims and creative styling, you can help conceal the shorter sections of hair and maintain a polished look.

Hair Care for Hiding an Undercut Growing Out

When hiding an undercut while it grows out, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips to help keep your hair healthy and strong:

1. Eat a healthy diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth and keep your hair strong and healthy.

2. Use gentle products

Use gentle, nourishing products that are free from harsh chemicals and sulfates. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent damage and breakage.

3. Avoid excessive heat and chemical treatments

Avoid using excessive heat and chemical treatments like perms, relaxers, and bleaches, as these can cause damage and breakage.

4. Protect your hair from the sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray. This will help prevent damage and breakage caused by UV rays.

5. Get regular trims

Visit your stylist regularly to get trims and maintain a healthy hairline. Your stylist can help you blend in the shorter sections of hair and keep your hair looking neat and tidy as it grows out.

By following these tips and tricks, you can easily hide an undercut while it grows out and maintain a polished, put-together look. Remember to be patient, experiment with different styles, and take good care of your hair for the best results.
