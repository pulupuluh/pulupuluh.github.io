How To Cut Short Layers On Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 31, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have short hair and are looking for a way to add some texture and volume, cutting short layers is the perfect solution. Short layers can help create movement and dimension in your hair, giving it a fresh and edgy look. In this article, we will show you how to cut short layers on short hair in a few simple steps.

Description

Cutting short layers is a great way to add volume and texture to short hair, making it look fuller and more dynamic. Short layers can also help create a more defined shape, making your hair look more polished and put-together. Whether you have a pixie cut, a bob, or any other short hairstyle, adding layers can transform your look and make your hair more versatile.

How to Cut Short Layers on Short Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to help section your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow-drying it. Make sure your hair is fully dry before you start cutting it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into four parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Clip each section out of the way so you can work on one section at a time.

Step 3: Cut the Top Section

Starting with the top section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the middle of the section and working your way down. Repeat this process on the rest of the top section, making sure to cut each section at a slightly different angle to create texture and movement.

Step 4: Cut the Side Sections

Next, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up, as you did with the top section. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the middle and working your way down. Repeat this process on the rest of the side sections, making sure to cut each section at a slightly different angle to create texture and movement.

Step 5: Cut the Back Section

Finally, move on to the back section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up, as you did with the other sections. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the middle and working your way down. Repeat this process on the rest of the back section, making sure to cut each section at a slightly different angle to create texture and movement.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all the sections, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and cut into them, using a feathering motion to create a seamless transition between the layers.

Tips for Cutting Short Layers on Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting short layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure precision and control.
  • Cut the hair at an angle to create texture and movement.
  • Make sure to cut each section at a slightly different angle to create a natural-looking effect.
  • Blend the layers together to create a seamless transition.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean cuts and avoid damaging your hair.

FAQ

Can I cut short layers on my own?

Yes, you can cut short layers on your own, but it's important to be careful and take your time. Make sure you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully to avoid any mistakes.

How often should I cut my short layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers lose their shape. As a general rule, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

Hair Care after Cutting Short Layers on Short Hair

After cutting short layers, it's important to take good care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips to follow:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it look dull.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can cut short layers on short hair and create a stylish and versatile look that suits your personality and lifestyle.
