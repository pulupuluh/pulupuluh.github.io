How To Use Thinning Shears For Short Hair In 2023

If you have short hair, you may be looking for ways to add volume and texture to your locks. One tool that can help you achieve this is thinning shears. These scissors are designed to remove some of the hair's thickness without sacrificing length. In this article, we'll show you how to use thinning shears for short hair in 2023.

Description of Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one or both blades. These teeth are used to remove some of the hair's thickness, which can help create a more textured, layered look. Thinning shears are especially useful for people with thick, heavy hair who want to add some movement and volume to their locks.

How to Use Thinning Shears for Short Hair

Using thinning shears for short hair requires some finesse, but it's not difficult once you get the hang of it. Here's what you need to do:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important to work with clean, dry hair so you can see how much you're thinning.
  2. Section your hair. Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't accidentally cut too much hair at once.
  3. Choose the right shears. You'll want to use thinning shears that are specifically designed for short hair. Look for shears with smaller teeth and a shorter blade length.
  4. Hold the shears properly. Grasp the shears with your dominant hand and hold them at a slight angle. Make sure the teeth are pointing in the direction of your hair's growth.
  5. Take a small section of hair. Start with a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Make sure the section is no wider than the width of the shears.
  6. Use the shears to cut the hair. Place the shears about halfway down the section of hair and make a few quick snips. Keep the shears at a slight angle to create a layered effect.
  7. Repeat on all sections. Continue working through your hair, section by section, until you've thinned out as much hair as you want. Be careful not to overdo it, as too much thinning can make your hair look stringy and uneven.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears for short hair:

  • Start with a small amount of thinning. You can always thin more later, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut.
  • Don't thin too close to the roots. This can create an uneven, choppy look.
  • Be gentle. Thinning shears are sharp and can easily damage your hair if you're too rough.
  • Use the right shears. Make sure you're using thinning shears that are designed for short hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I use thinning shears on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use thinning shears on curly hair. However, you'll need to be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can create an uneven, choppy look. It's also a good idea to work with a stylist who has experience using thinning shears on curly hair.

Q: Will thinning shears damage my hair?

A: Thinning shears can damage your hair if you're too rough or if you use them too frequently. However, if you use them properly and only thin your hair occasionally, you shouldn't experience any damage.

Q: How often should I use thinning shears?

A: It's best to use thinning shears only occasionally, such as once every three to four months. Thinning your hair too often can cause damage and uneven growth.

Hair Care Tips

After using thinning shears on your short hair, it's important to take good care of your locks to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are a few hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

By following these tips, you can keep your short, thinned-out locks looking their best all year round.
