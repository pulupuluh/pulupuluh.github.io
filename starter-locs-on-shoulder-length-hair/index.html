Starter Locs On Shoulder Length Hair Everything You Need To Know

Written by William Jul 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to start your loc journey and have shoulder length hair, you're in the right place! In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about starter locs on shoulder length hair, from the process to tips and hair care. So, let's get started!

Description

Starter locs are the beginning stage of locs, where the hair is twisted or braided to start the locking process. Shoulder length hair is perfect for starter locs, as it's long enough to hold the twists or braids, but not too long that it becomes difficult to manage. The process of starting locs can take a few hours, depending on the thickness and length of your hair.

How to

The process of starting locs on shoulder length hair involves the following steps:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or product residue. This will help your hair lock better.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small, equal parts using a comb. The size of the sections will determine the size of your locs.

Step 3: Twist or Braid Each Section

Twist or braid each section of hair tightly, starting from the root and working your way down to the ends. Use a locking gel or cream to help hold the twists or braids in place.

Step 4: Dry Your Hair

Allow your hair to air dry or sit under a hooded dryer until your hair is completely dry. This can take several hours, so be patient.

Step 5: Maintain Your Locs

Once your starter locs have formed, you'll need to maintain them by retwisting or interlocking your hair every 4-6 weeks. You should also avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your locs.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you with your starter loc journey:

  • Be patient - the process of starting locs can take time
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your locs
  • Retwist or interlock your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your locs
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping on a satin pillowcase or wearing a satin bonnet
  • Stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth

FAQ

Q: Can I start locs on relaxed hair?

A: Yes, you can start locs on relaxed hair. However, it's important to note that the relaxed hair will eventually break off as the new growth comes in. So, it's best to have a plan in place for transitioning from relaxed hair to natural hair.

Q: How long does it take for starter locs to form?

A: It can take anywhere from 3-6 months for your starter locs to fully form, depending on your hair type and how often you maintain them.

Q: Can I wash my hair with starter locs?

A: Yes, you can wash your hair with starter locs. However, it's important to use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or product residue.

Hair Care

To care for your starter locs, you should:

  • Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo every 2-4 weeks
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your locs
  • Retwist or interlock your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your locs
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping on a satin pillowcase or wearing a satin bonnet
  • Stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth

By following these tips and hair care routine, you'll be on your way to healthy and beautiful starter locs on shoulder length hair. Happy locking!
