How To Re Curl A Perm A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have a perm that has lost its curl, you may be wondering how to re curl it. Don't worry, it's not as difficult as it may seem. In this article, we will go over the steps to re curl a perm, as well as some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

How to Re Curl a Perm

Before we get started, it's important to note that re curling a perm can damage your hair, especially if it has already been chemically treated. If you're not sure whether your hair can handle it, consult with a hairstylist.

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help to gently cleanse your hair without stripping it of its natural oils.

After you've washed your hair, gently towel dry it. You don't want to rub your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz. Instead, blot your hair gently with the towel.

Step 2: Apply a Curling Product

Apply a curling product to your hair. This can be a curling mousse, gel, or cream. Make sure to evenly distribute the product throughout your hair.

If your hair is damaged or dry, consider using a leave-in conditioner as well. This will help to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Step 3: Use a Curling Iron

Using a curling iron, take small sections of hair and wrap them around the barrel. Hold the hair in place for a few seconds, then release. Repeat this process until all of your hair has been curled.

Make sure to use a heat protectant spray before using the curling iron. This will help to prevent damage to your hair.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

Once you've curled your hair, finish with a light hairspray. This will help to hold the curls in place and prevent frizz.

Don't use too much hairspray, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Tips for Re Curling a Perm

Here are some tips to make the process of re curling a perm easier:

  • Use a high-quality curling iron
  • Take small sections of hair
  • Use a heat protectant spray
  • Don't use too much hairspray
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner

FAQ

Can I re curl my perm at home?

Yes, you can re curl your perm at home. However, it's important to be careful, as the process can be damaging to your hair.

How long will the curls last?

The length of time the curls will last depends on a variety of factors, including the type of perm you have, the condition of your hair, and the products you use. On average, curls from a perm can last anywhere from a few weeks to a few months.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and prevent damage, it's important to follow a good hair care routine. This includes washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, using a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools, and using a leave-in conditioner if your hair is dry or damaged.

Additionally, try to avoid using too much heat on your hair, and don't brush or comb your hair when it's wet.

Conclusion

Re curling a perm is a great way to refresh your hair and bring back some of the curl that may have been lost. By following the steps outlined in this article, and using the tips and tricks provided, you can re curl your perm at home without damaging your hair.

Remember, if you have any doubts or concerns, consult with a hairstylist.
