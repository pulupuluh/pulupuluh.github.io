How To Thin Bangs Out A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Mar 06, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Thick, heavy bangs can be a real pain to manage, especially on hot summer days. If you're looking to thin out your bangs but don't want to risk a trip to the salon, then this guide is for you. In this article, we'll show you how to thin out your bangs at home using just a few simple tools.

How to Thin Bangs Out

Thinning out your bangs at home is a simple process that just requires a bit of patience and attention to detail. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips

Once you have these items, you're ready to get started. Here's how:

Step 1: Section Off Your Bangs

Start by sectioning off your bangs from the rest of your hair. Use hair clips to hold the rest of your hair back, leaving only your bangs in front of your face.

Step 2: Comb Your Bangs

Using a comb, carefully comb through your bangs to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to see where your hair is thickest and where you need to thin it out.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Using sharp scissors, start cutting your bangs at a slight angle. Be sure to cut small sections at a time, taking care not to cut too much hair at once.

As you cut, comb through your bangs to see how much hair you've removed. You want to aim for a gradual, natural-looking thinning effect, so take your time and pay attention to how your hair is falling.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

Once you've cut your bangs to your desired thickness, check your progress in the mirror. If you're happy with the result, you're done! If you need to thin your bangs out a bit more, repeat steps 2 and 3 until you achieve the look you want.

Tips for Thinning Your Bangs

Thinning out your bangs can be a bit tricky, so here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with dry hair. Wet hair can be difficult to cut and style, so it's best to start with dry hair.
  • Take your time. Thinning your bangs is a process that requires patience and attention to detail. Don't rush it!
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and make it difficult to achieve the look you want.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid any mistakes.

FAQ

Can I thin my bangs out if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still thin out your bangs if you have curly hair. Just be sure to cut your hair while it's dry and be careful not to cut too much at once.

How often should I thin out my bangs?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick your bangs are. As a general rule, you should aim to thin out your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

Thinning out your bangs can put extra stress on your hair, so it's important to take good care of it afterwards. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid heat styling. Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment. This will help to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles. Tight hairstyles can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and looking great, even after thinning out your bangs.
